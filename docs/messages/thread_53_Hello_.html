From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2005-07-01 21:14:39 UTC
Welcome Blue Butterfly!

This is a great group that will be happy to answer questions.
Another resource for you would be the yahoo new boxers group. This
was set up so that veteran boxers could help new ones in a friendly
open forum.

http://groups.yahoo.com/group/newboxers/

They are very patient and eager to answer any question you may
have. There are also stamp carving and other tutorials on
atlasquest (another letterboxing site).

http://www.altasquest.com

And finally there is lots of good information on Silent Doug's site,
including a glossary that lists many commonly used letterboxing
terms:

http://www.letterboxing.info

There are many different ways to enjoy letterboxing. Welcome to our
hobby/sport/pasttime/obsession!

Dewberry of
Gramatrick

From: Amy Proff Lyons (amy@aplcreations.com) | Date: 2008-07-02 16:30:00 UTC
Just checking in to introduce my family and myself.

I've been carving stamps and binding books for years and had never
heard of letterboxing until I stumbled upon letterboxing.org about a
year ago. I bookmarked it for myself until my kiddos were a little
older. Well, they're a little older (7, almost 5 and 1) and we've
been hunting letterboxes for about six weeks now. Our oldest is a
Wolf Scout so we're using letterboxing to bone up his orienteering
skills. Our middle boy likes to count off paces and dig in the dirt.
Our little girl likes to find all the shiny bits and dirt particles
she can and stuff them in her pockets. My husband likes to complain
about the heat until we find the box we're looking for and then he
remembers why this is fun again. We've decided he's definitely a
winter-time letterboxer.

My mom and dad and one of my sisters and her husband have also started
letterboxing in the last month and we're already plotting potential
stops along the highways and byways we'll be travelling this summer.

Take care,
Amy
