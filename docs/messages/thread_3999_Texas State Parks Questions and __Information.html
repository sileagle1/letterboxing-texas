Okay well.
I just talked to the park superintendent at Tyler State Park. He was aware of letterboxing and said "they" had had a discussion about it two or three years ago, but because it was so unknown they opted not to treat it separately, but to lump it together with geocaching. I told him there were a few fundamental differences, but that for their purposes, I could see that rationale. He didn't seem to care to get into the whole philosophical thing, so I didn't push for it. He did say that in his years at the park, only two people had ever inquired about letterboxing - me and a reporter. And he referred the reporter to the geocaching website.
The conversation was going very well, so I went ahead and told him I already had a box there, and it had been there for nearly five years, since before the policy was even a thought. I told him that as we have developed a clearer awareness of the policy, I decided I wanted to get my box "legal", to which he laughed and said they appreciate that. Because of the direction the conversation was going (as in, he was going to look up the website as soon as we hung up), I told him there were two other boxes there and that I had been in touch with the placer to offer my assistance in getting their boxes registered as well.
He emailed me the form and told me we could handle all of it by email. He said his staff was not very well-versed on either geocaching or letterboxing, so they wouldn't really have any idea what I was asking about if I were to try to talk to them and he wasn't there.
He seemed to me to be understanding of our use of the park. I dropped a few keywords like LNT andenvironmentally responsible and cooperative. His experience with geocachers was apparently very positive and it seems he expects the same from us.
So - I'm filling out permits and will email them back to him, then I'll head up there on Sunday afternoon to see about getting the boxes in good order, if they aren't already.
Lucy

Re: [letterboxingtexas] Re: Texas State Parks Questions and Information

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2009-03-12 21:42:44 UTC-05:00
You've been an example in this, Mike, and have said before that we need to get on board if we hope to stay active in state parks. I think the lack of consistency on the part of the parks has made it more difficult to try to decide how to step, and for my part, I've just avoided planting any more boxes in state parks while I mused over it.
The real sticking point for me, and for many of us I think, has been the renewal thing. Easy enough to apply for a permit - another story when it comes to doing the renewal. If all parks are willing tohonor renewals by email, it puts a whole different face on the issue. Heck - Tyler is apparently even willing to do the permit application by email. He may not be so willing for new boxes, but he surely was glad to handle it by email for my existing box. What I recognized would be a problem was if I had to talk to HIM and only him because his staff wasn't up to speed. That would limit the times I could go and get permitted if I had to do it face-to-face. He saw the issue there as well and was willing to be flexible enough to make it work. I guess it's just going to be a matter of figuring out which parks are going to be jerky and avoiding them and conversely, making a point to be friendly with the parks that welcome us.
What I really never saw coming was that a geocacher would create such a problem and force the issue. Not that it couldn't have just as easily been a nutcase letterboxer - we had one a couple of years ago that was pulling boxes because she didn't like the locations and I could easily see her using the policy to justify her actions.
