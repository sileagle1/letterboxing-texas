From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-07-12 21:48:13 UTC-05:00
Well that sure makes it easy, doesn't it???   At least you said choose the first sound I LIKE and go with it!
 
My mother goes by Granny because the guys that used to work on my dad's landscape crews used to call her that because she would get really grumpy with them when they banged up trucks or equipment - they would tell her she sounded like a grumpy old Granny.  She apparently took it as a compliment and that's what my kids call her.  Her mother was Grandmother to me and her dad was Granddaddy.  Her husband, my kids' step-grandfather goes by Granddaddy Arthur and my dad, who is deceased, they just refer to as Granddaddy.  My mother-in-law is BB, her initials, because she couldn't stand to have a "grandma-ish" name.  Her husband was to be GB, his initials, but he wound up being Durr because our nephew, the first grandchild, heard her calling him by his first name and that was as close as he could get.  All the other grandkids call him Grandpa.  My mother-in-law's mother is Nana and everyone knows her by that (I was introduced to her as Nana and called her that when I met her the first time in Junior High!)  Her husband was Papa.  I like Nana because it brings such warm fuzzy feelings for me because of her, but it's kind of like a Hall of Fame football player's jersey - it gets retired because of its association with greatness.  I can't imagine our family EVER having another Nana!
 
You know, thinking about this is almost as hard as coming up with a trailname was.  The other day my co-worker's grandson called for her and thought I was her when I answered the phone - he apparently calls her "GranGran".  I was caught off-guard thinking "GranGran.  Hmm...how does that sound?"

I am glad to see them so enthused, but sometimes I wonder if I’ve created a monster!
