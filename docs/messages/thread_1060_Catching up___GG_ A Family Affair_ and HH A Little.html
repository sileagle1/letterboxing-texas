Catching up...GG, A Family Affair, and HH A Little Boys' Ball Cap

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-07-15 15:57:42 UTC-07:00
Okay, so by now y'all have read that GG showed up
yesterday at a box that TexasLuvBug had planted wayyyy
off the beaten path. But I didn't tell y'all how nice
that little trek was.

I had posted a few days ago about how much I liked the
way she had written the clues, and sure enough it was
a great journey to the box! There are parts of
Cherokee County (like many rural counties in Texas)
where folks still live a pretty simple life. The road
she leads the finder out to find A Family Affair box
is typical of that life. It was so easy to envision
what she described had happened at certain given spots
because little has changed in many, many years (not
saying that TLB is many, many years old!) We passed
several old country homesteads and at one house
everyone was gathered outside because it was cooler
outdoors than indoors. When we passed by the first
time, they were clearly just finished up dinner and
putting away the dishes. As the sun was setting on
our way back, the dominoes game was gearing up! The
countryside was deep thicket alternating with rolling
hills and farmland. Made me appreciate being a Texan.
No one lives like we do!

The box itself is in an old cemetery - and I'm with
Wanda, I love old cemeteries! We lingered there about
20 minutes, long enough to be checked out several
times by the nearby residents wondering what we were
doing in "their" cemetery.

Aside from GG's distinctive stamp, I found another HH
- A Little Boys Ball Cap. I'll be heading out to box
a little this weekend, so the little boy will have to
look for his cap in yet another place.

Lucy

P.S. No sign of any animals around THIS box of TLB's
- unless you count GG.
