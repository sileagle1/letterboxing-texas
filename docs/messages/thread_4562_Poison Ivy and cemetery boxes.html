From: Boots Tex (redbarn@eastex.net) | Date: 2010-06-09 16:24:49 UTC
On the one hand, manicured cemeteries have fewer places for boxes and they have caretakers. On the other hand, cemeteries that are a little more "natural" have many places to hide boxes, but they may develop weeds and, in particular, poison ivy. Maybe we could ask the caretaker at Holy Cross, if they have one, to remove the PI at the Davila grave. Of course, then he would probably find the box and, assuming it was trash, discard it. I hope you do go back to Oakwood Cemetery, it is definitely on the manicured side and you have to dodge the mowers. One of them was keeping an eye on me as I was trying to place one of the boxes but I think I convince him that I was studying tombstones. Definitely no PI in that place, though.

Boots Tex
