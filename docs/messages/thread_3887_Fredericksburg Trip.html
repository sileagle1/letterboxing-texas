Try to go to Johnson City at night to see the Christmas lights if
they are still up. The courthouse is awesome and the power company a
few blocks away has more lights in trees than I've ever seen. Also,
if you have time, Pedernales Falls SP is not far from there and is a
nice park with a few boxes.

That whole area is great, and if you follow the trail of letterboxes
you will experience most of it, including good wine. Have fun!

Re: [letterboxingtexas] Fredericksburg Trip

From: Cancan (cancan1959@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-12-29 23:41:12 UTC-06:00

Thank you for all the nice comments regarding Cock's Crow, this was my first plant. And I love the cavorting SE picture! 

The Hill Country needs boxes...lots and lots and lots of boxes.  Luckenbach makes me very sad and definately needs a rebox! Unfortunately every box has been rather short lived in that town...and I think a new placement would require it to be complete out of muggle territory. Where that is, I do not know because the last several placements, I did consider out of the way.  Same goes for the Unbroken Promises location...I was greatly disappointed to have missed that one.

I would love to know if my Extreme Turkey Trot is doing well...and would jump on the opportunity to come out there and "help" you...if we didn't already have plans.

So you're interested in boxes to hunt...
Johnson City-Johnson Windmill, Cock's Crow
Luckenbach :(,
Stonewall-LBJ State Park My New Hat, The Road to the White House, Scooby Doo & TX Peach
Enchanted Rock-Extreme Turkey Trot and Turkey Peak
Pedernales Falls State Park-Ogre, Ogress, Pedernales Possum, Ziggy's Gifts, Little Miss Comfort, Little Armored One:

Fredericksburg-Brautigam Garten, Cross Mtn, and House Cat Blues
N. of Fredericksburg near Willow City Loop (great wildflowers in the right season)-Follow the Rainbow to Gold
S. of Fredericksburg-Morris Ranch and Yellow Roads of Texas
Old Tunnel Wildlife Mgmt Area (9 miles south of 290-SE of Fredericksburg) Going Batty at the Tunnel and my two Grapetown boxes on the way there - BTW one block east of the Old Tunnel is the fabulous Alamo Springs Cafe- this place is in the middle of nowhere on a dead end folks and has great burgers and fries and onion rings and the best homemade desserts, actually everything I've had here was good. Try the Chedder Jalepeo bun if you get a burger and don't be surprised when they keep erasing the desserts off the blackboard (so if you want one better tell them to save you one when you order your meal...even at lunch!).
