Well - the "REAL" Army-Man hike is the one just featured in Texas Parks and Wildlife Magazine.  Those two guys still E-mail me about it.  The writer was actually worried on the trail.  Has anyone seen the article yet?  The web site still has the feature article for July.  I have the magazine here at the house.  http://www.tpwmagazine.com/  
 
Anyway - I was under the impression you-all wanted to do the N. Franklin Mountain Hike.  http://www.localhikes.com/Hikes/N_Franklin_Mountain_2320.asp
 
There is a letterbox up top - not many have found it because it is a significant hike.  But there are several easier ones below.  There are currently 12 active letterboxes in the area, and a 13th could be reactivated - cattle on the mesa.  I am going to need to put some miles on the new Toyota motor, so maybe I will go move the one near Guadalupe Mountains too.  That is a 1.5 hour drive one-way, but the park is beautiful.
 
If you intend to search for Hueco Tanks #1 - be sure to get your park reservations in EARLY.  In the fall it is a rock-climbers paradise and people sleep outside the gate waiting to get in.  there is a limit to the number of visitors in that park.  Many of you remember the unsuccessful search for Hueco Tanks #2.  I feel certain we found the location, but NOT the letterbox.  My relationship with the rangers allowed for me to return Hueco Tanks #1 to active duty.  http://www.photoshow.com/watch/Hb7SI6DE
 
My bunk-house can only put up two people comfortably, just two beds in it.  We could squeeze in 4 people, but two would be on old army cots.  Well - what did you expect?  <grin>.
 
This would be a good year for it, James will be home and he is aching for some hikes where there isn't anything being shot at around him.  And I am always up for a hike too.
 
You know - it occurs to me that someone should describe to the list what this reference to "ARMY-MAN HIKE" is all about.  We are tossing this around and there are a lot of new folks thinking you-all are coming to my area for boot-camp or something.  Not that I mind.
 
Well - time to take River and Daisy out for a little outing and socialization training - to the market.
 
Later.
