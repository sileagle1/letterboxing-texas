From: sileagle1 (sileagle@windstream.net) | Date: 2012-01-23 14:15:09 UTC
I hate to get into this whole issue again but basically the official stance of the State Parks is that any cache or box left in a park should be approved and permitted. That said, most parks don't enforce the rule and don't have the manpower to comply. There are three ways you can go: permit your box, ignore the permit and hope for the best, or don't place in the parks. It appears the Renegades have chosen to place by being stealthy (and hopefully responsibly), but everyone has to make up their own minds how to proceed. As for Brazos Bend SP, I don't know of any box currently permitted.

Re: Park permitting

From: Boots Tex (redbarn@eastex.net) | Date: 2012-01-23 15:07:17 UTC
We have never placed a box in Brazos Bend State Park, but We've probably got more permitted boxes in the state parks than anyone, and I spent a lot of time trying to get the state to recognize letterboxing and make the process of permitting easy for us, without success. My conclusion is that, as Silver Eagle says, parks don't care and thus don't enforce the rules (we are lumpend in with geocaching rules). In the end, I was told (indirectly, because they couldn't tell me officially) that letterboxing was not a problem that warrented their attention or resources. The only reason they sort of "embrace" geocaching is that since they couldn't beat them, they decided to join them in order to better enforce the rules. Since we have never been a problem, they ignore us. That isn't to say that, if a box is discovered accidentally, or turned in to them by a park vistor, they would not remove it or notify the placer, but they don't actively seek out boxes. So, like Barefoot Lucy says, if we practice good placement practices and stay away from sensitive areas, we should be okay. Asking for a permit just brings us to the attention of the local park management. Anyway, I no longer permit boxes in state parks. You should do what you think is right.

Boots Tex
