Has anyone noticed that more and more parks are turning into frisbee
golf courses? I had to retire my TSS: Armadillo box because the park
was turned into a frolf course. I found the box completely exposed

written some very nasty things in the logbook. Just one more
obstacle.

From: vkorenek (vkorenek@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-11-21 21:05:47 UTC
Sorry to hear about your box. I've played disc golf for over 2 years
now, and all I've ever met are super laid back disc golfers. Like
letterboxing, there is no governing body, but there are rules and
etiquette. People trashing your box casts a bad light on an otherwise
fun game. It is true that people may go to great lengths to retrieve
a disc, especially if it's a really good one, or one of their
favorites...and I'm speaking from experience :-) However, destroying
property and the environment just isn't cool.

Oh, I have nothing against disc golfers. I tried my hand at it back in Austin and I stank. I've had hikers, bikers, and paintballers stumble on my boxes. Usually they'll just sign in with something like, "Way cool box, dude! Wish I had a stamp!" This episode was an exception, I'm sure. But with the sport becoming more and more popular, I'm going to have to rethink some of my future hiding places. I love Z Boaz park, but two of the great hiding spots I've found there are in line with the course. I'll be steering clear of it now.

I guess I've just had a run of REALLY bad letterboxing luck. Maybe I need to rub GG's head.
