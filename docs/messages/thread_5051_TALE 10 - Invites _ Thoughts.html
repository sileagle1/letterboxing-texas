From: babybearlb2002 (babybearlb2002@yahoo.com) | Date: 2012-07-24 16:37:52 UTC
Looking back over the TALE events, emails, posts, etc, I've seen lots of old names that we have lost over the years. Would be great if we can get any (or all) of these early letterboxers to attend next year's event, and also new letterboxers. I am going to try to send emails to some of the early boxers that had posted to talk list (hope their email is the same) and see if they will come at least for the main Saturday event. Also, any time I do get an email from a new boxer I tell them about the Texas talk list and TALE, and suggest everyone do the same. I will also do some posting to National Talk list to try to pull in some out of state folks (Silver Eagle and Barefoot Lucy - any help y'all can give on that since y'all are more involved with these folks would be appreciated).

As for regional events, I think these do help, at least they did in the early years. These usually work best as just a day event, with a theme. Dewberry did a great job in Houston with this type of events. Could see events in Dallas, Austin, Houston, and San Antonio, but not sure who will want to handle these, and would ask that if someone does they do not schedule for Feb or March (due to TALE).

Also - If anyone has suggestions for a place to meet and eat on Friday before Saturday TALE, let me know. Ideally need to be close to Buescher (Maybe Smithville). Once I get handle on some options, will try to get up there and talk to place ahead of time to clear the way for what is coming!!! Will also get Silver Eagle to add sign up list to AQ for the Friday dinner so can get head count.

From: Boots Tex (redbarn@eastex.net) | Date: 2012-07-24 18:12:37 UTC
One thought occurred to me as I was reading this. It seems to me that, at every event, only about half the people who sign up actually show up. I know it's easy to sign up to "reserve a spot" sort of, but if you've never hosted one, you don't realize how much work, preparation and planning goes into an event, and a lot of that is based on the number of folks that are signed up. If you sign up, with every intention of attending, then your plans change and you see that you won't be able to make it, please take your name off the list so that it reflects a more realistic number of attendees. This is a good rule for all events, not just the bigger ones. Just a thought.

Boots Tex

From: Carol McNally (carolmcnally@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2012-07-24 22:18:49 UTC-05:00
Quintana is a great park..probably not close to Galveston...but still a great park..especially if there is a drought..because if there is no drought, the mosquitoes will pick you up carry you away suck your blood and leave you for dead amongst the cocka-burs in grass outside your tent...but, other than that,we LOVE Quintana...LOL..and there are a few boxes on the island, some at the bird sanctuary which I don't even have the guts to go in because i can see the mosquitoes swarming at the gates.  we did go there one year while there was a drought..it was beautiful.  The waster was blue, we found a puffer fish, gave to the park rangers to live in their aquarium (he later died), found a rattle snake outside the bathroom (which dh picked up and relocated in a safer area) watched movies that were shown by park rangers, had popcorn and Koolaid while watching the movie...tons of fun..The kids still talk about that trip.  Also went about 3-4 weeks ago..before all our July rains...and it was awful..LOL  the beach was fine but oh my!  the mosquitoes!  They would come up from the floorboards while you were in the bathroom!  I had more skeeter bites on my rear end than I could have ever imagined!  Came home scratching things that aren't polite to scratch in public!  Couldn't believe there were people tent camping in the mosquito fields!  They must have bathed in deet!  OK...sorry, don't want to trash Quintana..because we truly love that park and bryan beach..but please only go there when they are under severe drought conditions.
Carol McNally
