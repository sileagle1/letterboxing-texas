brand lock-n-lock boxes. In looking for something else, I finally

market) for $.99 each. I didn't buy them all up, instead leaving some
for others.

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-09-30 19:11:02 UTC-07:00
They are a type of container that some folks are
trying out right now. There will be periodic debates
on most of the lists about what is the best type of
container, best type of logbook, best type of ink,
best type of carving material. I almost always learn
something from the discussions.

Lock-n-Lock are the container of the current
discussions. Someone on Newboxers (I think)
discovered them and asked if anyone had used them.
They seal and also have a hinged lock on all four
sides, so the theory is they double seal.

Logbooks/contains/stamp pads/markers/rubber are just a
part of the never-ending quest to be a better boxer.
You might find the perfect container (or whatever),
but you never really stop looking just in case there
is one more perfect than the one you already have!
And if you don't ever find a more perfect container,
you find yourself trying to find a more perfect size
of the one you use. I guess we are just never
completely content!

In fact, there is a list just for letterboxing
products: groups.yahoo.com/groups/boxheads

I thought it was kind of a lame idea for a list when
it first began, but I joined it anyway because I join
most letterboxing lists to see what they have going
on. Turns out, that's a pretty cool list!

Lucy
