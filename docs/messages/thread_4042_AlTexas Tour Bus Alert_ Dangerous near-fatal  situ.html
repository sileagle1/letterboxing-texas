This morning, PuddleSplasher and I (Viewfinder) were driving along Shoreline Drive in Flowe r Mound, heading for the Northshore Trail at Lake Grapevine in the Twin Coves Park area (Mapquest 2000 Shoreline Drive, Flower Mound, to see how to get there from wherever you may be).  Suddenly a very big bus came flying past us on that narrow lane, passengers hanging out of the windows and off the roof of the bus,  with all kinds of loud raucous noise and dust and smoke flying behind them.  They continued just ahead of us to a dead end, where we skidded to a stop in front of two very old fake deer, but the bus didn’t stop at the rail!  It continued on down the little trail, heading right toward the lake!  Fearing that the bus was completely out of control, brakes gone, and all those passengers were going to wind up IN the lake, we gave chase on foot.  After a few yards, we passed a trail intersection, but the bus didn’t turn – just kept heading straight toward the water!  We started counting at that intersection as we huffed and puffed along after the bus, and about 50 steps on, the bus suddenly veered off the path to the left toward a small cluster of cedar trees, with a small oak right in front of the center cedar tree.  Amazingly, that bus veered around the little oak and came to rest just beneath the center cedar, with small rocks, bark and leaves flying up and then settling back down to cover it.  Seeing that the noisy passeng ers all appeared to be OK (probably because they are all packed in there so tightly and, we suspect, some of them might possibly be engaging in unprescribed medications as well), PuddleSplasher and I quickly turned around and headed back to the car to make our escape, glad to be free of that out-of-control group – but not before PuddleSplasher managed to slip a little Bonus Box under the bus to reward not only the bus finder, but also an other  finders who get there a little too late and miss the bus.  (Please do NOT take the Bonus Box if you are the finder of the bus; it will be posted as a permanent letterbox by PuddleSplasher). 
Warning:  Use caution in approaching this site we think some of those passengers are armed and dangerous, not to mention the heavy purple haze of sweet-smelling smoke mingled with the pungent scent of a bear and far too many passengers above the bus’ capacity all crammed together for far too long without showers!  -- Best wishes, Viewfinder
