How a hawk started letterboxing

From: Hawk (omkarmashanti@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-06-24 09:33:37 UTC-07:00
OK, since everyone seems to be telling stories about
how they started letterboxing, I figured I would add
my story to the list.

I first heard about letterboxing from Krohm, who was
not boxing at the time, but thought it sounded
interesting. I thought the concept sounded fun, and I
was going on a trip to Massachusetts, so I found some
clues for boxes in the Plymouth area, where I would be
spending a couple of days in July of 2004.

Plymouth proved to be historical, beautiful, full of
genealogy information (which I was seeking), and
cursed with rain the entire time I was there.
Fortunately, the two brave souls that were with me
(believe it or not, it was Hudson and Mysterious
Stranger!) had a pretty good sense of humor and
adventure, and they went hunting for boxes with
me...in the rain. The first box was missing, so the
three of us were quite discouraged. The second box
required a long walk (in the rain). Mysterious
Stranger was carrying packages, so stopped in a shop
to stay dry. Hudson and I pushed on, and finally found
our first box! It was Grist Mill, and it was such a
thrill to find that box that I've been letterboxing
ever since. We also found Town Brook while in
Plymouth.

When I returned to Houston, I started searching for
boxes like a maniac (don't laugh, y'all have done it,
too!). My third box was Park Angel (the original, by
Baby Bear), then Zube Zephyre (Silver Eagle), Lady
Justice (Baby Bear), and Farmer's Lair (Baby Bear). I
finally found boxes by Mother of Four, Fluffy Fury,
and Tall Texan. I decided that I would add my own
boxes to the hunt, and planted Candlelight Park (still
alive, but temporarily pulled because the tree it was
hidden in is scheduled to be cut down), The Bell of
Montrose (missing X2), Little Brown Bird (missing),
and Impossible Triangle (missing).

As of now, I have found over 200 boxes and have placed
nearly 75 (in four states). I am planning to replace
some of the missing boxes before planting more new
ones. I have met some wonderful people through
letterboxing, and am happy that Krohm, Hudson, and
Mysterious Stranger are willing to join in the fun
whenever they can.

Though I have been on a bit of a break from
letterboxing (not by choice!), I plan to carve more
stamps and then return to leaving my usual plethora of
"hawk droppings" for folks to find as soon as I can
get a break!

From: Curt Jagger (cjagger405@earthlink.net) | Date: 2005-06-24 13:24:20 UTC-04:00
My first LB was in Newtown, CT, at the Sugar Maple Demonstration Area of the Paugausett Forest. I found it on July 4th, 2004, so my first anniversary of boxing is coming up. Since I will be leaving CT for TX on July 3rd, I thought it woud be appropriate to leave a HH behind in that box before I go - especially since it was also my sister's (2txchic, Viewfinder) first letterbox (October, 2004). So as tribute, last night, very, very late, after spending thirteen hours cleaning out the house we are moving out of, I decided to carve an appropriate HH stamp. After I finished and was quite pleased with the result, I did a test stamp and realized I had made the same mistake Baby Bear had mentioned in his "story" yesterday, I forgot to reverse the letters! At that point, all I could do was laugh and put it away until this weekend when I'm a bit more rested and can do it right!
I love reading everyone's stories - keep 'em coming.
Puddle Splasher
Maribeth
