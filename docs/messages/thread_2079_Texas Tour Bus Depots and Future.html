Texas Tour Bus Depots and Future

From: TeamKing (teamkingus@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-08-15 21:01:23 UTC-05:00
Fellow Texans -

The Texas Department of Transportation recently sent me a warning
letter about passenger capacity regarding the Texas Tour Bus. Seems
we may be at the maximum safety level of riders allowed on our little
independent bus line.

So, what to do? Do we send the passengers back home? Do we need to
put them on other buses. . .going to places out of state? Something
else?

The suggestion has come to our attention to create 'depots' for the
passengers. Here's how it works. . .a few interested parties will
create a box in their areas - Houston, DFW, Austin, East Texas, etc.
- each of these boxes should be named appropriately - the creators
decision. Then, the Tour Bus will need to do a round trip through
these depots and drop off passengers. By the time it reaches the last
depot, the bus should be empty. It will then continue on its journey
picking up new passengers. Note: the new passengers do not have to be
Texans - we may be pushing our luck coming up with interesting
people! - but they do need to be people. No stamps of other items or
places please. The bus will continue its journey around the state
picking up new passengers and giving everyone something new to find.

Meanwhile, the depots will become attractions in themselves. Holding
a handful of permanent passengers ready for any boxer to find -
especially new people.

So, if you are all game with this plan, is there anyone interested in
creating a depot for their area of the state?


Thank you for your time.
We're constantly striving to keep you out there boxing.


TeamKing
Operations Director
Texas Tour Bus

Re: [letterboxingtexas] Texas Tour Bus Depots and Future

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-08-15 21:19:53 UTC-05:00

I've got a qualified interest in setting up a depot, but I do have some concerns brought up by the local Economic Development Board - they are, after all, very concerned with keeping up the neighborhood and welcoming new business to town:

1) Are there any special licensing concerns to be considered in setting up such a depot?  i.e. Will we next see that the Department of Health will become curious by the sudden gathering of individuals who will be living in one place? 

2) And what about Zoning laws?  Should we be be looking toward areas that are commercially zoned, residentially zoned, industrial zoned?

3) Will we be doing background checks on the station managers, to ensure the safety and wellbeing of the deboarded passengers?  No one who has a history of rubber abuse or abandonment?

4) What about insurance and liability?  After all, we know this group has become rather rowdy and station managers may be hesitant to take them on if they are going to be liable for property damage.

******************************

Seriously, I think the depot idea is a great one!  I think it would work to put one in each region (as defined by Silver Eagle, if you ask me my opinion), but I do think it would be a good idea to have you prescreen depot locations so they are strategically placed.

I also like the idea of sending out a bus to inflict on the rest of the country - maybe a smaller version, calling it something like Texas Tour Bus Gone Wild.  It'd be sort of cool too to require anyone else who finds it to add a passenger from their state.  But I guess that's another version of the game.

Thanks TeamKing for asking our opinions on it, but I lean toward doing what your preference is - it IS, after all, your brainchild.  I would like to take a moment to thank you again for creating it and turning it loose.  It has been great fun to follow and has added yet another unique aspect to Texas letterboxing and will continue to keep us pre-occupied in whatever form it takes, I'm sure!
