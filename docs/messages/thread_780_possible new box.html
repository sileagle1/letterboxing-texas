We lost a beloved hamster (I know, it isn't the same as a cat...but the kids
loved him) while my husband was gone last year...we made a little grave
outside in the garden for him, and then had a celebration of our good
memories of him talking about him over chocolate cake (soothes the souls of
children!) And I told the kids that it was a really sad thing that had
happened, and that if they felt like crying they should. A couple weeks
time and a new hamster fixed 'em all right up. Hang in there. Kids are
tough. -Mother of Five

From: Carol Lemons (lemonscl@houston.rr.com) | Date: 2005-04-06 19:25:52 UTC-05:00
Ohhhhhhhh, I am so sorry! How awful for both of you! I wish I had some comforting words of wisdom for you, but the truth is you just have to get through the pain, and it will get better each day.
 
My son did see our next door neighbor's kitten get run over. I think he was about 5 at the time. There was nothing to be done for the kitten, and I didn't want to move her, as that might have caused her more pain. It was raining, and I let him hold an umbrella over her. We talked about it after, and he felt sad about the kitten, but proud that he had been able to help her.
 
 Most kids are very resilient, but I think it's very important to talk about things. Your son might like to make some kind of memory book, with photos of him with his cat, and he could also draw pictures illustrating his memories of her....... The Lb is also a very nice idea, especially if he can help plan the stamp, where to plant the box, etc., so that he can let everyone know what a wonderful cat Fluffy was.
 
JOY
