Out of the way boxes

From: the carrascos (a.carrasco@earthlink.net) | Date: 2007-09-28 11:14:06 UTC-05:00
I'm with the other ladies on this one.  There is nothing like finding that cool spot in the middle of no where because of a box.    One of my favorite boxing trips of all time was way back when I was Mother of Four, and the only boxes in the whole state were placed by SE and BB.  The kids and I took a trip up through Waco to Dinosaur Valley, stopped at the Confederate Battlegrounds on the way back, and spent an entire weekend having a blast and finding **gasp**4 boxes.  But we saw that great suspension bridge in Waco, a bunch of baby javelinas, dinosaur tracks, and each and every one of us slipped and fell in the river.  Keep planting where you want to plant, and the people that do find your boxes will be awfully glad you did.  By the way, I read your clues to "The Home Run That Never Was" yesterday, and I think that is the best baseball story I have ever heard.  If I can ever get myself out to Midland, I know which box will be the first I find!  MO5

Re: Out of the way boxes

From: Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter (janet@redbarnretreat.com) | Date: 2007-09-28 16:50:03 UTC
Thanks, MO5, that's probably the favorite of all my boxes. We lived
in Midland in 1985 when they had their centennial celebration and
one of the local radio stations ran a contest seeking stories
about "life in Midland". I wrote this true story about an incident
that I witnessed when I was 13 years old (a long time ago!). It was
when the Babe was still the home run king. I won the contest and
the prize was a pair of custom-made ostrich boots (worth $1,000 at
the time) from M. L. Leddy (a well-known bootmaker in San Angelo).
I still have them and wear them on special occasions. Maybe that's
how I got the name Boots!

Boots Tex
