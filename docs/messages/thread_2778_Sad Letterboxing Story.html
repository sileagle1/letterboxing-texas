Sad Letterboxing Story

From: hookem77532 (hookem77532@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-05-19 19:03:20 UTC
Well, It seems I'm not having very much lucky planting. I planted 1
and it was stolen 3 days later so chose not to replace it since maybe
it was not a good place. May try again later at a different location.
Then I did a bonus box that decided to play with a local lawn-mower and
lost. Thank fully the box was found by a letterboxer and let me know
he (the stamp/box) was a total loss. My husband the comic he is said
it was just another casualty lost to letterboxing.
I am placing that one back ASAP since it goes with one that thank
goodness is hiding in a bush away from sharp objects.
Thought you guys would like a little laugh.
Happy Hunting!
Pastry Princess

From: Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter (janet@redbarnretreat.com) | Date: 2007-05-19 21:04:20 UTC
At least you know what happened to that one, although it's never any
fun to lose a letterbox, especially one in which you have a lot of
time or travel invested. Most of the boxes that go missing are
never heard from again. One of Lone Star Quilter's Quilt Block
boxes in Austin, Jacob's Ladder, has been reported missing, and her
tribute to her Mom in Midland, Guardian of the Record is gone, too.
She's a little discouraged about the rash of missing boxes.

Boots Tex

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-05-19 16:25:26 UTC-05:00
LOL...Hang in there!!!
 
Most boxers have a few flubs in their first few plants.  My first two?  I planted one in a very well manicured zoo.  Now think about it - zoos take really, REALLY good care of their facilities, or the good ones do!  And this was a good zoo... it was probably gone before I had even seen the monkeys...
 
My second one I planted in a historic area and thought that planting between an old stone cornerpost and the hurricane fence, amongst an accumulation of leaves, would be a great place.  And it was, until they had a cleanup day and the first thing they did was clean the fenceline.
 
Perhaps, as a talisman against lawnmowers, you should carve and plant a lawnmower and dare any more lawnmowers to destroy your boxes...
