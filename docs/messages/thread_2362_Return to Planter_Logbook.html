Return to Planter:Logbook

From: (kacmbc@aol.com) | Date: 2006-12-05 21:14:03 UTC-05:00
This past April, M-Dot and I went to Tempe, Arizona where he competed in Ironman, AZ. While there, I planted my first out of state letterbox, Ironman Arizona, along the marathon course.  Since then, I have enjoyed the e-mail updates on how my little box is doing.   A couple of weeks ago,  I was contacted by a letterboxer who lives near the area where my box is located, telling me that the logbook was full, and they offered to mail it to me.  We exchanged information and this evening, it arrived in the mailbox!  With a mug of hot chocolate, I sat down and carefully read the entries.  I have had so much fun looking at all of the stamps from letterboxers who stamped in!  Many left little notes about their journey, nice trails, they liked the stamp, tough to find with the construction etc...but the one thing that seemed consistent was that for the most part, they wrote how hot it was on the day the found the box!  One entry stated that it was 112 degrees! Wow! 
 
It really means a lot to me that other boxers took the time to write little notes about their adventure finding the box.  The first finder wrote that it was their first ever letterbox to find- what a bonus!  In the future, I am going to try to be more diligent in my logbook entries in the hopes that others enjoy reading through their logbooks as much as I did!
 
Runs for Chocolate  :)
