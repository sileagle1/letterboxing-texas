In honor of actually finding GG's stamp in one of my boxes today,
I've written a song for him. I hope you all will enjoy it and none
are offended by it.

OH, GRIZZLED ONE (To be sung to the tune of Oh, Christmas Tree)

Oh, Grizzled One,
Oh, Grizzled One,
You've been a great big mystery.

Oh, Grizzled One,
Oh, Grizzled One,
You've made terrific history.

I'm glad you stamped-into my box!
I hope you did-not get wet socks!

Oh Grizzled One,
I'm glad that "bus" did not
Cause you any injury!


Thanks,
Lucy Locket

Re: A Little Song

From: ThimbleCat (inklaura@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2005-12-31 16:07:12 UTC
How funny that a discussion (and lovely song) about the mysterious GG
would come up again in the group right now.
I took my sister-in-law and nieces along with us letterboxing on
Thursday. We found the Salado College minibox and the kids and I were
busy stamping in to our books while my SIL sat and eagerly looked
through the logbook. She suddenly made an excited noise and said "I
HAVE to find and meet the Grizzled Gryphon". I started laughing and
she explained that he must be a fellow (Grateful)Dead-head and she
felt a connection with him. It was quite hysterical, especially after
I explained all the mystery surrounding him. So even non-boxers find
him intriguing.

Also -- new boxers and a new box.
I took some of the extended family with us boxing Thursday. They had
so much fun designing and carving their own personal stamps, but
weren't sure what the big to-do was about finding the boxes. Once we
found one though, they caught the boxing fever and I heard "aren't
there any more that we can find today?"
They helped us plant a new box at our favorite park in Temple (Whistle
Stop Letterbox) so they are hooked for sure now and anxious for their
next trip to Grandma's house to see if anyone has found their box.

ThimbleCat of The Polka Dots
