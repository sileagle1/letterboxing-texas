Re: Life Insurance for Letterboxes

From: Silver Eagle (sileagle@alltel.net) | Date: 2007-04-09 17:17:17 UTC
Thanks, BL, for bringing this up here.

The problem as I see it is how do you ensure your boxes remain
findable after you are gone. This means finding a way to make sure
your clues remain accessible so the boxes don't become lost relics,
which would be nothing more than litter. Ideally, you would also
have someone adopt your boxes so they could update the status and
perform maintenance if possible.

In my case, my clues are on my own website which LBNA links to. This
means I would have to make sure someone kept my website alive or the
clues would have to be transferred to LBNA. However, just because
they are on LBNA does not mean they will last forever. What if the
administrators decide to delete all clues from letterboxers that have
not logged on in a set period of time? Also, the clues would not be
updated so missing boxes could only be surmised by "attempts" in the
status field.

One solution is to make the adoption process easier, but this would
put a huge burden on anyone adopting all my boxes. And what happens
when they leave the game?

The only solution I have come up with is to get my son to agree to
take over my boxes when I'm gone, but that is no guarantee by any
means.

As one boxer on the Big List stated, this problem should not be a
high priority for things to worry about after you are gone, but I
thought it deserved some discussion.

Silver Eagle
