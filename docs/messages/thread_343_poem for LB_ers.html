poem for LB'ers

From: samuraistampers (samuraistampers@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-09-22 03:20:43 UTC
I traveled thro' the woods
Thro' thorns of clawed beasts
I knew just up ahead
Would be an inken feast
The poisoned ivy of yore
Caressed my weary limbs
As bystanders of ticks
Clung to me as evil hymns
I knew it must be just up ahead
What? No, I'm just hiking
I grab my book, my box, my stamp,
And act like I'm just wiping
The burrs of various plants
When really what I've found
Is a Wonderland Around
The World of Letterboxing


To all who seek, ponder, endure, enjoy, and just pause to hear the
chickadee's love call-stamp your book and revel in this glorious
great American happening.
