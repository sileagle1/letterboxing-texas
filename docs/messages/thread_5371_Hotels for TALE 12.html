From: Bonnie Lind (bonnielind1@hotmail.com) | Date: 2014-05-12 18:50:43 UTC-05:00
If you are of a mind to stay in a hotel for TALE 12 in March 2015, reserve your room pronto!  There must be something big happening in Austin that weekend because most of hotels near the airport which is near Mckinney Falls State Park, are already full or outrageously expensive.  I was able to make my reservations at the La Quinta Inn & Suites, Austin Airport, 7625 East Ben White Blvd, but it wasn’t my first choice.  Keep in mind that you can cancel if you must.
 
It looks like there are plenty of campsites available, but it is probably a good idea to go ahead and reserve now, too.

From: Maribeth (cjagger405@earthlink.net) | Date: 2014-05-13 16:58:41 UTC-05:00

I leave next week. I hiked this morning with my loaded backpack for a few miles. Very manageable, in fact, after a mile or so, I actually forgot I was wearing it. I'm sure after the first three days (constantly climbing uphill) I'll be very aware of every ounce on my back. It's not bad though - I've got it down to 23.2 lbs. (27.4 fully loaded with water). After the third day, I'll be over Virginia's highest mountains, and mostly be hiking on a ridge (and will have eaten half my food), so it should be easier then. I'll be stopping in Troutdale, VA on day 5 to pick up my general delivery mail (resupply of food and some fresh clothes). In preparation for my hike, I've been hiking about 6 miles a day, but I have to average 9 miles a day on the trail to get to my destination. I'll try to check in a couple of times from the trail. I've got a very small letterbox which I'll be hiding somewhere along the way too.
