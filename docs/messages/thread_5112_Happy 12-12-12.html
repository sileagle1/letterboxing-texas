Happy 12-12-12

From: Bloomin Gramma Jo (mjrekedal@gmail.com) | Date: 2012-12-13 03:16:36 UTC
So how many got out and boxed today just so you could log that cool date in your logbook?
I planted one, and then hiked on up the cliff overlooking the 360 Bridge and got screaming meemie's beautiful stamp, as well as a great view of Lake Austin. Then decided to look for one more box fairly close and on my way home. This box has been on my "for later" list as it's on an out-of-the way mini trail. So found the trail, and made a great discovery: a good letterboxer not only has a pair of garden gloves in his/her pack, but must wear them to avoid being rewarded with a handful of cactus spines when bending over to find a box under a rock pile!
So that brought my boxing day to an end. Got home and tried pulling those mini spines out with a tweezer. Nope. So googled for a solution. and second great discovery of the day: best way to get small spines out is with Elmer's glue! Pour it on and let it dry. Then pull off, and voila, out come those pesky little spines! It actually worked!
So now I'll pack a bottle of glue along with my garden gloves!
What else do I need to know for safety on the trail?
Happy 12-12-12!
Bloomin Gramma Jo

I couldn't resist placing one today called End Of The World and if you believe the Mayan prediction you better get it soon.

Instead of finding boxes I decided to check on mine at Memorial Park and Houston Arboretum. Boy was it depressing seeing all the cleared areas and out of 6 boxes I only found 2: Kitirik and Risk Game-Horseman. The Horseman box was laying in a pile of debris and was dented on one side, but still waterproof with contents intact so I moved it close to where it used to be and will change the clues. Neither box had been visited since early this year but they are available and the trails are open now. The boxes I couldn't find were: Risk Game-Cannon, Risk Game-Soldier, Square Root Day, and LB Capital. Not sure if I will replace them or just archive.

On a happier note I did have an auspicious encounter with 2 Houston letterboxers I have not seen in a while: Dewberry and Mother Of Five. They were looking for my Bananagrams box and I have not heard if they found it or not. It was nice to see them again and made the day even more special.

Silver Eagle

From: The Carrascos (a.carrasco@earthlink.net) | Date: 2012-12-13 13:16:18 UTC-06:00
It was a very happy 12-12-12!
 
Dewberry and I decided to celebrate the day by looking for the bananagrams box.  Unfortunately, we couldn’t find it.  The are where it was hidden is now just a jumble of cut down trees.  But we did find Silver Eagle on the trail, which was even more fun than finding a box!
 
Dewberry and I have been seriously limited on the time we have to letterbox, although we do still see each other all the time.  Last February my 10 year old daughter (#4) was diagnosed with type 1 diabetes.  It was a very scary time, and dewberry helped us through by visiting in the hospital, bringing books and chapstick, and generally helping me see that this was not the end of the world, and that I could take care of this little girl.  She is doing very well.  The problem is that she needs insulin 4 times a day, and since the nurse is not always at her school, I must be there every day at 12:30 to give her the shot.  This seriously limits the distance we can travel to look for boxes during the school week!  (So all you Houston boxers take pity on us and plant some around town!!)
 
We both miss you all very much, and really miss being able to take off and spend the day in far off cemeteries eating picnic lunches and solving clues!  It won’t be long until #4 will be able to give herself the shots...and we will be back on the trails arguing if the pine is 15 or 20 steps off the trail.  Until then just know we are still round...slowed down but not stopped! Mother of 5
