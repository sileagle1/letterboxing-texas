From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-03-06 22:24:23 UTC-06:00
Hi Blue Butterfly,
 
When I traveled to Austin several times a month, I stayed all over town and finally settled on the Habitat Suites to be my home away from home.  It's an eco-conscious hotel, beautiful grounds, staff is very friendly (of course it helped that I stayed with them about 30 times a year, so they recognized me, but they were as welcoming the first time I arrived as they were the last), they have a full HOT breakfast buffet in the mornings that caters to a variety of dietary needs, a happy hour in the evenings that features wine and beer but also nice snacks (after a day of meeting food, this was often my supper).  The rooms are suites with a full kitchen, comfortable beds and some even have a fireplace.  Computer access, all the amenities. 
 
I liked it most especially because of the relaxed environment - after a long day of lobbying and negotiating and jockeying, I just wanted to relax and it was a lot easier at this place than in the big name hotels.
 
I have no idea how their rates are because I was staying under state contract rates, which were the same amount no matter where I stayed - be it the La Quinta or the Driskill.  However, because I was able to cook in my room and had a breakfast buffet, I was able to eat more nutritiously on the road and save money on eating out.  I won't ever stay anywhere else when I go to Austin, unless it's a tent.
 
Here's their website:  http://www.habitatsuites.com
