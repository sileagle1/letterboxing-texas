From: Boots Tex (redbarn@eastex.net) | Date: 2011-09-14 14:13:20 UTC
Fires are a natural part of nature in forests. The fires don't usually harm the trees, but clear out the underbrush and invasive trees. As long as there are no homes in the way, fire can actually be good for the forests. Unfortunately, home building in forests has become widespread, and that's the biggest concern. We live in the middle of the Sam Houston National Forest, which borders our property. So far, we have been very fortunate, but it only takes one idiot throwing a cigarette out of a car or leaving a campfire burning to cause a major fire. I think the trees will survive and may be better for the fire, but the proximity of homes can result in disaster for the people involved. Keep safe.

Boots Tex

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2011-09-14 09:38:37 UTC-05:00

I think that's the thing with the burns we are seeing right now - there is a large human element involved, so that where a fire would normally be healthy for a forest, as you said, is going far beyond that.

Several years ago, Fearless Fox, Dvir, and hiking friends of theirs were overnight hiking in Davy Crockett National Forest. They had a small camp fire in the morning, doused it and hiked on. As they were hiking, one of them happened to glance over his shoulder and noticed a whisp of smoke. They stopped and conferred about what they had done to extinguish the fire, and decided "it can't be". They hiked on and couldn't help glancing back periodically and noticing that the whisp of smoke was turning into billows and spreading across more sky area. They were really antsy by that time, but they had hiked far enough that hiking back wasn't going to do any good. Through this, they had tried their cell phones to call someone, but could not ever get a signal. They finally finished their hike a few hours later, and finally were much relieved to see that the fire seemed to be diminishing. They had left a vehicle at the end of their hike and so got in it to go back and pick up the vehicle they had started out at and they happened to drive close enough to the place they had camped to have an opportunity to see forest personnel finishing up a controlled burn. They were much relieved! You can't hike with any of those guys today except that they make a wide fire circle, circled with rocks, and when they put it out, they smother it, drown it and bury it.

Fires are a natural part of nature in forests. The fires don't usually harm the trees, but clear out the underbrush and invasive trees. As long as there are no homes in the way, fire can actually be good for the forests. Unfortunately, home building in forests has become widespread, and that's the biggest concern. We live in the middle of the Sam Houston National Forest, which borders our property. So far, we have been very fortunate, but it only takes one idiot throwing a cigarette out of a car or leaving a campfire burning to cause a major fire. I think the trees will survive and may be better for the fire, but the proximity of homes can result in disaster for the people involved. Keep safe.

Boots Tex
