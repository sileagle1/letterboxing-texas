all 300+ parks.

of placing boxes.

Is she in Texas? If so, Jeff of Birds of a Feather
has a great map-based interactive database for Texas
boxes. The website is:

http://birdsofafeather.netfirms.com/cgi-bin/lbatlas.cgi?

It looks like it is going to wrap maybe, so you may
have to copy and paste.

If she isn't in Texas, the best way I've found to find
boxes in a specific area is to use the "letterbox
search" function. I'm sure there are several ways to
get to it, but I always click on the big book, then on
my state, then at the top of the page I click on
"Letterbox search" and then select my criteria -
usually Texas, and a specific county or town.

From: Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter (janet@redbarnretreat.com) | Date: 2008-12-10 03:40:12 UTC
My experience is that most state park employees don't know anything
about letterboxing. Some do and don't approve and will tell you that
you can't hide the box in the park without a permit. They will go to
the extreme of confiscating boxes placed in their park. In that case,
you shouldn't place a box there. Others will ask you to explain, think
it's a neat activity that will lure people to the park and will say to
go ahead and plant away. In my opinion, it's okay to ask, but you
should never tell a park employee that a box exists in the park and
that you are there to find it. This would, in some cases, put the box
in jeopardy of being removed and you should never intentionally put
another person's box in danger. Like I said, that's my opinion, but, I
think, one shared by most letterboxers. If you have a favorite park and
you develop a relationship with the Rangers, like Mike has, that's the
way to go, but most of us don't have the ability to do that. When you
do place a box in a state park without permission, you should be
prepared to lose the box at some point, but there are many letterboxes
in state parks that have been there for several years with no problem.
In the end, you should use your own discretion where your boxes are
concerned, and let others do the same. Again, my opinion.

Boots Tex
