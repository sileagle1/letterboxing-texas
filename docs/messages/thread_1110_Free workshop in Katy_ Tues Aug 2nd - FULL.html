I've just returned from an extended (and hot) research trip to NOLA
for my upcoming book (Travelers' Guide to Haunted New Orleans) and

Anyway... The free stamp carving/logbook binding class is now full.

From: Eibhlin/Aisling (eibhlinm@gmail.com) | Date: 2005-08-02 09:14:36 UTC-05:00
Hi,

Sorry that you won't be able to join us, but in a few days I'll have
class notes and handouts online. It's not the same as being in a
class, but it should help anyway.

Meanwhile, I'm getting ready for tonight's class. We'll start promptly
at 7 p.m. at the Maud Marks branch library on Westgreen near Highland
Knolls. The meeting rooms are on your right as you enter the
building, but if you're not sure, ask at the library's front desk.

Remember to bring something to carve (eraser or pink stuff from
Speedball, or anything that can be cut into a rubber stamp) and
something to carve with (small-ish Xacto blade or block cutting tools
are ideal), and some ideas for stamps you'd like to create. Some
sheets of paper and a pencil with a soft lead (4B is ideal) will help,
for transfering your design to the rubber.

If you don't have a design idea yet, don't worry... the library has
lots of books with art in them that we can photocopy on their machine,
and then use in class.

Also be sure to bring your letterboxing kit so that we can exchange stamps.

If you're planning to work on a handmade logbook, the basic supplies
include some sheets of paper, scissors, embroidery floss or any heavy
thread or yarn, plus a needle to sew it with.

BUT... remember that you can show up with a smile and that's all, and
you'll still be able to learn things in this class. And I always
bring extra supplies if you forget something.

See you tonight!

Cheerfully,
Aisling (pronounced ASH-ling, the Irish way)
http://www.aisling.net/ - my photo is there. The picture is two years
old, but you'll probably recognize me anyway.
