JOTA (was Re: [letterboxingtexas] Latest.)

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-10-04 10:26:00 UTC-07:00
You should contact your District Executive or Council
office to find out what is being done in your area.
Also, here's a link, but I don't think it lists events
anywhere. There is almost certainly going to be
something near you - this is an international event
and surely the bigger areas get events set up.

http://www.scouting.org/international/jota.html

The patches are well worth $4 each. I gagged a bit
the first time I paid that, but when I got them I was
pleased.

As far as Tigers, I would say it would depend a whole
lot on the boy and a whole lot on the operator. If
the operator really relates to little guys, they'll
have a great time, and I've found that most of the
time that's the case. The little guys just don't get
an idea that they are talking to someone way far away.


Depending on where it's located, you could take them
to do a "5G Go-See-Take a hike with your den." You
could also probably call it a "4G-Visit a radio
station, television station, or newspaper office to
find out how people there communicate with each
other." You may not be doing exactly what it says,
but you would be doing the spirit of the requirement,
so if you were in my pack, our CM would say that would
be cool.

Since sometimes radio signals really stink, I always
try to plan something else to do in case the signals
aren't there. I have Webelos, so we're going to do a
3 mile hike and work on map and compass skills, as
well as flag stuff. I've got a cub scout letterboxing
"training series" planned for the park we'll be in on
the 15th and they will be helping me, so we're going
to scout out box locations too.

If your Roundtable is happening this week, you could
also ask around there. You DO go to Roundtable, don't
you???

Lucy
