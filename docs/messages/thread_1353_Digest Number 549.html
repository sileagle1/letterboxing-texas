You've brought up something that we thought about when we hid the State Fair box-our very first hide.  I thought we were just being paranoid, but now, maybe we should have just put a clear box out..  We thought about telling someone, but didn't even know where to start.
The first few boxes I placed, I left clear, but everytime I checked on them, those blue tops were visible and I was sure they'd soon go missing, so I went back and covered them all in camo tape and now they're really harder to spot even if some of the cover gets knocked off; I had noticed that just about every box I found anywhere in this area was camo-covered and that really helped hide it, but I always leave the bottom uncovered and print in permanent markers:  LETTERBOX: PLEASE DO NOT REMOVE and the letterboxing website.  I'm also afraid that just a clear container with plastic bags inside is pretty likely to be taken for litter and thrown away before anyone discovers what's really inside, especially in an area where there might be litter thrown into bushes (unfortunately, not too rare in city areas!), and I guess I figure that at least the camo covering might get a little more attention from a casual finder who might at least check it out before dropping it into a trashcan somewhere. -- Viewfinder
