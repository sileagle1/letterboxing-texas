From: Tracy Malone (momtjm@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-07-31 20:04:15 UTC
Greetings!



Shreveport is only about two hours West of us and we are also new
boxers. We found our first three boxes last weekend and had to
report one missing.



My husband is as enthusiastic about this hobby as I have become. We
have dozens of ideas for "plants" in East Texas but have not yet
attempted to carve a stamp.



We are currently known as the parents of "cowabunga kid" (a definite
ninja turtle fan) with his own ninja turtle stamp. We are still
considering our trail names and read with great delight the goings
on on this list.



Since there are no boxes really close to us we are looking at the
mystery boxes and one specific box "Gryzzled Gryphon Bait" the
clues are fun and have kept us learning as we try to decipher them.
The planters of this box Celtic Lions have corresponded and
encouraged us(as well as corrected us) and this is a fun way to be
connected even when we can not be hunting. We hope to attempt
finding this box (still one difficult clue and the drive to tackle)
in a few weeks.



We have also been intrigued with the discussion over the "I'm Sari,
I Tigress" and may try to seek that one later this year.



T

Am on vacation, but couldn't help but see this mention of one of my
plants, one of my favorite plants in fact.

I love it that others might be as interested in creating and finding
these type of boxes as I am. It thrills me to no end that more and
more of us seem to be having a lot of fun with our plants!

Variety is the spice of life and it's great that there are different
types of boxes for everyone. Some people like drive bys; others like
short or long hikes. Some people like straight-forward clues; some
like them a little more convoluted. Some people like to rack up their
F numbers; others don't pay attention to their numbers at all.

I believe that the journey is part of the process and part of the fun.
Hopefully, many of our boxes are geared toward that. If you need
help identifying which mystery boxes are easier or perhaps a little
nudge, I'm generally happy to do that. I am not very likely to
respond to an email that says, "Before I waste my time. . ."

If you like word games, check out The Saurus Rex.
