From: Maribeth Jagger (cjagger405@earthlink.net) | Date: 2006-08-13 20:19:27 UTC-05:00
I like to place a series close to each other in areas where there are no other boxes. If you're going to get people to come to a remote area to find a box, sometimes it's helps to entice them with several opportunities in a close proximity. Last summer I found eight boxes on one hike in CT and each stamp connected to the next until the entire picture was presented. The only problem with such a box is if one goes missing, you need to be able to replace it rather quickly. It's very satisfying for the finder though.

Hi Dirtdogger,
 
If you are planning on doing all of your letterboxing with your four-legged canine friend, then you might want to check out Dog Scouts of America.  They promote responsible dog ownership through education, a spay/neuter program and canine good behavior.  They also have letterboxing patches that can be earned by the dog when he/she goes letterboxing.  Of course, first you have to earn the "Dog Scout Award".  Still, if you're going to go letterboxing with your dog, you might be interested.  There are probably several of us with kiddos in Cub Scouts and Girl Scouts who also include Dog Scouts in the mix!  It makes for an interesting schedule at times!
