From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-02-16 08:59:58 UTC-06:00
This question comes up every now and then on letterboxing lists.  What's in your letterboxing bag right now???
 
I carry a backpack with me and it weighs around twelve pounds.  I CAN go light if I have to, but I like to keep all my letterboxing stuff together, so I just go ahead and carry it all rather than trying to unpack and repack every time I change my mind about what goes with me.  Most of the time it's no big thing to carry.  What always amazes me is what I manage to collect and never get rid of along the way.
 
So - what's in YOUR bag?  Tell us EVERYTHING!
 
Here's mine:
 
Big pouch:
1-14. ink pads of various colors (no duplicates - THAT would be silly!)
15.   An expired drivers' license - for ID.
16.   $5 - in case I run across a store in the woods I suppose (why DO I carry $5?)
17.   "In case of emergency" phone numbers
 
Middle Pouch:
1. Gallon sized baggie full of baggies of all sorts of sizes
2. Gallon sized baggie of carving stuff-rubber, exacto knives, 2 sets of gouges, charcoal pencils, sharpener, pink pearl erasers.  I guess I envision myself plopping down on the side of the trail and carving out a quick stamp???
3. Two very small - too small - containers.  Out they go!
4. My logbook
5. Lightning's logbook
6. Lock'n'lock box with markers (Marvy, if you care), Fearless Fox's personal stamp, Lightning's personal stamp, my personal stamp.  My three personal travelers (why do I carry a retired traveler around?  I don't know).  An odd unknown stamp - where did I get that?  Now that worries me a little - where did THAT come from?  Looks like - a bear?  about 1.5 x 1.5, hardly been inked. Anybody missing a stamp?  I might have found it.  Eesh...
7. Several old clues (G'bye)
 
Top pouch:
1.  Three disposable rain ponchos (used to be four, but I used one recently).
2.  Headlamp
3.  Gloves - these and my logbook are the two things I will drive miles to get back if I lose them.  The gloves have a stain on one finger which reminds me to always be patient.  The stain is a bloodstain I got at a box location.  When I saw that I was going to have to do a little foraging, I sent Lightning back to the car for my gloves, but I got tired of waiting and started poking around.  Almost immediately I got stabbed by something and my finger bled profusely.  I finally got it stopped, put my hands in my gloves, and got the box.  After stamping in, I noticed I had bled through the leather glove, leaving a large red (now dark brown) stain.  Every time I look at it I remind myself that patience IS a virtue and shortcutting isn't a good idea.
4.  My GPSr
5.  Leatherman
6.  Hitchhiker
 
Outside:
Left Strap - compass
Right Strap - whistle
 
Mesh pouches:
1. Bandaids
2. Liquid bandaid
3. Super glue (hardened up - hmmmm - useful)  Out with this...
4. 6 AA batteries.  I wonder which are good and which aren't...
5. 50 cents
6. unintelligle scraps of paper - I'm sure the meant something at some time.  Time to toss...
7. large acorn I found in Oklahoma a couple years ago.  Now I should put this in my nature collection, but at this point it has traveled with me for a long time.  I'm gonna have to think about what to do with it!
8. a piece of moss I found on the trail recently.  Nature collection - here it comes.
9. various pieces of trail trash...right in the trash can - two points!

--
Barefoot Lucy
"It's not about footwear, it's about philosophy"

Middle Pouch:

2. Gallon sized baggie of carving stuff-rubber, exacto knives, 2 sets of gouges, charcoal pencils, sharpener, pink pearl erasers.  I guess I envision myself plopping down on the side of the trail and carving out a quick stamp???

7. Several old clues (G'bye)

1.  Three disposable rain ponchos (used to be four, but I used one recently).

Let me warn you about buying a backpack - it's one of those things you'll never be completely happy with.  Once you shop for one, you'll keep shopping long after you've gotten the first one.  My backpack is about three years old - comfortable and familiar at this point, and yet I can't go to Gander Mountain without looking for a new one.  I've seen a couple fishing bags I'm considering, but so far I've resisted.  Some things I really like about mine - a loop for my keys inside one of the zip pockets and padded straps.  Outside pockets that strap tight against the bag (i.e. not loose so stuff can fall out easily).  Three decent sized pockets - not too big so things fall all the way to the bottom, but big enough to carry big things - like rocks.
