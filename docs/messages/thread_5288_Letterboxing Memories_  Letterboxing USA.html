From: Maribeth (cjagger405@earthlink.net) | Date: 2013-11-02 15:19:02 UTC-05:00

Thanks for yet another good stroll down memory lane, Silver Eagle. Before I found out about the Texas list (around 2004?), I was still posting on LBNA, and that was a scary prospect for a newby. I never knew when I'd get a good "schooling" from the powers that be. When Viewfinder told me about the TX list (I was still living in CT at the time, but plotting my move back to TX), I was thrilled to find a talk list of such friendly, FUN folks. I really literally could NOT wait to get back home and share the letterboxing experience with such cool people. Yeeeeessssss..... there have been flare-ups on the TX list from time to time, but generally they were quickly resolved, feathers were smoothed, and the issue was over quickly. During the time I was transitioning from CT to TX, the Gryzzled Gryphon mystery was the talk of the list, and it was incredibly fun (Silver Eagle, I hope you share that part of our history as well.) Shortly after returning to Texas, I was invited to meet Ruby Tuesday for coffee. I loved her immediately, and she represented everything I loved about letterboxing. A couple of months later was the JEN Barnraising event, and a mysterious, perplexing clue was left during the night at the shelter near Barefoot Lucy's campsite by the illusive Gryphon. More FUN! After finally solving the issue of who this shadowy mystery boxer was, we settled down to populate the state with boxes, trying to place at least one in every county in Texas (no small task). Throughout all this activity, I had to check the Texas list several times a day to see if the Texas Tour Bus was making a stop anywhere near me, so I could make a run for it! We had so few boxes placed in north Texas at that time, if a new box was announced on the Texas list, Viewfinder and I would totally change our plans and go immediately to look for it (always trying to beat Gryzzled Gryphon to be the first finders). Then came the Scarlet Pimpernel, and that consumed the talk on our list for months as we were all trying to solve this puzzle. So... from my perspective, the Texas list has been a very important part of my entertainment for years and hopefully will be for years to come. Again, thanks to those who keep it going.  Puddle Splasher
