From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-01-28 18:28:57 UTC
Ok, who's coming when to the gathering? Any earlybirds?

I have been soliciting boxes from boxers across the country to add a
bit of spice to the gathering. Last year we had the fabulous clue
game organized by the Celtic Lions, and I think something similar
might be fun.

Lucy has graciously agreed to be the maintenance woman on the boxes
that will be permanent for the out-of-staters. To that end, I have
asked that boxes/stamps be sent to me no later than February 25th.
Lucy is also scouting hiding places both in Rusk State Park and other
areas around Rusk/Palestine that would be appropriate (yea lucy!)

I am thinking of organizing a boxcar bingo game for the day. The way
I think it would work is that you would be given a card with the names
of boxes on it (maybe 5squares x5squares). You'll need to go out into
the wild, find boxes, stamp them onto the card in addition to your
logbook and try to get boxcar "BINGO."

To pull it off, I think help is required, to hide some of the
long-distance submissions on Friday or maybe earlier. And if you're
carving a box that you're going to hide and would like it to be part
of the bingo, I'll just need to know the name of your box in advance.

BTW, so far I have boxes/commitments from SewSoBizzy, So Hill
Hendersons, Dixie Kin, Penguin Patrol, Speedsquare, Fungus Woman,
Happy Hyper Hikers, Mumma & Bunny Boy & a couple of others I can't
recall right now. I think it should add to the fun for everyone.

Dewberry

From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-01-28 22:37:53 UTC
Well, I like games and I like letterboxes and I feel like letterboxing
games add another dimension to a gathering (as long as you don't
exclude poor Rudolf). Of course, my personal gathering experience is
really limited to 2 hours at last year's TX event, but I read
enviously about the fun things others do, and think hey, we can do
that too! I think since we're coming from such a wide area and never
see each other, we absolutely need time to meet, chat, exchange &
greet. You wouldn't want to overplan activities, but I thought that
one would be a welcome addition.

However, I could be totally wrong and off-base. Since I'm not a host,
I'm certainly willing to can the whole idea of the bingo game if
that's not what people want as part of the Texas gathering. I'll just
plant the boxes already sent, call off the others, and leave it at
that. Makes it much easier for me, rather than making oodles of bingo
cards and strategizing that whole activity.

We're hoping to put on a greater Houston area event sometime and we
can leave the games for that one, if that's the general preference.

I would definitely not want to step on our hosts' tootsies, or paws
and talons as the case may be.

Thoughts?
