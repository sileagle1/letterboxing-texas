From: Carol McNally (carolmcnally@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2013-10-31 20:14:43 UTC-07:00
I just have to say that I've never met SE but my family vacations every winter in Durango Co.  One year we decided to vacation there in the summer.  I was looking for things to do and found that SE had placed a box that was generally only accessible in the summer in Durango Co.  I manage to talk most of my family into hunting down a letterbox...and you have to understand, my kids have been tortured with Texas letterboxes (tromping through the woods in 100+ degree temps) so this one sounded cool..literally. SE brought us to a beautiful rec area and we walked up a path with beautiful views, even got great pictures of my kids with grandma with a beautiful backdrop....made it to the letterbox site..found the box....and low and behold there were coins inside!  How exciting for my kids!  they loved it!  
so everyone think what they will..but I love letterboxing.I don't do it much at home but if I"m traveling, I'll look for letterboxes...we recently went to the Davis mountains and looked for a lot of boxes..but didn't find any of them..but the thought of finding a box got us out of our camper (when it was raining) and sent us on  a mission to explore and find...which is just awesome in my opinion...

From: Debbie Kotlarek (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2013-10-31 23:07:45 UTC-05:00

I enjoyed your first story and am looking forward to reading more. 

 

We've been boxing for a little over 10 years and when we started I knew store-bought stamps weren't popular based on the sites I read.  I therefore designed our custom sig stamps (simple images) and emailed them to a website called Rubberstampman.com.  We received two nice stamps with wooden handles and we still use these as our sig stamps.  The first 3 boxes we planted had custom-made stamps I ordered from the same website.  However I then realized this would be a somewhat expensive proposition to keep ordering custom stamps, so we took the plunge and started carving.  My first box with a hand-carved stamp (our 4th plant) was 8 months after our first find.  We now have a bit over 300 boxes planted in 45 states, Puerto Rico, Mexico, Finland and New Zealand.

 

Wisconsin Hiker

From: Maribeth (cjagger405@earthlink.net) | Date: 2013-11-01 10:20:18 UTC-05:00

Re: Silver Eagle's carving memories!

Thanks for sharing your memories with us. I remember living in CT and having just discovered letterboxing. My LSS had a business trip to San Diego in the Spring and in order to escape the the 18" of snow around my house, I chose to tag along, LBing clues in hand for that area of California. One of those clues was at Torres Pines just up the coastline. It was a beautiful stamp carved by a guy in Texas, whose trail name was Silver Eagle (whom I later met on the letterboxing Texas site and was soon to become one of the letterboxers I most  admire and enjoy his friendship)! That trip was also my first attempt at carving my own stamp - a little chick to represent a Texas letterboxing chick (livng in CT but soon to move back to my home state). Okay, that stamp took about six attempts to carve and ended up looking like a duck on LSD. However, I proudly planted that box in La Jolla. (Easily the ugliest piece of "art" in that artsy little town.)  I never actually heard much about it being found, so I assume someone did find it and hurled it off the cliff into the Pacific after looking at the stamp. However, undaunted, I continued to carve stamps and they continued to get a little better.

I still enjoy finding a hand-carved stamp more, even if it's a very ugly "chick", because it seems more personal to me to see the placer's effort and handiwork. Having said that,  I always like to think of a statement made by our friend Gryzzled Gryphon: "I never met a letterbox I didn't like." (but then he's always been cooler than me!)
