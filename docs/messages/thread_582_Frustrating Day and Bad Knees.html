Frustrating Day and Bad Knees

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-01-21 18:12:58 UTC-08:00
My son and I got up and going bright and early this
morning to take the Ghosts of Nacogdoches Historical
Trail through Nacogdoches. I graduated from SFA, so I
carved a totally awesome lumberjack and was ready to
plant it today. But FIRST, we were going to do the
historical trail, a trail that would qualify us for a
scout patch if we successfully finished it. This is a
(concrete) street trail through downtown Nac, but even
though I didn't figure on having much of an
opportunity to plant it along the way, I kept my eyes
peeled just in case.

Well, the trail was very interesting - 3.5 miles of
urban (concrete) hiking, basically from historical
marker to historical marker. I've always loved Nac,
but now I really have a better understanding of the
town. Unfortunately, the tour book had pretty rough,
and in some cases outdated, directions. I figured
that out on the first leg, but I thought - "What the
heck? I can figure it out if we're a little off." Do
y'all know how important it is to know which side of
the (concrete) street you are supposed to be on on an
urban historical trail? Yep. Pretty important! The
street directions were confusing, so I was careful to
use my compass to confirm direction as we went. That
helped quite a bit until I came to one clue that said
"Turn North on xxx Street." I read my compass and
looked at the (concrete) street, and I was pretty
sure, based on compass reading and in spite of being
tired, that the (concrete) street ran East and West!
We took it anyway, and sure enough, the next street
confirmed for me that it was an E/W street, not N/S.
We repeatedly managed to get ourselves from point A to
point B using the directions and our letterboxer
instinct, and a lot of guessing, but it really ate
into our time, until we had to ditch the last two
points of the hike AND planting my really cool
lumberjack. Unfortunately, I had to get back to our
town to pick up my paycheck (it hasn't ever really
bothered me until today that my company doesn't do
direct deposit). We are both dead tired this evening
and ready for bed here at 8 p.m. - and my knees? Did
I mention that the entire hike was on CONCRETE? I
always forget what a difference it is to have soil
under my feet.

Wouldn't you know it - I decided that to save bulk in
my backpack as we hiked, I would go ahead and stamp
the box into my log and vice versa. UGH! I wish I
had at least not put the dates in! I NEVER stamp in
until we are at the hiding place and ready to leave
the box behind, but I thought I was doing myself a
favor by leaving my log and personal stamps in the
car. If it had been a micro, I could've easily placed
it - guess I'm gonna have to try a micro here soon.

Still though, it was a really neat trail and very
interesting! If you are ever in Nacogdoches and have
four or five hours to kill, give it a try! Just
remember, it's a lesson in patience when it comes to
the direction, but it IS do-able.

And I spoke to my West Texas crew a little while ago-
they didn't climb today as planned because of the
weather, and are tentatively planning to climb
tomorrow instead. Hubby injured one of his knees in
Carlsbad Caverns today. It's nothing major, but he's
trying to decide whether he wants to brave the
mountain. It wouldn't be much fun to decide half-way
through the trip that the knee wouldn't make it.

It sucks getting old, you know???
Lucy
