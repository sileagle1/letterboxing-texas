Neglected letterboxes

From: (candj1969@yahoo.com) | Date: 2016-05-23 12:16:09 UTC-07:00

Another example of how letterboxing has changed over the years:  Lone Star Quilter received a notice of an attempt on her letterbox called Home Again which is in a cemetery south of Brownwood.  The box was last reported as found in February, 2012.  The attempt was not that the box couldn't be found but because the tree (the fork of a good size tree about 5 feet off the ground) had grown around the box and they couldn't get it out.  Okay, Brownwood is centrally located, this is a nice box with a good story and a hand-carved stamp, in an easy to find and get to cemetery, which is well-maintained in a pleasant country setting.  It's only been found a few times. What's wrong with this scenario?  Why would it not be found for over four years?  I'm not faulting the finders for not getting the box out, but I think Wry Me would have figured out a way.
