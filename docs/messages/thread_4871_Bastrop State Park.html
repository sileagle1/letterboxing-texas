From: Lou (lou.catozzi@att.net) | Date: 2011-09-08 03:31:51 UTC
I just found a link to the latest Bastrop fire map and it appears that the entire park is inside the fire perimeter except for the golf course and the entrance station. Now this does not mean that every square foot of the park has burned over. In almost every wildfire there are areas of little to no damage scattered throughout the fire area. That being said, I fear that most, if not all, of the letterboxes located in the park are gone.

I do believe that what is commonly referred to as the "oldest letterbox in Texas", Lost Pines by Eoghan, is safe. Here is the link to the fire map for 9/7/2011.

http://media2.myfoxaustin.com/ktbcmisc/bastropfire907.jpg

Bastrop State Park

From: Boots Tex (redbarn@eastex.net) | Date: 2011-12-04 23:03:52 UTC
Bastrop State Park was opened to visitors Friday, 11-2-11, and since we were nearby on Saturday, we went in to check it out. Damage is severe in some places and most trails are still closed. Pine Warbler Trail was open, so we checked Lone Star Quilter's box "Lost Pine Warbler" and found it destroyed. I've posted a photo album in the Photos section. We didn't have clues to other boxes, but I think you can assume that they're all lost. The rocks around the box apparently served as a fine oven, reducing the box to a molten mess. I guess we'll have to go back in a few months and restock the park with boxes.

Boots Tex

Re: Bastrop State Park

From: Boots Tex (redbarn@eastex.net) | Date: 2011-12-05 16:13:49 UTC
You'd have to see it for yourself because there are some areas, like the photographs show, where the ground is stripped of all vegetation and then there are other areas that are untouched. Vegetation is already starting to show up, with little green sprouts everywhere. A massive cleanup effort is going on, with trees being cut and stacked and reduced to mulch. We didn't get to see all the trails, but I wouldn't be surprised if some boxes survived, if they just happened to be in the right place. As you know, forest fires are a natural occurance and I believe most of the trees will survive as part of that process. Fire can be a good thing if you only consider the forest and not the human loss, like homes and property that were lost. I'd guess that by next spring, there will be an amazing recovery taking place. Some of the asphalt paving has been replaced and at least one trail parking area that wasn't paved before has new paving. Work is in progress, so I would go ahead and plan TALE 10 for Bastrop if I were you. It might be good for everyone who happens by that way to pop in and check the progress and post a report on this forum. We also went by Buescher SP and asked about the Lost Pines Trail, where the Lost Pines box is located and from what I was told, I think the fire missed that area, although I didn't walk the trail.

Boots Tex

From: (sandstorm_62@yahoo.com) | Date: 2019-06-23 14:55:18 UTC
Unfortunately if the letterbox was not registered with the Bastrop State Park's office that it was more than likely removed in one of their clean ups that are hosted by the rangers. They will remove all hides if found and are not registered. Park officials are really strict on letter boxing and geocaching because of the hazards (falling trees, snake activity, ect...) due to the terrible fire and heavy flooding rains to the park in the past. It has been a real slow and disheartening recovery process for this park. I know many letterboxers are against the imposed regulations, BUT if you are willing to post it on the internet... why not be curtious and register it with park officials and make sure it is safe and will be enjoyed by your fellow letterboxers.

Thank you Stormie aka DustySandStorm the hyberd letterboxer/geocacher
