Thanksgiving Trip

From: Silver Eagle (sileagle@alltel.net) | Date: 2005-11-26 23:53:59 UTC
I just got back from my camping trip to South TX and
Thanksgiving in Austin and managed to get in some boxing.
I left last Sunday with my 2 children and we headed to
Mustang Island SP where we camped for 2 nights. We first
stopped in Victoria where I found Ancient Monkey God,
which was planted just after I visited the same park last
year. Then it was on to Goose Island SP where we found
Missing Key #1 and I placed a new box called Big Tree 2,
since my original box went missing. Consider it a new box
since it is a new stamp and box, but is still near the Big Tree.
Next we drove through Aransas Pass and boarded the free Ferry
boat to Port Aransas on Mustang Island. It was fun and we
got to see several porpoises. We made it to the park just
before sunset to pitch our tent among all the RVs and just
managed to find Treasure Island before dark. Unfortunately
all the other boxes that used to be in this park and in Port
Aransas are now missing, but it is still a fun place to visit.
The next day we drove back to Port Aransas to visit the UT
Marine Institute, which provided a free hour's worth of fun
looking at marine life in various aquariums. By now it was
starting to warm up, so we headed to Padre Island NS to play
on the beach. We had most of the beach to ourselves and
enjoyed clear water and clean sand for a change. It is hard
to believe that is the same Gulf Of Mexico we swim in here
in Galveston! As we left, I placed my TX Turtle 2 box just
outside the park and hopefully it will last longer than the
original did. This is also a new box with a new stamp. Then
it was back to camp and up early to see the sun rise out of the
Gulf. What a sight! As we headed north, I stopped in Corpus
Christi to place Corpus Porpoise 2. Again, a new box with
new stamp, so even if you got the original ones, now you
have more to look for next time you go down there.
We then headed to Beeville to look for Veterans Memorial box,
but came up empty. There were several people playing the
disc golf course so I could not look too long. Next it was on
to Three Rivers where we found Dancing Ducks a little wet, but
still dancing. We went on to Choke Canyon SP and set up our
tent (the only ones in the entire tent campground), then went
looking for the boxes in the park. We confirmed Southern
Magnolia is missing and tried to find a box with no clues,
just sketchy information from Busy Bee, but were not successful.
I hid a new box on the same trail called Choke Canyon Caracara
that hopefully will have better luck than others. We then drove
to another trail and found Gator Pond with Gryzzled Gryphon as
the only visitor. On the way back to camp we saw several wild
pigs and deer, and that night we heard owls and coyotes, so
really felt like we were camping.
The next day we had to head to Austin to meet my wife and
relatives for Thanksgiving, but we did not head straight there.
By winding along many back roads I was able to look for a few
more boxes. In Helena I came up empty looking for RIP #4, but
found Solidarnosc in Panna Maria. In Floresville we confirmed
Peanuts is missing, but found Rancho De Las Cabras.
In Stockdale I had something new happen to me; I could not find
a park. I drove down the specified road twice and never saw
the entrance, so missed out on the 3 boxes in Jackson Nature Park.
Corazon later sent me a picture of the entrance and all I can
say is I must have assumed it was a private ranch entrance since
there were so many off that road. Oh well, maybe next time.
In La Vernia we found Square & Compass, One Stoplight Town and
Simple Summer Series #1. I found the other ones in this series
last year but missed this one while it was being replaced, so
was glad to finally finish this great series. Our final box
was Panther Pride in Geronimo and I was glad school was out
since it would have been tough to get otherwise.
I had planned to do more boxing on a day trip to Fredericksburg
along with the Hill Country Christmas Light Trail at night, which
I highly recommend, but the weather stopped us and we came home
instead. It was a fun trip and thanks to all placers for making
it even more enjoyable!

Silver Eagle





Thanksgiving Trip

From: sileagle1 (sileagle@windstream.net) | Date: 2009-11-30 00:55:00 UTC
Over Thanksgiving weekend I was in the Hill Country and managed to place 5 new boxes, 4 of which are part of my Games Series. The Blue, Blue Fish box is part of my Dr. Seuss Series and was placed in San Marcos in a park I had never been to before. It is called Crook Park and connects with Ramon Lucio Park for a nice hike. It also has a Nature Center and garden area that made for a nice place to wait while my wife shopped at the Outlet Malls.

Two of the Games boxes are new versions of the 20 Questions Game: Thing and Place. The other two include spinners, which is my first attempt at animation: Chutes & Ladders and The Game Of Letterboxer Life - TX Edition. This last game gives you the chance to become one of your fellow TX Letterboxers as you play my version of the Game Of Life. Baby Bear can even be IRA Boxer and vice-versa. I hope you enjoy them!

Silver Eagle
