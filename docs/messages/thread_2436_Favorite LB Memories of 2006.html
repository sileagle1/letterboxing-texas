From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-01-01 00:17:22 UTC-06:00
I think I'm on caffeine overload - drilling holes in leather rounders (scout thing) and thinking about my logbook and the fact that I didn't mention some of my favorite LBing things that happened in the last year, mainly because some of them didn't hit my logbook, I guess.
 
How about:
 
The Great Book Adventure - okay, it DID hit my logbook, but I was sitting here thinking about the mad, MAD scramble to get all those literary travelers.  Wasn't that fun?  And the storybook walk?  And all the clever games to play?  And Atoy Adventurers showing up in perfect costumes?  And the sugar rush Sunday morning?
 
Hueco Tanks - what about Mike's monumental effort to recover and replace those boxes?  And I loved seeing it all through his pictures.
 
The snake showdown while boxing with my sister-in-law at Livingston State Park and us dubbing Lightning "snake slayer" for the rest of the trip through the park.  He's sound asleep in the living room right now, and he doesn't look like a snake slayer at the moment!
 
The week Fearless Fox and Lightning were gone and I was alone, so I went letterboxing every evening. 
 
The snake encounter in Temple inside a huge cedar/juniper tree - I would've left him my sunglasses, but I wasn't going to leave him my keys!
 
Those are a few of mine - what are YOURS?

From: Maribeth Jagger (cjagger405@earthlink.net) | Date: 2007-01-01 00:53:22 UTC-06:00
Okay... I'll play!
How about exactly one year ago when I saw the Texas Tour Bus had alluded me in Cleburne, but had resurfaced in Tatum? I loaded my letterboxing supplies, filled my Jeep with gas, and set my alarm for 2:30 a.m. I was on the road, driving through the eerie, empty streets of Dallas by 3:00 a.m. on my way to Martin Creek Lake State Park. I got there at 6:00 a.m. and snagged the Tour Bus right out from under Barefoot Lucy's clutches! (Sorry Lucy! I just couldn't help reminiscing! I know how you love that story.)
 
The Rusk Event was great. Even the cool, rainy weather added to the fun and atmosphere as we gathered around the fireplace and exchanged stamps, stories and memories. I'm really sorry we overwhelmed Big Bertha and put her out of business though.
 
The Meese Meet at Lake Grapevine was the first mini gathering for the metroplex boxers. It was so cool that in addition to the local boxers, Dewberry, Silver Eagle and Running Rain could come up for it too. And I always love seeing Blue Butterfly and Lucy Locket! And Gryzzled Gryphon - we have no idea where he actually calls home so I guess he's just "of Coryell County", but it was great seeing him again too. The Celtic Lions out did themselves planning this event.
 
When I was in Nevada, I found one of Sierra Sally's boxes, so I was thrilled when she was in Arlington at a conference and Viewfinder and I had the opportunity to meet her for an exchange.
 
I think one of the best parts is all the fun messages I've received from people who have found my boxes or replied back to me when I reported finding their boxes. What a wonderful community of letterboxers! I received a message today from someone in Connecticut who had found a box I placed up there. It's on a rather difficult trail which is not really "hikable" in the winter. I was excited to hear that the weather was nice and sunny up there today and people were out boxing and enjoying themselves. It's really fun to think that all over the country, on any given day, there are letterboxers out there having fun!

From: SandiBox (sandibox@msn.com) | Date: 2007-01-01 07:37:04 UTC
2006 was a great letterboxing year. My highlights:

Spending so much wonderful one-on-one time with SoccerChik. And
hearing her tell a friend, "I can't go to the mall today. I'm out
having fun with my mom." Knowing she'll be off to college in a
couple of years, I'll treasure forever the time we've spent talking
about our dreams, hopes for the future... and just plain ole' girl
stuff.

I learned to carve! Practice, practice, practice... that and a
really good light and a pair of very strong reading glasses to see
the details!

Letterboxing with my Mom in Galveston. Dashing into a souvenir shop
to buy umbrellas so we could keep letterboxing in the rain, only to
have it stop as soon as we walked out of the store.

Losing a planted box for the first time... and getting unexpected
help in replacing it. (Thanks again Mike!) Losing two more boxes,
and learning how to be a better planter.

Participating in my first postal ring, "All for August" (which
continued into September, October, November and December!) Checking
my mailbox with excitment for each new box... then finally having my
own little box return home and seeing all the stamps and comments.

Attending the Great Book Adventure and meeting so many of the
wonderful folks on this list! Going to Snap, Crackle and Clues the
next morning and boxing with Barefoot Lucy and Lightning. Completely
filling up my first personal log book! Letterboxing gluttony!

Meeting Runsforchocolate back out at Burroughs a couple of weeks
later to finish up the boxes we couldn't get to at the gathering...
fighting off the killer mosquitoes, then later enjoying that fabulous
salsa at Los Cucos!

Meeting Norasta and Guitarzan during their visit to Texas, and
discovering Mercer Arboretum. Can't believe I've lived in Houston
for over 30 years and have never been there before!

Playing Candy Land and overloading on sweet treats... including that
Italian chocolate!

2006 has been so much fun, I can't wait to see what 2007 will bring!

SandiBox

From: Silver Eagle (sileagle@alltel.net) | Date: 2007-01-01 18:05:45 UTC
Here are a few of mine:

1. Letterboxing in the snow with Baby Bear while our wives shopped
in Lake Tahoe, then actually winning at craps.

2. Relaxing by the fire and talking with Barefoot Lucy, GG, and Sea
Dove at the end of the Rusk Event, while drinking Bl's homemade Irish
Cream!

3. Getting to letterbox in New England during our family vacation
and actually finding a few new places to place boxes among the
thousands already there. It is always fun to receive notes from
letterboxers thanking me for taking them to new places, but it is
even more satisfying when the notes are from people that live there
and discovered new places they had never been.

4. Putting together a spur-of-the-moment driving vacation through
Oklahoma and Arkansas using letterboxes (some existing, some that I
wanted to place) as my main guide. Even my non-letterboxing family
had a good time.

5. Watching my daughter Running Rain enjoy getting a special stamp
at the Great Book Adventure Event for dressing up as Hermione. It
was also great to see Wanda again and finally meet Pete. The whole
Event was just incredible!

6. Squeezing in a couple hours between soccer games in Dallas to
attend the Many Meese Mini-Event that was put on by Ruby Tuesday. I
am very grateful the metroboxers picked that weekend to hold the
Event, knowing I would be in town. I did not get to find many boxes,
but getting to visit with all the people while eating moosicles and
moose droppings was well worth it!

7. Discovering that Houston is the LB Capital of North America, and
then placing a box in honor of all that made it happen.

8. Attending the Kiss From The Sugar Plum Fairy Event and reliving
my childhood memories of playing Candy Land. It was another great
Event put on by Dewberry and MO5, and was a great ending to a great
letterboxing year!

Silver Eagle

From: ThimbleCat (inklaura@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2007-01-01 19:53:41 UTC
New Year's Day always makes me want to box because that's the
anniversary of our first find. We are not prolific boxers due to
time constraints (We seem to have a severe case of volunteer-itis)
but we really enjoy finding or planting a box now and then. I have
forced myself to keep this as a fun and relaxing hobby and not get
too hung up on the perfect logbook or record keeping system or
scrapbook. I still have the urge to go overboard in the organizing
aspect of it all, but am fighting it successfully so far :).

In 2006, we really enjoyed attending the GBA and finally getting to
put faces with the trail names. It was crazy and hectic and soo
much fun! I am trying hard to figure out a way to get us up to
Somerville in March and avoid scout conflicts for that weekend.

2006 was the year that I was able to work with the San Jacinto GS
council to get boxing started at all the Council GS camps. I've done
some leader trainings at big training events and really enjoyed
seeing the leaders enthusiasm for the sport. It is really beginning
to take off and now troops can safely box on camping weekends without
fear of harming public boxes. Some scout volunteers in this area
are now starting to incorporate letterboxing into their Scout events
(planting a temporary trail of boxes similar to the storybook trail
at the GBA - My SIL Hibisue did this recently and it was a big
success). We still need a better way to distribute clues to leaders
so that is something I'll work on in 07.

My other goal for 07 is to plant more boxes (both public and GS
boxes) and actually MAKE more time to get out there and box. Here's
to another year of fun!

ThimbleCat of The Polka Dots

Some of my favorite letterboxing memories of 2006 include:
 
Every letterboxing adventure with the Frye Team including being chased by dogs, coming across an area infested with ticks, almost being rescued by Boots Tex, being at the farthest most point from the car in Memorial Park when the sky opened and we got caught in a downpour!

Planting my first out of state letterbox in Arizona last April and having lots of people visit the box!
 
Playing the Houston Letterboxing Challenge in June...and winning!  :)
 
Meeting and boxing with Sandibox in October and again playing Candyland with her in December.
 
Dewberry's Winter Wonderland Book Fair.  FINALLY! Some stamps that didn't require deciphering her nasty clues!!  :)
 
The Candyland event in December- what a great way to end the year surrounded by letterboxing friends...and then finding out the MO5's 5 year old son thought I was a teenager- too cool!
