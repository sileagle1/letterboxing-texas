Camping trip to the East Texas Pineywoods

From: Birds of a Feather Family (birdsofafeather_lbox@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-11-27 21:31:57 UTC-08:00
Hi,
 
The Birds of a Feather family have returned from a holiday camping trip to the East Texas Pineywoods area.
 
Here is an update on boxes:
 
11/24/2004
  • Silver Eagle's Spanish Flag - Found in good shape
  • Baby Bear's Tejas - Bag holding log book had couple inches of water and needed maintenance. Celtic Lions had left some note cards which we stamped in on. I put the notecards and stamp in new bags. I took the log book back to our camper and dried it out.  It is in sad shape. I returned the log book on 11/26/2004 to the box and found that some other boxers had stamped in on Thanksgiving, 11/25.

11/26/2004

  • Baby Bear's Hogg - Found in good shape
  • Barefoot Lucy and Crew's Starry, Starry Night - Found in good shape
  • Baby Bear's Stone Celt - We searched around the boardwalk platform but did not find it.

11/27/2004

  • Silver Eagle's Texas RR - The south end of the lake has just been recently cleared of trees.  I found the twin trees and dug through the debris, but did not find the box.
  • Birds of a Feather Family placed a new letterbox at the Rusk Unit of the Rusk/Palestine State Parks and will have the clues available in a day or so.

Sea Dove
