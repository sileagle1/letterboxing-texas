New Question - What's on your work bench???

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-03-21 21:08:00 UTC-05:00
NTC mentioned that we did the What's In Your Backpack? thing lately and I've been working really hard at getting my workbench in shape, so I thought we might have fun with "What's on your Work Bench? (or wherever you work at carving or logbook making)
I've sometimes teased my husband that he likens toilet paper to wealth and he wants to display ours for all to see.  We have a perfectly fine closet that it could hide in, but instead ALL the toilet paper we own sits in the package on the floor of our bathroom.  Well - my workbench must be my center of wealth - I want everything in view and in reach!
 
A couple of years ago I decided I needed some space of my own where the lady of the house wouldn't get irate with me for leaving shavings on the floor or getting ink on the table or accidentally carving the furniture.  I walked about the house, inside and out, with an eye toward the perfect place.  For awhile I considered a corner in the Living Room That Isn't a Living Room where there is perfect light, but after setting up shop there, I realized I still had to deal with the shavings/ink/carving situation.  I considered a place in the corner of the yard where no one goes and I briefly considered building myself a lean-to, but that was daunting in itself and knowing how I am about finishing big projects, realized I needed space before the decade is over. 
 
I finally did a commando raid on our garage, braving the stacks of stuff and the layers of dust.  I cleaned and cleared the corner workbench, right by the Very Dark Little Room at the back of the garage where things unknown are placed, right across the narrow walking space from the dryer, and right under the big giant open-faced fan that sounded like a 747 warming up its engines and blew gunk all over me whenever its switched was flipped (and for the record, it pulled enough power to turn off half the lights in the house too).  I staked my claim bravely and when my husband came home and discovered that I was missing inside, he took a shot on finding me in the garage (must've figured I was taking a load of clothes to the dryer) and found me there - exacto blade in hand, daring him to try to retake the workbench.  Now Fearless Fox is a wise man and he knows how to pick his battles - after 23 years of wedded bliss, he should.  He turned and walked away, but I knew it wasn't that simple.  To maintain control of the corner workbench, I was going to have to keep it occupied and filled. 
 
Well in the last months, I've been lax in guarding my space, and a couple of bicycles have been stacked in front of it, a ladder, some bottles of something were placed on the workbench and the storage shelves had eased their clutter back over near my collection of stuff.  Last weekend, I again retook the Workbench, cleaning out my craft cabinet and throwing a lot of stuff in and around it away, and taking other craft stuff stacked on the big metal shelves and placing it in the cabinet so I could make room for the intruding stuff around my Workbench.  I moved the bikes out of the way, the ladder too, and I again had my space and it was time to get things in proper order. 
 
So, my work area goes something like this:
 
ON THE BENCH:
A jar of clothes pins (I still haven't completely finished cleaning)
 
Ink Pads-roughly 2 dozen.  They are stacked in two stacks against the back wall of the bench, and last weekend I marked the color on the side of each one with a Sharpie so I wouldn't have to keep unstacking them to find the color I wanted.  Odd that it took me that long to think of it!
 
Baggie of index cards with cards cut down to size for LTC's.
 
A small vise to keep things together that are supposed to stay together.  I used it back when I backed my stamps.
 
Magnifying Lamp.  A must for over-40 eyes.  In the base of it is a shallow bowl for collecting small things.  I keep my current exacto, a gouge, and a couple of pencil sharpeners there, and that's where I put things when I'm working on a project so I can reach and grab without taking my eyes off what I'm working on.  On, and one of those dental tools with the twisty ends?  It seemed like a good idea at the flea market, but I haven't found a good use for it yet.
 
A coffee can with old markers, writing pens, a couple different awls,  I try to keep at least one of a particular writing pen that I am fond of in the can, but I am notoriously bad about stealing from myself, so this does'nt work out too well.
 
Box of Exactos.
 
Box of gouge blades.
 
A couple of cans of canned air.  Very helpful for cleaning up a project, or revealing "loose hairs" on a carving.
 
Large cutting board.
 
A stack of carving block - PZ Kut - White - B Grade, if you care.  A few other miscellaneous pieces, but mostly that.  A box with pink pearl erasers and other odd small carving chunks.  I like to keep at least 10 clean blocks on hand, in case I go into a carving frenzy and stampeaz is closed for a few days.
 
Somewhere on my workbench, you would find my cell phone, not that I care to take phone calls, but it is also my MP3 player and I thoroughly enjoy listening to music while I'm carving.
 
And finally, several books that inspire creativity or help solve clues.  Among other things, I have a book on making books and a couple of code books I keep close by, and a writing tablet so that if I suddenly think of something to write, I don't have to go to my inside desk to do it (and frankly, I get far more inspired at my workbench than I do at my other desk).  I have a notebook with my 101 list and a few other select inspirational items in it, and a notebook for keeping notes for myself in. 
 
SHELVES TO MY RIGHT:
Since I am left-handed, anything that goes to my right is pretty much storage only.  My little bench is hemmed in by a large silver metal storage shelf and I managed to eek out some room on the shelves to keep boxes, so I have several boxes that are wanting to be placed and several that are waiting to be filled.  I also have a couple of pieces of old backer board. 
 
TO MY LEFT:
On the left side of my bench are three small storage shelves.
 
Hitchhikers waiting to be turned loose.
 
A jar of corks.  A jar of corks?  Gotta put 'em somewhere.
 
Card file of old designs I've used in boxes and stamps.
 
Box of wrong stamps.  I used to keep these lined up around my workbench at eye level.  It made me smile to look at the parade of silly mistakes I made as I carved, or the moments when I made one cut too many, or I shortcutted by drawing directly on to my carving material.  I finally had to admit that there were so many boo-boos on my shelf that I needed to take them off display to allow room for other things, so I placed them in a hinged Tupperware (TM) container that has served in various capacities ever since I started letterboxing.  I have to admit - I really miss looking at my misfit army.
 
India Ink and calligraphy pens.
 
Circle Cutter.
 
Illuminating table for making tracing easier.
 
Rotary cutter.
 
A ziplok bag of brightly colored heavy cardstock.  Surely good for something!
 
And finally,
 
ON THE WALL IN FRONT OF ME:
I have a large pegboard - with pegs:
 
Embroidery Hoops.  I remember using them for something to do with letterboxing, but it sure is escaping me right now!
 
Epoxy
 
Glue gun
 
Sandpaper
 
Extra carving tools.
 
Coping saws.
 
Floral tape. 
 
Color - map pencils, markers (cheapo markers, not the ones we carry), watercolor pencils (have y'all used these?  They are cool!), oil crayons,
 
Leather punches
 
Woodworking gouges
 
Sandpaper
 
Cropodile - this is a cool tool SandiBox had when she did her logbook making demo.  I just HAD to have one!
 
And finally, a stack of store-boughts I need to put away.  I occasionally use them, but not very often.
 
ON THE FLOOR:
 
My backpack, packed and ready to go.
 
A plastic box that has stuff in it like patches and other things that I need to have nearby, but stashed away.
 
*************************
 
Looking at this list, I realized my backpack list was very logical and everything had a purpose.  My workbench?  Wow!  It shows my distractible nature all over it.  I guess the difference is that my workbench is the center of my creativity and my backpack is the essentials of the hunt.
 
So who dares take inventory of THEIR work area???
--
Barefoot Lucy

Lets see.
 
I have a few "Work Bench" projects.
 
Not really on the workbench, but in the computer....  The Great State of Texas - through the local Texas Parks and Wildlife office, has asked me to convert all the hikes I have posted on LocalHikes.com into a different format for their use in mapping the trails.  I will also need to survey and complete a form for each trail.  Currently, I have over 50 hikes listed.  But only about 25 in El Paso.  So I am slowly working on that.  I sent a message out for others around the state to see if they wanted to help - makes us letterboxers look good in their eyes - stepping up to assist.  Ann you need it a GPS that keeps a track-log.  Most of them do that.  If you are interested contact me.  I should have this project finished soon - well - within a month anyway.
 
Here in the electronics shop, I have a $6000 9-band short wave transceiver waiting for one more part before I do some repairs and upgrades to it.  This radio allows me to contact radio operators around the world.  I have many friends in Europe, as I lived there for 7 years.  And there are many soldiers both active and retired with radio equipment that I often speak with.  Also, it is fun to meet new people on the air and learn about where they live.  Ever hear of Ducie Island?  It is in the Pitcairn Island Chain.  Remember Mutiny on the Bounty?  I talked to VR6TC.  The "TC" is for Tom Christian, one of the descendants of the original "Bounty" crewmembers.  Cool, huh???  I also talked to KC4AAA - McMurdo Station.  Know where McMurdo is?  It is cold, and getting colder!  MIR, Space Shuttle, ISS.... yep - hams on all of them!
 
I have a "half-way" project running.  The first half of the project is in the electronics shop, but it will move to the garage soon.  It is a 6-band HF/VHF/UHF transceiver for my jeep.  To get it running I will need to install 7 antennas in the jeep.  But I have a bit of roof work to do to the jeep before I can install the antennas.  The last few parts for this radio were ordered yesterday.  It should be complete and ready for testing by next weekend.  I am also working toward getting the jeep ready for the radio and antennas.
