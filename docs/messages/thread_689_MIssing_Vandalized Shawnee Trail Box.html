MIssing/Vandalized Shawnee Trail Box

From: celticlions (celticlions@comcast.net) | Date: 2005-03-14 08:15:51 UTC-06:00

Well, a few days ago Kantexan was musing over the Samuraistampers' boxes
which keep going missing and/or being vandalized. I was feeling pretty lucky
that our boxes have had water damage, but no human damage. Today I received
a note from an out-of-town boxer who reported our Shawnee Trail box
missing/vandalized.
I'm really sad - that was my favorite box for a lot of reasons - the
location with the gorgeous bronze trail ride, the stamp that CactusLicker
carved and the cool "rock" Stillwater created to hide it in.
Stillwater just checked on it (he was on his way to work nearby) and called
a minute ago to tell me that the whole deep ornamental grass area had
apparently been used by homeless people to sleep...or at least that's his
best guess as to why all the landscaping has been matted down.
So...one more hazard to think about - don't choose an urban spot that could
be someone's bedroom!

Stillwater is bringing his "rock" home - maybe after Brazos we'll replace
the Shawnee Trail - I still think it's one of the coolest places in Downtown
Dallas and I want to draw folks to come there...just not for overnights!

RT

From: (blhjrh@aol.com) | Date: 2005-03-14 16:56:19 UTC-05:00
We just came from trying to refind Kantexan's Mondrian Series in FW this afternoon (those were my first TX letterboxes to find, and still my favorite series because they make such a great finished picture, so I wanted to restamp it on a card and send it to my sister, PLUS I had a cool hitchhiker to leave in one of these boxes!), but bad luck --  Mondrian #1 appears to have met the same fate as CelticLions Shawnee Trail, judging from the way the area has been trampled since the first time I located this box in November.  I hadn't rechecked the clues before we left, so I looked for all 5 boxes to be sure while we were there, and found #2 and #4 still in place, but when I got home and checked the clues, I discovered that both #3 and #5 have both been reported missing.  That's such a cool stamp, I hope it can be replaced and rehidden so that casual picnickers in the park or homeless people or landscape workers or Beignet-eating Aliens don't beam them up to whereever they take them!   We were able to refind another cool Kantexan LB, the Slumbering Family Tree in the Botanic Gardens nearby, still in good shape.
