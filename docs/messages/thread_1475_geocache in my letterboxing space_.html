From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2005-11-14 22:49:33 UTC
If I were you, I'd move it--at least 200 feet away. Or, contact the
geocache's owner and ask what his/her preference would be--maybe he'd
want them in the same spot, maybe not--but the gc was there first. As
more geocachers learn about letterboxing, I think the two hobbies are
co-existing better. BUT, there have been numerous episodes of gcers
finding lbs and thinking the stamp was an exchange item.

When we were at Brazos Bend on Sunday, I saw a social trail and
followed it. Sure enough, it led directly to a geocache. Batrick was
happy to find a baseball card in there to trade for. But I don't want
social trails leading to my letterboxes. . .they're much more likely
to go missing that way!

Geocaches typically get a lot more traffic than letterboxes. We did
share close proximity to a short-lived GC. In the 3 weeks that GC was
alive, our letterbox got more traffic than it had in the previous 6
months! A few gc'ers really liked the idea and (at least for a while)
went on a lbing binge. We found some weird items in our box, but
everyone was very respectful.

DB

NO! NO! NO! NO! NO! .....  and NO!!!!!
 
It the Geocache exactly in the same place?  If they are even a few feet apart, leave it there.  If you can give me the name and location of the cache or cache owner, I will contact them with this same suggestion.
 
Place the card for letterboxing in your box, and be sure the letterboxing web site and the name of your box are on the card.  That way, any Geocachers who find your box by accident will be introduced to yet another game.  And with a contact perhaps we can get the Geocache owner to do the same.  What an opportunity!
 
BTW - this is why I am combining letterboxing stuff in my Geocaches.  You get two for the price of one.
 
I made up cards that can be laminated in Plastic and placed in letterboxes and geocaches.  It is 1/2 a paper sheet large, and has one side for Letterboxing and one side for Geocaching.  I am willing to share.
 
Of course, someone may have a reason to shoot this down.  So I will watch for replies.
