My name is Steven Ridlehuber and I'm a Park Ranger with the US Army
Corps of Engineers at Bardwell Lake. We discovered your box located
next to the well in Waxahachie Creek Park. When we arrived at the
location the well was uncovered. Please remove the box from that area
and from around the well. Please do not place it around man made
structures, buildings, or bathrooms, etc. A good location for the box
might be on the hiking trail located further down the road in the park.


Thanks: Park Ranger Steven Ridlehuber

From: Maribeth Jagger (cjagger405@earthlink.net) | Date: 2006-01-27 13:28:57 UTC-06:00
Silver Eagle - this is very strange. Viewfinder and I were at Lake Bardwell Tuesday afternoon and indeed did find your letterbox. We rehid it in the exact location where you had it planted which as you know is no where near a well. I'm not even sure where a well is located there, but I assume it is in the campsite area. I know nothing about a well being "uncovered", as we didn't uncover any wells, but it does sound as though there must have been some human intervention involved. Strange... as we drove around, we noticed that there were only two occupied campsites in the entire park the day we were there, and they were no where near the area where the letterboxes are. There was quite a bit of wildlife there, we saw two large rabbits and an armadillo crossed the road in front of us. It was the end of the day and we didn't see other people on the trails, and it seems most unlikely that anyone could have seen us replacing the box. It also seems unlikely that anyone would have stumbled accidently upon your box. However, we did hear a pack of coyotes howling very close by. They were yippin' quite loudly and since the sun was going down it did make me a bit nervous. My guess is that a coyote picked up our scent and carried the box to the area it was found by the ranger. I have no idea why the top was off a well. Ask the ranger to hold onto the box and eventually one of us will be there to rehide it!
