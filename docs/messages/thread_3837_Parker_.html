From: Mike Olbrisch (mike-2007@elp.rr.com) | Date: 2008-11-04 13:49:21 UTC-07:00
Von Bonehenge's Quanah Parker "Da Parker" - 1 June 2000 - 4 Nov 2008.  CD, RE, RA, RN, CGC, and Spoiled Rotten Companion. 
 
It is with great sadness that I send this.  Last Sunday afternoon Parker asked me to let him go.  He came to me, and he licked my face, and he held out his cancerous leg.  The look on his face said it all - and it just broke my heart.  He was asking me to do something.  He was asking me to make the pain go away!  There was only one way I could make the pain go away.  He was asking for it.  He said it was time.  He said he had given me every moment he could.  He had nothing left to give.  So I did it.
 
At 0850 on Monday 4 Nov 2008 Parker crossed the Rainbow Bridge.
 
In April Parker was diagnosed with bone cancer.  Dr. Julie said he would live two to four weeks more.  Moni - God love her - got on the internet, found and started a treatment program.  She had a goal, but one step at a time.  First step was his birthday, 1 June.  He made it.  Next step was the trip to Oklahoma in July.  He made it.  Then my birthday - 30 Aug.  He made it.  Then my son's visit - early Oct.  He made it.  Next step on the list was to be Thanksgiving.  We had a huge Turkey dinner planned.  Parker would have surely had his share and more.  He couldn't make that.  And I couldn't ask him to hold on any longer.  He was tired.
 
Parker slept peacefully Monday night.  His pain medication helped.  He was on our bed.  I slept fitfully beside him.  Sometimes after a dream he would wake and lick my face.  This morning he followed me everywhere, he wouldn't leave my side.  He had a great breakfast, not sprinkled with medications for once.  He gobbled it up.
 
Dr. Julie, wonderful person that she is, endured my over-emotional demeanor and gave Parker the rest he needed.  I sat with him on his favorite blanket, hugged him and kissed him and assured him that I have never had a more wonderful dog.  And that I loved him.  And that I would miss him terribly.  That it was OK for him to go.  As he slid down I went down with him.  And when his breathing and heart stopped I think mine did too.  And then I cried.  We all cried.
 
Moni was looking for a photo of Parker.  Just Parker.  She doesn't have one.  Every photo is of Parker with me.  He loved me 100%.  It was mutual.
 
Parker was my companion.  He was with me everywhere - even to places other friends couldn't follow.  Especially those places - the high lonesome mountain trails.  Right up to his last seconds we were together. 
 
Parker earned 5 show titles.  We hiked hundreds of miles together.  We traveled thousands of miles to dog shows together.  We never just sat in the motel room after the show.  Every day - there was always a new adventure calling us.  Driving to and from the shows, there was always a back-road to explore, and we always stopped to smell the fire hydrants.  Parker volunteered at the Child Crisis Center.  He worked the Alzheimer's wing at Desert Springs.  He visited the old folks at many retirement centers.  He supported the El Paso Humane Society, marched in parades, and attended events in the Civic Center.  As a "neutral dog" he helped train dozens of therapy dogs.  He was the Franklin Mountain State Park Volunteer Mascot.  He loved camping and hiking.  And he would walk up to someone just to say "Hi".    He was always ready to go say "Hi".  He loved meeting people and socializing.  Those of you who met him, you know what a friendly guy he was.  He had a wonderful life.  I was lucky to have him.
 
Although his awards are listed from highest to lowest, I think the last one was the most important.  Spoiled Rotten Companion.  The rest of it was earned because he loved it, and because I loved him.  Our enthusiasm fed off each other.  He did it because I loved him, and I did it because he loved me.  I can't think of anything left undone.  I don't think I can stand to be without him.  But I couldn't ask him to suffer just for me any longer.  It was time.  Time for his pain to go away.  Time for my pain to start.
 
I have so many memories.  But right now I can't think of a one.  It just hurts so much.  I am sure with time the sadness will lessen, and I will remember the great times we had together.  The hikes.  The dog shows.  The adventures.  The love. 
 
I had a nick-name for Parker.  "The Finest Dog".  In our family - he certainly was.  No dog in our house has ever in the 20+ years Moni has had dogs attained the number of awards and titles Parker earned.
 
I guess God needed a good dog.  So he picked one.  The Finest Dog.
 
 
 
 
If it should be that I grow weak,
And pain should keep me from my sleep.
 
Then you must do what must be done,
For this last battle can't be won.
 
You will be sad, I understand,
Don't let your grief then stay your hand.
 
For this day more than all the rest,
Your love for me must stand the test.
 
We've had so many happy years,
What is to come can hold no fears.
 
You'd not want me to suffer so,
The time has come, please let me go.
 
Take me where my need they'll tend,
And please stay with me - till the end.
 
 
There is a bright side to all this.  If this is all I have to worry me, life is truly good.  God has blessed me with one of his finest creatures for 8 years.  We are supposed to care for our animals.  Sometimes I think it is the animals who care for us.  There is a new pup.  His name is River.  He will never replace Parker in my heart.  But I think - in time - I can give him the chance to make his own place.  As much as it hurts to lose them, the saddest times of my life were those times when I didn't have a dog.  I will get through this.  It will just take a few days.
 
Please don't call me for a few days.  I couldn't choke out any reply anyway.  I will try to answer E-mails. 
 
There is nothing left to say.....  except - I miss you Parker.  I miss you terribly. 
 
I feel so empty right now.  Something wrong with my monitor too - it's all blurry.
 
Mike.

Von Bonehenge's Quanah Parker "Da Parker" - 1 June 2000 - 4 Nov 2008.  CD, RE, RA, RN, CGC, and Spoiled Rotten Companion. 

 

It is with great sadness that I send this.  Last Sunday afternoon Parker asked me to let him go.  He came to me, and he licked my face, and he held out his cancerous leg.  The look on his face said it all - and it just broke my heart.  He was asking me to do something.  He was asking me to make the pain go away!  There was only one way I could make the pain go away.  He was asking for it.  He said it was time.  He said he had given me every moment he could.  He had nothing left to give.  So I did it.

 

At 0850 on Monday 4 Nov 2008 Parker crossed the Rainbow Bridge.

 

In April Parker was diagnosed with bone cancer.  Dr. Julie said he would live two to four weeks more.  Moni - God love her - got on the internet, found and started a treatment program.  She had a goal, but one step at a time.  First step was his birthday, 1 June.  He made it.  Next step was the trip to Oklahoma in July.  He made it.  Then my birthday - 30 Aug.  He made it.  Then my son's visit - early Oct.  He made it.  Next step on the list was to be Thanksgiving.  We had a huge Turkey dinner planned.  Parker would have surely had his share and more.  He couldn't make that.  And I couldn't ask him to hold on any longer.  He was tired.

 

Parker slept peacefully Monday night.  His pain medication helped.  He was on our bed.  I slept fitfully beside him.  Sometimes after a dream he would wake and lick my face.  This morning he followed me everywhere, he wouldn't leave my side.  He had a great breakfast, not sprinkled with medications for once.  He gobbled it up.

 

Dr. Julie, wonderful person that she is, endured my over-emotional demeanor and gave Parker the rest he needed.  I sat with him on his favorite blanket, hugged him and kissed him and assured him that I have never had a more wonderful dog.  And that I loved him.  And that I would miss him terribly.  That it was OK for him to go.  As he slid down I went down with him.  And when his breathing and heart stopped I think mine did too.  And then I cried.  We all cried.

 

Moni was looking for a photo of Parker.  Just Parker.  She doesn't have one.  Every photo is of Parker with me.  He loved me 100%.  It was mutual.

 

Parker was my companion.  He was with me everywhere - even to places other friends couldn't follow.  Especially those places - the high lonesome mountain trails.  Right up to his last seconds we were together. 

 

Parker earned 5 show titles.  We hiked hundreds of miles together.  We traveled thousands of miles to dog shows together.  We never just sat in the motel room after the show.  Every day - there was always a new adventure calling us.  Driving to and from the shows, there was always a back-road to explore, and we always stopped to smell the fire hydrants.  Parker volunteered at the Child Crisis Center.  He worked the Alzheimer's wing at Desert Springs.  He visited the old folks at many retirement centers.  He supported the El Paso Humane Society, marched in parades, and attended events in the Civic Center.  As a "neutral dog" he helped train dozens of therapy dogs.  He was the Franklin Mountain State Park Volunteer Mascot.  He loved camping and hiking.  And he would walk up to someone just to say "Hi".    He was always ready to go say "Hi".  He loved meeting people and socializing.  Those of you who met him, you know what a friendly guy he was.  He had a wonderful life.  I was lucky to have him.

 

Although his awards are listed from highest to lowest, I think the last one was the most important.  Spoiled Rotten Companion.  The rest of it was earned because he loved it, and because I loved him.  Our enthusiasm fed off each other.  He did it because I loved him, and I did it because he loved me.  I can't think of anything left undone.  I don't think I can stand to be without him.  But I couldn't ask him to suffer just for me any longer.  It was time.  Time for his pain to go away.  Time for my pain to start.

 

I have so many memories.  But right now I can't think of a one.  It just hurts so much.  I am sure with time the sadness will lessen, and I will remember the great times we had together.  The hikes.  The dog shows.  The adventures.  The love. 

 

I had a nick-name for Parker.  "The Finest Dog".  In our family - he certainly was.  No dog in our house has ever in the 20+ years Moni has had dogs attained the number of awards and titles Parker earned.

 

I guess God needed a good dog.  So he picked one.  The Finest Dog.

 

 

 

 

If it should be that I grow weak,

And pain should keep me from my sleep.

 

Then you must do what must be done,

For this last battle can't be won.

 

You will be sad, I understand,

Don't let your grief then stay your hand.

 

For this day more than all the rest,

Your love for me must stand the test.

 

We've had so many happy years,

What is to come can hold no fears.

 

You'd not want me to suffer so,

The time has come, please let me go.

 

Take me where my need they'll tend,

And please stay with me - till the end.

 

 

There is a bright side to all this.  If this is all I have to worry me, life is truly good.  God has blessed me with one of his finest creatures for 8 years.  We are supposed to care for our animals.  Sometimes I think it is the animals who care for us.  There is a new pup.  His name is River.  He will never replace Parker in my heart.  But I think - in time - I can give him the chance to make his own place.  As much as it hurts to lose them, the saddest times of my life were those times when I didn't have a dog.  I will get through this.  It will just take a few days.

 

Please don't call me for a few days.  I couldn't choke out any reply anyway.  I will try to answer E-mails. 

 

There is nothing left to say.....  except - I miss you Parker.  I miss you terribly. 

 

I feel so empty right now.  Something wrong with my monitor too - it's all blurry.

 

Mike.

the sun in its journey through the heavens.
