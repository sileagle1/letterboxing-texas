Boxin' in the RAIN!

From: (blhjrh@aol.com) | Date: 2006-09-04 16:14:20 UTC-04:00
Yes!   Rain! 
    It is raining right here right now in Northeast Tarrant County, and it couldn't be nicer out there!  No high winds, no lightning or heavy storms, just steady, light, cool, wet RAIN!  I was so happy to see that rain this morning that the thought of getting out in it and taking a little walk, and finding some new letterboxes along the way, was almost too much! 
    So we did - PuddleSplasher (what could be a better trailname for today?) and I drove a few miles up the road to Denton and thoroughly enjoyed discovering 3 boxes planted by Beneficus Rex.  Let me tell you, those stamps are really beautiful, and 2 of the 3 were in pretty parks along nice trails that had us confused for a minute, thinking we had to be somewhere other than Texas (love it as we do). 
    Found:  Sidewinder Ourboros (and that is a really neat, cool stamp!), My 1st Box (another gorgeous stamp), and Belle MBF (yes, another really good stamp).  Thanks, Beneficus Rex, for leading us to new parks and places we've driven past many times but never knew existed. 
    Also, a nice little serendipity:  we encountered a nice family of 4 out on their very first letterboxing outing, finding their first letterbox at the second one we went to this morning, so we introduced ourselves and they were able to do a stamp exchange to add to their first letterboxing experience (cute little girls in raincoats and umbrellas, covered in ink.  What a fun experience and they added to ours, too). 
    Along the way I was able to replace a full logbook at Whistle Stop Spur in Keller, and check on Rooster (still safely in the cemetery near Henrietta Creek Orchard).  A very nice letterboxing day, in the rain. 
    Viewfinder
