From: (sileagle1@yahoo.com) | Date: 2014-10-18 18:06:59 UTC-07:00

Recently I happened to read an article about some famous trees in Texas, and it referenced a book published in 1970 by the Texas Forest Service called "Famous Trees Of Texas".  When I showed the article to my wife, she said her father had bought that book many years ago and we searched until we finally found it.  It was a great read and I decided I wanted to go see as many of these trees as I could.  Of course, over 40 years has passed so some of the trees are bound to be gone now, but many are still around.  Over the next few months I plan to visit as many as I can and leave a trail of breadcrumbs in the form of letterboxes for y'all to follow.  I already placed one today in Columbus, and even though it is dead, the trunk is still impressive and history just oozes off it.  I hope you will follow me on my quest!

http://www.letterboxing.org/BoxView.php?boxnum=67790&boxname=Columbus%20Court%20Oak

Silver Eagle

P.S.  I heard the Texas Forest Service is planning on releasing an updated version of the book in 2015, so look for it.
