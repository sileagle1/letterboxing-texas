Siterep - Aug 3rd Madisonville to Waxahachie

From: tigger32167 (tigger32167@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-08-07 02:22:36 UTC
My navigator, JR, and I started towards Dallas on I45 at around 6
am. Our first stop was Lake Madison in Madisonville and found the
Madison Quilt Block from the Quilt Block Series, Mushroom Capitol
Microbox, and the Fiesta de Hongos box, which was 130 feet across a
grassy field where the grass was definitely above JR's head and in
places was up to my waist (I am only 5' tall). Now this wouldnt have
been too bad except it was 8 am and the dew was heavy, so both JR
and I were soaked by the time we finished, but the stamp was worth
it, as were the other two boxes in this park...great carvings. We
then proceeded to Corsicana and picked up the Wolf Brand box at a
really neat pocket park downtown. Inside of this box was the
Microsoft Office:Access HH which we picked up. We then went to
Pioneer Village and picked up the Pioneer Box. This is a really
neat place to visit. JR was not allowed to go into any of the
buildings, but one of the ladies in the office was nice enough to
watch him while I looked around. This Pioneer Village also has a
Lefty Frizzell museum. I didnt realize that he was born here. The
lady that watched JR for me also told me that Clyde Barrow's uncle
lived in the area and Bonnie and Clyde actually came through here
and Clyde went to the local florist and got Bonnie some flowers and
left a note that said something to the effect of "Do you remember
me?". Thought that was interesting. We then went to the
Petroleum Park and found the Petroleum Park box. The log book was
soaked, but someone had left another little log book in it. We then
went to Oakwood Cemetery and picked up The People's Path box
which is part of Boots Tex's Texas Governor's Series. Wonderful
Carving!!! This was a neat cemetery. Our last stop in Corsicana
was Community Park and picked up the Pitch In! box. We then
traveled to Kerens to the Kerens Cemetery and found the 132nd FA
Regiment Letterbox. The placement of this box was really neat and a
cool history to go with it. As we traveled back to I45 we noticed
the Bluebonnet Trading Post which had several large statues of
buffalo, elk, deer, and horses in front of it. JR saw these as we
were driving by and started barking at the statues. So if you go
out that way be sure and look for these. We then made it to the
Waxahachie Creek Park to pick up 3 boxes, but when I got to the main
gate to get a map, the lady informed me that all of the hiking
trails were closed due to the rains. So we continued on to
Italy and found the Texas Passport Series #3 - Italy box. Our next
stop was Waxahachie to the Superconducting Super Collider to pick up
the SSC - A Hole in the Ground in Texas. It was definitely an
eerie feeling out there. I am a science teacher, so I was extremely
interested in this location, and it was a really neat spot. Next
we went to Getzander Park in Waxahachie to pick up a couple of
boxes. Upon our arrival we started down the trails and realized
that the clues referred to logs that were fallen over the
trail...well there were numerous trees across the trail and after
checking 3-4 we came to a section that was totally blocked and we
couldnt find a way to get through. So we turned around and decided
to wait until another time. We then headed to Dallas to meet up
with Wag Time to go letterboxing on Saturday.
JB Kokopelli
