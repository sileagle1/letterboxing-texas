Hey all, we are holed up on the west side of Fort Worth. No offense
to anyone, but this is not the most scenic side of town. Also,
fighter jets fly over head a lot. However, we are more than delighted
to be here. If it wasn't 800 million degrees outside, we would have
gone to the zoo or some other attraction today--there are lots of fun
things for kids in the Ft. Worth area. I stocked up on supplies this
morning at a Target near here, which is funny, because I left a
fully-stocked house behind. Oh well. It was throwing out all the
stuff in my freezer that was the hardest.

We left Houston at 7:30 last night and got here at 4:30 this morning.
This is better for me, because my littlest one has asthma and I don't
want him in 90+ degree heat with no a/c. So, from DFW I can make it
to my mom's house in Missouri if need be.

I keep telling my kids it's an adventure and it certainly is!

Stay safe all. check in when you can.

Dewberry

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-09-22 17:29:15 UTC-07:00
We love the Dallas Aquarium.

Also, we almost always go to the Science Place to see
what's going on there. If nothing is happening that
we are interested in or haven't already seen, we spend
an hour or two roaming the park behind the Science
Place. Love the little lake...

The Science Place is above average as compared to
other places like it - we hit 'em all wherever we go,
but we make it a point to go back to the one in
Dallas. There is an IMAX theatre there to check out
also.

The Dallas Art Museum is worth a short walk through,
depending on the ages of your kids (it's worth a long
walk-through if you are kid-free). My 9 year old son
tolerates it if there are some cool Indian artifacts
or something on display that he can relate to.

There's a cool little restaurant - Kathleen's Art Cafe
- in the University Park area. Quirky menu. And a
neat pizza place in the same general area called
Slider and Blues - it's loud, but the pizza is good
and they have a game room.

And take them to the restaurant in the ball - Reunion
Tower. Go during off-peak times (we usually go about
3 p.m.), order an appetizer and drinks, and you can
hang out there for quite awhile and watch the world go
round - literally. My kids LOVE this! They have an
observation deck also if you just don't want to order
food or anything, but it isn't nearly as
comfortable...

My family is a little quirky, but we have this thing
about riding public transportation in whatever city we
are in - even when we lived in San Diego, we rode mass
transit for entertainment. Dallas has a great transit
system and it gives you a nice birdseye view of the
city. There is a run that takes you through downtown
and there are some pretty interesting looking places I
still want to visit along that run. The bonus is the
interesting people we see along the way - I think it's
a good place for kids to see another side of life
sometimes. We are big on people-watching and we have
a good time figuring people out and (ahem)
occasionally making a bit of fun of them between
ourselves (I know y'all won't believe that, but it's
true). Of course, we make fun of ourselves too, so
it's okay that we make fun of other people, as long as
they don't know we do it.

On the subject of public transit, we plan to have a
race across Dallas on our next trip up there. We're
going to split into two pairs and see which pair can
get from Point A to Point B 1) quickest, 2) using the
most forms of public transportation, and 3) cheapest,
and whichever wins two of the three categories wins.

So that's what happens when the hillbillies hit Dallas
- maybe the locals will have some new and different
ideas.

Lucy
