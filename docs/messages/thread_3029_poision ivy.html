From: Vicki Stecher (stecher@runbox.com) | Date: 2007-08-21 12:17:26 UTC-05:00
You have waited too long for a lot of products. There is a very expensive product called Zanfel (like $35 for a tiny tube) that really does help it stop spreading, but at the blisterering wet rashy stage, most products don't do a thing. One thing that can cause relief, albeit temporary, is heat. When you heat poison ivy it releases the histimines which is what makes you itch. So if you are having trouble sleeping because you are going insane, you can run as hot a water as you can stand on your poison ivy. It will feel good in a very evil sort of way, just like scratching. It will also make you want to scratch. But when you stop the heat, you will have a window where you don't itch, and that window is a perfect time to fall asleep! A hair dryer works really well too. But I agree with the last post, it is about two weeks until you are better and you will itch even when it is scabby.
 
One extreme thing I have done that really, really does help although it sounds awful...in some of your extremities (hands, feet, legs) you can scratch the worst parts until you break the skin. (Sounds awful but feels wonderful) then pour vinegar on the area. It burns and cauterizes the area and that part quits itching. If it still itches, you didn't scratch it hard enough. This doesn't work on sensitive areas though and is too painful for some places. It isn't painful at all really for legs, hands and feet, though. I've done this, my husband has done this and it works.
 
There is a wonderful product called Tecnu that you can put on after you've been exposed to poison ivy and it really does keep you from getting it. So each night after letterboxing, you can tecnu up and not have this problem again!
 
Good luck and I'm so sorry,
Vicki

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-08-21 13:04:13 UTC-05:00
We have also had some success with taking Benadryl to soothe the itching.  But as has been mentioned, once the blisters are open, it's too late to do anything but ride the wave.
 
One thing you really want to be cautious of is letting the open blisters get infected.  I'm not sure about the antibiotic characteristics of any of what has been mentioned, but you may want to throw some TAO on top of the other stuff for soothing - it may be just enough to stave off infection.
 
Zanfel is far better than Technu, but as mentioned, it is VERY expensive!  I think it works a little later in the game than Technu, but because of the cost I wouldn't use it myself - I'd rather just ride out a mild case, which is what I usually get.  My daughter, on the other hand, would use Zanfel in a heartbeat if she didn't catch it in time with Technu.
