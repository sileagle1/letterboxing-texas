Ahhhh...very good question and I'll bet there are other newbies wondering the same thing!
 
A traveller is a special stamp that usually involves answering a question or performing some sort of feat to get.  While I don't personally have a logbook for my travelers, some folks probably do have logbooks to go with theirs.  Here's a link to mine so you can get an idea what I am talking about:
 
http://www.letterboxing.org/HHView.php?hhnum=829&hhname=Lucy's_Personal_Traveler
 
Although you would usually get these at gatherings, you might also run into a boxer on the trail and you would want to be prepared in case you did, and you would want to know 1) if they have a personal traveler, and 2) the "something" necessary to get the traveler.  I have a list of boxers across the country whose PT's I would like to get, in case I ever happen to "bump into" them.
 
You can find out who has personal traverls (PTs) by going here:
 
http://www.letterboxing.org/HHFind.php
 
and choosing personal as the type of stamp and perusing the list for recognized names, or you can go here:
 
http://www.atlasquest.com/lboxes/search.html?gTypeId=11
 
and doing the same.
 
For your reference though, not everyone lists their PT anywhere, so you might have to find a way to diplomatically ask if someone has one.  There's a little finesse to getting PT's, so try to find a way to slip it into conversation - it's a lot more fun that way.  A traveler, by the way, is a great way to open up a conversation with a complete stranger at a gathering.
 
  Also, sometimes HH's are called travelers.
