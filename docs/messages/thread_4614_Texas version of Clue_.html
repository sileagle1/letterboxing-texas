From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2010-08-21 06:48:17 UTC-05:00

Hmmm...I've been trying to remember the details of how it worked. It's just not coming back to me. I'll throw out what I remember and maybe others that were there can add some details.

Stamps of every character, weapon, and location. All hidden, except one of each? I remember two of them went missing that very day and we blamed the Aliens, resulting in the lynching of the Alien Emissary. Not that that had anything to do with the game, except that as I think about it, Ruby did seem to be the one that kept getting hit by aliens.

Everyone got a booklet to stamp in to so they could keep track of who they had found/NOT found. It was quite a deal figuring out how to fold the booklet. A template of it may be available in the archives here. http://groups.yahoo.com/group/letterboxingtexas

Celtic Lions maintained a table at the pavilion for everyone to check in with periodically - but why? Can't remember. To confirm if we were right in our guess, but it seems like there was another reason.

I seem to remember that there was a challenge we could do to get an extra clue.

From: sileagle1 (sileagle@windstream.net) | Date: 2010-08-21 16:52:26 UTC
It was at our 2nd TALE in March, 2005 that Celtic Lions introduced the TX Wild West Clue game. We all carved stamps for it and Ruby kept it, so I guess the family still has it. I don't remember all the details, but I think they passed out envelopes with clues to the boxes to all the teams playing. They kept the envelopes containing the murder weapon, place and murderer that we were trying to solve. Each envelope contained a few clues, so you had to trade with other teams to get more clues. Once you had enough to make a guess, you went to Ruby with your answer. But even if you got it right you still went looking for the rest of the boxes since the stamps were so good you wanted them all.

If you want to read some of the comments from that event, you can look at old messages from this List, starting around #1830. Sure brings back fond memories.

Silver Eagle
