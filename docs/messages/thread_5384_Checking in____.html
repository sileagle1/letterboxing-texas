From: Mike Olbrisch (mike-2007@elp.rr.com) | Date: 2014-07-19 20:26:34 UTC-06:00

Sorry.  Yeah - I used to participate a lot more.  My interests have just drifted away from boxing and cashing I guess.

 

My old job was in at 0700, out at 1500.  I did get called in a lot, but I had a lot of free time then too.  So I was around a lot more, and did a lot more too.  I was happy there, until I got a boss who ordered me to ignore federal regulations.  I had to move on.

 

The new job is much more difficult.  Not nearly as difficult or dangerous as my military career was, but lots of 12-hour shifts.  The shift rotation is 45 days.  12-hour days are 0545 and 1745 shift briefings.  8-hour days are 0800 to 1600.

 

I start with a Mon-Fri 8-hour days as the relief op.  I am there if the primary operator gets sick, or we get slammed with a grid emergency.  When all goes well, the relief operator can do training on the computerized control system.  It looks like our real grid, it is just a trainer though.

 

I am OFF Sat-Sun-Mon.

 

Tue-Wed-Thu-Fri is 12-hour days, I am the primary shift operator.  I do have a relief op available, but I consider it bad form to miss a primary shift.

 

Off Sat-Sun.

 

Mon - 12-hour day.  Tue-Wed-Thu are 8-hour days as the second relief op.  Usually training.

 

Off Fri-Sat-Sun.  Mon-Tue-Wed is 12-hour nights.  No relief op available.  (don't get sick).

 

Thu-Fri off.  Sat-Sun 12-hour days.  Mon-Tue-Wed off.

 

Thu-Fri-Sat-Sun 12-hour nights.

 

Monday morning at 0545 begins 7 days off.  the following Mon at 0800 I begin the 5 x 8 hour relief days again.

 

Got a head ache yet?  I gotta keep a schedule on the refrigerator or I forget.  I have a wonderful boss who doesn't ask much more than to do your job, and gives better than he gets.  If I cover a 12-hour shift, he pays me for 16.  If I take a 12-hour day off, he charges me for 8.  He has my loyalty!  <grin>.

 

Somehow, even with all that off time, I just never seem to get anything done.  Right now we are in a huge upheaval at home.  We emptied the house into the garage and we lived in the bunk house.  We stripped the ceilings and floors and re-finished the walls in the main house, with porcelain tile on the floors.  We have not quite recovered from that and I am laying in nearly a whole new electrical system in the house (I did the garage and bunk house about 5 years ago).  It took me two days to dig a trench 1.5 ft wide x 3 ft deep x 90 ft long for the electrical work.

 

We still do dog shows all over the country.  Moni (Dane Lady) took her dog Daisy all the way to a US championship, then to an International Championship.  Daisy is now retired, and the new pup Maggie (Born on Christmas Morning, full name is Von Bonehenge Christmas Magic)  is coming along well.

 

Many of you will remember Parker, my black Dane hiking companion of old who died of cancer.  Well, his replacement dog River (Von Bonehenge White River of Dreams) has earned 5 obedience titles, equaling Parker.  And for the first time in my life I have two dogs at the same time.  I have a 2-y/o named Gray-Cee.  She is mostly gray.

 

DOGS - www.vonbonehenge.com/

 

My ham radio hobby has really come together after the VA helped me find a way to overcome my hearing loss and start enjoying Morse Code again.  As for shooting, I am still an NRA Expert in high-power rifle, and still a C-Class in USPSA combat pistol, and I still reload my own ammo.

 

www.qrz-com/db/kd5kc/

 

My son the Cavalry Scout-Sniper-EMT hung up his spurs after 3 combat tours and 6 decorations for valor.  It was the EMT that made him quit.  He said he couldn't hold up to losing another crew member.  I can understand that, a city EMT rarely knows the victims.  He lived with them.  He is well now, with a fine job, a really good wife, and t little girls.  He has his nightmares, but don't all soldiers?

 

My daughter is still my daughter..... that's about all I can say there.  <grin>.

 

Well - enough boredom..... I am still around.  I have really considered unsubscribing but you all are such nice people and I would really miss you.  Perhaps a dog show will match up with a TALE or something one day and we'll meet.

 

Mike - El Paso.  AKA Desert_Warrior.
