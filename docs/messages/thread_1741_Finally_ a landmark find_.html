Finally, a landmark find!

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-03-12 17:59:12 UTC-08:00
I'm almost embarassed to say I've been letterboxing
for 2.5 years and I've only today hit 100 finds!

SJZ is visiting, but she was hanging out with friends
yesterday, and Fearless Fox and Lightning had gone to
Arkansas to hike, so I was alone for the weekend. I
was thoroughly tired by yesterday evening, so I had
settled down to looking through my logbook and
cleaning things up in it, when I suddenly realized I
was only 4 boxes shy of 100! I knew I could probably
get those four at the gathering next week, but I
wanted to go to the gathering WITH 100...

So I figured out where I would have to go to get those
four, and realized the closest places with
concentrations of boxes I hadn't yet found were either
Waco/Temple/Killeen or Livingston/Diboll/Lufkin -
either direction about 2 hours for me. I opted to go
south, so with clues for six boxes in hand, I figured
I could hit my mark.

I went all the way to Livingston to start so I could
work my way home, and when I stopped at my first box,
all I could find was the cap - yikes! I was down to
five possibilities right away, so I quickly called SJZ
and asked her to look up the Nacogdoches boxes I
haven't yet gotten (because y'all know Lucy Locket
likes to plant new boxes as soon as I work Nacogdoches
over, and she now has cohorts in her efforts at
that!). SJZ obliged, so I felt a little better being
equipped with a couple more backups that I had
handwritten clues for.

I made my way to the state park at Lake Livingston and
quickly found Quetzel's State Mammal, but there were
muggles all around so I couldn't go in for it right
away. How frustrating! I finally decided I could
make like I was examining the ants near the box (the
ones I had earlier set my hand in while trying to
determine if I had the right place) and could possibly
retrieve the box inconspicuously. It worked out just
fine, which was great except that recovering well is
always more difficult than extricating when there are
prying eyes around, at least for me! I finally caught
a break in traffic and got the box rehidden, so I made
my way to the next starting point.

Now Lake Livingston State Park has a couple of pretty
unique features you don't see in just any old state
park - a pool and a boardwalk trail. The next box was
down the boardwalk. I had fun walking that, except
that as I walked my steps reverberated behind me and I
kept thinking folks were coming up behind me and I
would move aside to let them by. I finally wound up
walking on the edge so I could quit looking over my
shoulder. I'm sure I looked kind of silly! The box
was placed by Frye Team's boys, and it was a nice
hide. Fun box - and what's better, I happened upon
Chillin' at Tonkawa trying to make its way home. How
do you count that though? Hmmm...

I wanted to try autumn.wind's Treasure, but I thought
I had lost my clues to it until I realized much too
late that I had put them out of order. I guess it's
just destined that I will leave a town with one more
box for me to find. And that meant I had to find the
next two or I would have to detour to Nac before I
could go home.

I got back to Diboll and quickly got Frye Team's
Crawlin Down 59. I had tried for it on my way down
when I stopped to stretch for a few, but there were
construction workers close by and I was afraid they
would notice me lurking around. Luckily they were
packing up and leaving when I arrived back, so I just
lurked suspiciously about until they had vacated the
job site.

I was up to 99 - and I had one more box to check out -
Lucy Locket's Land of the Little Angel. It was the
only one in Lufkin I hadn't yet gotten. Another nice
trail and a very good hide. I thought it was a great
box to call #100! I thought it was cool it was one of
Lucy's too, since she has a way of keeping one or two
boxes in her back pocket to pull out just when I think
I've found all of hers (okay, I'm not really so
self-absorbed that I believe she does it deliberately,
but it sure SEEMS that way)!

I was able to make my way home without detouring to
Nacogdoches, although that wouldn't have been a
terrible thing either.

The drive was glorious - everything is brilliantly
blindingly busting out green and lots of trees are
blooming.

So - since I decided to do a little counting this
weekend, this is where I stand countwise:

Finds 100
Plants 33
Exchanges 48
Hitchhikers 9
Events 3
Postals 3
Virtuals 3
Games 36
and Traveling Event Stamps 7

And I guess I'm going to have to have an evacuee
category too - for Chillin' At Tonkawa!

Lucy

Lucy, you could have gone 20 miles farther south to Coldspring and
found our four boxes. Not only that, you would have been first
finder on all four. They're getting lonesome!
We were in Rusk and Jacksonville this weekend and I wanted to
apologize for not contacting you. I forgot to take your cell phone
number with me. We had a laptop with remote Internet access, but
couldn't get a signal. We were hoping you would call. We found
your Homestead Hitchhiker Hostel and took 3 and left 3. Also, and I
say this in all modesty, you are standing in the presence of
greatness...we found your mystery box, Spring Hope, and it was a
lovely place. In all, we found 17 boxes and placed 2. I'll get the
2 new ones posted tonight. They're called "The General Sherman"
and "Underground Railroad - Quilt Block Series No. 3" and they're
planted in Rusk State Park. We were planning on doing your Scout
Series this morning, but the park was locked and we didn't know what
time it would be open, so we had to save it for another day. We
also stopped by Nacogdoches on the way home and found Go Jacks! and
there was not another sould around the whole time. Good timing.
Maybe we'll meet another time to swap stamps.

Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter

Oh believe me, I seriously considered going on down to
Cold Springs, but I was concerned that the extra drive
time would cut into my daylight. As it was, I got
home about 45 minutes before dark, so it would've been
cutting it close, but it might've worked.

As it turned out, I was in the deep woods much of the
day yesterday without a phone signal so I couldn't
call out and you couldn't have reached me. I guess it
just wasn't meant to be! SJZ, my daughter, saw y'all
though. I had mentioned that y'all were in the area
yesterday, so when she and I connected yesterday
evening, she asked if there was a box in a particular
place and I confirmed there was (Little Red Chili
Pepper) - and she said "Well I saw some people
stamping in then!" I fussed at her for not stopping
and saying hello.

Just out of curiosity, did y'all find Blue Butterfly's
box in the Railroad Park in Rusk?

Someone had mentioned that Jim Hogg closes at some
certain time, which I didn't realize. I need to find
out their hours so I can include that in the clues.
I'm sorry you couldn't get to those boxes!

Glad y'all had a good, productive weekend up in this
area. Can you believe that just a couple years ago
(hmmm...last year even) there were maybe 5 boxes in
all of Cherokee County, and 3 of those were closest to
Alto?

Lucy
