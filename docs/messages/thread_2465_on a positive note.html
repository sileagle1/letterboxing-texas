on a positive note

From: the carrascos (a.carrasco@earthlink.net) | Date: 2007-01-04 20:44:30 UTC-06:00
One of my favorite things about letterboxing is finding a new place.  Some of the boxes that really stand out in my mind were very simple stamps, but places I never would have known about (or never would have gotten around to going to visit) without letterboxing.  Some of my all time favorites are: Don't Mess With Texas (what a cool bridge!) Gordon Grouper (I've already said that!) Mother of Texas (why else would I have an excuse to haul 4 kids around a cemetery) LLet's LLunch (Austin isn't the only weird place) and the Mother of all strange places....Stonehenge 2.
 
I have also come to have a love/hate (many times more hate than love) relationship with mystery boxes/puzzle clues.  The Tour Bus is great, because it is a surprise every time.  I like a box that makes me say, "What a cool ______!".  But more than anything I love a new place.  (Which is not to say I'll quit finding boxes in places I've already been!)  MO5

Have to agree with you on this one too! I have discovered just local parks that are actually close to my neighborhood that I didn't even know existed without there having been a lb planted there.
