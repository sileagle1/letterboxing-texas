--- In letterboxingtexas@yahoogroups.com, Barefoot Lucy
wrote:
>
> Now if y'all have very good memories, ya'll remember
> that I went through Nacogdoches ONCE already back in
> the summer and got all the boxes that were there. I
> was quite proud to be able to say that I had gone to
> Nacogdoches and conquered the box scene there and
> there was nothing left to be had for my logbook. I
> was especially proud that I had done it in the summer,
> which is NOT my favorite time of the year! Well
> almost immediately, Lucy Locket set out some more
> boxes and I was forced to let them sit for awhile
> before I could get to them. I've now made two trips
> back in the last month or so, the second one being
> today and I thought I had cleaned up all those boxes
> again, but you won't believe what I found when I got
> home - a very nice email from Lucy telling me she had
> planted another one JUST TODAY! Yep. I had emailed
> her asking whether any of the four boxes still
> remaining were suitable to take my
> just-out-of-the-hospital mom to, and in the course of
> her reply she casually mentioned that she planned on
> planting another this weekend, but I just couldn't
> believe it would be so since it was supposed to be
> raining! It was true though, and now, I STILL haven't
> managed to get ahead of her!
>
> So anyway, I went back to Nac today to visit my sick
> mom (who wasn't just-out-of-the-hospital after all -
> they kept her a few more days after some yukky test
> results), and I dragged my kids out of bed to go with
> me. Now bear in mind, I sent my husband off to
> scoutmaster training at 5 a.m. this morning, but I was
> generous and had told the kids to be up and ready to
> leave at 9:30 sharp. No big deal for Lightning, but
> SJZ - well - let's just say she doesn't see the
> daylight version of 9:30 very often. By 6 a.m. I was
> all organized and ready to go, and by 7 I was bribing
> SJZ with a big breakfast if she would get up early and
> go. I finally had her out of bed by 8, and we hit the
> road - still better than 9:30 anyway. The morning was
> overcast and gloomy, but I was ready to find those
> LAST 4 boxes. I was also planning on trying some
> geocaching too, so I really wanted plenty of time
> since I haven't yet been too successful at that!
>
> We entertained ourselves on the hour drive to Nac by
> trying to dismantle a Magic 8 ball to prove to
> ourselves how many sides the little answer cubie thing
> has. Not a good idea in the car. Fortunately, we are
> letterboxers, so we were well equipped with ziplok
> baggies and we were able to salvage the 8 ball project
> before it really did a number on the car, and we
> deferred it until we could address the project with
> adequate tools (all we had to work with was my
> all-in-one tool).
>
> We hit the church where Blue Butterfly's Tres Cruces
> box was and pulled in. Nice secluded spot, so we
> figured we would have no prob with muggles. Well,
> except the one guy bagging up leaves - lots and lots
> of leaves. We roamed the cemetery forever, pretending
> to be really interested in the headstones (which we
> really were, now I think about it). Finally, he moved
> far enough away for us to go in for the kill (oops, do
> you call it a kill if it is in a cemetery???). We
> pulled out the box and found a lovely stamp, nicely
> done, and a hitchhiker -Sunshine #2 (I think - I'll
> doublecheck after I get my bag out of the car). We
> took our time stamping in and enjoying the cool
> morning, and wondering if we really had to huff on our
> stamps to make them print better since it was so
> humid, or if the wind was enough to dry the ink so
> that huffing was even more important than usual.
> Interesting conversation.
>
> On we went to find our first geocache of the day and
> surprised ourselves by being successful! So far, we
> haven't been doing very well with the GC'ing, and I've
> been hitting Mike up for hints. It was fun to finally
> find one using real coordinates instead of guesswork -
> our GPS put us within 3 feet and we could really,
> truly see where we thought it should be, and sure
> enough it was! Inside it was an invitation to an
> event that happened six months ago. We didn't see a
> need to RSVP, so we signed in and on we went....
>
> This time, we stopped at a place where there were two
> GC's and one LB. We went ahead and went for one of
> the GC's first and after much searching and apparent
> repeated shifts of the earth's magnetic fields (did
> y'all notice that this morning?), I found myself
> standing on the side of a very steep hill reaching
> across to a Y in a tree, bracing myself with one leg
> around the tree and reaching into the Y with my arm.
> Suddenly I realized I must be quite a sight, but I did
> get the cache, so we were 2-0! It was at that point
> that I could kind of see the fun of GC'ing. We took a
> finger and left a carabiner (this was the part
> Lightning really liked about gc'ing - aside from
> watching comfortably from a set of steps while his
> over-40 overweight mom hung precariously onto a tree,
> he got to choose the booty we traded for), replaced it
> and headed on down the Lanana Trail, had a very nice
> walk - quiet for a change, since the bikers and
> walkers that are usually on that particular trail were
> apparently scared indoors by the threatening weather.
> We came upon another of Lucy Locket's Vivaldi, and we
> were rewarded with Summer this time - we had
> previously found Autumn at Lake Striker. Nice stamp.
> SJZ and I thought about leaving the finger in the box
> so we wouldn't get poked with it anymore, but
> Lightning wouldn't have any part of it and promised to
> quit poking us, which he did. Suddenly we realized we
> had promised my mom we would be there at noon and it
> was already 12:30, so we high-tailed it out of there
> and headed to see her, leaving behind the second GC.
>
> We stumbled into Mom's room inky-handed (from the 8
> ball and the LB's) and scratched up, dampish and
> generally disheveled, so we finally had to own up to
> what we had been doing. She didn't get it. I sent
> Lightning out for the logbook so she could SEE for
> herself, but she STILL didn't get it. Scratch mom off
> the possible letterboxer list. She did, however, fall
> for a gag with the finger.
>
> We visited for a couple of hours until we had worn her
> out with tales of our tantalizing life (mmmhmm), then
> headed off again, this time in the pouring down rain,
> to the SFA Experimental Forest. The kids started to
> mutiny, refusing to hike off down a trail looking for
> a letterbox in the downpour. I invited them to stay
> in the car, but told them I would be going by myself.
> We compromised and I pulled out the $1 disposable
> ponchos I keep in my LB backpack and we went happily
> down the trail. The trail really isn't that long and
> it has pretty heavy cover, so although it was raining
> pretty hard, it wasn't as bad as it might have been.
> We found Vivaldi's Winter by Lucy Locket and did the
> poncho shuffle to keep the logbooks dry. I think this
> was my favorite of her Vivaldi series.
>
> And finally, as the sun was threatening to set, we
> made a mad dash out to the Carl Monk Scenic Overlook
> to find the last in the Vivaldi series - Spring. We
> got to this secluded spot and there were three guys
> goofing around there, throwing something around. When
> they saw that we weren't just pulling through, they
> stopped and started taking pictures of each other.
> Odd. These guys were wearing skulls and chains and
> didn't look like photography buffs. We just got out
> and pretended to be stretching and enjoying the view,
> but all the while working out where the clues would
> lead us. Where WAS the final resting spot for the
> box? Right by where the guys were goofing around.
> Then I had a vision that they might've been tossing
> around the box when we drove up. Yikes! We edged
> closer to them non-chalantly and I guess we made them
> nervous (I can see how some skull-wearing guys would
> see us ink-stained vagabonds as a threat), so they
> jumped in their car and left. We went happily to the
> spot where the box was to be and - it WAS indeed
> there. It hadn't been made into a makeshift football
> after all! It felt good to finish Lucy's little
> series and in such a place as that beautiful lookout.
> Our other hunch had been that the skull guys were
> letterboxers, but that didn't prove out either, since
> the last to log in was Gryzzled Gryphon and to my
> knowledge he doesn't wear chains and skulls.
>
> All in all, a very good day. We waited out the sunset
> at the lookout (although the lookout doesn't point in
> the right direction for a good sunset view), then
> headed on home. As we passed through Sacul, we
> noticed that they were having their monthly Bluegrass
> gathering, and I pleaded with the kids to stay with me
> for it, but they weren't so inclined and I had already
> presumed on their good natures all day long. But I
> have made a note to myself - "Bluegrass - Sacul - 4th
> Saturday of the month. Take a letterbox next month in
> case I get inspired!"
>
> BarefootLucy
>
> P.S. I poke fun at Lucy Locket's habit of planting
> boxes right after I go through her area, but I'm glad
> she won't let me get ahead of her. I guess I need to
> get a few more planted that she hasn't found and see
> if I can't get ahead of her that way! Maybe y'all
> will be privy to the Battle of the Lucys!
>
