From: Bonnie Lind (bonnielind1@hotmail.com) | Date: 2012-05-07 09:29:13 UTC-05:00
I’d love for us to have a Rodeo and Wild West Show.  Great idea to have a stagecoach.  Also great idea to have established rules and guidelines.  When a traveling box is planted, I think it would be smart for someone to announce they were going after said box.  Corazon and I were tempted make the drive this weekend, but we weren’t keen on driving all that way and not knowing if we were to be rewarded.  And if we couldn’t find it, is it because we were looking in the wrong place, or had it already been picked up.  Anyway, I’d like to make that recommendation.
 
I would also like to suggest that the retired boxes still be circulated--from person to person.  I would like to be able to get the Circus from Bell Motel (or whoever has it) and stamp at my leisure instead of frantically stamping them at an event.
 
It would be fun to be able to count all the stamps as finds.  I hadn’t thought about that as the reason to get the box.  I just wanted to see all the clever stamps!
 
Mosaic Butterfly

I guess great minds think alike. We were just talking this morning about the retirement of the Circus and starting a new traveling box. My idea, since we have had a bus and a train, was to have a stagecoach, like the Butterfield Overland Mail. But I like the idea of Bozo wearing baggy pants and dodging raging bulls. Would Bell Motel be willing to start the Rodeo and Wild West Show? If the circus is making a farewell tour without new passengers, there's no reason that the Rodeo couldn't have its first performance simultaneously, is there? The Circus has been around for over three years so that's not a bad run. I have a couple of observations about the traveling boxes. The first is that, in my opinion, there should be a size limit for the stamps. There are some really large Circus stamps and that just makes the box fill up faster. I know there were some published rules or guidelines for the first two buses, such as prompt notification when a box is found so that folks won't travel a great distance only to find that the box has already been picked up. I was considering a trip when Bell Motel reported her find, and I appreciated the notification, but that hasn't always been the case, so maybe we should revisit the rules. I know the traveling boxes have owners, but I think all of us feel like partial owners and feel the responsibility of ownership. One other point, and I'd like to hear other opinions on this, is that the first two boxes counted as one find for every stamp, so you could really pad your find stats by finding the box. I think that generated a lot of excitement and was a good reason to go out of your way to grab the box. For the circus box, all of those 42 stamps only count as one find. That's a lot of work for one find. What's your opinion?

Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2012-05-07 10:46:52 UTC-05:00

I'm not saying we have to do it like we always have, but in the past, the heartache with passing the box person to person is much like what happens with postals - they get stuck somewhere never to be seen again (anyone who has done postals knows exactly what I mean). I suppose if we could make a rule that the box has to be handed off to a member of this group - not mailed and not hidden - and that the person receiving must post a message here to say they have received it, it would work fine. That way the person who makes the hand-off knows who they gave it to and the person receiving it has to acknowledge that they got it.

The other thing that occurred to me was having several regional gatherings for the specific purpose of stamping the Traveling Boxes. They could be UPS'd between the keeper (Baby Bear) and the regional host. BUT - I wouldn't want Baby Bear to get stuck with all those UPS charges, so we would have to work out a way for him not to "bear" the burden. Just an idea...

From: Lou (lou.catozzi@att.net) | Date: 2012-05-07 16:21:57 UTC
Since I had the Circus before it was highjacked and Bell Motel hasn't chimed in yet, allow me make a few comments.

The original container for the Circus was very full when I got it. Every stamp was in it's OWN Zip-loc AND wrapped in cloth or some other custom wrapping. 42 such stamps take up a lot of room. Perhaps the stamps could be repackaged somewhat to reduce space.

Because the original container was so full, I moved all the contents into a 2.5 gallon pail from Lowe's which I then camo'ed with spray paint. I still have the original container. In fact it was in the Jeep when the Circus went missing. Apparently the Jackal didn't want it. Anyway, the CURRENT container was approximately 2/3 full when I last saw it so the potential is there for further additions without fear of overflowing.

The former container is available if someone wishes to start a new traveler. Perhaps the new box could be declared a sort of cootie in that it can only be passed directly from one letterboxer to another rather than hidden or mailed.

I feel that people should be free to count finds any way they choose. That being said, at least amongst the boxers who regularly contribute to the AltasQuest forums the consensus seems to be that you should count letterboxes found, not stamps found.

PI Joe - who is relieved that the Circus is back in good hands.

From: (cjagger405@earthlink.net) | Date: 2012-05-07 15:00:54 UTC-05:00
I haven't read this entire thread yet, but if I'm repeating what has already been said, then ignore me! (It's really not rude, I ignore myself half the time!). Remember back about six years ago when Ruby Tuesday had the birthday box and it would be sent to whomever had the next birthday? (That was when we only had about 25 people on the list.) Whoever received it was responsible for sending it to the next birthday person on the list (postage and insurance and it wasn't cheap but it was optional to receive it and soooo worth it!) . It was a box to behold, with celebration items, stamps, notes, little fun gifts etc. If you signed up to receive the box on your birthday, you assumed the responsibility for the box and for getting it mailed to the next person within a few days. Everyone knew who had it so it would have been embarrassing not to move it on as required.  I had just arrived back in Texas from my four years in Connecticut when the box came to me, and I felt instantly like I was part of something special - which I was! Texas is a big ol' place, so obviously the traveling boxes don't move from point A to point B without an effort. I think it might be worth considering mailing these boxes so it levels the playing field. With the Birthday Celebration box, it would be sent back to Ruby Tuesday occasionally so she could do maintenance and restock it with "presents".
Puddle Splasher
