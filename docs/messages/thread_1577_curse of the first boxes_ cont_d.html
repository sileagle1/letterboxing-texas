From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-01-03 23:29:42 UTC
Okay, the kids and I were checking on our first ever box, Rabbit
House, placed a little over a year ago (12/31/04). Its condition can
only be described as weird.

On top, inside the box was a hitchhiker, that must have been there for
months--as Runs for Chocolate left it back in the summer (July).

Below the hitchhiker was a baggie with the soaked logbook for Rabbit
House, but no stamp. Huh???? Below that was a pen. The inside of
the box was dry, but dirty.

This box had a bonus stamp, and it was also missing, but rooting
around, we found the lid to the bonus box and the stamp, but could not
find the original rabbit house stamp.

This one I will recarve, because the box was intact. I just think a
careless boxer accidentally took the stamp, plus didn't seal either
the baggie or the box well (either that or a geocacher found it, but
there was no corresponding trinket, so that's less likely--and the
geocache that was in this park went missing back in the early fall).
This carving was crude, but it was our first ever, so I never recarved
it primarily so other boxers could see what first carvings look bad
for everyone! Now, it'll look better after I recarve it, but I'm
still sorta sad about it. Some cool boxers and hitchhikers have been
through there, and the logbook is hard to read. Sigh.

And I do feel bad about that hitchhiker that has been sitting there
for 8 months--maybe I'll get that one going somewhere with more
action. Note to hitchhiker placers: Make sure your email address is
on the logbook too, not just the outside of the baggie, because the
host box may get wet!

Dewberry

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-01-03 17:21:57 UTC-08:00
How odd! I hope you eventually learn the story of
what happened!

I know what you mean about liking to leave those first
rough carvings out there. Our first Killough Monument
stamp was - crude would be a compliment, but it was
our first! I have one imprint only of it, and to my
knowledge it was never found before it went missing.
We really didn't intend on replacing it, but when Ruby
Tuesday asked very nicely if we would, we realized the
time was right to do it. The new carving is much
prettier and shows a better command of the tools, so
for those who like to go for good stamps, they'll like
the new one better.

Even though the first Killough Monument wasn't as nice
as some of our later stamps, I would've left it in the
wild forever if I had a choice. That first box - that
first stamp - have a little symbolism in my mind.
They represent us stepping out on a limb and diving
headfirst into something new and different - making a
commitment.

And as you mentioned - in encourages new boxers to go
ahead and place their first because we all started
somewhere!

Bummer for lost boxes - especially our firsts!
