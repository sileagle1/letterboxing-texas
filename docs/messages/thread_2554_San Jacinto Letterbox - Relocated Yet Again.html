San Jacinto Letterbox - Relocated Yet Again

From: Silver Eagle (sileagle@alltel.net) | Date: 2007-01-29 23:11:10 UTC
Since I was visiting my Dad in Pasadena today, I decided to take him
to San Jacinto Monument since he had not been in a long time. While
there I decided to check on my letterbox and it's a good thing I
did. The area had recently been clear-cut and my box was nowhere to
be found. As I was looking, a park maintenance man drove up and
asked if I was looking for a Geocache. I said yes, and he said his
crew had found one there the other day and he had it in the shop if I
wanted it. So I followed him to the shop and retrieved my box, along
with a real geocache that they had found nearby. I asked if it was
ok to hide the boxes in the park, and he said it was fine as far as
he knew. He described the are that the geocache was found, so I
replaced it as best I could, wedging it in a tree crevice out of
site. As for mine, I did not think its previous spot was good any
more, so I relocated to a more remote spot. Hopefully it won't be
too hard to get in summer. This is the 4th hiding place for this box
since I placed it in 2002, but at least it is still alive. I can't
believe how lucky I was that the box was not thrown away, and that my
timing was perfect to get caught looking for it, which led to getting
it back. I'm still amazed that this park allows boxes while others
don't, but maybe he did not represent the authorities. Anyway, I
have updated the clues, so reprint them if you plan to look for this
box.

Silver Eagle
