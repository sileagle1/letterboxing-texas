From: Mike Olbrisch (mike-2007@elp.rr.com) | Date: 2012-03-28 05:08:36 UTC-06:00

Don't know yet.  Sorry, this will be a little explicit, but maybe also interesting to some...

 

The whole trip will be determined by a blood smear on a microscope slide.  So the timing may happen very fast.  This is a virgin female, so we do not know her timing yet.  To top it off, we lost two engineers at work (young men just out of school seeking more experiences) so the boss has been leaning on me to cover a lot of it until the new people are ready to sit a shift alone.  I should be pulling a 5-day shift right now, but he scheduled me for 6 days.   That will make things a bit tighter with the timing, I should have been off next Tuesday.

 

As is natural for this stuff, she is a little smelly and a little messy.  We had the same offer from the stud dog owner, but we thought it better to stay away from the stud dog and his home.  So we sorta open-booked a La Quinta very near them.  I do not know what else my wife has scheduled.  I do know I want to climb Packsaddle mountain near Kingsland in Llano county.  Beyond that I have no plans.

 

I guess what I am saying is, THANK YOU FOR THE OFFER, but we have a place picked out and it is probably better we keep her a bit isolated.  She is very friendly, but could be 150 pounds of messy.  Because of the timing, actually the inability to pin down the timing, we are looking at a Window.  Our best guess is a window between next Wednesday and the following Monday.  I go on shift Monday night, it might be a tough shift if I don't get a little sleep.

 

I do believe time can be made for an exchange,  But it will probably be a short-notice thing.  And it will need to be at a dog-friendly place.  We never leave any dog alone in a vehicle.  Being early April, the temperatures can and may heat the van to a dangerous level.  A park, a restaurant patio where dogs are welcome, something like that.

 

Again, thanks very much for the offer.  You-all have a great day, I need to go relieve the night shift now.

 

Mike - El Paso
