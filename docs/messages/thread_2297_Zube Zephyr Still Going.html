Zube Zephyr Still Going

From: Silver Eagle (sileagle@alltel.net) | Date: 2006-11-06 14:40:02 UTC
I did some maintenance yesterday on my first placed letterbox
(9/22/02) and I am glad to say it is still alive, though it has
weathered a few storms (literaly). In the early days before any
letterboxers existed in my area besides Baby Bear, the only visitors
it had were from Geocachers. Luckily, they just signed in and left
the box alone. After about a year, I noticed a few letterboxers had
stamped in, but since there was no TX Talklist then, I had no way to
contact them and they had not contacted me. The logbook was damp, so
I rebagged and rehid under the same log on the ground. Then, after
the second year, I got a report from an out-of-town letterboxer that
they had found the box floating in the creek! Thankfully they had
seen it there after not finding it behind the log and replaced it for
me. It took me awhile to get back out there to check on it, but in
its third year I replaced the box with a camo one and used a bungee
cord to attach it to a tree up off the ground. Unfortunately, the
logbook was so wet that most of the stamps were barely visible, but I
decided to dry it and leave it there. However, the logbook has
continued to get worse (the wire binder is completely rusted through)
so I finally decided to replace it. It is a good thing I moved the
box to higher ground since I could see the creek had flooded again
and the box might have been lost forever. Even though it is a store-
bought stamp, this box has special meaning to me and I would hate to
lose it. If you find yourself in the area (the train ride is still a
fun family thing to do for free), please visit and stamp in, even if
you have found it before.

Silver Eagle
