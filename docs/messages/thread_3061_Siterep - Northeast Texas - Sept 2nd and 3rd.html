Siterep - Northeast Texas - Sept 2nd and 3rd

From: tigger32167 (tigger32167@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-09-03 22:56:01 UTC
Sunday - Sept 2
Well after the long letterboxing adventure with Wag Time on
Saturday, my navigator JR and I got up Sunday morning and took off
for Nacogdoches. My first stop was to pick up the Lamp Lite
Playhouse. This was a really cool location and a very nice stamp.
Next we went to Maroney Park and found the Good Job! box in really
good shapeand a really nice location. Our next stop was the SFA
duck pond to look for the Happy Easter box. However after trying to
dodge a couple on the pier that really needed to get a room,
we had no success finding this box. So, on we went to the Stern
Hoya soccer complex and found the Farm House box exactly where it
should be. We then left Nacogdoches and drove to Hemphill to the
Willow Oak Recreation Area to find the Willow Warbler Microbox by
Silver Eagle. I am sad to report that this box is no longer there.
The bridge is still not passable. Next we went to Logansport, LA to
find A Unique Landmark. I did manage to find a geocache but no sign
of the letterbox. This was a real disappointment that I was unable
to find it..but it was a wonderful location. Just to know what that
piece of granite truly symbolized..really neat. That was our last
stop for the day

Monday - Sept 3
I really needed to go to Tyler today and pick up a few items, so
decided to pick up a few boxes on the way. My navigator, JR, decided
that our first stop should be in Gallatin to pick up Pap-pa's
Community Center box. This was a really good first stamp for a new
young letterboxer. Our next stop was in Whitehouse at the city park
to pick up another stamp by this new young letterboxer called My Old
Stamp. The trick to this box was actually getting it out from under
the building. JR could get under the building pretty easy, but he
hasnt quite learned yet to get the boxes for me (of course I really
want him to learn to bring them to me and return them and hide then
in their spots). After finally getting down on all fours (that is me
getting down on all fours, not JR) I finally managed to retrieve
the box. Again, nice stamp for a young boxer. We then proceeded
to Tyler to Rose Rudman Recreation Area and found the How Hot Is It?
East Texas box. Wonderful stamp. Tx Dragonfly did a really great
job. We then continued around the trail and found the Ring the Bell
box. This box really got me, since it was at a bell that was in
honor of cancer patients. It has been almost a year since my best
friend lost her battle to cancer and I have to admit this
box brought tears to my eyes. It was a beautiful stamp also done by
Tx Dragonfly. Both of these were so intricately carved. After
running my couple of errands, we went to Faulkner Park in Tyler.
This is a really pretty nature trail. I had seen the signs numerous
times, and never made it out there. I was glad that I
finally did. When I first saw the name for the box there (15
Minutes) I really had to wonder what the theme of the stamp would
be. Well, I won't give it away, but it was really one of those AHA
moments. Again, it was nicely done. From there, we went home.
