I have had a few scary situations while letterboxing, but nothing
serious (knock on wood). I've actually had more happen before I
started letterboxing, as I'm sure many of you have, too. Maybe that
can be the next topic when this one is complete.

Once I was bitten by a scorpion while putting a box BACK. I'm always
careful when retrieving a box, using a stick to poke around first,
but I never worried too much about putting it back. Now I do.

Another time I had to retrieve a box from within a hollow tree. It
was wedged about arm's length up inside and I could not dislodge it
with a stick, so I reached up and grabbed it. When it finally came
down, there was a big snake coiled around the box and he was mad!
Not sure if he was poisonous, but it still made me almost shed my
skin. Stamping into that box and putting it back was the hardest
thing I have ever done letterboxing.

Well, maybe not the hardest. Once in Louisiana I had to cross a
spillway that had NO Trespassing signs all around it and could open
up automatically if the bayou got too high. The clues said it was ok
to cross, but I really had to suck it up to make that crossing,
especially since the water was really flowing. And the whole time
I'm on the other side I kept worrying it would open and strand me
there.

Actually, I've had way more occasion to feel nervous about being
thought of as the predator than being afraid of one. I usually
letterbox alone, and whenever I go to a park with Mothers and kids,
I'm always afraid they will think I am some pervert out to get them.
I mean, what would you think if you saw some strange guy lurking
around in the bushes! I always half expect the cops to come if I
take too long stamping in. And it is probably even worse when I box
with Baby Bear. Just think how bad it looks when people see two guys
emerge from the bushes with smiles on their faces!

Silver Eagle

From: Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter (janet@redbarnretreat.com) | Date: 2007-04-27 03:17:41 UTC
There are dangerous situations in nature other than the critters
that we should all be aware of, especially when we're letterboxing
with kids. There are rocks and creeks and lakes and drop-offs. We
just got back from McKinney Falls SP and it has a trail that runs
along a cliff that has a pretty good drop down to the creek, and
even little trails that loop right out to the edge for an overlook
with no rails or warning signs (which kids wouldn't read anyway).
Another is the trail at Blue Hole Park in Georgetown which also runs
along the edge of a sheer drop-off down to the river. If you
haven't been to a place before, an adult should probably take the
lead and watch out for natural hazards. Nature is beautiful, but
not always kid-friendly.

Boots Tex

From: Amber (Cooper) Lujan (ladida@rocketmail.com) | Date: 2007-04-28 00:58:45 UTC
My LB partner and I went and found FIVE out of FIVE of the boxes we
were hunting!
Only, look what was in the fifth location:
http://farm1.static.flickr.com/196/475043532_1cf9a21b5d_b.jpg
Yeah, that's the LB right there . . . surrounded by hundreds of Daddy
long-legs!

Okay, so it was less "Scary" and more "creepy." Either way, I yelled
to my partner, who was waiting on the trail with my baby (who always
goes LBing with us), "YOU'VE GOT TO SEE THIS!" She came walking over
with my baby in her arms and was really surprised. "I've never seen
such big daddy long-legs." I have, but I've never seen so many in one
place!

You should have seen me pulling that box out with two sticks while my
LB partner was swiping spiders off my legs and back. After we got the
box out and walked to a place where we could stamp I shook off like a
dog. Man, that gave me the heebie jeebies!
