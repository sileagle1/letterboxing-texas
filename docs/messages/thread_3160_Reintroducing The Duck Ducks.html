Reintroducing The Duck Ducks

From: Tara Burton (mselly13@juno.com) | Date: 2007-11-03 18:56:13 UTC
Well, now that I've posted a few comments and gotten some feedback
I'll answer some questions.

We box as husband Jeff, wife Tara and daughter 3. Yes, for you more
experienced boxers, we are very careful with the 3 year old. She
and I stay in the truck more often than not. Don't want to spoil
anyone's box or secret. Her part is getting to press the stamp into
our logbook...she thinks that's just the greatest and then we feel
she can't hurt, damage or lose any part of a box. So yes, we have a
great amount of respect for your treasures.
We live in Quinlan which is southern Hunt county. Very few boxes in
the immediate area. There are a few listed in Greenville, but of
the 4 we were only able to find one. We did contact the placer and
never heard back. One of very first finds was one placed by
Stargazer, Nana's Hummingbird...very nice. I think that was
probably the one that got my husband hooked or maybe it was Adams
Trail - Celebrate Our Military Day.
We chose our trail name because our daughter collects those little
rubber ducks with different outfits on. She must have a hundred and
still collecting.
My parents have a lakehouse in Tool Texas so we tend to box a lot in
Henderson County...Thank you Adams Trail for providing so many for
us.
As I said my husband is an experienced Scouter. He reached the rank
of Life before quitting as a child. I started my son at Tiger and
Dad took over when he crossed over to Boy Scouts. His son started
as a Bear. Both boys made it to Life before teenage wonders took
over and they gave up. We belonged to Troop 312 out of Royse City.
(Which is debatable as to whether it was the first troop in Texas
or not)...this particular troop and my husband made camping a
priority. Camping at least once a month was required. My husband
filled the role of Assistant Scout Master for 4-5 years. They went
to all the local camps and Camp Hale in Oklahoma. We highly
recommend Camp Hale. They also did many weekend camps at various
places.
My kids are now daughter 21, son 20, daughter 18 and son 18 plus the
baby 3. We only have the 18 year old son and baby at home. He is
senior this year and because of his football schedule we haven't had
a lot of time to box lately. The only out of town game that had
boxes was Farmersville and we were almost late getting the blow up
tunnel to the game because we just had to get Brother Ben and The
Quilter's Cross before it got dark.
We also find we like to box in more rural areas...haven't tried any
in Dallas...we just don't like the city.
We have not planted any yet. Maybe that "itch" is what we've been
waiting for deep down. I do plan on buying him the Carving Block
sampler pack and the Speed ball Stamp carving kit for
Christmas...any recommendations on these or other items. He has
been fiddling with little pink erasers so far.
When we go boxing it seems to be very spur of the moment jump in the
truck and take off for the weekend. No preplanning. We usually
stay at hotels that have a computer we can access to get more clues
that we may not have printed yet. Where do most of y'all
stay...camping or hotels?
We missed the meet in Eustace a few weeks back...it taught me to
check these posts more often. But we are planning on attending the
March meet and looking forward to it.
I think that covers everything but if anyone has any tips or ideas
they want to throw out there we are open to listening.
Thanks...everyone seems to be very friendly and welcoming and thus
far we have had nothing but wonderful experiences. Thanks to all!

tara
The Duck Ducks

In a message dated 11/3/2007 1:56:35 PM Central Daylight Time, mselly13@juno.com writes:
We also find we like to box in more rural areas...haven't tried any
in Dallas...we just don't like the city.
You might be surprised to discover just where "in the city" some of those boxes are - surprisingly, there are lots of nature centers, nature trails, wildlife and bird refuge areas, not to mention some really nice large parks in urban areas, many of which have been discovered by letterboxers who previously didn't know they existed, and of course, there are lakeshore trails all around Lake Grapevine and other lakes that have been swallowed up in recent years by urbanization, but still remain woodsy and rural.  Don't overlook Collin Co., Dallas Co., Tarrant Co., and Denton Co. - they may be large urban now, but they still have some lovely wild places and lots of historical and interesting places where letterboxes can be found!   Even most suburban areas now have at least a greenbelt and nature trail, if not a larger nature center to discover.  That's the beauty of letterboxing -- you never know what you'll discover along the way!
