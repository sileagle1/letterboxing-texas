TTB2, Nevermore.........

From: iraboxer (iraboxer@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-07-19 02:44:17 UTC
Once upon a midnight dreary, while I pondered weak and weary,
Over many a quaint and curious posts of letterboxing lore,
While I nodded, almost sleeping, suddenly there came a beeping,
As across my mind came crawling, dreams of my email calling.
"Not another post," I muttered, each one more and more appalling,
Only this and nothing more.

When the message met my eye it was in the hot July,
The Texas Tour Bus 2 was moving, and it chilled me to the core.
Eagerly I wished the morrow; vainly then would be my sorrow
If someone else would go and find it, leaving just a ghost behind it,
In the cemetery find it, leaving nothing to remind it;
Just a hole and nothing more.

Deep into that darkness peering, long I stood there wondering,
fearing.
Doubting, dreaming dreams no mortal ever dared to dream before.
But the silence was unbroken, and the darkness gave no token,
And the only words there spoken were the whispered words "We're here!"
This I whispered, and an echo murmured back the words, "We're here!"
Merely this and nothing more.

By the outhouse in the graveyard, I looked long and I looked hard.
Doubting, dreaming dreams no mortal ever dared to dream before.
I squinted, looking through the leaves and rocks, and then I spied
the letterbox
I held it tightly to my chest daring not to sneeze or hiccup,
Then I ran back down the road and I put it in my pickup.
Merely this and nothing more.

The Ghost of IRA Boxer
