I would add that WOWMOM brings up one of the key points about hunting letterboxes, but she also brought out a not-so-private joke amongst Texas letterboxers.

 

The story as I remember it goes that Silver Eagle got an email from an unhappy park ranger who was terribly perturbed about the placement of one of his boxes.   He was thoroughly puzzled because it just didn't sound anything like where his box in that park was.  All we could ever figure out was that he had found someone else's box that HAPPENED to be near a well, so the ranger googled something he found in the box and came up with Silver Eagle's clue, which happened to say "Recover well".   The ranger, upon reading this became VERY unhappy because we ne'er do well letterboxers had hidden something in their well and wanted to be sure everyone recovered it after finding.   After all was sorted out, Silver Eagle's box was fine (and it was covered quite nicely) and the other box wasn't as bad as they first thought.

 

So the gig became that many folks post in their clues "Recover Well" (you'll see many variations, but the most common one in Texas IS "Recover Well")  to give a little smile to Texas boxers and to gently remind each other to make sure the box is covered well before you leave the area.

From: Maribeth Jagger (cjagger405@earthlink.net) | Date: 2007-06-21 14:59:44 UTC-05:00
The one piece of advice I wish I had been given when I first started boxing was to take my time and savor each box! Each box has a unique perspective brought by the person who placed it. Sometimes it's wonderfully written clues, sometimes a magnificently carved stamp or handmade logbook. Sometimes it's an amazing hiding place. Then there are those "blue diamond" type boxes that seem to combine the best of everything. Just remember that whoever placed that box took the time and trouble and offered their creative talents just for your enjoyment - so do exactly that.... take time to enjoy and appreciate each box. Unless you hear the bugle call of an army of mosquitos descending upon you (or a hunger bear), take time to look at the stamps that are already in a logbook and write a brief message.  In other words, take time to smell the roses!
Puddle Splasher
