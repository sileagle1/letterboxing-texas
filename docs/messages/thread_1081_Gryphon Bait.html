Gryphon Bait

From: celticlions (celticlions@comcast.net) | Date: 2005-07-18 10:05:19 UTC-05:00

I have to tell you guys the truth – I was very upset about the Gryphon box being missing - more so than any other box we’ve lost.  We had absolutely no hopes of actually catching dear GG, but we loved the idea of possibly making contact with him/her through a humorous box.

So when we first checked on it and found it missing, Stillwater and I searched all the surrounding bushes, hoping it had just been misplaced. When I finally had to admit it was gone, my LSS tried to cheer me up by saying he was positive GG had snabbed it and was chortling somewhere about foiling our plans. Barefoot Lucy had another entertaining thought: that GG had taken it and would re-hide it somewhere! This actually DID cheer me up! I would love to think we had engaged him/her in playing!

But it kept nagging at me -so yesterday evening, after successfully finding Cadenza’s “No Directions Needed” box ( really great stamp & location, BTW), I asked Stillwater if he would help me look through the bushes and pond one more time for the Gryphon Bait box. I know he thought it was a doomed expedition, but he humored me. (That’s what good LSS’s do, isn’t it?)

Well, I’ll cut to the chase – we FOUND it!!!

Here’s the weird part – there were no other stamps other than ours & Tx2Chics, but it was in an entirely different place than the original spot. The box has a Velcro strap to attach to the branches of the bushes, but it was strapped onto the base of a bush and was sitting on the ground. The tiny plastic baggie was missing, but the log and stamp seem undamaged. Instead of over the 2nd drainpipe, it was kind of close to the 1st drainpipe – several feet away.

Stillwater & I have been musing over this for awhile, but can’t figure it out. Our best guess is that perhaps a Noxer stumbled upon it and opened it up, looked at it & just didn’t know where to put it back? It truly wasn’t in a spot where folks would normally look or linger, but LSS thinks that perhaps kids found it, showed their parents and were encouraged to put it back, but goofed slightly? (I think he has way too much faith in most kids and most parents!)

So, sometime today, Vandal and I will replace the box with discrete rejoicing!!!

 

Lessons learned:

1.       When one of your boxes is reported missing, it doesn’t hurt to look around in unlikely places!

2.       Camouflage tape is GREAT! ( We must have looked within inches of the box without seeing it)

3.       It’s not worth letting a lost box ruin your day – I was in a blue funk for hours – what a waste of precious time…

 

 

Doing the “Box-Is-Found” dance around the kitchen ;-),

Ruby T.
