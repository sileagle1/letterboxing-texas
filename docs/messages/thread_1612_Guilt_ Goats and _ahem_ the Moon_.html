Guilt, Goats and {ahem} the Moon?

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-01-20 09:49:08 UTC-08:00
Now that I have your attention, I'll tell you about my
letterboxing weekend last weekend!

Now by the time you finish reading this, you'll know
exactly what was meant by "guilt", but I'm going to
leave it that you have to go find the boxes I found
this weekend to know where the other two items come
in.

Well, as I mentioned last week, I intended to spend
last weekend LB'ing since Fearless Fox and Lightning
went on a weekend hike. I decided I would return to
the area where we held the JEN gathering since I never
did get to go out and search boxes, except the tricky
box placed by Gryzzled Gryphon! In that light, I
called my bro-in-law and his wife in Kerens and asked
if I could spend the night so I would be perfectly
positioned to hit the trail at first light. I
gathered my clues and mapped out my plan, and when I
got to Kerens, my bro-in-law fell right into my hands
by suggesting that I get his wife to drive me while I
go find the Corsicana boxes that night. So off we
went - with one teenager in tow. My LSS called as we
were leaving McD's (remember, teenager in tow) about
10 p.m. to see where I was (I guess he knew where his
kids were, so he was checking to see where his spouse
was). I told him we were going to hunt a couple boxes
and he expressed concern - he doesn't like us night
boxing without him. I told him his bro (a cop) had
assured us it was a safe part of town and besides, we
had a gun with us. To which he said he "hoped
everyone else was safe." Funny guy.

Anyway, we got to the site of the first box in honor
of Wolf Brand Chili, only to find that the gates were
locked at some undetermined time of day! Hmph. Well,
we knew where the park was anyway... So we went on to
find the Petroleum Park box. I love the way Catpawz
find these little out of the way places. We showed up
there and while I was diligently digging my gloves and
a stick out of the car, my sis-in-law stuck her bare
hand into the hiding place and pulled out the box.
Clearly, I will have to read the disclaimers to her!
Finding that there really WAS something there, she was
intrigued. I did the deed while they watched, then we
headed back to her house with a 50/50 success rate.
Knowing that she was playing with the hook and was
debating whether to bite, I offered to carve her a
stamp. She knew exactly what she wanted, so I carved
it and by midnight she had her own personal stamp -
and it was shortly after midnight that she suddenly
realized she had no way to say "No" when I asked her
to get up at the crack of dawn and drive across two
counties to begin letterboxing our way back to her
house. GUILT! A wonderful thing!

So we got up wayyyy earlier than her alarm clock
acknowledges exists and brushed our teeth and put our
hair up in pony tails, threw on warm old clothes in
layers because it was C-C-C-OLD and left. I gave her
navigation duties and we headed out across the
countryside. It was beautiful as the sun came up -
the only problem was that we were heading west and not
east! We made it to Italy and found Catpawz' passport
box there (nice location) and she took lots of
pictures. As we were getting out of the car to start
looking, she took the clue book and refused to give it
back. Hmmm. I showed her how to go through the whole
process of stamping in and so forth and she got to try
her new stamp. She deemed herself Flutterby and she
sports a gold fairy stamp. We jumped in the car and
started moving again and eventually found ourselves in
Cleburne - our intended destination. I had hoped to
be able to go a little further north to Burleson and
Joshua as well, but I knew she had time constraints so
we didn't try to get on up there.

We passed by the cemetery where Delivery Girl's "Where
Have All the Flowers Gone?" box is while we were
trying to figure out how to get to Cleburne State
Park, when suddenly we realized that the big cemetery
we had passed was THE cemetery we would have to find.
Sure enough - and after one of those "this HAS to be
the place, but where is the ????" moments - we found
the box in a very clever hiding spot.

We found a place to eat lunch, then headed on out to
Cleburne State Park to find the several boxes there.
Now I learned - again - that all of Texas is NOT the
gentle, rolling hills of East Texas! I made a mental
note that it is NOT a good idea to
eat and then go letterboxing in CSP, AND that it is a
good idea to wear my hooves when I go back. Despite
being taken by surprise by the terrain, we had a great
time in the park and thoroughly enjoyed every box we
found there. We found Delivery Girl's Hot Chicks and
Chick's Rule, and Silver Eagle's Cleburne Catfish, and
we also found Baby Bear's Northern Exposure. All
great boxes!

We left Cleburne and headed to Waxahachie, stopping
first to find Celtic Lions' Hole in the Ground box at
the site of the Superconductor Supercollider monster
project. Very interesting site! We were interested
in the fact that the windows were clean and there were
huge dumpsters full of fresh trash, despite the
project being abandoned 10 or more years ago. Now I
haven't mentioned that Flutterby is a newspaper woman,
so she had her camera with her and felt it was worthy
to take pictures. I got a little uneasy after our
discussion of the clean nature of the site when she
started snapping pictures all around! I said
"Umm...you ummm...do realize this was the site of a
one of the most secretive non-secret federal project
that was ever doomed to failure?" To which she
responded "I'll flash 'em my press credentials if they
have any questions..." Hmmmm. I wonder what that
meant? Special jail cells for the press???

On we went to Getzendaner Park and Viewfinders and
Puddlesplashers boxes there. As she had done
repeatedly all day long, Flutterby took the clue book
and wouldnt give it up. After we identified the
trail we needed to begin on, we were distracted by
some teenagers who were using tree service equipment
to hoist themselves wayyyyy up high in the trees and
then swinging themselves across the creek there. Very
interesting. They had quite an operation going!

We went off in search of Viewfinders Red Barn for JEN
and Puddlesplashers Celebration of the Texas
Countryside. That is the coolest park and great
little trails. We located each of the boxes without
attracting undue attention of the tree jumpers,
although we noticed them watching us trying to figure
out if we were watching them. Lots of fun. And at
one point, we had another of those I KNOW this is the
right place moments. Or I should say, Flutterby did.
Ive had enough of those on my own to know that if I
have any doubt about being in the right place, stop
and rethink it. I got a kick out of her absolutely
insisting we HAD to be in the right place and the box
was simply not there. I had a feeling we werent on
track, so I finally wrestled the clue book from her
hands and convinced her we needed to walk a little
further. We did finally find the box and the clues
fit exactly the place that we eventually got to. We
had a big laugh about that!

By the time we finished the Getzendaner boxes, the sun
was setting and we headed back to her house. The
sunset was just beautiful, but we were driving east
and not west! We hit her door just as her company was
getting there (the reason we couldnt go on to
Burleson and Joshua) and Ive wondered what they
thought about us showing up disheveled and
ink-stained. I had gotten a phone call on the way
back that I needed to go on home, so I didnt get a
chance to hear her explain. Bet it was a good one
though!

The only unfortunate part of the trip is that I didnt
get to plant two boxes Ive been carrying around for
Navarro County because I had to cut my trip short.
Otherwise though we found Italy, Chicks Rule, Hot
Chicks, Cleburne Catfish, Northern Exposure, Hole in
the Ground, Petroleum Park, Celebration of the Texas
Countryside (all three), Red Barn for JEN, and Where
Have All the Flowers Gone? We also found a bonus box
my first and hers. We netted some pretty cool
scenery and new terrain, and saw the moon from one
more angle than we expected. Hopefully Flutterby will
come along with me again some time, but we ARE going
to have to talk about her hogging the clue book.

Lucy
