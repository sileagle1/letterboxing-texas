We've been out of touch for the last couple of weeks, visiting Junction and South Llano River State Park.  That park has absolutely no Internet and no cell phone service, so we were really isolated, and haven't been following the recent letterboxing news.  I think Martin Dies, Jr. is a great park and an excellent choice, well worth the extra miles for the Central Texas folks.  The park is heavily forested, with many miles of trails (bring your own SPORES).  There are many camping spots and cabins, on a lake with creeks.  The cabins are new, having been added since the hurricane damage a few years ago.  If you saw the damage after the hurricane a few years ago, you wouldn't recognize it now.  The clean-up and restoration has been amazing.  I'd advise you to make reservations now, as it is very popular in the spring.
