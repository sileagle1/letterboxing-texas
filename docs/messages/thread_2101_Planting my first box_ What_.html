From: (blhjrh@aol.com) | Date: 2006-08-25 19:00:41 UTC-04:00
Just try it, Janet.  It's pretty easy to follow the prompts.  Just click on the appropriate symbols to code the box at the top (and click HELP if you need explanations), then type in the clues in the box - just like an email message, then keep clicking the appropriate boxes and buttons until you get to SAVE.  Unless you're going to redirect readers to your own website or another website where you've posted your clues, you can ignore that URL box.  Of course, you'll want to do it on both AQ and LBNA, because some people only look at one of those sites and will miss your box otherwise.  And, if you make a mistake, you can always cancel or delete and start over!  Believe me, it's not that hard.  If I can do it, pretty much anyone can do it.
Viewfinder

The only big suggestion I can give for responsible letterboxing is to use discretion finding and rehiding boxes, and always be sure to recover them well to protect them!  This is sometimes part of the fun, being stealthy to avoid detection when the area is busy or crowded and you have to use subterfuge to find a box, stamp, and replace it without anyone discovering what you're up to!  But once you start placing your own boxes, you'll become even more aware of how protective the box owner is, although, once out there, they are really on their own more or less anyway, and anything can happen.  Still, when it's your box, you want it to be safe!  -- Viewfinder
