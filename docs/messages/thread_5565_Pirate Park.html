Pirate Park

From: Vicki Bell (vicki@bellmotel.com) | Date: 2016-07-22 18:26:56 UTC-05:00
Just for grins, I thought I'd post a little sit-rep about the Pirate Park in Austin, TX.  Four years ago, Mazy Days had the great idea for us to keep adding pirate-themed stamps to the Gus Fruh entrance area of the Barton Creek Greenbelt.  At the end of May this year, floods raged through the area, and Barton Creek turned deadly.  I decided to check on all the Pirate Park boxes today, and I've contacted the planters to let them know what I found.  Six out of eighteen boxes were missing.  Here is a list:
Weigh Anchor! -- missing
Aye! -- found in good shape
Pirate Kids (2) -- first one missing, second in good shape
PIrate Joe -- found in good shape
The Royal Fortune -- buried in mud, but dry and in good shape
Ahoy! -- missing
Here There be Dragons -- found in good shape
Queen of the Irish Seas -- buried in mud, but dry and in good shape
Hello Sailor -- buried in mud, logbook ruined, but stamp ok.  Cleaned stamp and box, and re-hid.
Walk the Plank -- missing
Loot (5) -- #1 missing, #2 ok, #3 missing, as it's tree was uprooted, #4 buried in mud, stamp ok, but logbook ruined (I cleaned and re-hid), #5 in good shape
Hello Pirate Kitty -- found in good shape
Computer Pirate -- found in good shape
It is way too hot to be doing this!  -- Bell Motel
