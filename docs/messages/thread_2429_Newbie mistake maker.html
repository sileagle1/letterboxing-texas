From: Mike - RR (mike-2007@elp.rr.com) | Date: 2006-12-30 17:25:34 UTC
Whoa whoa whoa - easy people.
 
I am just an outsider.  I have no horse in this race.  So please let me try to smooth over the ruffled feathers.
 
I am not one of the lucky ones either.  I was not born a letterboxer.  In fact, I spent a number of years as a GEOCACHER <GASP> before I ever heard of letterboxing.
 
I too have tried to be the best letterboxer I can be.  I have taken in a box that was nearly washed away in the El Paso floods.  I have replaced boxes for owners of ones stolen.  And I have planted a few.  Of course, who can forget the EXTENSIVE searches for Hueco Tanks - I <recovered and soon to be replaced> and Hueco Tanks - II <never found - http://www.photoshow.net/KD9KC/adventure >.
 
Have I made mistakes?  Heh heh heh - sometimes I wonder how I survived 20 years of soldiering.  My parachutes always opened, and the bullets always missed me.  And somehow - my bullets mostly found the target.  My one aircraft accident I walked away from.  Other than being nearly deaf in one ear from an AT-4 and a back injury kept in check by exercise, I was lucky.
 
So whats my point?  Just THIS.
 
Who on this list was born letterboxing?  If you were not, you too were once a newbie.  Who on this list never made a mistake?  Even made a snap-judgment that you regretted later?  If you have - welcome to the club.  We have a lot of members.
 
What has been done here that cannot be fixed?  We have some people upset about their boxes.  From the report, they were trashed before being found.  We have some boxes destroyed and needing to be replaced.  And we have a newbie who was excited about boxing and when faced with a situation made a snap judgment to try and help.  A newbie who is now ready to pack it in and quit over trying to be nice.
 
Can we all just take a step back - and take a deep breathe - and admit maybe some mistakes were made all around?  A little mentoring.  A little box repair.  A little "I'm sorry" too.
 
Hey PutpleInTex - come letterbox in El Paso.  I won't bite.
 
The rest of you - Happy New Year.
