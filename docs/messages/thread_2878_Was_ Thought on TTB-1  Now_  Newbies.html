From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-06-25 13:37:13 UTC
I think it's interesting how many people just sort of "try"
letterboxing out...and then I guess the hobby doesn't really call to
them, because they just disappear. Or, they pop up every now and
again, with long stretches in between. Life is busy and everyone's
doing a million and one things, I guess.

I'm really intrigued by those people who go so far as to actually
hide a box (usually just one). It shows some level of interest and
familiarity with everything. But you've never heard of them and you
never hear from them again.

Lots of people use LBNA (or AQ) and never join a list. Some people
get deeper and deeper & find the many many layers.

dewberry

I enjoyed the hunt in both letterboxing and geocaching right off, but
it took on a whole new level of interest when I realized that I enjoy
carving. My first several stamps were a chore, I considered it a

found a series of letterboxes in Moultrie, Georgia in which the

that impressive, and now I carve more than I plant.

of-towner just passing through who planted one box on his way by. He
may have lots of boxes somewhere else.
