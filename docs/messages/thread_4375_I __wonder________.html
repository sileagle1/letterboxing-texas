On Sat, Feb 13, 2010 at 4:08 PM, Tasha Morris <keishkakeishka@yahoo.com> wrote:

The TTB2 was one of two reasons that I even joined this list (still haven't figured out that mystery box!), and I've gotten such a thrill every time the clues are posted, and try to drop everything if it's even remotely close to DFW.

Seeing as I'm the one who ended up in Lockhart vying for it (and being in the wrong place wasn't all bad - fun really - Rastaman only complained a little, and we really enjoyed the park, and we have a fabulous story that my family now laughs about whenever we get together - they all make fun of my LB addiction), I'd absolutely hate for it to get retired. That is until I get my grubby little hands on it, and then I'd be fine with it being retired and becoming and event box.

See how selfish artists can be? :-D

Honestly, being one that hasn't seen the TTB2 yet, I can't speak to how full it is or anything, and I've never been clear on if I was supposed to contribute a new stamp - I've gathered that I'm allowed to - I've just assumed for the last year that there would be instructions in the box. I haven't seen the circus either, and would love to see it get more play too. If retiring the TTB2 is the best way to ensure the circus gets moved more often, and to ensure that TTB2 doesn't get lost, then so be it.

I'm well aware that I'm just being a selfish little artist who's been enjoying the thrill of the chase.

> so that folks can stamp it if they so decide. I realize that isn't

> to to find it. However...
>
> I think we need to retire it for a couple of reasons. First of all, it is

> to it, and that is really part of the fun of these traveling boxes.
> Secondly, if we retire it, perhaps the Circus will get more play. Thirdly,
> it is now a letterboxing treasure - something that would be a true loss to
> our circle if anything happened to it, like as in the first Tour Bus.
>
> I suggest that if we retire it, it be moved to a slightly larger container
> and become an event box. With the extra room, folks could contribute to it

> who are too far off the beaten trail to have seen it. Make no mistake - I

On Sat, Feb 13, 2010 at 5:35 PM, Tasha Morris <keishkakeishka@yahoo.com> wrote:

The finding part is definitely part of the thrill for me... But I'll have that chance with the Circus still, right? I'm more than willing to be unselfish and agree to retiring the box as long as I'll get to see all the fabulous stamps at TALE and still hunt for the Circus.

The whole Lockhart thing was actually really funny - I spent all of Thanksgiving weekend trying to convince one relative or another to make the 3 hour round trip with me, and none of them would. The whole town boasts that they have the best barbecue in Texas, with three different Q restaurants making top ten in TX lists. I finally convinced my hubby that if he would just take a different route home so we could stop there, then he wouldn't have to stop at the 8 boxes that are on our normal route that he had promised to let me find on that trip. We had a fun time trying to sneak onto the golf course (which, in hindsight, hubby was right, the box would have never been on a golf course!) and getting in trouble with park rangers, who bought the story that I was just wanting to check out the course for my uncle, an avid golfer. Now, whenever I ask family to go boxing with me, they always ask if they're going to end up on a golf course in Lockhart. Good times, good times. :-)
