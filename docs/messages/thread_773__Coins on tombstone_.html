I have been very interested in the whole conversion -- my grandparents are from Poland and I asked my mom if she was aware of any custom regarding this and she said she had never heard of this as a polish tradition.   ANITA

Wanda <wsthm@hotmail.com> wrote:

Curiousity got the better of me, and I called the visitor's center
this morning, but the only lady who was there, said she had no idea.
She had never seen such a thing, although she did say that she had
heard of 'little children leaving a teddy bear on a brother or
sister's grave'. She said that she visits the cemetery quite often,
but had never seen that, and only attends to her family's
gravesites. She said that she would ask the other visitor center
volunteers when she sees them.

So then today, I asked a few ladies I was working with, and they had
never heard of it either.

Part of my puzzlement comes from the fact that it is modern day
money (I think.) on a very old grave. I don't remember the date on
the tombstone, (1800's???) but I wouldn't think there would be
anyone alive now who would have known the child. So to me, that
somewhat rules out the 'remembrance token' thing.

Busy Bee
