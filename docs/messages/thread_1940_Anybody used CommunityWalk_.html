Anybody used CommunityWalk?

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-06-21 20:05:40 UTC-05:00
I've developed a thing for maps over the last few months, and I've been working toward trying to learn about customizing maps.  I have been enamored with Birds of a Feather's Texas Letterboxing Atlas since it first began, but I wanted to find a way to make my own map with more specific locations and MY statuses of letterboxes.  I've come across a few programs and sites, but they were overwhelmingly complicated.  I needed something online that I can work with easily...
 
www.communitywalk.com is a customizable map site.  Admittedly, it isn't as flexible as I would like, but it's pretty cool!  I mapped all the letterboxes in Cherokee County and I'm working on other counties now and I've noted them with my status - found, unfound, mine, whatever.  I've loaded their clue link into the marker, and I've marked parks that aren't on the maps.  As I find boxes, I can easily change their status.  I've also added in some geocaches, and I'll note points of interest that may be applicable to letterboxing for me (like historic sites where I might like to place boxes). 
 
I've had a lot of fun playing with it the last few days.  I don't see myself ever abandoning Jeff's atlas, but this has been nice for planning for me.
