Started out the morning going north on 175 to Frankston and found
the All Aboard? box at the depot/library. This was a really good
carving. There was also a geocache in the same bush as the
letterbox, but neat place to visit. We then went to the Kickapoo
Battlefield box in Frankston and again nice carving and great
history behind the sight, which was where the retaliation occurred
for the Killough massacre that took place right out of
Jacksonville. Our next stop was between Poynor and Brushy Creek at
Foster Cemetery. This is the sight where Cynthia Ann Parker was
originally buried before being moved. The box is called In Memoriam
C.A.P. Again this was a nice stamp and really great location.

Started out the morning going north on 175 to Frankston and found
the All Aboard? box at the depot/library. This was a really good
carving. There was also a geocache in the same bush as the
letterbox, but neat place to visit. We then went to the Kickapoo
Battlefield box in Frankston and again nice carving and great
history behind the sight, which was where the retaliation occurred
for the Killough massacre that took place right out of
Jacksonville. Our next stop was between Poynor and Brushy Creek at
Foster Cemetery. This is the sight where Cynthia Ann Parker was
originally buried before being moved. The box is called In Memoriam
C.A.P. Again this was a nice stamp and really great location.
From there we went to the Brushy Creek Arbor in Brushy Creek to get
the Of Pines and Prayers box. This was really a nice place and
again a great stamp and a great history to go along with it. I have
really been impressed by the history, location, and stamps of
Frankston Native, who placed these four boxes. Just hope that they
continue to put those stamps out there. Next we headed to Athens to
find The Sheriff box which was in tribute to Jess Sweeten. The box
was located near the old jail and was a really nice carving. I had
already found some of athens dicer's boxes and the history, stamps,
and locations were also really great. Again, hope they keep it up.
The next stop was the park behind the Community Center in Malakoff
to pick up the Little Bunny Fu Fu box. The box was in great shape.
We then journeyed down to Seven Points and went to the library to
find Adventures of Adams Trail2. This box is actually in the
library, and Adams Trail did a phenomenol job on the box and
placement..the stamp was also really nice. We then went to Seven
Points Park to find Love is in the Air box. We found the right
tree, but no box was to be found. Our next stop was Kings Cemetery
in Seven Points where we were able to pick up the Hot and Cole/Harts
on Fire box. The box was in good shape. We then continued on to
Mabank to the Tri County Library to pick up A Butterfly by Adams
Trails. Again this was a great job on the placement and a nice
stamp to go with it. We then went to a park near the rodeo grounds
in Mabank to pick up The Blue Daisy. The box was safe and
sound. Jr then navigated me to Phalba to a really neat little one
room school house. The people who own the property are really
taking care of the grounds, but the school is deserted even though
the bell is still out front. The box that is placed there is An
Apple for my Teacher and it was in great shape. Next we
headed out to find The Drifter in Goshen Cemetery between Eustace
and Phalba. This was a neat old cemetery and the history about the
area was really interesting. The box was in great condition. We
then went to Palestine and picked up the Blueberry Scout: Dot box
in Davy Dogwood Park. Nice carving and the box was safely tucked
under the log. We then found our way to Old Palestine Cemetery in
Palestine and found The Railroad Man - Texas Governors Series.
Again Boots Tex did not let me down on the carving of this
governor. He did a wonderful job, and the box was in great
condition. So all in all, it was a great day. JR and I found 14
out of 15 boxes. Thanks again to all the wonderful boxers out
there.
JB Kokopelli
