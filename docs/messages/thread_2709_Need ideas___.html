Okay, I've carved about 85 little personal stamps and other boxers have sent along about 30 more little personal stamps for our Cub Scout campout.  That puts me at about 115.  I understand we are going to have a bumper crowd at this event, so I'm still looking at needing to carve about 50 more.  Not to mention needing to carve some for staff, some for stations, and do the boxes to be planted - the theme for the planted boxes is Texas.
 
I'm about out of ideas for the personal stamps - can y'all throw me some ideas out for boy-ey stamps that work well in about 1.5x1.5" format?  Single color...I've done monkeys, giraffes, elephants, snakes, frogs, toads, bugs, boats, planes, helicopters, trucks, motorcycles, balls, sports equipment, dogs, cowboy hats, tents,
 
I could use some Texas ideas too - I have a cowboy, a cowgirl, a cow skull..,I guess I actually have more time on the personal stamps, which have to be done by 04/27, but the planted boxes have to be ready to go by 04/20...
My brain is tired!

Hmmm... you've got Texan boys here. Some of them will be sports fans.
What about logos from some of the major sports teams? A&M, UT,
Tech...U of H. A baseball fan might enjoy Rice. The Astros and the
Rangers. Do the Rockets still play? I am soooooo not a sports fan...
But you could do two for A&M: an ATM logo and a gig 'em thumb, and
two for UT: A UT and a longhorn logo.

How about really really simplified Texan landmarks? Like a rocket or
stars for Nasa? A seashell for Galveston? Pegasus for Dallas?

Any stamp or coin collectors among them? You could do a simplistic
picture of a stamp or a coin. What other hobbies do boys have?
Computer games? I don't know how that could be a stamp, but maybe you
do. A TV?

What about academic symbols? Like a book, or a calculator, pencil,
pen, for those who are into school.

Or band symbols, for band geeks? (I always use the word "geek" with
affection. :-)

I hope that helps! This is a heck of a project for you to take on!
