Hooking a prospective letterboxer

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-04-15 03:54:44 UTC-07:00
We've been the subject of rolled eyes and quickly
changed conversations at family gatherings since we
got involved in letterboxing. You know - when you get
together with family and they ask what you've been up
to? I say "Well, you see, there's this new hobby
we've taken up..." and after I finish talking about a
recent excursion, they say "Uhhhh....yeah. How's the
new job going?" and I say "Well you know I sell
steeples now..." and they head to the bathroom because
they don't know what to say about that either.
Fortunately, we take it in stride (we've always been
considered the weirdos of the family!) and laugh about
it later.

Well, surprisingly, my brother-in-law was mentioning
letterboxing to his wife, and she was very
enthusiastic about trying it! She loves the idea of
carving stamps, hiding them, finding them - the whole
9 yards. Bear in mind, this is the last woman on
earth I would've expected to perk her ears up at the
idea, but she has surprised me before, so I realize I
shouldn't underestimate her. Well, needless to say,
she now wants to go on an outing. Problem is, she
(even more than I) is in a dead zone - Corsicana
(Johnson-Ellis-Navarro counties area). I want to make
sure her first trip out is a lot of fun. She could
come to Jacksonville and I could take her to get the
stamps we've already found, but I'm thinking I would
like to find some new ones too. I'm thinking possibly
going to Mexia and then to Waco from her house.

Soooo....I see on the map that Baby Bear and Silver
Eagle are the only ones who have placed any stamps in
that area, and I also see that most of them have been
recently checked (and I noticed that Jr. Texas Ranger
is missing as of yesterday). Has anyone else gone and
found the Waco/Mexia stamps? What is the one or two
"must finds" in that area? Would it be possible to
get all of them in one day, bearing in mind we may
have four adults and five kids in tow (two of which
probably won't be much into it?) May leave the guys
behind with whatever kids don't want to go...

I'm thinking we'll go in about three weeks, unless she
really wants to hurry up and go, in which case it'll
for sure be a one day trip. Otherwise, we may camp
and take a couple of days.

Thoughts are appreciated!
Lucy

From: Silver Eagle (sileagle@alltel.net) | Date: 2004-04-15 12:46:28 UTC
I know what you mean about family or friends looking
at you like you are crazy when talking about letterboxing.
Other than my brother Baby Bear, nobody has shown enough
interest to try it, though they will at least tolerate
my indulgence.
As far as your trip, I think you could do several in
one day and still spend some time enjoying the places
they take you. It is too bad that several of Baby Bear's
boxes are now missing in that area (Comanche at Old Fort
Parker SP & Val Verde Canon at Confederate Reunion Grounds SP)
but most of mine are still there as reported to me just a
few days ago. If you start from Corsicana early, you could
go south to Confederate Reunion Grounds SP for my Confederate
Flag box and enjoy an easy hike while learning a little
history. I would allow about 1 hour to not rush. Next head
west to Waco for my Texas Ranger box. The box will only take
about 15 minutes to find and return, but if you want to see
the Ranger Museum, allow at least another hour. Next drive
to the Old Suspension Bridge to look for Baby Bear's Don't
Mess With TX box. It won't take long to find his box but
this would be a great place to eat a picnic lunch so allow
another hour or so. Next in Waco, drive to Cameron Park and
look for Baby Bear's Baylor Bear box. The box will take
about 30 minutes but you might want to allow more to explore
the park. My Jr. Texas Ranger box was just reported missing
its stamp & logbook so if you have time maybe you could
verify that for me. If it is true I would appreciate if
you could collect the box for me since I don't intend to
replace it and right now it is just trash. The other box
in this park (Liberty Bell) has also been reported missing
but you might want to verify that, too.
Now if you have time and energy you could make one more
stop on your way back to Corsicana. Go north to Lake
Whitney SP for the Gone Fishin box. I have not gotten
this one yet or heard anything about it since it is fairly new.
If you are camping, this might be a good spot, too. Then
you could go further north the next day to Dinosaur Valley SP
for the 2 boxes there.
A future trip could be to Dallas area for my Texas Star box
and the three others in the area (Stampbook box, Spider's Lair
& Halloween). Hope this helps and that you have a fun enough
trip to "hook" the future letterboxers!

Silver Eagle
