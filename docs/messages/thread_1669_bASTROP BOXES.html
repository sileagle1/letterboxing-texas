From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-02-04 07:22:49 UTC-08:00
I haven't been back to Bastrop since the first
gathering two years ago, but I can tell you it is
great place to box. There are some really good boxes
there.

Don't forget that Buescher State Park is also there.
One of the first letterboxes in Texas is in Buescher -
Eoghan's Lost Pines. I think you'll have to see if
someone has clues for it because I see the link on it
is dead. It's one of those stamps you need to get if
at all possible because of its historical value, not
to mention it's a fun box to find.

We had been in letterboxing for about six months when
we went to the Bastrop gathering, AND our area was
sparsely populated and only had boxes because of
Silver Eagle and Baby Bear. When we went to Bastrop,
we were overwhelmed by the people AND by how many
boxes there were to find! Funny that my county -
Cherokee - WITHOUT a state park now has more boxes
than Bastrop County!

Anyway, I know you'll enjoy Bastrop!

Lucy

Bastrop is a great place to letterbox. This is a beautiful park and
there are many great boxes. We were there December 31. There were a
couple, though, that we couldn't find. One was Skinny Pine. The
waterfall was dry and I'm positive we were in the right place, but
there was no box under any of the rock ledges. The other missing one
was These Boots are Made for Walking. We found the right tree and
what was left of a box. It was melted like it had been in a fire and
there was no stamp or log book. Have fun!

From: "Just Doin' It" Grammy Pat (grammypat@txcyber.com) | Date: 2006-02-04 20:28:29 UTC-06:00
What a glorious day. We found 6 out of the 9 we had clues for. "These boots
were made for walking" is indeed gone, but we found Trail end,10th regiment
and Houston Toad. We went for Lost loblolly and poison ivy and then hubby
had the great idea that we should take the old road down and try to find the
lookout. Well we missed the trail and ended up following the power lines
down. All the way to the highway. Then we had to go through the woods, no
trail, trying to get back to the trails. Eventually we found a place where
we could get out onto the highway and we had to call the park rangers to
come and get us. We were at least 5 miles from the park!
Lesson learned, STAY ON THE TRAILS!!!
We decided not to go back for Cool Cat because after being lost for an hour
and a half I just wanted home. We did go to the other park and get one
more.
If anyone has not been to Bastrop Park you are missing a treat. The
campgrounds were really nice, everything was clean and the trails were very
well marked (unless you decided to leave them)
I still cant figure out how it was uphill both ways!!

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-02-05 19:30:47 UTC-08:00
I hope it isn't true that Skinny Pine is missing!
That is a favorite of my family's, and the first of
Lightning's several letterboxing misadventures
happened while hunting that box. Without giving too
much away, he was crossing the stream there at the
waterfall and slipped and went over. It isn't a huge
waterfall, but he was a little guy - 8 years old and
short for his age. He was hanging on by his fingers
and Fearless Fox and I (we were on opposite sides of
the stream) quickly assessed which of us was closer
and in better position to grab him. FF wound up
grabbing him and pulling him up and I hauled him over
to the path side of the stream. It is rarely boring
to go letterboxing with him!

As for These Boots, that one was placed at the Bastrop
gathering and we never could find it. Lots of other
folks did - not us. Hope it's still around too
because I want to someday go back and prove I can find
it - this time. Doesn't sound promising though...

Lucy
