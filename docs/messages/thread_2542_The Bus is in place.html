The Bus is in place

From: gryzzgryph (gryzzgryph@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-01-27 01:20:39 UTC
The rain, ice, and bowl games being over (for now), the Original
Texas Tour Bus has settled down and is available for callers.
Therefore, find yourself in the home of Coach Gordon Wood's seven
time state champion Lions and proceed from there north by northwest
to a body of water named after that town. There you will find a
state park bearing the same name as the body of water. Enter that
park and find the trailhead near the entrance to the Council Bluff
campsite. Observe the mapboard at the trailhead -- you will be going
to marker #11. Start down the trail and continue along the top of
the ridge with the body of water on your right. Upon reaching marker
#6, the trail will narrow for a time before joining a wider path.
Shortly thereafter, the trail will turn sharply to the right and
drop suddenly among a giant's playground of blocks and walls. Follow
the trail as it hairpins back to the right and runs along a lower
shelf with the body of water on your left. Continue past markers 7,
8, 9, and 10 (most of which have their numbered signs on the ground
beside the posts). At marker #11 (which still has its number ON the
post) count 30 steps and see a large boulder on your right with a
smaller boulder to its left. In between, at the break, are several
trees. The Original Texas Tour Bus is parked at the base of the
smaller boulder, near the break, beneath the usual forest debris.
Upon obtaining the Bus, you may either retrace your steps or
continue down the trail to a barely legible marker #16, at which
point you will find a rocky path ascending. Consider that path to be
your "Stairway to Haven" and follow it up to a subsequent stairway
and then to a path that will return you to the original trail at
marker #2. Or, just continue down the trail to the fishing pier and
then take the return trail (located several yards down the road
leading away from the pier) to the Council Bluff campsite. Take the
opportunity to explore this interesting park and its unique
facilities.
Best regards, Gryzzled Gryphon of Coryell County
