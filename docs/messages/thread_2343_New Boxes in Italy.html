YES!!!!!!
 
We are actually renting a car now (instead of taking the train everywhere) so that we can search for the letterboxes in Italy!!
 
If any of you would have been with us on our last excursion to Sicily, where we had to rent a car...you would realize what a sacrifice I am making with my life!  Neither of us knew how to drive a stick and apparently, that was all they had at the rental place in Palermo.  The rental car agent gave us a 45 second tutorial in which she pointed to the stick, said "this is what you use to switch gears" and then handed us the keys.  By trial and error, the husband taught himself how to drive a standard.  Our poor car received a few scrapes along the way, we almost got the car stuck between two buildings in Agrigento, and the nail marks in the leather, where I clung for my life, are probably still there!

Re: New Boxes in Italy

From: Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter (janet@redbarnretreat.com) | Date: 2006-12-04 13:32:03 UTC
You are brave souls. Italian drivers are just crazy! Our daughter
and son-in-law did all the driving and they have gotten very good at
it, but even so, we knocked off a couple of mirrors on the narrow
streets. But, saying that, driving a car offers a lot more freedom
and the opportunity to see the REAL Italy by going places the
tourists don't go. You couldn't get to any of the letterboxes by
riding the train, except maybe the one in Pompeii. These boxes are
all in great locations and worth finding. The one called "Old World
Charm" is in a small medieval village on the top of a mountain and
the town and the view are spectacular. Good luck and have fun!
I'll try to post a few pictures today.

Boots Tex
