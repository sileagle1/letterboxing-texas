From: Silver Eagle (sileagle@alltel.net) | Date: 2004-04-28 16:59:22 UTC
I have seen the SPEEDBALL Speedy Stamp Basic Carving Kit at
Michaels Craft store, but I also found it online. Here is
just one link (or do a Google search on Speedball):

http://www.misterart.com/store/view.cfm?
cat_id=247&group_id=8634&store=003

It comes with two blades, a handle and some pink carving
medium. I also bought a smaller blade (#1) for detail work.
It is not linoleum cutters. Hope this helps.

Silver Eagle

From: bcostley (bobbyeubanks@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-04-28 23:45:31 UTC
I find that x-acto knives still wind up being better for detail in
some cases (e.g., lettering and other places where you need to make
tight curves). So while I certain recommend getting the speedball
cutters, keep the x-acto handy.
