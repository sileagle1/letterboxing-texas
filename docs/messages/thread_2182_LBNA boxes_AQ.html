From: the carrascos (a.carrasco@earthlink.net) | Date: 2006-09-25 20:59:58 UTC-05:00
As I am sitting here looking through my logbooks and deciding which boxes are in fact "Blue Diamonds" it occurred to me that there are many, many boxes in Texas that are not listed on AQ, especially some of the older boxes around.  The other day I had the pleasure of meeting a new Houston boxer, yippikiyo, and she made the comment that she usually looks for clues only on AQ.  I just wanted to remind the newer boxers out there that while there are many boxes listed on both sights, there are many more that are only on LBNA, so don't forget to look there for clues as well!  There are many "Blue Diamonds" there that won't have that icon, but are well worth looking for!  (And I am not at all opposed to the diamond deal, I think it will be very interesting to see what people think!)  MO5

From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-09-26 13:07:38 UTC
Exactly!

When people email and ask for help choosing boxes, I take into account
what they're looking for, how long they want to hike, how comfortable
they are driving around mega-opolis, whether they have kids. When I
ranked the boxes (and I did rank my finds; took all of 5 minutes), I
took into account SOLELY our experience. Just because I found them to
be great boxes/experiences, doesn't mean someone else would!

I don't think you can expect to ever get a completely "right" set of
rankings, because experiences are so subjective, people aren't all
ranking the same boxes, and they're not all using the same criteria.
HOWEVER, to me it's kind of fun.

For me, I started with a base of 3. I added points for any of the
following: great view, cool hike, interesting hiding place, unique
container, handmade or altered log, creative clues, beautiful carved
stamp, etc. I took away points if there were things that were
off-putting about the box. I felt free to be honest, because unlike
with online comments, the placer is not seeing the ranking. I
appreciate anyone's efforts, but let's be honest, some boxing
experiences are more rewarding than others.

I don't have a lot of ego about our boxes, so it doesn't bother me if
someone else ranks one I like a lot really low. It's supposed to be
fun and I plant for those who share our sense of humor, knowing full
well that's not the general public. Not all placers' boxes are going
to be right for everyone. So if I put a box out there that you hate;
that's okay, you're probably just selectively not going to look for my
boxes.

I don't think that it's anything to get all worked up about; people
really flew off the handle (change, change, change), without seeing
how it shakes out and if it's at all helpful to anyone. Me, I'm going
to wait and see.
