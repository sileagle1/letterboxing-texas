TEXAS TOUR BUS 2 has hunkered down!

From: Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter (janet@redbarnretreat.com) | Date: 2007-01-08 22:13:33 UTC
The last report had TTB2 going south on I-45, slowing down to exit at
New Waverly. Somewhere, the person driving the bus was unsure of
where to go, so picked up a pilot/navigator, which promptly turned the
Wrong Way, losing the motorcade. Taking over again, the rancher from
Central Texas turned left at New Waverly and headed east toward
Coldspring. Just before getting there, he turned south again and then
left into Double Lake National Recreation Area. He didn't have to pay
to get in, but I did. Bearing right, the bus went all the way to the
parking area, where it jumped a curb and went between the concession
stand on the right and the swimming hole on the left, then past the
ampitheater to the trailhead sign at the junction of trails. Just to
the left was a red, white and blue sign for the Lone Star National
Recreation Trail. Passing that sign it went 88 steps (I counted
them), to a downed tree lying perpendicular to the trail, then turned
right and followed that tree to where it crossed another log which was
running parallel to the trail. The driver got out and cleared a spot
to park at the junction of the two logs, then covered the bus with
slabs of bark. I don't know what's going on, but it looks like that
bus is hunkering down for a spell.
