From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-01-12 13:28:54 UTC-06:00

Hey Boots!

 

What your son gave you was a very specific Harry Potter definition, and when they referred to muggles in the series, they were referring to the non-magical folk as folks who were also uninformed.   That's where the reference comes in in letterboxing.

 

Geocachers readily adopted the term muggles to mean the uninformed public the ones who had no clue about geocaching.   Letterboxers wanted to use a different term, so it was tossed around on one of the national lists for awhile and the somewhat-of-a-consensus came to be we would use noxers (non-boxers).   Personally I really don't like that term because it sounds too much like the word "obnoxious" and I think it sets me in the mindset that the uninformed are obnoxious.   I would rather think of them as people who are to be pitied for their ignorance instead of disdained.  A fair number of boxers agree and choose to use the term muggles, and noxers has ever really only taken off in some areas, although most boxers who were around at the time of that discussion know what it means.   Boxers generally understand both terms to be those who are unaware of letterboxing.

Okay, so muggles are those who have never heard of letterboxing, but
happen to stumble upon one accidentally. The ones who don't respect
the box, like whoever destroyed the ones in Rusk near the long
footbridge, are not only to be pitied, but disdained. The ones who
stumbled upon my General Sherman box in Rusk State Park and signed
in it and complimented it and left it for the next "muggle", (for a
period of five months), are to be recruited! They're future
letterboxers, they just don't know it yet. We've only been aware
of letterboxing for about 15 months and have seen the activity boom,
at least in the eastern half of the state. What's it going to be
like in 3 or 4 years? Maybe we should all resolve this year to pick
a county that has no letterboxes and plan a special trip to plant
some there. Share the fun with those who are letterbox-poor.

BT
