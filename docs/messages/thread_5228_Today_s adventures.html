From: Mike Olbrisch (mike-2007@elp.rr.com) | Date: 2013-07-28 21:38:03 UTC-06:00

Yep - someplace here in the German souvenirs I have a box full of Volks-March medals from when I was a soldier over there.  Started to throw them out once, but didn't have the heart to trash them.    Haven't seen them in a few years... but they are around somewhere.  The ones I enjoyed most were the 100km bicycle volks-marches.

 

The only problem I see in combining the two hobbies is that letterboxing is a somewhat secretive operation, you try to avoid crowds.  Volksmarching involves several hundreds or even a few thousand people on a good weekend.  If the box is anywhere near the volks-march route, you will not be alone.

 

Mike - El Paso.

That is true about "regular events".  We now have Year Round Events (YRE) where you go to a start location listed, register, take a set of directions and set out on your own.  Perfect for letterboxing if you have down the YRE before and can figure out where the two might come together (or at least be close by).
 
For regular events I will pick a location to go to after doing the volksmarch - one that is on the way to/from my house and the event.
