Update on TPWD policy regarding letterboxing

From: Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter (janet@redbarnretreat.com) | Date: 2007-09-10 21:07:41 UTC
As you all know, I've been in contact with the Texas Parks and
Wildlife Department regarding their policy for letterboxing in State
Parks. I hadn't heard from Mr. Good lately, so I sent him an email
today to see if any progress had been made. He sent me the
following reply:

"I had to spend a couple of weeks in the field unexpectedly, with
limited email access, so I'm way behind on emails. I have drafted a
new policy that will include letterboxing and hope to get it
approved by the end of the month. I don't foresee any opposition to
this activity. Kevin Good"

As you can see, Mr. Good hasn't elaborated on what the new policy
might be, but I think we can assume it will be included with
geocaching as an approved activity with restrictions. I found the
following questions and answers on the official TPWD website
concerning geocaching which might indicate how they will also see
letterboxing:


Q: Is geocaching allowed in Texas State Parks?
A: Yes, geocaching can be a great way to enjoy parks and geocaches
can also add to the educational experience of site visits.

Q: What should I do if I want to place a geocache in a Texas State
Park or Historic Site?
A: First, visit with the park or historic site superintendent and
discuss the location where you intend to place the cache. Some parks
or historic sites may be entirely off-limits to geocaching due to
the sensitive resources contained within the sites. The
superintendent may limit placement of caches in some areas due to
archeological or historical resources, endangered species or
concerns for visitor safety. If the placement is approved, obtain a
copy of a geocache permit in order to complete the contact and
placement information.

Please feel free to make any comments or suggestions.

Boots Tex

Re: Update on TPWD policy regarding letterboxing

From: Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter (janet@redbarnretreat.com) | Date: 2007-09-11 14:33:04 UTC
I think hunting has a better lobby than we do and hunters spend more
money. If you get the TPWD magazine, you know that they are always
featuring articles about hunting and fishing. I've yet to see an
article about geocaching or letterboxing. They are heavily funded by
hunting and fishing licenses. Am I being cynical? I have asked Mr.
Good on two occasions to meet with us personally and, while he
hasn't rejected the request, he hasn't addressed it either. He's
just ignored it. I suggest that we wait and see what the new policy
has to say. If there are restrictions that we can't live with,
maybe then we could mount a campaign to see how many letterboxes we
can place in state parks legally. I mean, let's all go to a state
park and fill out permits and take up the rangers' time showing us
where we can hide boxes, then go ahead and place a lot of boxes. If
they end up being removed, consider them expendable. If you can, go
back and place more, within their rules. At least they would become
aware of letterboxing, and maybe even learn to appreciate it.

Boots Tex
