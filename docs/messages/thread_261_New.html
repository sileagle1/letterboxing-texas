From: Birds of a Feather Family (birdsofafeather_lbox@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-08-13 09:12:05 UTC-07:00
teamking,
 
Welcome to the group. 
 
Don't worry about carving the perfect signature stamp before you go out looking for letterboxes.  Some folks start off with a store bought stamp first.  Others have carved new signature stamps as their skills have developed.
 
Things to think about: 
  • bring a damp paper towel in a ziplock for cleaning the ink off the stamps
  • bring along box maintenance items - ziplock bags, etc in case the box that you find needs some attention
  • let the box owner know that you found the box and how it is doing. I also like to let them know who were some of the previous visitors.
  • be prepared for hazards of the area - hiking distance, water bottle, mosquitoes, snakes, etc.
  • Sign up for the http://groups.yahoo.com/group/newboxers/ talk list
As mentioned in a previous note, the best place for clues for Texas letterboxes is www.letterboxing.org.  The www.letterboxing.org site currently has 288 entries for Texas.
 
I have been gathering information for my Texas Letterboxing Atlas (shows letterboxes on a map) http://birdsofafeather.netfirms.com/cgi-bin/lbatlas.cgi and have identified 306 current and historic letterboxes for Texas.  With owner permission, I have listed 251 boxes. 
 
Some alternative sources for letterbox clues:
 
http://groups.yahoo.com/group/letterbox-basement/ - Older boxes of unknown status which no one claimed and reentered into the revised www.letterboxing.org database.  Has at least 8 Texas letterboxes listed.
 
http://www.lbworld.org -  Steve Sheeley's (Rayvenhaus) site. There are a 4 Texas boxes listed here - 3 of MonkeyToes and 1 of MissMoon. These 4 boxes are also listed in
www.letterboxing.org  Has some nice features but no easy way to list boxes in a state - they offer by zipcode. Future feature is search by location on the globe.
 
http://www.atlasquest.com/ - Ryan Carpenter's (rscarpen) new website. Currently only has 2 Texas letterboxes listed (Brett and Penny). Ryan has a nice advance search for specifying criteria including kid friendly, exclude boxes I planted, and exclude boxes I found.
 
http://www.letterboxingcentral.com/ - no Texas letterboxes.  This site states it is not attempting to compete with other clue sites.
 
Boxes not registered with clue sites:
 
The Mattke family distributed clues for a letterbox near Bastrop at the Texas Letterboxing Event but have never published clues that I know of.
 
Eoghan has a few letterboxes that are listed on the www.letterboxing.org site but has pulled clues for others. 
 
Several of the Texas Letterboxers also have created hitchhikers which have no set home. Some of their travels can be found on Wanda and Pete's Hitchhiker page http://alum.wpi.edu/~p_miner/travelers.html
 
See you on the trails!
 
Jeff (Sea Dove)
