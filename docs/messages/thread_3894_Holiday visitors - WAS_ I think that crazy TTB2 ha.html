From: Mike Olbrisch (mike-2007@elp.rr.com) | Date: 2009-01-03 07:20:50 UTC-07:00
Sounds like you had a nice visit.  Nice to have family around during the holidays.
 
No word from James since Christmas.  That's not unusual - he has been out of touch fot 6 weeks or more at times.  Scouts live on the front-lines....  phones - they don't need no stinkin phones!  Still - he is supposed to be back at Ft. Hood by 10 Jan 09 the last word we got.  We were going to travel to welcome him home.  He said not to bother.  It isn't like he is homesick to see us, and he would rather get off the plane and sleep than go out with us anyway.  He will be home soon enough - and for ever. We will keep our Christmas lights up till we hear he is home.
 
I am working on a Parker geocache.  It will have his engraved training collar in it, and some of his dog tags on the chain.  I think his chip tag and his therapy dog tag.  This cache will not be a trade cache - but rather others will attach a tag from a dog they lost to the chain, and tell about it on the web site.
 
I have not figured out how to make it a letterbox yet.  Other than a stamp of Parker.  Any ideas???  Names?  Remembering Man's Best Friend?  Parker's final cache?
 

Mike - KD9KC.
El Paso, Tx.

> After loitering at my house for several weeks, those crazy
passengers
> of the Texas Tour Bus 2 took off this morning in a big ole hurry! I
> thought I heard the bus driver say something about going to Austin,
> but wasn't sure I heard correctly. MeMa of 7 is still visiting from
> Baltimore, so she, SoccerChik and I jumped in my car to to give
chase.
>
> They headed west on I-10 for 40 miles or so, and made a quick side
> trip to San Felipe. I think the Ghost of Davy Crockett wanted to
say
> hello to some Old friends of his. Next, they veered back onto I-10
> and continued west to Columbus where they stopped at a regional
LCRA
> park to feed the kitties.
>
> We chased the bus as it turned northwest on Hwy 71, but lost it
when
> we stopped in Ellinger for some to-die-for kolaches at Hruska's
Store
> & Bakery, then swung into Rosemary's Vineyard (MeMa's first name is
> Rosemary and thought it would be cool to take home a bottle of
Merlot
> with "her" label).
>
> We thought we'd lost them for good, but as we neared LaGrange, we
> heard the unmistakable sounds of a home-grown Texas band
> (Uh...huh..huh..huh) and caught a glimps of the passengers downing
> some brewskies. Before we could even park the car, the were off
> again! I thought they made a wrong turn when they stopped at
another
> LCRA park just outside LaGrange to whistle at some scantily dressed
> ladies, but they only tossed something out the window and made
their
> way back onto Hwy 71.
>
> We didn't know where in the heck that bus driver was headed! About
> 35 miles southeast of Austin, they stopped at Kendra Macon's
> Roadhouse for some of the best burgers in the county. Between bites
> of a HUGE bacon cheeseburger, Willie mumbled something about
Houston
> Toads. I think that boy's been smokin' some funny stuff! After our
> long chase we were hungry too, so we rested for a spell on the
patio
> and enjoyed our grub.
>
> Next thing we knew, those crazy passengers ran out and jumped back
> onto the bus, zipping across the road into a park, and leaving me
> stuck with their tab! After settling up with Kendra, we tried to
> follow their path of destruction. Seems they almost mowed down some
> guys in a golf cart and ran through the entrance gate. The ranger
> was still shaking his fist at them as we came through. The way I
hear
> it, only two wheels of that bus were touching the ground as they
made
> a quick left onto Park Road 1A.
>
> As we made the corner, we saw some kids on bicycles pointing up the
> road toward an overlook in the northeast corner of the park. We
> followed - always keeping right on Park Road 1A. We parked at the
> overlook, and although we enjoyed the view, we didn't see that
darned
> bus anywhere!
>
> As we returned to our car, SoccerChik noticed a sign across the
road
> indicating that this park was the largest remaining habitat of the
> Houston Toad. Maybe Willie was onto to something after all. And
> guess what?!! There were tire tracks heading east past the sign,
and
> down the trail. We hurried down the trail ourselves, and soon came
> to a trail post declaring no fires or bikes allowed... it didn't
> forbid buses, which is good, cause those tire tracks were still
going
> east on the trail, down a hill. We continued for another 58 steps,
> passing two reflective trail markers nailed to trees. We were
nearly
> at the bottom of the hill and saw another of those reflective trail
> markers nailed to a tall pine tree on the left. We stopped there to
> catch our breath for a second. That's when we noticed the tire
tracks
> veered left into the forest. They seemed headed straight toward a
> pair of pine trees about 15 steps from the trail, but the tracks
> disappeared at the pine on the right, under the boughs of a large
> fallen pine tree that rested against it.
>
> I fear that crazy TTB2 has gotten lost in the loblolly pines! We
> were exhausted and decided that someone else would have to rescue
the
> passengers from their folly. We returned to our car and headed home
> to Houston.
>
> Hope ya'll had a festive New Year's too!
