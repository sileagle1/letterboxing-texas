From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-10-30 21:12:53 UTC-06:00
I still like using a good old-fashioned orienteering compass.  I don't do anything that requires such precision that I have to calculate declination, or that my declination has to be precise, and I trust myself to do my own readings more than I trust an electronic compass.  I KNOW Mike will come back with about 50 reasons why I'm being silly about that, and on most those points, he WILL be right, but that's still how I feel!.  So bring it on Mike....lol
 
I guess I've sort of made a hobby out of trying out different compasses - I can hardly go into an outfitters store without looking over the compass selection, and it seems that I really like Silva compasses overall.  One feature I am careful to look for is a needle that floats in fluid because it moves more smoothly, not jerky.  I also like one that has dayglo markings because it is easier to read as it is getting darker (over-40 eyes kick in about dusk).  I like one that has real clear map arrows and two red slashes and a red needle for settling north - I know this seems like aesthetic detail, but it makes it easy for me to quickly see that everything is lined up (again, over-40 eyes). 
 
I also am careful not to use a lanyard with a metal hook - instead I hook it through the hole and loop it back through so it is secure.  That way there is no chance the metal messes with the reading.  Again, probably a bit of overkill, but I threw a perfectly good compass away once because I thought it was reading badly, only to realize later that the lanyard I kept off the compass had a hook that had somehow become magnetized and was probably more the culprit than the compass itself was.
