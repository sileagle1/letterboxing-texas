Susan, I suggest that when you go to get your stuff to
carve, get plenty of material so you can practice,
practice, practice!

For my birthday in November, I asked my family for a
carving kit. My husband, in classic fashion, went all
out and bought me every kind of tool he could find,
and followed up at Christmas with even more. I have
more knives now than I will ever know how to use, but
it has been nice because I've gotten to try out lots
of different ones and figure out what I like. If I
were going to choose just one set though, I would go
with the speedball carving tips and a simple wooden
handle. Exacto does make a set of blades that seem to
be pretty good for detail work too.

As to material, I've so far tried Speedball's "pink
stuff" and "white stuff" and I've also tried
Mastercarve. I haven't really decided which I like
best, mainly because I can't tell if I don't like some
of the material because my carving technique is still
clumsy or because the material isn't that good.

The one thing I've learned very well is to not get
attached to a stamp until it is finished - that way I
don't feel bad about throwing it away and starting
over if I'm not happy with it.

Lucy

From: Wanda (wsthm@hotmail.com) | Date: 2005-08-07 20:25:44 UTC
My grandson, 'Cloud'...the boxer formerly known as Zach, then
Caballo Diablo, then Caballo... had a good idea for a letterbox
today and already carved the stamp this afternoon. It looks like a
10 year old carved the stamp, but I am really proud of it and the
idea he had. We plan to place it in Beeville, hopefully this week.

Also, I've got a letterbox I've been carrying around for some time.
I can't find a suitable place to put it. We took another drive to
Veteran's Memorial Park (200 acres!) in Beeville where I thought
surely I could place it, but came away disappointed. That park has
got to be the most patroled and mowed park I have seen anywhere. For
awhile I was going with a friend playing disc golf in the early
morning, and there were city and county employees swarming
everywhere, each time. Last week my daughter-in-law and grandsons
went with me around noon, and there were mowers and supervisors all
over the place. There is a creek that runs through the park, and
some gullies, so we have to avoid flood prone areas. It doesn't help
that the letterbox is slightly larger than normal, but I do hope to
get it planted eventually.

I finally attempted to re-carve my personal stamp, and even though
it is a bigger and simpler design that the one I did back when I
first started letterboxing, it is a mess. I am still going to try to
salvage it, but I don't know if I can or not. I seem to have 'lost
my groove' as far as carving goes. Probably because I haven't carved
much of anything in so long. This one wouldn't even make a good
abstract!

Speaking of that, I love that idea! Thanks Aisling and Joy! That
information may come in handy some day. I'd love a nice multiple
print of an artsy abstract stamp in my log.

Another little discovery came about a while back when I couldn't
find the Staedtler MasterCarve in the medium thickness I prefer. I
had to get the really thick (1"?) kind. It turns out that it is
thick enough to carve on both sides and sometimes, depending on the
design, you can carve on an edge too. So I've done 4 (I think it is)
carvings lately that either put together 'scenes' of sorts, or have
the image on one side and the name of the letterbox on the other
side.

So wish Cloud and I luck this week as we do a little box maintenance
and scout out locations for letterboxes. ;)

Busy Bee (Wanda)
