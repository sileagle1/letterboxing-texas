News from the Big Thicket and other Texas places

From: janetsredbarn (janetsredbarn@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-05-03 04:50:06 UTC
Monday morning Lone Star Quilter and I decided to take a letterbox
hunting trip. We had been feeding and pampering our wonderful and
beautiful quilting guests for 12 straight days and needed a break.
So we headed over toward the big thicket area, knowing full well
that we might be on a sight-seeing trip thanks to Hurricane Rita.
We attempted to find Baby Bear's Pitcher Plant near Warren, but are
reporting it as lost. Then we headed up to Martin Dies, Jr. State
Park at Jasper. The park is open now, but most of the trails are
closed. Henhouse Ridge camping area is open, but Hickory Ridge is
only partially open for camping. We found a trail that was not
marked as closed, but, as it turned out, probably should have been.
We actually found Hawk's Squirrelly Camper and DrooP's Wading Bird
letterboxes. The short bridge to the island was damaged, but had
been partially repaired, so we considered that an invitation to
cross it. A large pine tree was lying across the bridge and we had
to crawl under it, which we did. I didn't say we were smart, just
adventurous. The trail to Squirrelly Camper was blocked by a huge
tree, maybe 3 feet in diameter, which we climbed over. Next, the
top of a large fallen tree covered the trail and we had to make our
way through the woods around it. We found the box right where it
should have been, high and dry. Encouraged by the success, we
crossed the long bridge to find Wading Bird, which we did. It was
in good condition as well. All the small trees with the blue trail
markers on them were broken and lying on the ground. We did see one
alligator from the bridge.
Deciding we had pushed our luck, we headed south to Village Creek
State Park at Lumberton. Monday was the first day that park was
open since the hurricane and the damage was evident. All the trails
were closed and signs were posted prohibiting the use of the trails
under threat of prosecution. We decided those three boxes could
wait for another day. On to Anahuac to attempt to find Baby Bear's
Coastal Night Lights, but no luck there. We have reported it as
missing. Better luck with Silver Eagle's Anahuac Anhinga
Letterbox. Although it had not been reported as found since June
2004, it was sitting right where it belonged and was in great
shape. The Anahuac National Wildlife Refuge is a wonderful place to
visit. This is a coastal marsh area and is abounding with birds of
every description. We were there just before dusk and we saw a
dozen or more different types of birds. Thanks to Silver Eagle for
taking us to this beautiful place. You should all make it a point
to find this letterbox. Then, it was on to Galveston to spend the
night and rest up for another day of 'boxing. We found Hawk's
Celtic Cross, Lucy Locket's Water Can, Dewberry's Pirate's Puzzle,
Team King's Tribute to England Series #7, Quetzel's State Tall Ship,
and Hawk's Sea Aggie. Hawk's Cavalla letterbox is missing. We
attempted to find Celtic Lion's Oleander Island without success.
Having found only 9 boxes in the better part of two days, we decided
to up our count by finding the Runaway Circus Series by Birds of a
Feather. It was almost on the way home. We got there about 4:30
and knew we would have to hurry. The gate had a padlock and chain
but no closing time was posted. We found all 8 boxes as well as
Dewberry's Time Travelers with its 2 boxes and Runsforchocolate's
box, Seven. We had just replaced all the boxes when a security
guard showed up and chased us out so he could lock up. We ended up
with 21 finds for the trip, so were well rewarded. Thanks to all
you guys out there for placing all the great letterboxes for us to
find. Happy Trails!

Boots Tex
