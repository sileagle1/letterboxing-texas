4th Annual TX LB Event

From: Baby Bear (babybearlb2002@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-03-06 17:13:55 UTC
Wow! I had so much fun, the weekend was over in a flash. Friday
pushing Silver Eagle and Clueless Collector to get loaded up so we
could get our campsite before dark. Enjoying conversation with
Barefoot Lucy and Lightening until late into the night. Up in the
morning Saturday(6:30am Clueless) to hide our boxes and find some pre-
hidden ones. Then over to the Pavilion to light a fire and warm the
chili. Letterboxers arriving ongoing from 9am to the last one (Runs
for Chocolate) at noonish. Fun with the trivia and demonstration.
Off to find more boxes in the park (still missed one). Back at
5:30pm to eat again, and talk the rest of the night!! Finally shut
off the lights in pavillion at 9:30pm. COLD CAMPING! Up early on
Sunday, pack up, find a few more boxes, then off to Independence and
Washington-on-the-Brazos to find more boxes (fun running into other
letterboxers at the park). Then over to Fanthrop Inn, and to
Hempstead for Silver Eagle to hide a box, then took them home. Raced
home to give LSS attention for being gone all weekend.

Thank you to all who attended, for the food, letterboxes,
conversation, and your friendship. I really enjoyed having time to
talk to everyone, and getting to know you better!!! Until we meet
again, keep hiding and searching, and stay away from the bees and
Park Rangers! And to my brother, Silver Eagle, you deserve most of
the credit for another enjoyable weekend!!

Baby Bear
from Cypress, Texas

From: (kacmbc@aol.com) | Date: 2007-03-06 12:38:44 UTC-05:00
To echo the sentiments of everyone else, a big thanks goes out to Baby Bear and Silver Eagle for organizing the event on Saturday.  What a beautiful day at Sommerville!  It was great visiting with familiar faces and meeting new ones.  After the letterboxing trivia game, many ventured out to find the planted letterboxes around the park.  Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter were kind enough to let me travel with them, and we had a fabulous time out there searching for and finding the boxes! 
 
Just as we were about to set out on our adventure, M-Dot, my LSS showed up on his bike ( he biked from Houston to the park) so we visited with him for a few minutes.  He apparently napped in a spot of sun for a couple of hours afterwards while we were out letterboxing but did manage to be somewhat social after the nap!  As the sun started to set and temperatures began to drop, we packed up the car and headed home.  The conversation in the car centered around the day and how much fun we both had at Sommerville.  Michael didn't do the letterboxing part of the day, but he really enjoyed the conversation with others around the big camp fire.  Our Texas letterboxing friends are truly some of the nicest people around. Thanks again for a wonderful day!!
 
Runs for Chocolate  :)
