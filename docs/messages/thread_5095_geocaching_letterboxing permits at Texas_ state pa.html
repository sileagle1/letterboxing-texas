If you ask me, this is a vast improvement over the previous form. For one thing, it gives a space for a physical description of the location vs having to give either GPS or make notes on the back of the form or attach the clues themselves. For another, it does take away the 1 year requirement - that's been a hang-up for alot of placers over the recent years as we've hashed and rehashed the permitting process. I also like the fact that they've made them guidelines and not rules - it doesn't indeed allow the parks some leeway in enforcement.

I have a feeling they realized that by making hard and fast rules that didn't apply well to some parks, and by having the 1 year expiration, they were imposing a lot of extra work on park personnel and rather than doing the work, the parks were simply ignoring the requirement altogether unless they had experienced issues in sensitive areas. And I think making it a guideline serves to allow the placer to feel that they can be a part of the process rather than being dictated to with onerous requirements.

In my opinion, all good moves by TPWD. I've understood from the beginning that they want to make sure they are protecting their parks, but I also understand letterboxers who have stayed under the radar because the premise of permitting runs counter to the covertness of the game.
