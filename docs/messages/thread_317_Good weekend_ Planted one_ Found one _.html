From: CelticLions (celticlions@comcast.net) | Date: 2004-09-12 18:52:08 UTC-05:00
Sometimes it makes me dizzy to think about living in Connecticut and
having a choice of hundreds of boxes to find each weekend!
We were pretty thrilled to find ONE - Silver Eagle's Texas Star - we
have meant to drive down to Fair Park for a while, but never got around
to it.
It was a little unnerving to be able to see the letterbox from about 30
feet away! Yikes! What a vivid reminder that we all need to try to
re-hide with wind, rain and animals in mind!
There were lots of Fair Park employees milling around, setting up for an
event, so I posed by the hiding spot while my husband snapped away photo
after photo as I covered it very thoroughly with my left hand hidden by
a backpack....those folks must have thought we were demented tourists or
an incredibly old besotted honeymoon couple...;-)

Planted our first in a series of "Texas Bronze" boxes - THE SHAWNEE
TRAIL - it's in Pioneer Park in downtown Dallas near the Dallas
Convention Center (where our youngest was showing off his school's Solar
Car in the Technology Expo this weekend) - a very picturesque place with
over 40 bronze longhorns on a cattle drive.
A couple of folks on this list have offered to beta-test the clues, so
we'll be sending them out privately first, but if you plan a trip to
Dallas, please write & ask for them.

Hope everyone else had a great weekend!
