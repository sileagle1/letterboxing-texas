Boxing Adventure

From: Mary Jo (mjrekedal@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-08-12 16:16:03 UTC
So Bird Dog and I decided to venture out in the hot Texas afternoon
to locate a new box, Love & Friendship. Back pack loaded and water
bottle filled, we arrived at park and found hiding spot bench only
mildly sweaty. But said bench was occupied with a couple enjoying
their own form of love and friendship! We dauddled, admired the
surroundings, examined the flora with intense interest, even
considered feigning exhaustion with need to sit on the other end of
the bench (am I that duplicitous? Yes, I do confess!) But finally a
family with boisterous boys came along, shouting, running, jumping--
activity I would normally frown upon, but inwardly welcomed that day.
Friendly Couple decided to move on to a more secluded bench. And
boisterous boys ran off in search of more adventure farther along.
Retrieved box, admired a truly gorgeous carving, learned some
Japanese word origins (thanks for that added bonus, AnnaBee), and
ready to re-hide. When along comes Family B with baby in stroller, a
Bird Dog magnet. He loves babies even more than I. He admires baby
and strikes up conversation with mommy while daddy takes numerous
pics of surroundings. I sit patiently, again feigning interest. Oh,
the subtleties of l'boxing! Family B finally moves along and I can re-
hide unobserved. Day's count: one box in one hour, consuming one
bottle of H2O. And every minute worth it all!
Happy boxing,all, even in the heat!
Bloomin Gramma Jo

From: Vicki (vicki@bellmotel.com) | Date: 2008-08-13 05:15:52 UTC
Last April, my husband (Whiz Gee) and I went to find the box "Sakura"
in the same park, and discovered that Sakura's hiding place is
apparently the number one spot in Austin to get one's picture taken
for prom. After we had found the box and opened it, we endured wave
after wave of badly tuxedoed/shimmery dressed teenage couples, posing
on the bridge in front of us with their parents snapping picture
after picture. 10-20-30 -- we started wondering if they had all come
on a bus! Still, we stubbornly claimed our bench, waiting for a
break in the action so we could re-hide the box. After 20 minutes or
so, we called Lionsmane, the planter of the box, and described what
was happening as it happened, including a fashion description of each
outfit. We all couldn't help laughing our heads off. Finally, the
coast was clear for a brief moment, and Whiz Gee quickly re-hid the
box. As we walked back through the park, we encountered at least
another 30 teens headed for the bridge! The second bus, maybe? We
wondered if Lionsmane should alter his clue to read, "Avoid prom time
of the year"! Definately one of our funnier finds! -- Bell Motel
