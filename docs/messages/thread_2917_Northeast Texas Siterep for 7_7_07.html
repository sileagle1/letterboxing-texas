Northeast Texas Siterep for 7/7/07

From: tigger32167 (tigger32167@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-07-09 02:51:43 UTC
About a month ago, my cousin, Wagtime, and I decided that we were
needing to go on one of our letterboxing marathons. So we set the
date as Saturday, July 7 (7/7/7) and what a beautiful day it turned
out to be after all the rain that had taken place. We started out
in Jacksonville at 6:30 am and headed to Uncertain to Caddo State
Park to pick up two Blue Butterfly boxes which was the Caddo Lake
series. Both boxes were in great condition and no problem in
finding them. If you have never been to this park, it is
really beautiful, especially around the lake area. We then headed
to the town of Uncertain, to pick up the `Uncertain" box, which we
found with no problem, except we were met by a big black dog that
alerted his owner to us (we were at the dead end of a road with a
house next door). The owner came outside on his porch to see what
his dog was barking at and then he proceeded to keep an eye out for
what we were doing. Finally Wagtime made friends with the dog and
while Wagtime looked in the trunk for some mysterious item, I was
able to sneak the box back to its location. Upon leaving Uncertain,
we noticed that on the side of the road there was a photo
opportunity by a building with a mural of a smiling frog and an
alligator that said "Where the H*** is Uncertain, Texas?" So
needless to say we did take a picture. A little further down the
road was a building that was called Hog Wallow with 2 hog heads and
a motorcycle. We then proceeded to Jefferson to the public boat
ramp to get Silver Eagle's "Skritz" box and we were quite concerned
about the box because the water was covering the entire parking
lot...however good news, the box was in place and not in danger. At
the boat ramp there is also a beautiful old train trestle that goes
over the water. We then proceeded to find "The French Connection"
box which we were able to find...but I won't give any hints for it
since it is a mystery box. The next box that we looked for was "The
Haunted Letterbox" which is at the most haunted site in Jefferson
(this is also a mystery box, so won't give any clues to that) but we
did find it and it is a wonderful stamp. By the way, Jefferson does
give haunted tours on Friday and Saturday nights at around 8pm, if
you are interested). Our next stop was at the Daingerfield State
Park. There are 2 boxes listed here, one is a series that is a 2
1/2 mile walk that we decided to wait until fall/winter to attempt.
We did attempt the "Danger" box, but we think we took the wrong
trail so we decided to do some additional research and find
it when we go back for the series. Our next stop was Omaha to look
for Barefoot Lucy's "Tag" box. We finally managed to find the
historical marker that it was located at and after diligently
searching around the tree that I was certain it was at, I finally
managed to pull it out of a hole with about 2-3 inches of water in
it...good news, lock n lock boxes are water tight and the contents
were totally dry. By the way if you go to look for this box,
be sure and watch out for the thorn tree that seems to jump out at
you..I ended up with a nice little sticker in my finger. We then
headed towards Texarkana. On the way we saw a couple of people on
horseback and as we got closer to them, we realized that it was
actually a wagon train with 3 covered wagons, with a sign that
read "Texarkana, Texas to Tombstone, Arizona or Bust." So kind of a
neat site. We made it to Texarkana to find the "Aggie Bridge Troll"
box, however we did not know exactly where the college was located
so we drove around and ended up going into Arkanasas for a little
while before finally stopping and asking for directions. Once we
made it to the college, finding the box was no problem and it was in
great condition. Our next town was De Kalb, and picked up
the "Hoss" box by Silver Eagle which is located at the grave of Dan
Blocker who played Hoss for many years. We then realized that we
were going through a section of Titus county and realized that this
was a virgin county...so a trip through Mt Pleasant was in order to
place a box. While we were going through this town, we did come
upon a large yellow building with lots of junk around it,
called "The Garage Sale." We didnt stop, but decided to add that to
our list of places to come back to. We did manage to place a box in
Mt Pleasant, so Titus is no longer a virgin county. We then managed
to find the "Seven Gables" box in Mt Vernon which was a really neat
location. Next we started towards Pittsburg. On the way, we
noticed the Pilgrim's Pride Distribution Center with a huge bust of
Bo Pilgrim. We had to stop and actually take a picture. Down the
road from there we noticed a huge estate with the Pilgrim's Pride
logo on it...not sure if that is where Bo lives...but Bo certainly
knows chicken. In Pittsburg we managed to find Trail Cat's "Going
on a Mouse Hunt" series at a neat old cemetery. We then made it to
Gladwater to pick up the "Reel-em-In" Box. When we got to the
location of the box, we again had to use some stealth to retrieve
the box since there were some close by picnicers who did not seem at
all interested in what we were doing. The box was totally exposed
but we did manage to stamp in and tried to hide it as best we
could. We were now approaching 7:15 pm and decided to skip Kilgore
and Longview for now. We instead made our way to Overton to pick up
Blue Butterfly's "Overton Mustang" box. When we got to the park,
there were 4 kids playing at the park...no parents. We made our way
to the bridge and on the side of the bridge was the lid to the
letterbox. We were both extremely concerned, but I went ahead and
checked the location for where it should have been and the rest of
the box with stamp and log book were right where they should have
been and in perfect condition. We stamped in and put the lid back
on and rehid. Next we made it to New London and Wagtime picked up
my "New London Explosion" box. It was also totally exposed but we
were able to take care of that and the contents were safe and
sound. While we were there we did notice a log book that was laying
near it. So if anyone visited this site and is now missing their
log book, please let me know. because I have it. At this point we
were really losing daylight, but wanted to let Wagtime pick up
Blue Butterfly's "Joe Roughneck," however when we got to the
location, it was obvious that someone else had been looking for it,
and the box was no where to be found.

Boxes planted: 1 by JB Kokopelli
Boxes found: 13 by JB Kokopelli
15 by Wag Time (+ found 1 HH)
Hours on road: approximately 15
Miles traveled: 505
Total Trip....PRICELESS

Good job with the report, cousin! 
This was a very long, but very productive, route and we had a blast on it.  Wonderful locations, wonderful stamps, and although there was a lot of steam and humidity, not a single drop of rain fell on us.  That part really felt strange after daily storms for 2+ weeks.   I had packed an umbrella, rain poncho, extra t-shirt, and at least 2 extra pairs of socks (anticipating wading through creeks or really big puddles) and needed none of it.  We were completely worn out by the time we got back to J'ville around 9pm.    
 
Now the real question is, how nuts are we to be planning another one of these treks this coming weekend????
 
WagTime
