Mission Tejas boxing trip

From: Christine Borgfeld (christine@borgfeld.net) | Date: 2013-06-08 22:26:05 UTC-05:00

I spent yesterday at Mission Tejas State Park, and I’m sorry to say I was only able to find one box: Puddle Splasher’s Dogwood box.  The others were clearly missing or the landmarks were gone and we couldn’t find the right spots.  The boxes with clues going around the pond were the most obviously missing.  There is no ground cover in this area and  all that remains of the stumps are holes in the ground.  Logs which were supposed to have boxes were typically charcoal.  I’d say the boxes in this area were lost to fire but most of the landmarks were still recognizable. 

 

On the other side of the park (by the El Camino Real), I had a lot of trouble following clues and finding landmarks.  The signs have clearly changed and the trails don’t match the maps or the clues.  So some of these boxes may be there but we didn’t have any luck finding them.   There were a couple we missed looking for and frankly I was too fed up to go back for them this morning.   It was a discouraging trip – we had much better luck in Memorial Park!  Anyway, here is a list of the boxes we looked for:

 

Assault by Luck Locket – missing (found the stump and remains of logs but no box)

Indian Trails Quilt Block Series by LSQ – missing (found tree and rock)

Wearing the Cinco Peso by Boots Tex – missing (found the very cool barbed wire fence which is only supported by the trees which grew up around it.  We went way up the hill and checked every log we could find)

Texas Caddo Indians: Fire by blue butterfly – missing (found tree right where it was supposed to be)

Texas Heritage: Eugene C. Barker by lionsmane– missing (this whole area is cleared of ground cover

Pretty Kitty by Atoy Adventurers – the only landmark we could find was post #5, signs and benches referenced are gone

Dutch Oven Delight by Corazon – not quite sure where this is supposed to be but we found a geocache looking for it

Rachel’s Gift by LSQ – No luck finding the strange looking tree.  Several trees have fallen in this area so it may be gone

Tejas by Baby Bear – missing (not much left of the charred log)

Pop it up by Bailey’s Bunch – couldn’t find the right spot, there were no trails to the left only a clearing for the powerline and we didn’t find it there (FYI – this had a find in 2012)

A Caddo Legend “Coyote and the origin  of Death” – trails don’t match clues (although it has a find in 2012)

Dogwood box by Puddle Splasher – found it!

Top-Down Texas by Puddle Splasher – missing (found remains of stump)

Tejas Mission Impossible by Silver Eagle – missing (stump is now a VERY deep hole)

Birdie, Birdie by bailey’s bunch – missing (no recent fires in this area, the whole area is covered with sweet gum trees about 6 feet tall)

 

Regards,

Open Space

From: Boots Tex (redbarn@eastex.net) | Date: 2013-06-09 12:24:36 UTC
It's amazing how much a park can change in just a few years, isn't it? It's sad that the boxes are gone, but we have to remember that this is a natural process. It's an opportunity to go back and place new boxes! Mission Tejas holds some good memories for me. In 2007 we started a tradition of making a camping trip with our son and his family, our grandchildren, and we chose Mission Tejas State Park. When we checked in, along with the trail map, they gave us a list and clues to the five geocaches that were in the park. The next day, we all carved a stamp and made letterboxes, then went to the office to ask permission to place our in the park. The lady ranger had never heard of letterboxing, but she was immediately excited about it and called in the park manager. I gave them the LbNA website and she looked it up and they thought it was a great hobby and said we could place as many as we wanted, no forms to fill out, no permit required, so we planted our first boxes. Later in the day, one of the rangers, an older man, came around to the trailer and gave the three grandkids bug collection boxes and explained how to catch the bugs and told them to be sure to release them every night and catch new ones the next day. Too bad more parks are not as people-friendly as Mission Tejas. We'll probably place new boxes there. Thanks, Open Space, for the wonderful report. Don't look at it as disgusting, just as an opportunity. We're leaving next week for our annual camping trip. We're going to Dinosaur Valley SP for a few days then out to Fort Davis for a week or so. Hoping to find a few boxes along the way and maybe place some.

Boots Tex
