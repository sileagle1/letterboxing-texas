The Pink Stuff

From: Boots Tex (redbarn@eastex.net) | Date: 2010-09-01 16:03:09 UTC
When I first started carving, I tried the material that was available at Michaels, which was the pink stuff, the gray, crumbly stuff (don't ever use it!) and Master Carve. I liked Master Carve at first but when I started doing more detailed carving, it didn't seem to work as well and didn't hold up. Then, I found PZ Kut from Stampeaz and both the white and orange types worked very well for me, and they were cheaper than the pink stuff, so I've been using them ever since. However, Stampeaz had to stop making both because some chemical in the formula was banned by the government. They've experimented with other formulas, but I haven't tried it yet. I ran out of my supply of PZ Kut recently, except for scraps, so I decided to give the Pink Stuff another try and I find that I really like it! It's a little softer than PZ Kut, but I carved one of my detailed portrait stamps on it and it seems to work fine, so I guess I just didn't give it a chance at first. If you buy it at Michaels, be sure to print out a 40% OFF coupon. I found one easily online. The coupon makes it cheaper than the Stampeaz price for the same thing and you don't have to pay the shipping. The 6" x 12" sheet, with coupon and tax, is about $11.00 and you can get 18 2" x 2" stamps out of it.

Boots Tex

I've always been a fan of the pink stuff. It has held up forever for my signature stamp so I know it's durable. Never tried the white PZ cut but tried the orange and didn't like it, it did not take a pencil transfer well and that was mostly what I was doing. I felt like it didn't hold the ink well either but I probably should have tried sanding it. In any case, I just tried the new stuff from Stampeaz and really am hooked on it now because it takes a lovely iron on transfer. Finally I can get good detail. It's very firm though. I'll probably use the new stuff for detailed images I want to transfer with an iron and use the pink stuff for pencil transfers.
