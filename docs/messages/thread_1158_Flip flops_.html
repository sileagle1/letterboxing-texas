Flip flops?

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-08-01 19:00:47 UTC-07:00
> Got to stop wearing flip-flops on the trail!).

****Oh my! I truly, truly spend much of my life
barefooted, but I DO wear shoes on the trail!

A few months ago my family was on a really nice trail
and heading back toward the car when my son decided he
had to go to the bathroom, so he double-timed it out
of the woods. He had begged me to let him take his
shoes off (and truthfully, I was on the verge of
taking my own off too because it was a beautiful day
and the trail was so clear), but I had told him to
keep them on.

As we walked on, our daughter was slightly ahead and a
movement on the trail caught her attention - a snake
striking repeatedly at her! It was a very small snake
- a baby really - but it's back had been broken and it
was scared and striking at anything that moved. As we
examined it, we realized it was a baby copperhead! My
son had apparently stepped on it as he ran past.

That was enough to convince me - keep those stinking
shoes on, no matter what!

Lucy

From: samuraistampers (samuraistampers@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-08-02 04:03:23 UTC
ok, y'all,
all this talk of flip flops and fire ants and snakes just convinced
me I have to share my "shoeless" adventure I had last week in Port
Aransas.
First of all, I planted a box there as I noticed before I left for
there, that none of the boxes previously planted there have survived-
I hope mine fares better-we'll see.
Then, my son and I were boogie-boarding in the Gulf. I kept
remembering as I bobbed in the warm waters that the newspaper
reports of shark attacks always mention that the victim was in waist-
deep water..........................gulp! So, while I was busy
watching for fins breaking the surface I didn't notice the GIANT
JELLYFISH at my feet! 30- seconds later I was running for shore,
begging my son to PEE ON MY FOOT!!!!!! as I had heard that was a
last resort for the pain if you didn't have any vinegar (as I
didn't). Well, needless to say..............I had to endure the
pain for the next hour or so as my son was NOT going to do that
(even behind the dunes!) "Mom, that is so gross!"

On another note-I just want to reiterate how much I love this
group! We took something that could have resulted in a flame war on
the big list (criticism of artistic stamp carving abilities) and
made it into a forum for uplifting and wonderful quotes and
philosophies on life and creativity and trying your best!

Keep on boxing!
-Kantexan

"What would you attempt in life if you were guaranteed not to fail?"
