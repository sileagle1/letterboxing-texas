I've said it before and I'll say it again - you have a wicked streak MO5!  Fabric scraps!
 
Fabric isn't something I much use with Cub Scouts, but I keep all sorts of scraps on the eventuality that I will have a life outside of scouts one day - I even buy remnants at Walmart that I really like - because I have this idea that I can decoupage with fabric.  I used to sew quite well when I was younger (actually took sewing lessons!) and loved going to the store to pick a pattern and select a fabric, then prepare the fabric to sew.  The smell of the fabric running through the sewing machine was wonderful because the sewing machine would eventually warm up and make that smell that much more distinctive.  Now the extent of my sewing is stapling two pieces of fabric together if I have to.
 
Last night when She Who Reads and I were in Hobby Lobby, we strolled through the fabric section so I could buy a yard of brown felt to wrap stamps in.  While we were waiting to get it cut, two ladies were ahead of us with some exquisite silky stuff and they were very animated in their discussion of some decorating idea for a wedding in which that fabric was to be used.  I turned to SWR and said "Does it seem a little ironic that we are standing next to these women who are buying fabrics for a beautiful wedding and we are buying felt so that I can hide it in the woods?"
 
And on that note, does anyone remember qiana?

Time for me to chime in too on this one. I am the queen of too much
craft stuff! I have so many scraps and containers of all kinds it
takes TWO rooms to hold them. One is my stamp office - yeah, a whole
room for stamping! Paper racks and file cabinets and an entire tall
cupboard for nothing but STAMPS! All sorts of markers and ink pads
and scrapbook acccesories galore. Who needs a fancy dining room
anyway. I got so addicted that I had to make stamping into a job!
Now those cool beans spiral binding machine, laminating macines, and
die-cutters are TAX writeoffs, yippee. Brownie troop needs a last
minute craft project? No problem and certainly no need to visit a
store.

Then there is my Quilt Studio also known as The-room-overflowing-with-
fabric-and-other-miscellaneous-craft-stuff. I have 3 machines, a
flannel wall for projects in progress and a huge floor to ceiling
stash of quilt fabric. My dear sweet husband even built me a
cutting table storage unit just like one a pro quilter had in her
book! Who needs guests anyway. They can just sleep in one of the
kids beds right?

And sadly, I'm a sucker for boxes, jars, containers, and tins of all
sizes too. I have about 47 formula cans in the scary room just
waiting for a project. Plus an entire bin full of AOL CD cases,
Altoid tins, and International Coffee tins, that I just can't bear to
pitch. And those really cool Laughing cow cheese wedge circle
boxes. Oh the fun!

Thank goodness for Flylady. She has kept me in somewhat control.
She says you can't organize clutter. True, but my craft goodies
aren't clutter!!!!!

Sister craft collectors UNITE!
