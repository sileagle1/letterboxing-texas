And Now, The Rest Of The Story

From: Silver Eagle (sileagle@alltel.net) | Date: 2005-06-23 03:08:56 UTC
I have tried to piece together the history of letterboxing
in Texas, and this is what I have found so far:

The first letterbox in TX was Duck Creek #1 in Dallas, placed
by someone named Julie on August 16, 1998. She ended up
placing several more Duck Creek boxes, but all were gone by
the time I started boxing (I confirmed this myself).
The next people to come onto the Texas letterboxing scene were
Eoghan and Pumpkin Pricess in the year 2000. They lived in
Austin and placed several boxes in that area in 2000 and 2001,
but moved to Colorado before I started boxing. Eoghan's
Lost Pines box, placed on 11/04/00, is currently the oldest
confirmed letterbox still alive in TX.
There were a few other boxes in Texas when I started, but
they were placed by non-Texans passing through. These include
3 in Brownsville and 2 in Hueco Tanks. I have confirmed that
all 3 in Brownsville are missing, but have not tried the ones
in Hueco Tanks. The clues can be found in the letterbox basement:

http://groups.yahoo.com/group/letterbox-basement

Eventually, other TX letterboxers started showing up in 2003,
and Barefoot Lucy started the TX Talklist when there were only
a handful of us. We then had our first Event in March, 2004,
where 35 letterboxers showed up, surprising me to no end.

Even though letterboxing has grown way more than I ever expected
in Texas, I still get a kick out of the occasional note
I get from a new letterboxer way out in some remote part of
Texas that found one of my letterbox seeds and got hooked.
And that's the rest of the story.

Silver Eagle
