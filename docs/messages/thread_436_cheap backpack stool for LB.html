Great idea for letterboxing. I have found that my
"letterboxing kit" has grown from being just a small
fanny pack with my stamp, pen, ink pad, and log, to
include a 3-ring notebook (for clues), a tupperware
container (with emergency box-making supplies), candy
box (with microboxes to plant), and an occasional
full-size letterbox to plant. Add to that my camera,
"snake stick" (actually a monopod for my camera), and
a water bottle, and I really need more backpack room!

As far as a Kansas letterboxing group, I'm not sure if
they have an organized on-line group. I have had some
emails with Okiebookworms@yahoo.com, as they did some
boxing in Kansas and seemed to have some contacts. If
you're headed that way, check out the boxes I planted
a couple of weeks ago!

Hawk

How fun to be mentioned for help (we're also known
as "OkieBookworms") since I've been listening in lately. We're
hopefully going to get to come down next weekend to DFW to box.

I don't know of any talklists for Kansas. My brother lives there
and he usually uses the main talk list. Wes who runs the lbna
website lives in the Kansas City area.

There are many in the Kansas City area. Check ahead for some of the
ones in Wichita. Some of them have been missing but are still on
the list. I'm pretty sure they list their missing status on the
clues.

I can recommend the boxes that Hawk placed around Cottonwood Falls,
KS. It was a beautiful area with a surprisingly interesting
National Park.


Have fun!

You could e-mail one of the placers in the area and they can give
you some clues as to good finds. (My brother and sister-in-law
are "RyanandAllison.")
