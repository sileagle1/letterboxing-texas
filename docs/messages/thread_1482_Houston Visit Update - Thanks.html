Houston Visit Update - Thanks

From: Lightnin Bug (rpboehme@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-11-18 12:20:09 UTC
I just wanted to thank the local who sent me emails regarding my
business trip to Houston this week. This would include Silver Eagle,
Team King and Dewberry, who provided some recommendations.

I did manage to find a little time to box, but was mostly kept busy
with work things. Here are some comments on the boxes attempted and
found:

Nov. 13 - "Houston Heros" - What a touching place and cool box. The
innards were a bit wet (cracking container), but the baggie did keep
the business stuff dry.

Nov 14-15 - too busy to box and Texas size thunderstorms!

Nov 16 - Lunchtime - San Jacinto Monument area - Did not seek "San
Jacinto" because the park admins are letting the landscape revert to
a high grass prarie, so whatever path existed is no longer there.
Did not feel like bushwhacking at the time. Was able to amble out to
find "Tickling the Ivories". Tried for "Battleship Texas" but
failed. The spot is pretty wide open and I think it got muggled.
Did manage to hide the "TX Corundum Conundrum" micro box there. The
stanp is a crystal form of a ruby/sapphire gemstone. Have fun
searching.

After Work - Headed up to near Intercontinental airport to find some
dangerous critters. Found "Scary Scorpion" and "Sstep Safely"
without a problem, but could not find "The Hills are Alive" (many,
many newly fallen pine needles) or "Gator Watcher", though I am sure
I had the proper fallen tree. I did not seek "Big Bug", as I was
running out of daylight.

You guys have showed me the light in the proper creation of Micro
Boxes. I think I almost got it right with the one I placed for
y'all, but realize I can change one thing. I used a length of cash
register paper tape, while most of the boxes I found has little books
created.

Thanks for the fun and pressure release during my business trip. I
hope that I can meet up with one of youze guyz (revealing my origins
here) next time I come to Houston.

Take care,

LB
