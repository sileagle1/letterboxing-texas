From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2011-06-12 22:02:15 UTC-05:00

I'm so glad everyone had fun at yesterday's event! I had a great time planning it, especially since I had a "team" this year - my Mom, NacTrailCat (this IS her correct trail name!), was great help for those midnight (or later) moments when I woke up with ideas and needed some help figuring things out - a quick email to her and she came through with ideas! She came up with several of the "quest ideas" and the images that I carved. Draconis viridis, who has been my constant letterboxing compadre since we started this insanity, was his normal laid-back calm self who understands letterboxing and letterboxers AND his mom - I would bounce ideas off him and he would say "Interesting", or "Ummm...Mom?" or "Nice!" And Happy Feet, SJZ's boyfriend who has only recently been introduced by us to letterboxing but who seems to have gotten a handle on it and was ready and willing to help any way I asked of him. He had a great time yesterday! Happy Feet and d'vir were both on the ready to jump in the car with letterboxers if they needed help finding parks or locations, or to go bail someone out if they needed some help at the festival, or whatever was needed.

I thought that maybe those of you who weren't there would like to hear a little about the event: The premise this year was a scavenger hunt followed by a treasure hunt. Letterboxers participated as teams and had to work together to achieve 12 "quests" (list following) to earn the clues to five letterboxes. In the past, I've hidden boxes in the festival area, but we had so many letterboxers originally RSVP'd for the event that I was hesitant to hide boxes for so many people to find in such a high traffic area. SO - boxers had to work their quests at the festival, then were able to leave and find boxes in the community.

Here's a list of the list of quests:

  1. Find the event registration table there you go, one down already!
  2. Make a donation to our local food pantry. I HOPE you will recognize their booth when you see it. Tell em Wendy sent you.
  3. One member of your team must present themselves to the dunking booth as the Mayor of Dartmoor and ask if they may sit in the dunking booth for one time slot. (THIS COUNTS AS TWO QUESTS)
  4. Identify a local elected official and ask them to autograph your tomato.
  5. Ask one of the street vendors to show you their loveliest hat in Spanish.
  6. Have a team photo taken in front of the nocturnally illuminated indigenous symbol of kinsmanship.
  7. Have a team photo taken of the team red carding a soccer referee.
  8. Kiss a fish.
  9. Find Boogie Butt and show him your best disco moves. Get him to sign your tomato.
  10. Take a team picture with a news team or news reporter. Get them to sign your tomato.
  11. Talk to the tomato vendors and get them to tell you ten tomato varieties and write them down. Get one or all of them to sign your tomato.
  12. Get a photo with the most distant visitor you can find who is not a letterboxer holding your boxing buddy.
  13. Get one member of your team handcuffed by a police officer. Get the officer to sign your tomato. (getting the team member unhandcuffed is at the discretion of the team)
  14. Find a current local newspaper and take a photo of its headline.
  15. Get a photo of one of the water truck drivers holding your boxing buddy.
  16. Get a photo of one team member in the drivers seat of a classic car holding your boxing buddy.
  17. Tome una fotografa del equipo delante del Tazn de Tomate

We gave each team a bag with some items that would help them achieve some of these quests and we also asked them to bring some items themselves.


I talked to various members of the community that would be able to help our boxers. They were incredibly enthusiastic about interacting with participants, even while not entirely understanding what they were up to. I encouraged them to hassle the questors, but they wound up being too nice to take advantage of the opportunity (and the couple of folks who were really going to work on them didn't get "found"). Our group wound up helping out two fundraising/charity groups - our local food pantry and also a youth group that was raising money to buy mosquito nets to go to missions in Africa. They helped the youth group by sitting in the dunking booth, which I thought was really over and above!


Through the planning process, Fearless Fox would shake his head and say "I think you may be taking things too far." I was sure hoping I didn't, and if I did, I hope y'all will forgive me!


I would like to take this opportunity to thank JBK for getting out there and diligently checking all her boxes around town to make sure they were in good order. As I was preparing the event, I read over her clues and realized how much she has embraced Jacksonville's history by planting boxes that tell stories about our town. I think her boxes really helped the gathering attendees savor the local flavor.

We had seven teams with 35 boxers participating. After all was said and done, we gave awards for various elements of flare, enthusiasm, stealth, and spirit. We had a really hard time deciding who got what because everyone came out with their A game and played hard with us all day long!

Now what shall we do next year?


--
Barefoot Lucy
