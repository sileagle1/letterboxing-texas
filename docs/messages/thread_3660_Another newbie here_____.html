Another newbie here.....

From: garrett2056 (garrett2056@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-07-19 17:05:40 UTC
I've been box'n and lurking since September of last year and have
enjoyed every minute of my new hobby. Thanks to all of you whose
boxes I've been priviledged to find thus far. They've always
inspired me to go for that "next box" anxious to find another great
stamp or amazing location.

The excitement of my first few finds was thrilling but I think I've
come to appreciate lately the simplicity of this past-time. You
plant, I plant, we re-hide well and appreciate each other's work at a
pace that is comfortable for anyone who wants to participate. I used
to wonder about people who enjoy fishing. I couldn't understand how
they could appreciate something so still, quiet and bor-r-r-r-ing.
I'm getting older I guess `cause I think I'm getting it
now.......It's the time spent not the number of fish caught.

By way of introduction I run a part-time medical transcription
business out of our home and am a stay-at-home, homeschooling mother
to our teen-age son who will graduate the end of this coming school
year. I'm already planning and have clues printed for the Waco boxes
as hopefully he'll be attending Baylor after that. My husband's not
much into boxing now as he's much too busy but he loves being
outdoors so I'm thinking after retirement and things slow down for
him we'll be enjoying searching for boxes together.

A couple of times lately I've gone boxing with my mother, various
friends and other relatives but mostly I head out with my almost 7-
year-old neice, Baby Dur. She enjoys going "hiking" as she calls it
but doesn't much care for the heat. We'll get back in full swing as
a team when the weather cools some but I go out whenever I get the
opportunity with or without her.

Because of gas prices the way they are and the fact that there are
minimal boxes in my area I've decided to try my hand at carving and
planting thus the primary reason for introducing myself as I've
planted 4 in the area recently (Sunnyvale, Dallas, and Richardson)
and have more carved and ready to plant so be watching for new boxes
on this side of Dallas. Feedback as to reliability and accuracy of
clues for these first few boxes at least would be appreciated.

Looking forward to meeting you on the trails!

Mthr21

P.S. If you decide to travel to this area to find boxes and have a
box just itchin' to be planted please bring it with you. It would be
much appreciated and I would be more than happy to keep an eye on it
for you.
