Ink Pads lesson

From: Susan Tashiro (stashiro@awesomenet.net) | Date: 2005-04-25 10:27:27 UTC-05:00
OK, now for a lesson in ink pads...lol.
 
There are lots of different types of ink pads available.  Some are waterproof and some are not.  Some are quick drying and some are not.
 
Dye Ink Pads:  Quick drying and some are water resistant.  Example of water resistant are "Memories" by Stewart Superior Ink and  "Archival Inks" by Ranger Inks. Some are available in the 1" square "Ink Spots" that are great for limited space.
 
Pigment Ink Pads are slower to dry and can smear if not allowed to dry thoroughly.  They are also available in water resistant, but usually have to be set with a heat source to make them permanent. I personally don't like this type for letterboxing.
 
Palette Hybrid Inks (Stewart Superior Inks) are a new technology blending the best of the dye and pigment inks into one pad.  They are waterproof and are quick drying.  They come in a multitude of colors.  These are also available in 1" square mini ink pads. These are great pads, but are rather expensive.
 
The type of ink in a pad will written either on the lid or on the bottom label.  Refills are available for all ink pads, but not at stores like Hobby Lobby and Michaels.  You have to be careful when buying pads from these large chain craft stores.  There is no way of knowing how long a stamp pad has sat in the warehouse before making it to the shelf.  Buying ink pads from a rubber stamping store or a scrapbooking store is your best bet for getting good quality ink pads.  There are also lots of reputable online sources for buying ink pads.
 
Hope this helps.
