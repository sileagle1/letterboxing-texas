letterboxing mishaps

From: robyn.kennedy (woowooshoo@aol.com) | Date: 2007-04-15 02:46:32 UTC
Sometimes we (me, Squished Hiker and Brods) go letterboxing after I
get home from work. I am in the Army so after being in boots all day
I switch to my favorite footware flip flops and usually a pair of
shorts and then we head out the door. Generally, on our first box I
realize I have done it again. There I am with shorts and flip flops
on getting gnarly bites, scrapes and bumps on my legs and dirt stuck
in my toes. And I think to myself "DOH!" I have tried to pay
attention but it seems I always either have on shorts or flip flops
and I think "Oh this one probably won't be so bad" and of course I am
wrong. Sometimes Squished Hiker just seems amazed that I have done it
again.

I know that you all have some funny letterboxing mishaps and I would
love to hear them. They will make me feel a bit better about being a
little scatterbrained sometimes.

WooWooShoo

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-04-14 22:33:03 UTC-05:00
Although I don't go out in flipflops (not that I wouldn't, but I have a bad knee that dictates pretty good shoes when I'm on rough terrain, not to mention I've run into a couple of dicey situations that wouldn't have been good for dangly toes), I invariably wear shorts.  In fact, it's gotta be really, REALLY cold for me NOT to wear shorts.  Consequently, my legs are always pretty torn up.  I used to stop and promise myself I would be smarter next time, but I've learned better and now I stand in the middle of brambles and say "You know you shouldn't come out here like this, but you're gonna do it anyway, so who am I to tell you otherwise..."
 
Funny though - I wear loafers and sandals and dress shoes all day and change into boots to go out, and you wear boots all day and change into flipflops.  I wear hiking boots with shorts and capris.  It works for me, but it makes my LSS a little nuts when he suddenly realizes we're in the middle of Walmart and I'm dressed like a freak!

From: janet clark (jayceeinbedford@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-04-15 21:30:00 UTC-07:00
Mishaps, nah, it always goes exactly as planned. I always find the box the first time out, have everything I need. . .
 
So, anyways, the first box: No problem. Now, fast forward to that night. I'm super excited about letterboxing and go hijack DD#1 and her DF (they had just got home from happy hour with friends). DF is wearing brand new tennis shoes that probably cost over $100. It's dark and the box is off the beaten path and behind some shrubbery. I made them follow the clues like I had to. The only light we have is the glow from our cell phones. All is going good till we realize a smell is coming from the bottom of his shoe. He had stepped in pile of doggy doo. He hasn't gone out with us ever again.
 
The next day, I go looking for a box, Curse of the Witness Tree. This is now one of my favorites because it took me three tries. When the clue says compass, I think you actually have to have a compass. Yeah, I'm pretty sure that's what it means. And, don't use Mapquest if you don't have an exact address, you will end up on the other side of town if you do.
 
That's all I can humiliate my family with for now.
 
Janet, Blooming Flowers
