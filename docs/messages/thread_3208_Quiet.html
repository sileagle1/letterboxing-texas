From: Silver Eagle (sileagle@alltel.net) | Date: 2007-12-01 01:18:04 UTC
I, too have been busy lately, but not with Christmas shopping. The
usual soccer and scout activities are still going strong, but I'm
also coaching my daughter's basketball team. It is a labor of love,
but quite time consuming. Also, my Dad is not doing well these days
so I have been trying to spend more time visiting him across town.
Now none of these things would normally stop me from letterboxing,
but I have also grown somewhat cool toward our hobby.

Lately, I no longer feel the urge to rush out and find every new box
that gets placed, and this might not be a bad thing. Maybe I'm
finally ready to slow down and "smell the boxes". Part of this
change in attitude is due to the overwhelming number of boxes now
in the Houston area. I used to get excited whenever a new box was
placed nearby, but now I don't even print the clues out right away.
I'm not complaining about all the boxes, but I don't feel compelled
to rush like before. Another factor is the natural progression of
excitement loss for any new hobby. When you first discover something
new, you can't get enough of it, but eventually your ardor cools.
The great thing about those of us still here after many years is that
even though the newness wore off, we still enjoy letterboxing at some
level and won't give it up. So I tip my hat to all of you that
continue to pursue this hobby, through good times and bad, and here's
hoping we all add a little spark to our letterboxing lives soon.

Silver Eagle

From: Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter (janet@redbarnretreat.com) | Date: 2007-12-01 02:16:41 UTC
We found our first box almost exactly two years ago, so we certainly
don't compare ourselves to pioneers like Silver Eagle and Barefoot
Lucy, but even in those two years, letterboxing has changed
somewhat. It's not as intimate as it used to be when there were
relatively few letterboxers. I think it's just growing pains, and,
as more people find out about it, the problems may only increase. I
think the main difference is that, like you, we tend to be more
selective. There are so many letterboxes in our area (sorry, Mike),
that we realize we can't get them all, so we look for our favorite
placers, knowing that we will find a good letterbox waiting for us.
Then, we look to see what other boxes are in the area and find them
if we have time. Sometimes, if we don't recognize the trail name,
we may put that box off til later. Sometimes, later never comes and
the problem is, we may be missing some great boxes. It's a Catch 22
situation.

Boots Tex
