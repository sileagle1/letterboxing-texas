Rainy Day Letterboxing

From: ruby (celticlions@comcast.net) | Date: 2004-07-01 18:08:15 UTC-05:00

Just wanted to check in with a long soggy story of first time findings &
placings!
Our whole clan (CelticLions) drove down to Rockport on the Gulf coast
this past week, full of plans to find & place letterboxes. Flicker of
the WildBirds was with us and had already placed 3 in Arkansas, but has
never found one...so, filled with anticipation we assembled 3 KEY-themed
boxes to plant on Key Allegro (the manmade island off Rockport)!

Okay - first tip to other newbies out there:
Make sure there are PLACES before you plan your boxes with specific
themes!!!

We spent four or five very wet days dodging lightning and trudging
around Key Allegro looking for likely spots - nada! Everything was
private property or underwater!!!
However, we did find 3 fantastic boxes planted not too far away by folks
on this list, and 2 more on the trip home:
Bonefish & Super Froggy placed by Baby Bear (very cool stamps)
Big Tree & Texas Ranger planted by Silver Eagle (loved that tree - neat
area and the riverwalk by the Brazos was gorgeous)
R.I.P #3 placed by Wanda (the stamp made me gnash my teeth in envy -
very detailed!)

AND - (I was thrilled!) THE CAVE DEER hitchhiker...

Flicker came up with the idea of making our letterboxes into "missing
keys" and hiding them OFF Key Allegro on other "keys" or islands (Talk
about making lemonade out of lemons!) - but the weather conspired
against us. So we placed one "missing key" in the area, and will just
have to find places for the other two on future trips!

Thanks to all the TX letterboxers who gave us such fun this past week -
we'll return the favor ASAP!
Ruby Tuesday

From: ruby (celticlions@comcast.net) | Date: 2004-07-02 12:24:26 UTC-05:00
Well, despite the rain all week, we had a great time in the Rockport
area! Just having the whole family together was wonderful - the two
older guys are out of the house so we love having the chance to get
together again for real family time.
Our youngest son is the stamp carver so a few rainy days gave him time
to practice a little - it's funny how much more detailed his stamps were
as he progressed - his first stamp was our "family" stamp -
"CELTICLIONS" and I bet he's going to want to re-carve that one -
especially after seeing the great stamps in boxes we found!

The box we placed is listed as "Missing Key#1" and we piggybacked on
Baby Bear's Bonefish directions, listed under Lamar, in Aransas County.

The sun is shining in Dallas today so we may go looking for letterboxes
around here - did someone say that the Bob Woodward Park box was
replaced?
