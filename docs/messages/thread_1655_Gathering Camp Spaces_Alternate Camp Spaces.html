Gathering Camp Spaces/Alternate Camp Spaces

From: Connie Terry (connie@lavernia.net) | Date: 2006-01-28 21:14:20 UTC-06:00

Don’t know if anyone else has had this problem, but:

Wednesday of this week I requested a tent camping space for the weekend of the Boxcar Event at Rusk State Park.  The next day I got an email reply that there were no more spaces available.  After getting that message from the campground and assuming that others might have also gotten similar messages,  we contacted Mr. Dave Mason who has ample room for camping at his nearby ranch.  He offered that group members could camp there if needed.  No one has expressed interest in the alternate location thus far.   We told Mr. Mason we would tell him how many people would be needing space by Monday.  If no one speaks up before Monday, we will advise him that the space will not be needed.

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-01-28 19:44:49 UTC-08:00
I would like to add that I passed right by this
particular spot on my way to Nacogdoches yesterday
(and Connie's husband also emailed me some details and
pictures about it yesterday because I expressed an
interest in the Fly-In held there).

The site is about 13 miles from the State Park in
Rusk. This ain't some little pasture someone is
willing to let folks pitch tents in if they don't mind
pushing the cow poop out of the way - it's a nice
setup. While I only pulled over and checked it out
from the roadside, this looks to be really good
alternate camping for the gathering. Albeit the
plumbing ain't great, but I'm betting the portapotties
aren't that bad for the number of folks we're talking
about either. And if you just have to have a shower,
you can sneak in early and take one at the State Park.


Anyway, that's my .02 about that site - I'd stay there
in a heartbeat if I didn't have a house in the same
drive distance.

Lucy
