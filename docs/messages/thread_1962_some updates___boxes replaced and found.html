some updates...boxes replaced and found

From: (blhjrh@aol.com) | Date: 2006-06-30 16:52:46 UTC-04:00
    My LSS and I have been traveling a little lately (KY, TN, and Lake Tahoe area) and very busy in between, so I haven't been on this list much recently (haven't even read many of the posts and feel like I'm behind!).  I noted several new members, so welcome to Texas letterboxing! 
    Update on the Grapevine Heritage Park boxes:  I have replaced (June 28) the first (and so far, to my knowledge, ONLY) missing letterbox of all those I've planted so far:  In the Creek - Lauren's Turtle.  It was sad that it disappeared because it was the very first stamp I ever carved and I was pretty proud of it, and the first letterbox I made, though not the first I placed.  But it's now back out there, same location in Grapevine's Heritage Park Botanic Gardens, not far from In the Tree - Meghan's Bird, which was also temporarily pulled until the soggy logbook could be dried sufficiently.  Many of the earlier stamps in this logbook are now history, though.  I'll be checking on the others as I can get to all of them, but I've heard pretty frequently from finders on most of them and no others have been reported missing or wet!
    This week I also found PuddleSplasher's Tombs and Tees letterbox in the Bear Creek Cemetery in Euless, immediately adjacent to the DFW Airport and the Bear Creek Golf Course.  What a great place!  This old, quiet, historic cemetery, tall cedars and oaks surrounding it.  I was sitting and stamping in and kept thinking I heard voices, then realized that just across the creek beyond the trees, golfers were teeing off!  And then, the planes lifting off overhead, although with the tree cover, they were hard to see.  Really cool letterbox, too!
      Lake Tahoe in early June  - beautiful views of the deep blue lake, snow-capped peaks around the lake, and tall tall pines along the trails.  What could be better?  Finding a great box in a cool place with interesting stamps!  Found 4:  Birds of Tahoe #2 by ArtTrekker, Rainbow Bridge and Patty Reed's Doll, both near the Donner Pass, by doublesaj&Old Blue, and Golden Bear in Reno, by PuddleSplasher.  All great stamps, wonderful finds!  Looked for 2 others which we weren't able to find, one I'm pretty sure is missing at Inspiration Point overlooking Emerald Bay, and one at the Donner Pass Summit that I just couldn't get to for remains of snow and ice covering up the trail and the downed trees where the box is probably waiting. (Wish just a little of that were here in Texas with us!)   The area is full of boxes that we just weren't able to look for (of the 2 of us, only 1 is passionate about letterboxing.  The other is a pretty good sport, but would rather just hike, explore, and ignore the boxes!)  I was pretty excited when I got home and entered these recent finds (including about 6 others in KY and TN and Arkansas last month) to discover that I'd passed the F300 mark several boxes ago.  Even more exciting, though, is the discovery that while I've not been paying attention, at least 15 or 20 letterboxes are around this area that I haven't even looked for yet - spread out in every direction!  Yay!  Many more days of letterboxing right here at home!
