Wow! I had a house full of visitors this week. Stinky Monkey, Roarin' Lion and Baby Sister were all here. Lucy Locket decided to come down and spend some time with us and brought a bus load of people with her. I told her there wasn't much room left for visitors but we could manage for a couple of days. This was a very rambunctious group! They unloaded and spent a few nights just hanging out and having a little R&R before the bus headed back down the road. They were very glad to be in East Texas away from all the hustle and bustle of the big DFW metro area.
Alas, all good things must come to an end, so I loaded the bus and headed to Tyler. I told the riders that after all that rest, they needed a little exercise. So,I made my way to the itersection of Cumberland Rd. and S. Broadway (hwy 69) and turned west. I followed the road around and made a left. Parking at the trailhead on the right, I took the rowdy group on a little hike. I followed the trail to a "Y" and went right. From the culvert farthest up the trail, I walked another 156 steps to an Oak tree on my right and looked left. The crew was getting hot and needed a rest so I took them about 22 steps off the path to a fallen tree to rest in the shade. They all climbed into the hollow end of the fallen tree for a nap. I wished them happy trails and headed back home to a very quiet house.



Blue Butterfly

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2009-07-18 23:33:19 UTC-05:00

Hi Heather,

Well your first message DID eventually post, and it looks like you posted it about the time I saw the message that it had been hidden!

Lightning and I had just finished the daunting task of mowing our yard which had not been mowed in far too long since our lawnmower had been in for repairs for awhile- it was horribly thick and difficult to mow, and it proved to be quite a job, and I hurt my shoulder in the midst of it while trying to restart the mower with the pull cord (I hate those things!) so we rewarded ourselves with lunch at Lupe's. A nice big lunch with Cokes and everything. We returned home happy with our job, sluggish from our meal, and pleased to be almost cool and dry again.

And then I checked my email. And there was the message by Blue Butterfly. 11:51 was when it was sent. And it was 2:10-ish. A wedding we were to attend at 6:00. I calculated that to be roughly 2 hours 20 minutes of lost time on my part and 3 hours 50 minutes before we were to be dressed and clean and at the wedding. I was also contemplating an Urgent Care visit for my shoulder. Time was tight.

I looked to see if there was a post by you or Texas Dragonfly or even JBK or Maydelle Queen Bee to say the box had been retrieved, almost hoping there would be that message so I would know a trip to Tyler was unnecessary. There wasn't. I knew I had to make the trip on the off chance you were off studying and TX Dragonfly was out of town and JBK was working cows with her dad and MQB was on another mission of some sort.

I dragged my stinky, sweaty self back into the convertible, wishing I had the AC'd van but Fearless Fox was off in it. As I pointed myself toward Tyler, I called Fearless Fox to see if he was anywhere near an intercept course, but he was at the lake in the opposite direction. He offered that I could come to where he was and get the van, to which I told him (in my best DUH voice) "I'm going after the TOUR BUS. You KNOW I'm on a timeline. This is a RACE!" Translated - "That would be like stopping for coffee..." He's pretty quick and right away said "Oh - yeah - you'd better hurry." and hung up so as not to distract me while I made my way out of town.

And off I went. Now if you remember, this is the convertible I lovingly call Fearless Fox's Midlife Crisis. If you've ever seen it, you know it was a pretty sad midlife crisis. In fairness, he bought it from a buddy who needed cash so it wasn't exactly a midlife crisis. Not to say we don't have a good time with it, but as convertibles go, it's not much to look at. So anyway, I hit the highway and cranked up the music and was glad for the wind whipping through the car, further drying me off from the mowing extravaganza.

Now - I'm used to driving a mini-van. Mini-vans don't particularly drive fast unless you really make them, and besides that, in Police Officer World, mini-vans pretty much fly under the radar unless the driver is flagrantly mis-driving. I don't really tend to drive badly, and I really am not a speeder most the time either. But I was in the convertible. Convertibles, even homely ones, attract the attention of police officers. And this little convertible does like to fly. Add to that the fact that I had been telling myself "Hurry, hurry" for the last fifteen minutes or so, and you see where this is going. I got pulled over, having missed the reduced speed sign in my preoccupation to get where I was going. Geez...and again the mantra "This would be like stopping for coffee..." crossed my mind. Thankfully I had not walked out of the house without my day planner, which is where my driver's license lives. I almost did. The officer greeted me and we exchanged pleasantries and I gave him my license. As he wrote stuff down from it, I noticed his name. He had the same last name as a scoutmaster friend of mine, who happened also to graduate from my high school a couple of years ahead of me. I dropped the name. The officer smiled and acknowledged that was his dad, and we began the Exchanging of the Pedigree, as a friend of mine likes to call it. I tacked on my scouting credentials and he quit writing. He went back to his car and returned a few minutes later to say he had called his dad and got the a-okay on me and was going to give me a warning. Then he asked me where I was headed in such a hurry. I told him I was on my way to pick up some folks who had just gotten to Tyler on a bus. I did not lie.

And off I went. When I arrived at the trailhead, there were two cars there. I just knew I was on the heels of someone and that I might actually get to meet Heather or see one of the others on the trail. I contemplated leaving a quick note on the windshields to say something like "If you just got the LB, you're a turkey" or something along those lines, but I realized that would be freakish for a nonletterboxer to find that, which almost certainly one of them would have been so I didn't. As I walked the trail, I startled a deer on the trail. Again, I had a sense that I was too late - the deer appeared quite traumatized, as if it had just been assaulted by a wild bunch of hooligans. I found my way to the hiding spot very easily, took a deep breath and went in for the find. No box. I searched the ends of the three pieces of downed tree there, although it was quite obvious from the description which one it had been in.

At that point, I realized I had spent three hours sweating my rear end off in the yard, eaten a big meal, then immediately went back out into the heat - in the middle of the afternoon - for a hike - in the middle of July. For nothing. Now y'all know how I hate the heat. What was I doing???? I realized I had been uncontrollably drawn out of my AC'd house for this siren of the Texas LB world!

There was nothing to do but get back in the car and drive home, stopping off at the lake to tell Fearless Fox I had come up empty-handed. He seemed to be as bummed as I was. I eventually got back to the house and checked email, only to see that Heather's message was sent just minutes after I left the house. If only I had had this email address directed to my phone...that WILL change as I prepare to keep watch until it moves again.

Nice job, Heather, and congratulations on snagging the Bus! By the way, there was some poison ivy in the area, so you may want to scrub your ankles and hands with Tecnu or the anti-PI agent of your choice.

--
Barefoot Lucy
"Not about footwear, it's about philosophy!"
