Checking statuses before looking for boxes

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2009-05-24 09:23:22 UTC-05:00

It seems like I have had a rash of no finds lately - on boxes I have either retired on AQ or archived or marked MISSING or RETIRED on LBNA. I feel really badly when that happens - I hate it badly enough when someone drives for one of my boxes and they don't find it, but when they go after it and they never had a hope in the first place, that's a bummer.

SO - I want to make sure our new boxers have noticed that on AQ, there are statuses, and "retired" means the placer has either removed the box or confirmed it is gone. "unavailable" probably means the placer has pulled it for maintenance, or perhaps it's in an area with seasonal availability. "Unknown" means - well - who knows the status? You might get lucky and find one of those. And of course, "Active" means that as far as anyone knows, the box is in place and active.

On LBNA, the retired boxes are (or should be) archived, so you won't see them in a normal search. Some folks, like me, put the words MISSING or RETIRED in the title of the box and leave them in the listing for awhile before archiving, so that anyone who might be behind on logging won't have to go digging for them.

I'm all for the idea that you never know if you'll find a box when you go looking for it, but it really is a shame to go looking for a box that is known to be missing before you ever left your computer screen! Of course, I guess the possibility is that all these folks had printed off clues quite awhile ago, but that doesn't seem too likely for ALL of them.

Hope this helps?

--
Barefoot Lucy

Very true!  And a great story.  Funny how things happen that way.  But you have to admit - that's not the norm.  Usually when they are marked "retired" or archived, the placer is pretty darned sure (as you were) that the box is gone OR they actually have taken possession of the box.   Otherwise they leave the status as Unknown. 

One of the things I DO really like about AQ is the fact that it has the status flags.  If both the finder and the planter use them properly, they are a great tool.  Of course, on LBNA boxes are completely removed from search listings when they are archived, so no chance of hunting retired boxes unless you specifically go looking for them there.  And then - you don't know why or how it was archived unless the planter or the webmasters make a note in the clues.

I just felt very badly because the latest reporter was very disappointed not to have found it.  I would hope that folks who go looking for boxes knowing the odds are stacked against them would at least have a philosophical attitude about it.  I feel like the latest one I have in mind just didn't notice or understand the designations...

Every time I get a report like that, it makes me stop and think whether I have been keeping my clues updated.  I think I have - can't promise I'm always great at it - but in the cases where they've been reported missing lately, I've gone back and checked and found they were already noted.

Oh well.  I hope someone gets a little something from my note.  I always feel disappointed with them when they don't find my boxes.

Lucy

The finder followed the original clues and found it, so it had never really been missing, even though the report had come from a reliable letterboxer who detailed the fact that the location of the box had been destroyed by construction crews. I'm sure that was true and that the box was fortunate to survive the damage and remain in the same place. It's now been found 3 times in the last week. So, you never know for sure unless you go check it out for yourself or someone stumbles upon it for whatever reason.
