From: Mike Olbrisch (mike-2007@elp.rr.com) | Date: 2007-11-23 16:39:02 UTC

James just called.

He said they had a good Thanksgiving, considering where he was. Mosul. We
talked about a 1/2 hour total. He said there was a bit of sadness that they
were not "HOME" with their families. But he said "I was with my other
family, so its all right".

He said that the "Wild West" days of Iraq are over. Oh - there is still
plenty of danger. But the Iraqis have pretty much taken over the fight.
You hear a battle off in the distance. Then the word comes down, this or
that Iraqi unit has just kicked ass on this or that terrorist camp. Not
like it used to be.

Gee - wonder why you don't get this on the alphabet soup news channels!
Think they might have an agenda?

His best friend Kris was at Mosul last deployment. He was the young man who
earned the Silver Star at Tal Afar. And is still in rehab because of it.

Anyone wanting to send James any cards or mail, his address is:

SPC James H. Olbrisch.
K-Trp 3d Sqn, 3d ACR
FOB Marez
APO AE 09334

OR - - - change James' name to - To Any Soldier - and it will get to some
deserving trooper in his unit. Ahhh - Cavalry. If you ain't CAV, you ain't
SHIT! There is a reply to that - it usually invokes an ass kicking from the
CAV guys. But that's a story for another time. I still miss it!

I told him the old saw - STAY ALERT - STAY ALIVE - - TACTICAL TACTICAL
TACTICAL. He said he was where he belongs, doing what he is supposed to be
doing. Ya gotta love it. He does.

Oh - Re-Up asked him what it would take to get him to re-enlist. He said
"You asking for a list"? Yes was the answer.

I want a promotion. We can work on that.
I want to go to Ft. Carson. Everybody wants that, we can try.
I want a $50k bonus. We can do that.
I want a Unicorn. A Unicorn?
Yes - a Unicorn. Guess it ain't gunna happen, is it?

Yup - ya gotta love him!

Y-all have a great day after thanksgiving.

Mike - KD9KC.
El Paso, Tx.

From: Mike Olbrisch (mike-2007@elp.rr.com) | Date: 2007-11-25 00:15:40 UTC
Well - he was born in Alabama - at Fox Army Hospital.  He spent almost 4 years in Germany.  But other than his own military service and a few vacations "with the boys", he has mostly lived in Texas.  He has probably only done about 10 or 12 letterboxes while he was at Ft. Hood.  And likely fewer Geocaches.
 
Dunno what his needs will be yet.  Previously - hard candy and small cans of chopped green chilie went over good.  I think I told you the story about the SPAM.  <grin>  But they are in a different climate now, the northern mountains.
 
Books and magazines seem to go over good.  Westerns like Louis L' Amore and Zane Gray.  Mysteries.  Anything but war novels.  We sometimes shop the $5 DVD isle in Wal-Mart.  Seems DVDs are great, but they never last - the sand eats them up after a while.  It gets into everything.  I shop the thrift stores for books and DVDs too.  And - I always toss in a few AA and AAA batteries, the guys have MP3 players and such.
 
I will be stocking the unit library with books I know they need.  I already got them the 4-volume set of sniper training from MSG Owens.  And of course On Killing and On Combat by David Grossman.  Tactical Shotgun and Tactical Pistol by Gabe Suarez.  But they need stuff to take their minds away from it too.  And when they leave for home they usually leave all the books behind for the next crew.
 
Don't worry about them getting lax.  If it was a maintenance unit I could see it.  But Cavalry isn't like that.
 
Well - it is cold and snowy here, so I am going to light the fireplace.
 
Y-all take care.
 
Mike.
