Re: Need a little help...

From: ThimbleCat (inklaura@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2007-02-05 16:05:21 UTC
Lucy - When I teach the letterboxing class for Girl Scout leaders, we
make a simple logbook (1/2 sheet of carstock folded with folded 1/2
sheets of typing paper inside - hole punched and secured with brads
or twine) and a simple stamp too.
I give them all a small square of fun foam and kid scissors and lay
out a few hole punches in various sizes and let them go to town.
It is fast and easy for them to cut out a simple star, heart, initial
letter, etc... from fun foam. Some of the designs even get more
elaborate - teacups, unicorns, cats, crowns, etc... Then they attach
their fun foam "stamp" to a film canister with a square of double
sided adhesive foam (found in the framing section).
It is pretty fast and they feel a bit of ownership in their stamp,
simple that it may be.
Just something you may want to consider.
ThimbleCat
