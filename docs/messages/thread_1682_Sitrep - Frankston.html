Sitrep - Frankston

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-02-10 05:28:03 UTC-08:00
I've been holding on to this one for a few days
because I had hoped to verify that what I found isn't
true.

Lightning and I went out on a short jaunt last weekend
to avoid the germs floating around our house from the
sick folks. We headed toward Frankston so that we
could check up on a box on the way and so we could
find Blue Butterfly's new boxes in the area.

On the way to Frankston, we stopped at The Spring to
fill up with water (fresh delicious spring water - you
just can't beat it!), to find a Geocache and to check
up on Texasluvbug's Water from the Rocks. We found
The Spring to be flowing well and we found the cache,
but we couldn't find the letterbox. I'm really bummed
about that...that is a great place to have a box! I
had previously found it, so I was fairly sure I could
remember where it was, but there was no sign of it. I
emailed TLB to see if she had had any reports of it
and I also emailed known finders to double check if I
was in the right spot to find it. All signs point to
it being missing, although TLB hasn't yet written me
back to say whether she knows it to be there or not.
SOOOO...if you hope to go for Water from the Rocks,
realize it may not be there. It is a good place to
stop anyway though - just for the water - so if I were
in the area I would go ahead and stop and drink up,
and while there look for the box, if for no other
reason than to prove me wrong!

We went on to Frankston to find another couple of gc's
and another letterbox. We were pretty successful on
all counts, except that the final leg of one multi-gc
was behind a fence that said "Closed". I'm still
trying to figure out whether it is acceptable to a
gc'er to cross a fence or not. I decided I wasn't
real thrilled with the idea, so I let that cache lie.
We did find a benchmark on that run though, and that
was pretty cool. I was impressed to know that there
are little places all over the country that are
designated as basically indisputable location markers.
I went home and told Fearless Fox about them, and he
burst my bubble when he said "Oh yeah, we have one of
those in the front yard - I buried it when I built
that burm". Hmph. He says he only found out you
aren't supposed to bury those things a year or two
ago, so I wonder if he feels like going out and
unburying it now. Are they like the tags on pillows
and bedding?

Anyway, we got on to Frankston and went in search of
Blue Butterfly's Pine Hill. We found it - no problem
- in an odd little part of the country I had never
come across before. The only problem we ran into is
that it is a really friendly neighborhood - folks kept
stopping and asking us if we were okay and if we
needed some help. I kept telling them no, instead of
telling them "YES. You can help us by leaving us
alone and forgetting you ever saw us here!" I
sometimes wish we could carry the flashy-thingies that
they had on Men in Black.

We went back to Frankston and found yet another cache
and a pretty amazing little museum. I love little
hometown museums. I've seen the one in Frankston for
years and never been there at the right time (Tues,
Thurs, Sat). It was convenient because we also needed
to go to the bathroom by that time, so we popped in to
browse a bit and use their facilities before heading
to find some lunch. Well, you don't just browse a
small museum like that - the curator caught us at the
door and walked us through the whole thing, giving us
the grand tour. Two hours after we walked in, we
still had to go to the bathroom, but we were a LOT
smarter than we were when we arrived. It was very
interesting and because of the research I had done for
JEN, I had the good fortune of being able to shed a
little light on one of the "mysteries" she had spread
through the whole museum - pieces of her ongoing work
of identification and description that had escaped her
and previous volunteers.

We finally broke free of the museum and beelined to
Dairy Queen to grab a bite and use their facilities,
only to find I had left my billfold at home! Yikes!
We had planned on staying out another few hours, but
our stomachs got the best of us (it WAS 3 p.m. and
long past lunch time by then), so we headed home with
plans to try again for Blue Butterfly's other new box
this weekend.

Lucy
