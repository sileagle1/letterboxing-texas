Big News in Rusk, Texas

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-03-11 14:28:26 UTC-08:00
I was in Rusk again this morning setting out a box and
I was an eyewitness to what may well be the biggest
news in Rusk in quite a long time (aside from standard
courthouse type stuff).

I had been out planting a box and I realized I was
really hungry so I headed to the Family Dollar to pick
up some trail snacks and a little water. Don't ask my
why Family Dollar - it's just sort of where I shop in
Rusk. As I approached, I noticed that there was quite
a lot of traffic going on, especially for a Saturday
morning in Rusk! As I got nearer, I realized
something big was going on, and suddenly, I knew I was
not going to be able to stop at Family Dollar...

For those of you who have ever lived in a small town,
you know that nothing gets the locals going like a big
fire. Well the Family Dollar blazed away this morning
- when I got there at 9:15, it was fully engulfed and
the fire department was there and half the town was
already present and the other half was rushing to the
scene. I wound up getting a front row seat in the
parking lot of the Brookshire Bros, which thankfully
also sells trail snacks and water. It's been quite a
long time since I've sat on the hood of my car and
shamelessly watched a building go up in flames!

So thankfully we're not going to Rusk next weekend to
make news, because it'd be tough to top that fire!

Lucy

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-03-11 19:15:21 UTC-08:00
I love small town life too - even though sometimes I
miss San Diego and all there was to do there. The
beauty of small towns is what drives kids out of town
- at least for awhile: you can't do anything without
everyone knowing it! I just returned from a quick
trip to Walmart and I ran into three folks I knew, two
of whom knew SJZ was visiting this weekend, and she
only got into town at 3 a.m. today!

The fire this morning was such a throw back for me - I
remember about 30 years ago when there was a huge fire
in downtown Jacksonville when the major pharmacy
caught fire and burned to the ground. Those were
pre-Walmart days and everything centered on downtown,
so losing a building there affected everyone.
Hundreds of people showed up to watch it burn and we
all stayed downtown for hours - well after our
bedtimes. We were telling Lightning and SJZ not that
long ago how the local radio station used to announce
everything almost on a play-by-play basis. Every time
a siren went off, everyone would run to their radios
to hear what was going on. We lived by radio! And
would you believe - Rusk still has a radio station
that is not that far off of what we grew up on in
Jacksonville? You listen to the daily trading post
program and catch up on the gossip!
