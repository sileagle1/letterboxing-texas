Introduction from robotswithdreams2

From: Amber (Cooper) Lujan (ladida@rocketmail.com) | Date: 2007-04-07 05:20:47 UTC
Heyall. So, I just got into letterboxing recently with a friend of
mine. I found out about letterboxing, oddly enough, on a crafting
website I frequent: craftster.org
This was the post that introduced me to the hobby:
http://www.craftster.org/forum/index.php?topic=5191.0

When I read it I just knew letterboxing was for me. My friend and I
had already been visiting all these parks and historical places in our
area unawares there were treasures to be found in the very spots we
had stood!

We made our stamps the day after we found out about letterboxing, but
we were really dumb and made these stamps that were like three inches
long each! Well, they look neat, but after we found our first box
(which was a microbox) in Landa Park (New Braunfels) we ended up
having to go home and make smaller stamps.

We just placed our first box the other day, locally (we live in New
Braunfels), and Corazon found it today and introduced me to this Yahoo
group. It seems you all have quite a community! I hope you'll
welcome me in!

Besides placing our first box we have found quite a few in the area.
Also, my friend (robotswithdreams1) went to OK to visit family and
found some there, and she brought back a hitchhiker, which we placed
. . . somewhere local (but not in NB). Hope it falls into good hands.

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-04-07 09:27:28 UTC-05:00
I had that same sensation when I got started - I had walked a particular trail a hundred times, probably even stopped to rest on a particular log, and when I discovered letterboxing I could not believe there was a box right there on that very trail!  I knew the trail so well I could visualize the exact location of the box based on the directions.  I could scarcely wait to go look behind that log to see if there was really something there - there was!
 
If you really like your big stamps, hold onto them and carry them with you if you want!  Not all boxes are micros, and some boxes will take a larger stamp.  Sometimes boxers use more than one image, especially depending on the size of the box.
--
Barefoot Lucy
