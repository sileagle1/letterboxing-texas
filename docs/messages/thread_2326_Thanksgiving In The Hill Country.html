Thanksgiving In The Hill Country

From: Silver Eagle (sileagle@alltel.net) | Date: 2006-11-27 03:37:18 UTC
While Barefoot Lucy was camping in Arkansas, my family and I were
visiting relatives in Austin. On the way up, I finally got a chance to
visit McKinney Roughs Park just outside Bastrop. The weather was
perfect for hiking and it was a great place for it. I managed to hide
a box there to entice some of you to visit. It is one of the Lower
Colorado River Authority Parks and I plan to visit others in the future.

While in Austin, I did not think I would get a chance to letterbox
since I wanted to spend time with relatives, but as luck would have it
a new letterbox was posted the day after Thanksgiving that was less
than 1 mile from where I was staying! Luckily I had Internet access
and just happened to check New Letterboxes and there it was. So, I
told everyone I was going to walk off all the turkey and headed to the
box. Needless to say I was First Finder and I felt fortunate, indeed.

That night we drove part of the Hill Country Christmas Trail to see all
the lights and it was great, as usual. We have done this a few times
before and I highly recommend it. From Austin we drove on Hwy 71
toward Marble Falls and stopped at a scenic winery called Stone House.
It was a nice place but we got there too late to stay long. In Marble
Falls I tried to find the 2 boxes that have been there for years but
failed on both. We then ate at Blue Bonnett Cafe and had good ole
Country Cooking. It is ranked as the number 1 place to eat breakfast
in TX by at least 1 magazine and you can have breakfast any time of day.
After eating it was finally dark enough to see the lights, so we drove
down to the river park where they put all kinds of decorations. The
whole thing is free (donations requested) and well worth a visit. As we
left town heading south to Johnson City, the view of all the lights
reflecting on the river was spectacular!

Johnson City is my favorite place to see Christmas lights and it did
not disappoint. The courthouse is covered in lights and the area around
LBJ Birthplace is amazing, with lights going all the way up huge trees
in white, red and green. There is even a free hayride to see it all.
We then headed back to Austin ready for Christmas to arrive.

Before heading home I did manage to find a few boxes in Zilker Park
while my wife shopped downtown, and they were a lot of fun. All in all
it was a great Thanksgiving and I hope all of you have a great one, too!

Silver Eagle

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-11-27 08:28:50 UTC-06:00
It is SUCH a shame that I didn't know about letterboxing when I travelled to Austin frequently.  I went to Zilker Park several times and sometimes I got to do some goalie coaching for a soccer coach friend of mine there, but being alone most of the time and tired after working all day, I wound up spending a lot of my Austin time working by laptop in my hotel room.  What a waste of perfectly good outdoor time!
 
For three years or so I went to Austin one week a month every month, and one month every quarter I spent 3 weeks there.  During legislative sessions, I spent even more time there, so that the hotel I always stayed in reserved me the same room.  I knew I was there too much when the clerk would say "I didn't know you had left!".  I generally stayed out of trouble, but I also had no fun (unless you count the stalking incident...), and it sure would've been nice to have gotten out and burned some energy while there!
