From: Lois -James (adamstrails@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-04-29 22:23:42 UTC
I went to the Athens Arboretum again today and as the office was open I
asked them if they had a letterbox turned it and they said Oh, you are
the letterboxer and said that they had an artical about letterboxing in
their news letter for this month. They gave me a copy and introduced me
to the lady in charge of the news letter. They also gave me a letterbox
and said that someone had found it on the trail and turned it in. It
was not my "Little Miss Muffet" but Catzpaws2004 Texas Passport Series
#1. I took it home with me as I was too tired to make the trail today.
It is in good shape and needed no repairs so I will return it into the
wilds soon. I will let you all know when it is again available.

Here is the web page for arboretum. Just click on Spring News Letter
for artical. http://www.eastexasarboretum.org/

From: JimGregEricRachelKeli Chancellor (chancelier_de_lbu@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-04-29 15:34:30 UTC-07:00
That's really cool that letterboxing is so well accepted there.  I say this because we recently visited a place where the opposite was the story.  During a trip to Natchitoches we dropped in at Fort St. Jean Baptiste for a cool tour and hopefully to find  a couple of letterboxes.  The clues for the boxes stated they had been moved inside to the visitors center due to problems with snakes.  When we asked about the boxes we got a very negative reaction from the people in the visitors center.  They said letterboxers had lipped into the park without paying and trampled on areas that visitors were not allowed.  When we asked some others that worked at the fort they told the story a little differently.  In fact, they mentioned geocachers.  I'm not certain of their history with letterboxing, but they could not help us with the boxes and told us they were probably destroyed.
Jim
