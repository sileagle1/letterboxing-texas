I wanted to take the time to thank Silver Eagle, Baby Bear, Barefoot Lucy, and Dewberry for all their efforts to make the gathering such a great time.  When things go off so smoothly it is easy to forget about all the hard work that goes before, but I think you all went above and beyond the call of duty.  The chili was delicious, and BINGO was so much fun!  I don't know quite how dewberry found so many hiding spots in such a small space, but she really outdid herself!  Please send our thanks to all the "non-Texan" boxers who took the time to carve such great stamps for us to find.  My little armadillo girl (who still can't quite spell armadillo) is still reeling from the fact that she actually met Silver Eagle and Baby Bear.  The first thing she did this morning was call and brag to her cousins.  Thanks again to all of you for making this weekend such a blast!  MO5

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-03-19 19:16:26 UTC-08:00
MO5,

I wanted to tell you how much I loved your little girl
- she was just adorable and very polite! She very
diligently made sure that I signed her and your
logbook, and was only a little put off when she
couldn't find her name tag that spelled armadillo for
her. She finally said "well, would you just spell it
for me please?"

I also had the pleasure of dining with FourFitzs
Friday night and sitting next to their lovely little
girl, Reagan. She was a pleasure too!

And Graham and Patrick, Stinky Monkey and Roarin' Lion
(didn't get their real names), Kendall and Tyler,
Compass Points' daughter...I would've taken any of
them home with me! And then the ones I didn't meet
personally, but saw running around...

Really, I thought all the kids were great. Manners
are a really big thing with me, and at the end of the
day I realized there wasn't a single kid there that I
thought was rude or unruly - truly, they all get tired
and have their moments, but don't we all.

Rarely do you put a whole bunch of kids together and
have so many that are lively yet polite and enjoyable
to be around. I wonder why it is that we had such a
high proportion of good kids?

Lucy
