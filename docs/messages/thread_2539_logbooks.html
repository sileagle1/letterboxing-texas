From: the carrascos (a.carrasco@earthlink.net) | Date: 2007-01-24 20:55:54 UTC-06:00
  I have been thinking about Lucy's question about logbooks.  I think what I like best is to find a DRY logbook :)  When I make mine I try to make them with an imprint of the stamp on the cover, especially my boxes that have several parts that need to be put together, so the finder realizes, just for example, that the skeleton should have 2 arms, or that the hotel has guests to find.  It is always fun to find a logbook that has something "extra" to it; a cool cover, extra information, a box dedication, interesting binding, something along those lines.  Of course, the grand champion of all logbooks is the one in the Happy Birthday Texas PLB, but I wouldn't expect to find something that spectacular out in the wild!  I have done several postal rings now, and the logbooks that some people make are totally amazing.  MO5

From: the carrascos (a.carrasco@earthlink.net) | Date: 2007-02-17 21:51:49 UTC-06:00
I think making logs can be a really creative part of putting a box together, and it is a lot of fun to look at all the paper options out there!  Even more fun, for me at least, is looking at all the stuff (read junk, or even trash)I have around the house and thinking of ways to use it to make a log.  I always cut apart the backs of the countless notebooks the kids bring home at the end of the school year, I have used a TIDE box, old silk flower leaves, instructions for a lego set long ago dismantled....my logs aren't ever anything very fancy or spectacular, but it is fun to see what you can put together from what you have lying around.  Of course, you must understand that I am the one who actually had 25 empty coffee cans in the house to turn into drums when armadillo girl was in kindergarten, and I know that there are at least 40 empty toilet paper rolls in the drawer in my bathroom.  Maybe I need a 12 step program for learning to throw away potential craft materials.  MO5

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-02-17 22:05:10 UTC-06:00
LOL...You and me MO5.  You and me.  We are two of a kind.
 
In my garage right now, I have no less than 25 plastic koolaid containers.  Coffee cans?  What size?  And industrial sized food cans.  At least 50 old check boxes.  Countless baby food jars (although I doubt I can top you on that!).  Mayo jars, mustard squeeze bottles, Jelly jars.  Butter tubs.  Plastic rolls from adding machine tape.  Paper tubes from toilet paper and paper towels.  Stacks of newspaper.  Cardboard tubes from rolls from my label printer at work.  Pill bottles?  What color?  Amber or green?  Film canisters (you gotta keep film canisters - they will be dinosaurs soon!).  Round mailing tubes - of course!  If nothing else, they make great rain sticks.  A big stack of bushel baskets.
 
{sigh} And then there's the stocks of normal craft supplies - fun foam, construction paper, tens of thousands of pony beads, ribbon in every imaginable pattern and width.  Googly eyes in all sizes.  Pom pom balls in all sizes and colors - AND animal patterns.  Origami paper.  Craft sticks.  safety pins.  straight pins.  Paints, paints, paints - mostly acrylic, but some model paints and some spray paints. 
 
In writing this, I finally realized I can never quit Cub Scouts - Lightning will soon be in Boy Scouts, but I have to stay in Cubs until I use all this stuff up, which will probably be NEVER!
