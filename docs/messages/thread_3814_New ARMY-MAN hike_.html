From: Mike Olbrisch (mike-2007@elp.rr.com) | Date: 2008-10-17 23:08:11 UTC-06:00
Toughest hike in these mountains I have done to date.
 
Standard timing for a hike is hours = (miles/2) + (1 x (every 1000 feet elevation gain)).
 
At almost 9 miles, that would be 4.5 hours.  With 4114 foot elevation gain, that means about a 9 hour hike.  But the middle 3 miles is bush-whacking and rock-hopping.  I don't think I could do it in 9 hours.  But then - I am Army-Man.  This is my kind of hike!
 
Your goal is the letterbox on N. Franklin Mountain.  That is an Admirable goal.  8 miles and 3050 feet, 7 hours.  And that is a reasonable hike, mostly good trails. 
 
I can't wait to be your guide!
