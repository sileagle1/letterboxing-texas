I live in Newtown (just up 34). Last year (my first year too) after reading
an article in the Danbury newspaper about letterboxing, I looked it up on
the net and my husband and I immediately rushed off that day and located
our first LB in Newtown. Then I traveled the state of CT locating all 30 of
the Ct State Forest Letterboxes (became an obsession), then turned to the
LBNA! My sister, who lives in Texas, was visiting me in October so I
introduced her to LBing by doing 5 of the state LBs, then we did one in
Rangely, Maine which was extremely cool! A month later when I visited her
in TX, she had already done about a dozen down there and we did more! Now
she's probably done 100 and has just placed her first four as well as a
couple of HHs. Like the rest of us, LBing is her drug of choice! I used to
log in with a store bought chick simply as Maribeth or as Tx Chick. When my
sister and I LBed together, it would be 2txchicks. She is now Viewfinder
and I am Puddle-Splasher. My stamp is now a Jeep (grill) with a canoe tied
to the top and splashes flying off the side. With the nicer weather
arriving, this week I found the LBs in New Milford at Lovers Leap, two in
Roxbury's Land Grant at Moosehorn (a very vigorious and beautiful 2 mile
hike ending by very loud series of waterfalls), and of course the 3 in the
Ansonia Nature Center. Like many other LBers, I have a stack of clue sheets
printed out and ready to go! Since my sister has just placed 4 (or maybe 6
by now) new boxes in Keller, Texas this week which are being visited
already by the very active Tx Letterboxers group, I thought that would be a
great place for your HH to start some great travels so I sent it to her to
make the circuit down there before heading on! I had just picked up a HH in
La Jolla, CA two weeks ago and it had started its travels in New England,
and is now in TX!
I will be moving back to Texas at the end of June, but my husband wants to
hang in and work here (Sikorsky) at bit longer so I will be spending half
my time here and half down there. (I have two little grandsons and they
have pulled me back!) Hope to see you out there on the trail!
Maribeth (Puddle-Splasher)
