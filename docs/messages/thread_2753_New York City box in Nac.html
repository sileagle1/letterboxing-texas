From: Ashby (ashby@ic.org) | Date: 2007-05-06 07:38:46 UTC-07:00
Hi all,

Thanks to everyone who has looked for Random's New
York City box in Nac and has rehidden it so carefully.
We went to check on it today and it is STILL there --
over a year so far! The log book was full, so I am
fixing that up this morning, so there will be more
empty pages for whoever comes next. Thanks for being
such a fun group of people, who demonstrate the idea
that "fun" does not have to mean "irresponsible and
destructive". I appreciate you all very much (and
letterboxing, too).

ashby

Lightning and I had a good time going after that box the first time, and we had a good time watching the folks we were with find it the second time.  When we went the second time, there was a soccer game going on just feet from the box, so it was a lot of fun trying to cover for the person getting the box.  This one is a good example of a box that requires some subterfuge at times but that isn't foolishly placed so it is too difficult to get without discovery.
