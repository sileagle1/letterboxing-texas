More about Compasses (And Hey Mike, Guess what I got today!)

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-11-04 23:54:16 UTC-06:00
After our recent discussion about compasses, I was pleased beyond
words as I was having the same discussion with my mother today and she
mentioned that she still had my dad's old army compass.
Unfortunately, the lens is cloudy now, and apparently there is a part
missing from it (although I can't tell exactly what she was referring
to when she said that).

This particular compass is - (hmmm...trying to calculate in my head) -
45 years old or so. In my hand, it has the feel of a fine piece of
equipment. Something meant to last a lifetime, including some certain
amount of time on a battlefield. I HAVE to get this compass
refurbished and begin carrying it. It's wonderful. It doesn't have
the cool features I like about my Silva orienteering compass, but it
has the feel of quality and reliability and it is elegant in the
simplicity of its design. One purpose and one purpose only - to tell
direction.

In addition to the compass, she gave me his mapreading glasses (or
whatever they are called - I don't know). Like magnifying glasses on
stand, and you would set the stand on the map and look through the
glasses - I can sure see the benefit of these in reading topo maps. I
also got his splendid stopwatch. Wow. I'm really glad she never gave
these things to me when I was younger, because I feel I'm only just
able to really appreciate the workmanship behind them and the value he
placed in them during his lifetime.

My dad, whom many of you know I loved dearly and have the utmost
admiration for (see my Go Jacks! box for more), carried these items
and used them as daily tools of his trade as he taught mapreading and
surveying in the Army, and later as a Forester. I had lost hope that
I had the type of skills he valued in terms of navigation, yet through
scouting and letterboxing, I have managed to discover that they
weren't absent, only well hidden!
--
Barefoot Lucy
"It's not about footwear, it's about philosophy"
