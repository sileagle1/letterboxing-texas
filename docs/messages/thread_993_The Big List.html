From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2005-07-04 21:29:37 UTC
While we're having this discussion, I want to add my two cents. I
think the big list is fabulous. There can be a problem with
flaming, but I want you to show me a group of more than 2000 people
that doesn't have its misunderstanding and disagreements.

Generally, what is perceived as a flame is much more likely to be
miscommunication. That happens in my work life with email A LOT.
Far, far too often it is more a question of someone misunderstanding
someone's tone, because email/chat lists do not offer the nuances of
body language or voice pitch that help us to fully understand what
others are saying. I think anyone who has been around the Internet
at all probably knows EXACTLY what I'm talking about.

On the Big List there are very experienced boxers and newbies.
There are people of all different political views, religions (or non-
religions), ethnicities and sexual orientations. This makes for a
fabulous, diverse group. And what do all these people have in
common? They like to traipse around and look for plastic tupperware
with a little "prize" inside. The sociologist in me thinks that the
dynamic exchange of ideas that comes out of that construct is
terrific. That said, I think civility is called for. Those who
have a repeated history of complete incivility should not be
tolerated by the group.

And finally. I like hand-carved stamps. There, I said it. I
prefer a not-very-well-executed hand-carved stamp to a gorgeous
purchased one. I'll go to a box that has a bought stamp, because I
enjoy the experience. But, I think the placer should just mention
that fact in the clue. Full disclosure is a positive.

Didn't mean to ramble on. I guess that's what happens when a PR
person/sociologist/English major starts talking about group
dynamics. The sociology of groups was my absolute favorite
undergrad course, but that's another post!

Happy 4th all!

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-07-04 21:55:52 UTC-07:00
I think the old adage "If you have a choice of
assuming positive or assuming negative, assume
positive until you have a reason to do otherwise." is
a good adage to use with the big list, and email
communication in general. Someone said something in
direct response to one of my posts there the other day
that really almost got me going, but I took into
account the source and his penchant for very dry,
cutting humor. Knowing that I really enjoy his humor
when it isn't directed at something I said, I stepped
back and assumed that he was using his humor at that
time as well. If that wasn't the case, don't tell me
because I'm still blissfully ignorant. LOL

I also enjoy the big list, but I tend to stay off of
it if there are a lot of flames going on. This stuff
isn't real life enough for me to merit getting my own
outlook dragged down by it. But when it's clicking,
it makes very interesting people watching and I think
it's given me better insight into letterboxing.
Frankly, it's where I get my little dose of big world.
I'm on other lists with other folks who basically
think just like I do, do things I do, believe as I
believe, etc. It gets a little boring, so I enjoy
that list when it isn't being dysfunctional.
Unfortunately, it's been pretty dysfunctional lately
and I hope it cycles out of it all soon.

Dewberry, I'm glad you posted in support of the big
list because I realized after I posted my message that
I might have seemed pretty negative about it and I
certainly didn't want to come across as downing it.

Lucy
