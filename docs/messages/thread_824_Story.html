From: Susan Tashiro (stashiro@awesomenet.net) | Date: 2005-04-27 10:00:33 UTC-05:00
One of my well hidden boxes (or so I thought) was accidentally found recently by non-boxers.  I am passing the email I received from the girl on to the list.  This email was sent thru the LBNA website.
 
 Hello, what a story I have to tell you...

I met this guy two years ago and became really close to him, fallen in love and even though he lives in New York and I live in Texas.. we managed to work this whole long distance relationship by sending pictures and what have you. So we finally decide to meet at Garner because it is one of my most favorite places to vacation and as soon as we get there decide to go exploring by making our own trail and not following what is set for vacationers. So we come across this steep hill and slide down it and go walking through a dry river and soon enough reach a difficult path so we climb up and by then I am so very tired and out of breath. As soon as my boyfriend helps me up I spot a small plastic container and for some reason I became very curious and discovered it was one of your boxes. So we look and see names and stamps placed in it so we decide to write our own names and I left it a little something. So we write down your web address and it was an incredible moment.

Finding that box made our first time meeting so incredibly special because who wouldn't want to find a secret box while exploring nature?

Thank You for being so orginal.
Elida Gonzalez and Michael Fessel
 
This box was hidden well off the beaten track.  Obviously finding this box made a great memory for this girl.  New letterboxers??  I just hope they re-hid it well.
 
Have others received notes like this?
