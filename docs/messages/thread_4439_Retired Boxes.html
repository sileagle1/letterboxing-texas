Retired Boxes

From: DeDe (bluebutterflytexas@yahoo.com) | Date: 2010-03-13 15:10:20 UTC
On July 13, 2005, I planted my very first box at Love's Lookout. Lucy Locket had carved the stamp. which was one of her very first ones. It is missing, so I am retiring it for now, but look for a new box and a new carve in the near future. It was so much fun going there eating lunch and checking on the box. I was always looking at the stamp thinking how good LL had gotten at carving stamps.

Also, My Box at the Dallas Arboretum, Flowers, Birds, Leaves and Trees, planted Sept. 10,2005  is missing. My husband and I were there yesterday (awesome tulips! and Ferdinand the Bull) They have "cleaned" the area where the box was. As most of you know, this too was one of my very favorite places. Don't think I am going to place another box there anytime soon, but who knows. It is very difficult to find a "safe" place there.

I had to replace the logbooks in these two boxes several times over the years, and was always awed by who stamped in and how many Boxers visited these locations.
Thank-you!

Blue Butterfly

Oh no. My grandsons were the self-appointed guardians of your Arboretum box.
