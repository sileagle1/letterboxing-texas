From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-10-31 21:40:48 UTC
I understand why you're upset. I think you have a right to be. But,
there are lots of facets here.

If you pull your clues (which is certainly your right), then you
definitely drastically reduce the number of people who are going to
find your boxes and hopefully you protect them a little bit from the
casual, careless letterboxer.

If you leave your clues open & accessible, they are more likely to be
found by people who are new to the game and haven't done their
homework. This may put them in greater jeopardy. But, it also could
be your box that inspires someone enough to really get into the game,
and then they could turn into THE planter in your area who plants cool
boxes for you to find.

And of course, even by asking people to write you there are no
guarantees. Some finders have found a lot of boxes, but have never
absorbed the conventions. They leave boxes out in the open, hurriedly
dashing to the next one, never making sure that the last one is hidden
or sealed correctly. That I've seen repeatedly. (And that's almost
more frustrating than a newbie who hasn't yet learned).

It's the ongoing struggle...and people take different approaches based
on the level of accessibility/risk factors. You are the only one who
can weigh the trade-offs and come up with an arrangement that you're
comfortable with.

Personally, I publish almost all my clues (although there are a few in
unconventional spots). I do make some of them harder than others.
And if boxes go missing, I retire them and usually plant something
new. I don't expect my boxes to last forever. And although I have a
moment of mourning when one goes missing, it doesn't usually last too
long before I've thought of something else I'd like to plant.

dewberry

We did that with our 4-H group. We met with our kids and talked about it. Next meeting, each family carved a box stamp AND each member made a sig. stamp.
