Might I make a suggestion to the group.

 

Beside being a retired soldier and hiker, I am also a nationally ranked shooter, and somewhat of a survivalist.  I cannot help it, military training in a tough qualification forced me to develop these skills.  The alternative wasn't pleasant. 

 

I am also a ham radio operator and trained storm spotter.

 

Recently - because of some disaster training, we had a chat on-line about disaster preparedness.  My part was my "BUG-OUT" kit.  This is a large closet I have filled with everything I would need if some disaster struck and we needed to "bug-out" quickly.  BUT - that very closet would also be useful if we needed to shelter in place were bugging out impossible or not warranted.

 

Perhaps we could start a discussion on survival equipment here on the list.  I would be happy to present my BUG-OUT list to the group.  And who knows - there certainly will be things I missed.  It can only help me too.

 

This might be a good thing to start in a few weeks - once things settle a bit more.

 

Somebody let me know if it is a good idea, or forget it.  And when to start.

Subject: [letterboxingtexas] Checking in, finally!

We just got electricity about an hour ago here in Coldspring, so this
is our first chance to check up and check in. I'm glad to hear
everyone seems to have made it through IKE. We lost power at 4:30 AM
Saturday, and so we've lost all the food in the refrigerators and
freezers, but that's the worst damage we incurred. We haven't been
able to buy gasoline here and the only grocery store had limited
supplies and no power, so we've been living on canned goods and such
and staying close to home. Our children in Humble and Kingwood still
don't have power, but thank God we are all safe. We are fortunate that
the cold front came in and saved us from having to deal with the heat.
I'm planning an afternoon at Double Lake to check on letterboxes in the
next day or so, and I'll report on that when I can.

Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter
