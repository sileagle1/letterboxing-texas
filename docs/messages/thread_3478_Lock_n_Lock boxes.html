Re: [letterboxingtexas] Lock'n'Lock boxes

From: Maribeth Jagger (cjagger405@earthlink.net) | Date: 2008-04-08 01:32:31 UTC-05:00
I really like the Staedler tools (which I ordered from Mr. Art on the internet). Fearing I would lose one or they would get dull, I bought another set at Asel Art in Dallas (Plano) recently when I saw them. Today, a letterboxer told me that I can pinch the #1 nib to make it narrower for more detailed carving if I heated it first and pinched it with pliers. So I tried that since I have two #1's and it worked. I had to carve a very intricate stamp for an LTC ring, and I was pretty pleased with the final product. That stamp is going to eventually end up as a planted letterbox in Denton for the North of the Lake event in June.
 
I was able to purchase my Lock n Locks from Target until just recently. They've always been difficult to find in the right size. The new "improved" LnL's have a little vent at the top to keep food fresh.... but logbooks soggy. I'm afraid they may be phasing out the original style, so if you find any... buy lots of them!
Puddle Splasher (who is up in the middle of the night because I started carving that stamp and couldn't make myself stop!)
