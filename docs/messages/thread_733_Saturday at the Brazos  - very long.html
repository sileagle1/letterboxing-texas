Saturday at the Brazos - very long

From: celticlions (celticlions@comcast.net) | Date: 2005-03-21 22:17:53 UTC-06:00
The whole CelticLions crew had a wonderful time Saturday, beginning with
coffee and beignets at Boxdn's site, where we finally could place faces with
all the names we've been reading for almost a year! Hawk and Krohm, Barefoot
Lucy, DrooP, Corazon & Navigator, HummerLAdy, CalicoRose, BusyBee...the list
goes on... It's funny how you conjure up an image of a personality and it's
great fun to finally have a physical entity to attach it to!
After coffee, we hurried back to our campsite to catch a little family time
- despite all of us living within 45 minutes of each other, we don't always
have a chance to just unwind together.
We finally packed up the cornbread & CLUE and headed over to the gathering.
It was chaos! We were a little late and the frenzy of exchanges was in full
swing. CactusLicker & I kept pointing out to each other the faces which
matched names we'd seen in logbooks or on the Texas list- Silver Eagle &
Baby Bear, Kissin'Fishies, DrooP, Catpawz, HapyHomeschoolers, BusyBeeFamily,
and then we found out that Wanda had arrived!
We rushed over to meet her - CactusLicker & I have just loved her posts to
the main list forever - they were our first introduction to real
letterboxing stories and we learned a great deal and were totally
entertained by them! As CL says, she is the "Goddess of Letterboxing"!
I went back to getting the CLUE stuff ready, and trying to make sure we
could begin at 2:00 as posted. After the envelopes were finally all handed
out, I was momentarily distracted by thinking we had lost the three solution
envelopes that Stillwater had just pulled from the box - okay, more than
distracted - I was hyperventilating at the thought we might have passed out
ALL the stamps!
It was fascinating to watch the various ways folks went about playing the
game! Krohm was our Grand winner, coming in first, followed by Hawk,
Clueless Collector, Silver Eagle and Wanda! (The list is missing, so I know
there are two more winners, but don't have the names - please let me know!)

Back at the campground later that night we played some serious "Monday
Morning Quarterback" trying to jot down ideas & suggestions for whoever
organizes the NEXT Clue game!!! (If you have ideas, please write to us off
list at celticlions@comcast.net and we'll include them).

That night we all settled down, with family members divided between the
Winnebago we have just inherited from my in-laws and a tent. Sometime around
midnight, I woke up to what sounded like a house ( shades of OZ)had fallen
on the camper...not a tree, because it wasn't in just one area but ALL OVER
the roof of the Winnebago. I opened the door to the bathroom, and the
plastic cover to the skylight was raining down in huge chunks, followed by
gushing rainwater.

We looked out the side door and suddenly realized that the awning - which
had been attached to aluminum poles staked into the ground in four places -
had been torn up out of the ground and flipped backwards, hardware and all,
over the roof of the Winnebago.

Now, as I mentioned, this is a vehicle we were given by my in-laws - it's
been around a long time - and we discovered that it has leaks in a few
places...we spent most of the rest of Saturday night placing bowls & buckets
and towels to catch the drips...and making room for the tenters who were
soaking wet. Please picture the 6 of us plus two dogs in the Winnebago
bumping into each other...

One funny note - one of the things The Gentleman grabbed from the tent to
"save" was the "Boxing on the Brazos" shirts from BoxDN!

Ruby T.
