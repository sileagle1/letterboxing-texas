Re: [letterboxingtexas] Re: Then came the rain...

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2010-04-02 13:38:50 UTC-05:00

The City of Jacksonville has a very nice campground right on the lake, but if you are considering it, you need to reserve right away. Tomato Fest is a busy weekend and the campground is usually full all summer long. Here's the website for it:

http://www.jacksonville-texas.com/Residents/LakeJacksonville/LakeJacksonvilleCampgrounds/tabid/278/Default.aspx

The number to reserve is 903-589-3510.

Otherwise, the closest campground is the Texas State Railroad park in Rusk. Gotta say - I'm not real impressed with it anymore - it's okay just not as nice as it used to be. There's a nice KOA in Rusk. Rusk is about 18 miles from Jacksonville. If you like rustic camping (i.e. no water, no power) I know some private land where you could camp that's pretty nice, and also on the lake.

The first year, I had a Cub Scout booth, so I just had a box at my booth. We didn't eat.

The second year, my daughter was really needing kitchen stuff and Maydelle Queen Bee is a Pampered Chef consultant, so we had a Pampered Chef party at my house. I planted boxes to lead folks in rather than giving directions. And we ate.

The third year, last year, I involved several of my friends and boxers had to find them and do or answer or say something to get the clues to find the boxes. And we ate.
