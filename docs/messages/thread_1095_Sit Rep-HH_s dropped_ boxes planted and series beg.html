From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-07-23 14:22:52 UTC-07:00
My son and I ran a box out to a specific location
today and planted a couple more on our way home.

Michelle kindly agreed to take Ant M to Kansas, so my
son and I went and planted a box along her route so
she could pick up Ant M and take her on to Kansas for
tornado season. I'll post the clues to that one
tomorrow as soon as I know she's passed through so Ant
M doesn't accidentally get picked up by someone else.
I WILL say though - it's somewhere in Buffalo, along
with a couple new boxes within 10 miles of Buffalo.

My LSS husband has been wanting me to do a series of
brands - you know, like ranch brands? On the drive to
Buffalo, I spotted a couple of brands I could carve
and drop on my way home, so that's what I did. I
invited anyone who wants to contribute to this series
to go right ahead. I'm calling it the Branding Iron
Series. The nice thing about cattle brands is they
are usually easy to draw because they are intended to
be almost a signature, not just on the rumps of
cattle, but in correspondence and notes and so forth.
So....for you folks who are a little afraid of
carving, why don't you go out and find a ranch with a
distinctive brand and add your effort to this series?
I really like the idea of doing branding irons because
they are so closely identified with Texas!

In addition to the host box I planted and the HH I
left there, and the two Branding Iron Series boxes, I
also may have accidentally left a little bit of a pest
problem in one box - oh well, Time to Spray!
