From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-12-25 20:19:48 UTC-06:00
I've waffled for a couple of years now about getting a Dremel.  I mainly wanted one because some folks say they carve great with them.  That seemed like a feeble reason for me to get a power tool - on a MAYBE I will like carving with it, but I've really gotten to looking at them in the past year and just about had myself convinced I could use it for other stuff too.  THEN - Dremel introduced the CORDLESS, STYLUS tool and I was sold.  I narrowed down my "what I want for Christmas" to an iPod and a Dremel, so I left it to Fearless Fox to decide.  He opted for the Dremel!
 
So we finally got our Christmas stuff all done today (we globetrot on Christmas Day) and I scrambled up some scrap rubber, found a corner with good light and went to town trying to carve.  This is too cool!  I love it!  And I think once I get the right attachments, it will be awesome.  Lightning is surprisingly diplomatic for 10 years old, but he popped off with a "Now you won't be hurting yourself all the time".  Hmmm...I suppose he was referring to my inate ability to carve my hands up while I'm carving stamps?

Re: [letterboxingtexas] Re: Got me a new TOY!

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-12-25 20:57:58 UTC-06:00
Well, I'm going to regret this I know, because it's going to show how ignorant I really am about this!  But what the heck - we can all look back at this and laugh about what a newb Lucy is about this new toy in a couple of months when I have it figured out and have bought something like a thousand attachments for it and a dozen books about how to use a Dremel.
 
I've actually tried all the tips that came with it.  Even the ones that would obviously not be very good.  What the heck, right?  The highlights...
 
I first tried out the threaded tip mandrel, and it took off like a shot across the rubber.  I had to keep a very firm hand with it, but I managed to peel away a good bit of rubber quickly.  I also managed to get some tight corners.
 
The screw mandrel makes absolutely perfectly round holes in the rubber.
 
The carbide cutter didn't work at all well.
 
The one I've found that works really, really well though is an engraving cutter.  It takes a light touch and can get into tight spaces with excellent precision. 
 
What I really like best so far is that it doesn't require a tight grip (except on that threaded tip mandrel), and it doesn't eat away a lot of rubber.  The problem with how I carve now is I tend to have a heavy hand and I cut deep.  My stamp images are pretty good, but the stamp itself looks pretty rough (as was discussed in a previous thread - like I whittled them).  With this, it seems I'll be able to get a lot better control over the depth of my cut. 
 
My husband also got me a circular saw attachment and a plane attachment.  I had no idea why the plane, except that we have Pinewood Derby coming up and I figured I could use it on cars.  Finally he told me I can fix the front door now, which requires that we throw our weight into it to open since the wet season has kicked in and the door has swollen.  I told him this is a FUN tool, not a work tool, but I'm sure I'll do it anyway just to see how it works at that.  I'm blown away by all the cool things you can get to go with a Dremel.
 
On another note, I'm thoroughly impressed with Dremel's documentation.  I read the book as far as the Operation Instructions, then started experimenting.  After I finished that, I picked the book back up, turned a few pages and found a detailed description of each attachment, what its purpose is, what material it works best on and what size shank it has.  Turn another page and it has more detailed information on how best to use certain attachments. 
 
Nice.
