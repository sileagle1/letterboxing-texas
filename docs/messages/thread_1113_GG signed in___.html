From: celticlions (celticlions@comcast.net) | Date: 2005-07-27 16:30:37 UTC-05:00
Be still my heart!
While I was hanging out on the couch for the last few days, feeling
absolutely wretched, our elusive Gryzzled Gryphon signed in to the Gryphon
Bait box dedicated to him/her and wrote a most erudite and courteous note to
us!
Several of us had made all kinds of conjectures about the nature of this
famous Texas letterboxer - analyzing the size of the handwriting, trying to
decipher the code, tracking where & when Gryphon appeared at various boxes -
obviously when you present a group of letterboxers with any kind of mystery
we just can't help being compulsive about solving it!
Lucy, BusyBee and Dewberry - you will not be surprised that your guesses
about his/her character seem to be right on target - if emails are anything
to go by! The note we received was incredibly modest, charming and
intelligent.
Soooo...now we just need to persuade him/her that we are actually harmless
(!!!) and would love to welcome this infamous letterboxing specter to the
Texas Letterboxing list!

I'm "blind-copying" this note to dear GG, in hopes this will be the
invitation necessary to prompt a new membership - but honoring his/her
privacy at the same time by concealing the address.

Ruby Tuesday
(Do you suppose this means we have a chance of attracting the Beignet-Eating
Aliens, too???)



Re: [letterboxingtexas] GG signed in!!!

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-07-27 15:10:49 UTC-07:00
That is WONDERFUL! What a gift to receive while you
were feeling so badly...

I suppose I don't mind if he wants to remain
anonymous, but I do want him to know how much richer
he has made letterboxing in Texas! To my knowledge he
was the first person ever to find one of my boxes -
and I waited months to find a log entry and was
beginning to despair of ever having one discovered.

It has been such fun to know there is a boxer who
ferrets out and logs even remote boxes such as A
Family Affair - and how much fun it was for me to know
that he and I had been boxing the same area the same
day!

And I have two questions (at least) for him - 1) Why
has he never hidden any boxes of his own? and 2) May I
please see his logbook? Wouldn't that be interesting?
Has he ever boxed out of Texas? And
ooooohhhhh....does he have any exchanges????
Hmmmm....

When I emailed the placers of a couple of HH's I found
week before last, I made sure to tell them of their
own good fortune in having a GG stamp in their
logbook. One of the placers was very excited, having
heard us mention GG on the big list. He even went off
on his own internet search, trying to figure out who
he is (but of course, he didn't get any further than
we did!).

I'm glad to know that the nature of his messages have
perpetuated our image of him!

Lucy
