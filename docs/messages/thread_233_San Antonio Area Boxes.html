San Antonio Area Boxes

From: remymom (remymom@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-07-26 05:51:05 UTC
I am in San Antonio for the weekend... and had a great time
letterboxing on Saturday, despite the heat! I came down from
Amarillo, and stopped off in Lubbock real quick- so here's the boxes
we did the past 2 days:

Buddy Holly letterbox (Silver Eagle's)-Lubbock- 7/23 it's alive and
well! We also ran into a cute but scrawny stray black cat while we
were there- he's apparently living in the bushes at the base of Buddy
Holly's sculpture. We did our stamp exchange, put the microbox back
in its place (and kept the kitty from seeing- hehe) and then went to
the convenience store at the corner nearby to get a small can of cat
food and brought it back out for the kitty. He was HUNGRY, and
devoured it. My sons loved the kitty more than the box, but I was
happy to find the box. :-)

Raymond Russell Park box- (Angi's- Anderson Family I believe)- San
Antonio- 7/24 I know the web site said it was missing since Dec 03
but we went to look anyway- and didn't find it. I really do think
it's missing because the area rocks looked somewhat rearranged (well-
alot rearranged) and we could see a spot where it might've been but
nothing there. So, we're confirming that it's still missing.

Friedrich Warbler Box (Silver Eagle)-San Antonio- 7/24 It's alive and
well! I took a friend with me (and without my kids) and it was his
first letterboxing experience where we found something (the Raymond
Russell one was his first hunt) and he thought it was REALLY cool to
find a box and see what it's all about. :-)

Texas Armadillo box- also in Friedrich Wilderness Park (Anderson
Family)- San Antonio- 7/25 We looked for this one today (it got too
hot to keep going yesterday so we'd stopped even though we were
already out there) Anyway, this one was marked as missing Dec 03 on
the web site too, but we wanted to check and we didn't find it. Found
the tree mentioned in the clues, but nothing there, and looked all
around nearby and downhill too- in case it might've been washed off
by runoff- but nothing. So we're confirming that it is still missing.

Comanche Feather (Wanda's)- San Antonio- 7/24- VERY cool place! The
box is alive and well too. I wish I had taken a camera with me for
the views we had from up there. I never knew there was that stone
tower out there. :-)

Peanuts box (Baby Bear's)- San Antonio- 7/24- Nice easy hunt! Saw 2
baby deer while we were out there (and a few CRAZY joggers who were
out running in near-100-degree temps) and it was the first box that
my friend actually found himself! :-) I hope he is hooked, but I'm
not sure if he will sign on and get a nickname and all that though.
He did sign all the logs with mine, and if you ever see a "Kalel"
it's my friend.

Anyway, that was our exciting weekend of hunting. If I get out to do
more I will report back.

And for those who are on here alot... is it important if I email the
box owners directly also, or will posting here be enough to notify
everyone? If I need to email people directly, it'd be no trouble.

Thanks everyone for the fun boxes!

Bunnybee from Amarillo, TX
