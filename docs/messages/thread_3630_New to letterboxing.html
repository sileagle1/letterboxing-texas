Hi, I just joined the group. My trail name is ToniaO and I live in
Uvalde. The first box that my husband and I found is the Barney's
Toilet Seat Museum box. I'm 51 years old and a native Texan, born and
raised in Big Spring. I lived in Lubbock for 18 years. I'm a
vegetarian and my spirit animal is a rabbit. So incorporated those
facts, I'm using this as my stamp:

http://i269.photobucket.com/albums/jj67/ToniaO/lb_stamp.jpg

I have ten thumbs when it comes to "artistic" endeavors except cross-
stitching. I do plan to try to carve my own stamp but I doubt I can
make one as cute as my rabbit!

Hi Tonia, I'm Susan (aka CalicoRose).   Welcome to Letterboxing and the group.   I wish you had been in Uvalde when I was really "in" to letterboxing.  I was the only one and I finally gave up since it's not much fun to find your own boxes.   Corazon and I walked around the Pioneer cemetery trying to find a spot for a box but we didn't plant one...don't remember why.  Let me know about the Dead Wood, please.  Since I left Carrizo Springs 2 years ago, my boxes in that area have been orphaned.  I also planted a box dedicated to Popeye at Crystal City.  As far as I know, there has only been one person to find it.  I'm not sure it is even there any more.  There are lots of places in the Uvalde, Leakey, Rocksprings and even Carrizo Springs and Crystal City area.  If you ever want ideas for planting just drop me a note and I'll be glad to help.
 
CalicoRose

Re: [letterboxingtexas] Re: New to letterboxing

From: Tonia Ollerton (tonialmo@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-06-25 06:44:04 UTC-07:00

Hi, Susan.  I too wish I'd gotten into letterboxing earlier!  I only found out about it when one of my friends on vegweb mentioned it and gave the link to letterboxing.org.  We moved to Uvalde from Lubbock in August, 2004.  My husband has been teaching in San Antonio so *if* we can get our house ready to sell soon and *if* we can sell it, we'll be moving.  Those are ultra-big ifs, though. We should still be here through the summer.  We plan to go look for the boxes in Memorial Park when I get off work today.  It'll be about a 100 degrees and there shouldn't be very many people at the park!  I'm going to take our digital camera, snap pictures of where we think the boxes should be (if they're not there) and email you and Corazon (Connie) to confirm we found the right places.

 

We have a trip to Big Spring to visit my mother planned for late July.  Last night I was mapping out our route and looking for letterboxes that we might find along the way.  We'll go up Hwy 55 to Rocksprings (Mohair Sam), then from there to Sonora (Sonora Butterfly), San Angelo (Fort Concho and Lily Pad), Sterling City (Windmill City) and a slight bit out of the normal way to Garden City (Gone to Texas) and then Big Spring (A Deaf Artifact).  We'll go over to Midland and look for the two at the Petroleum Museum there and the two at the Sibley Nature Learning Center.  Then on the way home we'll go to Abilene to look for the Buffalo Gap one and also visit the Abilene Zoo because the exhibit with the Meerkats wasn't open when we went last time.  Then down to Eden (Cow Patty Bingo) and Menard for the Lost Jim Bowie Mine one and then get any at Rio Frio and Concan that we haven't already found.  We plan a weekend at Garner State Park weeekend after this coming one so we should be able to find most of the ones there and near there.

 

If any one knows of any other ones that would be near those that I haven't listed, please let me know.  I'm thinking about putting one in Big Spring and getting my niece to check on it once in a while.  Either at the Spring in Comanche Trail Park or the museum on the former Webb Air Force Base or maybe at the Vietnam Memorial which is also on the base site. 

 

I have such big plans!
