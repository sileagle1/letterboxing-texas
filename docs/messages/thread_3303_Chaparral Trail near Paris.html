Here's a little info from a Texas Co-Op story on the Chaparral
Trail...

http://www.texas-
ec.org/publications/texascooppower/archive/300train.aspx

Excerpt: The Chaparral Rail Trail, a nonprofit corporation, sought
government grants to develop a hike-and-bike from Farmersville to
Paris, but opposition in Pecan Gap blocked the funding. The town's
aging citizenry feared liability risks and thought maintenance costs
would outweigh benefits. "These people aren't going to ride a
bicycle," Cheney observes. "We figured it was a lot of expense for a
few to enjoy." The nonprofit group is taking a piecemeal approach to
developing 56 miles of right of way as funds become available.

Rail trail projects have expanded nationally since 1983 when Congress
created a railbanking program. Railbanking prevents rights of way
from reverting to pre-railroad owners or otherwise being sold or
broken up. Communities use the routes as interim recreational trails,
hoping that someday another railroad might replace lost tracks. The
chances of that actually happening are extremely remote, experts
agree.
