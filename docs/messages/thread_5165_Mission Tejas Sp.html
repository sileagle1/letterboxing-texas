Mission Tejas SP
I got a report from a Geocacher that my box at Mission Tejas SP had been damaged in a controled burn. I decided to make the trip down there Tuesday, and printed out all the boxes in that park, with the intentions of checking on as many as possible. There have been several controlled burns all over the park, so it was difficult looking for some of the boxes without the landmarks in the clues. Here's my report:
Birdie, birdie by Bailey's Bunch.....Found the post, did not find the box (burn area)
Dogwood Box...Puddle Splasher....Found box, part of it was melted, but I saved the logbook and stamp. I replaced the box, bags and added some index cards just in case.
Indian Trails - Quilt Block Series by Lone Star Quilter...part of the box was melted, I replaced the box, and bags. the logbook and stamp was burned on one edge. I left those in there, since they are usable.
Nabedache by Boots Tex....Couldn't find this one.
Spanish Flag by Silver Eagle.....almost 100% sure this one is missing.
Tejas Mission Impossible by Silver Eagle...this area has changed A LOT!! (burn area) No stump, I dug in the hole where there had been a stump, but to no avail.
Shiner Bock: A Texas Tradition by Viewfinder......I am sure I found the tree now mentioned in your clue, but no box.
I tried to find ASSUALT by Lucy Locket, and my box Texas Caddo Indians : Fire
The landmarks for these boxes are gone, so I will have to go back later with Lucy Locket and try to find more boxes. Event the trail across the pond, has a name now, Cemtery Hill Trail, but Lightening Trail is still there.
Blue Butterfly

    I spent yesterday at Mission Tejas State Park, and I’m sorry to say I was only able to find one box: Puddle Splasher’s Dogwood box.  The others were clearly missing or the landmarks were gone and we couldn’t find the right spots.  The boxes with clues going around the pond were the most obviously missing.  There is no ground cover in this area and  all that remains of the stumps are holes in the ground.  Logs which were supposed to have boxes were typically charcoal.  I’d say the boxes in this area were lost to fire but most of the landmarks were still recognizable. 

     

    On the other side of the park (by the El Camino Real), I had a lot of trouble following clues and finding landmarks.  The signs have clearly changed and the trails don’t match the maps or the clues.  So some of these boxes may be there but we didn’t have any luck finding them.   There were a couple we missed looking for and frankly I was too fed up to go back for them this morning.   It was a discouraging trip – we had much better luck in Memorial Park!  Anyway, here is a list of the boxes we looked for:

     

    Assault by Luck Locket – missing (found the stump and remains of logs but no box)

    Indian Trails Quilt Block Series by LSQ – missing (found tree and rock)

    Wearing the Cinco Peso by Boots Tex – missing (found the very cool barbed wire fence which is only supported by the trees which grew up around it.  We went way up the hill and checked every log we could find)

    Texas Caddo Indians: Fire by blue butterfly – missing (found tree right where it was supposed to be)

    Texas Heritage: Eugene C. Barker by lionsmane– missing (this whole area is cleared of ground cover

    Pretty Kitty by Atoy Adventurers – the only landmark we could find was post #5, signs and benches referenced are gone

    Dutch Oven Delight by Corazon – not quite sure where this is supposed to be but we found a geocache looking for it

    Rachel’s Gift by LSQ – No luck finding the strange looking tree.  Several trees have fallen in this area so it may be gone

    Tejas by Baby Bear – missing (not much left of the charred log)

    Pop it up by Bailey’s Bunch – couldn’t find the right spot, there were no trails to the left only a clearing for the powerline and we didn’t find it there (FYI – this had a find in 2012)

    A Caddo Legend “Coyote and the origin  of Death” – trails don’t match clues (although it has a find in 2012)

    Dogwood box by Puddle Splasher – found it!

    Top-Down Texas by Puddle Splasher – missing (found remains of stump)

    Tejas Mission Impossible by Silver Eagle – missing (stump is now a VERY deep hole)

    Birdie, Birdie by bailey’s bunch – missing (no recent fires in this area, the whole area is covered with sweet gum trees about 6 feet tall)

     

    Regards,

    Open Space

    MISSION TEJAS STATE PARK – AUGUST 31, 2015 SIT-REP

    By Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter

     

    I’ll tell you right up front that we found only one of the 20 boxes on our list. We got to the park at about 9;30 AM.  It was a beautiful day for letterboxing.  The park is generally in good condition.  Some of the trails are getting a little overgrown and we are beginning to realize that it is heavily chigger-infested.  The control burn changed a lot of clues by eliminating landmarks and some changes have been made by the park.  Blue Butterfly and Open Space both filed sit-rep reports in 2013, and we considered them when deciding priorities. We had a lot of boxes on our list and were pretty sure we wouldn’t be able to check on all of them, so we made the decision to eliminate a few from the list which we thought were very likely missing.  They are, along with our reasoning:

     

    Birdie, Birdie Baileys Bunch – It has 3 consecutive “attempts” and wasn’t found by Open Space

    Assault Lucy Lockett – wasn’t found by Blue Butterfly nor Open Space, 2 consecutive “attempts”

    Texas Caddo Indians Blue Butterfly – wasn’t found by Open Space, not found at all since 3-6-2009

    Spanish Flag and Tejas Mission Impossible Silver Eagle – archived by Silver Eagle and not found by Blue Butterfly nor Open Space

     

    We checked on a few others that met the same reasoning simply because we walked right by their original location.  We attempted to find the following boxes:

     

    Pop It Up Baileys Bunch – Simple directions, but could not find the trail that goes left off the main trail.  It has been allowed to revert to forest.

    A Caddo Legend Baileys Bunch – Landmarks could not be found, couldn’t make clues fit

    Texas Trail Riders SandiBox – Landmarks could not be found

    Dogwood Box Puddle Splasher – Found the white rock, did not find the box

    Top-Down Texas Puddle Splasher – Found 2 holes, either could have been the stump, but nobox in either

    Little Dutch Girl Adams Trails – No X marks the spot, found no box

    Tejas Baby Bear – Log is completely gone, as well as the box

    Shiner Bock: A Texas Tradition Viewfinder – No reference point, might have found tree, no box

    Dutch Oven Delight Corazon – The bridge has been extended and modified to eliminate the steps

    Tejas Bell Princess Aurora – Box not found, replaced, new clues will be posted (planter is our granddaughter)

    Indian Trails Lone Star Quilter – Box not found, replaced, new clues will be posted

    Rachel’s Gift Lone Star Quilter – Box not found, replaced, new clues will be posted

    Wearing the Cinco Peso Boots Tex – Box not found, replaced, new cues will be posted

    Nabadeche Boots Tex – Box was damaged in burn, picked up and returned to placer.  It was replaced, new clues will be posted.

     

    The only box that we found was The Pipebenders – Fraggle Rock Series by Will’s World.  It was alive and well, although not found in several years and with a couple of “attempts”. 

     

    As Silver Eagle suggests, Mission Tejas could stand some restocking.

    We enjoyed getting out and the park is still beautiful.  How long has it been since the burn, at least 2 years, maybe 3?  It's recovered completely, which is the purpose of a controlled burn.  It looks as good or better than before.  The only evidence of the fire is charring at the base of trees, that kind of thing.  Stumps and logs were completely destroyed, but there are plenty left.  We just need to understand that this is a part of the natural process and that plastic boxes won't last forever.  I think that, after an event, most of the boxes have been found by most of the letterboxers, and after that, that don't get many finds, especially in out-of-the-way places like this.  I don't think they're even worth replacing after a couple of years unless they are special to the planter.  Just let them go and place new ones, which might entice the regular boxers back to the place to find and place new boxes.
