delurking for a moment I am...

About asking permission to place boxes, so far when I've actually
asked permission to place a box I've received a 100% enthusiastic &
supportive response! I was rather intimidated about even raising
the question at first but after I overcame the fear, I put together
my best 'sell' on why it was imperative a letterbox be placed in
that venue. It worked perfectly! In one case the lady not only
agreed but even begged for several dozen sets of clues, info sheets
& encouraged me to speak with the local historical society &
elementary school principal about the hobby--to encourage others to
participate in it. Another instance I used the Victorian history of
letterboxing as a great segue into why that particular Victorian
botanical conservatory really needed a letterbox! It worked like a
charm. Unfortunately, a recent renovation to the conservatory
eliminated its perfect hiding place and now I'm trying to find
another spot in which to hide the letterbox.

Of course, there are some places where I haven't yet asked
permission (various parks & what nots). I'm really careful about
not knowingly violating any existing policy that prohibits hobbies
such as letterboxing within a park or region. I also try to take
great care when placing a letterbox so as not to cause unnecessary
damage to the surrounding environment or a potential threat to
animals/wildlife in the area. In light of our country's current
heightened security awareness, I'm also very cognizant to avoid
placing boxes that might create a 'security frenzy' that may require
a call to the local E O D element. Ex: don't hide boxes close to
interstate bridges/overpasses, major tourist attractions, major
traffic thoroughfares, etc. My particular region is teeming with
these sorts of spots.

So, IMveryHO I've had a very encouraging & successful experience
when it comes to asking permission to place letterboxes (3 for 3).
If you create a solid set of persuasive tools before you go in to
ask, it usually helps in the negotiation process. I also like to
highlight the fact that Dartmoor Natl Park, the Connecticut State
Park/Forest system, the Biltmore Estate and numerous other 'official
venues' not only allow letterboxing on their premises but
vociferously encourage it, as well. This helps TREMENDOUSLY!!!

This is a great discussion topic!
