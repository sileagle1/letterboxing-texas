Our adventure

From: (kacmbc@aol.com) | Date: 2006-06-14 00:05:41 UTC-04:00
We left The Woodlands at seven a.m.-Starbucks and LBNA clues in hand
The girls of Frye Team and their runner friend, ready to drive across this hot dry land! 
We took  242 to 59 and headed north to the San Jac county line. 
Hit our first letterbox- it was just after eight, right on target , we were doing fine!
 
Boots Tex “End of the Trail” – First Finders were we!
High fives all around and the girls screamed “yippee”!
After leaving our mark, we continued on the trail…
Nothing could stop us…not rain, sleet or hail!
 
Then there were the spiders…big spiders…and their big webs…
 
But we pressed onward to find Lone Star Quilter’s Turtle Stampede
First Finders again..how lucky were we?
What a cute little bridge we sat on to stamp in the letterbox
No turtles today  but we did see some rocks!
 
Back to the car- more letterboxes to be found
Only this time we weren’t counting on seeing the hounds!
Yes, after we located Saint Hyacinth and started heading back,
Up ahead on the dirt trail were several dogs in a pack.
 
“Doggies!”  Exclaimed the girls, “Freeze!” said the Mom
RFC started clapping and got on her phone
And quickly dialed “information” and then The Red Barn Retreat
Boot Tex answered!!  This couldn’t be beat!
 
Explaining  the dilemma- he helped to calm their nerves.
He stayed on the phone as they walked past the curves.
Safely back on the side trail, heading to the car
We picked up the pace- it just seemed so far!
 
Once at the car, the main road had to be crossed
For on the other side was another letterbox!
The Merch Fire Tower- just 2/10 mile
Ahhh…an easy one!  We said with a smile!
 
We found it with ease, there were no tricks
But on the way back we discovered the ticks!
Crawling up shoes and clothes and in hair
Soon we realized – they were EVERYWHERE!
 
We quickly picked them off as fast as we could
And ran back to the car- out of the woods!
One more letterbox before we called it a day
LSQ’s Buzz saw- we were well on our way!
 
Pass the sawmill, turn right in the lot
Check the clues again before we began our trot.
Looking up we see a maroon suburban pull in
Not a stranger getting out- just a letterboxing friend!
 
Seems Boots Tex was worried after that phone call we made
So he came out the check on us to see that we were safe!
We visited for a while and met Princess Aurora, too
And so this poem, Boots Tex, is our thanks to you!
 
We found Buzz Saw- third first finder of the day
Then piled back in the car and headed on our way…
Home, this time, covered in sweat, spider webs and DEET
While the Frye girls sang Christmas carols and quickly fell asleep.
 
-Runs for Chocolate :)
