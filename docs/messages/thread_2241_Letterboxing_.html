From: Mike Olbrisch (mike2004@elp.rr.com) | Date: 2006-10-17 03:18:25 UTC

Well - it was a sad weekend.

One day last week we were out traveling with the in-laws from Germany, my
wife got a call on her cell-phone. Her best friend - her mentor - the
matriarch of the Riverwood line of Great Danes had just succumbed to cancer
after a 9-year battle. We were hoping she could hang on till we could get
to Tucson to say our good-bys. It just didn't work out. She was 60.

My wife was crushed. Our dogs are what they are today because this lady
took my wife aside and showed her why our dogs were losers, and how to breed
winners.

Then 1 hour later, my wife gets another call. The dog she sent out with a
professional handler, one that was bred between her and her friend, had won
the show and earned enough points to make it to the National Finals again
this year. I had to drive for a while, she couldn't. She was certain her
friend influenced that win from above.

The funeral Friday was hard. I am not a stranger to death, but it was
usually swift and violent for me. My wife took it hard. But Saturday was
even worse. We met with the surviving husband. A really great guy. He
can't keep the dogs. He has no clue. So we worked with him for hours. We
sold the top champion for $5000. We placed the puppies in good show homes.
It seems he didn't know he is sitting on about $20k in frozen semen. But we
talked about so much more. I lost it when he told us how she died in his
arms hugging him tight and telling him she loved him. I don't know how he
did it. I think I would rather die quickly in battle.

Anyway - we got a late start Saturday headed back to El Paso from Tucson.
We decided - what the heck - no kids - no dogs - no hurry. We cruised all
of SE-Arizona. Several letterboxes, some Geocaches, visited the Titan
Missile Museum, the Tombstone Courthouse, just stopped when we were tired,
and just enjoyed ourselves. Took time out to appreciate just being
together. Some of the letterboxes were neat.

Anyway - the only letterboxing I get to do is when we are on the road. So I
got to take every opportunity.

Next weekend - Albuquerque. Got to pick up that dog as the handler passes
through.

Do you think she might win the national this year? She is a good dog -
champion bred!

At least I picked up 17 letterboxes in the last two weeks. None within 100
miles of El Paso.

Mike.
