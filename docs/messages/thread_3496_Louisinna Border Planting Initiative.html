From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-04-15 13:01:11 UTC-05:00
Good point - there is one very active geocacher in Rusk County primarily (and a few others who are pretty active too), but he is also very amenable to letterboxing and in fact, carries a signature stamp with him.  HOWEVER, he has placed caches everywhere!  His name is Fire2Water.  I would suggest you go to www.geocaching.com and do a search for geocaches around zip code 75653 (Henderson) to get a feel for where in Rusk County he has planted.  You may or may not be able to tell some of them, but the map on the cache page is usually pretty helpful for narrowing down where they are.  Of course, if you have a GPSr you can load it up with coordinates, then when you get ready to plant you can get a coordinate for your chosen spot and look to see how close it is to any caches in the area.  A lot of work, but do-able.  The big thing is - keep your eyes open and if somewhere looks like a good hiding spot to you, it might well look like a good one to a geocacher, so check the area well to make sure you aren't tromping on someone's spot.  We manage to live fairly harmoniously with cachers in our area thankfully!
 
I've set up a blog page to describe the initiative, and I'll post bits here and there about the counties and areas (along with any helpful information anyone else provides to me).
 
http://www.tx-lahandshake.blogspot.com
 
I've also set up a database on the yahoo group website so you can post your boxes:
 
http://groups.yahoo.com/group/letterboxingtexas/database
 
Let's have fun with this!
