memorial park letterboxing guide

From: Christine Borgfeld (christine@borgfeld.net) | Date: 2013-05-04 20:47:30 UTC-05:00

I’d like some feedback on an idea.  In 2012 I created a map of memorial park with many of the boxes marked on it for my own use.  This will be updated for the event next weekend.  I think it would be valuable for others as well so I’d like to share it by creating a guide for the park.  Basically it will be a list of boxes with their approximate locations marked on the map.  This should help people plan visits since the number of boxes there can be overwhelming.  For puzzles boxes I’ll only list those which are explicitly listed as being in the park (some are not), and I’ll only mark locations if it doesn’t give any clues to solving the puzzle.  I “think” I have all of the puzzles solved.  I also know some boxes have restrictions, but people won’t be able to get clues to those anyway. 

 

Any thoughts or concerns about this?  I’ll put the guide on the files section of the yahoo group and my blog.

From: Vicki Bell (vicki@bellmotel.com) | Date: 2013-05-04 22:00:08 UTC-05:00
Hmmm...  Although I am the world’s worst at “clue” writing (I always write simple directions), I understand that letterboxing is a games of CLUES, not directions.  I have to admit that I get a huge sense of satisfaction out of realizing that I have found the beginning place of a clue that has been written as a clue, not as a direction.  I do enjoy the camaraderie of Events, where we share what clues we’ve deciphered that day with each other.  I am also a huge hypocrite, in that I would gladly take any directions to all the boxes in Memorial Park, but I wonder how planters who worded their clues as clues would feel about someone else posting directions to their boxes online.  I think I would have to check with each planter before posting directions online.  That being said, I truly appreciate that you have mapped out the boxes so we can check on them next weekend.  I’m interested in hearing what others think.  -- Bell Motel
