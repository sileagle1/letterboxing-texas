Palo Duro Canyon SP - LB Permit Now Required

From: Silver Eagle (sileagle@alltel.net) | Date: 2006-09-14 12:51:42 UTC
I got an email last week from the head ranger at this park requesting
that I fill out a permit for my Palo Duro Mouse 2 Letterbox. It seems
they now require it for this park, and I'm worried other TX parks will
soon require it, too. It might not be a big deal, but it could result
in many of my boxes being confiscated if they don't like my placement
and I am unable or unwilling to move them. My experience so far has
been that they don't like boxes placed too far off trail, since this
causes people to form new side trails. While I can understand this
concern, I don't want to hide my boxes too close to the trail since
they will inevitably get discovered by non-boxers. Also, the permit
is only good for 1 year, then it has to be re-evaluated. This could
amount to a lot of time and paperwork.

I also don't like putting my real name, address, phone number and even
Driver's License number on a form for a box I have little control over
once it is placed. What if someone puts something dangerous in one of
my boxes and I get blamed? I much prefer the stealth approach to
placing boxes in a respectful manner.

I sent them my address and they sent me the form, but I'm still
debating whether to fill it out and send it in. What do ya'll think?

Silver Eagle

From: Cindy Harland (droopwv@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-09-14 07:28:59 UTC-07:00
State parks in WV already have this rule and not only do you have to renew every year they want the letterbox MOVED every year to stop excess traffic to one area. I just bypassed it and planted my boxes and hoped for the best. I know that is probably not the best thing to do but so far the park rangers have not looked up the boxes or sent me any letters or emails. Moving a box every year is just out of the question. I understand the idea of protecting the parks but these parks are criss-crossed at all times of the year by visitors wanting to get off of the path. Kids play hide and seek at night (I've been there) and run all over the parks--in my opinion this is what they are for. I might get some flack for feeling that way but I can't see any change in the parks in WV and I grew up there and we use the parks a lot. I guess if the parks start checking the letterbox sight and finding the boxes we don't have a choice but to pull them or go with their rules.
DrooP

From: Mike Olbrisch (mike2004@elp.rr.com) | Date: 2006-09-14 23:31:38 UTC
 
WELL - time to throw my $0.02 in.
 
First - this is not a PARK REQUIREMENT.
 
It is a STATE REQUIREMENT.  TPWD headquarters implemented this about 3 years ago.
 
It came about because a Geocacher in El Paso put a Geocache on the edge of a cliff.  Someone went looking for it, (A very spry old man) and gave up when he saw the location.  He told the rangers it was too dangerous.  They agreed.  The cache owner was asked several times to move it to a safer place - or remove it.  It did not happen.  A year later, the flap started.  REMEMBER THIS - we will come back to it.
 
The rangers were about to BAN Geocaching, letterboxing, and anything resembling it.  When I heard about it (being a park volunteer) I stepped in and asked them WHY they were condemning a good group of people because of the troubles with one.  Kinda like ALL rangers are bad just because one is grumpy.
 
Anyway, I got involved too late to stop it completely.  It had already gotten to state HQ.  So a compromise was found, the contact sheet.
 
Anyone on here more than a few weeks knows I work with the local rangers on all my hides.  I even have permission to put a letterbox in Hueco Tanks.  In case you don't know it, THAT isn't something everyone could do.  That place is SO protected.
 
My opinion (Sorry MR Silver Eagle Sir) is if a ranger contacts you, they already have you and your box.  You have two choices.  Pull the box completely, or fill out the sheet.
 
Some will say there is a third choice - do nothing.  Remember the part I told you to remember?  Well, doing nothing about it once they know about your box and contact you, and it just makes ALL letterboxers look bad to the rangers.
 
I cannot and will not tell you what to do about this - you have to decide yourself. One Geocacher caused all the cachers (and letterboxers) to now fill out forms to continue to place in state parks.  What the final straw will be that gets us banned from state parks, I haven't a clue.  I just know it will not be me, I will work with the rangers.  They have a job to do too.
 
BTW - MR Silver Eagle Sir, your Mogollon Mask letterbox is still safe in my desk, as is two of my hybrids.  Those trails are still not passable, and not scheduled for work in the next several weeks.  I will continue to monitor and keep you informed.
 
JMHO - YMMV - DTTAH.
 
Mike.  El Paso.
