Introduction and possible visit to TX

From: Wisconsin Hiker (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2005-10-26 22:04:33 UTC
Hi!

I recently joined your regional group because we're considering
locations for a trip over Thanksgiving week. My hubby (Martini Man)
and I like to travel and also like to hike. Since we discovered
letterboxing in July 2003, we have had a lot of fun combining boxing
with our hiking. We've found boxes in 15 states and even found two in
Scotland (also have placed boxes in 7 states & Mexico).

What we'd like for a November trip is somewhere with reasonable weather
(not too cold and not too rainy) and with some good hiking. We prefer
to do hikes that are in the 3-15 mile range. I'm hoping some of you
could provide some advice and suggestions re: whether there are certain
areas in Texas you might recommend that have this type of hiking and
preferably letterboxes along the hikes.

Thank you!

Wisconsin Hiker

From: the carrascos (a.carrasco@earthlink.net) | Date: 2005-10-26 18:09:39 UTC-05:00
I would suggest the Texas Hill Country...one of the most beautiful places
you will ever see. There are many state parks close together, so you can
stay in one location and drive to other parks for day hikes. Our favorites
are Lost Maples state natural area and Enchanted Rock SNA, but there are
many other parks in this region with the type of hike you are looking for.
For outstanding beauty you cannot beat Enchanted Rock. Check out the Texas
Parks and Wildlife page for more info on each park. Inks Lake State Park
usually has camping, and you can stay there and drive to the other spots.
Silver Eagle and Baby Bear have boxes in almost all the parks in this area.
Come and see...you won't believe Texas looks like this! -Mother of Five

I have to second this - the Hill Country is

exchange.
