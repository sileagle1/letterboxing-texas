Favorite Letterboxing Moments

From: Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter (janet@redbarnretreat.com) | Date: 2007-01-03 14:43:57 UTC
We remember the places that we went to because of Letterboxing, like
the serene setting of Anahuac National Wildlife Refuge at sunset,
and finding intact letterboxes next to giant trees that had been
uprooted by Hurricane Rita in Martin Dies, Jr. State Park. Killough
Monument, despite the desecration, was still a special place.
Hiking five miles up and down a mountain at Lost Maples. The
thousands of Dogwoods in bloom at Jim Hogg Park in Rusk.
Accidentally finding GG's WOM box at Rusk while looking for another
one. Chalk Ridge Falls Park near Belton, which is gorgeous, but
only has one box, which was planted in 2002. Planting boxes in the
letterboxing wasteland of West Texas so El Lobo would have some to
find without having to drive 300 miles. Finding letterboxes in
Italy that our daughter placed just for us, and planting two others
there. We didn't go there because of letterboxing, but we made it a
letterboxing event. Attending our first gathering, the Great Book
Adventure, and meeting all those incredible letterboxers. And, of
course, trying to rescue damsels in distress along the Lone Star
Trail. There are many more, but these in particular, stand out.
