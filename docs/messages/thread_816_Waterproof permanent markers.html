Waterproof permanent markers

From: Susan Tashiro (stashiro@awesomenet.net) | Date: 2005-04-25 09:38:38 UTC-05:00
If you are looking for waterproof permanent markers to use for coloring our handcarved stamps, I recommend the Zig Memory System markers.  They are water based making them "rubber friendly".  Other permanent markers (i.e. Sharpies, Prismacolor, etc.) are solvent based and will eventually break down the rubber, unless the stamps are properly cleaned and conditioned after using these markers.
 
They are a bit on the expensive side, but are well worth the extra money.  The retail price is usually around $3.00 each.  They come in many colors.  These can be readily found in scrapbooking and craft stores.  They come in dual tips with a fine tip on one end and a bullet tip on the other and in a brush tip.  The pigment ink is acid free, archival quality, lightfast, waterproof, fade proof and non-bleeding.   They are put out by EK Success.  I found a good selection of them at  http://store.scrapbooking-warehouse.com/brushables.html  and http://store.scrapbooking-warehouse.com/zigwriterpens.html  (nope, I'm not affiliated with them, but I have ordered from them and have received great service.  They offer $5.99 flat rate shipping)
 
Hope this helps,
