Barefoot Lucy wrote:
 I don't know how to explain the difference between being bold and being foolhardy, but let me encourage you that being bold makes letterboxing a whole lot more fun!
 
While I've never been one to sit around and keep my fingernails clean, I haven't been one in my adult life to really go out on a limb either.  I think the turning point for me was a few years ago when I really wanted to go letterboxing in the summer (I hate heat) and my husband said "well let's go".  I whined a bit about the heat but he was tired of hearing about not being able to go letterboxing so he didn't let up until I got my rear end up and went.  I made a plan of attack and we headed out early one Saturday morning and stayed out all day finding boxes.  We were standing in the middle of some forest somewhere and I was drenched in sweat and stinky and I finally realized it wasn't killing me to get hot because I was having fun.  Being drenched just means cooling off and stinking of sweat was no big deal where we were.  It was all a matter of perspective.
 
Somehow that was a change in my mentality and I realized I could probably step out a little further on the limb and push myself in other areas as well.  Steep hills, rough trails, heavy brush, long walks, water - while I try to be smart about where I go, I've realized I can go a lot more places than I originally thought.  I've also learned that I bounce pretty well and I dry off nicely and just about anything that might happen to me can be fixed.  I've yet to have a serious injury, but bruises and scratches and sore muscles are temporary souvenirs of the journey that I can handle.  It's a lot more fun to just go than to stand on the trail wondering if I should.  If I can do it, so can you!
