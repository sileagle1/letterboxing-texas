Botanic Gardens Outing

From: jamarsarc (jamarsarc@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-11-02 14:08:47 UTC
I am a member of the homeschool group who was planning an outing to the
Botanic Gardens. I sent a note to your moderator explaining how we
would be going about searching for the boxes since there would be some
new people with us. There will also be some more experienced
letterboxers in the group.

There will be no more than one adult and two children at the box at one
time and the rest of the group (a rather small one at that) would be a
safe distance away and would look like any other group of children
enjoying the gardens with their friends and parent(s).

The logbooks would be handled only by one adult - no children will be
touching anything to do with the boxes - and everything will be put
back in place with the utmost care and concern for the safety and
integrity of the box.

There will be no stamping done on this day - if a family so desires,
they may come back to the box at another time and leave their stamp.

It was never my intent to cause any problems or trouble on any list(s),
I only wanted to introduce my friends to this amazing adventure!

I apologize for any misunderstandings that have come about and hope
there are no ill feelings towards our home school group. We have an
amazing bunch of families and they are all very respectful of other
people's property - no matter what activity we are doing.

Thank you for your concern and enjoy your day.

As one of the planters of some of the Botanic Gardens boxes, I appreciate your response on this issue.  Of course, when we put a box out there and post clues, we lose control over what happens to it, but we can't help being a little protective of our creations, especially some that are our personal favorites or are in particularly vulnerable locations because of high traffic, enthusiastic groundskeepers, etc.  This summer has been particularly bad even for some long-time letterboxes; many of us lost boxes that had been in place for a couple of years or more to unusual flooding , and then we've experienced a recent rash of apparently-careless finders not taking care to reseal bags and boxes or to replace them carefully, with resulting destruction of logbooks or boxes, so I think we're all a little super-sensitive to any potential problems.   Obviously, you have planned carefully and this outing should be a good experience for everyone involved, and I want to express again my personal appreciation for your care.   Thank you, and enjoy the day!
