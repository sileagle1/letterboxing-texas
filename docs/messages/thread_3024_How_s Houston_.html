From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-08-18 01:25:03 UTC
My bayou (White Oak) is much more full than usual, but well within
its banks. I'm not so worried about it. I am concerned about Dean &
what next week brings. In so many places around the state (like
here), the ground is saturated and the reservoirs are full.

Yesterday was bad in spots, fine in others (we hardly had any street
flooding in my area of town). It's all a function of which area gets
too much rain too quickly. And there's really no predicting that.
When I saw the CNN guy on the news talking about how badly Houston
was hit by Erin, that wasn't my experience at all (but unfortunately
it was fatal for some).

From: SandiBox (sandibox@msn.com) | Date: 2007-08-18 01:41:04 UTC
Brayes Bayou is a little higher than normal, but still looking pretty
good. And although we saw several heavy bands of rains, my side of
town (Sugar Land area) faired pretty well yesterday. But DH arrived
home from work yesterday a little early and a lot stressed by his
commute. He works in Deer Park, about 45 miles from home. That area
is closer to the coast and experienced power outages and some
flooding. He got home about half an hour early... but had actually
left work 2-1/2 hours early. He said the water was about 4 feet deep
in some places. Even in his truck, he had to back-track and find
alternate routes. The news showed several cars in that area
completely under water.

I am also concerned about Dean adding more water to already saturated
ground, and to waterways that are already above normal levels. Keep
us in your prayers.
