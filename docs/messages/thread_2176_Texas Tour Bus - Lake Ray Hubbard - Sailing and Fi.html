Texas Tour Bus - Lake Ray Hubbard - Sailing and Fishing

From: janet clark (jayceeinbedford@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-09-24 18:51:04 UTC-07:00
I'm going to make this short and sweet so that I can get this onto the airwaves.
 
After driving through Dallas (and I've got some stories to tell about that drive), the passengers decided they wanted to see what else they could do on a Sunday afternoon, they shopped their pocketbooks empty in the West End.
 
I thought of the great windy day we were enjoying. What better to go with wind than saleboats and kites? So, I asked them if they would like to go to a lake. They said, 'Sure."
 
Well, we headed East on Hwy 30, at Exit 64 we exited (If you are coming West on Hwy 30, it's still Exit 64). This exit is Dalrock Rd. At the exit we went on into Elgin B. Robertson's Park, the actual address is 500 S Cooke. It's South of the freeway.
 
When coming into the park there is a T in the road. The sign in front says, "Bayview Marina, Weekends Cafe, etc." We turned right there, it's a natural way to turn. We saw sailboats to the right of us. Up ahead on the left were some young kids and their parents (I'm assuming) flying kites. We drove around the big circle grass area with some trees. After leaving the T in road we noticed that we drove .8 miles around. On the right was a telephone pole with the number/letter combination: 7N17E71. I don't know why the passengers noticed it, but they did. Joan yelled, "STOP." And, if you know Joan, you better do what she says. So, we stopped right by the rusty trash can by the telephone pole. A little ways a way on the left was a brick structure, we noticed it was a bathroom. That's what Joan needed (if she'd lay off the Dr. Pepper, she wouldn't be in such a rush). So, while Joan went to the bathroom we looked to the right at the lake. Right in front of us was 2 trees. The second past the telephone pole, it's a big tree that droops to the ground. It was a neat tree, we don't know what kind though. We're hoping someone will know that species of tree. But, if you crawl under the branches you can stand up under there. If you were on the South side of the the trunk and you looked 5 feet up the trunk, you'd see the bus waiting. . .
 
Let me know how long the bathroom break was.
 
Thanks,
