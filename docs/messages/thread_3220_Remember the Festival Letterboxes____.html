From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-12-12 21:33:20 UTC-06:00
Actually, you should still be able to see the box, but you have to go to Basement on the front page of LBNA and then go to Texas.  You'll have to scroll through all the boxes listed (or do a search for a keyword) for the state of Texas.
 
There are two ways a box gets archived there: 1) the owner archives it because it is retired or missing, or 2) the owner is non-respondent and folks have tried to reach them and have eventually contacted the Webmasters and told them about their experience and the Webmasters archive the box.  It's easy enough for the owner to get the box back into the regular listing using the "unarchive" feature. 
 
And for reference, the Webmasters are very much open to anyone who wants to adopt a box that has been abandoned if you write and describe the efforts you have made to contact the placer and if you tell them you want the box.  I always thought it was a big hairy deal, but it really isn't - BUT - don't go nuts with that.  You need to have put in some real effort to reconnect the box to its owner.
 
The logic behind archiving is to keep the active listings clean of defunct abandoned boxes without deleting old boxes and affecting folks' counts.  As Sea Dove mentioned, an archived box does still show up in an owner's listing under Member Services and it does still remain in an owner's P count.
