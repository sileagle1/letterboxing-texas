From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2009-10-17 10:51:25 UTC-05:00

It IS finally here and I'm soooo glad!

Today is Jamboree on the Air - the worldwide initiative between ham radio operators and scouts to give scouts the opportunity to talk to one another all over the world via amateur radio. As y'all know, I got my ham license last November, but I've scarcely had time to work on my radio and get it operating. I finally got it last night (red goes to positive, right Mike?) and I'm taking it with me today to the Jambo site to get the ham operator to help me tune it up with a meter. I'm hoping that by the end of the day, it will be fully operational! This year, rather than having a group of hams come to a centralized location and bring their equipment (which, among other things, means mobile antennas which are a pain in the neck), one ham,K5RN, has invited us out to his house to operate from his shack - I can't help thinking this will be wayyyy better for the boys (and me). We've also arranged for other area hams to be on the air during this time so that we can talk more locally if we have difficulty getting distant signals. The skies are clear and beautiful so everyone is eager to get outdoors, and based on all reports, communications are already off to a great start - I'm itching to get on out there, but I'm trying to finish up a stamp for our scout letterboxers who will very likely be there.

Meanwhile, Jim of Palestine is heading up our next Cub Scout District event and I have volunteered to carve him some stamps so he can place some boxes at Pirtle for down time activities.

So - I'm not getting out boxing much at the moment, but I sure am thinking about it!
