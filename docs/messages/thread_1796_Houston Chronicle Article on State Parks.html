Houston Chronicle Article on State Parks

From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-04-06 15:40:42 UTC
There's an alarming article about the state of Texas parks in today's
Houston Chronicle

http://www.chron.com/disp/story.mpl/metropolitan/3774811.html


In no surprise to anyone who's recently been to our parks, TPWD is
reducing staffing and operating hours across the state at parks.
There is definitely a budget crisis and facilities are showing their
age--don't expect new facilities, just patch jobs on old ones. Our
per capita funding for parks is close to the bottom of the US, and we
have a lot of parks to maintain.

The Chronicle article says that Lake Houston SP is being transferred
out of TPWD control, but it doesn't say to whom or for what purpose.
I found this article that goes into greater depth about parts of the
crisis--it just says control of LHSP is being transferred to local
authorities.

http://www.centralvalleybusinesstimes.com/stories/001/?ID=1574

--DB
