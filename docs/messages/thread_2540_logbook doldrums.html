From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-01-26 07:22:37 UTC-06:00
This reminds me in a very indirect way of something I did when I was 15 - I was on an 8 week bus trip with 32 other people through the Great Northwest.  Every time I sent a letter home, I had everyone on the bus, most of whom were insanely creative or at least caught up in the spirit of such a trip, sign the envelope with short messages of their own or artwork.  The envelopes wound up awfully cluttered and looking back I'm surprised they ever got home - poor postman.
 
I ran across a few of those envelopes a few months ago and they were truly works of art!  They were visually appealing and spoke volumes about the mood of the moment both on our bus and in the world of that time.  I can imagine that having a page that opens out to allow folks to stamp anywhere they want and that forces people to stamp closely to conserve space would create the same sort of effect.  And I guess that is one of my frustrations with standard logbooks - by having page after page, we inadvertantly find ourselves conforming to the expected and "staying inside the lines".
