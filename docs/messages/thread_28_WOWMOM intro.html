From: carie78504 (carierraine@hotmail.com) | Date: 2004-02-02 21:35:38 UTC
Hi,
I just wanted to introduce myself, I am called WOWMOM because I like
to take my 5 year old son on adventures, and when went letterboxing,
he couldn't stop saying "WOW MOM!", the name stuck. I live on the
border of Mexico and we finally just got two boxes! (thanks to birds
of a feather). I am the official planter of doomed boxes, seeing any
attempt I make, the box dissappers or has to be rescued by someone :)
I think I'll just stick to finding for a while. I will be at the
gathering, just got my campsite reservation, and look forward to
meeting everyone. I would love to see what everyone carves with, how
to make nice log books, and some advice on how to plant boxes that
won't disappear. I will bring ... ummm..ohh! some newbie enthusiasm
and a kid who can sniff out clues from a mile away, but needs to
learn to be quiet about it. He's like a hound dog on the trail of a
rabbit!
Look for me at copperas creek, I am in the blue popup with the red
ram truck. If my brother comes we will also have radio,TV, VCR, DVD
player, XBOX, GameBoy, phones, ... (He had one bad experience
letterboxing in Aransas Wildlife Refuge being eaten by mosquitoes,
and now he refuses to come out of the trailer.)

We'll see you soon,
Carie

P.S. My friends don't get it, I even took time from my Disney
vacation to letterbox. I'm lovin' it.

From: BabyBear (babybearlb2002@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-02-02 14:59:42 UTC-08:00
I know what you mean about my friends not getting it.   I usually get strange looks from them, especially when they find out I take vacations just for searching out letterboxes.   I too found the Disney boxes.  The thing they don't understand is that I have found many new and wonderful places (the wilderness lodge at Disney is great!!!) due to letterboxing.
 
I think the only way to really get someone to understand is to take them out and find a box.   I recently went on a trip to Salt Lake City for business, and told my partner I was going to hike downtown and look for a box.   He wanted to go, but was laughing most of the way.   After trudging through the snow, finding the landmarks, and finally the box (with a Hitchhiker!), he could not wait to tell everyone at the meeting about what he had done.  
 
I honestly would be just as uninspired if it had not been for my brother bringing along some clues for a box in Spokane on a backpacking trip we went on a few years ago.   We had just enough time to give it a shot, and thank goodness we found the box.   That sealed the deal.  Soon we had come up with names (Silver Eagle for him, Baby Bear for me), and began trying to populate Texas, as the state did not offer much for finding.   Except for the petty fights on the LBNA talk list, this has been a great experience, and hopefully a life long hobby.
 
Hope you enjoy as much as we have, and look forward to seeing everyone at the 1st Event!!!
 
Baby Bear
