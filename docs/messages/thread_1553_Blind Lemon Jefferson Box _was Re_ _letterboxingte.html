I like cemetery boxes, too.  I like discovering old graves, reading names and dates and looking at the symbols and epitaphs, even the differences of stones and plantings in old cemeteries.  Also, when you have the time to really explore, you discover mysteries and questions - why are there so many graves with the nearly the same death dates?  Was there an epidemic or a fire or some other major catastrophe?  And I love to look for really old dates and think about what the area might have looked like then, and wonder if someone in some future time will also notice these same old graves and think about the lives of the people who were here then.  It's always sad to see infants' graves, with the little stone lambs.  Often, there are new graves in among old cemetery markers, and that's interesting, too, to realize that it's still an active cemetery, even though some of the earliest settlers in the area are buried here.  And often there are rabbits, mice, other small animals (including, sometimes, snakes) living there, also.  And always, birds.  Sometimes they are on tops of hills, and you have long vistas, and sometimes they appear abandoned, lost, and you wonder if anyone comes here anymore.  I think the inhabitants would welcome a quiet, respectful visitor, and if my letterbox brings another interested, curious, respectful person to discover the old cemetery, I think that's a good thing.
