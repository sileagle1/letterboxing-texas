Re: [letterboxingtexas] Re: New letterboxers

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2010-07-27 14:44:39 UTC-05:00

I was just curious because if they are local it might be worth a note to let them know there is a nearby active boxer with a very similar trail name. Not to ask them to change their trail name, but to let them know they may not be unique and might get confused with someone else. Some people care about that, some dont.

Wisconsin Hiker did that for me a long time ago my sister-in-law was trying her hand at letterboxing and she wanted to go by the trailname TX Flutterby. WH let me know there was a nationally known very active letterboxer named Flutterby (from CT I think), so there might have been some overlap. I didnt mention it to my sister-in-law because I didnt figure she would get too serious and I didnt figure their paths would cross, but it was nice to know just in case it was something she/we cared about. As it turned out, shes kind of dropped off, so it really didnt matter.

When Lucy Locket showed up on the scene, I felt a little weird having another Lucy around, but now I cant imagine her (or me) with any other trail name and its kind of fun having such a cool letterboxer as a name sister.

And finally, can you imagine poor Bear Food? Entering this harmless seeming game only to find him/herself as an unwitting pawn in the ongoing war between the Bear and the Ghost? Oh My

From: Boots Tex (redbarn@eastex.net) | Date: 2010-07-27 23:58:28 UTC
When I met the young lady who would become Lone Star Quilter, she had a father named Charles, a brother named Charles Jr. and an uncle named Charles. Her father was called Charles, her brother was (and still is) called Chuck and her uncle was Charlie, so they had it all worked out. Since my given name is Charles, it presented a little problem, but it's worked for 40 years. It seems that, whenever her father and I were mentioned in the same conversation, it was obvious, in context, which Charles was which. So, if Boots wants to be Boots, there's probably a reason for it and I'm sure it'll work out. Hey, Boots, if you're tuned in, let us know how you chose your trail name and where you live and anything else you'd like to share. As for Bear Food, you're on your own. We might end up calling you Honey; that's bear food, isn't it?

Boots Tex
