If you don't want people to know there is a bonus box, I wouldn't list it.  If you don't care if they know, you could just list the main box but put number of boxes as 2 (or more if there are more bonus boxes).


As far as temporary boxes, if you list them then archive them, they won't show up for others to log as finds, but will show up as places by you.  If you don't archive them, you could say they are retired in the clues or even as part of the name of the box.  I personally wouldn't even list them since I only log finds to help me know which boxes I still need to find, and if the boxes no longer exist it wouldn't matter.


As far as listing boxes on both sites, it would be hypocritical of me to expect everyone to list on both when I don't.  I have come to realize that most newer boxers (and many that have been around awhile) prefer AQ over LBNA and that will probably continue to the point that very few even use LBNA in the future.  The only problem this gives me is that boxes I have found on AQ that aren't listed on LBNA I never logged, so now I don't know which ones I still need to find on AQ.  However, I realize this is my problem and should not expect y'all to post on LBNA if you prefer AQ.  So don't post to LBNA if you are only doing it for the few of us that still use it exclusively.  I wouldn't blame you, but I do blame Ryan.  Way back when he created AQ I begged him to just help LBNA webmasters make changes that he wanted to put in AQ, or at least use the same database of clues for his site so we wouldn't have 2 separate repositories.  But he wanted his own site and the letterboxing world was split.

From: Debbie Kotlarek (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2013-10-24 15:48:18 UTC-05:00

I'm also one who prefers LBNA and only list my clues there, but I do look at both LBNA and AQ for clues for boxes to find. I'm surprised at the number of people that only look at AQ for clues.  I figure the more serious boxers will also look at LBNA.  I've seen some people claim that "everyone in their area only uses AQ, so they don't bother looking at LBNA".  However they may not realize that people vacationing in their area, or newer letterboxers in their area, may be listing clues on LBNA.  They miss out on those boxes.

 

I don't log anything on-line, since I prefer to keep my own record of my PFX where I can list and count things the way I like to.  I never list any of the bonus boxes I have planted on any web site.  They are intended to be a surprise for those that notice the clues in the logbook.  I haven't changed the way I list clues just because some people feel a need to log everything on-line.
