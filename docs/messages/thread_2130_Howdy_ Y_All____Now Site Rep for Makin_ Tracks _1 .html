From: Brandi J Rider (brandi@busybeesfamily.com) | Date: 2006-09-04 12:05:27 UTC-07:00
Hi Moore9KSUcats!  And welcome to Letterboxing! 
 
I have seen your name pretty regularly on the geocaching site.  I've seen several of your caches that I thought looked interesting...like the one having to do with "measurement".  Other than those type of "themed" caches, the whole Geocaching thing just didn't stick with me.  But...I'm really glad to see you begin letterboxing!  YIPPEE!  Now we can look for great letterboxes by someone who is so obviously creative and thoughtful.  :-)
 
My son and I went today and found both boxes.  We are so impressed with your first carvings!  And what a beautiful park...I live in Georgetown and never even knew that park was there!  We especially liked the trail signs...like the one that points to GT Country Club with the golf balls in the path!  LOL!  My son really wanted to see if one of those balls would come out, but I was able to convince him to leave them for the next person to find them.
 
Makin' Tracks #1 was very easy to find.  The only problem was that we had a hard time wedging the rocks back...they wanted to keep sliding down the hill.  I finally got them in place and wedged dirt up under them and then covered the dirt area with leaves so it looked more undisturbed.  Still, you may want to keep your eye on that one.  Hopefully the rains won't dislodge it.
 
Makin' Tracks #2 was a bit harder.  Still not too hard, but I kept thinking it had to be in that big animal burrow.  Walked right past the box several times before I thought...well, might as well check here.  Voila!  Nice hiding spot.  ;-)
 
I like what you did to the containers.  Makes them very difficult to spot.  I use camo tape, but it's probably much more labor intensive than your method and your way works, too.  As I said...I walked right past Makin' Tracks #2 SEVERAL times!  LOL!
 
Welcome again to letterboxing and glad to see you found our little group!
 
Thanks, Batty B of the BusyBeesFamily  :-)

Well, we have 3 more in the works... I don't know how long until we

constructive criticism... that is how we learn!

more boxes, though. That's ok... we can have fun planting more for
other folks to find.
