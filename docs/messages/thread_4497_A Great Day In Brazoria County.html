A Great Day In Brazoria County

From: sileagle1 (sileagle@windstream.net) | Date: 2010-04-10 00:23:34 UTC
I just got back from an interesting trip south to Brazoria County. I originally intended to only go to the San Bernard NWR to see the migrating birds, but while researching the area I found many historical sites to visit, too. I saw the original grave site of Stephen F. Austin before he was moved to Austin, a replica of the first Capitol of TX, and a large statue of Stephen F. Austin. I would have also seen the largest live oak tree in TX, but the rangers were not there to let me in.

As extra incentive for you to visit I placed 3 boxes while down there. Try to go soon before it gets too hot and the bugs come out.

Silver Eagle
