I really have to agree with this - finding a good place for that Tour Bus is essential!  The advantage is, you can expect it won't remain in its hiding place long, but you do want to be sure it's really concealed and away from any possible accidental finders!  The main thing is to keep it travelling, on the road, where the next eager finder can rush in and swoop it up swiftly and keep it travellin' Texas!  And finding it is a big responsibility; it's so full of exciting passengers that it takes half a day to read the logbook and stamp them all, plus creating your own new stamp-passenger to add, so it's a big deal!  I know I've been lucky enough to have it twice, and hope sometime in the next year to be able to encounter it again and discover more new passengers, but the main thing with a big bus is - Safety for the bus and its important passengers!  Wherever it rejoins the road to continue its tour, I'm sure there will be eager finders with their gas tanks full ready to go after it!
