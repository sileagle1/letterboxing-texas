You participate in a world of dog shows where dogs are constantly
being judged for every little detail, measured against an
"ideal"--size, coat, teeth, manner, walk, etc. Why judge one dog
"better" than another? To a lot of us, that seems very
arbitrary--different judges have different "ideals" after all.

Similarly, there is an "ideal" letterbox FOR MY FAMILY. I am fine if
people want to put their best efforts out there, and I'd probably,
given enough time (a biggie), money (another biggie) & energy (another
biggie), go find them, even if they were far from MY ideal.

But, I don't have unlimited time, money & energy. So, I try to, the
best I can, seek out those boxes that call to me. . .and because of
that, I make different choices when it is just me and when it is the
whole family, when I'm traveling or when I'm here, and so on, down a
long list of admittedly subjective criteria. I throw in some wild
cards, and I try to find boxes from as many placers as I can, and I
try to seek out new placers when I can, because I want to be
encouraging of people who are just getting their toes into this hobby.

And I find that I like thoughtful letterboxers--people who have given

some thought to finding a special location, even if it just has
meaning to them
some thought to the stamp (custom, store bought or hand carved) and
why it should go in that location,
some thought into improving their carving skills
some thought to the clues and how to make them fun for the finder,
some thought to how to make the logbook fit with the box theme, and
some thought to whether it is a pleasant walk and
some thought to a good hiding place.

And I don't get why that's such a negative.

Dewberry

Exactly WRONG.  I compete in an area where each handler and each dog is expected to perform to a standard.  Every dog that gets a qualifying score is awarded a ribbon and eventually a title.  There is nothing subjective about it at all - purely performance driven.  It the dog doesn't heel, we lose.  If the dog doesn't clear the jump, we lose.  If the dog and handler perform each and every step 100% correctly we get the came title that every dog gets for the same performance.
 
That is why it is called an OBEDIENCE TRIAL.
 
Color-size-shape-coat-teeth, it matters not.  PERFORMANCE.  You can do it, or you cannot do it.  You keep enough points to qualify, or you lose too many points due to poor performance and you do not qualify.  Clear standards written in a rule book available for all to see.  Nothing arbitrary about it.
