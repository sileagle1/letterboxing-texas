From: tigger32167 (tigger32167@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-08-07 01:02:27 UTC
Wednesday - Aug 2
Started out the day in The Woodlands, north of Houston, and drove to
Ft Bend county on the south side of Houston. Went to Brazos Bend
State Park and on the way down the trail to find the Bonnie Parker
box a raccoon was coming towards me on the trail. As he crossed the
bridge, he saw me and decided to show me the direction that I needed
to go. I ended up losing him, but did see a lot of spider webs on
the side of the trail with tons of large spiders just hanging
around. Found the Bonnie Parker letterbox with no problem and in
good shape. The next trail that I went on had a mommy and baby deer
grazing on the side of it and they wished me good luck on finding
the box. I then noticed that a pair of eyes were watching me from
the water..it was Allie the alligator...she also wished me good
luck. A little further down, Ricky the raccoon wished me luck.
Thanks to all the friends that I was making, my luck was good and I
found the Good Luck letterbox in good shape. After returning to the
car to drive to the next trail, I spotted another mommy deer but she
had 2 babies with her. A little further down were about 8-9 mommy
and baby deer grazing in a group on the side of the road and then
continued a little way further to see another group of 9-10 deer
having their morning breakfast. The next trail that I attempted was
the Red Buckeye trail to find the LA and TLRE box and the Taz box
but the trail was closed due to floods. My next stop was a trail
that had several boxes on it, however it also had the observation
tower on it, and on this day a group of about 30 summer camping kids
decided to visit it also. So there were too many kids to feel
comfortable to make this attempt. So I decided to leave Brazos Bend
and come back another time to pick up the rest. My next stop
was Seabourne Park in Rosenberg. This park has a beautiful wetland
area with a pond and a board walk that goes over one end of it. As
I was crossing it, I saw another set of eyes near a little hut.
Ollie the otter was busy repairing his house. He said hi and told
me where to look for the box at. I managed to find the
Rosenberg box in great shape. My next stop was Brazos Park in
Rosenberg where I managed to find the Bully box. The log book was
very damp, and I did not have a log book small enough to replace
it. My next stop was Morton Cemetery in Richmond. Saw the grave of
the founder of the Confederate Museum of the Old South Plantation
(this was written on the headstone with a confederate flag on front
and back) on my way to the letterbox. Also this is the resting
place of President of the Republic of Texas Lamar and the
mother of Texas, Jane Long. The box that is here is in tribute to
her and is called the Mother of Texas. The box was in good shape.
Went to Cullinan Park in SugarLand to retrieve the Widow Skimmer
box. I found the correct location but there was a geocache there
instead of a letterbox. Found the SugarLand box at Oyster Creek
Park in SugarLand with no problem. If you go to this park, be sure
and look over the bridge at the river. I saw at least a hundred
turtles swimming along in this river. Went to Lost Creek Park
and found the Lamp Post box, Triforce box and the Fence Me In box.
I attempted the Texas Sprint Champion box, but unsure if I was at
the right place or not, so can't say if the box is there or not. My
next stop was Camp Sienna to find the Sienna Sunset Microbox. It
was in good shape. I then went to Kitty Hollow Park and found the
Tweety box. I also attempted the Snappy Snapper box in this same
park. The box had previously been reported as missing, and I also
could not find it. There is a neat old house that they are
currently restoring in this park that is very interesting, but I
don't know what the history of it is. I then decided that it was
time to call it a day and decided to head to my parents. Once I got
there, my mom and I decided to look for the Bird in the Woods box on
a trail around the golf course that my parents house backs up to.
We decided that we could handle this without a map and just pick up
the trail from their house....BAD DECISION...we got lost and
realized that neither one of us had a cell phone with us...and
didnt know where we were or how to get home. About 2 hours of
walking and finally decided to ask a mounted security officer. She
told us which way to go, and we realized that we were still about 30
minutes from the house. We finally managed to find the house, but
needless to say the letterbox will have to wait for another day.
JB Kokopelli

From: Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter (janet@redbarnretreat.com) | Date: 2007-08-07 01:57:50 UTC
When we first moved to southeast Texas (Sugar Land) from Midland our
son was about 14. His cousin of the same age came to visit and we
went to Brazos Bend State Park that weekend. The two boys decided
to take fishing poles and catch a few, only they didn't have any
bait. They found a piece of half-raw chicken that someone had left
on a barbecue pit and thought that would make fine bait, so off they
went. It wasn't long before we heard them hollering and then we saw
them running toward us as fast as they could run. It seems that
hunk of chicken on the end of the fishing line had attracted the
attention of a large alligator. Our son started reeling in his line
and the faster he got, the faster the alligator got. When he pulled
the bait up on shore, they thought they had it made, until the
alligator decided he would just join them. That's when they came
running and hollering. Luckily the gator didn't take the hook and
their fishing poles were still lying there on the bank. I still kid
him about that, but that was a scary thing that I wouldn't advise
trying.

Boots Tex

hunk of chicken on the end of the fishing line had attracted the

the bait up on shore, they thought they had it made, until the
alligator decided he would just join them. That's when they came
running and hollering. Luckily the gator didn't take the hook and
their fishing poles were still lying there on the bank. I still kid

Wednesday - Aug 2
Started out the day in The Woodlands, north of Houston, and drove to
Ft Bend county on the south side of Houston. Went to Brazos Bend
State Park and on the way down the trail to find the Bonnie Parker
box a raccoon was coming towards me on the trail. As he crossed the
bridge, he saw me and decided to show me the direction that I needed
to go. I ended up losing him, but did see a lot of spider webs on
the side of the trail with tons of large spiders just hanging
around. Found the Bonnie Parker letterbox with no problem and in
good shape. The next trail that I went on had a mommy and baby deer
grazing on the side of it and they wished me good luck on finding
the box. I then noticed that a pair of eyes were watching me from
the water..it was Allie the alligator...she also wished me good
luck. A little further down, Ricky the raccoon wished me luck.
Thanks to all the friends that I was making, my luck was good and I
found the Good Luck letterbox in good shape. After returning to the
car to drive to the next trail, I spotted another mommy deer but she
had 2 babies with her. A little further down were about 8-9 mommy
and baby deer grazing in a group on the side of the road and then
continued a little way further to see another group of 9-10 deer
having their morning breakfast. The next trail that I attempted was
the Red Buckeye trail to find the LA and TLRE box and the Taz box
but the trail was closed due to floods. My next stop was a trail
that had several boxes on it, however it also had the observation
tower on it, and on this day a group of about 30 summer camping kids
decided to visit it also. So there were too many kids to feel
comfortable to make this attempt. So I decided to leave Brazos Bend
and come back another time to pick up the rest. My next stop
was Seabourne Park in Rosenberg. This park has a beautiful wetland
area with a pond and a board walk that goes over one end of it. As
I was crossing it, I saw another set of eyes near a little hut.
Ollie the otter was busy repairing his house. He said hi and told
me where to look for the box at. I managed to find the
Rosenberg box in great shape. My next stop was Brazos Park in
Rosenberg where I managed to find the Bully box. The log book was
very damp, and I did not have a log book small enough to replace
it. My next stop was Morton Cemetery in Richmond. Saw the grave of
the founder of the Confederate Museum of the Old South Plantation
(this was written on the headstone with a confederate flag on front
and back) on my way to the letterbox. Also this is the resting
place of President of the Republic of Texas Lamar and the
mother of Texas, Jane Long. The box that is here is in tribute to
her and is called the Mother of Texas. The box was in good shape.
Went to Cullinan Park in SugarLand to retrieve the Widow Skimmer
box. I found the correct location but there was a geocache there
instead of a letterbox. Found the SugarLand box at Oyster Creek
Park in SugarLand with no problem. If you go to this park, be sure
and look over the bridge at the river. I saw at least a hundred
turtles swimming along in this river. Went to Lost Creek Park
and found the Lamp Post box, Triforce box and the Fence Me In box.
I attempted the Texas Sprint Champion box, but unsure if I was at
the right place or not, so can't say if the box is there or not. My
next stop was Camp Sienna to find the Sienna Sunset Microbox. It
was in good shape. I then went to Kitty Hollow Park and found the
Tweety box. I also attempted the Snappy Snapper box in this same
park. The box had previously been reported as missing, and I also
could not find it. There is a neat old house that they are
currently restoring in this park that is very interesting, but I
don't know what the history of it is. I then decided that it was
time to call it a day and decided to head to my parents. Once I got
there, my mom and I decided to look for the Bird in the Woods box on
a trail around the golf course that my parents house backs up to.
We decided that we could handle this without a map and just pick up
the trail from their house....BAD DECISION...we got lost and
realized that neither one of us had a cell phone with us...and
didnt know where we were or how to get home. About 2 hours of
walking and finally decided to ask a mounted security officer. She
told us which way to go, and we realized that we were still about 30
minutes from the house. We finally managed to find the house, but
needless to say the letterbox will have to wait for another day.

LOL...my whole family thinks we're nerds too.  We think they are uninformed.  We feel sort of like the Men in Black - you know, WE know what's out there, but everyone else is oblivious to it.
 
The first Christmas that we letterboxed, we mentioned it at the family gathering and many of them began eying us warily and moving away. 
 
The second Christmas, we mentioned it again and they said "You still do that?" (much as they would have said "You still belong to that cult?") BUT my sister-in-law sat me down and asked me about it and went boxing herself later in the year.  We slipped out early and planted a box - on Christmas Day.
 
The third Christmas, my sister-in-law and I were discussing it and a cousin got in on the conversation and the rest of them rolled their eyes.  Just a few short days later my husband, Fearless Fox, and son, Lightning, talked
his sister's husband into going with them on a grand adventure to retrieve the Texas Tour Bus - only to be foiled by the dastardly PuddleSplasher.  That Christmas I made my mother-in-law an exquisite personal stamp.  It was beautiful but she didn't appreciate what it was.  She looked at it puzzled, sniffed it, and put it back in the box.  Oh well (she DOES still have it though).
 
The fourth Christmas, some of them even asked if we had found any good boxes lately but regretted it later as they had to extricate themselves as we continued discussing the boxes we have found, and my sister-in-law and I dared to ask for boxing supplies for Christmas.  We didn't get them, but the seed was planted (HA - no pun intended).  And on the other side of the family, I introduced my mother and step-dad to letterboxing and gave my mom a very nice letterboxing kit for Christmas.
 
In anticipation of this next Christmas, my mom (Nac Trail Cat) and her husband have discovered this year that they are completely hooked on letterboxing.  My 9-year-old nephew is a frustrated letterboxer because his parents won't take him boxing, although he learned about it and loved it at Cub Scout camp in April.  I'll have to kidnap him and take him with me.  My sister-in-law still likes to box, but she doesn't get out much.  I think we will plant several boxes on the family land and hand out clues and stamps for entertainment. 
 
They do come around - eventually.

tower on it, and on this day a group of about 30 summer camping kids
decided to visit it also. So there were too many kids to feel
comfortable to make this attempt. So I decided to leave Brazos Bend
and come back another time to pick up the rest. My next stop
was Seabourne Park in Rosenberg. This park has a beautiful wetland
area with a pond and a board walk that goes over one end of it. As
I was crossing it, I saw another set of eyes near a little hut.
Ollie the otter was busy repairing his house. He said hi and told
me where to look for the box at. I managed to find the
Rosenberg box in great shape. My next stop was Brazos Park in
Rosenberg where I managed to find the Bully box. The log book was
very damp, and I did not have a log book small enough to replace
it. My next stop was Morton Cemetery in Richmond. Saw the grave of
the founder of the Confederate Museum of the Old South Plantation
(this was written on the headstone with a confederate flag on front
and back) on my way to the letterbox. Also this is the resting
place of President of the Republic of Texas Lamar and the
mother of Texas, Jane Long. The box that is here is in tribute to
her and is called the Mother of Texas. The box was in good shape.
Went to Cullinan Park in SugarLand to retrieve the Widow Skimmer
box. I found the correct location but there was a geocache there
instead of a letterbox. Found the SugarLand box at Oyster Creek
Park in SugarLand with no problem. If you go to this park, be sure
and look over the bridge at the river. I saw at least a hundred
turtles swimming along in this river. Went to Lost Creek Park
and found the Lamp Post box, Triforce box and the Fence Me In box.
I attempted the Texas Sprint Champion box, but unsure if I was at
the right place or not, so can't say if the box is there or not. My
next stop was Camp Sienna to find the Sienna Sunset Microbox. It
was in good shape. I then went to Kitty Hollow Park and found the
Tweety box. I also attempted the Snappy Snapper box in this same
park. The box had previously been reported as missing, and I also
could not find it. There is a neat old house that they are
currently restoring in this park that is very interesting, but I
don't know what the history of it is. I then decided that it was
time to call it a day and decided to head to my parents. Once I got
there, my mom and I decided to look for the Bird in the Woods box on
a trail around the golf course that my parents house backs up to.
We decided that we could handle this without a map and just pick up
the trail from their house....BAD DECISION...we got lost and
realized that neither one of us had a cell phone with us...and
didnt know where we were or how to get home. About 2 hours of
walking and finally decided to ask a mounted security officer. She
told us which way to go, and we realized that we were still about 30
minutes from the house. We finally managed to find the house, but
needless to say the letterbox will have to wait for another day.
