Upcoming trip, trip last weekend, and lesson learned

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-11-23 18:12:50 UTC-08:00
My daughter, Elmo, and I are advancing forward with
what is turning into a military expedition as we
prepare for our Dallas letterboxing trip this weekend!
Our fellers left this morning for their 26 mile hike
in Arkansas, so we are on our own! We are leaving
tomorrow evening and will be gone until Saturday.
I know that we have more boxes picked than we can
find, but hopefully we'll be able to make it all over
the metroplex - look for our stamps soon in a box near
you!

We had a wonderful weekend last weekend - took
Lightning to his first karate tournament in Tulsa and
turned the weekend into a letterboxing weekend as a
bonus. It WAS my birthday weekend after all, and
that's what I wanted to do!

We left home a little late Friday evening, passing
through Paris around 9:00 p.m. Scoped out the
Passport Series box there and decided we were hitting
it at a good time, so we went ahead and snagged it -
imagine our surprise to find we were the FF's! Even
GG hadn't ventured up that way to catch it. It was a
nicely done box - way to go Catpawz!

We made our way on up to Tulsa and finally rolled into
a hotel room about 1:00 a.m. After what seemed like
ever so slight of a nap, we headed to the tournament,
only to find out it started an hour later than our
dojo had announced, so we were bummed to hear we
could've had another hour's sleep. We had a great
time at the tournament in spite of being tired, then
found a little hole in the wall restaurant, Ed's All
American Coneys, and grabbed a bite to eat. We had
seen Ed's advertised on the bulletin board at the
church where the tournament was held and decided a
good ol hot dog was just right for a mid-afternoon
lunch. Now we like to pride ourselves on being
friendly here in Texas - but I can't recall the last
time I had the red carpet rolled out for me the way
Ed's Wife rolled it out for my family. She was
Texas-friendly without being intrusive and we wound up
telling her our story and heard hers, and she even
walked us to the door as we left. I wished I had had
a box looking for a home with me, because I would've
left it in Ed's and Ed's Wife care. I vote here and
now to make Ed's Wife an honorary Texan!

I had done careful calculations and knew that Oxley
Nature Center and Mohawk Park were going to give us
the greatest payoff box-wise, so we headed off in that
direction. We had a couple hours of daylight, but
even less time before we would get locked into the
parking lot, so we decided to make good on one trail
only that day and come back the next day.
Unfortunately, Baby Bear's box had been reported
missing, but Silver Eagle's box was there on the trail
with another box by a local. We found the local box
first and I was shocked to see how rough it looked
after a couple years in the wild. I also expected to
find that Oklahoma boxes were pretty lonely, but I was
greatly surprised to find that wasn't the case at all.
There were lots of stamps from all over the place
there, but not very many familiar stamps at all! We
went on to find Silver Eagle's box, and it was in
pretty rough shape too! It too was 2 years old, so
I'm wondering if the rugged condition of both boxes
was due to the harsher climate in Oklahoma or the fact
that they were both relatively old boxes. SE's box
was also much visited, and I found it comforting to
see his familiar style as we ventured off into what
was for us uncharted territory.

We got up the next morning and didn't get out as
quickly as I would've liked (our daughter, Elmo, was
with us and while she is doing much better, she is
still really slow getting started in the morning), but
we managed to hit the trail at Mohawk Park with
several hours to box before we had to go. We took a
wrong turn getting back to Oxley, but we found
ourselves in the area where Safari Man's series was
placed. We went ahead and got most of that series
before heading back to Oxley. Safari Man's boxes were
a lot of fun and I find his carving style to be
intriguing. We were becoming aware of our time
constraints, but we were getting painfully near a
landmark, so we didn't want to quit.

After such a good run at Safari Man's series, we were
hyped up and ready to get Donna's Oxley series - and
that's when we hit a dead-end. It was as though we
were reading the clues and not understanding them. It
has to be one of the strangest things I've encountered
while letterboxing. I may not always jive with
someone's clues, but usually someone else in my family
does, so that one of us can take the lead and find the
box no matter what. It just wasn't the case with
these clues - we didn't get a single box after we
arrived at the Nature Center. We searched and didn't
find over and over again, becoming more frustrated
with every box we attempted. Now let me say - I KNOW
these boxes are doable because Silver Eagle told me
what a great series it was. And since it may be a bit
of a spoiler to tell you why I know they weren't
missing, I'll just say I'm fairly certain they were in
place. It was US - maybe because we were really
fatigued, maybe because we were feeling the time
pressure, maybe the boxes just weren't our style.
Despite the fact we didn't hit our mark and we missed
several boxes we tried for, the location is absolutely
phenomenal and we had a great four mile hike (left
Elmo in the car for that - she was up to a half mile
or a little better, but we had done her in with
that!). We found the Nature Center to be very
informative and the staff every bit as friendly as
Ed's Wife. Also, while hiking we got to see a
red-tailed hawk catch and eat it's lunch. Very cool -
he flew not even six feet off our right sides as he
whisked his meal back to the treetops. Don't be
discouraged by our failure to find these boxes - if
you are ever in the Tulsa area, go for them!

SO - lessons learned from our excursion last weekend.
Read the clues well, even the parts that seem
irrelevant. I learned I really like having a
confirming clue to support the main clue (this must be
the accountant in me - you know, double-entry
bookkeeping - something to balance against). And
finally, read your clues in advance so you can do any
research you need, especially if you are on unfamiliar
turf - one clue told us the box was behind a bur oak
tree, which was well and good until we realized none
of us knew what a bur oak was!

Lucy

We had a wonderful weekend last weekend

Sounds like a fun trip. Let me tell you about our return from the Tucson
Dog Show last weekend. It too was a fun trip. This is the "REPORT" I sent
to our friends upon our return.

Most of you know we were traveling to Tucson for the dog shows at the
fairgrounds over the weekend. Moni does the dog shows for real. I just play
at it. As long as Parker is having fun we play . But since I am getting more
active in radio again, I do enjoy the ride.

In our free time, Moni lets me do other stuff. Like Geocaching and
Letterboxing. If you don't know what they are by now, go here:

http://www.geocaching.com

http://www.letterboxing.org

On the road - she drives (her van anyway). And I play navigator - ham radio
operator - GPS operator..... I also serve drinks and food etc. On the way
TO the shows she discovered she left her Letterboxing kit at home.
So she missed out on the "Lordsburg" and "The Thing" Letterboxes on the trip
TO Tucson.

The dogs did well. Sammy won her class all three days. Parker got a
qualifying score every day, and got THIRD on Friday. For a Great Dane to
place that high in an all-breed shoe against the snappy little dogs is
fantastic! We had a great time Geocaching inside the city late Saturday.

We like to sight-see on the return trip. So I planned the return trip to hit
some letterboxes. The Javelina Letterbox and Bisbee Box to be exact. You can
read the clues here:


box>
http://www.letterboxing.org/BoxView.php?boxnum=7108&boxname=Javelina_Letterb
ox


http://www.letterboxing.org/BoxView.php?boxnum=18228&boxname=Bisbee_Box

Since we needed a break (for the dogs) I planned the breaks to snag
the letterboxes at the breaks. After these, I planned for us to shoot for
home.

As I read the clues for the Javelina Letterbox, I thought it was somewhere
before Tombstone. Surely before Bisbee. Well, there was LOTS of construction
on 80, and I figured we missed the first clue or it was removed or
something. By the time we got to Bisbee, I had wadded up the clues for
Javelina Letterbox and tossed them on the floor. Maybe next time....

Without giving up too much - we found the Bisbee Box just fine. The "PARK"
clue wasn't very clear. There is more than one park in Bisbee, and there is
one that better meets the clue than the one intended. But the "BREWERY
GULCH" clue was spot-on. Moni parked the van to hide where the letterbox was
hidden. She took Parker and Sammy out of the van. Pretty soon everyone was
around the dogs, and nobody saw me when I pulled out the letterbox. I took
it to the van, stamped up, and re-hid it again before joining Moni and the
dogs. We had a great time walking the Great Danes around Bisbee. They always
make people run. Some run away from us - others run toward us! Had we known
then that Javelina Letterbox was still ahead, we would not have wasted so
much time in Bisbee. So when we had enough, we broke off contact with the
crowd around the dogs, and headed to Douglas and back to I-10 and home.

I made several contacts on the Bisbee repeaters on the way to Douglas. In
Douglas I switched over to the EARS system and chatted a bit more. The radio
got a REAL WORKOUT. More on that later. Out Douglas - still on 80 now going
north. Somewhere before Rodeo I picked up the Mega-Link on 145.170.

If you look at a map - 80 starts at Benson AZ, swings southeast to Douglas
on the border, then back northeast to Road Forks NM. So we were still on 80.


Now picture this. We are cruising up 80 at 65 mph headed for Road Forks. I
am talking to someone on the ham radio. I shout to Moni "What did that sign
say? Quick - go back!"

THE FIRST CLUE !!!!!

Ah-ha!!! Where did I toss the clues????? Got them! A village named "DOOR".
Was this it? It looked promising.

We talked it over. We had spent so much time in Bisbee because we gave up on
this one and now it was near sunset. I punched up the GPS for sunset time.
1.5 hours at the most and it would be dark. Should we? Moni says "Lets see
how far we get, we can always come back later". God love her!
