From: Boots Tex (redbarn@eastex.net) | Date: 2010-02-16 17:44:58 UTC
Barefoot Lucy mentioned buying some Walmart brand plastic food containers a while back and testing them in the microwave or dishwasher? I've had trouble finding Lock & Lock boxes lately, at least in the size that I like, so I've been experimenting with other "L&L knockoffs", in particular Sterlite Ultra Latch that I can get at the Walmart in Conroe for $2.00 (2.5 cup size) and Snap 'n Lock by Snapware (2 cup size) that I got at Bed, Bath and Beyond for $2.99. I've put out a few of each and they seem to be working okay, but I haven't given them the microwave and dishwasher tests. I think I'll do that today. I have use some small 3" x 4" boxes that I got from DixieGraphix, but I don't really like the fact that the logbook has to be so small, 3" x 2". She also sells a 5" x 7", 2.3 cup size that would probably be good, but they are over $4.00 each plus postage. I'll bring a couple to the TALE7 event so everyone see them. All said, I still prefer the Lock & Lock if I could get it in the right size. They're made in Australia, so maybe that's the problem, or the knockoffs are putting them out of business. I'll also bring a variety of carving material to show. We can have a show and tell for new boxers.

Boots Tex


Re: Boxes

From: Boots Tex (redbarn@eastex.net) | Date: 2010-02-16 19:40:26 UTC
The microwave test went well. I filled each box about halfway with water and set the microwave on high for 5 minutes, which boiled the water. When I took the boxes out, they were "vacuum sealed". I pried the lids off both and they returned to normal shape and fit perfectly and tight. The other two boxes are in the diswasher. I put paper towels inside and sealed the lids so I could tell easier if they leak at all. It's a sanitizing diswasher, so it will be hot and humid in there, like East Texas in the summertime. I'll report later.

Boots Tex

Re: Boxes

From: Boots Tex (redbarn@eastex.net) | Date: 2010-02-16 22:24:20 UTC
The dishwasher test is over. The Sterlite box, $2.00 at Walmart, was in perfect condition and the paper towel inside perfectly dry. The Snap 'n Lock box, $2.99 at Bed Bath & Beyond, was also in perfect condition, but the paper towel was slightly damp, not wet by any means, but a little damp. Based on my tests, I'd say the Sterlite box will make a fine letterbox, and at $2.00 is very cost effective. The other one would probably be fine, too, since the dishwasher was likely a harsher environment than nature, even in Texas, but it costs more. I have some of them, and I'll use them up, but I'll go with Sterlite. Lock & Lock boxes are proven by several years experience, so you can't go wrong with them. Now if I could figure out how to keep critters from chewing on them.

Boots Tex
