From: the carrascos (a.carrasco@earthlink.net) | Date: 2008-01-03 22:31:57 UTC-06:00
I've been thinking a lot about dewberry's question on goals for 2008 letterboxing.  My goal is to put a sense of adventure back into my letterboxing outings.  In Houston, there are so many boxes to find, that I could never leave the city, and still never have enough time to find all the boxes out there. (And, believe me, I am not complaining about this!)  But when we first started letterboxing, boxes were few and far between, and every find involved something unknown, and many involved a spectacular road trip with the kids eating junk food (bribes work wonders!) all the way to a new adventure.  We took many short weekend trips to places I never would have had the inclination to visit otherwise, and the memories of these trips are treasures to me.  So my goal for 2008 is to pack the car, lots of juice boxes and moonpies, and get out of town for a few weekends to meander around discovering surprises along the way.  Who knows when the nest Stonehenge or suspension bridge or civil war cemetery or colony of bats or beautiful backroad or.....is at the end of the day's drive?  MO5

From: (blhjrh@aol.com) | Date: 2008-01-04 11:11:35 UTC-05:00
I like your goal and agree completely!  The most fun is to find a place that's completely new to you, get to explore it looking for a letterbox and have all these serendipitous discoveries along the way, whether in your own area or way off in a totally unfamiliar place.  The nice stamp & logbook are just frosting on the cake, but even if you don't find anything, it's still a good time.  
I think my goal parallels yours:  it's not about the count and certainly there are enough letterboxes around now to be a little selective rather than compulsive.  It should be an adventure!

Re: goals for 2008

From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-01-04 17:39:14 UTC
For those looking for the antecedent, my question was posed on the
Gulf Coast boxers list, rather than here, but I'm happy to have talk
about it here too.

I'm not a goal-maker when it comes to letterboxing, more a
see-what-come type of person. But, I have some series and boxes that
have been percolating in the back of my head that I'd liked to see
actualized.

We'll be going on a trip to Seattle in March to see my sister, with a
side trip to the Oregon Coast. And this summer, we'll be taking a
Colorado/Wyoming roadtrip. Both of those are exciting and fun.

But, I love little sleepy Texas towns, quirky roadside attractions,
diners with ancient waitresses & delicious pie, and just driving
around and seeing something that makes you go...STOP this car this
instant! And that's why I like tooling around on Texas backroads and
what keeps me going for mini-adventures and letterboxes in the middle
of no-where.

I really appreciate people who plant boxes at the spots that may not
be the biggest tourist attraction in their area, but that are
interesting, odd & fun. I know that these boxes do not get nearly the
amount of traffic that boxes right on the Interstate do, but I really
love them when I find them, and enjoy looking back through my logbook
remembering the adventure. That's why I'm hooked.

dewberry

One of the nicest, most relaxing vacations my husband and I had was a leisurely, no-destination trip exploring the OK and TX panhandles, years and years ago.  It was prompted by a trip the previous summer to the USSR, and after that, we thought west TX and OK would be a definite step up in comfort and luxury!  Our goal each day was have lunch at a cafe on a town square and have BIG iced tea and pie with BIG meringue - and we were never disappointed!  Along the way we discovered pretty city and state parks, explored the Palo Duro canyon, saw lots and lots of sky and grain elevators, found some really pretty scenery unexpectedly in OK, and it was great!  Of course this was long before we knew anything about letterboxing (about in 1988), but that would sure be fun to do again and plant a few!   vf

Re: [letterboxingtexas] goals for 2008

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-01-04 12:18:02 UTC-06:00
Yep - quality.  That's going to be my letterboxing mantra this year too.
 
I want to improve the quality of my carving and make better logbooks, among other things.  I also would like to strive to make my boxes more challenging to finders, either by putting them out on longer walks or making them puzzle boxes. 

Also, since my letterboxing time seems to be at a premium for the time being, I want to use the time I have focusing on finding boxes in those cool out of the way locations several of y'all have mentioned. 
 
I'm hoping to spend this next year paring down in many ways since life seems to be just a little too full at the moment.  I like to think of it as pruning - you prune a tree so that it can become stronger and more fruitful!
