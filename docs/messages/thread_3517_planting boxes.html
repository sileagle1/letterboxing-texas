From: Mike Olbrisch (mike-2007@elp.rr.com) | Date: 2008-04-26 08:14:49 UTC-06:00
Well - about 80-% of my boxes are in fee areas.  There are a few advantages. 
 
People who pay to use an area are more likely to be respectful of that area.  And more respectful of your box.  That's not an absolute, but a good rule of thumb.
 
Another good point is that parks are kept pretty clean, which makes for a more enjoyable outing.  No one likes wading through trash to find a letterbox.  Ever hike southern AZ?
 
Folks may not want to pay the fee for just one box.  So put several in the area, and they will have more incentive to stop.
 
Most of my letterboxes (and Geocaches) are set in parks.  This helps gain income for the local parks, AND introduces the seekers to the parks in the area.
 
The disadvantages is that folks of limited income might pass by the box because of the fee.  Also, many parks have hours of operation, so access to your box might be limited to those hours.
 
All things to consider.  Personally, a fee for park entrance wouldn't be a show-stopper for me.

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-04-27 14:08:19 UTC-05:00
Add on to what Mike said with the fact that many letterboxers have state park passes which exempt them from park fees for the period of the pass - one year.  The pass covers however many people are in the car, so that if you take one vehicle in with four folks and they would normally have to pay $3 each (sometimes more), it only takes about six park visits a year to pay for the pass. 
Aside from that, I don't sweat paying a small park fee myself.  We'll usually find something of interest in a park besides just the letterbox.
