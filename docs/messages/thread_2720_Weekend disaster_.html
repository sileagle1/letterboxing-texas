Weekend disaster.

From: Mike - KD9KC (mike-2007@elp.rr.com) | Date: 2007-04-16 04:23:54 UTC

Howdy.

Went to a dog show in Casa Grande AZ this weekend.

Trip started out at 0730 Friday morning. Horrible headwind most of
the way. That big Ford E-350 doesn't get good fuel mileage to begin with.
Played ham radio on the road while the wife drove. Set the GPS to get us to
Tucson, then split off the road at Oracle AZ to go after the first
letterbox, the Tom Mix microbox near Florence AZ. Nice stop, nice box, nice
history. We knew who Tom Mix was - in a general sort of way. Now we will
be looking for some of his movies. But.... When we got to the box my wife
noticed she didn't have a memory stick in her camera. Fortunately - I had
my hiking camera along. It is small, not as good as hers.... But it works!
Stamped up, read the history, walked the dogs, then on the road again.

Got to the motel in Casa Grande at 2pm. Too early to call it a day.
I was supposed to meet a ham in Scottsdale to buy a special used radio from
him, we were to meet Saturday night. This was Friday night. Called him on
the phone. Not home. But we thought we would search for a letterbox out
that way, then call again. We picked Tortilla Flat. TOO COOL! About a 60
mile drive one way. And what a wonderful drive, really beautiful. And the
prickly pear ice cream was great. Even the dogs liked it. But - problem.
The wife was on the last page of her logbook. We needed a new one.

Near sundown we started back. Called that ham a few times - not
home. So we drove back towards the motel in Casa Grande - letting the GPS
find are stores and Michael's - hobby lobbys along the route. NO LUCK!
Bummer. Arrived back at the motel well after 9pm, fed dogs, then ourselves
- and bed.

Saturday morning. Up early- check E-mail - and on to the
showgrounds. Dogs ready. Our turn comes - its going good. But - - -OH-NO!
The judge only likes brown dogs.... We get 3rd place - not even a real look!
Oh-well - always tomorrow. Back to the motel - feed the dogs, then out the
door. Looking for an art store in Casa Grande for a sketch book/log book.
Moni grabs the log books from the pocket in the door where we keep the
letterboxing stuff and we carry them inside the store. But..... NO LUCK!
We settle for something that will do - but not what we wanted.

Now we decided to go the other way. On to Picacho Peak - there are
at least two and maybe 3 letterboxes there. We are ready! Drive 30 miles
to the park. Pay the fee at the ranger booth. Follow the clues to the
place where the hike starts. Gear up, dogs out - water full, and grab the
letterboxing kit. Where are the stamps. GONE! Must have fallen out of the
van at the art store. In any event - GONE. BUMMER. No more letterboxing
this trip!

We headed back. I asked the GPS for local attractions. Casa Grande
Ruins Park. Hmmmm - hit the GO TO button, and off we go. What a NEAT piece
of history! Visit it if you have the chance. National Park - no
letterboxes. Ask me, they don't belong there..... Delicate historical spot
- stay on the path.

About 5pm I call the ham. He is home, come on out. Punched his
address in the GPS and off we go!

Let me make a side-note here. My vehicle GPS is a GARMIN NUVI-350.
It is WORTH EVERY PENNY I spent on it. TALKING turn-by-turn directions
through a strange city is priceless. At one point the traffic jammed up and
I just punched the DETOUR button. It recalculated a new route in about 10
seconds and got us out of the jam. Painless traveling... Anyway...

Got to the ham's house. What he was selling was worth about $1000
to me. He was offering it for $650. I asked him some technical questions
about it, and he didn't know how to check it. I sent him a document. After
reading it, he lowered his price to $500, partially because the radio had a
problem, and partially because I came to pick it up. Well - I got there,
and there was about $300 is accessories (Ham radio can get expensive) that
he didn't mention. No - the price was still $500. What a DEAL.... So we
loaded up in the van and hit the road. Wife decided the dogs needed to
stretch their legs. NOT A PROBLEM! Punched PARK into the GPS. It found us
a BIG BEAUTIFUL park in Scottsdale. The dogs had a great time. It was near
a tennis court. Did I mention that Great Danes love Tennis Balls? No?
Great Danes love Tennis Balls!!! And there were several. Parker couldn't
decide to pee or chase tennis balls. Finally he grabbed two tennis balls in
his mouth and then found a good tree. At dark we loaded up and started
back. Arrived back at the motel well after 9pm, fed dogs, then ourselves -
and bed.

Sunday morning. Up early- check E-mail - and - OH-NO!!! The laptop
display is out. No computer! Damn. So - pack up, check out, and on to the
showgrounds. Dogs ready. Our turn comes - its going good. But - - -OH-NO!
The judge only likes brown dogs.... At least we get 2nd place today - but it
might as well be last, only first place gets the point.

We lost - so we may as well hit the road. No stamps, so no
letterboxing on the way home. The wind is still blowing - but now it is a
right cross-wind off the rear quarter. Much better mileage. The wife is
driving- its her van. I play radio most of the trip - talking to friends
all over the SW. Even San Antonio! We hit I-10, stopped for a short
breather-picnic in Texas Canyon, and home before 6pm. Dinner was at Pizza
Hut - AFTER the dogs were fed!

I got a great deal on a special radio. We lost our stamps. We lost
the dog show both days. And who knows WHAT it will cost to repair the
laptop.... Oh-well.... Its only money! I'll make more.

Mike - KD9KC.

We DO usually have a good time.
 
Think of a judge kinda like someone passing a driving test.  Once you get the license, those perfect stops and such degrade over time.  Same with the judge.  Once they get a judging license, their personal preferences can come out.  there are some who think a "proper" Great Dane is brown (fawn) and any other color is second-rate.  Others look at structure and movement independent of color.
 
Myself - I did obedience.  You perform to a standard.  Or not.  Your dog does it also.  Or not.  And you can easily tell when you made an error.  The judge knows it, you know it, and the crowd knows it.
 
As for my stamp - I can place an order today and be letterboxing by the weekend.
 
If you INSIST that I carve my own, just go ahead and and drop me from this list.  Because I will never be letterboxing again.  I just don't have the time to take on carving.  Carving is an art, and I am not artistic.
 
UPDATE:  Some good news - still got 42 days warrantee on the laptop.  They will send a repairman to fix it.

The weekend just got better - retro-actively.
