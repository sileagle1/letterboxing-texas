From: (blhjrh@aol.com) | Date: 2008-01-07 19:36:29 UTC-05:00
Here's what I don't want to be:  The last finder on a really good letterbox that goes missing!  Sometimes it happens to all of us, but who doesn't feel guilty and responsible when a letterbox disappears and we were the last finder?  Especially if that box had been in place for some time, had lots of visitors, was well-hidden, and then -- disappears after our visit?  Just thinking about that gives me the shivers and sure makes me want to practice that LB philosophy of "rehide better than you found."  Of course, that doesn't mean "Move the box to a different location, " but to carefully replace the covering debris, bark, or rocks, after making sure the contents were sealed up tightly, and looking around to be sure no one is watching when you put it back.  Not leaving an obvious sign that "something is here - look here!"  Boxes do go missing and for lots of unavoidable reasons, but still,  ... well, that's what I don't want to be: the last finder on any letterbox that then goes missing!
