It seems like besides Pavilion space another thing to consider would be trails.  I think the Rusk event was great, and I had a great time there.  However, there wasn't much hiding space, and if we want to have some room to plant and find boxes, we need a little more trail.  I know the first event was held in Bastrop, but many Texas boxers weren't letterboxing at the time (or had rodeo tickets that day, ahem-ahem) maybe we could consider that park again.  In my humble opinion it is one of the prettiest parks in Texas.  But we'll go wherever you decide to meet (as long as it doesn't interfere with rodeo plans :) MO5

I agree about the trail space - while the Rusk area has a lot of good trail, that park didn't and I think we need to have lots of trail in close proximity to the gathering area.  Of course, I think that's less important if the event is lower-key and doesn't have a lot going on at ground zero, so we can spread further out to find and plant.
 
I would rather go to a different park we haven't been to before, but if there were a lot of votes in favor of Bastrop, I wouldn't mind going back there - it really is a beautiful part of the state and there are tons of trails.  It's funny looking at that area now, because at the time of the first gathering, my surrounding area (say, 8 counties around me) had <10 boxes and I thought I was going to heaven when I saw how many Bastrop had!  I just checked the Bastrop listings and my county now has about five times as many boxes as Bastrop County!
