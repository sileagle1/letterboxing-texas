Box Maintenance Outing to Fairfield

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-12-04 17:24:38 UTC-08:00
Unbelievably, my family had a free day today - well,
my daughter had stuff, but the rest of us were
footloose and fancy-free! My husband, my son, and I
piled in the car and headed to Fairfield to check the
box we planted there in the Spring. I knew when I
planted it that I hadn't used a very good box (it was
the only kind I could find in Fairfield), and figured
I had better get it into a better box before the
winter months hit in earnest.

We hit Fairfield State Park about 2:00 this afternoon
and eased our way to the trail. We took our time
making our way down the trail - I just love that
particular nature trail - it looks different every
time I walk it! My normally cautious 8-year-old son
was feeling especially daring today and wanted to try
walking all the fallen trees, especially the ones that
ran across ravines. He also ventured out to the end
of a point - only to find that it is apparently used
as a community potty by some type of animal - it was
the strangest thing - this little narrow outcropping
was literally covered in dung of some sort (much
bigger than bird dung)! As he was turning to come
back, he slipped and slid face first halfway down the
12 foot embankment - once he managed to stop himself,
we all had a good laugh and he readily climbed back up
the embankment to retrieve his walking stick and climb
back over to us to get checked out - he was pretty
scraped up and had one pretty good cut, but other than
that, he was mainly dirty. He thought it was the
weirdest thing how his legs were shaking afterward -
we tried to explain the aftereffects of adrenalin to
him, but he just thought it was strange his legs were
shaking. He was still game for being daring, for
which I was relieved - I just don't know what to do
with a cautious kiddo!

We continued on and as we were approaching the visual
landmark for the box, we heard something rooting
around and looked up to see an armadillo - completely
unaware of us and enjoying a feast of some sort in an
old rotten tree. We crept close to him and yet he
never seemed to realize we were there - we were so
close I could have reached out my walking stick and
tapped his shell (visions of their verrrryyy long
claws helped me practice some self-control). Finally
he got the idea he had company and moved away
nonchalantly. We followed him a bit, but got bored
and decided to go for the box.

We went back to the landmark and immediately
remembered the hiding place. We went to it, but began
to doubt we were remembering the right place because
it looked as if nothing could be there. I dug a bit
anyway, and sure enough there it was! It was very
well hidden! After opening the box and seeing that
the inkpad had totally puked its guts inside its bag,
I was really worried about the log, but it was in good
condition. Then came the moment of reckoning - I
opened the book and turned pages until I got past our
stamps, turned one more page to see if anyone had
visited (much accustomed to blank pages), and I found
none other than our elusive Grizzled Gryphon's stamp!
Did we ever figure out who that guy is? He had
visited it on 7/11 - nice stamp he has. The next log
entry was the one that totally startled me - having
seen Silver Eagle's and BabyBear's images numerous
times, I only had to glance to know they had been
there - what startled me was the date - TODAY!!! I
know some of you know that feeling of realizing you
have followed fairly closely behind another boxer - it
must be reflex to suddenly perk up and search your
surroundings for somebody walking away from the
vicinity! Needless to say, they were nowhere in
sight, but it was so weird to know they were just
hours ahead of us! Wow! Having met them at the
gathering last Spring, I made my husband drive through
the camping areas to see if we could spot them so we
could stop and say hi. Didn't see 'em so maybe
they'll tell us if they were camping there when they
send off their sitrep. That was so cool though!

As to the box, it's tucked away in a better box now,
and I left my personal stamp pad in the box and took
away the old one. Cleaned the stamp up a bit and
sealed it all up and rehid it. I was glad to see it
was okay - I had a good time carving that particular
stamp and then planting the box - I would've been
really bummed if it had been damaged!

Now I'm thinking I really need to check my boxes in
Tyler. I'm excited to see who all has been to see
them!

What a nice day it was!
Lucy
