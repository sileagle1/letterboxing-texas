Fun day

From: Vicki Stecher (stecher@runbox.com) | Date: 2008-04-08 22:32:08 UTC-05:00
 Today Stinky Monkey, Aslan, Sneaky Cat and I went to piano lessons in Canton early and took about two hours to leisurely letterbox. First we did Frogkisses Birthday Series which was great fun. Not only was the walk very enjoyable, it was on concrete which meant Stinky Monkey did his own walking with minimal falling down. The directions for the boxes were perfect. Stinky Monkey had to go everywhere the big boys went and if they bent over to look for something, he had to bend over as well. He mimicked everything. At one point Sneaky Cat was using a stick to scratch at a leaf pile and Stinky Monkey immediately picked up a stick and scratched at the ground. It was extremely amusing to all of us.
 
We homeschool and on Tuesdays we go to Canton for piano lessons during the day. So the park was completely deserted. Because it kept threatening to rain and spit on us a bit (the downpour came later!) I let the kids stamp in the logbooks but I did the writing for the sake of time. Stinky Monkey and I shared a page, but Aslan and Sneaky Cat had to have their own.

At the second box we came across a great spider. It was huge and brown and running around with a blue/grey egg sack attached to it by a thread of web. It was greatness. We spent almost as much time watching the spider as we did stamping. But the absolute favorite thing of the day was the directions to box #3...it had us count cracks in the sidewalk. Sheer utter joy. I think boys like nothing better then yelling out the numbers as they jumped on the cracks. I figured that even though we were loud, the park was deserted and kids probably count cracks anyway, so I let them do it loudly. Even Stinky Monkey yelled "DAH!" with every crack.
 
After the birthday series (Aslan wistfully asked, "Do you really think it's their birthday?" in a "wish it was my birthday" kind of way.) we went searching for two more boxes. We didn't find one (combination of mud/not being able to figure out the directions/possible stupidity on our part) but the other one (First Monday) was fun. The boys really liked reading the tombstones. They tried to lever up the top of a broken one back into place before deciding that it would probably cause an hernia. The box was sitting on the ground, completely exposed in the correct place, but all was well on the inside. So we buried it under leaves in the same spot.
 
All in all it was a lovely addition to our Tuesday outing. The weather spit at us but the torrential downpour didn't start until we were on our way home. My kids talked non-stop about the spider, the birdhouses, the gravestones and the rain. We can't wait to catch a few more. There are so many in that area that we are hoping to do a few each time we go to piano lessons for a while. Edom, Ben Wheeler, Wills Point, Kaufman...all can be picked up either coming or going.
 
Such great fun! My thanks to all of you who added to our day!
LadyBird BeetleBug
