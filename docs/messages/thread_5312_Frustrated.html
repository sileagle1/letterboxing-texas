From: (sileagle1@yahoo.com) | Date: 2014-01-14 06:34:00 UTC-08:00

Well the new year is starting out just like all the other recent years.  I have already gotten 2 emails about my boxes missing stamps, obviously taken by Geocachers.  With all the other bad things that can happen to letterboxes, this just seems so avoidable and therefore most frustrating to me.  You can hide a box in the most remote place, put it under rocks or sticks, even make the clues word of mouth and it still can be compromised by a Geocacher looking for a nearby cache.  I know it does no good to complain on letterbox sites because we aren't the culprit, so I am thinking about posting on a Geocache site.  Though it will probably do no good, I would like to suggest they change their policy of taking things from boxes, or at least change the wording to say not to take stamps.  Maybe if they are aware of us they will respect us?  With Geocaching proliferating so much faster than letterboxing, it won't be long before all letterboxes are missing stamps and it won't be worth placing any new ones.

Silver Eagle

I'm registered on both letterboxing sites and on geocaching.com (a letterbox clue sent me to geocaching for potential help finding a letterbox...and I still haven't found it!).  I've only been letterboxing since August 2013 and have found letterboxers trashing geocachers and geocachers trashing letterboxes from almost day one.  I think both "hobbies" take the same type of skill...looking for that perfect spot to hide a box/cache and being sneaky trying to locate one (try being sneaky with two dogs in tow).  I found a geocache when I was trying to locate a good spot for a letterbox...darn, got beat to the spot!  But now I know where to find at least one geocache when the dogs go for their geocaching badges.  All that said, here's a thread on the groundspeak website that started in 05/2012 and continued through 2013.  Lots of fingers pointing. http://forums.groundspeak.com/GC/index.php?showtopic=296477  Missing stamps is a continuing problem.  One thing mentioned on this forum is some geocachers said they needed to train their newbies about letterboxes and to leave the stamps in the box.  Some newbies said they were glad they had seen this thread because they had never heard of letterboxing.  This is on the Geocaching site:  http://www.geocaching.com/about/cache_types.aspx     Letterbox Hybrid:   "If there is a stamp inside a Letterbox Hybrid, it is not an item intended for trade. The stamp is meant to remain in the box so that visitors can use it to record their visit. To read more about letterboxing, visit Letterboxing North America."   So I think there is knowledge of a problem and there is some desire to remedy it.  BTW...the dogs and I spent Sunday picking that perfect site for placing my next boxes.  Got a little lost, but found a lost man and his two daughters and we all managed to get back to the cars.  First time I went out without a compass.  That won't happen again!

I came across an interesting feature recently, in a geocache,no less. Geocaching has something vaguely like our hitchhikers called travel bugs. They are meant to move around from cache to cache. However, I came across a travel bug that was part of a larger series of puzzles for solving the locations of other geocaches and this TB was attached to the geocache container with a short length of wire rope and crimped connectors. 

Maybe by attaching our stamps to the container with something people will be a little better idea that the stamp is not a trade item and is meant to remain in the container.

I don't know if this helps, as the only problems I've had were caused by my own planting newbie-ness so far, but I put the word SWAG on the box with one of those big, red crosshatches over it.  Short and sweet.  Here's an interesting note about labeling the container.  At Open Space's Delicious Delights event, NLW and I were hunting a box and we could see a family of bikers right where the box was.  Sure enough, we called out to them b/c at some point it became clear they weren't letterboxers (and we asked them, too).  Even with the writing on the box clearly identifying it as a letterbox, they were convinced it was the cache they were seeking, b/c, well, that's what was supposed to be there, and the minor difference in wording just didn't penetrate.  We explained it all and they went their merry way.  They were incredibly nice, but it was interesting how little information erodes a preconceived notion!  I'm not suggesting you not label the box, just that it was amusing at how little difference it made in that instance.  I guess they're as gung-ho and excited as we are.  I'm kinda hoping we converted them after telling them about boxing.
