DFW boxing adventures

From: Christine Borgfeld (christine@borgfeld.net) | Date: 2012-05-28 22:01:11 UTC-05:00

We had a wonderful time boxing in the DFW area this weekend.  Mosaic Butterfly, Wry Me, and I boxed in several places including the Fort Worth Botanic Gardens, Oakwood Cemetery (Ft Worth) Mansfield, and several spots around Cedar Hill.  We also met Gryzzled Gryphon and Frost Maiden for a late lunch Saturday. 

 

It was warm and dry with perfect weather for Memorial Day weekend.  The Botanic Gardens were beautiful and we enjoyed the boxes there (especially the Mythology series which is alive and well despite being listed as unknown).  I only wished we had enough time to get all the boxes there.  After lunch we stopped at Oakwood Cemetery for Viewfinder’s and Black Widow’s boxes which were all a treat.  On Sunday we enjoyed finding most of Frost Maiden’s boxes in Mansfield which has some beautiful parks with paved trails.  Later that afternoon we visited several boxes around Cedar Hill and were fortunate enough to run into some other letterboxers!  We were at Viewfinder’s box in Cedar Ridge Nature Preserve when we met Hootin’ Hooters.  They were on a cross country road trip from  North Carolina and had never met other letterboxes before.  We had one of those awkward moments when we were holding the box and ready to put it back, and they were watching us in hopes we would leave!  Finally my husband whispered that he thought they were boxers, about the same time they asked “are you looking for something?”  So all ended well and we got a new exchange. 

 

So thanks to all the DFW boxers who planted there and made our trip such a pleasure!  We left behind a few boxes for you that I hope everyone enjoys.  Now I need to continue logging finds. 

 

Open Space

 

PS.  One rather strange highlight of our trip was the butterflies.  Our campsite at Cedar Hill State Park was packed full of butterflies and we couldn’t keep them away from our food and trash!  I think the park needs a butterfly stamp…..

Dont know what the deal is this year with the butterflies, but here in Northeast Texas weve had an absolutely wonderful showing of butterflies this spring!

Im kicking myself for not planting some butterfly-friendly plants this year, but I just wasnt in the frame of mind to see everything I plant burn up like happened last year. Not only have they been plentiful, but theyve been big and varied.

Butterflies are a great pick-me-up!
