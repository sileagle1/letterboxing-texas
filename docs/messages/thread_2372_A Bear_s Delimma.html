A Bear's Delimma

From: Baby Bear (babybearlb2002@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-12-08 14:24:07 UTC
While searching for LB's in Memorial park this week with Silver
Eagle, I told him about my adventure in finding the mystery
box, "GW". He suggested I share this story with everyone, so all
get get a laugh at my expense as he did. Don't worry Jeff, I will
not disclose details on the box location.

I arrived at the park with the clues I had figured out in hand (but
still not clear). So I got to the location I was sure of, then
began going through the options. After 30 minutes of searching I
heard a noise and figured it was more joggers coming by (I had
already hid behind a tree for the set of 10 that had run by 20
minutes ago). I turned to look and got chills when I saw a huge dog
(rotty)with a spike collar running at me. I knew I could not out
run him, so I just hoped he would not attack. Turns out he was
friendly. Actually he was to friendly. He kept jumping up on me
wanting to play. I waited about 5 minutes, looking down different
paths to see if someone would come to claim him, but no one came.
So I went back to looking for the box, thinking he would leave.
NO. He kept following me everywhere. Then a jogger came along and
he ran up to her. I could tell she was scared so I yelled that "he
is friendly, but he is not Mine". Unfortunatly he did not go with
her, he stayed with me. So I decided I needed to shake him. I
walked back to the main part of the park, as I could here people
playing out there. I thought if he would see others he would go to
them. He walked ahead and scanned the park, as I backed off slowly
down the trail. This worked for about 30 seconds, when he turned
and saw me way down the trail and came galloping back. Okay, plan
B. After following me for awhile he wondered into the trees. I
walked up the trail then hid behind a big tree. Soon he realized I
was gone and started to search. He ran up the trail, stopping on
occasion to listen for me. As he approached my tree I worked my way
around it so as he passed he did not see me. He continued up the
trail about 50 yards, stopped, then turned around and came back to
my tree. Foiled again. Did I mention I was trying to find "GW"
box. It had now been 1:30. I was about to just give up and
leave, as I was concerned about this dog chasing someone else, when
a lady came running up another path calling the dog. Finally, the
owner, I'm free. Not so fast. She then wanted to talk. 30 more
minutes gone. She departed with dog, but as I was plowing through
the brush, she came back by again (probably wondering at this time
why I am still in the same area and digging through the brush). In
fact she came back and forth about 4 times, until another person
joined her and the left the area. Now I could focus on the box. I
found it (in original place I though it would be), after 2 1/4 hours
of dog infested searching. At least I did not in trouble with a
Park Ranger for once!

Baby Bear
