Siterep - Aug 4th

From: tigger32167 (tigger32167@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-08-07 03:18:19 UTC
We left Wag Time's house at 6 am after going to bed around 12:30. I
do have to give credit to Wag Time for being up before me this
morning. She is normally the late night, late morning person, and I
normally am in bed early and get up early, but she beat me this
time. The first find was I See the Moon in Arlington. Wag Time had
already found this box, but she took me to it so that I could locate
it as well. Our next stop was at Wag Time's Garden of Angels in
Arlington. This is a really neat site that is dedicated to people
who have been murdered. It has a lot of white crosses with victims'
names on them. The original cross was for a girl who was murdered
at the site of the garden. There are also crosses with the names of
the Yates kids from Houston. We attempted Red Eared Slider at the
UTA campus in Arlington for the second time and we still were not
able to find this box. We attempted the 22 Hour Day box in
Arlington and it was also missing. We then went to the Prairie
Springs Cemetery in Burleson (which by the way is the Crepe Myrtle
Capital of the World) and attempted the Wild Cherry LB, but it was
also missing. Our next stop was Joshua to the First Baptist Church
and we found The LUV Box, which was right near a rail road and a
train came by right after we stamped in and blew its horn, which
caused our hearing to go away for a few minutes. Went to Joshua
Cemetery in Joshua to find Jewel in Joshua. We found the right tree
but no box. We looked around and finally located the box a couple
of trees down from where it should have been. We replaced the box
after we stamped in. Looked for A Walk Down Old Main in Joshua, but
could not locate the right street nor the bank with a fountain
nearby. We then went to Cleburne to the Rose Hill Cemetery
and found the Where Have All the Flowers Gone lb at the Veterans
Memorial. A side note about this area of the cemetery is that there
is a plaque there that lists members of the confederate army from
Johnson County who were held as POW's and who are buried in Illinois
in unmarked graves. They all have the date of their death as 1863.
We then went to Splash Station in Cleburne and found the Spirit of
Cleburne box. This was kind of neat because the train engine is
fenced in with a chain link fence about 10 feet high and a
sign that tells you to not touch because this is part of a feral cat
community where the cats are wild. The city maintains them. We did
see about 4-5 cats in the enclosure. We then made it to Dinosaur
Valley State Park and found the Howlin at the Moon box. We did see
a couple of dinosaur tracks. The view from this box is beautiful.
We then went and found the Dinosaur Tracks box and left the
Microsoft Office:Access HH in this box. We then went to Granbury to
the boat ramp to find the Afloat in a Boat Box. We
found the right location and the rock it was supposed to be under,
but the box was gone. Our next stop was Houston Street Park and
found the Legends of Granbury Series #3 - John Wilkes Booth box and
picked up a HH. We then went to Shandlin Park which connects with
Houston Street Park and found the Flutterbies box, but did not find
the Let's Bug Out box. We then went to Granbury Cemetery and saw the
grave of Jesse James (at least that is what folks in Granbury say).
We did look for the Legends of Granbury Series #1 - Jesse James box,
which had already been reported as missing, and we did not find it.
While we were in this cemetery we decided to look into a mausoleum
that was unlocked. Upon entering we noticed there was a large hole
where a body should be laying (the mausoleum holds about 6-8 people
in it). There was no body, but it definitely had an eerie feeling
to it. We also saw the grave of Davy Crockett's grandson in this
cemetery. We then went to look for the Hear Ye Hear Ye box at the
courtyard square and it was missing. We then looked for You Scream,
I Scream box off the square in Granbury and did find this
box. We then stopped at Rinky Tink's Ice Cream shop and got ice
cream to finish off our time in Granbury. (yes, even JR got ice
cream). We then traveled to Weatherford so that Wag Time could pick
up the Badge of Friendship box at the Greenwood Cemetery. JB found
this box on her way to Arizona back in June. While we were here we
came across the historical marker for Bose Ikard who was a black man
who worked as a scout for Goodnight and Loving on their trail drives
and was the inspiration for the Deets character in the book/movie
Lonesome Dove. We then went to Caddo so that Wag Time could pick up
the Tejas Caddo box in Caddo Cemetery that JB again had found on her
way to Arizona. The box was in good shape. It is 8:17 pm and we
still have to travel back to Grand Prairie...so our day is done.
One side note about today, during our journey we managed to see
several of those big blow ups that car dealerships have on their
buildings. We saw three rather unique ones today that I think are
worth mentioning...a Frankenstein, an Elvis (who was as white as a
ghost) and a pot of money in bills and coins at a bank. Just
thought I would share this with everyone.
JB Kokopelli

We left Wag Time's house at 6 am after going to bed around 12:30. I
do have to give credit to Wag Time for being up before me this
morning. She is normally the late night, late morning person, and I
normally am in bed early and get up early, but she beat me this
time. The first find was I See the Moon in Arlington. Wag Time had
already found this box, but she took me to it so that I could locate
it as well. Our next stop was at Wag Time's Garden of Angels in
Arlington. This is a really neat site that is dedicated to people
who have been murdered. It has a lot of white crosses with victims'
names on them. The original cross was for a girl who was murdered
at the site of the garden. There are also crosses with the names of
the Yates kids from Houston. We attempted Red Eared Slider at the
UTA campus in Arlington for the second time and we still were not
able to find this box. We attempted the 22 Hour Day box in
Arlington and it was also missing. We then went to the Prairie
Springs Cemetery in Burleson (which by the way is the Crepe Myrtle
Capital of the World) and attempted the Wild Cherry LB, but it was
also missing. Our next stop was Joshua to the First Baptist Church
and we found The LUV Box, which was right near a rail road and a
train came by right after we stamped in and blew its horn, which
caused our hearing to go away for a few minutes. Went to Joshua
Cemetery in Joshua to find Jewel in Joshua. We found the right tree
but no box. We looked around and finally located the box a couple
of trees down from where it should have been. We replaced the box
after we stamped in. Looked for A Walk Down Old Main in Joshua, but
could not locate the right street nor the bank with a fountain
nearby. We then went to Cleburne to the Rose Hill Cemetery
and found the Where Have All the Flowers Gone lb at the Veterans
Memorial. A side note about this area of the cemetery is that there
is a plaque there that lists members of the confederate army from
Johnson County who were held as POW's and who are buried in Illinois
in unmarked graves. They all have the date of their death as 1863.
We then went to Splash Station in Cleburne and found the Spirit of
Cleburne box. This was kind of neat because the train engine is
fenced in with a chain link fence about 10 feet high and a
sign that tells you to not touch because this is part of a feral cat
community where the cats are wild. The city maintains them. We did
see about 4-5 cats in the enclosure. We then made it to Dinosaur
Valley State Park and found the Howlin at the Moon box. We did see
a couple of dinosaur tracks. The view from this box is beautiful.
We then went and found the Dinosaur Tracks box and left the
Microsoft Office:Access HH in this box. We then went to Granbury to
the boat ramp to find the Afloat in a Boat Box. We
found the right location and the rock it was supposed to be under,
but the box was gone. Our next stop was Houston Street Park and
found the Legends of Granbury Series #3 - John Wilkes Booth box and
picked up a HH. We then went to Shandlin Park which connects with
Houston Street Park and found the Flutterbies box, but did not find
the Let's Bug Out box. We then went to Granbury Cemetery and saw the
grave of Jesse James (at least that is what folks in Granbury say).
We did look for the Legends of Granbury Series #1 - Jesse James box,
which had already been reported as missing, and we did not find it.
While we were in this cemetery we decided to look into a mausoleum
that was unlocked. Upon entering we noticed there was a large hole
where a body should be laying (the mausoleum holds about 6-8 people
in it). There was no body, but it definitely had an eerie feeling
to it. We also saw the grave of Davy Crockett's grandson in this
cemetery. We then went to look for the Hear Ye Hear Ye box at the
courtyard square and it was missing. We then looked for You Scream,
I Scream box off the square in Granbury and did find this
box. We then stopped at Rinky Tink's Ice Cream shop and got ice
cream to finish off our time in Granbury. (yes, even JR got ice
cream). We then traveled to Weatherford so that Wag Time could pick
up the Badge of Friendship box at the Greenwood Cemetery. JB found
