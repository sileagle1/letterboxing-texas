Okay, you guys really DO scare me sometimes! And
today has been especially scary!! Starting with
krohm's post this morning and ending with Steve's
above - oh MY!

Here's the scoop on patches...at least as I have it
right now:

I've run through several patch places I know of,
including my latest favorite, and it appears that
basically you can't get less than 50 anywhere, and 50
is only slightly less expensive for the lot than 100
is. The cost per patch if we order 50 will be about
$3.60 per person, whereas it will be about $1.90 if we
order 100. I say, let's order 100, unless I start
getting a rash of orders to force our quantity upward
of 100 (we're standing right about 50 right now - I'll
post the list tomorrow since I left it at work). How
it works is this: they charge you based on quantity
and % of the patch that is stitched. Personally, I
like 100% stitched - they look finer. But if someone
has an issue with the price, I'll look at going with
75% stitched. And if anyone is considering using them
for exchanges and FF prizes, as Steve suggested, the
price may be a consideration (by the way, I kind of
like that idea myself!).

I figure postage will be roughly .50 per patch (I
haven't worked it out that far yet). Of course, if
you are planning on going to the gathering and would
be willing to wait, I could just bring them to the
gathering to save postage.

I'll post the artwork on the group in the files
section for y'all to see, but basically it looks like,
well, the logo on the group homepage. Imagine that!

There is a product called Aleene's Tack it Over that is available that can be used on the back of the patches.  This is a glue-like substance that is placed on the back of patches that allows the patch to be removed from a garment and then stuck back on to allow for laundering without affecting the patch.   It will stick over and over again.  It is usually available at Hobby Lobby, Michaels, and Joanns.  I have used this many times and it works great.  If it starts to lose it's stickiness, all you have to do is apply a bit more.
