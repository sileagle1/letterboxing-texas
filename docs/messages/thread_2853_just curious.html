From: (blhjrh@aol.com) | Date: 2007-06-21 18:57:24 UTC-04:00
I was First Finder for a  that had been in place for almost a year.  It wasn't really in a remote location, but a little off the beaten track.   Actually, finding a letterbox or having one that isn't readily accessible and therefore frequently visited can be a cool thing - you feel pretty special when you find it.  I have a couple that are in fairly remote locations that probably won't get many visitors (in fact, one of them has been in place for about 8 months and has had 1 person log a find so far), but when I hear from someone that found one of those boxes, it's a big deal to me, too!

From: Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter (janet@redbarnretreat.com) | Date: 2007-06-22 02:23:54 UTC
A lot of finders don't bother to register the find on either LbNA or
AtlasQuest, so you don't get notified. I'm not sure why. That
could be another thing that I would like for new letterboxers to
learn to do: notify the placer when you find their letterbox. I
don't know how you educate people if they don't subscribe to a list
like this one. I like to know when my boxes are visited.

Boots Tex
