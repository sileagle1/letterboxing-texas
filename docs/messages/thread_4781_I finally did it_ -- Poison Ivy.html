From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2011-05-09 14:08:52 UTC
I never had poison ivy reactions until a few years after I started letterboxing. I had spent many a summer tramping through Missouri woods with no reaction at all, so I thought I was immune.

When I got it the first time, it wasn't a big deal, just a bit on my hand...but the second time I had scratched my head and ended up with poison ivy all over my skull. So painful and horrid in the middle of a Texas summer. Now, I just look at the stuff funny and I start to break out.

We should all, as planters, come armed with knowledge of what poison ivy looks like--avoid hairy vines in winter!

But as finders, we should understand that sometimes the stuff just happens. At planting time, there's no poison ivy there and over the course of the growing season it appears (sometimes years later). Bring your tecnu, but be flexible about whether a box is worth finding.

For me, a box in the middle of a poison ivy stand is a box I'll try later. I actually have a few boxes I can't do maintenance on because I can't reach them.

For the sensitive among us, it's nasty stuff masquerading as an innocent little vine.

Effect of Carbs and Sugars on PI outbreaks (was Re: [letterboxingtexas] Re: I finally did it! -- Poison Ivy)

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2011-05-09 23:02:08 UTC-05:00
I've really only been doing serious low carb/low sugar for a couple of months now and that's been for health reasons. After a severe bout of pancreatitis (which struck the day after we got home from TALE), what had been pre-diabetes became full-blown diabetes thanks to damage to my pancreas from the illness. Because I'm not a heavy drinker and I didn't have gall stones, the default explanation for the pancreatitis was my diet, which wasn't all that bad to begin with, but the experience left me with a fervent desire to never travel that road again. I immediately began reducing fat in my diet (the primary dietary cause of pancreatitis) and after the diabetes diagnosis hit, I started looking at reducing carbs and sugar as well. It's just very nearly impossible to find a happy medium between fats, carbs, and sugars.

All of that said to say that I'm probably not a great test case for the effect of carbs and sugars on PI. For one thing, I've had very little PI exposure since the weather has started warming up, and for another, what exposure I have had has resulted in outbreaks on my hands, which I've treated with Technu immediately after exposure. Add to that the fact that I'm still working to find the right dietary balances and I'm taking a fistful of new medication, and I'm still really recovering from being so sick and my body is all out of whack anyway, it's a wonder PI exposure isn't making me turn purple. Who really knows what's working and what's not working for me? If I had not been on a low carb/low sugar diet, would I have gotten a lot worse cases of PI? Does the fact that I got PI in spite of my diet negate the idea that diet can cure it?

I am interested to hear an explanation for why diet might help recover from PI. Guess I might google it in the morning....LOL.
