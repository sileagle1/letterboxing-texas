Good boxer gone BAD!

From: Puddle Splasher (cjagger405@earthlink.net) | Date: 2008-07-21 15:51:18 UTC
Okay! When good boxers go bad, it's an ugly thing. I woke up early
this morning and went into the living room to finish knitting a hat I
was working on. I love to be in there early when the birds are
singing and the light is just right and there's NO TV blaring in the
background. I noticed my backpack slung over the back of a dining
room chair, where I hung it yesterday when I came in from finding a
couple of really cool boxes in Denton (35 miles from my house). I
picked it up to take it to my workroom, but paused and decided to
look at my logbook at the two new stamps I got yesterday. Then I
pulled the items out of my little plastic bag inside the backpack to
reorganize it (which is something I rarely do). In fact my regular
routine is to leave my backpack in the back of the Jeep for a few
days before bringing it in and throwing it on a chair in my workroom
for a few weeks before grabbing in one day to rush out the door to
find a box that caught my interest (like the ones yesterday). I guess
the universe was telling me I needed to take a closer look in my
backpack this morning because to my great surprise, I found I had not
only my Puddle Splasher stamp, but also one of the stamps from the
boxes I found yesterday. NO WAY! Did I say these boxes were 35 miles
from my house? That's 70 miles round trip! Since these boxes had only
been planted a few days ago, I could imagine a disappointed
letterboxer holding a box in their hand, dismay on their face, as
they sadly realized there was no stamp in the box. Obviously, I had
no choice but to hop back in the Jeep, stamp in hand, and after a
stop at the gas station, join the traffic heading west on 380 for
THIRTY FIVE MILES and return the stamp to it's hiding spot! Mission
accomplished, but I hope no one came searching for that box between
the hours of 6:00 p.m. last night to 8:30 this morning! I realized
what I had done when I opened the box. I had taken the nicely folded
paper towel which was supposed to be wrapped around the stamp and
placed it back inside the plastic bag... not realizing the stamp was
not inside the towel! So by the time I drove the 35 miles BACK to
McKinney after replacing the stamp, the morning light was gone, the
birds weren't singing and the TV was blaring, and I'd been putting up
with heavy truck traffic for an hour and a half on 380! It's sad when
a good boxer goes bad!
Puddle Splasher
