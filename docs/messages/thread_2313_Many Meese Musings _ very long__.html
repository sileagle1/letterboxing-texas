Many Meese Musings ( very long!)

From: Ruby Tuesday (celticlions@comcast.net) | Date: 2006-11-19 12:55:50 UTC-06:00

Thank you to everyone who took part in the Metroboxers’ first gathering – the Many Meese Mini-Meet at Lake Grapevine yesterday!

 

The morning began with clear skies, and crisp, cool beautiful weather that warmed as the day wore on. No sooner had Stillwater and I started carrying boxes from the car to the picnic table area than the first boxer showed up – iamthebatchler – and he was carrying his first letterbox to plant, “Moose With a Date” – it was a great way to start the day, chatting with such an enthusiastic new boxer! (Sometime I’m going to have to get more details on his unique carving method – sounds like very interesting use of materials!)

Then Beneficus Rex and his appropriately named daughter, Pure Joy appeared – I’d been looking forward to meeting him after reading his Personal Traveler clues and a couple of funny posts on the MetroBoxers list – he was the brave soul ( and very good sport) to undertake the infamous Orange Ball recipe! The always delightful PuddleSplasher and her LSS showed up, prepared for making that tasty chili, and I noticed that he and Stillwater seemed to be holding an informal LSS support group later…would have been interesting to eavesdrop …;-)

It was such a treat to have Dewberry and her Gramatrick crew drive up from Houston for the event – we were especially impressed that her LSS dressed in Moose costume – particularly since the moose costume we ordered for Vandal did not arrive – (and Vandal swears he had nothing to do with the package going missing …). Dewberry’s sons, Gramapillar and Batrick are very experienced letterboxers in their own right, and definitely added to the gathering. (Did everyone find the “Mickey Moose” box? I would give anything to have had a tape recorder going when Gryzzled Gryphon offered his original lyrics to that tune!)

Dewberry’s Goat Cheese Goop was delicious – can’t wait to make that recipe for my crew!

And yes, our legendary Gryzzled Gryphon did put in an appearance at the gathering! And yes, he did bring the clues to a new Moose box – “Give Meese A Chance”. (Those of you who have had to humbly admit to aging eyes will be gratified to know that he brought printed clues! But wait – wasn’t deciphering his handwriting part of the challenge?;-))

QueenBeeloved and her hive arrived ( I nominate her LSS for an award – what a great guy) and I was treated to a glimpse of one of her new stamps – ( gnashing teeth in envy) – is it fair for a relatively new boxer to be so talented a carver? We’re so grateful to her for carving the 3 Event stamps and creating such a perfectly themed sign-in scroll – for anyone who wasn’t able to attend, perhaps we can persuade her to make an event box with the stamps for the next gathering?

Part of our own CelticLions crew, Flicker and her LSS showed up with Austin the bouncing Boxer. Flicker is actually the one who jump-started us into this activity about three years ago, and we need to lure her back in!

Blooming Flowers showed up, bringing “Moose Oop” and the mysterious and much-anticipated “Moose Turds” Potluck treat – both of which turned out to be terrific. Sharon of the PEAS brought a delightful event box, “Mr. and Mrs. Zeus Moose” - very cute and creative – I finally grabbed an up-close & personal look at it at La Madeleine’s, after watching folks enjoying it all day.

This was my first chance to meet Monarch B and PomPom Bear – some of the nicest people are letterboxers, aren’t they? PomPom Bear is another young boxer who impressed me with how enthusiastic and self-possessed a young person can be in dealing with adults. (And I know that the huggable muffin moose now has a good home!)

The gathering had been originally motivated by the chance that Silver Eagle would be in the area on that day, so we were thrilled that he and Running Rain were able to join us. I know he probably cringes a bit when we refer to him as the “Father of Texas Letterboxing”, but he and Baby Bear are the reasons many of us are involved in this sport/hobby/addiction/cult – and we’ll always be grateful.

I caught up with Bluebutterfly, Lucy Locket and Roarin’Lion as they were going thru the Happy Birthday event box – it may be that we’ve finally found a Texas Letterboxer with a May Birthday – Stinky Monkey, so we need to make sure he gets to stamp into that calendar! (And I loved hearing the reasons behind those trail names;-)!)

Speaking of trail names, I have no idea why Strawberry’s LSS is called Big Red – can anyone help me on that one? But I always enjoy watching folks trying to get Strawberry’s personal traveler!

A serendipity for the gathering was the appearance of Shiloh, who drove down from Arkansas for the event. What an interesting guy – one bit of fascinating info in his profile is that he helped make the knives for the second Rambo movie – how cool is that???

A few years ago there were only two boxers in the Metroplex – CelticLions in Dallas and Kantexan in Arlington – and for a while we pretty much just had to look for each other’s boxes, with an occasional boost from Silver Eagle and Baby Bear going through the area! You can’t imagine what a kick it was for us to finally meet Kantexan and her SamuraiStampers yesterday! And she is every bit as much fun as we expected! (The funny thing is I expected the SamuraiStampers to be smaller and younger, because I had an impression of them from their first boxes they created way-back-when). Kantexan pulled out a piece of carving material and cut out the shape of a stamp we’ve been talking about doing for a couple of years – “MetroBoxers Unite!” – it’s a pair of boxer shorts divided in half, with one half to be hidden in Dallas and one in the Arlington/Fort Worth area…;-) Look for clues coming soon!

The only dampener on the day was that Barefoot Lucy was stuck at home with strep throat – hope you’re feeling better, Lucy!

Just as we were packing up for the day, a young couple walked up to the Moose Crossing sign and introduced themselves as The Cruisers. I felt absolutely terrible, because it was around 4:00 p.m., and we had posted the gathering as lasting until 6:00, but assumed everyone who was going to come already had scattered out to the trails to hunt meeses. Luckily they were able to come to La Madeleine’s later that night and we thoroughly enjoyed getting to know them. Can’t wait to drive to Benbrook and look for their boxes!

The gathering at La Madeleine’s was lovely and low-key – and unfortunately low-light! It was a nice wind-down for us all after a long day and we were so tickled that Viewfinder managed to  join us, particularly after some harrowing adventures in Austin – (which you need to hear first-hand) – and which provoked a tongue-in-cheek debate between PuddleSplasher and me about the priorities between death-defying injuries and letterboxing clues….discuss amongst yourselves.

 

I can see looking at the list of RSVPs that I missed out chatting with the Hoods, Stargazer, Nicolette and JB Kokopelli – so obviously the only solution is to do this again in a few months!

 

Again, thank you to everyone for bringing your contributions for the potluck lunch – thanks for creating all the great Moose boxes – and thanks for making this so much fun and so easy!

Ruby Tuesday of CelticLions
