From: Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter (janet@redbarnretreat.com) | Date: 2007-10-02 00:28:29 UTC
A while back a very nice new letterboxer found one of my boxes and
asked how she could get a list of all my planted boxes. I told her to
go onto AQ and find me and click on my logbook and she would get a
complete list of my 51 planted letterboxers. She wrote back telling
me that she could only see 38 of my boxes and only 11 were part of my
Texas governors series, which is what she was interested in. I went
into AQ myself and, logged in as me, Boots Tex, I could view all of my
boxes, including all 19 governors. However, logged in as Lone Star
Quilter, I could only see 38, including 11 governor boxes. Can anyone
tell me why this happens? I am a premium member and LSQ isn't, but I
don't see why that would make a difference in viewing someone's
logbook.

Boots Tex

From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-10-02 02:47:02 UTC
Ryan's always responsive to those kind of questions. Shoot him an
email and ask him.

My guess is that they are set at a certain number of logged finds.
You have 600 logged finds; LSQ only has 100 and something. There are
F levels of 250 & 500.

If you want a quick check with one of the boxes she can't see, go into
edit boxes section. The new Pcount and Fcount restrictions (because
now you can restrict to people who have planted x number of boxes, not
just found) are on the same page as the clue.

If you send Ryan a note, he'll copy your finds to LSQ's logbook too

the google maps, so for all those who have entered a park, but not an
address, google maps will be able to map it with just a park name.
This will help tremendously in finding nearby letterboxes. Not sure
if he's implemented that part yet, but it's coming. He's been testing
on some Houston boxes.

From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-10-02 18:48:16 UTC
There are no longer premium-member only boxes. People didn't like it,
as it seemed too much like paying for clues, so that option was
removed (a long time ago, maybe 18 months or so?) F-count
restrictions were offered in place of premium member boxes. Now,
there are P-count restrictions. The theory is that planters
understand better how to rehide & care for someone else's box.

I rarely use restrictions, but it's an option for planters.

dewberry
