Finally got to get out and GO!

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-10-17 09:01:07 UTC-07:00
As the weather began cooling off a few weeks ago, it
was all I could do to NOT go letterboxing - but life
kept getting in the way! Life threatened again to
interfere this weekend, but we prevailed!

We got up bright and early yesterday, packed our gear
and grabbed a couple of the boxes we've had stacked up
and waiting to go out for the past couple months. We
knew we had to be in Rusk at about 2:00, but that gave
us the morning to roam where we wanted, right?

Before we hit the trails, we stopped by a cub scout
training here in town for what was supposed to be a
quick check to see if they needed anything, and I was
asked to present at the last minute - so there went
the morning (had fun anyway - I LOVE scout trainings -
they are soooo fun!). We quickly readjusted and
decided we would plant one box, and we would plant it
in the park we had to be at in Rusk before we had to
meet some of our cub scouts.

The training ran into the afternoon, so we had to
leave early because we had promised some little guys
we would meet them at the Jamboree on the Air in Jim
Hogg State Park and then take them on their first
hike, and we didn't want to let them down. By then,
we were running very late, but we made it to Rusk in
the nick of time. Not knowing these boys very well, I
hesitated to plant a box with them in tow, and where I
wanted to plant the box was a little far to take them
anyway, so again we adjusted our plans and decided we
would take the box out to its spot AFTER we finished
with the boys - by then, I was already pretty tired,
but that seemed to be the only solution.

Well, the boys loved their hike and didn't want to
head back to the trail head - it seems like we looked
at every spider, every bug, every mushroom, and every
tree and tuft of grass - we even accidentally ran
across a baby water moccasin whose back appeared to
have been broken so he couldn't run away. He kept
striking at us (to which we had to tell the boys "Yes,
he's just a baby but he's still VERY poisonous, and
NO, it isn't a good idea to get your face that close
to him!). We herded the boys along and I went back
and euthanized the snake (I hate to kill any kind of
critter, even poisonous ones!). It had been quite a
hike by the time we got back to the trail head. I
love it when these guys get excited about what they
see in the woods, but we had busines to tend to!

Finally, we sent the boys off with their mamas, who
had stayed at the playground with their younger
children. We STILL had a box to plant, and I had had
my heart set on boxing a little this weekend ALL week,
and we were in the right place and had the right
stuff, but my shoelace on one shoe had managed to
strip itself so it would no longer stayed tight and
tied. We looked around the park and saw that the
Jamboree was still going on although the crowd was
very light at the moment, there was somebody set up
with a P.A. system singing gospel music and trying to
gather a crowd, and there were at least three birthday
parties going on - awfully crowded! Well there was no
way we were going to leave without planting that box!

We headed back into the woods, this time toward an old
mining area we wanted to plant the box near. As we
were getting into the area we wanted to plant the box
in, we ran smack dab into one of the birthday parties.
What man's idea of fun would be taking 18 (yes,
eighteen) 6-year-old boys and girls walking in the
woods BY HIMSELF??? They were about 3/4 mile out too!
We finally got ourselves clear of the party (and I
could hear a plaintive little voice say "I have to go
to the bathroom! just as their voices faded out of our
hearing). We started searching out "the spot" when my
husband's cell phone rang - and he had to report to
work to fix a computer problem! We were determined by
then to find our spot, so we finally got it and I sat
down to write clues and rest my feet (did you know it
is really important to the comfort of your feet for
your shoelaces to work properly???) while my daughter
hid the box. I like the spot quite a lot myself, and
I hope the directions are right! I posted it late
last night - it's called Starry, Starry Night and it's
in Cherokee County - and I felt particularly satisfied
to be posting a new box.

Next weekend, I'm going boxing for real!!!
Lucy
