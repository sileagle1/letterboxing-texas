Water is inching up

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-07-06 16:39:37 UTC-05:00
Well they put Jacksonville on water rations, or more correctly, sewage rations today.  We can't wash clothes or dishes or makes excess waste water because the sewage plants are full and so is the lake.  Supposedly this is at least a two day ration, but I'm sure it will be longer if the rains keep coming.
 
Being a Red Cross emergency responder, I got a call about an hour ago to be on standby because if it begins raining again we will be setting up an emergency shelter and evacuating the lake area at the very least.  My in-laws live at the lake (on high ground) and their piers are all underwater and even their floating pier is tugging at its anchor points. 
And the sky is cloudy.  Gotta love the rain, but I'm thinking we need to tilt Texas up on the eastern side and let it flow to the west!

Heh heh heh - there was a time when weather didn't matter.  It was more than an equalizer.  It was usually worse on the enemy than us - our radars and night-vision/thermal imaging systems out-performed their best.
 
But I am through with that.  I can be picky about the weather I shoot, hike, and camp in now.
 
I don't really mind the rain.  But a replacement spotting scope is $500, and a replacement competition rifle is $1000+.  I could afford that - if I had to.  But the stuff I have is working fine and if I take care of it I will probably be dead before it needs replacement.  Keeping it out of the rain is taking care of it.
 
Or maybe I am getting soft!  <grin>
 
Now - my son James would shoot in the rain.  But WE (taxpayers) pay for his rifle, and he can get another just by asking for it.  And he don't use a spotting scope to see his hits.  He uses reactive targets.  They fall down when he hits them.
 
They get ALL the good toys!
