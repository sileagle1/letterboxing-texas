Mercer Arboretum

From: Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter (janet@redbarnretreat.com) | Date: 2007-09-05 23:04:43 UTC
We went by the Mercer Arboretum and Botanical Gardens today to check
on a letterbox that had been picked up by an employee. The box was
placed by our son, Fish Tree and is called "Thelma's Garden". It
turns out that it had already been retrieved by him, but we (Wills
World and I) talked to the lady about it. She told me it was found
in a flower bed by a gardener and brought to the office. They
emailed my son and told him he could pick it up. This box wasn't
originally placed in a flower bed, so I don't know how it got there,
but I thought I'd just remind everyone that the folks at Mercer have
no problem with letterboxes in the arboretum, but they ask that they
not be placed in or near a flower bed and that they don't require a
finder to walk in or across a flower bed. She said that they first
became aware of letterboxes when they noticed a bed very badly
trampled and found a box in it that was full of trinkets. We had a
discussion on the difference between a letterbox and a geocache.
So, if you plan to visit Mercer anytime soon, be aware
that "Thelma's Garden" is temporarily missing. By the way, the lady
had looked at the clue sheet for this box on LbNA and thought it was
a wonderful story about the Mercers and the gardens. Please watch
where you place boxes in this beautiful place so they will continue
to be Letterbox-Friendly. Thanks,

Boots Tex

Mercer Arboretum

From: Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter (janet@redbarnretreat.com) | Date: 2008-12-20 03:55:25 UTC
We spent some time in Humble today visiting family and I had a couple
of hours to kill, so, taking a cue from Silver Eagle's recent trip to
Brazos Bend SP, I decided to go to Mercer Arboretum and check on the
Murder boxes. I hadn't been since Hurricane Ike, so it was about
time. This was an impromptu trip, so I didn't take any clues with
me, just trusting my memory to locate the boxes. The park took a
heavy hit from Ike, and a lot of trees and limbs were down. A lot of
clean-up had taken place, with fallen trees cut into pieces and
removed from the trails. Also, a lot of underbrush has been cleared
as a part of the process, so the park looks a little more open.
Murder at the Arboretum Box #1 was uncovered and about 6 feet away
from its tree, but hidden from the trail, so it was put back in its
place. I passed by The Pinata Box and recognized its location, so I
stopped and it was partially visible and two of the snap tabs on the
box were undone, but inside the box was dry and the logbook okay, so
I put it back and covered it well. Box #2 (Sterling Seagull) was at
the base of its tree instead of wedged between the trunks, but it was
covered with leaves and dry inside. Box #3 (Kid Kodiak) was found in
the right place, but a large limb from the big oak tree had broken
and fallen over the box and its branches make the box a little more
difficult to get to, but I was able to do so and left it where it
was. This one had been lost and replaced once before, so I was glad
it was there. Baby bears, in my experience, are generally high-
maintenance (but worth the trouble). Box #4 (Suzy Shoeless) was fine
and snuggled in its hollow branch. Box #5 (Rubus Trivialis) was not
so lucky. It was right where it was supposed to be, but the box was
full of water. Fortunately, the Zip-Loc bag was sealed tightly, so
no damage was done to the logbook. I don't know how water got into a
sealed Lock-n-Lock box, but it was full. Thanks to all you finders
for sealing the bag, I appreciate it. I thought Box #6 (Jogsfer
Jellybeans) was gone, as it certainly wasn't in its proper spot, but
I found it in a pile of wet leaves about 20 feet away, so it's good.
Box #7 was snuggled in and dry, although almost all the vines and
undergrowth had been removed from around it. Two of IRA Boxer's
boxes, The Ice Box and The Lunch Box are missing and, due to his
condition, will not be replaced. So, all seven boxes have survived
the first year (except that pesky Kid Kodiak that had to be replaced)
and are ready for another good year.

Boots Tex
