From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-04-14 16:16:43 UTC-05:00
I've been thinking it would be nice to do a planting initiative in the greater Longview area since we have so many new boxers over that way.  Doing an initiative there would also serve to tie Shreveport and that little oasis of boxers there in with a larger group until Louisiana fills out a little bit.  It would also bring Carthage and Marshall in a little closer to a box-populated area, and I know there are some waiting boxers over there.
 
The nice thing about doing an "initiative" instead of a gathering, is that folks can make a jaunt through there at their leisure and plant a box or two.  It also makes it easier to cover a wider area.  If you recall, last year I tried to get a gathering up for the area north of I-20, but several folks (myself included - that was when my husband had surgery) wound up having to back out.  Instead we declared a range of counties and a period of time and encourage boxers to focus on that area.  It was quite a broad area, but we got it done and it was because of that initiative that we actually filled in our section of the state on the LBNA map.  At that time I did an "event" stamp and sent it to anyone who planted a box in the area (although I apparently missed sending one to someone, because I still have one in my stuff - let me know if I promised to send you one and you never got it). 
 
So what does anyone think about that idea?

My old boss is actually from Harleton, so I've heard a little about where Harleton is!
 
As far as stamps, what Blue Butterfly said is by far the general concensus - plant what ya got because everyone has been there and appreciates a new carver's efforts.  And YOU will appreciate it when you go back to check your first boxes and see how much your carving has improved.  Thing is, no matter how long you've been carving, you can always get better in some way.  I've been getting quite a thrill out of my own carving lately because my lighted magnifying lamp has helped me take a step forward carving-wise.
