From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-06-20 20:25:29 UTC-07:00
That really, really is a bummer! I'm so sorry for you
- but I know what you mean!

The only stamp my husband has ever carved, and our
first box to plant, went missing also. It was in a
pretty secluded place, but it appeared that there had
been a big cleanup and my box apparently was a
casualty of it. Somehow, we just haven't had a taste
to replace it. I think I could bear other boxes going
missing, but that was "the first".

Let me encourage you to keep the faith - someone WILL
come and find your boxes - there WILL be visitors to
your area that care to find your gift. Just as I was
bummed to find our first box was missing (and, by the
way, it had never to my knowledge been found), I can
remember being ecstatic when I went to check a box,
fully expecting to find that the log was empty as
usual, and discovered not one or two, but three
visitors to it. I had quite a long wait, and several
checks, but it finally happened, and it was worth the
waiting!

I wish I lived a little closer to you because I would
go find your boxes just to give you that feeling!
There are boxers closer to you and I'll bet you won't
have to wait much longer before you find visitors. If
not, I'll be swinging down that way in a few months
and I'll gladly hunt them down and stamp them with
enthusiasm!

As to the bug thing, I'm not myself especially
susceptible to insects (must not be sweet enough), but
I was privvy to a discussion recently among some
scouters who claim that eating a sulfur tablet the
night before you go out will ward off biting insects.
I would imagine it wards off most anything once you
start sweating sulfur, but hey...if it works, just get
everyone else to take one too and you can stink in
unison!

Lucy

From: celticlions (celticlions@comcast.net) | Date: 2005-06-20 23:11:51 UTC-05:00
Len Nire Ben wrote:

<< How frustrating! The other two in the area are *amazingly enough* still
there but untouched. We have virgin boxes out there!!! Jefferson county,
Nederland and Port Neches. >>

Let me just echo Lucy here - have faith! Keep placing boxes and they will
come! It seemed like forever that we were the only boxers in the DFW area,
but then Kantexan & the Samuraistampers started up - then Catpawz - then
Viewfinders...there are actually boxes within driving distance which we
haven't had time to hunt down!

In the meantime, if you're just going crazy waiting, you might consider
Postal Letterboxing! Granted, there's no hike involved, and no challenge to
finding the boxes, but it's a great offshoot of the main
sport/activity/hobby.
We first started PLBing because for the first 6 months, there was only one
letterbox within an hour's drive of us and we were desperate to try to see
lots of other stamps and logbooks and get to know other boxers.
It became not only a valuable education in letterboxing skills such as
carving, but also a great grounding in letterboxing etiquette. And we had
the opportunity to get to know folks from all over the country and to see
their signature stamps.

Might be something fun for you to take part in until those other boxers
start springing up in your neighborhood!

Cheers!
Ruby Tuesday

From: Hawk (omkarmashanti@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-06-23 11:37:00 UTC-07:00
Thiamin (Vitamin B1) works well to ward off critters.
It's water-based, so your body flushes out the excess.
I take a bunch the night before boxing, and I don't
need repellent. The only problem is that the mosquitos
still swarm near you and drive you nuts as you wipe
sweat out of your eyes and try to pry open a container
with the other hand and balance your stamp on your
knee... :)

Hawk
