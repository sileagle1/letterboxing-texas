Long-retired boxes, found again...

From: (candj1969@yahoo.com) | Date: 2015-09-06 08:13:11 UTC-07:00

Silver Eagle, this is why we hesitate to retire our boxes:


Yesterday, I heard from Walksfar, who found a box of mine in Benjamin, Knox County, Texas.  This is remarkable, in that the box was planted in December, 2009 and was only found once, interestingly, by non-letterboxers, actually the subject of the box, who lived in Benjamin.  The box is called Meinzer's Coyote and is a tribute to Wyman Meinzer, a very well-known photographer, the official photographer of the State of Texas.  He and his wife discovered the box while doing a google search, and his name came up with the LbNA clues for this letterbox.  They went immediately to the cemetery and found the box and liked the idea of the game of letterboxing.  They even registered with their own trail name on the LbNA site.  After that, the box went missing and was never reported found again.  The next year, 2010, we were in the area, so stopped by and looked for the box, but couldn't find it, so we retired it.  Walksfar was searching for another box in the same cemetery but was unable to find a grave marker that was referenced in the clues, so was looking randomly at the base of trees and found Meinzer's Coyote.  This was yesterday, over five years after the box was retired.  My problem is that she didn't have the clues for the box, so we don't know if its current location is the same as indicated on the original clues.  

  Even more remarkable, Walksfar contacted me again today and reported finding another of my retired boxes, this one in Waco's Oakwood Cemetery, a governor box called "The Lawman".  This box had four consecutive "attempts" and was retired in 2013.  She reported that it was laying out in the open at the base of a tree with a hole in it, so she assumed that's where it belonged and put it back in the hole.  Unfortunately, that doesn't match the clues and since she thought it was in the right place, didn't record clues that could be used to locate the box.  I'm thinking that maybe I should place a note in the clues that the box may be in a hole in a tree?  Or should I just leave it retired?  Any thoughts on that?

   At any rate, thanks to Walksfar for letting me know that the boxes still exist and taking care of them for me.
