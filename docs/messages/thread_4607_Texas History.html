Here is a link to King Fisher's grave in Uvalde:

http://www.findagrave.com/cgi-bin/fg.cgi?page=gr&GRid=18144

Since you are a relative, you could probably get away with placing a letterbox inside the little metal fence over the grave. That would be cool. I'm a fifth generation Texan myself. In fact, when King Fisher was born in Collin County, my GGGrandfather was building the Bethlehem Baptist Church north of Farmersville (see my letterbox Brother Ben). Maybe he knew your GGGGrandfather, Job (or Jobe) Fisher. I guess we could speculate forever about that.

From: Carol McNally (carolmcnally@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2010-08-11 09:18:05 UTC-07:00
LOL...My husband is the McNally and the are from Michigan but it was really funny when we found out that Leander McNelly was known to chase King Fisher down a few times!  I'm a Kellogg, The King Fisher relation comes from my Dad's side, I think his grandmother was the daughter of King Fisher.  I don't have my family tree on this computer and it's been a while since i looked things up, but  i grew up with my ingraining in me, WE are TEXANS and King Fisher was your relative and no matter what you read he didn't kill "that" many people...LOL  I love that part!  The Pendencia is where he had his ranch, I've found an old Texas map that has the Pendencia on it and want to order it one day...I love old Texas maps!  We (the Family) are in possession of King Fisher's gun, the one that was on him when he was shot.  I've always wanted try to tie my family history into my trail name but never have been creative enough to come up with a name and a stamp so I'm still in trail name limbo...and we've been letter-boxing for 5 yrs...although not actively all that time.
  Would love to do a box in King Fisher's honor, i even have a picture of him and was trying to carve it at one time but never finished....and I'd really want it to be planted close to his grave site and we don't get through that part of the country often...one day i guess.

Thanks!

Carol
