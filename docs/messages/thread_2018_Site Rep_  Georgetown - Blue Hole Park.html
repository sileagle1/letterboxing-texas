Hi everyone!

Just thought I'd post a little note for anyone who may be passing
through Georgetown and hoping to box a little.

Back on July 22nd, Georgetown had a brief but ferocious storm that
left much of the city without electricity for over 12 hours. This
same storm damaged many of the very old trees in town...either
downing them completely, damaging them with high winds or with
lightning strikes.

Pickett Trail in Blue Hole Park is almost impassable due to the many
downed trees on the trail. We went this morning and found Boots
Tex's Three Legged Willie (AWESOME box, btw!) and had to actually
crawl THROUGH a huge downed tree on the path. Once at the rock
ledge described in the clue, we had to work our way AROUND (and off
the path) another downed tree to get to the actual ledge.
Anyhoo...Three Legged Willie is in great shape, especially
considering the damage to the area. I highly recommend this
box...it's really cool!

Unfortunately, this means that some of the boxes on the trail are
going to be difficult to reach. When we were climbing through the
first tree, we lost count of our steps, so just kept going until we
reached the correct area. If you're not familiar with the trail,
however, it might be difficult to find.

Also, immediately after the correct rock ledge there is ANOTHER tree
blocking the path. This one, too, can be passed, but it requires
literally CRAWLING through the tree! The kiddos could get through,
but I was totally stuck and had to have them yank me backwards out
of the tree! So...I was not able to check on the status of Batty
Bat.

I am going to email the city's Parks Dept today and let them know of
the status of the trail. Hopefully they will clear it off pretty
quickly.

Boxes that ARE still accessible include: Silver Eagle's Pocket
Wocket (in another area of the park), Baby Bear's Jiminy Cricket
(clear spot on trail) and Three-Legged Willie (with a couple of tree
obstacles). Also close by is Lone Star Quilter's Georgetown Quilt
Square (different area of the park).

Good luck! Batty B
