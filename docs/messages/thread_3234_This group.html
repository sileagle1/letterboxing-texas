From: Puddle Splasher (cjagger405@earthlink.net) | Date: 2007-12-24 18:02:47 UTC
I might suggest that you go to the "messages" site on the
letterboxingtexas group, and go back to the oldest messages and start
enjoying reading through some of them. It will give you a real sense of
the history of this group. This group is "owned and moderated" by a
couple of the original letterboxers in the state and started just
before the first annual statewide event in Texas about five years ago.
It's a very friendly site and the members strive to keep it that way.
Although Texas is a vast state, the annual state events have been well
attended over the years and some real friendships have developed (at
the Events as well as mini-meets and over the internet).
In fact, we've had visitors from Rhode Island (Wanda), Tennessee
(MoWizLiz), California (HummerLady?), Louisiana, Oklahoma, New York and
other states attend our events which makes them all the more fun! In
fact, when we've learned that a boxer was going to be visiting the
state, we've been known to throw together a mini-meet in their honor...
like when MoWizLiz and Dewberry visited the Dallas/Ft Worth area a few
months ago.

Whereas most of the "talk" on this list is letterboxing related, we
have been known to get off the subject a wee bit from time to time,
usually with some good spirited bantering. There was once a subject of
what "stuff" we each had stored in our closets (baby food jars etc)
that we just couldn't get rid of... that actually stemmed from a
discussion of letterboxing supplies taking over our houses, but soon
left the "letterboxing track" and took on a life of its own. It was a
lot of fun... plus revealing the junk you have shoved in your closets
is a good way to get to know each other better!

In other words, welcome to the list, KNSs, and enjoy the fun. It's a
nice group of people.
Puddle Splasher

From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-12-24 20:22:35 UTC
There is a lot of "regionalism" for lack of a better word in
letterboxing. In different sections of the country, people have
different approaches to hiding, clue writing, cooties, exchanges,
travelers and more. People are more or less hardcore about different
aspects, etc. For instance, here, we for the most part don't mess with
cooties or fleas, although plenty of gatherings in other areas are
crawling with them. It's one of the things that I think is interesting
about letterboxing in different states--you get inspired and intrigued
by different ways of doing things.

This extends a lot to which websites are used. I much prefer the
mapping abilities of AQ, but a lot of Texas letterboxers don't use AQ
at all. But, then Eli was here recently visiting from Atlanta, he
forgot to check letterboxing.org, because everyone in his area uses AQ
and people often don't list their clues on letterboxing.org any more.
It didn't occur to him that there would be clues only listed there.

In general, I have a love-hate relationship with yahoo groups. But,
then, I often have that same relationship with the boards at AQ.

dewberry
