From: (vicki@bellmotel.com) | Date: 2008-09-22 09:01:45 UTC-05:00
I have to share the poem that I recently received to accompany my birthday gift from my mom.  Mom is 71, and thought Letterboxing was a great idea until she actually tried it with me in June in Little Rock.  We came home with very few stamps (most were missing), but at least 80 chigger bites apiece from the UALR campus, and a scare from having arrived at McArthur Park right after a stabbing had taken place between two homeless drug addicts.  Neither of us had the heart to continue.  However, for my birthday, she sent me  something called a "Soil Scoop" -- a foot-long, skinny, scoop-shaped trowel, and this wonderful poem --
 
This strange tool will do you well
When searching the shrubs or in a well
For Letterboxes hidden out of sight
Close to the ground buried tight
Against a rock or next to a pool.
It keeps you safe from bugs and snakes,
Helps you seek and squeal with delight,
As you find a treasured Letterbox!
 
I bet she'll be willing to try Letterboxing with me again!
-- Bell Motel
