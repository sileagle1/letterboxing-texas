From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-07-28 11:48:24 UTC-05:00
I realize I'm a little late on the topic, but I've been out of pocket for a few days and just read up here, and I have to say something.
 
I guess we don't know enough about either of the situations described to really speak to them specifically - we don't know who did it (maybe it was the owner, maybe not - maybe a total stranger, maybe not) nor why they might have done it.
 
Many placers put a lot of time and effort into their boxes - spending time at the location and learning about it, choosing the image, writing the clues, choosing the exact hiding spot, etc.  I myself take forever to plant a box because between all of these particulars, plus my schedule is such that getting all that done AND getting back to a location is sometimes nearly impossible for quite some time, AND I don't want to plant a box I can't reasonably do maintenance on, etc...
 
SO - if someone were to come along and jack my box, even in fun, I would be greatly perturbed.  For one thing - how do I know the perpetrator took the time and effort to choose a new hiding place for my box like I would choose for it - based on low likelihood of discovery, avoidance of flood areas, prevention of social trails, view, any of a number of other things?  And why would I think anything besides the perpetrator liked my hiding place and so moved my box?  And how do I know I want my box associated with theirs?  There are any of a number of other reasons I might not care for having my box appropriated by someone else.
 
HOWEVER, anyone who knows me knows I'm always game for a little fun.  My comments above aren't meant to say that a joke that includes the placer of the hijacked box in the execution wouldn't be great fun.  And maybe that's what happened in both of these cases - I don't know.  I probably wouldn't participate in that particular joke on just any old day, but I can see that it would be funny as a joke on the finder, but not on the placer. 
 
In my opinion, and anyone is welcome to disagree - it is NEVER a good idea to move a box unless it is in immediate danger of being destroyed or otherwise harmed. 
 
BarefootLucy
"It's not about footwear, it's about philosophy"

From: Puddle Splasher (cjagger405@earthlink.net) | Date: 2008-07-29 17:11:04 UTC
I am often inspired to hide a letterbox by a certain location. I
spent quite some time and a couple of trips trying to find a safe
place to hide "The Mick" letterbox, so that it would be close to
Mickey Mantle's tomb. It wasn't easy! I even moved it once after I
originally hid it because I was afraid the first spot might be
vunerable. I would be plenty irritated if someone decided they should
take it upon themselves to move it for me. To me, it just sounds like
muggling. I have another box ready to be planted soon. I have spent
several months researching the spot which relates to the box, and an
afternoon walking around the area observing it so see if it would be
safe. I usually do put quite a bit of thought into the placement of
my boxes so that it will provide the finders with a positive
experience.

I think I would refer to the AQ Code of Conduct: "Do not plant
letterboxes near other people's existing letterboxes without
permission. Also, do not use someone else's letterbox as a starting
point in your clue for your own box without permission. This is
called poaching and while we may not always know if you're breaking
the other rules, we always learn when you break this one. Not only
might the original letterbox planter not appreciate your efforts, but
the added traffic with people getting your letterbox might cause
unacceptable environmental damage or risk exposing the location of
the original letterbox. Instead, plant your box on a different trail
or at least one quarter-mile away from the original letterbox's
location."

Okay... that's not exactly the same thing, but close! I think we all
try to respect that rule and also realize it means not to mess with
other people's boxes, except when we are doing a little first aid or
have their permission. It's probably a good time to remind people
that unless a box is in emanate danger, it's never okay to remove a
box that doesn't belong to you. It's just flies in the face of
letterboxing tradition.

I have found some "piggyback" boxes in other regions of the country
and that can be fun. That is when the planter puts clues in one of
HIS OWN boxes which lead you to another box. You can also do this
with someone else's box ONLY if you have their permission first.

I'm really not an old grouch, but I think it's important that we not
stray too far from the code of conduct introduced by the original
letterboxers so that we don't have chaos.

Puddle Splasher

I realize I'm a little late on the topic, but I've been out of pocket for a few days and just read up here, and I have to say something.
 
I guess we don't know enough about either of the situations described to really speak to them specifically - we don't know who did it (maybe it was the owner, maybe not - maybe a total stranger, maybe not) nor why they might have done it.
 
Many placers put a lot of time and effort into their boxes - spending time at the location and learning about it, choosing the image, writing the clues, choosing the exact hiding spot, etc.  I myself take forever to plant a box because between all of these particulars, plus my schedule is such that getting all that done AND getting back to a location is sometimes nearly impossible for quite some time, AND I don't want to plant a box I can't reasonably do maintenance on, etc...
 
SO - if someone were to come along and jack my box, even in fun, I would be greatly perturbed.  For one thing - how do I know the perpetrator took the time and effort to choose a new hiding place for my box like I would choose for it - based on low likelihood of discovery, avoidance of flood areas, prevention of social trails, view, any of a number of other things?  And why would I think anything besides the perpetrator liked my hiding place and so moved my box?  And how do I know I want my box associated with theirs?  There are any of a number of other reasons I might not care for having my box appropriated by someone else.
 
HOWEVER, anyone who knows me knows I'm always game for a little fun.  My comments above aren't meant to say that a joke that includes the placer of the hijacked box in the execution wouldn't be great fun.  And maybe that's what happened in both of these cases - I don't know.  I probably wouldn't participate in that particular joke on just any old day, but I can see that it would be funny as a joke on the finder, but not on the placer. 
 
In my opinion, and anyone is welcome to disagree - it is NEVER a good idea to move a box unless it is in immediate danger of being destroyed or otherwise harmed.
