Galveston boxes

From: Boots Tex (redbarn@eastex.net) | Date: 2010-08-03 14:18:22 UTC
Sparked by a thread on the AQ board, I did some research on the letterboxes listed for Galveston. Only 10 boxes are listed on AQ for Galveston and it appears that most of them are missing. There are 19 listed on LbNA, but 4 are by Hawk, 2 by Birds of a Feather and 1 by Quetzel. I believe that none of them are still letterboxing. One box by Birds of a Feather called "Felix" was reported found about a week ago, but according to a note posted in January, the box is "a mess", with water-logged logbook and geocache trash in the box. I guess it's a miracle that any letterboxes survived Ike at all, but it's a shame that there are so many still listed as available when, in fact, they are probably gone. If someone were to make a real effort to find and report on all the Galveston boxes, could the missing boxes be archived even though the placers are not available? I just think the island needs to be cleaned up so that potential finders wouldn't get so frustrated trying to find boxes that aren't there.

Boots Tex

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2010-08-03 12:07:37 UTC-05:00

It is actually possible to get boxes archived, but not because the boxer is inactive. This is because boxes CAN last long beyond the boxers activity.

Heres how we approach it on LBNA, and I think its pretty much the same approach on AQ. Both sites are amenable to removing listings if appropriate.

On LBNA, we assume a box is good unless we hear otherwise from a finder. We ask the potential finder if they have already contacted the placer and ask them when they did it and what the outcome was (i.e. a bounce message or nothing at all, or ???). We look at the listing and how long it has been since it has been found and how long since the owner has logged on. We used to try to contact the placer as well, but unless I happen to know them, or they have logged on recently, Ive quit even trying and I think the other webmasters have as well, because I usually have no luck either.

If the placer has been inactive for over a year, and depending on the potential finders report, we either archive the box (they say We found it but it was a nasty mess and we took it with us because it was trash at this point) or if they have expressed interest in adopting, we sign it over to them. Let me just say we dont go archiving boxes just because someone writes and says its missing. We ALL know how that can go! BUT if someone were to write and say I followed the directions and the whole section of railroad track where the box was hidden has been removed, or something very specific like that, well archive the box.

Sometimes it comes to yukky judgment that is difficult from a distance, but it isnt like the box is gone forever, its just in the basement, so if we make a mistake it can be reactivated.

I know that Ryan/AQ handles things very similarly, but the key with either site is to be specific in what you have done to contact the placer (dont dump it on us to do the legwork because were volunteers too), what the result was, and what the issue was. Also, express what you found, and how you are willing to help (i.e. adopt).

Hope that helps!

> > someone were to write and say I followed the directions and the whole

> >
> >

I think as far as the October event is concerned, we'll confine our search to Galveston Island. That's probably all we'll have time for, but if you have more time, the other boxes along the coast are probably in the same circumstance as the Galveston boxes and could use some help. I'm making a list of the Galveston boxes and collecting comments that different folks have sent regarding their status. If anyone happens to go letterboxing in Galveston before the event, let me know what you find. We may get down there for a day trip soon, but won't be looking for lost boxes (we'll save that for October), but if I get a chance, I'll keep an eye out for likely hiding places. I have one box ready to place, so if I find a good spot, I might go ahead and place it. I bought a new kind of box and I'd like to try it out. It's a little expensive for a typical letterbox, but Galveston may call for extreme measures.
