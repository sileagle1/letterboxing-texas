Silver Eagle Travel Log

From: Silver Eagle (sileagle@alltel.net) | Date: 2005-01-01 19:26:32 UTC

I just got back late last night from our
South Texas trip, so I thought I would give
everyone a trip report. We had to cut
the trip to 4 days so I was not able to
go to Corpus Christi area to replace my
2 missing boxes. However, I did manage to
place 5 new ones and find several along
the way. We left on Tuesday, 12/28, and
our first stop was victoria, where
I waded through the mud to find Monkey Toes'
Box Kite box. I was surprised to see I
was only the second one to find it. We
then went to Riverside Park and I found
both boxes there and realized I had just
missed Birds Of A Feather by 1 day!
Next, it was on to Goliad SP where I
checked on my box (damp but ok) and found
the other ones there alive and well.
After eating a picnic lunch on a beautiful
day, we drove on to Skidmore where I found
the Beehive HH Hostel. There were 3 HH
that had been held hostage for over 6 months,
so I took all of them and left one. I plan
to release them in the Houston HH Hostel
soon. We finally got to a birding spot
south of Falfurrias where we spotted many
birds only found in South Texas, and I
placed my Falfurrias Finch box. We spent
the night in Edinburg and got up early to
visit the Scenic Wetlands Birding Center.
We saw more birds and I was first finder
of WowMom's box. Next it was on to the
World Birding Center at Bentsen-Rio Grande
State Park. What a great park! We rented
a 4-seat bike and rode all the trails, seeing
many new birds, finding WowMom's box and
placing my Green Jay box. Even the kids
had fun at this place. We headed east to
the Santa Anna NWR to look around. No
boxes there, but a nice place. Then it
was on to the Valley Nature Center in
Welasco where we found Birds Of A Feather's
box and enjoyed the peaceful oasis in that
town. That night we stayed on Padre Island,
which was what the kids were waiting for.
I got up early the next morning to do more
letterboxing in Brownsville and promised
to meet up with the family at the beach
around noon. I drove to Gladys Porter
zoo and tried to find WowMom's box but
did not find it, even though I'm pretty
sure I was at the right place. Then it
was on to the Sabal Palm Audubon Center &
Sanctuary, where I placed my Sabal Palm box and
enjoyed quiet trails with many birds. Finally,
I drove the long Boca Chica road to look for
the only old box supposedly still alive in
the Brownsville area (Clarksville box).
Once again I came up empty, even though I
found the green sign buried under mud and
grass. I think the box was swept away.
On the way back to Padre I stopped at
Port Isabel Ligthouse and found Birds Of
A Feather's box there. There were several
visitors to the box, including many from
out of state. The last day, as we headed
home, we stopped at Laguna Atascosa NWR
for more birding and I placed my Aplomado Falcon
box there. Then we stopped in Kleberg Park
in Kingsville where I placed my last box, Kingsville
Kiskadee before heading to the King Ranch Visitor
Center. After that it was just a long
drive home, happy but tired.
Thanks to all that hid boxes and I hope
you get a chance to find mine soon!

Silver Eagle
