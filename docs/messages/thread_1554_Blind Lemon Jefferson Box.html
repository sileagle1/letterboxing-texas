I also love cemetery boxes--in Texas they are too often the only
history that remains. I think the most important thing is for the
finders to be respectful--of any mourners, of the sacredness of the
place, and of the graves themselves. The periphery is usually the
best way to go, although I do have one box hidden in the middle of a
cemetery, just not on a grave.

This box in particular was really high on my to-do list, from the
moment T-Dog planted it. We just have to get up that way--for the TX
boxcar event if not before. When I was a kid, I loved to listen to
the Blues records my dad had bought cheaply at the PX. I don't listen
so much now (can anyone say Radio Disney, blech!), but it's a fond memory.

I would encourage those of you who may not have planted a box to
consider doing so. It's a great way to
1. Improve your carving skills.
2. Better understand what goes into a box (makes you a better finder).
3. Show off favorite spots in your community.
4. Return the favor for those who have planted before you.

Don't worry if your carving skills are still amateur. You get better
quickly and practice is the only way to improve.

So, calling all Houston boxers--you should take pity on TeamKing &
hide some boxes. They're desperate for some more Houston-area boxes
(and I won't complain either).

Cheers! Here's to a great 2006,

Dewberry
