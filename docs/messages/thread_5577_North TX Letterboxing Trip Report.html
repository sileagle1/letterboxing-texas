North TX Letterboxing Trip Report

From: (sileagle1@yahoo.com) | Date: 2016-12-02 21:28:22 UTC-08:00

I recently returned from a four day letterboxing trip to North TX where I went to some parks I have never been to before and some that brought back fond memories.  My first stop was Fairfield Lake SP, site of the recent Event hosted by Open Space.  There are a lot of great boxes there and I added one for future visitors.  Hopefully they will last longer than my first box placed there in 2004, which was confiscated by the rangers.  In December of that year, Baby Bear and I visited this park during our second “TX letterbox seeding” trip and each placed a box.  However, unlike our first trip where we only placed boxes hoping others would get hooked, there was a box to be found, too.  Earlier that year someone named Barefoot Lucy, who eventually became a staunch letterboxer in East TX,  had placed her first box in that park and was waiting rather impatiently for somebody to find it, so that was the main reason for going to that park.  When we found the box we were surprised to see somebody named Gryzzled Gryphon had already beat us to it.  Now back then there was no “contact the placer” or status indicator on clues, so the only way to know if somebody found your box was if they emailed you, which we planned to do when we got home.  Of course GG never emailed anyone, which is why he remained an enigma for so long.  So when I got home I was surprised to see I had an email from Barefoot Lucy before I had a chance to even tell her she had 3 finders of her box.  It turns out she happened to visit the park the same day to check on her box and saw that we had just stamped in, so she made her LSS drive all through the park looking for us!  She was so excited that she added a sweet note at the top of her clues that you can read on her archived box called Oh Deer:  http://www.letterboxing.org/BoxView.php?boxnum=8335


Next I found a few boxes in cemeteries on my way to Purtis Creek SP, a park I had never been to and did not have any boxes until now.  Then it was on to Lake Tawakoni SP to place another box and find a treasured Governor box by Boots Tex, which was the main reason I went here.  I was not disappointed.  The next day I found and placed a few boxes on my way toward Dallas, including one for Stevie Ray Vaughan where he is buried.  Way back in 2003 I had placed a box dedicated to him near his statue in Austin, but since it eventually went missing I was very happy to be able to place another one here.  Then it was on to Cedar Ridge Preserve to find some old boxes from longtime letterboxing sisters Puddle Splasher and Viewfinder.  I was very happy to find both boxes, one from 2006 and the other from 2008, and I left one for them to find just like in the old days.

I ended the day at Cedar Hill SP where I planned to find several boxes placed there but was disappointed to find out most of the trails where the boxes are located were closed due to flooding back in 2015, so I was only able to find 1 box: Life Is Good Near Dallas by Mosaic Butterfly.  I did find some trails that were open so I placed a box hoping others will repopulate this nice park.  I had not been here since placing one of my first Dallas boxes, Texas Star, back in 2003, and I did not recognize it.  That trip was memorable for me because I tried to find the first letterboxes placed in TX:  the Duck Creek boxes placed in 1998.  Unfortunately they were already missing.


The last day I boxed in several places as I made my way home and finally made it to Berry Springs Park in Georgetown.  Once again the main draw was boxes by Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter and I found both.  I am embarrassed to admit that I have had these boxes on my radar since they were placed way back in 2006 and only now got them.  Oh well, better late than never.  I was impressed with the Georgetown area and have plans to place more boxes there in the near future.  Finally, I placed a couple more in Austin, one of them in a new (for me) place called Balcones Canyonlands Preserve which I think connects with St. Edwards Park.


Though it wasn’t as much fun as my old trips with Baby Bear, it was still great.  Thanks to everyone who placed boxes that tempted me into doing it!
