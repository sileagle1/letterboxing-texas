keep on not being sure wether i should send it or not, but decided to

so do you ever wonder what letterboxers do in real life? i know i do -
i wonder what do these people do when they are not letterboxing.

Anyway.. I am origionally from England and been living in Texas since
september of 2002.
I work with an organization called Youth With A Mission (YWAM), and i
work in the MercyWorks department, Which focuses on long term and short
term disaster relief and medical trips, and we are also working on
expanding into microfinace too.
Anyway, everyone involved in YWAM on waht ever level is responsible for
raising thier own financial support. There are no paid workers in YWAM.

And at the moment it is difficult to obtain visas etc to be here. I
absloutley love Texas and think of this as my home, and would never
want to leave.

I am currently in the process of having to renew my visa, and i am
needing to increase my monthly support by approximately $500 a month,
this feels like a huge ammount when you have no idea where in the world
i could even increase my support by $1 from.
Anyway, i am sending this out because i need prayer support and
encouragemnt - and i thought there may even be somone out there who
would maybe be able to even help a little!... but please pray that God
will lead me ot open doors to be able to fundraise from - i don;t get a
whole lot of support from people back in England.

If you would like more information on what I do or have any questions
e-mail me at Purplenic1979@gmail.com (just cos i'd rather this not get
too personal in a group setting, and don;t want people who aren;t
intersted to have to recieve more e-mails )

thanks for praying - i appreciate it
PurpleNic

It's funny that you would bring up wondering what others do when they aren't letterboxing.  I have this picture of certain lb'ers that I don't know personally living this awesome life (somehow immune to rising gas prices) just driving around in an RV from state park to state park planting boxes as they go!!  LOL!!

I think the best thing about finding letterboxing is that it has given our whole family a ready-made treasure hunt that Daddy and I don't have to plan!!  We have gotten to go on hikes in places we'd never heard of prior to this and spent a lot more outside time than I think we would have otherwise.

From: Christine Velez (christineauger@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-06-10 22:48:35 UTC-05:00

I'm also a homeschooling mama - of 3.  One of 'em was just born in April, which is why I've been laying low lately.  I'm a violinist and violin teacher as well, and have played (professionally) all over the place, including Carnegie Hall.  We moved to TX two years ago, and letterboxing has proven to be an excellent way for me to get the lay of the land.  Through letterboxing I've also gained an appreciation for Texas that I definitely did not have when we moved here.   I love to travel, camp, cook, and build things.  I recently entered the world of raising backyard chickens.  My husband is Puerto Rican and as a result we are attempting to raise the kids in a bilingual house... but I'm getting sick of saying "espanol, s'il vous plait!". ;)

I have a 3-year-old, a 2-year-old, and an infant... that fact combined with the recent talk of snakes and poison ivy is making me think that we'll get back into letterboxing full-swing in the fall.  Unless of course there happens to be a box out there hidden near a nice cool floating-river...

And I tend to type too much once I get started.

Chrispy (mama to Mono, Nutcracker, and Twinkle)
