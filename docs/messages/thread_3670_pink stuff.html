Just so you know there are other options: I use PZ Kut just because
I happen to like it better than the pink stuff (not that there's
anything wrong with the pink stuff!). I get it from Stampeaz.com,
which can be accessed on the Atlas Quest websit under "merchandise).
You can even get a sampler kit that contains the pink stuff,
MasterCarve (which is soft, but holds up well) and the two colors of
PZ Kut (the orange is harder than the white). Also, I carve my
stamps with an Xacto knife, which is also a personal preference.

However, if you're anywhere near Richardson (north of Dallas on 75) the Asel store there has the smaller 4 x 6" sheets. Obviously, you don't have to pay shipping, but then the gas price may equal out the cost of shipping if you order several sheets at once. Finding them at Hobby Lobby or Michaels is just hit and miss. From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-07-26 01:50:50 UTC
I love the PZ cut. I use the acetate sheet transfer method and I love
the stiffness of the PZ cut--I never could get the hang of carving
mastercarve, and the pink stuff is somewhere in between.

Webfoot is fabulous. I ordered Tuesday. Today, I have my
supply....quick and simple. And since you can order big sheets, you
can get lots of stamps out of one sheet.

BTW, I'm a gouge carver. I admire those who carve with exactos, but I
love my gouges.

From: SandiBox (sandibox@msn.com) | Date: 2008-07-26 04:45:21 UTC
PZ Cut Grade B White for me. I primarily use the Speedball gouges -
For the really intricate areas, I made a #1/2 by squeezing the #1
into a narrower groove. I use a #1 for regular cuts, and a #5 to
clear large areas.

For my logbooks, it depends on how much time I'm able to invest. I
use the codex binding instructions from Atlas Quest, and love the
professional looking results. But when I need a quick logbook that
still looks great, nothing beats my Bind-It-All. And if you're trying
to justify the expense, you can also make a personalized photo
calendar or family recipe book to give as gifts. :)

And now I'm trying to talk my mom into giving me her Crikut die-cut
machine. I LOVE crafty gadgets!

happy to discover the one in SugarLand. Yum-yum.

Now back to your regularly scheduled programming...

I am a big fan of the work of Wyman Meinzer, who lives in Benjamin and is the official photographer for the state of Texas. In doing my usual research for our trip out to that part of the state last December, I discovered that nothing had ever happened in Knox County, Texas. Most of the county is taken up with large ranches and populated with javelina, wild turkey, coyotes and good upstanding citizens, but that's okay, because that's my kind of country. To get on with the story, I had a book of photographs by Mr. Meinzer which included a very nice picture of a howling coyote (not the letterboxing kind, mind you), so I made a box using his coyote as my stamp and I called it "Meinzer's Coyote". It had never been found until today when I got a very nice notice from Mrs. Wyman Meinzer as follows:
> > >
> > > Dear Boots Tex,
> > > Wyman and I found the box with the stamp of a depiction of his, coyote, at the cemetery tonight. It was loads of fun. Wyman and I had a great time and we thank you for your kind words about Wyman's work. This was our first box to find, and will now search for others and even place some of our own.
> > >
> > > How cool is that? Re: [letterboxingtexas] pink stuff

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2009-10-09 21:36:27 UTC-05:00

Nooooo...Pink stuff isn't bad. I like pink stuff myself, I just like Stampeaz better. And, for that matter, Master Carve has its place too. I switch around sometimes myself, but I always come back to Stampeaz.

I think carving media is a whole lot like logbooks, and containers, and websites, gouges vs knives, markers vs ink pads, long hikes vs drive-bys, and all the other little preferences we have.

Everything has its advantages and disadvantages and a whole lot has to do with what you like and what works for you. I think the only medium that is universally disliked (and I say universally in a relative sort of way because you do run across the occasional letterboxer who loves it) is White Stuff (not Master Carve, but the white or buff block made by Speedball), and that because it crumbles so badly and doesn't weather well.

The key to any of this is YOU - what works for YOU. If you really like the stamps you turn out using pink stuff, then there is not a thing in the world wrong with it. Perhaps your carving style doesn't work as well on other media, but it works great on pink. Should you change your carving style so you can use "what everybody else uses", or stick with pink stuff and do what works for you?

My .02,
