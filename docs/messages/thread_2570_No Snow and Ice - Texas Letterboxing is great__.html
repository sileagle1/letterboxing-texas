No Snow and Ice - Texas Letterboxing is great!!

From: Baby Bear (babybearlb2002@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-02-09 21:28:20 UTC
I just got back from skiing trip to Colorado, and took some clues
along with me. 1st stop, Coors brewery for tour, tasting, and to
find Silver Eagles box. First, it was 15 degrees outside, and I was
not dressed from the plane ride for this. I followed the clues,
found the bridge it would be under, and bent down to find a huge
chunk of ice. No way I was getting that box. Due to the snow, 0
degree temp., and ice in other locations, I gave up on finding boxing
in the mountains. So, on the last days as we went to far side of
mountains, I searched for boxes in Glenwood Springs, after placing
box #247 in Glenwood Canyon (GREAT PLACE!). One was easy, as it was
right down the street from hotel and above ground. The other ended
up taking me 2 hours, across snow, ice, more ice, more ice, fell
down, more ice, but luckily the box was not frozen in the ice, just
the rocks that surrounded it. I wrestled it out, and was able to log
the box! On the last day as we headed to Denver we stopped at Red
Rocks park, and I decided to check on my old box there. NOPE....
snow plows had buried the spot under 5 feet of snow, which had turned
into an ice boulder. Isn't it nice not to have to deal with this in
Texas (at least most of the time). Not sure how you make an Ice-
proof box.

Baby (Polar) Bear

I know what you mean, Baby Bear.  A year ago my husband and I were in CO for some snowmobile adventuring, and tried to find a letterbox right next to the highway on Fremont Pass during a really heavy snowfall, icy slick roads, freezing temps, but an absolutely beautiful day (for a heavy snowfall day!).  Found the clues exactly, marked the precise spot visually -- but the short distance off the road - about 15 ' -  to the letterbox would have required me to attempt to cross about 6' deep snowdrifts and trek through what was probably 4-5' of deep powder to reach it.  You know, I sat there in the jeep staring at the spot and really, really considering whether or not I could do it, but finally settled for taking photographs of the place and hoping to make a return some summer day.  I was also thinking, if I'd been with PuddleSplasher instead of my reasonable husband, we'd have probably tried it, gotten ourselves buried in the snow up to our necks, frozen ourselves with completely wet clothes -- and got the letterbox!  But my husband does have good sense, so no attempt was made that day.  Besides, I thought I'd probably need an ax to open the box once I got it anyway, and I didn't have an ax handy.  Too bad.  About that time a snowplow came along and threw even more snow onto those tall roadside drifts, so I knew it wouldn't have been a good idea to try it anyway.  I'd have been buried until the spring thaw, most likely.  I guess sometimes you do have to use a little restraint in certain situations, or at least that's what my husband always tells me.
