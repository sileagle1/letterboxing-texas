Not that a new letterboxer would know, but the very first thing listed
under Code of Conduct/Respect, on AQ is "Do not publish clues or stamp
images that are not your own unless you have the owner's permission."

to see my stamp image published on the internet for the very reason you
stated: it ruins the surprise.

From: Vicki Stecher (stecher@runbox.com) | Date: 2007-10-10 07:35:49 UTC-05:00
I wouldn't care at all if it enticed folks to get started. It isn't like the posted the entire thing to the world. It is to a select group of folks with children who might want to start this hobby. The thing is, most folks with children want to know what they are getting into before they drag their kids into it. And the idea of letterboxing, to a beginner, is quite foreign. So showing them exactly what it is and what it consists of is like tasting the party cake before you order it.
 
I would be honored. Think of all the families who may start letterboxing because your stamp enticed them! Who cares if it "ruins" the suprise for the select group of people? Those kids will be just as excited to find the stamp whether or not they know what it looks like. For kids, it is the finding that is important. It is the adults who are all about the stamp.
 
Just my opinion.
Vicki

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-10-10 10:13:56 UTC-05:00
I would care. 
 
And it's not that I would mind so much, but that they are setting a bad precedent right off the bat for the kids they are trying to introduce to the hobby.
 
Now I love kids - don't get me wrong.  Heck, I have one that hangs around my house all the time and I spend hours a week with bunches of other ones.  BUT - there are quite a few kid groups (homeschoolers, scout groups, 4-H, whatever) that are approaching letterboxing.  I think that's fine, except that SOME of their leaders are not helping them understand that on the other side of that box is a planter, someone who went to a lot of trouble to put it out there, and other finders who want to find the box in good order without having the hunt spoiled by bad rehiding or missing boxes.  They approach it as they would an afternoon at the movies, or a day of roller skating, or a night at the bowling alley.  Letterboxing, in my opinion, is very much an interactive game and they miss that point. 
 
Posting a picture on a website without permission from the owner of the box is a hint that the leaders of the group aren't getting and conveying the whole picture.

From: Amy Townsend (aymztx@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-10-10 10:27:50 UTC-07:00

I agree.  If they wanted to present the hobby to the THEA group, they should have created a stamp and logbook of their own and posted the images from that.  I think that would have been the safest and most respectful option. 

I present letterboxing, geocaching and terracaching on my blog, but I did not post pictures of stamps or logbooks.  I try to take a scenic view picture or two around the cache area instead.  I provided links to the existing websites which give plenty of information for someone to get started.  I also put links to the events that I plan to attend.

Any pictures that show geocaches or letterboxes I've found, that show the actual container or the containers location(NEVER the contents), are not identified.  Since I participate in all three it would be difficult for anyone to know which box was pictured.

aymztx

From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-10-10 18:32:40 UTC
The only time I've seen it be okay is when the poster gets the
permission of the hiders whose stamps he's showing. Cyclonic has an
online logbook like that, where only those who have given him
permission have their stamps shown. Otherwise it's a definite
violation of letterboxing etiquette.

I really wish that folks would read a little bit more about things.
Although it's true that this is a hobby with "no rules," there are
social conventions and generally accepted practices. Most of these
things are common sense, but that doesn't help when people don't have
sense.

I love it when the stamp is a fun twist or has a surprise to it. I
don't really want to see before I open that box...

dewberry
