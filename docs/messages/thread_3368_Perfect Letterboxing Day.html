Perfect Letterboxing Day

From: Silver Eagle (sileagle@alltel.net) | Date: 2008-02-28 01:39:32 UTC
Today I had what I consider to be a perfect letterboxing day, or as
close to it as I can get. I finally made it to Mercer Arboretum to
get all the new boxes there and they were great, especially the
Murder series by The Texians. If you haven't found these boxes yet I
highly recommend them. So, how do I define a perfect letterboxing
day?

First, the weather has to be great. Sunny, cool enough to keep the
bugs away and to keep me from sweating while hiking, low wind to not
scatter clue sheets, and dry enough to sit on the ground to stamp
without getting wet. Today was all of these.

Second, the location has to be beautiful with nice trails. For those
of you that have been to Mercer, you know it meets this criteria.

Third, the location has to be uncrowded. I only saw about 5 other
people on the trails all day!

Fourth, the letterboxes have to be fun, fairly easy to find, and
memorable. The boxes today were just awesome!

Thanks to The Texians and IRA Boxer for a great day!

Silver Eagle

P.S. It was really strange finding boxes hidden by a dead letterboxer
(IRA Boxer) and then notifying him of my finds. Not sure if I will
get a response, or if I want to!
