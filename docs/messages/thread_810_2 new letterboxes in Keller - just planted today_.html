2 new letterboxes in Keller - just planted today!

From: tx2chic (blhjrh@aol.com) | Date: 2005-04-23 02:09:03 UTC

I finally found a good place to plant the Old Native Letterbox, as I
hope you will agree if you find it in Bear Creek Park in Keller.
And, while I was scouting its hiding place, I found another location
just crying for a letterbox, so I carved a cool (I think) stamp and
planted a letterbox in oldtown Keller, just off Hwy 377 (Keller Main
Street): the Whistle Stop Spur. I hope you enjoy finding them as much
as I enjoyed carving and planting them (there is a FF item in the
Whistle Stop Spur). And while you're in Keller, you might as well walk
the Big Bear Creek Greenbelt trail just less than a mile (OK, one-way)
and find the other 4 letterboxes along that trail, especially now that
wildflowers are blooming. (Too late to beat the GG to find these other
4,though) -- Viewfinder (tx2chic)

Keller is about 10 miles north of Ft Worth, easy off I 35 - that's about a 5 1/2 hour drive for you (that is, AFTER you get out of Houston!).  But Ft. Worth is such a nice place to visit!

Re: [letterboxingtexas] 2 new letterboxes in Keller - just planted today!

From: (blhjrh@aol.com) | Date: 2005-04-23 09:05:37 UTC-04:00
And about the wildflowers - about two weeks ago they were still sparse near Johnson City west of Austin, but we saw lots of bluebonnets up around Marble Falls and Llano, and last week we saw plenty of bluebonnets along Hwy 287 around Decatur (northwest of Ft Worth).  They've been blooming around the DFW area along I 20 south of Dallas and both north and south of the DFW airport (Grapevine, Irving, Euless) for several weeks.  Now the Indian paintbrush and primrose are THICK through all those places (and out here in Keller, too).
