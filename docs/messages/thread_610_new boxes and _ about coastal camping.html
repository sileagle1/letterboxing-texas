From: celticlions (celticlions@comcast.net) | Date: 2005-02-13 17:29:35 UTC-06:00
I'm so tickled that you guys are planning to come to the gathering!!!

Haven't stayed at Goose island, but we did drive around and through it last
spring. There appeared to be wooded sites and sites in concrete-type
structures on the water edge. BabyBear might be a good resource for you.
It was so rainy when we were there, I don't have a good feel for the park,
but I do recall a lot of bugs. The trees were interesting - so windswept by
ocean storms that they were often bent over almost double, and very tangled.

We CelticLions are pretty happy with any camping situation so we may not be
the most discriminating of critics!
(There are 3 nearby boxes, BTW!)
See you in March!
Ruby T.

From: Silver Eagle (sileagle@alltel.net) | Date: 2005-02-14 02:57:12 UTC

You might try asking BOXDN or one of the other Louisiana
boxers if they have room at their campsites, since I
think they booked several.
Another option would be to try staying at Stephen F. Austin
SP, since it is closer than the parks you mentioned and
I don't think it fills up that often. It is north of
Brazos Bend SP, so you could stay there the night before,
come to the Event, then go back for another night in
the direction of home. Another advantage is there are
a few boxes in the park. Also, you could explore the
area around Washington-On-The Brazos, especially if the
wildflowers are blooming then.
If you do decide to stay at Goose Island SP, be sure to
allow enough time to go to Aransas NWR to see the Whooping
Cranes and find several boxes there. Glad to hear you
are planning to come!

Silver Eagle
