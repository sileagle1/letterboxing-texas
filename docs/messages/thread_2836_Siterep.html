From: tigger32167 (tigger32167@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-06-17 01:27:56 UTC
hey there to all...i just got back from a wonderful trip to
arizona. thought i would share some of the highlights with ya'll.

Day 1
i left here (cherokee county) at 5:18 am on monday, may 28th and my
first stop was the dallas area to take a break at my cousin's, wag
time's, house. after stretching our legs we were back on the road.
i then stopped in weatherford and found the badge of friendship
lb at the greenwood cemetery. this was a really neat cemetery where
oliver loving is buried. my next stop was caddo to find the tejas
caddo cemetery lb. this box had a really great stamp and a great
location. from here i made my way to stanton to pick up the a
few old soreheads lb at the st joseph's catholic cemetery. i
loved the stamp, and the cemetery was very interesting. from
stanton i traveled to midland and got a little confused looking for
the homerun that never was lb, but finally got my act together and
located it. i then looked for the 5 guardian angels lb..found the
crosses, but unable to locate the memory box. i then attempted the
nodding donkeys lb at the oil museum in midland, but a couple of
muggles decided to look at the oil equipment as well, and they
werent leaving anytime soon, so decided to attempt that one on my
way home. i then went to the commemorative air force museum and
located the pliska aeroplane lb. after leaving there, we went to
the odessa meteor crater and found the texas celestial series
#3-meteor box. this was a really neat location. my navigator, jr,
was not exactly happy with the location, because he realized that he
was allergic to a yellow flower that he was sniffing and started
sneezing. i think he must have sneezed about 20 times before he got
it cleared out. jr and i then decided to stop off at monahans state
park and we stopped and played in the sand for a little while before
we stopped for the day.

Day 2
We were out of the motel by 5:45 and went to pick up the ro-deo de
ode-ode cowboy box in pecos before dark. thank goodness for the
streetlight. from here we went to mentone and picked up the
everybody's got to be somewhere lb right at day break. this was a
neat find at a neat little town. it is the county seat of loving
county even though it only has a population of 15. we then went to
kermit to look for the it's not easy being green lb, but
no luck with this one. our next stop was roy orbison's hometown,
wink, and found the only the lonely lb. we then went to imperial
and found the horsehead crossing of the rio pecos. i loved this
location and the story behind it. you could actually picture the
cowboys taking their cows across the pecos river. next stop was ft
stockton and found the displaced donkey and abundant water
letterboxes. we then went to marfa (no we didnt get to see the
lights) and found the marfa lb and the giant in marfa lb, which had
the box of popcorn hh in it. we then proceeded to valentine. on
the way to valentine, we saw a solid white blimp in the air that was
attached to the ground by a very long wire. there was a sign that
said U.S. Air Force TARS. not sure what it is but if anyone knows
anything about it, please let me know because i am very curious
about what it is. we made it to the prada "store" which is actually
an art exhibit with actual prada pieces from the 2005 collection,
outside of valentine, but could not find the prada lb.
throughout the trip from ft stockton to valentine, we did see a lot
of antelope in an area called antelope vally. we then traveled
through el paso into new mexico and through the white sands missile
range. after going through alamogordo, we entered the mescalero
apache reservation. we did see an ancient volcano on the way to
ruidoso, which was really awesome. we then spent the night at
ruidoso.

Day 3
Back on the road about 6 am and went to capitan to pick up the smoky
the bear lb, but the area was not open yet. this is a really
beautiful area. we drove through lincoln county where the lincoln
county wars were held. these wars involved billy the kid. the
town of lincoln was extremely interesting and also is the sight of
billy the kid's last escape. we then arrived in roswell and placed
3 boxes for my cousin wag time. we went downtown and located the
ufo museum, didnt go in because we were there too early. from here
we made it to fort sumner and picked up the billy the kid microbox
at the sight of billy the kid's grave. this was kind of interesting
because there is a "jail" built over the grave to protect the
tombstone from being stolen. went back to capitan and saw
the grave of smokey the bear and found the smokey the bear lb. from
here went through the valley of fire recreation area which was
really neat. lots of volcanic rocks. spent the night in deming, nm

we made it to phoenix on day 4 and stayed in phoenix for a week but
only found 3 letterboxes during my stay. we found the sun city
microbox and the sunny surprise lb and the surprise stadium lb. we
decided that letterboxing in 110 degree weather was not fun. after
the first week, we went to tucson and while we were there, picked up
8 letterboxes. one of the lbs that we found was at an adopt a
roadway sign where the roadway had been adopted by the tucson area
letterboxers, which was really neat.

we had a great trip and all total
30 letterboxes
6 hitchhikers
cant wait to go again, but for now glad to be home.
