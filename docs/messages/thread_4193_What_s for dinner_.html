Chicken Saltimbocca is chicken breasts that have been pounded flat and thin, filled with prociutto ham, Provolone cheese, diced tomatoes, a bit of garlic and onion, and then some spices. Roll it up really tight and secure the rolls with toothpicks. Then, it's cooked in some olive oil and white wine until they are done. I usually serve the saltimbocca with a nice white wine sauce over it.

I stopped using an actual recipe years ago - this is my go-to entree when I want a nice dinner with the hubby. I've used the basic idea of it to do all sorts of other chicken recipes - like chicken filled with spinach, feta cheese and pine nuts for a Greek kinda dinner, etc. You can find some saltimbocca recipes here:
http://allrecipes.com/Search/Recipes.aspx?WithTerm=saltimbocca

Happy Eating and Happy Trails!
