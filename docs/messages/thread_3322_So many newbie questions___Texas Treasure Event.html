You can look on the AQ gathering page for some of them:
 
http://www.atlasquest.com/events/event.html?gEventId=508
 
AND you can look on LBNA at:
 
http://www.letterboxing.org/HHAdd.php
 
and choose Personal in the drop down box.
 
AND you can look at the list of participants and cruise around and explore their homepages for possible PT clues.  Sometimes you can just ask someone if they have a PT and what you would need to do to get it!  Just always remember to be polite in asking, as getting some folks to give up their PTs is more a matter of finesse than of knowing the answer.
 
Sometimes finding the clues takes as much sleuth work as solving the clues!

Re: [letterboxingtexas] So many newbie questions!//Texas Treasure Event

From: janet clark (jayceeinbedford@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-02-03 19:57:37 UTC-08:00
So your legwork. I didn't for the Gread Book Adventure and I only got a few. It's worth investing the time beforehand. When you get to the gathering, it can be overwhelming. I kept thinking that I wasn't spending time looking for boxes as much as I wanted, it just took so long meeting everyone. After the gathering, I read somewhere that some people just don't worry about the boxes. Others still, plan on coming back the next day to find them. It's mostly about meeting the people to a lot of boxers.
 
Another big faux pas I think I made at GBA was just asking someone for an exchange. I was so superexcited that all I could think of was having that person's (the person I 'knew' from this list) stamp to have. Now I realize that sitting and getting to really know that person is what it's about. Oh well, live and learn. I wasn't trying to be rude, sometimes it just comes naturally :)
 
I really hope I can come to this gathering. I've got to stop planning vacations around the same time as the TALE. Last year it was Riudosa, NM this year the Carribean. I love these vacations, but they are keeping me from the Event. ARGH!
 
Janet, BF
