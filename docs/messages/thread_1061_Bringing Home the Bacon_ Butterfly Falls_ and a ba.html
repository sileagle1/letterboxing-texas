Bringing Home the Bacon, Butterfly Falls, and a barefoot HH, note about Spring Hope (Subtitled- Tears DO work)

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-07-15 20:50:21 UTC-07:00
Got out again this evening and went to a beautiful
little park in Jacksonville that really isn't so
little any more - Nichols Green!

TexasLuvBug has planted three boxes there and I
already found one of them. I decided to go for the
two newer ones, thinking "piece of cake - no problem".
Well, nice walk-yes, easy-no. Don't let the fact
that this is a small-town city park fool you - there's
a lot more to it than you would think!

Going after Bringing Home the Bacon proved to be very
difficult because there can be a lot of traffic on the
nearby sidewalk, and folks can slip up on you quietly.
What caught me by surprise is that I could be so
close to a sidewalk I had walked many, many times and
yet I had never noticed the short trail and the
beautiful little peaceful spot by the creek. If you
go for this one, DO be prepared with a couple of
different cover stories because you might need them.
For myself, I wasn't prepared with cover so I had to
improvise. I was stamping in with my back to the
sidewalk when a man noticed me off the trail and was
concerned. He called out to me, and I just kind of
mumbled something off, and he started down the trail
to check on me. Fortunately, when my daughter was in
drama class she used me to help her learn how to
create tears on command, so I used that skill to my
advantage - I started crying, then turned toward him
and replied that I was "fine - just fine." He wasn't
sure whether to continue to my side or not, so I
mumbled something about "just had an argument with
someone and ..." He said something comforting and
went on! Wow! Now I can see how that could backfire,
but I suddenly realized people get really
uncomfortable with other peoples' pain, especially
that of strangers! I felt a little bad about playing
off his sympathy, but I figure he was happy enough
that I let him off the hook by saying I would be fine.
I felt a little weird with one hand on my backpack
and ready to swing and wiping my tears with the other!

I went on to Butterfly Falls, but I didn't see TLB's
armadillo - but then I was wearing tenny shoes, not
flip flops, so he wasn't lured out by my toes.

GG had visited both of them yesterday - busy guy our
enigmatic man is! Also found a HH, but I can't
remember the name of it. I do remember that it is a
single bare foot, so it may not leave my hands too
readily!

I went and checked on my Spring Hope to see if GG had
visited it - he hadn't. I was bummed. I wonder why -
if he didn't have time to decipher the clues, which
are not real direct, or what the reason was. Oh well.

Lucy
