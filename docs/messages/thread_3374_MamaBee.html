>>>MamaBee wrote:  Still, I’ve purchased so much carving medium and thrown away so many unsatisfactory attempts – I am about making myself crazy trying to carve something decent for next weekend and my PT stamp – which at this point is a great idea in my head, a little better on paper – but still a mess in MasterCarve.  So thanks for reminding me that we all have to start somewhere.



Are you kiddin' me??  Aren't you the one that planted the  "Let's have a picnic" series at Emma Long Park in Austin?  I just found 'em all on Monday.  And the stamps were terrific!!!  It was a beautiful day and a great hike.  Fortunately, I only encountered a couple of people walking dogs, because at one point, I sounded like an elephant crashing through the trees!  I do have to let you know that should you revisit the boxes that you planted, the cheese box location might look a bit different.
For the cheese box<This path goes up a steep incline. 30 steps up the incline look for a 3 foot stump in front of a group of large rocks. The Cheese is nestled behind the base of the stump just under the ledge of the rock.>

Well, you weren't kidding when you wrote 'steep incline'. LOL.  I couldn't find anything to even grab on to when I climbed up there.  The cedar tree limbs were as brittle as could be, and due to dry conditions, the  footing was very loose and hard to get a good stance when retrieving the box while standing like a goat on the side of the hill.  I put my hand down on one of the boulders right behind/next to the stump, and it became dislodged and rolled down and nearly took my legs out from underneath me.  I've got a nice little bruise and scrape on the side of my leg.... and that 'group of large rocks' might not look as though you remember them. LOL.  One is now rolled down the hill a little ways.  Gee, you wouldn't believe how much noise it made rolling over brittle sticks and stuff!  BUT, no worries... the tree stump and box are in tact!  And I got that dang stamp, even with my legs shaking like crazy!  In fact, I had to stamp it in my book twice because I was shaking so bad and messed up the first one.
Your clues were right on and I enjoyed the challenge, in spite of my clumsiness!  Only two of the water crossings had water and I was able to step across on rocks so no wet shoes.  I hadn't planned on doing those boxes on Monday.  I was going to look for the Arboretum cow boxes, but had 10K training in Austin and got pretty sweaty.  Was afraid that people at the Arboretum might find my odor offensive, so that just left doing boxes near dead people and dogs.  Went to Oakwood Cemetery first and then to the off-leash dog trail at Emma Long Park, where your series of stamps were.
Thanks again for the challenge!

Mim Z
