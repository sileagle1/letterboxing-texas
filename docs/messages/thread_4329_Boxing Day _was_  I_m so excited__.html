From: adamstrails (adamstrails@yahoo.com) | Date: 2009-12-29 01:57:26 UTC
We letterboxed on Boxing Day. We went south for Christmas Holidays, where it is warm.LOL. We went down to Goose Island State Park on Dec. 24 where we attempted to find "Big Tree 2" They had done a major clean-up around the tree. Brush hog and all. No box was to be found. We enjoyed the park and the big tree. Glad we went even if did not find the box. We did find "My missing keys" by Celtic Lions in the Park. It was in very good shape and well hidden. We also went to Aransas National Wildlife Refuge. Enjoyed the walk and the look out tower. Saw some Whooping Cranes. No box. We forgot to look for 2 boxes that were reported there but the one behind the big tree was not there. Then we went into Rockport. Did not go into the Beach Park. It cost $4.00 to enter and we were tired and it was getting late so we skipped that one. The Wedding one was not there. We were staying at the Goliad State Park. We knew that the letterbox there was reported missing but we checked anyway and also asked about letterboxing and geocashing in the park and the assistant ranger we talked to said they did not allow it and also he was sure there was none there.

On Dec 26 Boxing Day, we went to Yorktown, Tx and found Setting Sun. Love the big trees in the area. We have big trees here but not like the ones in South Texas. We found a bonus a H.H. in Setting Sun called "My Favorite Cartoon" It has traveled almost all over the US and is still in great shape and still hiking. We will sent him on his way soon. We went on to Panna Maria and found Solidarsc by Corizon. We just loved the church. Took lots of pictures. It was worth the trip just to see the church. Talked to a sweet lady there too who told us about the church and the town. We could not stay longer or we could have boxed, and boxed and boxed. Lots of interesting places. Adams Trails

From: Boots Tex (redbarn@eastex.net) | Date: 2009-12-30 20:58:11 UTC
The Ranger's remark sounds to me like he had never heard of letterboxing but didn't want to admit it. Texas Parks and Wildlife has a program in place that allows both geocaching and letterboxing with certain restrictions. First you must fill out an application for a permit and then it has to be approved. This can be a simple procedure or it can take, literally, months based on my experience. Most parks have certain areas where letterboxes would not be allowed and I suspect that at Goliad, you couldn't get a permit to place a box inside the Mission. You have to respect that. Some parks have environmentally sensitive areas where letterboxes wouldn't be allowed, nor should they be. If you ask for a permit application instead of asking whether letterboxes are allowed, you should be given one, even if they have to go find out what it is and where it's stashed away. If you are reasonable about where you place the box, it shouldn't be a problem. This is something new for the parks and park rangers, and will take some time. Each one interprets the rules differently, or have to learn the rules, but if you are polite and patient, you can usually manage to get your box placed and permitted. There are some obvious exceptions, like Hueco Tanks, but generally, letterboxes are, by rule, legal in Texas State Parks.

Boots Tex
