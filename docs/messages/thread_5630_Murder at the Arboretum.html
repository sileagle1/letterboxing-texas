Murder at the Arboretum

From: (candj1969@yahoo.com) | Date: 2018-10-09 08:29:54 UTC-07:00

Last week, I decided to check on all the boxes for the Murder at the Arboretum series at Mercer Arboretum.  I hadn't done that in a very long time and the boxes haven't been found or even reported on since 2014.  With clues in hand and a beautiful day, I headed out.  I quickly discovered that the clues no longer worked.  Landmarks were gone or overgrown.  A bench was no longer there.  An entire restroom building was no longer there.  A tree had been cut down and the stump overgrown.  I was able to locate the original spots for every box, but if you had never found them before, it would have been impossible.  Unfortunately, six out of the seven boxes were gone.  The only one that was left was the last one, number seven and it was laying out in the open.  The good news is that it was in very good condition considering it had been out there for nearly eleven years.  I can't believe it has been that long.  I picked up the box and have retired the series.  I considered trying to salvage it by making new boxes, but it wouldn't have been the same.  Three of the suspects are no longer active letterboxers.  The murder victim is now a ghost.  So, thanks for all the comments we got over the years, it was a lot of fun.

I guess anyone who was not participating during the old days of TX letterboxing wouldn't know who the characters were or anything about them, and that is a shame. The group of boxers during the mid-2000's was a fun group and came to all the events, but more importantly they shared experiences and stories on this TX Yahoo Group. We were like a family and I miss that. Murder at the Arboretum was so fun because it had some of these boxers as suspects, with names close enough to their real trail names that you knew who they were, and the stamps were their faces expertly carved by Boots.  Here is a list of the characters with their real trail names and status:

IRA Boxer - ?:  Unknown boxer with signature stamp of red boxer shorts and white hearts who used to heckle people on this Yahoo Group, especially Baby Bear.  He was the murder victim of this series but came back later as the Ghost of IRA Boxer and still participates.

Sterling Seagull - Silver Eagle:  still participates
  
Kid Kodiak - Baby Bear:  still participates

Suzy Shoeless - Barefoot Lucy:  early letterboxer who created this TX Yahoo Group but no longer participates

Rubus Trivialis - Dewberry:  letterboxer known for her cryptic puzzle boxes but no longer participates

Jogsfer Jellybeans - Runs for Chocolate:  letterboxer who ran marathons so she could eat more chocolate but no longer participates

Detective Gandolph de Grey driving the Blueberry Beetle - Gryzzled Gryphon driving his Cranberry Cruiser:  Infamous letterboxer who remained a mystery to us for years except for his car, a purple PT Cruiser.  He still participates, though not online.

Most people assumed Baby Bear murdered IRA Boxer since they feuded so much, but that was not the case.  In the opening crime scene, IRA Boxer has "blue bruises" or something else on his neck, and Detective Gandolph de Grey driving the Blueberry Beetle wipes his hands.  Enough said.

If you want to read about some of the stories from the early days of TX letterboxing, go to the Files section of this Yahoo Group and click on Letterboxing Memories, then click on the Doc files 1-6:

https://groups.yahoo.com/neo/groups/LETTERBOXINGTEXAS/files/Letterboxing%20Memories/

Silver Eagle

P.S. I have created a series of boxes for Live & Breathe XX called TX Letterboxing History that incorporates some of these stories

From: Charles Watson (candj1969@yahoo.com) | Date: 2018-10-11 11:15:25 UTC-05:00

 

From: Boots Tex

 

Lone Star Quilter and I lived in Sugar Land from 1989 until 2003, so we were there when Silver Eagle and Baby Bear started planting boxes in that area in, what was it, 2000 or 2001?  We had our chance to be in on the fun earlier in the game, but didn’t know about it until LSQ’s brother found the book in 2005 and brought it when he came to visit us on Thanksgiving.  We were living in Coldspring at the time and owned a quilting retreat center.  We immediately made sig stamps and started finding boxes near us.  When we joined this group and announced our presence, one of the ladies who was a regular visitor to our retreats, told us that she was also a letterboxer and that her trail name was Runs for Chocolate, so she was the first real letterboxer we met.  After boxing for a year or so, I started thinking about a series with a murder mystery theme.  One of my hobbies has always been art, and I liked to draw and paint portraits of people, mostly members of my family, so I wanted to use portrait stamps for my boxes.  My first attempts weren’t very good, but I kept practicing.  When we found one of the Tour Bus boxes, I carved stamps of George and Laura Bush for the box.  Somewhere between George and Laura, I figured out how to carve portraits that could be recognized as real people, so I was ready to start planning my series.  We had found some boxes at the Mercer Arboretum and Botannical Gardens and decided that we would use the arboretum side for the series.  The name came naturally: Murder at the Arboretum.  Then I thought about the characters.  I needed a victim and I was originally going to use a real letterboxer with a slightly disquised name, but LSQ was afraid I would hurt someone’s feelings.  I was going to call him Michael del Paso, so you can see her concern. My second choice was “Pirvert”, who would chain all his boxes to a tree,  but she didn’t like that one either.  The only other thing to do was to create a new letterboxer that I could use for a while to place a few boxes and become an obnoxious presence on this group site.  He would be “I are a” boxer, which evolved into IRA Boxer.  The name led to the signature stamp.  Next, I needed a group of suspects, so I started taking pictures at some of the events that Dewberry used to have around Houston and I picked out several that I thought I could carve stamps of and who I was pretty sure wouldn’t be too upset about being suspected of murdering a fellow letterboxer. It actually took me a few months to set it all up and have IRA Boxer get acquainted with the letterboxing community.  He even attended events and stamped the event posters.  When it was all set up, I checked out the arboretum for good hiding places for the boxes and wrote out the clues to make sure they all worked, then filled in the details to make the story interesting.  I’m still not sure where I got the picture for the murderer or why I chose him.  I think when I made the stamps, he just fit the part.  Typecasting, I guess. My plan was that, after IRA Boxer was murdered, he would simply disappear and would never be heard from again.  I liked him so much that I couldn’t let him rest, so he has enjoyed an afterlife.   The rest is history.  I’m glad you all enjoyed it

 

Boots Tex

Wow, I never knew the whole story behind IRA boxer and the Murder series, so thanks for sharing.  I still remember the first time I saw the IRA boxer stamp and the commotion it caused.  It was a gathering in Houston that Dewberry hosted at Memorial Park that many of us attended, and afterwards Dewberry and Mother of 5 emailed me to ask when I stamped the event poster.  I couldn't remember the exact time and asked why they wanted to know.  They said somebody named IRA boxer had stamped it after I did and they were trying to figure out who it was.  For months after that we all wondered who this guy was until later that year at Telge Park, Dewberry and Mother of 5 happened to see you coming out of the woods after stamping into a letterbox they were about to find.  When they opened it up, they saw the IRA boxer stamp freshly imprinted in the logbook and knew his identity. So ended the greatest mystery in TX letterboxing since GG.
Thanks for giving us so many years of fun and great boxes!
