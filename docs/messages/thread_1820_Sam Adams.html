I lurk on this list quite a bit and am intrigued that Lucy has broached this topic.  As a military spouse - it is an issue we face everyday.  I conduct an "Air Force 101" class for brand new military spouses and in our intro we talk about the "myths" surrounding the military and try to dispel those for these young spouses.  One myth that is always brought up is that military members are all ultra-conservative and right-wingers. 
 
Thus the political - but in 20 years of service with my husband - I have found all opinions represented throughout the military.  While we all support our "team" - those among us who are deployed - we also support our "opinions" or politics.  May not agree with them - but support the right to have those opinions.
 
So - to me, the "patriotism" is supporting my spouse and his co-workers and being aware of my roots and heritage.  The "political" references my thoughts and views regarding modern-day issues.
 
I welcome further discussion and insight into this and always welcome a challenge to my thoughts - it helps me learn to keep thinking outisde the "box!"

Hmmm...  interesting question.  My first thought was "Simple.  History is in the past, and politics is in the present or future.  But after thinking it over, I am not sure there is a clear answer.  In reality, everything is political and religious.  Every one of us was raised in some kind of environment.  Our belief system is a combination of what we were taught when young, and modified by our perception of what we learned later on.  Most historical events were caused by a political or religious event of some sort.
 
For instance, my earlier reference to the British coming to dis-arm the colonies.  I have read this account in a number of places, including some of the federalist papers.  It was generally accepted that were they to be dis-armed, there would be no further recourse but to submit to English rule.  And so true, it is hard to fight against the best weaponry of the period using rakes and shovels.  It was a good plan, if it could be pulled off.  The British failed, and we are free as a consequence of that.  In the 1930s the Germans pulled it off, and we all know what happened there.
 
I think that our political and religious beliefs cloud everything we do to some extent.  To not be so would be to deny everything you were taught and learned.  For instance, this statement.  God does not exist, Allah is the only true deity.  For some, this is true.  For others, it is false.  What makes your side of that statement more right than the other side?
 
Yes - I am now convinced that there is no one right answer for your question.  However, I do look forward to reading other answers.  My beliefs are not set in stone.  I can learn more and make changes.
 
Bring it on!!!  <grin>
