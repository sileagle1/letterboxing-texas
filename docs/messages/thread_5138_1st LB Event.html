From: Christine Borgfeld (christine@borgfeld.net) | Date: 2013-01-30 18:03:57 UTC-06:00

Atlas Quest has a good page on tips for attending an event:

http://www.atlasquest.com/events/tips2.html

 

Most of us have logbooks with blank pages for letterboxing.  The best kind have blank pages made of good quality smooth blank paper (white cardstock is the best for pages if you are willing to make your own book!).  Some people have different books for events and regular letterboxing but I just use one and put everything in it.  You can easily get 50+ stamp images at an event, especially if this is your first and you don’t have everyone else’s signature stamps.  My best advice is don’t forget to eat lunch (I learned this one the hard way!).  After the exchanges, try to get together with some other boxers to go out boxing in the afternoon.  This is a really good way to get to know some people and make new friends.

From: Bonnie Lind (bonnielind1@hotmail.com) | Date: 2013-01-30 22:35:01 UTC-06:00

I remember being so very nervous for my first TALE.  The link Open Space provided has great advice on attending events.  Here are some of my own recommendations.

 

As a first timer, there are a lot of folks who will want to meet you and exchange signature stamps and personal travelers with you.  My advice is to find a comfortable spot at a table and let them come to you.  I know this sounds goofy, but don’t forget to notice with whom you are making exchanges.  It is so easy to forget to look up because you are concentrating on stamping.  Also, Open Space is right when she said not to forget to eat lunch—there will hardly be a break in the action!  Everyone is so nice—it will be hard to excuse yourself to grab a plate to eat.

 

A lot of folks plant letterboxes the day before or the morning of the event.  Find the table with all the new clues, and get out there and find some letterboxes.  Some people put off letterboxing until Sunday, but there may be some letterboxes that are just temporary, and you might be sad to miss them.

 

Concerning Personal Travelers (PTs), if you have the time and inclination, go to the event page and click on Personal Travelers.  Here you will be able to see the listed PTs.  You will be able to do some research to see what is required for each one.  All that is required for most of them is to just ask.  Here is a tip for managing your research:  save your notes using Create Note, even if it is “just ask.”  Then, just before the event, use the Print Checklist button from the event page.  Print Checklist will list all the folks with whom you haven’t exchanged and also all the available PTs and event stamps.  If you made a note of the PT requirement, that information will show up, too.  The funny thing is, I do this for every event, and I rarely have time to reference it during the event. 

 

Plan on hanging around a while.  In years past, there have been many tales told at the campfire after the event.  As of now, there is not a burn ban at Buescher State Park.  Additionally, there may be some night boxing…

 

By the way, a couple days after the event, Atlasquest automatically sends out an AQ-mail that makes it so easy to log your exchanges, PTs and Event stamps.  Luckily I’m a procrastinator and found this out the easy way.

 

I guess it all boils down to having fun!  Meet new people who share your interest in this quirky hobby, eat some delicious pot luck, plant/find some wonderful letterboxes.

 

Looking forward to meeting new friends and visiting with old ones,

Mosaic Butterfly
