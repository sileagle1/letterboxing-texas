From: Semalee (Semalee@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2005-06-06 16:02:03 UTC-05:00
Yes, the HH should be stamped into the box that you found it in by the previous placer, I always check though, cause I have found a few that were not.  Make sure that the box stamp is in the HH log too.  Then you stamp your stamp into the HH log and the HH stamp in your log.  I put mine by the stamp of the box I found it in and I write notes about it.  Then you drop it at your next box *if it will fit*, but there are a lot of microboxes in Houston, so that is kind of difficult.  There is also the Houston Hostel where you can leave it too.  When you drop it at the next box be sure to stamp the HH in to the box and the stamp the HH log too...  
 
Is that about as clear as mud?

As long as we’re on the subject of Hitchhikers, I just wanted to let our newer Texas letterboxers know that there is a great site to track the travels of HHs:

http://alum.wpi.edu/~p_miner/travelers.html

 

Just wait until you create your first HH, and send it out into the world – you’re going to be checking this site constantly waiting to hear how far your offspring has traveled! For instance, our first HH was Texas Tick – we now realize it was WAY TOO BIG to be a HH, but we had a lot of fun creating it. It had some info about Lyme disease, but at one point also contained lime-flavored TIC Tacs (we can never resist a pun).…and thanks to the kindness of strangers ( and some familiar boxers, too!), it continues to travel around.

 

To log the travels of a HH, write to:

letterbox_travellers@yahoogroups.com

 

(And by the way, when you write, say “thank you” to Pete (of the dynamic duo, Wanda & Pete), who must spend many long eye-straining hours maintaining that site!)

 

NOTE: Letterboxing etiquette suggests that you reveal the date and the name of the box where you found the HH, but not the name of the box where you dropped it off. It’s more fun for seekers if you give a general description of the area, such as “piney woods” or “Cow Town”. If you look up the HH on Pete’s site and find a lot of data missing, it would be oh-so-kind of you to add that info, too. Hawk once found that infamous Texas Tick and was kind enough to write to me with a full account of its travels to date. It was very much appreciated!)

Just thought of one more thing!
If you find a HH in a box and you don't want to take it or think you won't
be able to move it along to the next box quickly, feel free to stamp into
your logbook and the HH log, but leave the HH in the letterbox.

POSSIBLE REASONS:
1. You won't be letterboxing until the next full moon
2. Your better half has recently found out how much you've been spending on
Tupperware, and you're grounded
3. The FBI has been putting up posters of your face due to suspicious
lurking near urban locations
4. Your children only know you by your trail name, and you're thinking you
may need to spend a little more time at home...
