Okay, I'll bite. I''ve been watching this discussion but haven't had time
to sit down and participate in the discussion, but what the heck - I'll skip
fixing supper in favor of this topic.

I probably have a little bias in favor of media coverage, although my
feelings have changed a little over the years. We had a local reporter who
did an article about letterboxing when my family had been boxing for about a
year. I was very uneasy doing an interview by myself since I was relatively
inexperienced. She had also contacted Silver Eagle and Baby Bear, since at
that time we were the only three boxers who had boxes in this county. We
emailed back and forth about it between ourselves, and we agreed that we
would be forceful about not participating in the interview unless she agreed
to a few terms - not givng explicit directions to any boxes, not taking
photographs of boxes, emphasizing the need for stealth. She interviewed
Silver Eagle by phone as I recall and Baby Bear by email. Lightning and I
then spent an afternoon with her being interviewed and then inviting her
along to plant a box I was placing specifically for the newspaper article.
For the most part, she did a very good job and she did a lot of research.

Aside from that somewhat positive experience, I also worked quite a lot with
the news media in one of my scout functions and I found local reporters to
be invested in digging up cool local twists. I kind of like reporter type
folks, and I think at least half of them have their hearts in the right
place, and heck - that's a better percentage than lawyers (sorry Gryzzled
Gryphon and Cattitude and any other lawyer letterboxers...).

The only down side is that it really didn't bring anyone into the game that
stuck around. Blue Butterfly and Lucy Locket came along shortly thereafter,
but they didn't see the article. JBK came along a little after that, but
again, they (they were a they back then) didn't read the article either.
The only boxer that came in as a result of the article missed the point for
the most part, planted a bunch of boxes that were great carves and lousy
hides and clues. She went incommunicado after about six months and left us
with a bunch of untended boxes that went missing quickly, and perplexed new
boxers who got discouraged when they couldn't find the boxes she had listed
and left behind. How long did it take us to finally get her boxes off the
clue sites?

So ANYWAY - yeah, I kind of like the media, but I don't think it does much
for the game and has the potential for really doing some damage.

As far as the Statesman article specifically, I think it was poorly done. I
don't know what the format of her column is - maybe it's a "What I did this
weekend" column, so from a journalistic point of view, I guess it's fine.
