Hello and welcome!  You are getting started for the same reasons I did and I hope you enjoy it. 

 

You really don’t need much in the way of supplies to start.  At a minimum you just need a pen, an ink pad and something to keep the stamps you find on (a notebook, index cards or whatever you have handy.)  You can start simply by finding some boxes and sign into the logbook in the box.  You can also stamp your fingerprint into the book – whatever you are comfortable with.  The inkpad lets you keep an image of the stamp in the letterbox as a souvenir and log that you found the box.  Many people use a sketchbook or blank journal for this.  Others stamp index cards which they later put in albums or scrapbooks. 

 

After you find a box, be sure to restore everything the way you found it for future boxers.   It’s especially important to reseal everything (most boxes will have plastic bags to keep the logbook dry) and re-hide it so casual observers can’t find it.  If boxes are under rocks or sticks please replace these as well – they provide camouflage and sometimes protection from local wildlife.  If you haven’t found a box before – they are usually in small food storage type boxes covered in camouflage tape or painted a dark color. 

 

Once you find a few boxes you may consider getting a personal stamp and creating a trail name.  This is basically your “signature” which other boxers will become familiar with as they see it in logbooks where you have visited.  Most people start with a simple rubber stamp they purchase at a craft store.  Later you can look into carving a stamp if that is something you are interested in. 

 

This link at atlas quest has some good information for beginners:  http://www.atlasquest.com/about/

 

One other thought….there are lots of offshoots to letterboxing with special terminology that gets confusing fast.  When you are leaning online, focus on traditional letterboxes first and don’t worry about things like postals, hitchhikers, or LTCs.   You really don’t need to understand these to enjoy the hobby. 

 

Hope this helps and feel free to ask more questions

 

Open Space (Christine)
