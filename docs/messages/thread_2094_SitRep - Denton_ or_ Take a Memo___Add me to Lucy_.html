think about stuffing stockings since it seems we are headed for
colder weather as we drop below 100 this week :)
We have been out and about traveling over the last week and managed
to get a few letterboxes too.
Last week we stopped in Corsicana for Pitch In -great stamp! and I
love Corsicana- didn't much care for the webs guarding the box
though.I enjoyed visiting the area where my older children grew up,
played ball and got into mischief as younger kids.
We continues toward San Antonio and got Texas Ranger- amazing stamp
and we were followed by a very quiet duck who scared the begeebers
out of us but made for a great laugh the rest of the afternoon.
Next stop was for Whistle Stop and they are not kidding -even in the
afternoon heat that is one amazing playground nearby- we really had
a blast (and an excuse for ice cream)We stayed there a good while
delaying our SA arrival a good bit.
As we continued we stopped for the Texas Salamander and thouroughly
enjoyed our time there- my 4 year enjoyed the nearby exibit of
animal scat- see there is something for everyone- they left that out
in the Family Fun article :)
Once in San Antonio I had great plans and more than a dozen
potiental boxes but alas the 4 year year old teamed up with my 3
year old grandson and ...well we got one at Mission San Juan
Capistrano- a great place for little boys but maybe not for Grandma
at 103 degrees.
A fun trip and 5 new stamps. I am only an hour from Dallas and visit
there frequently but I have yet to get any Dallas
boxes...Lucy...heading West anytime soon?

I also purchased some supplies to start trying carving..bandaids,
speed ball, bandaids, erasers, bandaids, carving block...you get the
idea..now I need a tetnus shot and I can begin.

Having fun this summer,

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-08-22 08:24:28 UTC-05:00

Tracy,

I met that duck too!  There had been a fellow eating at the table next to us and feeding the duck while we were getting Texas Ranger.   When he got up and left, the duck felt strongly that we should pick up the guy's slack and feed him scraps.  The only scraps we had were rubber scraps though, which DID look like carrot peels, but we couldn't bring ourselves to feed the poor duck rubber.   He apparently really wanted rubber though, because he stalked us until we left the area.  But I'm wondering did a little guy come and talk to you for awhile while you were there?   The first time I went for that box, I had a fellow come over and talk to me while I was stamping in.  He paid no heed to what I was doing while he talked, and thankfully left before it was time to put the box back.   I swear I saw him the second time too, but maybe he was afraid of the duck because he didn't come over that time!

I've really been wanting to get further west lately, and south and north, but I can't seem to get out of my own town for some reason!   This trip to Denton was the first I've made in awhile and it wound up not being very fruitful for letterboxing, although we had a great time and had some offshoot adventures that wouldn't have happened if I hadn't had letterboxing on the brain.   Maybe when the weather cools
