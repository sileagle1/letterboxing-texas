From: sileagle1 (sileagle@windstream.net) | Date: 2009-12-11 22:20:12 UTC
I'm not saying my boxes are great, but they are some of the only ones down that way and are in some great places to visit. My Sarita Songbird is at a rest area right on Hwy 77, and Kingsville Kiskadee is just off Hwy 77 in Kingsville. Just look on LBNA in south region. I think the boxes in Bentsen-Rio Grande Valley SP are all missing, but it is still worth going since you will see birds found nowhere else in the U.S. You can't drive in the park but can rent bikes, which my family really enjoyed. Have fun!

Silver Eagle

From: Boots Tex (redbarn@eastex.net) | Date: 2009-12-11 22:55:27 UTC
Have you tried the Letterbox Search feature on LbNA? You can search by state, region, county, city or a word or phrase in the box name or clue. I really like that the boxes are listed by county. If you want all the boxes in a county and nothing else, just pick (for instance) Texas and Cameron County and you'll see all the boxes in Cameron County. Don't neglect LbNA or you'll miss some great boxes. Also be sure to check for mystery boxes.

Boots Tex

From: Cancan (cancan1959@gmail.com) | Date: 2009-12-12 17:36:40 UTC-06:00

Dear Dawn's Glory,

There is a shortage of boxes and boxers in the valley. My box at Zapata State Park still does not have a logged find...although it is in a very remote place ;)

If you are going to carve, might I suggest one for the Iwo Jima Monument at the airport in Harlingen. Let's talk at next weeks event, this used to be my district and I loved the valley and was always going to do one for the monument.

If Gma Helen is a history buff, I HIGHLY recommend a visit to the MOST (Museum of South Texas) in Edinburg, it is a very well done museum.

Kind regards,
Cancan
