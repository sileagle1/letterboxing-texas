Hitchhiker procedures

From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-01-21 02:53:45 UTC
Hey all,

Just a reminder about the way to handle a hitchhiker. I picked up
Quetzel's Bee on the Move at the Fairfield Letterbox yesterday, and
it's missing a few box stamps. Hitchhiker logbooks are stamped with
the stamp of the host box and with the stamp of any letterboxer who
moves it along or who takes its stamp image as a visitor without
moving it anywhere. The person who places the HH in the host box is
supposed to stamp the new host box in the log along with the date
left, but often people forget. In that case, the person who picks up
the HH should do it.

So, when you pick up a hitchhiker, check to make sure it has the box
it has been hiding inside is stamped in the logbook. I didn't do
that, just grabbed it, and I got home found that it doesn't have the
Fairfield stamp in it. However, the HH log is also missing the stamp
of the previous box it visited as well, so I thought I'd offer this
reminder. It can certainly get confusing.

And, with the gathering coming up in a few months, I thought I'd ask
if anyone needs clarification on a personal traveler and what it is
and how it works?

Dewberry
