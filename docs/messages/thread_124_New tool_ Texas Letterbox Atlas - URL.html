Jeff has worked hard getting coordinates for all my boxes
along with Baby Bear's and his own to put in this atlas
and I have been pushing him to get this finished ever
since he suggested it to me. I think it is a great idea
to visually see GENERAL locations of letterboxes (the
coordinates are not exact so they don't give away the
location of the letterbox using GPS) and I would love to see
this feature available in other states. Unfortunately
it requires someone with programming skills and time
to compute coordinates of all boxes desired. The other
issue is approval of the owners of the boxes. After the
uproar over putting status of letterboxes in a database, I
would bet many people would object to this atlas. That is
why Jeff is only putting boxes in that have given approval.
Please give it a try, and if you like it, give your approval
to have your boxes added. Keeping it evergreen as new boxes
are placed will be tough for Jeff, but I plan to help if I can.
For you non-Texan programmers, I hope you bring it to your
state, too. Thanks Jeff!

Silver Eagle

> All,
>
> The Texas Letterbox Atlas is a website for making and
> viewing maps showing the general locations of
> letterboxes in Texas.
>
> I developed this out of the desire to be able to see
> the relative closeness of various letterboxes so I
> could more effectively plan letterboxing excursions. I
> hope that you find it useful also.
>
> The only letterboxes that will be displayed are those
> for which I have received approval from the
> placer/maintainer or the letterbox is currently in the
> letterbox basement (unclaimed).
>
> *****************************************************
> If you would like your letterboxes to be listed, send
> a note. Just let me know if it is for all boxes or
> just specific boxes.
> *****************************************************
>
> Mystery boxes will be listed but will be placed in the
> center of the city or county or if a complete unknown
> will displayed over the waters of the Gulf.
>
> The Texas Letterbox Atlas currently has 138
> letterboxes which include the Texas letterboxes for
> Baby Bear, Silver Eagle, and Birds of a Feather
> Family.
>
> Texas LB Atlas supports:
>
> - associating web pages with a letterbox. Where
> possible, each letterbox has a link to it's clue page.
>
> - searching for letterboxes containing a word or
> phrase
>
> - pre-defined views - Choose from US, Texas, or a
> specific Texas county.
>
> - zooming and panning in or out on a map
>
> - mapping a GPS point (Note: Texas LB Atlas uses a
> mapping engine that uses the "North American Datum
> 1927" (NAD-27) system with decimal degrees and not
> Degrees:Minutes:Seconds.
>
> - requests for a new feature to be added. Anyone can
> request a new feature be added by mapping the location
> of the feature and then clicking on the "Add a New
> Feature!" link
>
> - distinguishing between unclaimed, mystery,
> unpublished, and normal letterboxes
>
> - grouping of features that are in close proximity
> (varies based upon the scale of the map). Zoom in to
> see what is in the group.
>
> - quickly locating new features added within the last
> 4 weeks by displaying a pin of a different color.
