From: monkeytoes108 (cruschhaupt@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-01-27 04:07:44 UTC

I've been working on this German Immigrant trail series for a while
now, and I think I've finally got a good idea on how to procede.
There are six identical historical markers commemorating German
immigrants and the founding of New Braunfels. One of each marker is
located in Indianola, Victoria, Cuero, Gonzales, Segiun, and New
Braunfels. A box will be hidden in each city and each set of clues
will start at that marker. Now for the advice part...

While researching, I came across a great and heartbreaking first
hand story of one immigrant who travelled this path. My idea is to
devide the story into six parts and put multiple copies of one part
in the pertinent box. The stamps I'm working on also relate to that
part of the story. The finders could take a copy from each box. Once
you find all six boxes, you will have collected the entire story.

What do you all think about that? Would it be worth it to you?
Would the suspense be too much to take? Would you care about the
story or just getting the stamps?

I thought about leaving a codeword on the inside of the box for the
finder to email me and I would then send that section of story.
Maybe that would be a good backup if all the copies get taken.

I couldn't get to Seguin or NB very often for maintenance, so I was
hoping to find a caretaker for those two.

So much to think about. This idea has haunted me since I found
Silver Eagle's "French Flag" box.

-MonkeyToes
