Hoping for good weather this weekend!

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-01-19 16:06:32 UTC-08:00
Hubby and daughter left today to go to West Texas to
climb Guadalupe Peak. They took a box with them, but
since they will be mostly on National Park land, I
don't know if they will be able to plant it. Thought
about planting it anyway and making it a WOM box, but
decided to think on it for awhile (we're going back in
a few months). I hate to violate the rules we would
like to see changed.

Since they skipped out on us, my son is going to play
hookie from school Friday and he and I are going to do
a little hiking and camping of our own. You never
know where little packages will drop behind us! I'm
hoping to get most of my boxes checked while we are
out prowling too.

Let's all cross our fingers for a good letterboxing
weekend in my neck of the woods, and Y'ALL'S too!

Lucy

Re: [letterboxingtexas] Re: Hoping for good weather this weekend!

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-01-20 10:39:54 UTC-08:00
You can place boxes in National Forests, but not in
National Parks. Go figure.

There are some rules on the letterboxing.org FAQ page.
Go down under Etiquette, Conventions, and Rules and
click on "Are there any rules?". That's a pretty good
start - those rules, plus the National Parks one. I'm
sure others will chime in with rules not found on the
FAQ page, but there are actually very few rules that
courtesy, conservationism, and common sense won't
guide you through.

Don't worry about the rules - they aren't terribly
daunting. Just remember about National Parks - and
actually, there are ways around even that one, and
possibly some folks will tell you how. As for me, I
will regretfully stick to that one too, in hopes that
good behavior on our part will eventually lead the
powers that be to reconsider their ruling.

Lucy

Re: [letterboxingtexas] Hoping for good weather this weekend!

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-01-20 16:31:03 UTC-08:00
I just got off the phone with them. They spent the
day at Roswell (sci-fi freaks they are) and went on to
Carlsbad Nat'l Park where they were watching the
sunset and looking across the border at the mountain.
They were reluctantly getting ready to head back to
wherever they are camping. Over the next two days,
they plan on climbing Guadalupe and going down into
Carlsbad Caverns, among other things.

Baby Bear, when I talked to them, I told them you have
a box in the vicinity and they plan to grab it while
they are there. Unfortunately, they didn't take their
stamps or a logbook, but my daughter is writing a
journal so she'll stamp into it and they'll draw
something to record their visit, if that's okay. I
got a kick out of my husband, who is always willing to
go letterboxing, but doesn't ever suggest it. I told
him Baby Bear had planted a box there, and he turned
to our daughter and told her "Baby Bear planted a box
and we have to get it!" Now see, he was at the
gathering last year, but he hid out in our campsite
the whole time. When I talked to my daughter, I asked
her if she thought he had any idea who Baby Bear is.
Turns out he does, but only from your stamps. We're
gonna get him hooked yet!

I had a terrible time being indoors at my desk today -
it was absolutely gorgeous in East Texas! I'm off
work tomorrow and my son and I are going to hit the
trails!

Lucy

(who is counting the days until March!)
