From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-05-08 21:58:20 UTC-05:00
KC did a great rendition of Ticket to Ride, and I frankly liked her version much better than the Beatles - she made it sound like she had lived it.  Of course, I felt that way about most of the Carpenters' music.  I confess - I am a huge Carpenters fan, but an even bigger John Denver fan.  AND I STILL AM A FAN OF BOTH! 
 
When I first began learning to play the guitar, I broke out mostly on gospel music, but I also got a Carpenters songbook, a John Denver songbook, and an Olivian Newton John book and I learned many of the songs out of all three.  ONJ never quite fit me, although I loved her music.  I eventually got several more JD songbooks and I can sing and play most of his songs by heart, even now.  BUT - when I really want to get soulful with some good music and my guitar, I go Carpenters.  My current genre that I am exploring is folk music and I find I can really wallow down into it too!  The Dreamsicles are awesome...
 
My family had land and a greenhouse/landscaping business as I was growing up and we had a fertilizer salesman who worked our area one day a week.  He set himself up with a little travel trailer on a piece of our land and when he hit town, he would come up to the house with his guitar and he taught me how to play honky-tonk and I taught him how to play JD.  We would jam until the wee hours of the morning when he was in town.  When I was 18 or 19, he got me an audition and a virtually guaranteed gig for me to go and play and travel with an Oklahoma bar band who needed a guitar player and vocalist.  I never had the nerve to do it because I just knew my mother would have a cow.  The issues were 1) I wouldn't be in college, 2) I wouldn't be in church, 3) I WOULD be in bars, and 3) I'd be traveling with a bunch of guys.  That's one of those paths not taken that I DO regret.  I can still play a heck of a Folsom Prison Blues though...
