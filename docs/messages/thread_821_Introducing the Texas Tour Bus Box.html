Introducing the Texas Tour Bus Box

From: TeamKing (teamking@entouch.net) | Date: 2005-04-27 07:48:34 UTC-05:00
Ready for a ride? You've got to find the bus first!

Introducing a new moving box -'Texas Tour Bus' Here's how it will
work:

Like a bus, this box will move around the state and pick up passengers.
I will plant the box in a location. The first person to find it (and
make sure you are prepared to pick it up if you ARE first finder!) will
pick it up, ADD A PASSENGER, and then replant it in a new location.
(You can take it home to place your stamp in the box. Just make sure
you don't keep it too long.) All of the stamps in the box should be of
people. They can be real or fictional. You could make them characters
or historical figures or real people - maybe yourself! You can also
add anything you want with your stamp if you like - FF certificates,
HHs, FF gifts, etc. Once you add your stamp and plant the box, then you
will need to give the clue/location to this Yahoo List group. (Please
don't list your clue in any other location! This is just a special box
for the Texans and should only be reported and discussed on this
letterboxingtexas list. I will add a general one to Atlas Quest if you
want to log your find - but no clue or info will be with it.) I will
place a logbook in the box so you won't need to add one with your stamp
(unless you want to add a small one for your particular passenger.)
Either way, let's keep the name of the box changing so people will be
able to keep up with it by adding your special location or such after
the name Texas Tour Bus.

Here's an example that hopefully will clarify:

I plant the box in Memorial Park in Houston. I call the box 'Texas Tour
Bus - Memorial Park'. I email this list with the clue (format it like
the lbna site - with box name, date, your name, and the clue/location).
Then someone goes and finds it. That person picks up the box and
reports to this list immediately that they picked it up. (we don't want
everyone out hunting around for it and it's already been picked up!)
They then add a new stamp to the box and go out and plant the box in
another location. (Maybe this person is going to Dallas for the weekend
and they decided to plant it up there or somewhere in between.) And on
it goes. After a while, the box should be full of stamps. I thought
maybe we could let it float around until the next gathering and then
whoever has it can bring it to the gathering and the placers can get
their stamps back and do whatever they want with them. Also note that
the box itself will be a big Tupperware big enough to hold plenty of
stamps, but perhaps a bit trickier to hide.

It's just a way to add some variety and maybe get a box out to some of
the areas of the state and boxers that don't get as much traffic as we
do in Houston. Bit of a race, bit of a surprise, opportunity to get
some more finds. BUT, you've got to be the FIRST to get to it, or you
don't get the box! :)

If any other issues come up, we will just resolve along the way.

One other thing there are TWO box stamps (non-passengers) AND neither
were done by TeamKing. I recruited 2 of my favorite carvers to do them
so hope you will enjoy.

Sound good? I'll be listing the first 'stop' within the next 2 wks so
keep a lookout. From there, who knows where it will end up!

TeamKing
