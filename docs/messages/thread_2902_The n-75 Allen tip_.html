RE: [letterboxingtexas] Re: The n-75 Allen tip.

From: Kirbert (PalmK@nettally.com) | Date: 2007-07-04 10:52:46 UTC-04:00
Maribeth Jagger wrote:

> My plan now is to make the rounds and check on which of my boxes may need replacing, retiring or repairing after the latest floods.

My "Dr. Barr's Table" letterbox in Lakeway I know for a fact is 25
feet under water right now. However, I had expected it to be 25 feet
under water every now and then, so it is chained down and hopefully
watertight. A geocacher in the area is going to check on it for me
when the level of Lake Travis gets back down around 670 or so.

He got really lucky with his nearby geocache. I had warned him that
it gets deep here, but I guess he decided not to do anything about
it. When he heard the lake level was rising (the level in Lake
Travis has been known to rise 20 feet in 12 hours, amazing for a lake
that's that big) he dashed out there and found he couldn't get
anywhere near it, the GZ was already under many feet of water. Then
he spotted his ammo can floating near shore and managed to retrieve
it. Being an ammo can, it was watertight and everything inside was
dry.

Last time I was out there, last September, it was so dry that the
nearby arm of Lake Travis was nothing but a rivulet among the dried
mud. All the floating boat docks were sitting on the rocks.

-- Kirbert
