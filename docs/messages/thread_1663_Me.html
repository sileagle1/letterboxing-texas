About Chicago Style Hotdogs (I am from Chicago) let me share a message I just sent the Friday Night Group.
 

To The Friday Night Crew.

Last Friday night Martin (WB5LJO) suggested a restaurant to me. Moni took me there today for Father's Day. I liked it, so much so that it is worth reporting to you-all.

The place?

Chicago's Street Food.

2400 N. Mesa St, Ste D.

NO WEB SITE - but I have a copy of the menu. Maybe Moni should talk them into some web work..... Or maybe GOOGLE just didn't find them. Anyway......

Upon entering the door, the initial search is always for the right signs. They are there, the unlicensed possession bla bla bla..... Good to Go. There is a bar of a sorts in there, but no 51-% signs in sight. So we continued on in.

The atmosphere - it's as if you were sitting on the patio of a sidewalk caf inside the city. Lit, but dim, like the sun isn't coming down between the buildings. The music wasn't loud, but was noticeable. Blues and Jazz, what Chicago music is known for.

For an appetizer, we each started out with a CUP of Chile. CUP - is a misnomer. It is a small BOWL, 6" in diameter and 3" deep. I like Chile, glad I didn't ask for the BOWL! Beef, pork, and ground beef with a touch of beans. Smothered in Cheddar cheese and chopped raw onion. Just like I remember it from Chicago.

For the main course, Moni had the Dick ButKus Burger, (Famous Chicago Bear player

http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dick_Butkus) Big slab of ground beef, with caramelized onions and cheese melted on top, served on a Chicago style Kaiser roll. Fries or a house salad on the side for no extra charge. The onions and cheese was just like I remember it.

For my main course, I was tempted to have the Wrigley Field Hot Dog. It is named after the baseball field where the Chicago Cubs play (

http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wrigley_Field). But I was hungry, and Moni pointed out that we had hot dogs Friday night. So I picked the Hancock Italian Beef sandwich. It is named after the John Hancock Center (http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Hancock_Center) in downtown Chicago. It was as good as ANY Roast Beef Sandwich I ever had in Chicago! The beef was a little dry, but they offer it dipped. It was served with a small dish of ajure sauce. I poured some of that over the beef and it was perfect. Being a cheater, I took the French Fries. Was I ever GLAD! Crispy fried and VERY HOT! A little salt and catsup and it was like when I was a kid!

The Wrigley Field Hot Dog sounds like the AUTHENTIC version, Vienna Sausage, and all the right fixings, including the special salt, sport peppers and pickle spear. NEXT TIME - THE WRIGLEY FIELD HOT DOG FOR ME !!!!!

For desert we split a piece of cheese cake. Even that was good, lots of cream-cheese, and just sweet enough with a nice firm graham cracker crust.

Truth, the only bad thing in the whole place was the coffee. Well - coffee can be bad anywhere. New kid - didn't know the machine. But they didn't charge us for it either.

I was so impressed that I had a 30 minute chat with the manager after we ate. The discussion with the manager turned in to a "Do you remember this place in Chicago" game. The man has been there, or he studies well. He welcomes the evening business. If we have a group of more than 4-6, he says call ahead a few hours before and he will reserve us a place.

I recommend we go there some Friday night. How about - NEXT Friday night? It will likely be Mike-Moni-Reiner-Paul. Anyone else it welcome, but that is the usual 3rd Friday group.

Oh - and best of all - Those of you who know Freddie - KC5WPH.... His daughter works there!

What say you - Friday night crew?

 
Y-all need to visit El Paso now!
 
Mike - KD9KC
