Thoughts on a fall event

From: Christine Borgfeld (christine@borgfeld.net) | Date: 2013-01-05 09:55:16 UTC-06:00

I’ve started working on ideas for an event in the fall.  For this year NLW suggested Lake Houston Park for the location.  Wry Me and I visited it before the holidays and it looks very promising.  It’s a former state park now run by the city of Houston.  They have added an entire new section and completely moved the entrance.  It’s a little strange because the park is split in half over a creek, and to drive on the other side of the park you have to leave and come in the old entrance on the other side.  The trails are nice, they have some new cabins, and the dining hall was being renovated when we were there.  It has tent campsites (including two group camp sites), but we didn’t see any facilities for RVs. 

 

For the last two years I scheduled events on the 3rd weekend of October.  This seems to work out well for the weather so I’m leaning toward the same time if there are no other big standing conflicts? Starting on Friday afternoon seemed to work out well for everyone so I’ll do that again for those who can make it early.  Any thoughts or feedback before I make reservations?

 

Regards,

From: Christine Borgfeld (christine@borgfeld.net) | Date: 2013-01-05 15:38:28 UTC-06:00

How many people are interested in tent camping?  One option is to get a group campsite.  The standard campsites there are also set up well for group camping.  There are 3 areas, each set up in a circle.  For each area you park outside of it and walk into the clearing.  The sites are arranged around the outside of a circle and in the middle is a covered platform with water (and a big stainless steel wash basin), and it has a ring of benches nearby.  I think this was a girl scout camp long ago so that would explain the way it’s set up.  By the way, it looks like there was only one set of power outlets in the middle of the whole camping area.   

 

If anyone has questions about the facilities, let me know.  We explored them pretty well.
