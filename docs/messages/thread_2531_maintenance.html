From: janet clark (jayceeinbedford@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-01-21 17:21:58 UTC-08:00
Well, we tried to LB today. Last night Gary and I bought bikes at WalMart so that we can ride with Cassandra. He blew a tire putting air in (the hose thing wouldn't come off the stem). So, we went back to the house (we had rode to the gas station). Cassie and I were sitting there wondering what he was doing and he drives off. I yell, "Where are you going?" and he says that he is going to buy a tube.
 
About 10 minutes later (That's the limit on my bum sitting on a curb), I tell Cassandra to go see if Daddy left the door unlocked. He hadn't. And for some reason he can't hear his phone ring either. So, an hour later he calls to say he's on his way home. I told him he locked us out, and of course he says, "Didn't you have your keys?" Now why would I need my keys to go bike riding? I already had a full fanny pack (gotta have snacks).
 
So, Cassie and I did ride around the apartments while we waited for him. Mind you I haven't been on a bike since before she was born (she even asked if I knew how to ride). I think I'm bow legged just from that hour.
 
That was my letterboxing adventure for the day. I didn't think y'all would want me to hide a box at Grubbs Nissan. So, I didn't.

Not to be a jerk - but the wife and I NEVER go out without the right stuff to be self-sufficient.  We all hope the worst never happens.  Then when it does, we are double-hung.
 
Suppose we did exactly what you did.  I have the keys.  Now - I get hit by a car while riding.  Do you expect ANYONE to have enough presence of mind to remove the keys from my pocket before the ambulance leaves?  Not my wife. 
 
I think I would, but I am also practiced in stressful situations enough that I will kick the weapon out of the hands of the enemy I just killed while scanning for another target. 
 
I hope none of you EVER get experienced enough to keep your head that clear.  So carry extra keys.  Even if it is only a door key tied to your shoe.  Or tucked under the seat of your bike.
