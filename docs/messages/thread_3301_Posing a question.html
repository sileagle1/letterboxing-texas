Naaa....  you will use my rifle.  It hardly ever misses.  And I will let you shoot some store-bought ammo.  Then some of my hand-loads.
 
The idea is that each and every round must be made exactly the same as the one before.
 
Commercial ammo is made using a set volume to measure the powder.  The load is close.  But not perfect.  Some times the powder is a little tighter or looser packed.  Much like stacking firewood instead of just randomly tossing it on a pile.
 
Thinking of the wood pile - regardless of how it is stacked, the wood weighs the same.
 
Gun powder is the same.  The VOLUME may be more or less depending on how it is stacked.  But the weight is the weight.
 
So when I load match ammo, I have a very accurate scale - 0.1 gr accurate.  (1/70,000th of a pound).  I hand weigh each powder charge before dumping it into the empty shell.
 
I buy the primers in lots of 5000.  That way they are all made from the same compound mix and fire the same.  Same with the bullets.
 
At 600 yards, a difference of just 50 feet per second of velocity will raise or lower the bullet point of impact about 4 inches up or down.  1/70,000 of a pound of powder is about 50 feet/second of velocity.
 
And now you know WAY MORE than you ever wanted to know about competition ammo.  There is much more, but we will save that for a time when you come for lessons.  I could have you out-shooting the Border Patrol Explorers in one day.  If you can hold the rifle up, the rest is all a mental exercise.
