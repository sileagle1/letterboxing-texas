Re: Heading out to west TX..

From: Boots Tex (redbarn@eastex.net) | Date: 2013-07-01 03:26:18 UTC
We just got back today from a 7 day stay at Davis Mountains SP. We had grandkids and then relatives from Midland, so didn't do a lot of letterboxing, but did find a few. Silver Eagle, you are correct that your two boxes in that park were apparently destroyed by fire. We found a piece of one of the boxes. "Buffalo Soldiers" by El Lobo and "Texas Flag" by Azroadie are alive and well and worth finding. Astro D's box "She Really does Wear Prada" at Valentine was safe and dry and definitely a must find. It is in need of a new box as three of the four tabs are missing from the lock 'n lock box and it is pretty brittle. I'm sure Astro D would appreciate the maintenance. The other box in Valentine, "Be My Valentine" by CW Sun Seeker is alive and well. "Texas Celestial Series #1 - Jupiter" by Star Seeker is alive and well. The box was broken and we replaced it with a new L & L. It's a great box and is very near the McDonald Observatory, which is also worth a visit. If you go down toward Del Rio, check out El Lobo's "No Country for Old Letterboxers" in Sanderson. It was placed in September 2009 and we found it yesterday. We were first finders. It's sort of an historic box. It is the only box in Terrell County, which was the last remaining county in Texas without a letterbox. Unforrtunately, Viewfinder, we didn't get to look for your box in Marfa. The only time we got down there, we were with family who were not letterboxers and were unsympathetic to our desire to look for it. Some people just don't understand. I intended to carve stamps and place a couple of boxes, but we were so busy sightseeing that I wasn't even able to do that. I know, I should have planned ahead, but what can I say?

Boots Tex

From: Boots Tex (redbarn@eastex.net) | Date: 2013-07-01 14:15:16 UTC
The weather was great, warm to hot during the day, cool at night. This is a desert environment. We had some rain late in the evening on 3 or 4 or the 7 nights we were there. Some mosquitos in the evening, but not bad, use a little Off. Javelinas were around the campsite early in the morning sometimes, and they will get into the trash if if's in their reach, but they are park beasts, used to people and not around during the day. Bears are present in Big Bend, but not a problem at Fort Davis. Always be aware of snakes, but not normally in camping areas, just normal precautions should be taken. We stayed in a travel trailer, so had a full hookup site, which included sewer connection and cable (roughing it). Balmorhea is about 40 miles away and wasn't overly crowded. We had a great time.
