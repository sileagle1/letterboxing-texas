From: the carrascos (a.carrasco@earthlink.net) | Date: 2008-01-29 09:03:52 UTC-06:00
I found my favorite box I've never found this past weekend.  Dewberry and I ran away from home for the weekend for a trip to go letterboxing in Austin WITH NO KIDS.  We decided to look for 360 bridge, a box by the Brown Knight which has been around for a long time.  The hike led us up the cliffs to a spectacular view of the bridge and the river.  Then on through the trees where the smell of cedar trees after 2 weeks of Houston rain was oh so good for the soul.  We searched and searched, but never found the box.  What we did find were some guys hitting golf balls off the cliffs into the river, a lovely walk in good company, and a hidden little part of Austin I had never seen before.  It was a perfect letterboxing outing.  I think it is just a good reminder that finding the box is a treat, but not finding the box most certainly does NOT mean that is was a "wasted" trip.  MO5

EASY.  This one.

When you read the clues - think of this data - which you might not know - and I DID NOT know.  There are TWO Nature Loops up there, separated by a 2-mile hike.  Each has the same signs.

You mean there is more than one?  <grin>
 
We will be back - Albuquerque has a big dog show every year.  And I am looking forward to the hike!
