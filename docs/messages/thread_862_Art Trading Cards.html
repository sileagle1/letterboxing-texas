Well Lucy, it's simple.
Artists make small pieces of art (usually flat) about the size of a
playing card. They are usually traded with other ATC'ers. On the back,
there is usually a little bit of info about the artist.

Common themes are collages, pen drawings, stamps (that's what are LB
info'er does) and a lot of other things. It's endless what you can do.

type of art.

Hi,

Yes, ATCs are like art on a trading card. They're small works of art,
either originals or reproductions, and the format is usually very
small... 3" x 5" or smaller, with most being 2.5" x 3.5" like baseball
cards, Magic cards, and so on.

I finally fixed my FTP problem and updated my webpage:
http://www.aisling.net/gallery/gallery-cards.htm

All eight (so far) of my May 2005 ATCs can be printed, full size, from
links on that page, too. Most of them are 3" x 5" when you print them
(at 150 dpi) because I'm used to swapping that size at RedDog Scot's
swaps. However, you can adjust the scale so that they'll fit on a
smaller format card as well.

If I find a LB that's also a geocaching box (with treats in it), I
often leave a few ATCs as small gifts.

Cheerfully,
Ais
