I was just wondering if I should remind people that it is out there!!  I haven't heard otherwise.  Here are the clues in case you lost them:
 
-----

"Rumor spreadin' 'round in that Texas town, 'bout that shack outside La Grange - you know what I'm talkin' about.
Just let me know if you wanna go, to that home out on the range.
They gotta lotta nice girls. ...
Well, I hear it's fine if you got the time, and the ten to get yourself in.
And I hear it's tight most ev'ry night, but now I might be mistaken.
Have mercy."

If you can guess who's hit song this is then you have a clue as to who the newest passenger is!
 
Directions: La Grange is west of Houston and can be reached by travelling I-10 to the La Grange exit, Hwy 77 and turning north.

Once in La Grange you will want to get on Business 71 aka Travis street. You will take this through downtown, passing the courthouse (on the corner of Main and Travis). Just past Main, but before the bridge you will see Boss' House of Steaks on the right and River Park Car Wash on the left. Also on the Left there is a sign for river access. Take the road towards the river.
 
Park in the parking lot (do not go down towards the boat ramp).  Go away from the river to the cement support beam for the bridge.  Facing the beam look to your left and you will see a row of wooden posts.  Follow the posts counting 19 posts.  Now continue around to the left and count 11 more.  You should be in fromt of a large tree with vines wrapped around it.  Box is wedge up in the hole in the base of the tree.  It is wedged up and to the left.
 
Good luck!!
 
P.S.  You can also find the first 2 boxes of  our new series on weather, You Are My Sunshine and Raindrops Keep Falling on My Head while you are there.

Texas Tour Bus

From: (kacmbc@aol.com) | Date: 2005-09-18 17:55:52 UTC-04:00
Since gas prices have been so high lately, members of the bus thought of cost effective ways to reduce their fuel expenses.  They came up with a great plan!  Off they headed to Plano, Texas where Lance Armstrong was recruited as the next member on the bus.  With his ability to power a bike, certainly the bus would be able to draft off of him, saving energy and gas to boot!  Lance agreed, but only after stopping at the State Fair of Texas grounds in Dallas, to pick up Big Tex ( thanks to the Frye team!!)  Lance led the bus through Central Texas and when they hit Meridian, everyone on board agreed it was time to take a break. 
 
The letterbox is hanging out at Meridian State Park, near Meridian, Texas. 
 
Directions to the park:
The park is located about 3 miles southwest of Meridian off State Highway 22. Take Hwy 174 from Cleburne, Hwy 144 from Glen Rose or Hwy 6 from Waco, then west on Hwy 22 and proceed to the park entrance. Once you have paid the entry fee, take the 1 1/2 mile road towards the left, to the Shinnery Ridge Hiking Trail.  ( there is a parking area/turn around point on the left side at the dead end of the road).   
 
Clues to the Letterbox:
Park and find the small sign stating "Shinnery Ridge Trail".  To the right, you will see a larger wooden sign with information about the flora/fauna of the area. The trail starts  to the right, behind this sign.  From here, take 56 steps on the trail until the trail turns into a flat, rocky surface ( different from the rocky "steps" you will encounter when you first start).  From this point, count 143 more steps ( there are lots of big cypress/juniper trees on both sides of the trail) until the trail will curve to the right.  From this point, take another 33 steps.  Stop, and to your right, you will see a large dead tree about 15 steps off the trail.  Behind the dead tree is another large Juniper/Cypress tree.  The Texas Tour Bus is hiding behind this tree buried under rocks and branches!
 
While at the park, make sure you stop by and see Silver Eagle's "Grinch" and Baby Bear's "Soaking up Texas" letterboxes!

All you folks in DFW… get moving!  The Bus is in the station and ready for a new passenger!  It is placed right next to one of my other boxes (which I noticed is missing – dangit!).  Check the clues for Angry Trees in North Richland Hills.  It is at Cross Timbers Park.  I am having computer difficulty and cannot access my previous directions to the box, but to the best of my recollection this is how you get there:

 

If there’s a discrepancy, go with the clues of lb.org…

 

Go to Cross Timbers Park on Starnes (west of Smithfield) and park as close to the restrooms as possible.  Take the trail right behind the restrooms. At the first fork, go right.  The path will fork again and you will continue straight (right).  There are a couple little paths that enter the main trail, but stay on the main trail.  It will start to curve left and then right again.  Once you are in the middle of the curve, stop.  You should be able to see the low u-shaped branch, but don’t go under it.  Look to your right.  About 10-15 ft off the trail is a 3 trunked tree with the middle trunk tied in a knot.  The Bus is behind the tree BEHIND the knotted tree and is covered with leaves.  My other letterbox had been in the tree in front, but the Bus is in the tree BEHIND the knotted tree.

 

Good luck and go fast! This is an awesome box.

The Texas tour bus is on the move again. They talked about going
north, but knew that there were still some boxers that wanted to see
their wonderful faces in this area, so they landed in Keller! Dan
Rather is on board now, he didn't have much going on so decided to
take a ride! His birthday is Halloween which maybe explains the
craziness that surrounds him.

To find the bus take Keller Parkway (1709) to Keller Smithfield Road
and go south for about 1/2 mile. Turn right into Keller Smithfield
Activity Node. Park at the south end of the parking lot. On the
west side, just off the sidewalk, you will see a path between two
large trees, one on each side. Follow the "s" shaped path and stop
where the path straightens. You should see the hike/bike trail in
the distance ahead. From this point (at the end of the "s") count
about 16 steps. On the left side and just off the trail there will
be a cedar bush entwined with an oak twig and some vines. The bus is
under this cedar bush! To help make sure you are in the right spot,
at about 13 steps there is an oak tree about 1 1/2" diameter just on
the left edge of the trail.

Good Luck!
