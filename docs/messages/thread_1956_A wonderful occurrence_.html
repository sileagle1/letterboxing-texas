A wonderful occurrence!

From: Michael P. Olbrisch (mike2004@elp.rr.com) | Date: 2006-06-29 23:57:46 UTC

Today I was called upon to demonstrate my marksmanship abilities in a most
satisfying way.

In a power plant, 4-legged and winged creatures can cause all sorts of
problems. Get across the wrong wires in the switchgear, and the ensuing
ball of plasma will destroy anything it touches. One of my additional
duties is the careful removal of these creatures. The plant allowed me to
select an air rifle for the task.

Over the last few days we have had a Golden Eagle living in the plant. We
knew it was there, and were avoiding getting too close. But this morning it
was discovered that while alive, it was not moving. Closer inspection
revealed that it had a severely broken leg.

I didn't know this development when there was a knock on my door at 1330. I
was requested to grab my air rifle and jump on an ATV. I did so.

I know what you are thinking. Me too. And I didn't know how to tell them I
wouldn't kill it. WRONG!

I was requested to kill a pigeon and feed it to the eagle. Killing a pigeon
- easy. I selected a young one, hoping it was tender.

I collected up my kill, put it in the ATV, and headed out to the eagle.

When I got there, the eagle was sitting on one leg right on the edge of the
pond. I removed my safety gear and set the air rifle down so to not scare
it. I do not believe I needed to worry. I approached the eagle slowly. I
held the dead pigeon in front of me and spoke in soothing tones while
wiggling it. At about 6 feet I knelt down. I looked the eagle in the eyes.
I saw no fear at all. I laid the pigeon on the ground, then shook it so it
would flop around like it was wounded. Then I tossed it to the eagle's
feet. After a long look at me, it began to eat. And I slowly backed away.

The raptor rescue league was called. They captured the eagle with a large
net. The last things I saw the eagle do was to grab the pigeon remains, and
then turn to look at me. I swear - in my mind I heard it say THANK YOU.

I gathered up my gear, put the rifle at sling-arms, and went back to my
shop.

Good luck Mr. Golden Eagle.

The rest of the day I felt real good.
