From: Mike Olbrisch (mike-2007@elp.rr.com) | Date: 2007-09-06 11:44:34 UTC
Welcome to the group.  I am Mike in El Paso, the lone far-west Texas Letterboxer.
 
I have fond memories of Shreveport, for the few short hours that it was.
 
I had been on special assignment to Redstone Arsenal in Alabama for about 45 days when Gulf War-I started on 2 Aug 1990.  Soldiers from my unit had deployed by 6 aug 1990.  I was ordered to complete my present mission before returning.
 
I had driven from Ft. Bliss to Redstone Arsenal, so on 1 Oct 1990 I loaded up my gear.  Then drove to the Huntsville airport to pick up my wife.  Knowing I only had a little time before deploying into combat - she flew down to make the trip back with me.
 
Being a soldier meant being broke.  So the first night we made Vicksburg MS, parked the van on the east side of the Mississippi River, and with a loaded gun under my hand we caught a few hours sleep in the van.
 
Next day was a hard push, I wanted to make Dublin Texas - when a retired Warrant Officer and friend would put us up overnight.  But about Shreveport we got hungry. 
 
Somewhere on the west side of town, on the north side of I-20, we found a place to eat.  I wish I could remember the name.  We selected cheap things from the menu and placed our order.
 
Soon the food started to arrive.  But it was not what we ordered.  I said so to the waitress.  She called the manager.  He explained to us that he noticed the military sticker and gear in the van, and the waitress told him we were very price conscious.  So he re-placed our order.  On him.  I am still speechless today.
 
My friends tell me I was home for two weeks before leaving again.  I remember about two days of it.  All to soon I was in it.  Although assigned to an Ordinance Corps unit, I ended up seeing combat with 24th ID (mech) of the 18th Airborne Corp.  I guess I was good enough - I made it home.
 
Anyway - welcome to the group.  Come visit El Paso - 8 Letterboxes served.
 

Mike - KD9KC.
M.A.R.I.E.
El Paso, Tx.

From: Mike Olbrisch (mike-2007@elp.rr.com) | Date: 2007-09-07 11:49:22 UTC
It was a tough trip.  How do you act normal when you know what you are headed for is not normal.  On top of that - my wife knows me.  I never said "Go do that".  I always said "Follow me, we are going to do that".  You lead from the front.  She knew what was going to happen, just as I some nights sit and cry (yes - some men do cry) for my son James.  Ya gotta know that I know every detail of what he does, because I did it myself.  We talk about the same things now that I thought about when I was taking my turn.  Only he gets newer and neater toys now.
 
The worst of it on the home front was the SCUD that hit the barracks in Saudi.  My wife saw the reports on TV, and saw them dragging out a body that looked like me.  I was far north by that time, but I had been living there weeks earlier.  It was a few days before she got word that I was safe.  God bless whoever called her, I still don't know who it was.  I cannot imagine what she went through.  She never talks about it, my son told me.
 
I understand your Papa.  I feel it too.
 
If you get the chance, read Into the Storm by Tom Clancy and Fred Franks.  I was with the 18th Airborne Corps, 24th Infantry division - mechanized.  My vehicle was destroyed just outside the city limits in the fight with the republican guard at As Salman.  I would have lost my legs if it wasn't for the extra sand bags we packed in.  That is a story in itself.  <grin>
 
Well - y-all have a great day.  And whenever you get the chance, THANK A SOLDIER.  Today's soldiers join KNOWING they will fight.  And the hardships never get easier.
 
Mike.
