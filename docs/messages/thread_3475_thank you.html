From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-04-07 23:19:28 UTC-05:00
I think I can safely say your thanks is appreciated by all who have planted, BUT - once those boxes are out there, every person who visits them plays a big role in their ongoing success, so that every box you visit with care owes a little more longevity to YOU.  That's one of the things I love about letterboxing - it isn't all about the planter OR the finder OR the box OR the journey - it's about the whole picture.  Through a box, a planter shares something they find significant with others who follow to find the box and every time someone finds the box, they leave something of themselves behind and take a little something of the box with them.  It's a way for us to bind ourselves together and to share an experience without having to be there at the same time.  A box is a dynamic little capsule of history and stories and it changes every time someone opens the lid.
 
The little things matter - they really do.  Not too long ago, Jim found one of my boxes and the container was broken and it was in disarray.  He had just enough supplies on him to fix it until I could get to it.  He wrote me about it and I thought I would make it out that day to fix it because there was a big storm coming that night and I wanted the box to be tucked in properly.  As it happens, I got very sick that day and simply couldn't get out there to take care of it, and I was sick enough it never occurred to me to ask Fearless Fox to go tend it (he's a most excellent LSS who WOULD do something like that in a pinch!).  It was several days before I was well enough to even think about my poor box, but I did finally go check it and it was safe and sound.  I got it into a new container and spiffied it up a little and it's happily hiding for its next finder.  If Jim had not had stuff to patch it up, it would NOT have survived the storm that came.  (Thanks Jim).
