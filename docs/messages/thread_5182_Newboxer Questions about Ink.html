Sorry if this has been asked before. I searched the board and found lots of great info about inks, but I still have some questions.

There are many inks that will work. Many boxers love and prefer the Marvy markers. I have tried them before and they are nice. Others use ink pads, cat eyes, or (sometimes) chalks. I personally use a set of mini Stampin' Up inkpads - I bought a color family just for boxing. I also have a set of SU markers I keep in the bag for detailed coloring. I've never had a problem with them fading or running. Enjoy and we hope to see you on the trail!

From: Bonnie Lind (bonnielind1@hotmail.com) | Date: 2013-04-13 15:01:03 UTC-05:00
Hey, Campfire Critters Mom,
 
Here is my take on inks:  yes, the colorbox pigment inks take too long to dry.  I don’t like them for letterboxing, but there are those who do.  I like and use the chalk inks.  I bought some in a “que,” about 5-6 colors in a stack.  It is convenient for letterboxing.  I also use Memento dye inks.  They dry fast and are colorful.  The ink I really like to use is Versafine.  It is water resistant (permanent), juicy and will last a long time.  I recommend picking up at least a black Versafine pad.  I don’t care for it when folks use metallic ink on letterbox stamps. I am sure it looks pretty in their logbooks, but it mucks up my inks.  There, I said it.  BTW, Michaels has all their inks half off today. 
 
I like using my Stampin Up markers.  I don’t worry if they are permanent.  I’ve been using the same Stampin up markers for years and they are still in great shape.  I have SU markers for the trail and SU markers for home use and I don’t mix them.
 
Please let me know if I can help in any other way.

From: Christine Borgfeld (christine@borgfeld.net) | Date: 2013-04-14 09:38:25 UTC-05:00

Hi Campfire Critters.  Welcome to the group. I was out boxing the last couple of days and missed your introduction.  I used colorbox inks for quite a while and you can use them if you want.  They have some great colors.  The only risk you have is that they will leave some color in the facing page of your logbook when you close the pages.  I usually solved this by just keeping some loose sheets of paper with me to put between the pages.  Or you can just stamp on one side of your pages and not worry about it.  I did have one color (I think it was a dark red) of colorbox ink that never seemed to dry completely, but all the others were usable.    

 

Personally I love pigment inks.  I’ve switched to buying the Tsukineko brand inks which all seem to dry faster. 

 

As for markers I’m sure you can use the Stampin Up markers. 

 

Best Regards
