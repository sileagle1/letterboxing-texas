I still train ROTC cadets.  And I still put a hurt on them.  May 1st will start my 12th year of Army retirement.  I would give up this wonderful job I have and go back in the army if they would take me.  They will not.  But I think I could still put a hurt on the young troopers.

where she was with him to the Air Force academy where she is a nurse.

> The former Marine made me think of you.

A lot of my friends tell me that.

> I thought they did a good job of the movie. Brought back a
> lot of painful memories of that day though.

Painful memories. Want to see me bawl like a baby? Sit down with me
and watch Black Hawk Down. They were my friends. Mike Durant wrote a
book... In the Company of Heros. I have a personally autographed copy
on my shelf. I have not read it. Not sure I can. He lives because of
Sgts Gordon and Shurgart, two heros who died defending Mike until they
were killed. Needless deaths except for Klinton - wouldn't give them
the equipment needed to complete the mission he gave them. It took the
Pakistani military (with the right equipment) to save them.

Why wasn't I there? I was tied up on a counter-narcotics mission to
the south. Curse that mission, I should have been with the others. I
might have made a difference.

That is the difference between me and normal people I guess. No, I
don't consider myself normal. Normal people looked at flt 93 and said
to themselves "I am so glad I wasn't on that plane". I think to myself
I wish I had been on that plane. I could have made a difference. What
could they have done to me with a box cutter? A little pain, a little
blood.... but a determined attack would prevail. It had to, or die.

Ah-well - see.... you got me started. Some days I regret retiring.
Should have stuck it out.

> Have a good day,

Are you kidding. First they give me a power plant to play with. Then
they pay me lots of $$$$ to play with it. Then the send a bunch of
great people to play along with me. And I don't have to leap out of
aircraft or get shot at anymore. In the army overtime was expected and
not compensated for. Double-time meant a run at 180 steps a minute.
In this job, double-time means $60/ hr. How could I possible have a
BAD day?

You take care.

Mike.
