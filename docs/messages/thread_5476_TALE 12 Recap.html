TALE 12 Recap

From: (sileagle1@yahoo.com) | Date: 2015-03-10 17:40:05 UTC-07:00

Well another TALE has come and gone and I think this was one of the biggest yet.  Looking on the poster there are almost 60 stamps, so I figure there were at least 75 or 80 people attending.  And of those people, 2 couples were from California (Grumpy Grinch, DoubleSaj & Old Blue), 1 person from New Mexico (long time attendee Lionsmane), 1 person from Illinois (FungusWoman), and 1 couple from Wisconsin (Wisconsin Hiker and Martini Man back again).  There were also several kids, which is great to see for the future of our hobby.  I met a nice young man named Trail Ripper (Puddle Splasher's grandson) who was full of enthusiasm, Cutie Cat and Corvette Dude (children of PhotoPam), and Pink Cookie, who is growing so fast I hardly recognized her.  Even my son, Clueless Collector, showed up for the first time in years.  There were a lot of creative letterboxes, too.  So many I will have to go back for a full day to find them all.  And there were pranks, all against Baby Bear and me, though one of them was instigated by me.  The letterbox "Don't Mess With TX Eagles" was a prank against me and I have posted the clues on AQ with some background information along with the original clues (with some clarification) as written by the devious Wisconsin Hiker & Martini Man.  The letterbox "Everything Is Bigger" was a prank against Baby Bear and I think he has also posted the clues on AQ.  The final prank against Baby Bear was a collusion between Mosaic Butterfly and myself during the raffle to make sure he got the IRA boxers.  He was a good sport and even modeled for us (though not as well as GG did 2 years ago).

I am grateful so many showed up and contributed to the success of the Event, and happy nobody was injured this year.  I think everyone had fun, and Grumpy Grinch blew me away when they told me it was the best event they had ever been to in 12 years of letterboxing.  When I asked why, they said it was mainly because of all the nice people who welcomed them and went out of their way to make the day special.  I couldn't have said it better.  Thanks, y'all!

Silver Eagle

From: (candj1969@yahoo.com) | Date: 2015-03-10 18:42:12 UTC-07:00

You guys always host a great event, and they are always getting better.  I think you are getting a national reputation, well deserved.  It's nice to see that Texas is becoming a destination for letterboxing.  As you might know, we are still in the park.  We found some letterboxes Sunday, but it rained all day Monday.  We got out early this morning and some of the trails were very wet, had to wade through some puddles.  Puddle Splasher would have loved it!  We found all of the boxes on our side of Onion Creek (at least all the ones we knew about), but due to yesterday's rain, the creek was way up and the rushing falls made crossing too dangerous.  Even approaching from behind the TPWD offices wasn't a good idea because the trails were a muddy mess.  We tried to stay another day, but the park was booked up because of SXSW and Rodeo Austin, so we got kicked out and are leaving for home in the morning.  I guess we'll have to come back another day as well, but that's not all bad;  there are many boxes in Austin we need to find.  Keep letterboxing weird!

From: (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2015-03-12 13:45:27 UTC-07:00

We also would like to thank all you Texans for making us feel so welcome again this year!  Once again we really enjoyed the chance to get together with some of you for a Friday night meal (great location, food AND service!).  Saturday we socialized and then hit the trails.  Luckily Martini Man's new hat with the snake emblem did the trick and they left him alone this year!  Dusty Sandstorm also presented him with a special box grabbing tool to reduce his chances of getting bit and it worked great.  This meant we were actually able to attend the evening activities including the raffle, fashion show and night boxing.  Lots of fun!  We returned to the park on Sunday even though the forecast was not good.  The rain held off for a while and we met several other groups out on the trail.  Even though the rain did eventually arrive, we were prepared with umbrellas and backpack seats, so we were able to continue boxing until 6 pm.  We couldn't quite get all the boxes, but we made a valiant attempt.


Special thanks to Bell Motel, Silver Eagle and Baby Bear for organizing and hosting the Friday & Saturday events and to everyone for your Texas hospitality (and letterboxes)!

Wisconsin Hiker
