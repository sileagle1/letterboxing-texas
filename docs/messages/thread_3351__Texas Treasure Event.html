From: Vicki Stecher (stecher@runbox.com) | Date: 2008-02-22 14:42:06 UTC-06:00
I wish I had a good story!! I was roller skating at a birthday party and I had been there all of ten minutes. I was a speed skater in highschool and I was reliving my youth...I was flying as fast as those old rental skates and my almost 40 year old butt would let me when a young boy there grabbed my shirt. He was just being funny and had no idea what the result would be,  ...which was me doing something similar to a triple salchow upside backwards and landing on my elbow. The kid felt terrible. (It was a private party, we all knew each other.) I didn't want him to know how badly I was hurt.
 
I knew instantly it was broken but had signed up to paint faces at the party...so I made it to a bench seat and had someone bring me paints...painted with my right hand and kept on my party face. It was funny though, as the arm swelled my face painting got worse and worse...not that the kids minded but it had my husband and I in hysterics later that evening... by the end of the party the butterflies were looking like ink blots.
 
Anyway, we went to the emergency room when the party was over and they said it was too swollen to cast. So I got this fiberglass thing and the promise of a cast on Monday... Then on Monday the orthopedist said, "Oh, no cast. We don't cast elbows...blah, blah, blah. I guess they want it to move which got me a great prescription to Vicodin to deal with the pain part.
 
So I'll probably be a vicodin junkie when this is all over with because I feel soooo good after I've had one...and I know the MINUTE it wears off. But when I'm in the good stage I can prop my arm up with a pillow and type, which has been nice since it is my only contact with the outside world right now.
 
I get it xrayed again in 3 weeks to see where we are at. There are lots of good things...not my right arm, didn't need surgery/pins, etc. etc. And the boy doesn't know that he broke my arm since I stayed the rest of the party. He thinks I fell some other time, which is good. He felt bad enough just making me fall, apologized about a million times.
 
The best part is that I can't change a diaper. So Stinky Monkey is being fully serviced by my  husband, who is quickly earning his sainthood.
 
Ladybird Beetlebug
