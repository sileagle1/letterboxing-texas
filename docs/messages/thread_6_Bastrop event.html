That's great you will be able to make it. I hope to
call Bastrop today and reserve the Group Picnic Pavilion,
then I will make the official announcement of the event.
I haven't thought of all the details yet but was roughly
planning on starting at 11:00 am so people driving in that
morning will have time to arrive. We can then meet and
exchange stamps for awhile, then eat, then letterbox.
Hopefully we will have the Pavilion all day so can use it
as a base to meet in between letterboxing. I will bring a
pot of chili, bowls and spoons, and I guess others can bring
whatever they want to drink and any other food items
they want to share. I plan on camping with my family and if
any of you are going to camp I suggest you make reservations
now. The pavilion is in the Copperas Creek Campground if
you want to be close but I think it has electric sites, not
for tents. For more information on the park go to:

http://www.tpwd.state.tx.us/park/bastrop/

Baby Bear will be carving the Event Stamp that will only
be available during this event, so anyone participating
will be part of history! If anyone has any ideas of how
to make the event better, please let me or the group know.
I have only been to 2 events (in Washington State) and they
were more like informal gatherings, but I liked it that way
since there was more time to talk to people.
If you plan on placing a box while at the event, be sure to
bring pen and paper so you can write the clues for the rest
of us to follow that day if possible. There are already 7
boxes in that park and 2 nearby, not to mention several in
the Austin area, so there should be plenty of boxes for
everyone to find.
Hopefully the weather will be nice, but the Pavilion is
covered so we can still meet even if it rains.
Looking forward to meeting my fellow Texas letterboxers!

Silver Eagle
