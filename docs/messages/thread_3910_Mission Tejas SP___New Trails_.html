Mission Tejas SP...New Trails!

From: bluebutterflytexas (bluebutterflytexas@yahoo.com) | Date: 2009-01-18 00:42:41 UTC
Today I ventured down to Mission Tejas, and found out they have opened-up two new trails! One is Old San Antonio Road and El Camino Real De Los Tejas! (El Camino splits off OSAR) Here is an excerpt from the info I got:

     "This is the only place on the El Camino Real De Los Tejas, that is open to the public where the visitors can actually walk in the original traces. This was the road used by famous Texans such as Moses and Stephen F. Austin, Jim Bowie, William Barrett Travis, Sam Houston, David (Davy ) Crocket, Cynthia ann Parker,  her family and many others."

There's more info on it, but how exciting is that? I can't hardly wait for TALE 6!!

Also, while I was there I checked on these boxes:

1. The Old Pepper Tree....dry and in good shape
2. Tejas Bell....dry and in good shape, but there is a lot of damage near this box, with trees still blocking the path.
3. Nabedache is good shape and dry

4. Indian Trails-Quilt Block Series.....Missing (sorry LSQ)
5. Fraggle Rock Series......Not sure about this one.


6. Spanish Flag...everything was wet and the 8ft. stump is now about 2ft. I re-placed the logbook and bags. I will be contacting Silver Eagle about this one. It is still available.

I had a great day hiking and I can't wait for you all to see what is on the new trails!
Happy Boxing!
Blue Butterfly
