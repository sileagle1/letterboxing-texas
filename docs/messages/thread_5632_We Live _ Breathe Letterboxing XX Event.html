There's one thing about the event that I regret and I think I should mention it.  The Saturday night music and band, in my opinion, wasn't promoted enough, maybe not at all.  When the program started, I got the impression that the music was all recorded and the program was all about the dance lessons.  I didn't realize that there would be a live band.  I still haven't seen anything about the band or the musicians mentioned.  I was talking to Bell Motel Sunday morning and she told me that the band was made up of some of the best musicians in Austin, which is saying a lot.  Lone Star Quilter and I didn't feel like dancing (I'm getting too old), so we didn't stay.  If I had known more about the band, I would have stayed for the music.  Bell Motel told me the name of the band, but I couldn't remember it when I got home, but I thought it was something like Texas Swingers or Lone Star Swingers or something like that, so I googled "Austin band Texas Swingers" and the word "swingers", much to my surprise, took me to a side of Austin of which I was not aware!  I never did find the band I was looking for, but I'd like to know more about them.  I would have liked to make a poster for them.

Thank you, Boots.  I'm glad that I was allowed to hire a band, and that folks got to hear a good one.  If there is ever another opportunity, I'll talk to you about that poster!  The band is called Lone Star Swing, but I must admit it has a revolving cast of characters.  The two most celebrated band members on Saturday night were Floyd Domino on piano, and Redd Volkaert on guitar.

Here is a little about them from Wikipedia:

Redd Volkaert (born 1958) was a successor to Roy Nichols in Merle Haggard's backing band, and is "among the country’s top Telecaster guitar slingers."[1] Volkaert won a 2009 Grammy for Best Country Instrumental Performance.

While best known as a Fender Telecaster player, with a personal collection that includes a 1953 Fender Telecaster, a 1951 Fender Nocaster, a 1958 Fender Esquire and a 1950s-style Hahn Telecaster.[2] Volkaert has lent his name to guitars made by other companies, including Asher.[3]

Floyd Domino is an American musician known for his work in the genre of Western swing. Floyd’s close association with Western swing coincided with joining Asleep at the Wheel at the age of 19. After 7 years with Asleep at the Wheel, he is now an “AATW Alumnus” who still joins them for featured performances. While Floyd is widely recognized for his years with Asleep at the Wheel and the revival of Western swing, Floyd began his career with a broad grounding in jazz, boogie woogie, swing and blues piano. He regularly performs as Floyd Domino Jazz Trio showcasing a unique blend of jazz and blues. Other performances include work with Willie Nelson, Merle Haggard, George Strait, The Texas Playboys, Waylon Jennings, jazz guitarist Herb Ellis, Kelly Willis, Bruce Robison, Monte Warden, Don Walser, jazz vocalist Donna Hightower, and the Crickets.

Awards:

  • 1978 Grammy : Count Basie's One O'Clock Jump Asleep at the Wheel (Instrumental) [1]
  • 2000 Grammy: Best Country Instrumental Featured Artist with Asleep at the Wheel Bob's Breakdown Asleep at the Wheel 's recording Ride With Bob [3]
  • 2002 "Instrumentalist of the Year" Academy of Western Artists Will Rogers Award (2002 [4]
  • 2004 Cowtown Society of Western Music (2004 “Heroes of Western Music”” Living Hero Floyd Domino [5]
  • 2006-2009 Top Ten Austin Best Keyboards Award [6]
  • 2017 Inductee, Texas Music Legends Hall of Fame 

Thanks to everyone on the Planning Committee, and all the other volunteers, for making this an amazing event.

-- Bell Motel
