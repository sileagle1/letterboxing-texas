A very tired Lucy

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-07-16 16:45:00 UTC-07:00
Well, I did it. I went out and letterboxed all day
long! In spite of the heat (y'all KNOW I'm not a fan
of Texas summers)! My LSS finally adopted a trail
name, Fearless Fox, after his Wood Badge patrol
(that's a scout thing). My son, Lightning, went along
and was quite a trooper! Only one missing was Elmo,
but I guess I'm going to have to get used to that -
{{sigh}}.

We planted three new boxes and found four, and dropped
a couple hitchhikers along the way.

Status:

Planted:
A Tribute to the Iron Ore Mines in Reklaw
Lost Rose near Mt Enterprise
Go Jacks! in Nacogdoches

Found, all in Nacogdoches:
My Buddy List
Recycle
Naturally Musical
Sam I Am

Hitchhikers that are on the loose again are

Foot Steppin'
Little Boy's Sports Cap

We had an awesome day even though we really, really
smell bad! I think I sweated from 8 a.m. until about
a half hour ago! And I STILL smell like Off.

For those of you unfamiliar with Nacogdoches, there
are tons of great restaurants there, but I always find
myself drawn back to Yakofritz. It opened when I was
going to college at SFA and was a favorite restaurant
of mine back then. I still really like it - it's
right downtown on Highway 21. You don't get a big
meal there, but it is downright tasty. And frankly,
who wants a big meal when you're pounding the trails?

Also, the SFA Experimental Forest is a classy (pun
intended) operation. To get there follow Silver
Eagle's clues to Sam I Am. Texas has some of the best
state parks of any state, but this SFA facility tops
our state parks as far as a good solid basic hike
goes. It is an educational facility for forestry
students at the university, but it is open to the
public - although it isn't widely known by the public.
Go some time!

Lucy
