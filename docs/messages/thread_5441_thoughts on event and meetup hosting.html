thoughts on event and meetup hosting

From: Christine Borgfeld (christine@borgfeld.net) | Date: 2015-01-25 23:10:21 UTC-06:00

Last post for tonight…Ashby made a comment about hosting meetups which had no response.   I’ve seen this happen to others as well, so I’d like to suggest something.  If you want to host a meetup or event, make it exclusive to letterboxing.  Letterboxing is a small community.  If you invite everyone to come to your local county fair, girl scout event, sporting event or other non-letterboxing activity, it’s unlikely that many people here will share a similar interest. Especially if they have to drive across the state and/or meet someone they don’t know.  Things which seem to work:

 

Traditional events – hide boxes for people to come find.  This works, if you build it they will come sort of thing. 

lunch/dinner meetups when you are traveling – in Texas or otherwise.  Announce when you will be in town and you typically get locals willing to join you for a meal. You will also frequently find locals willing to take you boxing.    

Letterboxing themed classes or meetups – turnout may be small here depending on the subject and may primarily attract new boxers
