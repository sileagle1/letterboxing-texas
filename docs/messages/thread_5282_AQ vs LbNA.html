From: Heather Carnes (heathercarnes@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2013-10-25 08:08:58 UTC-05:00
I don't find them all. I started in LBNA, moved fairly quickly to AQ, and now use each evenly. I stopped logging finds a few years ago, so AQ isn't as helpful as it once was. It can't bother me that I don't find them all - or even a fraction of them. I tend to look for a particular series or planter. Usually, I will look for interesting locales bear where I'm visiting. 

I use an app that pulls clues from both AQ and LBNA. I think it cost me $5, but I've been very happy with it. It gives the option to search by city or "closest 25/50 boxes", or "within 5/10/25/50 miles". It also tells an approximate distance from your current location. I haven't printed a clue in years.

Yep, same with us.  We use Box Radar on an Android phone, but only use it for spur-of-the-moment or extra clues.  Box Radar doesn't show mystery boxes or boxes that you need to figure out the starting point (I often only list the county, not city,  for the boxes I plant).  If you only use these type of apps, you'll definitely be missing out on some clues.  When we plan a trip, we look at multiple websites, figure out which ones we want and solve any puzzles/codes required, and then print out the clues.
