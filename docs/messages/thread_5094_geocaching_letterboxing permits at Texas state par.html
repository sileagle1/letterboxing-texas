geocaching/letterboxing permits at Texas state parks

From: Christine Borgfeld (christine@borgfeld.net) | Date: 2012-10-27 22:07:13 UTC-05:00

I was looking at the state park website today and came across the latest geocaching guidelines and permit.  I’m not sure when this happened, but its’ been updated to include letterboxing.  The guideline is now titled “Seek and find” activities including both geocaching and letterboxing.  It is also a suggested guideline – maybe they recognize that not all the parks are enforcing the rules the same way?  The permit includes letterboxes as one of the options for cache type.  They have also removed the line about the permit being valid for one year.  Unfortunately you still have to get approval before the box is placed. 

 

Here is the link: http://www.tpwd.state.tx.us/spdest/activities/outdoor_recreation/geocache/media/pdf/GeocacheSuggestedGuidelines.pdf

From: Boots Tex (redbarn@eastex.net) | Date: 2012-10-29 17:06:32 UTC
It's funny, that's exactly the points I was trying to get them to agree to almost two years ago. I was told then that they didn't have the time or the manpower to change the form or make another one for letterboxing. Off the record, I was told, by the director of the outdoor activities section of TPWD, that letterboxing was insignificant to them compared to geocaching and didn't warrant recognition as a separate activity. I'm glad to see that they actually did something, finally, especially, removing the 1-year requirement. However, if they are only making a suggestion.....what does that mean? If the 70 mph speed limit is only a suggestion, how can they give me a speeding ticket if I drive 80?

Boots Tex
