From: Doxie Mama (doxiemama8@gmail.com) | Date: 2014-08-05 16:53:21 UTC-05:00
So...

I decided to take a trip back to Huntsville to pick up a few more boxes since I was nowhere NEAR finding all of them at TALE. While there, I learned quite a few new things, so I thought I would share with you all.

In no particular order:

1) The woods in August look VERY DIFFERENT than the woods in March.

2) It is near impossible for me to match anyone's step count. I don't feel too bad about this though because two different boxers used the same landmark in their clue with different step numbers (to me, 50 vs 58 is a pretty big difference).

3) Not all clues are as difficult as they may initially seem (despite my loudly proclaiming in the woods "really? You have GOT to be kidding me!" after reading each step

4) "deer trails"? Ha!

5) Renegades are NOT my friends.

6) Stumps are not easily seen in the brush (see #1).

7) When going off-trail looking for said stump, it would be wise to have a buddy remain on the trail to play Marco Polo once said stump is finally located after 30+ minutes of wandering. Hindsight and all.

8) I have an AH-MAZE-ING spiderweb dance when large spiders are involved.

9) Hardwoods and pine trees actually have different bark, so my neck strain to see the top of the trees was completely unnecessary (who'd a thunk it).

10) Letterboxing in Texas heat is still better than going to work.

Overall a good trip. I found 100% of the 14 boxes I set out to look for and all were in good shape. In addition, coming on a weekday was a good choice. The trails were quiet and the campsites were scarcely populated. Now I just gotta find some time to come back for the rest of them.


Doxie Mama
