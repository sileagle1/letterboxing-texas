From: jaydawnlove@yahoo.com (jaydawnlove@yahoo.com) | Date: 2011-02-26 23:24:16 UTC-06:00
I like fleas and cooties and hitchhikers but not everyone will agree that they are fun... Spoil sports. I love carving them and letting my kids hide the cooties and fleas. I just finished 30 new cooties for tale8. But my kids just informed me that they dont want to go camping, they would rather go to grandmamas house for the weekend. Oh well, I guess that means more for my new cootie catcher event box.
If someone is curious while I'm on the trail then I tell them I'm on a scavenger hunt. Lol I need to find some certain plant, or something to that effect. If I'm ot planting then I tell them I'm preparing a scout hiking day. Taking notes on where to leave trail markers for the scouts and planting scavenger items for them to find during the hike. That makes people less interested in following you and asking more questions than you want to answer. I've known boxers to wait to replant for over 45 minutes. I'm glad you were in the clear after he left. If worse comes to worst then taking it with you to replant would normally be fine as long as you let the placer know you are doing it so if someone goes for it and it's missing everyone knows what happened and they can try again after you replace it asap.
Can't wait to meet you at tale and get your traveler!

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2011-02-27 00:02:44 UTC-06:00
Maybe spoil sport is a little strong? I don't particularly enjoy cooties and fleas, but I'll play along because some folks, and especially kids, do enjoy them. Most of the time, though, I just pass them along - I don't bother stamping in to them. I've heard of boxers in other areas getting testy about them, and they set cootie-free zones at gatherings where they have been a problem. As far as I know though, nobody has gotten particularly bent about them at Texas gatherings.

One thing I would caution though - make SURE a box you are passing along is indeed intended to be passed as a cootie. I think I may have lost a box at last year's gathering because someone thought it was a cootie and passed it. Don't know that for sure - just know I set it down with my stuff and then I didn't have it any more. I realized after the fact that it isn't a good idea to leave a small container in plain view if there are cooties on the loose!

Dawns Glory, I hate that your girls won't be there. They are a delight and I know Trail Whale would have enjoyed playing with them again (but his attendance is up in the air also - we're working on re-arranging his weekly visit with his daddy).

See y'all next weekend - and this whole conversation reminds me that I need to make sure I have whatever cooties I came home with in my bag so I can pass them along.

BarefootLucy
