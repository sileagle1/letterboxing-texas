In honor of Earth Day

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-04-22 15:20:39 UTC-05:00

In light of it being Earth Day, I thought I would take a moment to recap the Leave No Trace frontcountry guidelines that most of us should observe as we go letterboxing:

 

  1. Plan ahead.
  2. Stick to trails.
  3. Leave what you find.
  4. Manage your pets
  5. Respect other visitors
  6. Tote your trash.

 

If you find yourself in the backcountry, the "Stick to trails" becomes "Camp and hike on durable surfaces" and you get one extra guideline "Minimize campfire impact".

 

In scouts, we teach boys this ditty:  Proper Scouts Leave More Respectable Trails.  In letterboxing, we could change that to Proper Seekers Leave More Respectable Trails.

Heck, I don't even know when earth day is but I agree that Leave No
Trace is such a good idea. When we get to a place close to a box, we
think "Where is this box?" and instead of reading the clues and
checking out the trees and underbrush, we just look for the trail off
the main path and sure enough, there is the box. Not good but works
most of the time. Also, we then try to disguise the trail somehow
with sticks, leaves or other underbrush. More than once we have spent
more time disguising the trail to the box than stamping in. Another
point we want to make is wash your hands before you touch a box
because if you have been eating, you have smells of food on your
hands and that can transfere to the box. Wild animals can smell food
fragance that you may leave on the box.

Yesterday we went to the Athen's Arboretum to check on
our "Children's Stories and Rhymes Series". We appreciate all the
people who have log their finds in the books. Some of the boxes are
on their 3rd. Logbooks. All were fine except LITTLE MISS MUFFET. Even
the log that it was in is missing. Also, when we check on our boxes,
we also check on Catpawzw2004 Texas Passport Series #1 in the same
Arboretum. We found it missing also. Someone may have taken it for
maintains and will replace it but we wanted to let Catspawz2004 know.

Hey Lois,
 
You are so right about the handwashing thing - it should really be a part of a letterboxer's LNT because we are handling something and leaving it behind.  I always try to rub my hands in the local dirt to disquise my own human-y odor as well.
 
FYI - Catpawz is pretty much out of the game, at least for the time being.  I have no idea why, except that life sometimes gets in the way, but it's quite a shame because they (it's a pair of women) are hilariously funny and really good letterboxers besides.  I ran into them in the Hobby Lobby parking lot in Tyler around February of last year - I was walking out to my car and a car screeched up behind me and these voices were yelling "HEY LUCYYY!".  I turned around to see the two of them hanging their heads out their car windows yelling at me.  Now bear in mind, I'm from Jacksonville and they are from Greenville and we bumped into each other in Tyler.  Top it off with I had been trying just that week to get in touch with them regarding their box, Gephyrophobia, which had been in the Footbridge Park in Rusk.  We had a really nice visit and they said at that time that they had simply not had time to box and weren't sure when they would again.  I would go ahead and send that message to them, but don't be surprised if you don't get a response.  They said they check that email account occasionally, but it's often so full they just have to delete everything.
