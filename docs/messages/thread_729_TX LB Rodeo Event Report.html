TX LB Rodeo Event Report

From: Silver Eagle (sileagle@alltel.net) | Date: 2005-03-21 18:56:22 UTC

I arrived at Brazos Bend on Friday afternoon along with my son
Clueless Collector, nephew Antman, and brother Baby Bear. We almost
did not get a campsite since they messed up my reservation, but
thanks to a cancellation we got in. There was not much choice left
for campsites so I did not expect to be in a good location. Imagine
my surprise and delight when I find we are right next to the
Louisiana Letterboxers! As we were setting up the tent, they all
came over to meet us and invite us to share a campfire that night.
With that to look forward to, we went off letterboxing before night
fell and managed to get about half of the boxes in the park. Then
it was back to the campsite to eat and go to the campfire. BOXDN,
Grits, Barney, Hummer Lady and others made us feel welcome as we
talked into the night, soon joined by a few other Texas boxers. I
especially enjoyed talking with BOXDN since I lived in New Orleans
back when I was young and enjoyed sharing stories of that fun place.
He also showed me the shirt he made for the Event and I immediately
wanted one. He is not only a talented carver, but a darn good
designer of shirts! We finally went to bed about 10:30 but I heard
the LA group still going strong at 2:00.
The next morning, we got up early to finish finding the rest of the
boxes, and we noticed several boxes had been stamped at 12:15 that
morning by some in the LA group. What troopers, in my younger days
I might have been able to hang with them but now I could only shake
my head in amazement! When we got back BOXDN was making Beignets
for everyone and they were great. Then it was time to start setting
up everything for the Event.
The weather stayed nice for the entire day and it was much fun.
There was great food brought by many people, and I especially liked
the Jambalaya cooked from scratch by Tim of the LA group. I had
some, then let everyone else have a chance at it, then I finished it
off. Man did that bring back memories of good food in New Orleans!
That night the weather starting looking bad but Wanda needed a few
more boxes to tie her record for most in a day, so off we went in
the dark after some. We visited Mr. Howdy at Kissin Fishes campsite
and then the lightning started getting pretty bad so my family left
to go back to the tent, assuming everyone else would, too. Wrong!
They kept going and I'll let BOXDN tell his story of what happened
that night in the storm.
The next morning as we were trying to dry out, people starting
coming by to say goodbye and it was kind of sad to realize it was
over. Baby Bear and I helped Wanda map out the general locations of
many boxes and gave her suggestions for her next 2 weeks she will
spend in Texas before meeting Pete and heading up to the Oklahoma
Event. She will probably get F7000 while in Texas! She is a very
interesting person and I enjoyed talking with her. As she was
leaving she said that this was one of the best Events she had ever
been to. I told her she was just being nice since she has been to
Events all over the country, but she insisted it was true. I asked
her why and she said "because everyone is so nice down here". I
think that speaks highly of our group and I appreciate everyone
showing the kind of hospitality the South is famous for.
So long for now,

Silver Eagle
