The one part of planting a box that I really don't enjoy is making the logbook.  Consequently, I have boring logbooks.  They get the job done, but it sure would be fun to have a little nicer book in my boxes!
 
Anyone have any thoughts?  Preferences about what you like in a logbook?  Things you DON'T like in a logbook?  Cool ways to make logbooks?  My one preference is thick enough paper so that stamps don't bleed through if folks stamp on both sides of a page...
 
Bear in mind, this is a BOX logbook, not a personal logbook...

Re: Logbook doldrums

From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-01-25 01:26:03 UTC
I so appreciate it when someone takes a little bit of time on the
logbook. It can be as simple as a pretty paper up front, stickers on
the cover, an unusual shape to the book. I went through a period
where I was using all these one-of-a-kind logbooks. I've largely
gotten out of that, because in general, I'm trying to go as small as
possible.

I love Sandibox' hand-sewn logbooks and wish I had the patience to
make them. I often cut down paper to 2x3 inch size and use a binder
ring to hold it together. I often try to personalize it in some way,
but it depends on how creative I'm feeling. For instance, for my
recently planted bird house box, I cut a book down into the shape of a
little house.

I also like it when the logbook has a little more information about
the local history, the area, or some quirky fact.

To me, it's not a have-to, but it sure is an added bonus when the
logbook works with the stamp, the location and the clues to make a
unique total package. (and I also like it when the paper is thick
enough to allow you to stamp on both sides).

dewberry

From: Maribeth Jagger (cjagger405@earthlink.net) | Date: 2007-01-24 21:08:30 UTC-06:00
One of the most spectacular logbooks I've seen was created for the Old City Park letterbox in Dallas (placed by Viewfinder). The cover is a beautiful cross stitch of the gazebo in the park handmade by Ruby Tuesday. In fact the entire box is amazing. The box itself is a handmade wooden box with a hand-forged lock made by C2B2 of Colombia, MO, and the stamp is an amazing hand carved stamp by Raindear from Philadelphia, PA. Well worth finding! A wonderful joint, creative effort of some amazingly talented boxers.
