Actually, LBNA will do this, too. You can select an option to have
it email you every time someone logs a find. It is a generic email,
but at least you know someone has logged a find. I used to have this
on, but started getting too many emails to keep up with since I have
so many boxes. I still appreciate the emails I do get since the
person usually has valid information about the box or just wants me
to know they enjoyed it.


>I just wish more boxes were listed on both LBNA and AQ. I usually
>log first on AQ and notify the placers,
>and then go back and repeat the process on LBNA. -- Viewfinder


And this is my main complaint about AQ and why I don't use it. If it
was set up as a front-end to LBNA and not as a separate repository
for clues, then all the neat functions would apply to all boxes
listed, not just the ones in AQ. The way it is now, it feels like a
competitor to LBNA since some people only put clues in AQ. I made
the decision not to post my clues in AQ since I don't have time to
keep them in more than 1 place. I also only log finds in LBNA for
the same reason. The webmasters for LBNA have made quite a few
improvements in the past months and I am satisfied with it. My
personal feeling is that people should consider it the main place to
post clues and AQ as secondary, unless they are purposefully trying
to hide clues. Unfortunately, many people are posting to AQ and only
posting to LBNA if they remember to check the box that will do it for
them. I realize that eventually I will have to accept the fact that
AQ is destined to become the clue repository of choice, but I have
not given up yet. The better option would be to add some of the neat
search capabilities of AQ to LBNA, thus accessing more clues.
I guess I'm so loyal to LBNA because over the years I have worked
with all the webmasters to make it better, and I appreciate all their
hard work for no pay.
This is just my .02, and it does not mean I'm mad at anyone who feels
different or prefers to use AQ. When it's all said and done, I'm
just happy there are clues somewhere and many great boxes to find!

Silver Eagle

From: Michael P. Olbrisch (mike2004@elp.rr.com) | Date: 2006-04-20 04:09:22 UTC
I have one suggestion for LBNA.
 
I use a program called ARRL Travel-Plus.  This is for radio operators.  It is a front-end for a datebase of ham radio repeaters in the USA.
 
The program opens with a map centered on El Paso, TX (or wherever you set it).  I click on El Paso.  It asks me for a range, like a 25 mile radius.  I select the range and click OK.
 
Then I begin clicking on my route of travel.  At every bend in the route, I click a point till I reach the destination.  This draws a path from start to finish equal to the radius in width either side of that route.
 
Now I click on compile list.  Soon I have a list of every registered repeater along that route in the order in which I will come to them.  I only need to program the radio and hit the road.
 
Wouldn't it be nice if you could do that on LBNA to find letterboxes on your trips?
 
I have an older version of Travel-Plus I would donate to the web masters if they would like to see how it works.
 
Mike.
