From: playaseeker (playaseeker_1999@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-07-09 02:42:26 UTC
Hi everyone,

I need some guidance. We are planning a trip to Memphis (from Lufkin)
and I thought it might be fun to break the trip up with some
letterboxing. In the past when we have made this one day drive we have
stopped at playgrounds mapped along the way, and that may be what we
have to do again this year. Traveling with 2 little ones we have to
make alot of stops.

I have researched boxes in Jefferson, Little Rock and can only locate
one in Memphis. Anyone know of any at highway rest stops? Any
suggestions from anyone? We can't make long side trips or we wouldn't
make it in one day. But I am hoping we can do some letter boxing on
the way out and back.

Any input or a point in a direction to search would be appreciated.

Thanks,

Compass

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-07-09 08:37:08 UTC-05:00
Unfortunately, Arkansas is still pretty barren in terms of letterboxes (and apparently letterboxers - I have a box that has been on the ground in Glenwood since November of last year, with no visitors yet!), as apparently is Western Tennessee.  I do see that while there is only one in Memphis, there are a few others in Shelby County, and not too far from Memphis (Arlington, Collierville, and Germantown are all suburbs of Memphis, if I'm not mistaken)!  Also, don't forget to check out the mystery box category, because sometimes they will wind up being in the area you are heading to.  It doesn't look like that will help you much in this case, but you should try to make it a practice to check just in case.
 
Also, you might join the Midwest talk list and ask them if they are aware of anything in Arkansas and the Southeast list for Tennessee.  As it happens, the two states in question are split between two different talk lists.  Both lists are very friendly and helpful, neither has a huge volume of traffic so you won't get inundated with email, and they would both welcome you to join for a short time for the sake of your trip (and you may just want to stay joined - they are very nice lists!).
 
http://groups.yahoo.com/groups/letterboxingmidwest
http://groups.yahoo.com/group/letterbox-southeast

From: (blhjrh@aol.com) | Date: 2006-07-09 15:23:14 UTC-04:00
Hi, Compass.  I made that trip in May, from the DFW area to Memphis (and points beyond in TN and KY), and I found these boxes along the way:   I found 2 in Little Rock just off the interstate going through the city (American Eagle planted by Nancy Drew, and Victorian Fountain planted by Maddlove) which were both in interesting locations;  there were a couple of others closer to the river in Little Rock that I didn't have the time to go looking for.  Also, there's one at a highway rest stop on 1 40 just outside of Forrest City, AR (Adding a Splash of Color, planted by Wanda&Pete) that I found last summer and it was still there in February of this year).  Not too far east of Memphis there's a neat box at the Burrow Cemetery in Galloway planted by Steve, and there are 2 other interesting boxes north of Memphis in Henning (Roots of Henning) and Ripley (Ripley's Homegrown), planted by Gupta's Scriptiorari. Those locations are in small, interesting old towns worth the drive, if you have the time or happen to be going that direction!  Good luck.
