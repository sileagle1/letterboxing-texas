Killough Monument, Spring Hope, Analysis of Nalgene Bottles, and I Can't Believe I Did This...

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-08-19 21:15:38 UTC-07:00
I'm going to be moving Killough Monument this weekend,
so if you are going to be looking for it, PLEASE
reprint the clues....after I finish writing this
message, I'm going to put a note on the clue page to
indicate it is in transition, then will remove the
note when I'm done.

Also, I've pulled Spring Hope for the weekend. The
log was about full, and it was a little bedraggled.
I'm repackaging it and will have it back out Sunday or
so. Which leads to the next topic in my subject
line...

Awhile back, I started thinking toward trying to make
my boxes more ecologically considerate. I wanted to
find a container that was smaller and less intrusive,
but tough as nails, so I started looking for a good
nalgene container. If you aren't familiar with
nalgene, the only thing I can tell you is to go to a
backpacking store and ask them to look at a nalgene
drink bottle. I don't know about knock-off nalgene,
but nalgene brand nalgene is indestructible. You can
literally run over it with a car (as I've done with
one of my water bottles) and it bounces right back.
You can even bounce it on concrete on its edge and it
doesn't crack or splinter (I know because after
bragging about my water bottle to a friend, I let him
try to destroy it to prove to him how good it is - and
uhhh, I still use the water bottle and he now owns one
too). I found some 8 ounce nalgene bottles in a white
matte finish with a good waterproof lid, so I bought a
couple to try out ($2 each). To put the size into
perspective, my average woman-sized hand almost
reaches around the girth of the bottle and it extends
slightly beyond my hand lengthwise. Here are the good
points: The bottle size is great - you can get a
small log and stamp in it, with a short marker. It
was also incredibly waterproof - no moisture in it at
all (and this was a box that lay on the ground). The
matte finish meant that it didn't reflect light at
all, and even being white it hid easily and was easy
to cover. The only downside, and it's a biggie: the
neck is too narrow. You really have to roll the log
to get it back in the bottle, and I'll bet I've gone
through a whole box of baggies replacing torn ones
(thank goodness the bottle was as waterproof as it
is). I picked the larger of the two neck sizes
available at our closest backpack store, but what I
really want is a bottle with a neck as wide (or nearly
as wide) as the bottle itself. Soooo...the long and
short of it is - nalgene would make a great container
for waterproofness and toughness, if you - a) go to a
bigger bottle so the neck is larger or b) find the
same capacity bottle with a wider mouth. If anyone
runs across some, let me know!

And now - for my story! I went to pick up Spring Hope
about dusk this evening. I was trotting myself back
to my car with it in my hand, when a Harley pulled up
to the park, the rider nodded and rode around to the
back of the property the little park is on and parked.
He hopped off his bike and strode purposefully to the
area where the box was. As he got close, I was to my
car, so I tried to find an excuse to slow down a
little, but there was not much for me to do but to get
in my car and leave. I drove slowly away, and went in
the direction where I could watch him. He acted like
he had a purpose, but he really wasn't in the exact
right place for the box. I drove down the road and
turned and came back, and he was just hanging around
(I woulda been thinking GG was a biker, if GG hadn't
JUST been to this box a week ago). I drove down the
road the other way and turned and came back, and he
was still just hanging around (and by this time, I'm
thinking he's probably wishing that ignorant woman in
the white car would quit turning around and passing by
so she could stare at him). I finally decided I was
just going to have to stop back at the park and TALK
to him - now my husband would KILL me if he knew I did
this! By this time, the guy had gone back to his bike
and sat down on it, like he was just hanging out. I
got OUT of my car, walked all the way back to where he
was. I got close enough to him and I said "Now, I'm
not trying to get in your business, but would you
happen to be here looking for something?" (And then I
astutely pointed out to myself that I was sounding
like a prostitute or a drug dealer). He looked at me
like I was crazy, and asked me to repeat myself - with
a heavy Spanish accent. Fortunately, I had the
opportunity to reword my question, so I asked him if
he was participating in a game. And then he looked at
me like I was REALLY crazy - I could see him wondering
if he was understanding my English correctly or if I
had really asked him if he was playing a game. Well
now, you can imagine I was feeling really STUPID by
then! I started trying to explain - hide and seek?
And to make matters worse, I decided to try out my
Spanish - now realize, I know enough Spanish to 1) let
you know I'm really mad at you, or 2) tell you to do
plant farm type stuff. Other than that, my Spanish is
spotty enough to be dangerous. I finally managed to
extricate myself from this very awkward conversation
and retreat to my car and when I turned around I saw
him laughing to himself and shaking his head. I am SO
glad I was able to entertain SOMEONE today...sheesh!
But - I still wonder what the heck he was doing
hanging around in MY park!

Lucy
