Trip Report & New Boxes

From: Silver Eagle (sileagle@alltel.net) | Date: 2006-08-06 19:48:25 UTC
I just got back from vacation with the family in Arkansas and it
turned out nice, especially since I threw it together in less than a
week. We were going to Utah on a 2 week trip, but the thought of
all that driving lost its appeal and I scrambled to find somewhere
else to go that would be fairly close but new. I have been to
Arkansas before, but it has been a long time and thought it would
fit the bill.

I picked a few places I liked from previous visits (Queen Wilhelmina
SP, Mt. Magazine, Mt. Nebo SP & Petit Jean SP) and added a few more
(wine country around Altus, De Gray SP). I was able to book
everything except Queen Wilhelmina, so had to look for an
alternative place to stay in the area. Well, I planned to drive up
through eastern Oklahoma so we could go on part of the Talimena
Scenic Byway, a beautiful drive into Arkansas, and I had looked to
see if there were any letterboxes in that area. Sure enough, I
found 8 boxes hidden in a place called Beavers Bend Resort Park, and
it sounded so good I booked it. Another example of letterboxing
taking me to great places I never heard of before.

We made it as far as Mt. Pleasant, TX the first day and I managed to
get a few boxes along the way. I finally had a chance to visit
Killough Monument, and even though the stamp was missing from the
box, it was still worth a visit and I thank BL for taking us there.
Next we went to Kiepersol Winery south of Tyler and enjoyed a little
of the grape. It is a nice place with a B&B if you want a romantic
weekend. We then visited the Rose Garden in Tyler where we saw more
types and colors of roses than I had ever seen, so I placed a box.
I then found the boxes in Tyler SP and was very impressed with that
park. In fact, we ended up staying quite a while and swimming in
the lake at the swim beach. The only downside to this was I missed
going to the Tiger Refuge, but maybe next time. As we rolled into
Mt. Pleasant and found a motel, I noticed a strange smell. I asked
the clerk and she said it was the smell of chickens from the
Pilgrim's Pride plant in town. Sounds like a box opportunity to me,
but boy what a smell!

The next day we stopped in De Kalb, TX to see the grave of Dan
Blocker (Hoss) and hide another box, then on to Beavers Bend, OK.
What a neat place with much to do. We went to the nature center,
swam in the river, did bumber boats, rode horses and enjoyed the
view of the lake from the lodge. I managed to find 7 out of 8 boxes
and hid one of my own.

The next day we drove the Talimena Scenic Byway from OK to AR, and I
placed 2 boxes, one in each state. On one of the trails we saw the
most amazing thing; walking stick bugs dropping from trees by the
hundreds! I have only seen a few walking sticks before so this was
really cool. We then stopped at Queen Wilhelmina for lunch and it
offered a beautiful view. I had camped there with Baby Bear many
years ago, but fog had kept me from enjoying the view, so I was glad
to get another chance. From there it was north to Mt. Magazine, the
highest point in AR. I had been there before, but now there is a
new SP and Lodge up on top and they have done a great job. We had a
third floor balcony room overlooking the valley below where we
sipped wine while watching the sunset (kids gone to the game room).
I only wish I had booked more than 1 night here!

We stayed as late as we could before checking out, vowing to return
soon, and headed north to Altus and 4 wineries. Our favorite ones
ended up being Post Familie, where they offered grape juice tasting
to our kids while we wine tasted, and Chateua Aux Arc. After buying
a few bottles we headed for Mt. Nebo SP. We had a cabin with a nice
view, though not quite as nice as Mt. Magazine, but more privacy.
We hiked and mountain biked and swam in the pool, so it was another
hit with us.

Next was Petit Jean SP, which I remembered as a great park.
Unfortunately, I have never gone in the summer, so it was less than
expected, especially after the great places we just came from. It
was just too hot to hike and the waterfall was dried up, plus I
couldn't find the only box left in the park (Baby Bear's) so I
missed my only chance to find an AR box. I did hide one of my own
just outside the park. I recommend going to this park only in
spring or fall.

The next day we drove through Hot Springs and spent some time there
before arriving at De Gray SP. It is a nice place, but requires a
boat to fully enjoy it, and they are expensive and hard to rent.
They did have some fun activities like an owl prowl one night and a
hayride followed by square dancing the next night. You should have
seen my daughter and I cutting a rug at the dance!

The last day found us driving home with stops in Jefferson, Marshall
and Lufkin. My wife and I really liked the town of Jefferson and
she shopped while I found and placed a box. I missed the other box
there because I felt bad looking under rocks in a private yard, but
enjoyed the other one. In Marshall we stopped at the big pottery
store and my wife bought a few, then we stopped in Tatum so I could
find the 2 boxes there. I did not have time to go for all the boxes
in Martin Creek SP, so will have to come back again. The final stop
was in Lufkin, where I hid a new box to replace the old Lufkin
Lookout. I recently found out about a new set of TX Wildlife Trails
and this box is the first in my newest series dedicated to them.

I highly recommend this trip, or parts of it, to anyone wanting to
get away for awhile but not drive too far. Unfortunately, there is
a scarcity of boxes in that area, but I added a few to help out. In
fact, I almost placed (9) more than I found (16) on this trip.
