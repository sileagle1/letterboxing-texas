A new event just 30 miles north of the Texas border!

From: Elizabeth Waltz (liz.waltz@gmail.com) | Date: 2009-02-10 23:29:35 UTC
Hi everyone,

I am hosting an event at Lake Murray State Park in Ardmore, OK.
Ardmore is only 30 miles north of Texas on I-35. I thought I had
posted this event last week on here, but have been told it may not
have gone through, so I'll try again. The listing is on Atlas Quest.
Please sign up there so I can get an idea of numbers of clues to print
and food to have on hand, as well as other things. I'd love to see you
there!

Liz
(Penguin Patrol)

http://www.atlasquest.com/showinfo.html?gEventId=1134

This event is being held at Lake Murray State Park. It is 5 miles
south from Ardmore, OK and 3 miles east from I-35. The park is dog
friendly, but does require a leash be used. Map of the park:
http://www.touroklahoma.com/images/map_park/LakeMurray.pdf There is a
$3 day use fee per carload for the part of the park the pavilion is
in, and that $3 fee is waived if you are camping.

There will be over 35 movie based boxes planted for this event alone
that are visiting from all across the US! This park has over 8 miles
of trails with a variety of trail heads to enter in and out of. While
hiking, you can expect to enjoy lake side views, some dense pine area,
some large rocky areas that are fun to climb, and quite a bit of
wildlife. The trails are well marked and easy to navigate. Trails are
not very stroller friendly. Only some trails allow bikes. I will have
maps for you when you arrive in your packet of clues and info.

If you only want to come to meet with people and enjoy the lake, there
is no shortage of things you can do at Lake Murray. You can fish, ride
a bike, rent an ATV, ride a horse (they have horses you can ride for a
fee), visit the nature center, bring your boat or jet ski (or rent
one), or play golf at the course beside the park. More info can be
found on the website:
http://www.touroklahoma.com/detail.asp?id=1+5U+6446

Accommodations: Lake Murray has a wide variety of accommodations that
include tent camping, RV camping, cabins and cottages, and a lodge.
Camping is done on a first come, first serve basis - they don't take
reservations. They do take reservations for the cabins/cottages and
the lodge if that interests you. For more info on the cabins/cottages
or lodge, call (580) 223-6600. Camping fee info is on the website here:
http://www.touroklahoma.com/campingfees.asp

I will be there camping on both Friday and Saturday night. I intend to
set up somewhere at Buzzard's Roost, (not sure where since I can't
make a reservation). They have both tent and RV camping here on the
lake. All the sites at Buzzard's Roost have stone tables and grills.
(The sites at Tipp's Point don't have tables and grills for tents
which is the reason for not camping at the area of the pavillion). If
anyone wants to share marshmallows and stamps and stories Friday
night, that would be great! Let me know and I'll do something in the
way of a separate sign-up for that.

The day of the gathering is Saturday. I have reserved the pavilion at
Tipp's Point (called the Cisco Pavillion) for use all day, but don't
plan to start until 10:30 am. It is right on the water, so don't
forget your jacket if it is chilly. If you need help finding us, ask
for the LBOK group. If you arrive on Friday, I will get you your clue
packet at that point. If you plan to arrive Saturday and want your
clues before 10:30am, send me an AQ mail and I will work something out
with you.

For lunch, I will be providing pizza and/or pasta at a cost of $3 per
person (exact change appreciated!). Please bring either drinks or a
small dessert or snack (chips, little baggies of trail mix, ect) to
share with everyone. I'll provide cups, ice, plates, forks and
napkins. If you don't want pizza or pasta, please bring your own sack
lunch.

There will be a few event boxes (table boxes) on hand to stamp into,
but please don't bring any more unless you ask ahead of time. Most of
these event boxes will be available on Friday night only. I am hoping
most everyone will be out boxing for these 35+ wonderful stamps on
Saturday.

If I can answer any questions, don't hesitate to send me an AQ mail!
I'll send my phone number out when the date gets closer.

Website: http://www.touroklahoma.com/detail.asp?id=1+5U+6446
