Wild West CLUE Game Stamps

From: celticlions (celticlions@comcast.net) | Date: 2005-03-28 13:18:33 UTC-06:00

 

Hope everybody enjoyed playing the Wild West game of CLUE as much as we enjoyed organizing it - what a stampede!!! (Yeah, sorry - bad pun!)

 

Now that the dust has settled, I'd like to get your opinions on loaning the entire set of CLUE stamps out to the Boxalots, in Northern California, who are planning a "Gold Rush Romp" in Old Sacramento on April 30th. Honour (who is the Boxalot mom) is very interested in borrowing the set since it fits so well into their theme as well.

Here are some guidelines I thought we might suggest to Honour – or anyone interested in borrowing the set:

 

1.       Emails from 3 - 5 boxers with 80 planted boxes amongst them, who personally guarantee to be responsible for stamp set (we figure anyone who has created a number of boxes will respect the value of hand carved stamps!)

2.       None of these stamps may be placed “in the wilds”; they must be kept in the gathering area (lesson learned from the loss of Box#3 in a 48 hour period! If the new gathering organizers  want to place boxes outside the gathering area, they could carve their own stamps for those boxes)

3.       Complete set of stamps must be kept together and not loaned out to any other gathering or boxers without permission from CelticLions

4.       Complete set must be returned within 2 weeks of gathering date, mailed by Priority Mail with Delivery Confirmation to CelticLions

5.       Any missing stamps will be re-carved and replaced by borrowers!

 

What do you think, guys?

 

I may be a little on the protective side, after some experiences in sending out several Postal Letter Boxes – I’ve learned that you can’t expect folks to read your mind. It’s important to spell out your expectations very clearly and then everyone ends up happy!

 

Ruby T.
