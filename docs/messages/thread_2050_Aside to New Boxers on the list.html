Aside to New Boxers on the list

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-08-10 09:03:40 UTC-05:00

Before we get too deep into the "how to train newbies" discussion, I want to point out to the new folks on our list that this is an ongoing discussion nothing pointed specifically at this latest group of new folks or toward individuals.   It is a common thread on letterboxing lists because new boxers tend not to realize how important it is to be very diligent in recovering boxes and resealing.   You will find that you are so pre-occupied with all the other details ("Did you stamp the box's book?"  "Did you stamp the box's stamp into our logbook?"   "Arggghhh it didn't come out right NOW what do I do???"  "And what the heck is this extra stamp and log in this box what do we do with THAT?"), that you might focus less on box longevity through proper care.

 

Please understand those of us who have been at it for awhile are glad to see those of you who are new come in and get started, BUT we want you to do it right.   And that is the source of the ongoing thread how do we make sure newbies are doing it right and how do we as veterans help you get to that point?

--
Barefoot Lucy

From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-08-10 15:21:51 UTC
Sometimes it's not just the newbies though. We all are guilty at
times of being in a hurry (it's hot! The mosquitos are out! The kids
are whining! The non-letterboxing spouse is impatient!) and not
taking the extra time that is required.

So, sometimes I think the situation may be inverted from what you
might think--the newest boxers may be incredibly conscientious because
they're trying to do the right thing and there are those veterans who
get blase about the whole thing.

I don't want to be mean, but I will point out that I pay attention to
the condition of the boxes I find and who found them before me (and
when). I check on some of my own boxes regularly as well. Hiding
practices is one of the factors I use in deciding whether to share a
particular set of non-published clues with someone. . .

Dewberry

From: Janet or Charles (janetsredbarn@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-08-10 15:53:23 UTC
Sometimes it's not letterboxers at all. I went to check on
my "Double Lake" box a while back and it was partially exposed. I
noticed an armadillo rooting around in the leaves and twigs at the
base of a nearby tree and I could see my box getting rooted up by
that critter. When I checked the log book, the last entry was
placed by a non-boxer who had accidentally found it. She was a high
school student, and gave her name and the name of her school and
made a comment about how she liked the stamp. Right next to her
entry was another, probably by someone who was with her (but not as
mature) that said, "You need to hide your box better; I saw it and
I'm blind!". Of course, you can't do anything about rooting
wildlife or curious kids. I have seen boxes completely exposed and
have wondered how in the world they survived at all. Thanks for all
the good advice, we all hate to lose letterboxes.

Boots Tex

Don't forget those dratted raccoons. They seem to really like splits
in trees. I've seen claw and bite marks on many of my Memorial Park
boxes. That's why some letterboxers I know swear by using only a wet
towel to clean stamps and not babywipes as even the odorless kind have
an "odor" that will attract animals. I have no idea if that's true,
but I know raccoons can find my boxes.
