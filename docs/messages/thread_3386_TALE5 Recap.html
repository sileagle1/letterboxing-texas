TALE5 Recap

From: Silver Eagle (sileagle@alltel.net) | Date: 2008-03-03 04:58:09 UTC
Well, a combination of great weather, good food and a huge turnout
(about 60-70 people) made this TX Annual LB Event the best one yet!
People came from all over TX and even from New Mexico and Louisiana.
It was fun seeing old friends and meeting new ones, and it was kind
of like a combination family reunion and ice cream social. We didn't
have ice cream, but we had many other goodies. In fact, there was so
much to eat even my teenage son was satisfied!

The carving demos by Baby Bear & Boots Tex were popular and I want to
thank them again for volunteering. The LB Match Game was won by
Dewberry (or was it Mother Of Five, Puddle Splasher or all 3) and for
the second year in a row, Gryzzled Gryphon came in second. I will
try to post the questions and answers in the Files Section if you
want to try your luck.

We also had a great turnout at our campfire that night (about 15).
This is usually my favorite time at our Events since everyone can
relax without the frenzy of stamping and just enjoy swapping
stories. Fearless Fox and Lightning made us some great peach
cobbler, which went well with the Cranberry Meade used to toast the
death of the Cranberry Cruiser.

I have posted some pictures in the Photos section and feel free to
add any of your own:

http://ph.groups.yahoo.com/group/letterboxingtexas/photos/browse/4cab

I hope everyone had as much fun as I did and will come again next
year!

Silver Eagle

Re:TALE5 Recap

From: Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter (janet@redbarnretreat.com) | Date: 2008-03-04 23:42:49 UTC
Hello, everyone, as you might know, we haven't gotten home yet, we're
sitting in a McDonald's in Seguin logging on to read what everyone
said about the event, and we couldn't agree more that it was great
and SE and BB did a wonderful job as usual. We've found 13
letterboxes today and hope to get a few more before the sun goes
down. We were greatly honored by having our Murder at the Arboretum
letterbox judged Letterbox of the Year by such distinguished pioneers
as Silver Eagle and Baby Bear. If you don't know, our family,
collectively, is known as The Texians. This includes our two sons, a
daughter-in-law and a sister. We are planning a another series, but,
due to the success of our first effort, we are having a hard time
deciding exactly what to do. Oh, Lord, it's hard to be Humble.
Seriously, we all really appreciate your comments and invite those
who haven't found the box yet to do so. P. S.: Our travel trailer
must be haunted; we found a couple more of IRA Boxer's boxes in there.

We'll be home friday and catch up then.

Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter and The Texians

On Sun, Mar 2, 2008 at 10:58 PM, Silver Eagle <sileagle@alltel.net> wrote:

Well, a combination of great weather, good food and a huge turnout
(about 60-70 people) made this TX Annual LB Event the best one yet!
People came from all over TX and even from New Mexico and Louisiana.
It was fun seeing old friends and meeting new ones, and it was kind
of like a combination family reunion and ice cream social. We didn't
have ice cream, but we had many other goodies. In fact, there was so
much to eat even my teenage son was satisfied!

The carving demos by Baby Bear & Boots Tex were popular and I want to
thank them again for volunteering. The LB Match Game was won by
Dewberry (or was it Mother Of Five, Puddle Splasher or all 3) and for
the second year in a row, Gryzzled Gryphon came in second. I will
try to post the questions and answers in the Files Section if you
want to try your luck.

We also had a great turnout at our campfire that night (about 15).
This is usually my favorite time at our Events since everyone can
relax without the frenzy of stamping and just enjoy swapping
stories. Fearless Fox and Lightning made us some great peach
cobbler, which went well with the Cranberry Meade used to toast the
death of the Cranberry Cruiser.

I have posted some pictures in the Photos section and feel free to
add any of your own:

http://ph.groups.yahoo.com/group/letterboxingtexas/photos/browse/4cab

I hope everyone had as much fun as I did and will come again next
year!

Silver Eagle
