Ruth,
 
For the Blue Northern, the fallen tree is a big tall one and  the stump is only about 2 feet tall.  We had also searched the back side in the crumbling wood.  We actually found it in the top of the stump covered with some wood pieces and that is where we replaced it.
 
Jeff (Sea Dove)

carrascoruth2000 <a.carrasco@earthlink.net> wrote:

....

We spent this beautiful day hiking in Sam Houston looking for the
Lone Star Hiker box (which we found, safe and sound)and the Blue
Norther box (which eluded us).  This brings me to my plea.  We
counted and recounted steps...I'm sure we had the right stump.  I
know Birds of a Feather found this box just a couple of days ago
(actually, oddly enough, we were planning on looking for these boxes
the same day you all found them, but the rain kept us away.) I was
wondering if there is any help out there for us.  The clues say that
the box is hidden under some "back scraps" on the left side of the
stump.  We really searched, but found nothing.  The Lone Star Hiker
box was really buried in the top of the stump...we had to dig it out
with a stick.  The stumps there are really decaying and crumbly.  I
wonder if Baby Bear's box is also buried rather than just covered. 
We would love to go back and search for this box again if anyone has
any help to offer.  Thanks to Silver Eagle and Baby Bear for giving
us a lovely day in the woods (and puddles and streams!)
