I'm noticing more and more that new boxes are not being listed on LBNA, just AQ.   So, either new boxers are not aware of LBNA, thus do not list there, or the choice has been made (by them and older boxers) to only use AQ.    As for me, since all my "Finds" for 13 years have been recorded on LBNA, I do not use AQ as source for clues (since I have no way of knowing which ones I have already found).  I would suggest you post to both sites so that you have full coverage, and just in case one of the 2 sites becomes a problem.    Your thoughts?

Since I only log finds on LBNA, I also don't know which AQ boxes I have found, which makes it difficult to know which ones I still need to look for.  I guess I could go back and log all my finds on AQ, but that is such a daunting task I'm not sure I ever will.  For now, I just look at new AQ boxes that aren't on LBNA.

For those that only look at LBNA when someone posts here about a new box, I have a new one for you.  It is called Poppy Capital Of TX and I'm only mentioning it because the place is so awesome.  I have never stopped in Castroville before but decided to go see the wild poppies this last weekend.  They are at the end of blooming but still make a beautiful sight!  If you go within the next week or so they should still be there.  Directions and links to websites are in my clues.  The town is full of history and we ate at a good place called Le Chat Noir.  I have added a picture I took of the poppies as the cover photo for this Yahoo Group.

From: (candj1969@yahoo.com) | Date: 2015-04-20 17:46:43 UTC-07:00

I've been letterboxing for nearly 10 years and, since AQ and LbNA were both available at the time, I have always posted clues to both sites.  Recently, I post on LbNA first, then on AQ and link the clues to the LbNA site, so if LbNA goes down, I suppose those clues would not be available from AQ.


Since my finds are 99 percent in Texas, I find it easier to search for clues on LbNA, by county, than to do an area or city search on AQ, and also because I know that I'll find Silver Eagle and Baby Bear boxes listed there (and Mosaic Butterfly and Bell Motel and GG and Frost Maiden and I can't list all the wonderful Texas letterboxers, but you get the idea).  If you only search for clues on AQ, you're likely to miss out on some very good boxes.  Also, the option to search for most recent boxes appeals to me.  I do check on AQ, however, just to cover all bases.
