From: Boots Tex (redbarn@eastex.net) | Date: 2010-02-14 19:37:12 UTC
PZ Kut is manufactured by Stampeaz and is not sold anywhere else. However, currently they are having some kind of manufacturing problems, and PZ Kut is not available at all. Speedy-Carve, which is the pink stuff sold at Michael's and Hobby Lobby is your best alternative, and is the carving material of choice for a lot of folks. I'm surprised the stores near you don't have it. I have used Master Carve, but it's too soft in my opinion. Some people may still put first finders gifts in their boxes, but it's certainly an option, and I don't believe many people are doing that anymore. I'll let someone else talk about patches. I know you can get some patches at AQ Marketplace, but I don't know what other choices are available to you.

Boots Tex

Re: [letterboxingtexas] Warning this post contains lots of questions.

From: Tasha Morris (keishkakeishka@yahoo.com) | Date: 2010-02-14 11:46:53 UTC-08:00
Good questions!

1.  I order my carving materials online, usually through Stampeaz.  As Boots Tex mentioned, there's been some manufacturing issues with PZ Kut right now (something about reformulating it to make it environment friendly somehow), and Stampeaz is the only source for PZ Kut.  I order pink stuff from her when I need some, just because my local hobby shops only carry the pink stuff as part of the starter kit, and I don't need more gouges.  :-)

2.  Not everyone puts first finder prizes in their boxes.  I've yet to be the first to find a box, but from discussions I've heard that range to paper certificates, shrinky-dinks of the stamp image, a trinket related to the box theme, patches, or a special letterbox trading card made with the box stamp.  Really, it could be anything you want that will fit in the box, as long as you have something clearly indicating that the first finder should take it.

3.  I've heard of patch collections, and seen some pictures online from really big gathers of people with patch collection vests.  Other than that, I'm completely clueless, since I'm still relatively new too.  :-)

Rogue Artist

From: Connie Terry (connie@lavernia.net) | Date: 2010-02-14 13:47:55 UTC-06:00

Hello Ambroysa,

 

Questions are good!

  1. I like PZ cut orange, from Stampeaz and buy it 5 sheets at a time. That’s how you get the best deal on shipping. Right now I have grade B, but I prefer grade A. (If you want a sample to try out I can put a piece in an envelope and mail it to you), A question for you: What are you carving with?
  2. First finder prizes aren’t real high on my priority list. Once I put an LTC made from the stamp in the box. That’s it. In the past I’ve found first finder certificates and once the placer made a shrinky dink from the stamp and left it in the box. I took them because I was the first finder, but I wouldn’t race to a box just to get a prize. (But I would race so that my stamp would be the first one there, if that makes any sense.)
  3. Barefoot Lucy is the one you need to talk to about patches!

 

Looking forward to meeting you in March!

Ambrosya, I like the pink stuff just fine and it holds up well over time. I used to be able to buy big sheets of it (about 6" x 12") from Asel Art, but the one in Plano on 75 hasn't had it in a long time (even though I requested it and they said they get it for me). The thing about "first finder prizes" or any gifts left inside a letterbox, is "do your own thing". No one expects one, but sometimes a little surprise is fun. It's your box... and your opportunity to be creative. Have fun.
