*****A kid after my own heart! I really don't see the
point behind shoes - most of the time. But...

I was privy to a pretty gruesome foot injury last
weekend, so even though I hate shoes, I sure don't
want THIS to happen to me!

We took the scouts camping last weekend, families and
all. Saturday afternoon, the twelve-year-old sister
of one of the boys was playing near the water
barefooted (since the lakes have been so low, there
was beach to play on). I told her to put her shoes on
(which, even as the words left my mouth, I was struck
by the irony - but I was wearing my scout hat and
scout leaders have to say yukky things like that
sometimes) so she was heading to get her shoes when
she stepped on a stick and the stick punctured her
foot between her fourth and fifth toes. It came right
out and, being a puncture wound, never bled. We had
the designated nurse (a real RN nurse, and a
wilderness trained nurse no less) take a look at it
and she cleaned it up and disinfected it and put some
gauze on it and told the parents what to watch for.
We had forgotten the incident because by campfire that
evening the girl was walking fine.

My husband called the dad, also a scout leader, on
Monday for another matter and the dad told him he was
in Tyler with his daughter and they were getting ready
to do surgery on her foot! They surgically opened the
foot up because it had gotten very infected and they
left it open for several days to thoroughly drain it,
and only today did they sew her back up. We visited
them last night and they said when the foot became
angry red and swollen they had taken her to the ER.
They put her straight into the hospital and all this
began. The hospital staff told them that if only the
wound would've bled, it wouldn't have been nearly so
bad - so the old saying "No blood, no foul" doesn't
necessarily hold true!

As it turns out - and this is probably important for
all us outdoor dwellers to know - ERs have been seeing
a lot of nasty lake-related injuries. Not to a
specific lake, but all the area lakes. Apparently,
since lakes got so low in recent months and many of
them are still not up to normal, some really gnarly
stuff is growing in them right now and cuts and
scrapes that are picking up hitchhikers - and not the
kind WE have fun with!

It has made me re-evaluate my opinion of water shoes -
I hate those things but my family practically lives at
the lake for several months of the year, so I may
reluctantly dig my one and only pair of water shoes
out of the back of my closet!

Lucy
