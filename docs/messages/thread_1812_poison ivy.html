Re: poison ivy

From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-04-16 15:24:15 UTC
For 38 years I never got poison ivy. As kids, we'd go to summer camp
in the Ozarks every year and my sister would get these horrific cases
(one year she had a combination of poison ivy, heat rash, & sunburn
that made her look like a gigantic bloated raspberry when we got off
the bus; you should have seen my mother's face!). I'd never get
anything and assumed I was immune. I never worried about it and it
never crossed my mind.

Then, I started letterboxing. One day, I found this odd rash on my
hand. I had no idea what it was. Turned out to be poison ivy. Now I
have to be super careful and avoid it at all costs. It means that
there are times of the year I have to not go after certain boxes, drat
it (there's one of Baby Bears that is tantalizingly out of reach for
me). So far, I've had fairly mild cases (localized, itchy, but not
too yucky, no need for a steroid pack), but I know with each exposure
the reaction just gets worse.

Along the bayou across from my house, it has already sprouted vines
4-5 feet high. That is really advanced for April. I am assuming our
mild winter didn't beat the stuff back enough, but who knows!

Sometimes I get emails from finders complaining about poison ivy near
my boxes. It's finder-beware of course, but you can rest assured that
when I planted it, the area was PI-free. That's one
letterboxing-hazard I do my best to avoid.
