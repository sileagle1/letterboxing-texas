Okay guys and gals, I don’t even know how to start this note, but since you’ve already read the subject line, you know what it’s going to be about…..

 

Today at 11:50 Navigator picked me up for lunch, and our route took us by the site of my “Square and Compass” box.   We spotted a male figure with sandy blonde hair covered with brown hat walking from the box hiding place to a maroon PT cruiser.  We coulda, woulda, shoulda, stopped and made contact but our boxing supplies weren’t with us and I was definitely in lunch mode.  I didn’t know it then, but it was one of those rare opportunities that may never happen again.

 

Today when I left school I stopped to check the box.  Yeppers, it had been stamped in today by our own GG. 

 

GG, if you’re out there reading this and I am describing someone who is NOT you, please let me know….otherwise, Navigator and I will claim the status of being the only ones who have seen the Gryzzled Gryphon.

 

Corazon

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-08-26 18:58:59 UTC-07:00
Not an intrusion - we LOVE to tell about GG.

Hmmm...I would say a year or so? He was definitely
tickling our interest in March because I couldn't help
wondering at the gathering if he was there and we
didn't know it. No, here we go, I just did a search
and the first mention of him was November 14th, in a
post I just forwarded to the group...

This list is growing A LOT. Texas letterboxing is
growing A LOT. But a year or so ago, we all pretty
much knew each other and recognized our stamps in
logbooks. We began noticing a fellow that nobody knew
- and he had a COOL stamp and a VERY cool name and he
always signed his entry - in very small, very neat
printing - "Gryzzled Gryphon of Coryell County." Our
very astute Ruby (you will find that she has quite an
eye for detail!) finally made mention of him, and
suddenly several other folks spoke up that they had
seen his stamp also. It was then that we realized we
had a MYSTERY BOXER! What he has contributed to
letterboxing in Texas has been unbelievable - even in
his solitary activity. He reached into areas that had
not been boxed and encouraged boxers such as me who
had planted boxes but had not had visitors. He seems
to box rurally and in urban settings. He has become a
bit of the rich tapestry of letterboxing in Texas. I
invite you to do a search of the archives in the list
archives. Search "grizzled", "gryzzled", and
"gryphon". You will thoroughly enjoy the read and I
know you'll get a kick out of how silly we get about
him. Celtic Lions set out a box to honor him called
"Gryphon Bait" and it has had a life of its own too -
but he found it and subsequently made contact with
her. He's also made contact with Rebel, who I believe
is closer geographically to Coryell County. According
to them, he is puzzled why we are so taken with him -
well, we just are - that's all.

We in Texas letterboxing have several quirks of our
own - GG being one. Did y'all know that GG is
becoming known nationally? I guess we've mentioned
him from time to time on the Big List, and we have
other honorary Texans on this list, and between all of
that others know who he is - I even had an Ohio boxer
tell me he was trying to figure out who he is!

We in Texas also have BEA's...but that's another
story.

Lucy
