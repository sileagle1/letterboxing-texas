The Samurai Stampers wish all the Texas Boxers a Merry Christmas!
If you can't stand bad poetry, stop here! Read no more! Save
Yourself!

Twas' the night before Christmas
When all through the house
Not a Letterboxer was stirring
Not even a mouse

The tupperware was hung
By the chimney with care
In hope that new boxes
Soon would be there

The little boxers were nestled
All snug in their beds
While visions of Pink Stuff
Danced in their heads

And I with my stamp
And Ma with her stamp
Had Just settled down
To pore over some maps

When on the LBNA website
There arose such a clatter
I logged on to the web
To see what was the matter

In Houston, in Fort Worth,
In old San Anton',
The boxers were planting,
New stamps to be known!

I grabbed onto my pack,
My boots and my stick,
Headed out on the trail,
And made tracks right quick!

As I came home that night,
My F counts exploding,
I saw the man in red,
His cheeks were a-glowing.

"You've stamped through the night,
Your inkpad is frozen,
You must be obssessed,
Cause there's ink on your nose, then

"I'll take off in my sleigh,
And leave you with this,
Merry Christmas to all,
And Just Keep on Stampin'"!
