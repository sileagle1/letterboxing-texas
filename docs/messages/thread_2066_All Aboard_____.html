As the bus departed Weatherford, TX the driver decided to continue
down the "Goodnight-Loving Trail." While on the trail it stopped to
pick up another passenger; none other than Charles Goodnight. Now
that the partners are reunited, the other passengers become quite
captivated by their cattledrive stories.

The bus arrived today, Aug. 13th, in Mansfield, TX for a picnic in
Katherine Rose Memorial Park. Go to S. Cooper (Hwy 157) and Hwy 287
in Mansfield. You'll know you're at the right corner if you see an
IHOP on the SE corner and an On The Border on the SW corner. From
that intersection take Hwy 287 east to the first exit, Walnut Creek
Dr. Make a right at the McDonald's. Go appoximately eight-tenths of
a mile where you will see Katherine Rose Park on the right. Enter
and park in the lot.

Start trail at main entrance with the yellow post. Go straight,
passing the playground which is on your left and go through the
large pavillion. Continue to the fishing pond and pass pond with it
on your left also. You will see a couple huge rock piles - go
toward them. Make a sharp left turn on the trail where the stone
wall starts. Cross over the bridge (has orange safety fencing) and
plop down to rest on the bench to your right.

Turn around and see the clearing behind you while you are sitting.
Go to the clearing and once there look straight ahead and you'll see
a tree that has a broken limb crossed over the tree. There are also
jungle-type vines hanging in front of that tree. Take a few steps
and look behind the tree. At the base is a thin piece of bark
covering a hole where the bus is parked. You'll probably hear all
the passengers.

Once you have the bus, take the short cut back to your car. You'll
see the short cut once you get back to the large rock pile.
