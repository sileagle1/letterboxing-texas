HI, all! 
 
I managed to do a little boxing on my trip to Asheville, NC:
 
Treasures of Tannehill, a series of 10 boxes placed by Jason in the Tannehill  Ironworks State Park, Tannehill, Alabama. Found 6 of the 10 before the thunderstorm (I'll box in the rain, but not if there is thunder/lightning!) This is a beautiful park and rich in history.
 
Silver Maple, Trillium and Sycamore placed by Ginger at the Botanical Gardens in Asheville, NC. A great spot for a stroll; the flowers and plants are well marked, so even I can figure out what I'm looking at! :-)
 
West End OBCZ in Ashevill, NC. A very, very urban location, which made this one a challenge!
 
The Loose Lips series placed by BeachyBoxer in Oak Ridge, TN. I have driven past Oak Ridge on a number of trips and never stopped. The lure of 3 boxes however was irresistible! A very interesting bit of US history and well worth the trip.
 
Clatterbang! and Rusting Out placed by She Who Plants along I-40 in TN. Attempted Picknickers Leavings. I love boxes at rest stops- they really help break up those long drives!
 
Attempted also A Splash of Color in Forrest City, Arkansas, placed by WandaAndPete. By the time I got to this rest stop, it was dark, and the mosquitos were quite fierce. It was also still really busy. If I followed the clues correctly, the box is missing. However, at this point, I had been driving for over 14 hours, so I could have easily been confused.
 
Finally, in Lufkin, at the Forestry Museum, I found Silver Eagle's Lufkin Lookout and Baby Bear's RoadRunner. This is a great little museum, with some paved trails behind it, and it's free!
 
Happy Trails,
 
JOY
