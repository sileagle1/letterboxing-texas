Witch Hazel found

From: Sandy Milfeld (pugapalooza@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2007-10-30 19:10:02 UTC-05:00
Well, I went out to Azle today to check on Witch Hazel. She had been
reported missing last November. (Yes, I procrastinate.) I assumed
she was a flood victim, so didn't make it a priority. She was there,
in the exact spot where I planted her. She had obviously been under
water, but I had hidden her beneath a tangle of roots that went under
the trail. Those roots effectively "held her down" during the
deluge. Her box had a lot of water in it (Lock-N-Locks may be water
proof, but they obviously aren't flood proof.) Her logbook had a
little mold on it, but all of the stamp images and writings were
pristine. I was very excited. The last logged entry was October
28th, 2006. There was an attempt on November 27, 2006. If anyone
had gone out there, they would have found her, easily. I have to
wonder if her year-long inactivity was due to the attempted find.
(Of course, I know it's my own lazy fault for not checking on her
sooner.) And I'm officially revising my practice of ignoring boxes
with attempted finds from now on.

At any rate, she is retired now.

Stargazer

From: Maribeth Jagger (cjagger405@earthlink.net) | Date: 2007-10-31 08:30:30 UTC-05:00
I always feel a little elated when I find a box after it had been marked attempted. It's kind of like a double reward. As there become more and more boxes available to find, I would definitely encourage boxers to still continue to look for a box even if it has been marked attempted, because you never really know. It has also happened on occasion that a box was missing when someone looked for it, only to find out later that someone else had just found it and had taken it a short distance down the trail to find a suitable place to sit and stamp in while the other boxer was looking for it. That can especially happen on a weekend when more folks are out boxing.
Puddle Splasher
