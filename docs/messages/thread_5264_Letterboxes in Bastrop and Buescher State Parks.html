I'm sure all the good reasons you gave Ranger Trudeau are new to him, so maybe he'll think about them, but they're not new to TPWD's top management.  Each park, when confronted with letterboxing, seems to  handle the situation differently.  If we strictly follow all the written rules applied to permitting a letterbox, it would be a pretty tedious process.  By rule, I am required to file a permit application before placing a box.  I will be handed a geocache permit application, like the one Mosaic Butterfly filled out.  I will have to mark out the word geocache and write in the word letterbox.  Legally, I should fill in my name, address, telephone number, email address, drivers license number and the exact location of the box (before I place it).  The box will then, legally, have to be approved before I can place it, even though I don't know where I'm going to place it yet.  Typically, the park manager isn't available, so I will have to wait until he has time to look over the application, send someone out to check on the location, approve or disprove it, and get around to notifying me.  This could take several days.  One letterbox that I permitted took 3 weeks to get an approval.  If it had been disallowed, I would have had to make arrangements for retrieving it.  My response would have been to tell them to throw it in the trash.  Okay, I know that's not the way it works in practice, but that's the legal process according to their rules.  And, by the way, the permit is only good for one year, then I have to appear in person (legally) to renew the application.  In every case, they have allowed me to renew it by email, but that's not the policy.  Maybe we need to send a delegation to Austin to argue our case.

I'm opposed to the whole permitting thing, not because it's like geocaching, but because the ways in which it is like geocaching are at odds with established protocols and don't fit with the hobby.  These aren't letterboxers determining the perameters of the hobby, but, yes, bureaucrats from outside the hobby with no understanding of the hobby.  It's not a coincidence that those staunchly in favor are predominantly geocachers.  This feels familiar to you, but not to me.

throwing trash all over the park, something I see at every park
 
I understand their point of view perfectly.  Part of me is even a petty bureaucrat. I feel certain I'm not hurting anything with my little boxes.  I choose not to humor them and take my chances accordingly.
