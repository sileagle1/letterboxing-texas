Happy new year! Thanks for including me in your group. I moved to Texas
a year ago for work and have been letterboxing for about two years. My
(former) boss at MotorCities National Heritage Area-based in Detroit,
Michigan introduced me to the hobby. She had read about it in
Smithsonian magazine some years back and we were trying to incorporate
letterboxing into our Heritage Tourism program. I was immediately hooked
on this awesome hobby and saw it as a great way to spend some quality
time with my husband and two sons. Our family's first box (and closest
to our old house in Pinckney, Michigan) was the "Go to Hell" letterbox.
We almost missed it and were leaving the area when our oldest spotted
it! The hand carved stamp is Munsch's "The Scream." I have LOTS of
other favorites and acquired about sixty stamps from about a half dozen
states.

We have found some great "off the beaten path" places due to
letterboxing and have used them to explore our new home here in
Texas(Austin, San Antonio, Corpus Christi, Odessa, and Dallas areas. It
never ceases to amaze me that there are so many creative people, both
their carving and clues.

I will most likely be a lurker when I get the rare chance to get
online...my teenager monopolizes the computer.

Kind Regards,
Cancan of the Nelson Clan
