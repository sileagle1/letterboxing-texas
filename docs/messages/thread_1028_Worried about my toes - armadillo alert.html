For those interested, we have been watching a family of 6 baby
armadillos at the Houston Arboretum. From the parking lot, just go
down the willow oak trail toward the outer loop. They are usually
close to the junction of those two trails. Now, and here's the key,
you need to get the kids listening and not fighting or whining. You
hear them well before you see them!

A bonus, you can trade a hitchhiker while you're there!

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-07-11 10:32:16 UTC-07:00
I think I shared our armadillo experience quite awhile
back, but I'll share it again.

I've always been fascinated by armadillos - maybe it's
their prehistoric look, but I think they are among the
most interesting creatures I've ever seen.

Well, last year I had planted a box in Fairfield State
Park (about 45 minutes from the city park LuvBug
cited). Last fall my husband, my son, and I went to
check the box. Now bear in mind that my husband is a
LSS, but he loves to hike and he feels we need to have
him around for protection, so he was along on this
trip as usual.

On the hike in to the box, we followed the trail which
winds around a lake. My son ventured out to the end
of a point which drops steeply on both sides. We
noticed odd piles of "something" at the end of the
point, and finally realized it was a community
bathroom of some sort for animals. My son was
immediately ready to get away so he turned to come
back and misstepped and rolled down the drop-off on
one side. He probably rolled about 25 feet, but he
was fine and we continued on (he's the one that has
all the "adventures" on our excursions).

As we were approaching the bench that is noted in the
box clues, we heard something rustling in front of us,
so we got quiet and continued walking. As we walked
into the clearing around the bench, there was a
full-grown armadillo rooting for whatever it is that
armadillos root for. Well...I've seen armadillos all
my life but I've never been within sniffing distance,
so I felt that I should get closer to check him out,
against my husband's better judgment. The armadillo
STILL never knew I was there, so I got even closer -
because of the rougher terrain, I was wearing hiking
boots, so I wasn't at all worried about my toes. And
still, he had no idea we were there. I reached out my
hiking stick to tap him on the back to get his
attention and I would have done it, but my husband
reached around me (he was standing behind me ready to
yank me to safety and away from my own foolhardy
behavior) and grabbed my stick so I couldn't touch
him. He pointed out the armadillo's very long claws
and asked if I really wanted to surprise him. I
decided he might have been right, in that case anyway,
so we just watched him. Finally, FINALLY, the
armadillo acknowledged us, glanced us up and down,
then turned and walked away in not too much of a
hurry. My son and I followed him through the brambles
and the woods for a short distance, but we decided an
armadillo's life isn't very interesting after all,
especially when he knows he's being watched.

Meeting up with that odd character was like peeking
into ages past and getting so close to him that I
could've tapped him on the shell was indeed an amazing
experience. What's funny is that every time I see one
squished on the road, I mourn just a little because I
feel I've actually glimpsed inside the life of an
armadillo.

Now that was the very same day, and only moments
before, I opened my own box to find there were
actually stamps in it! I turned the page after our
own stamps, ready to find it blank yet again, only to
find our dear GG's stamp right there - in MY logbook!
I hardly dared to turn another page, but when I did, I
discovered that Silver Eagle and Baby Bear had also
stamped in - and on the very same day I was there
checking it! I involuntarily jerked my head up to
look for them retreating down the trail, but they
weren't there, so I insisted we drive through the
campground looking for them, and although we didn't
find them, we did find a scout pack we know and had a
grand ol' time hanging out with them for awhile.

Soooo....as TexasLuvBug says, it for sure wasn't the
stamp that day - it was the experience!

Lucy
