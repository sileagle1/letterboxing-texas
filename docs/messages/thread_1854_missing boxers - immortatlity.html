This idea has some merit, but I don't like the criteria.
I currently have a few of my non-U.S. boxes and my
Hitchhikers listed in AQ since LBNA did not handle
them a year ago. I hardly ever log into AQ and
could possibly go an entire year without doing so.
However, I keep the status of all my boxes updated
on LBNA and am still very much in the game, so I don't
think it would be right to put my boxes on AQ up
for adoption.
It's interesting that the main reason for this discussion
is the problem of updating the status of the box. This
is the same topic that has caused such discord on the
main list over the years. One solution was to create
a database of boxes with the status able to be updated
by everyone. That way it did not matter if the owner
participated or not, the people looking for the box
could put in a status of Not Found or Found. This caused
an outcry from many because they did not want their boxes
having a status updated by anyone else.
Now we have the ability to log a find by clicking on the
flag next to a box clue. One of the choices is to say
Attempted instead of Found, and I think this is as close
to a status update as we can get for boxes missing with
no active owner.
Even if a box does get adopted by someone else, I would
hope the original owner is still listed somewhere in the
clues, along with the original placement date. I feel
that person deserves to be recognized for as long as the
box exists (and possibly beyond). When I place a box, I
have the hope that it will remain in place beyond my years
as a letterboxer, and I am just vain enough to want credit
for it. I figure this is my only chance at immortality!

Silver Eagle
