Weekend finds

From: droopwv (harland@pnx.com) | Date: 2004-04-26 13:51:36 UTC
Hello everybody,
Despite the soggy conditions, I hauled my cousin, (who is visiting
from WV)around Sat and Sun looking for letterboxes. I carved him a
stamp, made him a logbook and told him that he was now a
letterboxer! Well, my tactics seem to have worked, cause he had a
blast and was eager to keep going even when we were still at Bastrop
Park at 7pm on Sunday night--still a good 3 hours from home!
We were expecting rain so we were not surprised to find it pouring
when we arrived at Lake Whitney SP at 9:15pm on Saturday. We went to
the camp site and decided we were better off sleeping in the
vehicle. It was really pouring and the lightening show was
fantastic.
Here is the rundown of boxes found and not found.

The Raven-not found (already listed as missing)
Steamboat Sam-found (great things to see here)
Comanche-not found (could not find Parker Springs sign, but found 2
posts where we thought it would be, box may be missing)
Ambling Armadillo-placed Fort Parker SP
Gone Fishin-found (nice park)
Dinosaur Valley-found (water WAY too high for track sighting)
Howling at the Moon-placed Dinosaur Valley SP
Houston Toad-found (great stamp)
Trail Rider-found * (log book is FULL)
Cool Cat-found (placed by one of our younger boxers-5 year old Lily)
Poison Ivy-found*
Lone Star Letterboxers-placed Bastrop SP

Roarin Roadster-HH released. (found at Hoggs Pecans)
Madison on the Lake HH released. (found Gone Fishin-Lake Livingston)

(*=I had already found these previously. My newbie cousin had not.)

SOMEHOW I completly missed the Dino Mite box on my list and did not
have the clues with me. But, as we did not see the tracks for the
high water we want to revisit soon-when we are begging for rain.

We stopped at Confederate Reunion SP and lo and behold it was re-
inactment weekend. We drove through the park but did not stop to
look for boxes as there were people everywhere. Another one I will
get back to for more exploration.

All in all we traveled about 1000 miles and had a great time--
despite the rain. I have discovered as much as I tell myself that I
will go to the park and get the box and go to the next park--IT
DOESNT WORK. I love the trails and sights too much. The wildflowers
were awesome this weekend. And, I just this moment found a tick
crawling on my pants. I carried some stuff in from the car this
morning and it must have been on it.
One note about Baby Bears Poison Ivy box at Bastrop-In March a
family must have stumbled across it while playing on the rocks. They
wrote in the log "we have no idea what this book is but we are
signing anyway". They were from Huntsville. We got a laugh from that
one. They must have put it back right where it belonged because
Eoghan and Pumpkin Princess were the next to stamp in.

I have rambled enough,
DrooP
P14 F35 HH3
