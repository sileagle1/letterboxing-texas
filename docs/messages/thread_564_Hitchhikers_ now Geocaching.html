On that note I recently needed to know what our
coordinates are in our town, so I went to
geocaching.com to figure it out. I found out there
are tons of geocaches right in my area! I guess I was
under the impression that to go looking for a
geocache, you had to have a GPS unit, but I found that
several of the local caches could be found without
one. Soooo.last evening, I couldnt resist I
loaded the kids up in the car and drove us all across
town to one of the local junior colleges and found a
cache! In it was $1.05 and in exchange we left a
rubber AFLAC duck. You know, it was kind of cool
(except I thought leaving money was a little lame, but
maybe thats not too uncommon and I shouldnt judge
it) and I could see geocaching in conjunction with
letterboxing, but in the end we all agreed that it
lacked one of the things we really enjoy about
letterboxing - spending some time going through the
logbook to see the cool stamps!

Lucy

I had the same experience. I went out to find a geocache in my immediate area and finally found it. (Those GPS' are not as easy as they seem!) Inside was basically what I'll call - Happy Meal Toys - only not cool new items that you could give to your kids, no, they were unwrapped and obviously used. I can't tell you the disappointment of it all. At least when I get a letterbox, there is something of value that I can 'take away' with me, no matter what the skill level. A treasure.

It was kinda cool in that I love techy stuff, but I'll definately be keeping letterboxing first and foremost. :)

It did give me an idea for a box for the gathering though. . .ha ha ha!

TeamKing
