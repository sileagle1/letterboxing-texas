Favorite Thing About Letterboxing

From: (sileagle1@yahoo.com) | Date: 2014-08-12 06:47:33 UTC-07:00

For years I have said my favorite thing about letterboxing is getting to visit new interesting places while looking for boxes, but I think it is now my second favorite thing.  I still like visiting new places, but it is more fun for me if I find them before other boxers and lead them there by placing a letterbox.  It is getting harder to find these "virgin" places, but on my recent Florida trip I managed to find several, all within an hour of Orlando.  I guess most people are too busy seeing Mickey to visit the places I found, but I'm not sure why locals haven't placed boxes there.  Oh well, hopefully they will find the time to visit now.What is your favorite thing about letterboxing?Silver Eagle

From: (mjrekedal@gmail.com) | Date: 2014-08-22 12:45:30 UTC-07:00

I was hooked 7 ys ago the first time we followed the clues and found a box off the highway. What a rush. I must say my favorite thing has been the thrill of solving a clue in cypher or code. Finding Double Helix in LA area took me all day to solve. (Fix your own lunch family. Gramma's busy deciphering a letterbox clue.) Now a close second favorite is finding boxes that haven't been found for awhile. Just got back from stop in Lake Murray SP in Oklahoma. Several boxes' log books hadn't been entered in 2-3 yrs. Took some digging and rethinking clues to imagine what was meant before fallen logs had become mush and trails reshaped. Meeting great folks, reading comments on the list -- all make this a great sport/pass time/hobby/obsession.

Bloomin Gramma Jo
