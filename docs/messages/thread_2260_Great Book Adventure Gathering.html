Great Book Adventure Gathering

From: Silver Eagle (sileagle@alltel.net) | Date: 2006-10-22 15:41:34 UTC
This literary LB Event was held yesterday, 10/21/2006, at Burroughs
Park in Tomball, TX, and was a huge success. Let me be the first to
congratulate and thank Dewberry and Teamking for putting on such an
extravagant Event, along with the many talented carvers across the
country that contributed stamps. Over 80 letterboxers attended and
were treated to many activities, including: Several literary theme
book walks with many boxes on each to find, a Bookcrossing Bookswap
where you could swap a book for another one and receive a stamp, and
dozens of Event stamps from all over the country. Everyone was also
encouraged to carve a literary-themed personal traveler with thought-
provoking clues, and it was fun guessing and receiving these stamps.
There was even a stamp for anyone dressed as a literary character.

I have created a photo album called Great Book Adventure Gathering and
put a few photos in it, but I did not take near enough pictures, so
please add some if you did. Thanks again for a fun day.

Silver Eagle

Great Book Adventure Gathering

From: CelticLions (celticlions@comcast.net) | Date: 2006-10-23 11:20:38 UTC-05:00

Can you even begin to imagine how many hours Dewberry and TeamKing put into that incredible Great Book Adventure gathering?

Wow!!!

Every little detail from beginning to end was incredibly well-thought out and designed to provide maximum enjoyment for the attendees. The Storybook stroll was pure genius – so creative – from our vantage point we could vicariously enjoy all the kids finding those clever boxes!

And even though she kept discounting her contribution, I know Mother-of-Five helped with it all, too. How does she do it with a baby on her hip???

It’s always fun to put faces with names – I loved meeting Queen Beeloved (and we are SCRAMBLING to find any box of hers in the future – her stamps are exquisite!), Daisy & Onslow, LionsMane (loved his literary-themed personal traveler), Janet & Cassie, Sharon of the Peas, BlueButterfly, RedBoomer ( very funny literary traveler!) &  Kyrasmygsd (so glad to find out how to pronounce that!) and of course, the elusive Gryzzled Gryphon.

We were so glad to see Wanda again and to meet Pete – we’ll never figure out how they can find the time to write a cheerful and complimentary note in every single letterbox they find…all twelve thousand of them! But then you spend a minute chatting with them and they just exude positive energy. (CactusLicker was especially touched to notice Wanda’s rather unique jewelry – the first finder items from his Caddo Indian box. She was so kind to CL a couple of years ago when he sent his first stamps up to two gatherings in New England – I know she played a large part in the fact that he has kept up his interest in this sport!

We had hoped to join everyone for the Chicken-fried chatter Saturday night, but CactusLicker’s girlfriend had been feeling under the weather all day, and she had been such a good sport to join in without complaint that we felt we owed her an early night.

The next day after CactusLicker & Laura had left for Austin around noon, Stillwater and I decided to try for Gryzzled Gryphon’s box before returning to Dallas – much more challenging than I expected…but I should have known!

 

Thanks again for a truly wonderful gathering!

Now we MetroBoxers really need to get our act together and plan a DFW event!

 

Ruby T.
