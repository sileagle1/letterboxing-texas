Homestead Hitchhiker Hostel retired

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-04-11 20:21:51 UTC-05:00
If you recall, I had said a few weeks ago that I would be retiring this hostel soon.  I think I said something like 30 days, but when I went out to check it this evening, it was pretty wet so it seemed like a good time to bring it on home and put it to rest. 
 
For the container, I had used a large rubbermaid container.  In the two years it was in the wild, the container turned milky and the camo faded some, so that even laying on top of the ground under the cedar tree as it was, it wasn't that easy to see.  I'm amazed it lasted, since after the day I planted it, I doubt it ever got recovered with ground fodder!  Still though, the only folks who ever ventured around it were the tractor dudes who mowed the field a couple times a year, so I guess it makes sense it stuck.  Anyway, those rubbermaid jobs are pretty tough containers, but this one has had a persistent problem with moisture.  Even looking at it in the light of my kitchen, I can't figure out why it has gotten wet several times.  Nice thing is - at some point in its past, someone must have stumbled across it and was worried about the moisture.  All the HH's were in their own baggies and the hostel logbook was in its own baggie, so everything should have been fine, but this person went ahead and added a baggie, placing all the pacakaged HH's and the hostel logbooks and stamp into the big baggie.  The result?  There was actually standing water in the box, but the logbooks were all as dry as a bone!  Thanks to whoever did that - they might've been fine without your baggie, but they might not have either.  Better safe than sorry!
 
None of the HH's in the box were by local placers, so I'll be sending them along in various boxes soon.  Suddenly I find myself with lots of HH's - I guess I need to get going and put them out, right?
