Missing Moose!

From: Maribeth (cjagger405@earthlink.net) | Date: 2014-01-14 17:33:24 UTC-06:00

I went out to Lake Grapevine today to hike and check on my series of Moose letterboxes (from the 2006 Moose Mini Meet). Wowzer. I haven't been out there in a couple of years and those trails have changed... actually they've multiplied. Now there are trails running up and down all those hills. It was easy enough for me to follow my clues to the first three boxes, but I had a difficult time finding the trails for the last two, however I finally did. Unfortunately, four of the moose must have decided to migrate to Maine as they are no longer at Lake Grapevine. I am happy to find one of the boxes (#2) is still safely in place after all these years, and the box is still in great condition. I will be changing the clues shortly and retiring the other four boxes but will leave the one original one in place. Those trails are so rocky and fun. I hiked for three hours (perfect day in north Texas to hike) exploring many of the new trails. Anyone in the DFW area looking for a good hike, that's the place!

Looking forward to seeing everyone at TALE! Puddle Splasher

From: Christine Borgfeld (christine@borgfeld.net) | Date: 2014-01-14 19:46:59 UTC-06:00

This discussion brings to mind one of dewberry’s boxes which survived for years within a few feet of a geocache (and allowed me to find it by accident).  I think it survived because the geocachers were constantly mentioning it on the log file of the nearby cache.  For example someone would log a DNF (did not find) on the cache with comments like “found the nearby letter box but no cache today.”  Geocachers may not go to the website but they do frequently read the logs for the specific cache they are looking for.   If a specific box is near a cache it may be worth leaving some comments on the log file for the cache. 

 

I used to write notes everywhere on my  box about not taking the stamp, but I’ve gotten sloppy.  Especially when hiding lots of boxes at an event! I do try to look for the general locations of nearby caches and avoid planting too close to existing ones.  That has worked fairly well so far.
