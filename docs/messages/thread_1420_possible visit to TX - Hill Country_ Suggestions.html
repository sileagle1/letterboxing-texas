First of all, great choice for your Thanksgiving vacation!
The scenery, hiking and letterboxes will be great, and the
weather should be, too.  Unless a cold front comes through
that week, the weather will be sunny, dry and 60's or 70's
during the day, 50's or 60's at night.  This is a great time
of year to visit TX and I hope you enjoy it.
If you start in San Antonio, definitely visit the Alamo
and Riverwalk, but when you are ready to hike, I really
like the Friedrich Wilderness Park on the northwest side.
There is only 1 box in the park, so for more boxes in an
urban park, try McAllister Park on the east side.  Then
drive up I35 to Austin, stopping in San Marcos if you
want to visit Aquarena Springs and the Outlet Mall.  The
Austin area has many boxes, some in the city and others
out in parks with trails.  My favorite places are Mt.
Bonnell, Zilker Park and Hamilton Pool Preserve.  Then drive
west on Hwy 290 towards Johnson City and visit Pedernales Falls
SP.  It has several boxes and a good trail overlooking the falls.
Continue on Hwy 290 to Fredericksburg, stopping at several
fine TX wineries if you are so inclined.  There is great
food and shopping in Fredericksburg, along with many B&Bs.
Just north of town is Enchanted Rock SP, with a trail up
the second-highest granite dome in U.S. and a few boxes.
Then head south on Hwy 16 to Kerrville and on to Kerrville-
Schreiner Park.  More good trails and a couple boxes.  Continue
south to Medina and stop at the Cider Mill for all things
apple from Love Creek Orchard.  Their apple ice cream is
the best!  Then head west to Lost Maples SP for more good
trails, one box and possibly some late fall foliage.  Then
head east, back to your starting point of San Antonio.
Have fun! 

Silver Eagle
