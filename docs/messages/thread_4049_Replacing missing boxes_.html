Replacing missing boxes.

From: Mike Olbrisch (mike-2007@elp.rr.com) | Date: 2009-04-12 10:32:48 UTC-06:00
One thing to consider is the distance too.  Many letterboxers plant boxes far away.  They may intend to replace a missing box - next time they pass that way.  It may take a while.  Heck - I have a missing box right now, it is only 50 miles away.  I just got the last of the pieces to replace it, but I do not have the time - and don't know when I will have the time to get out there.  It is Cattle on the Mesa - missing a second time now.  I am considering separating the letterbox from the Geocache.  I will be moving it to a better spot - when I get the chance....  With the spring overhaul season nearly over, we can start expecting time to be more available - until the fall EPA inspections start.  <grin>.
 
Best bet - as others have said..... contact the box owner, and give them time to respond.  Three months isn't too long to wait for a reply.  Some folks travel overseas, some have jobs that just keep them way to busy.
 
If you feel like it - come out to El Paso and replace all my boxes.  There aren't enough out here and I will move mine for you and plant them somewhere else!  <grin>.
 
Welcome to Letterboxing.
