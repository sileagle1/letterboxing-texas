From: Michael P. Olbrisch (mike2004@elp.rr.com) | Date: 2005-09-26 00:51:12 UTC

Good evening everyone,

Name is Mike. I am in El Paso, Texas. I am probably the farthest
west Letterboxer in Texas, being 5 blocks from the NM border, and 3 miles
away from the Mexican border. Before I get too far, your knowing more about
me will help you understand what I do.

I just turned 50. I am a retired soldier and combat veteran. My
current employment is as an electronics specialist at the local power
generation plant. I am married to a German named Monika, Moni for short.
Two kids. My son James (24) is a Cavalry Scout, Sniper, Paramedic and
Arabic Linguist on his second combat tour. My daughter Heidi (20) just got
married and moved out. Yes, the nest is empty... And we are enjoying it!

My hobbies are High-Power (service) rifle competition (near Master
Class), USPSA (combat) Pistol competition (C-Class), Ham Radio (extra
class), Hiking, Dog Training, Geocaching, restoring a 1976 Postal Jeep, and
now Letterboxing. I am the hike reporter for Localhikes.com. Some links at
the bottom.

I have been a Geocacher for nearly 4 years. Most of my caching is
done while hiking, or while traveling to dog shows. Moni has been caching
for about a year with me, has a ham license, IS the dog show coordinator of
the family, does the driving (her van), and hikes with me as her knees will
allow. She won't touch the jeep, and won't shoot against me - says she
doesn't want to embarrass me. Bless her heart! OH - we both have the Texas
Concealed Handgun License, and do carry. I prefer a semi-auto. But in
deference to Moni, we have matching titanium .357 magnums. Hers is purple,
mine is gray.

I discovered Geocaching while researching some land navigation
information to train ROTC cadets with. I discovered Letterboxing because
Geocaching is now allowing something called a Letterbox Hybrid. It is a
Geocache AND a Letterbox in one location. It looked neat, and so it
started. I will be converting a few of my Geocaches to Letterbox Hybrids,
and even adding a few more new ones in the future.

I am very much looking forward to learning more about letterboxing.
So far it looks simple enough. And there are not many in my area. Only
two, or three if you count the one I just put out. I am hoping to get more
local Geocachers interested in Letterboxing too.

ANYONE NEAR MY AREA - LIKE TO HIKE????

Thanks.
