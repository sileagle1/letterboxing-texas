Looks like we missed everyone by a day. We went to
Tyler today and got the boxes at Children's Park.
Barely got Heart of a Child though, I was able to find
one spot for me to stamp b/c that logbook was full.
We attempted to get the boxes at the University but
had the worst luck! The first one had two security
guards standing right by the bell tower talking. We
sat there for a bit to see if they would leave, but
instead they just sat there then started staring at
us. So we moved on to try to get the second box but
they were setting up for some big luncheon. There were
a bunch of people right by the place that box was
located. So then we got in our car and tried to drive
to the third and fourth box, but this cop started
following us! I sware, wherever we turned he turned!
So we decided to just get out of there. I'm sad b/c I
know those boxes are being pulled on the 10th, but we
won't make it back before then. :(

Blue Butterfly, I have to tell you something that's
funny. After the Children's Park boxes we went to the
State Park to get those. Neither one of the logbooks
had your stamp in it. My girls said "I can't believe
Blue Butterfly didn't get these boxes?!!! We actually
beat Blue Butterfly to a box!!!!!" They were so
excited. LOL
Then when I got home to log in our finds I noticed you
were listed as one of the past finders. So apparently
those logbooks had been replaced. Which would make
sense seeing as how those boxes were planted about 3
years ago. LOL
The girls were bummed. They thought we had actually
found a box that you hadn't found yet. LOL

Perky P

Re: Tyler boxes

From: Bloomin Gramma Jo (mjrekedal@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-04-27 17:31:31 UTC
Bird Dog and I are back safe and sound, and very refreshed from our
week in Tyler area. Sooooo many boxes, each taking us to interesting
places we never would have found on our own. So now having converted
my best marathon-worthy running shoes to trail trekkers and covering
myself in calamine lotion from 1st case of poison ivy since I was a
kid (L'boxer's badge of honor?), we are loggin in finds and
treasuring the memories.
I know what you mean about the guards, Perky P. We were there earlier
jn the week and managed to get all 4 boxes. Mostly thanks to my
intrepid hubby Bird Dog, who has become quite adept at getting out
his local map and asking questions about directions, with some
comment about how nice the area is and how much we are enjoying
getting to know such-and-sucn place. His other tactic is to carry a
small grocery bag and start picking up trash, both a good ploy and
helpful to the enviroment. I knew he would be a good l'boxer!!!
Bloomin Gramma Jo

Bloomin Gramma Jo, Did you or anyone get any ticks while
letterboxing. I am hearing that the ticks are out in force but so far
we have not had any bite us. Here is a warning I got through email.
Thought I would share.... You can take it with a grain of salt and it
will go down better. Adams Trails


"Please heed this warning. It could happen to you."

TICK WARNING!

I hate it when people forward bogus warnings, and I have even done it
myself a couple times unintentionally. ..
but this one is real, and it's important. So please send this warning
to everyone on your e- mail list.

If someone comes to your front door saying they are checking for
ticks due to the warm weather and asks you to take your clothes off
and dance around with your arms up, DO NOT DO IT!! THIS IS A SCAM!!
They only want to see you naked.
I wish I'd gotten this yesterday. I feel so stupid."

I think poison ivy is becoming the kudzu vine of Texas. It's already so bad this year. Since many of us do a lot of our boxing in the cooler months of the winter, our boxes are safe when we plant them, but a couple of months later can present a much different situation! One word: Technu... don't leave home without it!
