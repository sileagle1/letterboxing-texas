Memorial Park

From: Boots Tex (redbarn@eastex.net) | Date: 2013-04-28 14:59:09 UTC
My son, Wills World, and I spent a few hours in Memorial Park yesterday. The park is better than it was the last time I visited. The part directly behind the softball fields, up to and including the east-west section of the blue trail, is still a shambles of fallen trees and branches and the red trail in that area is mostly nonexistent. Our purpose for being there was to find, if possible, the five boxes which make up the Nada Clue series. The first box, Colonel Mustard was probably bulldozed into the ground and it would be impossible to replace the box anywhere in that area. We actually found boxes 2 and 3 by recognizing a couple of landmarks, even though the area is nothing like it once was. The last time I was there, I actually found box 4, so I knew exactly where it was, but, unfortunately, the multi-trunk tree had been chain-sawed down, even though it is still living, and the box is lost. Multi trunks are now growing out of the stump. Also, the area where box 5 was located is a wasteland of tree trunks and mulch. If the box is there, I couldn't find it. I made the decision to move the entire series to a different part of the park, which will require carving 3 new, but identical stamps and rewriting the clues. I hope to have that done before the Memorial Park Maintenance Event takes place. I don't know if I can make that event because it's on Mother's Day weekend and I don't know what plans have been made by the family. Other than that front area, the park looks good. There are many trees marked for removal, so you probably shouldn't place a box behind one of those. There were many bicyclists on the trails, so you have to be constantly attentive to them. The rains came in the middle of the afternoon, but we made it out before the downpours.

Boots Tex
