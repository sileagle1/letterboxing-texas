From: tigger32167 (tigger32167@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-10-02 22:35:42 UTC
well, in a year and a half that i have been boxing, i have never had
an incident like i had this past sunday when i was placing my
october series. i had my boxes ready to go and knew where i wanted
to place the october series plus two others that i had come up with
that seemed appropriate to the halloween series. so, sunday morning
i got up and left the house at around 7:30 am to beat the heat. my
first stop was at the rusk state hospital cemetery near my house. i
had heard a couple of haunted tales about this cemetery and thought
what a great place to place a box. i had been to this cemetery
before and knew that it was fenced in with a gate with a lock on it,
but also knew that i could place the box on the outside of the
cemetery without trespassing across state property. now first of
all, this cemetery is right on a "main" road, and has a green sign
on the road and an archway sign going into the cemetery. i also
should tell you that the cemetery is for people who were at rusk
state hospital who had no family to claim their bodies when they
died. as i was at the trunk of my car, i did notice a car that went
by, but thought nothing of it. so then i started looking around for
a good place to put the box. i was having difficulty, because
the "woods" next to the cemetery had a lot of underbrush/briars.
well all of a sudden, a security vehicle drove up and a security
guard from rsh got out and started questioning me and telling me
that i had been reported as being inside the cemetery (that would
mean that i had crossed over the gate) he noticed the box in my
hand, which was taped up with black duct tape, and asked me what i
had taken from the cemetery. i told him that i had just been
looking around to see what was there, and assured him that i had not
crossed over the gate...by the way, there isn't a "no trespassing"
sign anywhere near. i then explained that the box was a hobby of
mine, and while i was stopped i had gotten the box out of my trunk.
i did show him the box and its contents (he didnt give me a choice
on this) but i made no mention of my wanting to plant it at this
location. he continued to question me for about 5 minutes before i
was finally able to leave. needless to say, i dont think i will go
back and place a box there for anyone else to have the same
problems. but i do have to wonder, what are they trying to hide and
what do they think i would have tried to take from the cemetery?
again, the cemetery is about 4-5 miles from the hospital itself.
any thoughts from anyone???
jb

Well I find that very interesting too!  The history of that location gives me the creeps just thinking about it, and then to think that they were so protective is just bizarre!
 
If I remember, the hospital was a prison before it was an insane asylum, right?  And of course, now it is part prison and part mental hospital.  So there are probably inmates buried there as well as patients.  Hmmm... somehow the thought of the patients has always been more unsettling to me, maybe because RSH was known for the experimentation they did on mental patients many years ago.
 
It really does seem odd that the state would send security guards 4 or 5 miles to check out suspicious activity instead of just calling local law enforcement to check it out.  It IS, after all, JUST an old cemetery, right?  Maybe you could have gotten away with telling him you were doing research about your long-lost great-uncle who was rumored among the family to have died while at Rusk State Hospital, alone and rejected by his unenlightened family.  Don't know how you could've explained the box, except that it was part of a hobby you are involved with.

(a) Any person who wishes to visit a cemetery or private burial grounds
for which no public ingress or egress is available shall have the right
to reasonable ingress and egress for the purpose of visiting the

Genealogy is probably the best excuse for being in a cemetery, but
I've never been questioned about being in one. If I know I am going
to a certain cemetery to hunt a letterbox, I go into Findagrave.com
and see if someone is asking for a photograph of a particular grave
in that cemetery. I print out the name and, if I can, I find the
grave and take a photo, then when I get back home, I post the photo
on the website. It's a way for folks doing genealogy to get a
picture of an ancestor's grave without having to travel a long
distance.
