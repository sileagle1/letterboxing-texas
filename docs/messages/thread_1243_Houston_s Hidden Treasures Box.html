Houston's Hidden Treasures Box

From: the carrascos (a.carrasco@earthlink.net) | Date: 2005-08-28 13:50:48 UTC-05:00
Ok you all, the box is at the first treasure of Houston...a purple palace of piggy delights called PIGDOM in downtown Houston, just a little north of the Museum district.  I chose to start the box here because first of all the story behind the pig that was the inspiration for the houses decor is wonderful (you can read it and see pictures of the house at www.austinchronicle.com/issues/dispatch/2000-10-06/cols_daytrips.html );  but also because for me it represents one of the greatest things about Houston, you never know what you might find when you leave home.  There are oddities and surprises around every corner.
  Ready for the clues?  Ok, go on and park near PIGDOM (located at 4208 Crawford Street, Houston).  Get out, walk by, and enjoy.  How many pig puns can you find?  Stand facing the house.  To the right of the driveway you will see a small wooden "boardwalk" crossing the grass.  The box is right underneath this walkway at the end closest to the street.  Bring something to drop...as you may have to be awfully sneaky to get this box.  However, when we were there this morning we didn't see a soul, and even with all the kids doing pig imitations trying to summon the pig's ghost we didn't attract any attention.  Remember to let this list know when you find it, and even if someone else grabs it before you do I highly recommend you take the time to see this house.
  When you re hide post clues here, and also please e mail me off list as well, because I am going to list this on LBNA as well.  OINK OINK!  -Mother of Five
