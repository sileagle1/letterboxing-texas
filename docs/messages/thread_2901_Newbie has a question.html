What do you do when you see another person/family out letterboxing? Do
you speak to them or is everybody in super stealth mode out in public?
This happened to me today and I had no idea what I should do. I said
nothing to them and just stood around my car until they drove off. I
am still amazed that this hobby has existed under my nose and I had no
idea! Kinda funny actually.....4Armstrongs

From: Amber (Cooper) Lujan (ladida@rocketmail.com) | Date: 2007-07-03 16:31:40 UTC
This happened to me too, at Brackenridge Park in San Antonio when my
LB partner and I ran into CanCan and Timeonious. We didn't know what
to do either, so we just approached them and asked them if they were
letterboxing. Cancan seemed taken aback, but she warmed up to us, and
we ended up looking for our next box together (which wasn't there).

Anyway, I was relating this story at the San Antonio meet and greet
last month, and Grizzled Griphon said that this is what you're
supposed to do:
When you meet a suspected letterboxer you're supposed to say, "Excuse
me, could you tell me which direction is North?"

If they fumble in their pack for a compass, they're not a letterboxer,
but if they point up (toward the sky), they are.

Which I guess only works if they know about that secret!
