Austin Anteater

From: Christine Borgfeld (christine@borgfeld.net) | Date: 2012-12-31 14:20:15 UTC-06:00

While boxing last weekend, Wry Me and I made an unusual find.  We made a last minute trip to Austin to go boxing, and one of the boxes we searched for was the Austin Anteater.  This is one of Baby Bear’s boxes planted in 2003.  We found this box in its hiding spot (in an absolutely gorgeous place).   To get there you have to find another box with some very vague and somewhat dated clues (Box of Ants), and use that as the starting point.  Then you have to climb up several feet of a rock face to find it’s hiding place.  Unfortunately the stamp is missing, however  the logbook more than made up for that.  The book is in perfect condition.  Only a few people have logged it, but it has numerous stamps in it.  Even better it’s pages are full of sketches and notes by people who found this by complete accident!  Now this is in a pretty difficult place to find by accident, so somehow there are people climbing all over these rocks over the years.  From the notes it’s clear most people had no idea where the box came from, but they considered it a treasure and were delighted to find it.  Even more surprising, they all took enough care to put the box back so others could find it.  With all the bad stories we hear about boxes I thought this was well worth sharing!

 

Regards,

Open Space

From: sileagle1 (sileagle@windstream.net) | Date: 2013-01-01 16:04:38 UTC
Wow, this story brings back memories! Back when Baby Bear and I first started letterboxing (2002) there were only about 10 or so boxes in all of TX and the closest ones to us were in Austin. Some of the first boxes we tried to find were by Eoghan but we were not successful since his clues were pretty tough for us rookies. So my first finds in that area were Barton Creek Greenbelt, Hiking Pug and Mt Bonnell.

Over the next several months Baby Bear and I tried to find Eoghan's boxes (O-Knots, Gordian Knot, Wraith, Peacocks Cry) but were only able to find Peacocks Cry and some of the O-Knots. Then we noticed a new box placed in Austin by somebody called Ants1 and were excited to know another letterboxer was active in the area. Baby Bear was first to look for Box Of Ants and was successful. He liked the area so much that he went back to place his Anteater box as a tribute. I then made the trip and found both in early 2003 and that success helped me finally find all of Eoghan's boxes except Wraith and Gordian Knot. It turned out that Gordian Knot was missing and Wraith was thought to be, but Baby Bear and I made one last effort to find it and were finally successful. Later, when Ants1 no longer participated, Baby Bear adopted Box Of Ants but I doubt he has visited it since.

With all the boxes available now it is hard to believe we used to spend days searching for 1 box in a city over 150 miles away. I also remember one or two boxes by Mattke's that were very challenging and I hope the clues get republished for others to attempt them. Thanks Open Space for posting this story and allowing me to take another trip down memory lane as we start a new year.

Silver Eagle

From: Vicki Bell (vicki@bellmotel.com) | Date: 2013-01-01 11:08:48 UTC-06:00
I’m so glad that Open Space and Wry Me found “Box of Ants”.  I contacted the Mr. Mattke  about two years ago about “Box of Ants” because the logbook was full, and the box needed maintenance.  The Mattke family had placed two boxes in Austin, “Park & Hide” and “Box of Ants”.  Mr. Mattke was very nice, but said they had placed the boxes when they were home-schooling their children.  Now the children were grown, and he had moved on to other interests.  He asked me to adopt the boxes.  Baby Bear had already adopted “Box of Ants” on LBNA, so I adopted both boxes on AtlasQuest, and “Park & Hide” on LBNA.  “Box of Ants” was one of my first finds in Austin, but only because I lived very near it, and had the local knowledge to figure it out quickly.  When lionsmane was visiting some years back, he was the one who solved the “Park & Hide” clue, but it took me multiple trips to actually find the box.  Unfortunately, the link to the map that the Mattke’s used in the “Park & Hide” clue no longer works, so I hope the hint I posted is helpful.  I really admire how clever the clues were.  I am guilty of posting “directions” to boxes, not “clues”.
 
I mailed the “Box of Ants” logbook back to the Mattkes, since it was full, but here is their signature stamp image, which was the cover of the logbook. 
 
I originally found only the chewed lid of the original container, and the logbook in “Box of Ants”, so I have replaced the logbook, the carve, and the container, since Mr. Mattke was not interested in doing so.  This means I have committed one of the cardinal sins of letterboxing – replacing an entire box, but still listing it under the original owner.  I just can’t take credit for those marvelous clues!  And I did not want the box to “die”.  (I think some critter up there really likes the taste of pink stuff and Tupperware.)  The “Anteater” carve was still there when I found it, but I’ll be happy to re-plant it if Baby Bear would like to mail me a new carve (or give it to me at TALE). 
When I found “Park & Hide”, all the original parts were still there – with plenty of comments by geocachers who had stumbled upon it.
 
I actually drew out all the directions of “Gordian Knot” on a large piece of paper, and used it to find the hiding spot described, but no box.  Dawn’s Glory and I are convinced we found the Wraith’s hiding place, but again no box.  Still, Eoghan’s clues were so clever, that the solving was fun, even if the carve was long gone.
 
Happy New Year, everyone!  -- Bell Motel

The clues to Gunslinger are still on LbNA.  Eoghan's clues for Wraith and Gordian are gone unless someone has a copy of them. I know where Gordian and Wraith were planted but the original clues are really a big part of charm of Eoghan's boxes. I have the images and clues for the 4 O'Knots,as well. It would be interesting to try to recreate those store bought stamps with hand-carved stamps. Maybe we could have a contest to see who could come closest to the original images.

Another problem with the O'Knots is that the orienteering posts that used to be in Bastrop State Park are probably gone and I do not know if or when they will be replaced. I know that at least one of the four referenced posts in the clues did survive. Perhaps others did as well but the terrain has changed in much of the park. Many/most of the trees and logs and such are gone. At best we could recreate the spirit of the original boxes.
