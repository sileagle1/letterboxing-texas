This is a pretty good little safety tip....
 
Following the disaster in London, the Ambulance Service has launched a
national "In case of Emergency (ICE)" worldwide campaign.
 
The idea is that you store the word " I C E " in your mobile phone address
book, and against it enter the number of the person you would want to be
contacted "In Case of Emergency".
 
In an emergency situation ambulance and hospital staff will then be able to
quickly find out who your next of kin are and be able to contact them. It's
so simple that everyone can do it. Please do.
 
Please will you also email this to all the people you know, it won't take
too many 'forwards' before everybody will know about this. It really could
save your life, or put a loved one's mind at rest.
 
For more than one contact name ICE1, ICE2, ICE3 etc.
 
It's a great idea, store it in your phone then pass on the message to your
family and friends.

There IS indeed at least one story of a bomb squad being called out on a letterbox, so most letterboxers are very conscious of the potential problems of planting boxes in sensitive areas!   You'll find a few, as MO5 pointed out, that may not be wisely hidden, but even though the placer should've been more forward thinking in placing, it is up to the finder to be smart about sneaking about in sensitive areas too.   When planting, you should always think ahead to how a non-boxer might look at an unidentified and hidden container and bear in mind that if you are putting your name (trail name or real name) on the box, it would be YOU they come looking for in the event someone considers the box to be a threat.

Safety is and always will be a concern for letterboxers and it's a really good topic that comes up periodically on the various letterboxing lists.

 

Realize though, that only a portion of the risks we face are from the human factor.  Fortunately, I think most risks can be handled by observing the same four standards:   Be Prepared, Be Aware, Be Cautious, and Be Wise.   Now if I were telling this to Cub Scouts, I would have them yell each of those points as loudly as they possibly could so I could know they would remember them.   You can yell them if you want.  Yelling is good for a lot of reasons!

 

A multitude of situations can come up that affect your experience differently than someone else's experience and differently than the experience you might have had yesterday or would have tomorrow.  You have to be on your toes and you have to be flexible and willing to change up at a moment's notice.  If you aren't on your toes and constantly aware of your surroundings, you may miss seeing the big alligator nest across the trail until you are too late to avoid it.   Or you may miss seeing the hawk mama teaching her baby to fly overhead.  Or you might miss seeing the big guy hanging out by the van parked over by your car when you exit the trail.    Or you might miss the string of cutter ants carrying their crop back to the mound.  The risks and rewards of each letterbox are constantly changing.

 

Only you can decide how much risk you can take before the experience ceases to become fun and becomes scary instead.   For my family, we sometimes play a What If? Game and think up worst case scenarios and figure out how we should handle them so we can see ourselves successfully working our way through a difficult situation.   This gives me confidence and a sense of security so that I can have fun while I'm out there.  If I can think of a situation and I can't think of how I would successfully handle it, I would probably avoid the situation.  

 

While I think it's very important to know the risks, I also think it would be a shame to allow the risks to ruin the fun.   After all, we face risks every day of our lives, and yet life is worth living every moment to its fullest!
