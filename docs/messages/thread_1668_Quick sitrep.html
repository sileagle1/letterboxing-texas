I've got two sickies in my house right now, so I
wasn't really inclined to hang out around the house
after work today breathing in the germs. I headed out
to find a geocache that tricked me once (but only
once, as it turned out today!), and in the meantime I
checked on a couple of boxes in the same general area.
I have to say - after months of walking on dry
ground, it was really nice to slip and slide around a
little in the mud! Yea rain!

I checked Blue Butterfly's Love's Lookout box. It is
in fine shape and dry and cozy even after the rain.
Well visited AND well hidden - a nice combination!

I also checked my own Killough Monument box. I pulled
up to the very secluded area and noticed they had
brush-hogged around the perimeter, right in the area
my box was! My heart stopped for a moment because
this box has gone missing once already and I really,
really want to have a box there, but I didn't think I
could take losing another one in that location. I
approached the place it was and saw no sign of it,
until I saw that the hiding place was actually covered
with leaves. Dug a little and there it was, the box
was sadly crumpled under the weight of the cover, but
it was happy and dry, and had certainly not been
lonely! I replaced the container with one I had in my
car and headed off happy to have had a 4/4 success
rate (two gc's tried and found, two boxes checked and
found to be intact).

Lightning and I are planning to head off on a short
box run tomorrow - no sense sticking around the house
for us healthy folks! Nobody ever said I would make a
good nurse and besides, they are both adults and not
so sick as to not be able to take care of themselves
or else I would leave care packages at their bedsides
before I leave for the day! My mama's approach was
"stay in your room until you're well - see ya then!"

Lucy
