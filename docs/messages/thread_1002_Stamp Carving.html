From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2005-07-06 02:19:56 UTC
And for those who want some carving practice, Postal Letterboxing
may be an option to get into. They have a "newbie" pool so that
beginners won't feel intimidated.

I've just gotten into one ring, so I don't know the ins and outs of
how it works and can give no advice. But there are some folks on
this list who are real experts in Postal Letterboxing (think of it
as a postal art exchange) who I am sure would be happy to give some
pointers. You do see great examples of carvings from letterboxers
across the country. I have been amazed when I've peeked at
TeamKing's logbook. It's fun to see the work of people whose names
you know from the chat lists, but whose boxes you might never get a
chance to visit.

From: Boots Tex (redbarn@eastex.net) | Date: 2013-08-01 20:05:09 UTC
It seems that sales clerks in Michael's and Hobby Lobby don't know anything about carving materials or supplies, and sometimes it depends on where you live, some stores carry it, some don't.  I buy the pink stuff at the Michael's in Conroe and it can be found in the art department there, along with pens and pencils, oil paints, canvases, that sort of thing.  I think the pink is the best available.  Stampeaz makes her own material called Oz Kut and, although I have heard that it's very good, I've never tried it.  I think it works well if you are a gouge carver, but not so good if you use an X-acto knife for carving like I do.  There has recently been a problem in the manufacture of the pink carving material, which is made in Spain, so there's some available in stores that is inferior; seems like it crumbles or something, but I think they are aware of the problem and are working to fix it.

Boots Tex
