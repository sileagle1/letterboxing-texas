Dinosaur Valley State Park

From: Boots Tex (redbarn@eastex.net) | Date: 2012-11-14 04:42:31 UTC
I got two emails today from the Ranger at Dinosaur Valley State Park. They were about my two boxes there, Digging Up Bones and Howling at the Moon, which is an old box that I adopted from DrooP. Both of them were permitted in the summer of 2009, but expired after a year and were never renewed. He had the old permit for Howling at the Moon, but didn't have the old permit for the other one (I do have a copy). Lone Star Quilter has a box there, too, but he didn't mention it. That makes me wonder how he even knew about my two boxes. I answered him asking what I needed to do and pointed out that the new combination permit forms don't say anything about expiration after a year and asked if that would apply to old boxes. I'll wait to see what he says.

Boots Tex

Below is the answer I received today from Dinosaur Valley.  He still hasn't asked about LSQ's box.  I think one statement he made is very telling.  That's where he says, "I am not real sure what the policy is with TPWD".  Obviously, each park kind of makes up their own rules.  I intend to go ahead and permit the two boxes that I have in the park.

Boots Tex

"To try and answer your questions.... First off I am treating all the letterboxes and the geocaches in our park equally, asking them all to renew or fill out a permit. I do have your permit for howling moon but it doesn't say that it is for digging up bones as well. I will make a note on it so that we know this. I am not real sure what the policy is with TPWD, but I know that in our park when it comes to geocach or letterbox or any other types of these things, we want to permit them so that we know exactly where each one is as to make sure that it doesn't interfere with any cultural or natural resources.  We do not mind having this things(letterbox/geocach) in our park, in fact it helps us bring in customers, but we just need to make sure that they are in places where people will not get hurt or disturb anything of importance. As soon as we can get a new permit for your two boxes we would appreciate it.  I hope I have answered all your questions, if not feel free to remind me. 

Michael Hale 
Dinosaur Valley State Park
Maintenance Specialist III"
