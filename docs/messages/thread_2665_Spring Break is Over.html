Howdy everyone in Letterboxing world.
 This is DustySandStorm to let you know that during Spring Break, the crew (boyfriend, daughter and son) and myself went to Palo Duro Canyon Tx and was attempting to do both Letterboxs and geocaches there. For the most part it was great fun, really it was, until this 44 year old, overweight, out of shape, smoker   DustySandStorm aka ~Stormie~ "died" while on a little 2.8 mile hike (as the crow flies, that is! Turned out to be closer to 6 miles of rough terrian). I buried myself mentally at Sitting Up Mouse Cave. Physically had to bring the body back to school and work per orders of my 16yr. old daughter who said I wasn't getting out of it so easily just because I felt like I was dying,... So, this now ex smoker, on a diet, working to build endurance up, new adventerous spirit of creating a better person from former self plans to go back in July and place a "Memorial of this event" Letterbox / Geocache at Sitting Up Mouse Cave... so look forward to the listing, and my first carved stamp.
 
Yours Truely,

From: Mike - KD9KC (mike-2007@elp.rr.com) | Date: 2007-03-22 00:10:16 UTC
GO FOR IT!
 
3 years ago I weighed 275 lbs.  Today... right now after a 16.5 mile bike ride at 13.5 MPH, I weigh 209.5.
 
It was not easy.  My wife had to ride me - I love food.  She put  -US-  (that is important - work together) on the Atkins diet.  The first two weeks was tough.  Then my body got to burning fat.  Ant the weight started coming off.
 
Later - I checked with a doctor about some other stuff.  He noticed my weight loss (about 25 pounds by then) and asked how I did it.  So I told him.  He said he was against the Atkins diet in general, but he was more against being overweight.  So he ran up a few tests on me, and two weeks later told me to KEEP AT IT.
 
Now - I never was really out of shape.  I could still out-hike and out run my Cavalry Scout son even at 275 lbs.  But 209 is so much better.
 
I have had to make sacrifices.  Especially Coca Cola.  I loved it so.  But my substitute is now Diet A&W Root Beer.  I don't get pizza as often.  But I feel better.
 
After Sept 11th, the Army refused to take me back into Combat.  Overweight and high blood pressure.  Today that is no longer the case.  They have my son on the front lines, they think that is enough.  And if I go back, I damn sure don't want to be a cook.
 
So......  GO FOR IT!  You can do it.
 
Good luck.
