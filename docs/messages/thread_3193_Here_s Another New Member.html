I will try to have a few more boxes in El Paso just for your visit.  I am thinking you will want to skip the "High Point of El Paso" letterbox.  It is an 8 mile round trip and a 3050 ft climb.  The El Paso Tin Mines is a 6 mile hike, but only an 800 ft climb.  All the others are relatively easy.  A few are down-right simple, almost wheel-chair accessible.  There are 8 boxes in the El Paso area right now.  I hope to make it an even 10 soon.  I might even let you hike with Parker.  <grin>

From: Mike Olbrisch (mike-2007@elp.rr.com) | Date: 2007-11-25 22:23:05 UTC
There are four state parks in El Paso.
 
I volunteer at Franklin Mountain State Park.  It is 37 square miles of desert mountain - It runs from downtown El Paso to the New Mexico border.   Much of it is quite untouched, as it takes a good hike to get in there.
 
Note - a volunteer from any park is able to help in any park.  So I sometimes pop up in other places too.  That is how Hueco Tanks knows me.
 
Wyler Aerial Tramway is another park in El Paso.  It is a TRAM RIDE to the peak, with a nice platform up there for viewing the city.  I recommend a nice Friday  or Saturday afternoon trip, stay to watch the sunset, then watch the city lights come on.  Bring a picnic - there are tables up there.  I am trying to find a way to work a Letterbox into that place.  It will probably need to be kept in the gift shop and asked for.  I wonder what the boxing community thinks about that?  I will have to ask that question in another E-mail.
 
There is Hueco Tanks.  While not really inside the El Paso limits, it is only 10 miles outside the city and the park is considered part of the El Paso District.
 
Last is the Magoffin Home.  I understand that as of 1 Jan 2008 it will become part of the Texas Historic Commission, and TPWD will no longer "own" it.
 
Yeah - it is kinda lonely being the only real letterboxer out here.  There are a few that play at it, but not many.
 
Mike.
