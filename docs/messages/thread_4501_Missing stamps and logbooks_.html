It's not a good idea to replace a stamp, at least in my opinion, although that's a very generous thought. If a logbook is missing or ruined, then putting a replacement log in the box is the thing to do, if you have one with you. But a stamp is the soul of the box. It's what you take with you (the image) to remember the beautiful or historic place or the wonderful hand-made logbook. I don't think it would be considered proper etiqutette to leave a replacement stamp. You should contact the placer and let them know the situation, but it would be up to them to replace the stamp if they so choose. Does anyone else have an opinion on the subject?

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2010-04-14 20:53:44 UTC-05:00

Because y'all know I like to play devil's advocate ---

What if. A boxer were to carry a special sort of stamp they carve to place in a box where the stamp is gone or destroyed - say, an OOPS! stamp. When they find a box with a missing stamp, they leave their OOPS stamp in the box as a temporary stop gap until the placer can replace the stamp. Perhaps they could then keep the OOPS stamp for a similar occasion when they are out boxing, or they could return it to the carver.

This makes it very clear that the stamp in the box isn't the intended stamp, but it gives a finder something to stamp in with when they find the box before the placer can replace the original.

I know a lot of folks get really disappointed if they can't stamp in because the stamp is gone.

I think the idea of leaving behind a temporary stamp until the placer can replace is a thoughtful gesture, but I also get why it isn't a good idea. I don't think I would be offended if someone were to leave a stamp behind in one of my boxes so the next finder has a stamp to stamp in with, although I wouldn't really want them to try to emulate the spirit of the box.

BUT THEN - if someone were to do this, would the next finder be able to consider the box a find because they didn't find the box/stamp/logbook combination they were looking for? Hmmmm.....interesting.
