Texas Letterbox Atlas

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-06-11 05:35:32 UTC-07:00
I mentioned in a post last night that one resource we
in Texas have is the Texas Letterbox Atlas, a
volunteer project maintained by Jeff of Birds of a
Feather.

I realized there hasn't been much discussion of this
really cool site in quite some time, so possibly our
newer members aren't aware of it.

First of all, here's the link:

http://birdsofafeather.netfirms.com/cgi-bin/lbatlas.cgi?

Spend a little time navigating it and getting to know
how it operates - occasionally it is a little slow so
patience is in order, but this morning it was clicking
right along and www.letterboxing.org was dragging
horribly. Even on slow days, it is well worth the
wait because you are very quickly able to see what
types, how many, and which boxes are in an area. You
can also recenter and zoom, so you can easily move
around an area to see what boxes you can go for.

You say "You mean I have to send my clues to yet
ANOTHER site now?" NO! The TLA site isn't intended
to be a logging site, or to store clues, it is
strictly an interactive letterboxing map. It's great
for people (like me) who don't easily take different
boxes and relate their proximity to each other. It's
definitely different from either AtlasQuest or LBNA,
which overlap quite a lot in function. Instead, TLA
is a complement to either of those sites.

It looks like most placers have given permission to
post their boxes since the LBNA site has 637 boxes
listed in the state and TLA has 573. Jeff doesn't
list boxes if he doesn't have permission by the
placer, but he stays on top of it pretty closely if
you've given him permission. I know, though, that
Birds of a Feather is a really busy family, so if, by
chance, your boxes don't get listed as quickly as you
would hope, cut him a little slack because he's
probably tending to one of the many irons he has in
the fire.

You can add your own boxes by using the "add a new
feature" found at the bottom of a map page, so you can
add your own boxes. I did this quite awhile back, and
it seems like I was able to enter everything about it
and it went into a queue for Jeff to post.

So anyway, I just wanted to throw out a little
something for you new folks to chew on and try out!

Lucy
