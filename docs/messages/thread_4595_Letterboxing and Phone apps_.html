From: Heather Carnes (heathercarnes@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2010-07-25 12:36:33 UTC-05:00
I saw two apps (there are likely more) for iPhone.  One (about $2) only searched AQ.  Another (about $5) searched both lbNA and AQ.  I bought that one.  It is convenient and - as far as I can tell - accurate.  I can save boxes to a "backpack" so that they can be accessed offline.  That is convenient if you are ever able to pick up a box unexpectedly but are in a spot where you don't have any bars.  That being said, I usually just use the websites, too.  It's not something I use every time I box, but it has come in handy a couple of times when I found myself in a position to 'box but hadn't printed any clues for the area.
 
Necessity?  Definitely not.
Handy?  Yes.
Recommended?  Nyah.  Depends on your personal boxing habits.  I like having it, but I could live without it.
