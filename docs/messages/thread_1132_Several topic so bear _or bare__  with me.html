I cant speak about anything but the barn raising
it is in the very early planning stages, so all that
is known is what you have. It IS October 23rd and it
IS in Waxahachie. It will be an all day affair.

As Im sure you know, an old-fashioned barn raising
was held when new settlers came along. The neighbors
gathered together and raised a barn for them in a day,
so they could get their farm working. If these events
didnt happen, often settlers were doomed for failure
or at least an uphill struggle. In the same vein,
its difficult to get and keep new letterboxers if
there are no boxes around for them to find. Because
barn raisings are synonymous with the great frontier,
and since Texans still have a great frontier attitude,
it seemed only appropriate that we do our own sort of
barn-raising!

Johnson, Ellis, and Navarro counties currently have no
boxes (although they will probably have a couple
between them before the barn raising happens). We
would like to go ahead and seed these three counties
with boxes on the premise that If you plant them,
they will come. So on October 23rd we will all
arrive and spread out and plant boxes in the morning,
then come together around lunch time and share a meal
and do exchanges and possibly have a couple of other
fun activities. After weve spent some time together,
well head out and go find the boxes we planted, but
leaving them for letterboxers-yet-to-come.

My intent is to reserve a pavilion in a park in
Waxahachie to serve as home base and for us to gather
at. There is no camping except RV camping in
Waxahachie, but theres camping at Bardwell Lake about
twenty miles away. I imagine my family will camp at
Bardwell both Friday and Saturday nights.

Im in the process of gathering information about the
area, including town names, parks and trails, history
and that sort of thing so that we can come prepared
with boxes ready to plant. I had a really, really
cool website all fixed up, until my computer made
confetti of it! Ill rebuild it this weekend and add
to it as we get more information together!

Look to Ruby Tuesday for t-shirt info!
