From: Mike Olbrisch (mike-2007@elp.rr.com) | Date: 2007-08-16 00:04:59 UTC
 
Dear Mr. Good,
 
    Good afternoon sir.  My name is Mike Olbrisch of El Paso, Texas.  I am a volunteer in Franklin Mountain State Park.  This park is 37 square miles of desert mountain range and has only 3 rangers patrolling.  Being retired from the Army and quite capable in the outdoors, the rangers appreciate having me patrol the places they cannot get to.  Ask them about Mike and Parker, my Great Dane hiking companion.  We are well known out in the high places.
 
photo here - delete this text....
 
Mike & Parker on the eastern slope of the Franklins.
 
 
    Having a bit of an understanding of the TPWD budget, I have found some unique and interesting ways to help bring $$$$ into the park.  One of those ways is to post the hiking trails on the internet.  Please go to www.localhikes.com and find TX - El Paso.  I have nearly 50 hikes posted in total, although not all are in the Franklins.  I get many requests for more hiking information in the franklins from this site.  Perhaps if your parks are not listed, I may be able to help you get a local to volunteer to become a hike reporter.
 
    Something else I did, and the main subject of my letter is Letterboxing and Geocaching.  I placed some of the first Geocaches in the El Paso area in the Franklin Mountains.  This was done with the permission of the rangers long before TPWD had an official policy.  I wish I could say it was 100-% perfect.  But we both know that there are people who could screw up a hammer.  Still, the overall result was a net gain of visitors and $$$$, and only one negative incident.
 
    I started out just Geocaching.  When I learned of Letterboxing I was immediately interested in it.  I now actually enjoy it a bit more than Geocaching.  I quickly learned that the average Letterboxer was a little more responsible than the average Geocacher.  There are fewer Letterboxers and letterboxes are spread out, (we are working on that) so each boxer takes a bit of responsibility for every letterbox visited.  We kind of "watch over each other" so to speak.  Since the letterboxes are less frequently visited, and the average visiting letterboxer takes care of a letterbox problem if one is found, there is really less trouble here than with the average geocache that is visited frequently and deteriorates quickly because only the owner really watches out for it.  I could copy you several notes from our Texas forum (or you could subscribe - ??? Lucy?) to see for yourself.  When a damaged box is found, it is immediately taken care of by the visiting boxer and/or the owner notified and arrangements made.  I personally have rebuilt and replaced one letterbox in San Marcos TX, and recovered and replaced two in El Paso that were in danger after the July 2006 floods.  I suppose you did hear about Franklin Mountain State Park and the floods?  I was one of the first in, right after the dozer cleared a path for my jeep.  Yup - that bad!  They are STILL digging out some parts of the park.
 
    In any case, I am requesting that you talk to and consider working with the appointed (by us) representative of Texas Letterboxing to the mutual benefit of both parties.  I assure you sir, we are not interested in causing you trouble.  We are friends of the parks and friends of nature.  We use the parks.  We vote for the parks when on the ballot.  We practice LNT.  We would not wish to hurt or destroy that which we most enjoy - our hobby in the outdoors.
 
    Some of the current rules are a little tough to deal with.  The "check it every quarter" rule is a bit tough when the owner isn't from the area.  I handle the majority of it myself in the El Paso area.  I am kinda the "GO-TO" guy for Geocaching and Letterboxing.  Please don't rat us out, but I have a sort of under the table agreement with the 3 rangers.  I watch over the stashes in El Paso and interface for them.  So far with the exception of one incident we have had nearly 6 years of trouble free fun.  I do not have current data on the impact I have had in visitors and $$$$, but I know it is quite positive.  If you call the rangers I am sure they will back up my claim.
 
    I hope you will listen to and hear-out our Letterboxing representative.  We are all reasonable folks just looking to have fun in the end, just like everyone else.  I feel certain some agreement can be worked out.  It has certainly worked in El Paso, and in several other states - states that even put out letterboxes and geocaches themselves to draw visitors.  I am certain out representative would be happy to show you how to get started with that also.
 
    If I can be of assistance please do not hesitate to E-mail me.  mike-2007@elp.rr.com
 
Thank you sir,
 
Mike Olbrisch.
TPWD Volunteer.
El Paso, Texas.
