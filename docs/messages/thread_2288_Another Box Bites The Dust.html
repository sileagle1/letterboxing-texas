Another Box Bites The Dust

From: Silver Eagle (sileagle@alltel.net) | Date: 2006-11-04 22:15:40 UTC
Another State Park has confiscated one of my boxes, but at least
there is a chance it will be rehidden in the park. It looks like
the ranger is aware of my other boxes in State Parks, so it probably
won't be long before I here from them, too. Here is the email
followed by my response:

Silver Eagle,

Thank you for promoting our park (and other state parks) with your
hidden
letterboxes, I think its a great idea and a fun way for people to
get out and
enjoy the outdoors. Regretably, our park policy is to no longer
allow
letterboxing or geocaching here because of some problems we have had
in the
past. One example is that people have gone off-trail in sensitive
areas of the
park and overturned lots of rocks looking for geocaches. Any
alterations to the
landscape, even tracks from people simply walking off of established
trails,
take a long time to heal out here in the desert and disturb the
wildlife and
scenery. Moreover, if caches were placed in or near archaeological
sites (which
are often not easily recognizable to the untrained eyes of the
general public),
well-meaning letterboxers or geocachers could unintentionally damage
these
sites. Another major reason for our policy is simply that there
are so many
scorpions, poisonous spiders, and snakes out here that people
reaching into
crevices or overturning rocks is asking for trouble (and disturbing
protected
wildlife). It would just be a matter of time before someone was
bitten or
stung, and our park is a long way from medical attention.

If you are interested, I can talk to the park administrators about
the
possibility of relocating your microbox to an area in or adjacent to
a
developed area of the park such as the parking lot, trailhead,
picnic shelters,
etc. where there is no chance of impacting the natural and cultural
resources
we are charged with protecting and reduced risk to letterboxers from
defensive
critters. However, I am not certain that they will look favorably
on the idea.
In the meantime, your microbox sits safely in my top desk drawer.
You are
welcome to come by the park and recover it, or I can mail it to you
if you
provide postage and an address.

Note: Our park will be closed to regular visitation for hunting
from 5pm on
November 5th to 2pm on November 10th, and again from 10pm December
3rd to 2pm
December 8th.

Sincerely,

Jack Johnson
Interpretive Ranger
Seminole Canyon State Park
432.292.4464

And here is my reply:

Thanks for letting me know about my letterbox and offering to talk
to the administrators about placing it somewhere else that meets
their specifications. I would prefer my box stay somewhere in the
park if possible. If there is no place in the park that works, maybe
they could keep it behind the counter and bring it out for
letterboxers that ask for it. This would still promote visitation
to the park without any damage and involve little work on the
staff's part. Please let me know what they decide and I will change
my clues accordingly. Thanks again.

Silver Eagle

P.S. I understand your concern about damaging sensitive areas and
endangering wildlife, but I find it ironic that your park, and many
other State Parks, allow hunting. I'm not against hunting, but it
seems that has the same potential for damage as hikers going a few
steps off a trail.

Good point about the hunting - I hadn't ever thought of that particular point before.
 
All in all, I thought they took a pretty reasoned position, although it's another example of making a blanket policy because of problems with a few.  As you said, at least they contacted you and at least they left the door open. 
 
You know, I get their being liability-aware, but by virtue of being a natural area and having trails through that natural area and not conducting guided tours, they are risking the same liability.  I think a point to be made is that we are well aware of potential risks as evidenced by the disclaimer and warning printed on each clue automoatically - that covers them AND letterboxing in general from liability in the face of idiots who insist on being careless.
 
Good information to have...

From: Ruby Tuesday (celticlions@comcast.net) | Date: 2006-11-04 17:34:32 UTC-06:00

We recently received a very cordial email from the ranger at Fort Griffin – our letterbox there went missing a very long time ago, but he had done an internet search apparently to find our clues. He was complimentary about the historical detail in our clues and genuinely friendly-sounding in offering to help us locate a new spot. I thanked him and assured him we would ask for permission & help if we decide to replace the box. It’s a wonderful park with ruins of buildings from the original fort, not far from Possum Kingdom, so we may take him up on his offer.

 

I think we’re all going to be hearing from officials more frequently in the near future. AQ seems to try to protect clues from being casually found by internet browsers – don’t know how effective it is – but I’m not sure that LbNA does the same thing.

Perhaps I should send a query to Choi?

 

RT
