Tuesday's trip

From: (blhjrh@aol.com) | Date: 2006-08-09 21:19:42 UTC-04:00
Yesterday PuddleSplasher and I made a little circuit from Keller out west to Weatherford (getting that bus back on the road!) to the Greenwood Cemetery to visit Oliver Loving and Bose Ikard's graves,  and then looped south to Granbury looking for Kantexan's Legends of Granbury boxes.  Found John Wilkes Booth, but Jesse James seems to have departed this scene after all (but it was fascinating to read the inscription on his tombstone!).  Stopped in Acton at Elizabeth Crockett's monument in the cemetery there to look for Baby Bear's Davy Crockett, which I did find last year, but this year, lots of piled up brush around the rock wall with PI growing all over it sort of hampered our search and we came away empty-handed, but still, yet another fascinating historic cemetery (we seemed to be following a theme here!).  Then back toward Ft Worth with a stop in Benbrook to find the Memorial Oak - an amazing tree, which actually was the site last year of another letterbox, part of the Leather Caveman series which seems to have somehow disappeared from the earth and the websites, but a great location for a letterbox!  Going through Ft. Worth, made a quick stop in the Botanic Garden so I could stamp in PS's O'Keefe's Poppies - the only one I'd not stamped yet.  A good day, even if we didn't find all the boxes we went for, because the places were all great to explore!  And, oh yes, I did manage to place my Mozart's Magical Mystical Mystery Box, for which I'm still writing clues.  Very satisfying day, even in the heat!
