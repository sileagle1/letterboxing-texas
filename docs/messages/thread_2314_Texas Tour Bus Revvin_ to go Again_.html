Texas Tour Bus Revvin' to go Again!

From: samuraistampers (samuraistampers@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-11-19 23:37:50 UTC
The Bus is back in the Metroplex. It made a brief stop yesterday at
the Many Meese Mini Meet in Flower Mound. A moose tried to get on
board, but the bus is full up! It almost closed the doors on his
antlers but got away just in time. If you would like to find the
tour bus, you need to first become "Haunted by Waters"

After you have been haunted, make your way back to the concrete
trail and continue on the concrete trail until it dead ends. At the
dead end you need to go stand under the sign that warns about 2
wheelers staying on the concrete. From this sign make your way down
the dirt path leading into the woods. Go 54 steps until you see a
split thin tree leaning against another tree on your left about 10
or 15 feet from the path. Stay on the path and go another 56
steps. On your left you should see a medium sized tree with its
trunk split into 2 and then three trunks about 5 feet from the
ground. This tree should be 9 paces from the path. Stand on the N
side of this tree and go 6 paces at a bearing of 60 degrees. You
should now be at a rather large oak tree entangled in a mass of
vines and broken branches. On the east side of the oak is a fallen
tree about five feet away. Look on the west side of the fallen tree
at the end closest to the big oak. The box is hidden under a lot of
leaves and sticks.

Here are a couple of warnings for you: this is a pretty long trek-
about 1 and 1/3 miles each way. There are a lot of thorns between
the path and the box. Wear long pants and sleeves and gloves. I
apologize for the thorns, but I have found in my experience, that
thorns seriously discourage noxers, but are not going to keep a well
prepared LBer from the box. I wanted to make absolutely sure that
all the hard work the TX group has put into these amazing stamps did
not get stolen or vandalized! Finally, this box is HUGE and HEAVY!
I brought it in my backpack and rode my bike.

Have fun!
