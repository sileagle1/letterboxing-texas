From: Boots Tex (redbarn@eastex.net) | Date: 2010-06-18 02:29:10 UTC
We had a nice day letterboxing in Washington County today (one box was in Fayette County). We had clues for 14 boxes and we found all 14 and all were safe and dry. We also checked on my Flathead box near Burton that had been reported missing and found it to be alive and well and exactly where it was supposed to be. It was hot to be sure, but most of the boxes were in cemeteries and historic parks and so were mostly easy finds. And, I must say, they were all very nice letterboxes and I would recommend them all. Most of them have an interesting historic theme and story. Tomorrow, San Jacinto County will be blessed with the presence of Grizzled Gryphon and Frost Maiden, or so I hear.

Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter

I believe we found those two boxes around the time you placed them, but I can't find them in my log and I can't find them listed on either AQ or LbNA. Well, it's too late anyway. We were in Brenham yesterday but back home today waiting for a mysterious lb'er to come around. Who knows what mischief he's up to today? Yesterday we were hunting a lot of Baby Bear boxes in cemeteries (he seems to be obsessed with cemeteries lately for some reason) and kept seeing a flash of red (with white hearts?) hovering around his hiding places, like they're haunted or something. Strange. One of his boxes was being guarded by a Baby Deer (really!) We had to shoo it away to get to the tree where the box was hiding. We also saw a grey fox in a little nature park in Burton.
