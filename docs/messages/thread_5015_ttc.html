From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2012-05-07 07:28:04 UTC-05:00

We came to the same dilemma a couple of years ago with the second Bus. It was so full that the lid would hardly stay on. The decision was to make it an event box and a new travelling box was started - the Circus.

I understand that some folks haven't seen the Circus and would love to get their hands on it - the problem I see with allowing it to continue to rotate is that it almost becomes a liability to get because there is so much fabulous artwork and hardwork in it, that when the finder has to go out to place it, it is hard to find a place that is secure enough to ensure it won't be found by anyone besides us, and then all our collective creativity is gone in an instant, much like the first Bus. That, and if it is brimming over, the risk of the lid coming loose and the contents scattering become greater. The lid thing is easier to fix - the hiding thing is more difficult.

My .02
BarefootLucy
"Not about footwear, it's about philosophy"

From: Boots Tex (redbarn@eastex.net) | Date: 2012-05-07 14:06:50 UTC
I guess great minds think alike. We were just talking this morning about the retirement of the Circus and starting a new traveling box. My idea, since we have had a bus and a train, was to have a stagecoach, like the Butterfield Overland Mail. But I like the idea of Bozo wearing baggy pants and dodging raging bulls. Would Bell Motel be willing to start the Rodeo and Wild West Show? If the circus is making a farewell tour without new passengers, there's no reason that the Rodeo couldn't have its first performance simultaneously, is there? The Circus has been around for over three years so that's not a bad run. I have a couple of observations about the traveling boxes. The first is that, in my opinion, there should be a size limit for the stamps. There are some really large Circus stamps and that just makes the box fill up faster. I know there were some published rules or guidelines for the first two buses, such as prompt notification when a box is found so that folks won't travel a great distance only to find that the box has already been picked up. I was considering a trip when Bell Motel reported her find, and I appreciated the notification, but that hasn't always been the case, so maybe we should revisit the rules. I know the traveling boxes have owners, but I think all of us feel like partial owners and feel the responsibility of ownership. One other point, and I'd like to hear other opinions on this, is that the first two boxes counted as one find for every stamp, so you could really pad your find stats by finding the box. I think that generated a lot of excitement and was a good reason to go out of your way to grab the box. For the circus box, all of those 42 stamps only count as one find. That's a lot of work for one find. What's your opinion?

Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2012-05-07 10:31:56 UTC-05:00

Since I don't count finds or plants, I don't care either way.

BUT - if we wanted to be really hard core, we would stick with "you only had to go and find one box - it just happened to have a lot of stamps" so Traveling Box=1 stamp mindset. HOWEVER, considering that the Traveling Boxes are not your run of the mill boxes, and they do take forever and a day to stamp all the stamps, I can understand saying each stamp is one find.

On the flip side, if the Traveling Box were to only count as one find, then the carvers of all the stamps shouldn't get credit for a plant, but if each stamp equalled one find, then each carver could count their contribution as a plant as well.

As one who usually likes to play by the most traditional of rules, I would still lean toward doing the 1 stamp=1 find thing - in the instance of traveling boxes only.

FWIW, my .02,
BarefootLucy
"Not about footwear, it's about philosophy"
