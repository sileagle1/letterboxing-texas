What do you use to stamp with?

From: Tara Burton (mselly13@juno.com) | Date: 2009-02-01 19:38:33 UTC
I mean, when you get to a stamp and see that it's purple, what do you
use to stamp it in your book?

I ask this because we were out checking on some of our boxes yesterday
and found that a group that uses gold glittery for their signature
stamp had used it on all of our boxes. We didn't have anything with
us to clean them with so we will be going back today.

Should the color of a stamp be put in the clue?


Re: What do you use to stamp with?

From: Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter (janet@redbarnretreat.com) | Date: 2009-02-01 20:20:52 UTC
If the color is important to you, you should put that information in
the clues. Unfortunately, no matter what you suggest, you can't
control what color the finder is going to use. If all they have is
black, that's what they're going to use. Probably, some of them don't
understand or don't care that you put some thought into your stamp and
that the image was designed to be stamped in a particular color. If
the finder doesn't follow your suggestion, the stamp is messed up for
the next finder. Again, there's probably nothing you can do about it
other than clean it up when you find it, as you are doing, or hope that
the next finder will clean it up for you and use the right color of
ink. I have a box ready to place at Mission Tejas for the TALE6 event
and it really needs to be stamped in silver ink. I am going to make
that suggestion in my clues and place a silver stamp pad in the box,
then hope that finders will either bring their own silver ink pad or
use the one in the box. Problem is, I don't know how long the ink pad
will last, and I can't be there to make them use it anyway, so you just
have to do the best you can and hope the finder will be considerate of
you wishes. At that point, you've done all you can do.

Boots Tex

From: Puddle Splasher (cjagger405@earthlink.net) | Date: 2009-02-01 22:30:18 UTC
When I first started letterboxing in Connecticut, it was the custom
to include the stamp pad in the box. I don't know if they still do
that or not, but it surely was convenient! That way, when someone
found your box but realized they had left their personal stamp at
home, they could leave a fingerprint in the logbook! (I saw quite a
few of those accompanied by a note.)Around here (Texas)most people
seem to carry their own "arsenal" of stamps or pens to color the
stamps with, so we usually let the stamper choose. I have one
letterbox I hid in honor of my two newly born granddaughters (a
couple of years ago) and I placed a pink stamp pad in the box to
encourage people to use pink. Whether they do or not.... well I can't
worry about it! I imagine what concerns you is that the metallic inks
seem to leave behind a slick surface on the stamp and makes it
difficult for the next couple of boxers to get their ink to stick to
the stamp. I guess those that choose to use a metallic ink (and why
not?) should just be aware of that and clean the stamp after they've
stamped with it. It's just important not to use a scented cleaner
which may attract animals to the box. Complicated, isn't it?
Puddle Splasher

From: Christine Velez (christineauger@gmail.com) | Date: 2009-02-01 17:10:00 UTC-06:00

Here's another take on it:
I actually like it when the stamp isn't clean.  I have some really cool images in my logbook that have resulted from my ink mixing with the previous stamper's ink, and it creates an interesting link between myself and other stampers.  The "ghost" of their stamp image is combined with my image.

For what it's worth, I think that if ink color is important to a placer, then that placer should put an ink pad in the box along with a note that reminds every finder that it's important to the placer that all use the provided ink. 

As for me?  Eh.  Use whatever you want.  I consider my plants to be the property of the letterboxing world, and for the enjoyment of all.  Including those who use metallic inks.   I loathe the idea of trying to control how other people stamp into their logbooks.

Chrispy

Re: [letterboxingtexas] What do you use to stamp with?

From: Sandy Milfeld (pugapalooza@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2009-02-01 18:28:08 UTC-06:00
This brings up something I've been wondering about for a while.  I've had some nasty images in my logbook because previous boxers used metallic ink and didn't bother to clean it off afterward.  While discussing this with another boxer, I mentioned that I rarely carry stamp cleaner with me, and found it frustrating that the images were ruined by the metallic residue.  That boxer chastised me because, apparently, we are ALL supposed to be cleaning the ink off of the stamps...with cleaner....every time we stamp in.
 
 Now, I've heard that frequent use of stamp cleaner can ruin the rubber of a stamp.  I don't know if this is true, but I DO know that in order to "prime" the stamps that I carve, I stamp and let the ink dry, then repeat several times.  (A couple of stamp carving tutorials say the same thing.)  So, I assume that stamping in and leaving the ink (regular ol' ink) on the stamp isn't necessarily a bad thing.  I do, however, wipe each stamp with a paper towel when I'm done...or if I've forgotten a towel, I use my shirt.  (I only wear black, anyway. :-) 
 
Soooo, should we all be cleaning those stamps?  Should metallic users be cleaning them? 
 
Black Widow

Re: [letterboxingtexas] What do you use to stamp with?

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2009-02-02 07:41:25 UTC-06:00
I know for my part, I don't carry stamp cleaner - heck, I don't even OWN stamp cleaner!  If I'm cleaning a stamp at home, a damp paper towel usually does just fine.  If I come to a stamp in the wild that seems a little gunky, I have a damp paper towel that I keep in a baggie, and I try to clean it with that.
 
I do agree though, metallic seems to be worse than other colors about fouling a stamp. 
I hadn't ever thought about the point Chrispy and Vickie brought up about mingling our own colors with those of the finders already past, and that is a pretty cool idea; however, from a purely practical perspective, I've found that old ink can foul the tips of my pens, so I guess I prefer a clean stamp and I'll try to clean it myself if it's gunky.  The problem I've run into in cleaning before I stamp in is that there is sometimes a little moisture from the cleaning stuck in the crevices, and that affects the image too. 
 
Still though, no matter what the clean/unclean, metallic/non-metallic situation, this isn't one of those things that gets me worked up.

Re: What do you use to stamp with?

From: Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter (janet@redbarnretreat.com) | Date: 2009-02-02 18:58:14 UTC
I do carry stamp cleaner, and I'm not afraid to use it. I tend to
like clean images and, if possible, I stamp with the color that the
planter intended, based upon the image they stamped in the logbook.
Most of my stamps are intended to be black on white, but many of
LSQ's stamps are intended to be colorful and a black and white quilt
or a "muddy" quilt due to a dirty stamp just isn't the same. You can
do as you choose, of course, since she's not there to slap your wrist
(just kidding :) ), but since you're taking the time to find the box
and make the stamp image in your logbook, why wouldn't you take the
little extra time to clean it and use the right color or colors?
Stop and smell the roses is my motto.

Boots Tex
