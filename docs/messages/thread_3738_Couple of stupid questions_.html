From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-08-18 07:25:42 UTC-05:00
Heya Tara,
 
Theoretically a stamp shouldn't need to be cleaned very often unless the surface has been fouled by an odd color.  If you take it out of the box and it looks really ookey, go ahead and clean it, but ONLY use water and gently pat it dry with a paper towel or something similar. 
 
I would consider stamp cleaning as more or less a part of the owner's box maintenance rather than something every boxer should do - the reason being, if every boxer cleans the stamp every time the box is found, the stamp will wear out quicker from the extra attention.  BUT - as I said, if it looks really yukky go ahead and do it. 
 
The reason you would use only water is because most anything else you would use would leave an odor that might attract animals, or would have a chemical that could dry the stamp out.  Folks will say there are baby wipes out there without aroma, but I wouldn't want to tell someone else their stamp got gnawed up because I went against accepted thought on baby wipes and used one on their stamp anyway.  I usually moisten a paper towel and put it in a ziploc baggie, then use the same one all day.  If it dries out, re-moisten it with your water bottle.
