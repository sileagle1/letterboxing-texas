I always think it's a good idea to find at least one (preferably more,
from different finders) before you plant one...it gives you a better
sense of good hiding spots and gives you ideas about how you want to
set up your box. I really would suggest that. Even with doing that,
we made a lot of newbie mistakes...but sometimes you just have to
learn at the school of hard knocks.

All that said, I understand that someone always has to be the first in
an area, so I'm going ahead to give you more info. You also might
want to read the tutorials at
www.letterboxing.info
www.atlasquest.com
www.letterboxing.org

They are very helpful.

A letterbox is usually a plastic tupperware-type container. Many of
us prefer boxes under the locknlock brand name. You don't have to use
those, but you want a box that is not that hard to close and has a
good seal. You don't want the ziplock or other temporary type
containers (they don't close well after a bit). This will be the
outer layer of protection for the contents...and in SE Tx it rains!
You may choose to use camo tape on the outside of the box...we can get
into brands, but you want duct-tape like material, not cloth. Others
sometimes choose to paint theirs. I often leave mine un-cammoed.

Inside the letterbox, you will want to double bag (with the good
freezer-type baggies) a stamp (often hand carved or custom made...),
something to wrap the stamp up in to absorb excess ink (paper towel or
felt or cloth), and the logbook. Inside the log (either store bought
or hand-made) you will want to stamp your family's trail stamp and the
way that you'd like others to stamp the official logbook stamp.

Some people leave ink pads or a pen for signing in. These are
optional. I really don't suggest ink pads as they sometimes lead to
soggy messes, but if there's a specific color that people should
stamp, sometimes that's helpful.

You may decorate the box or the log as you see fit--it's a more
enjoyable experience when the whole thing ties together. However,
remember that the box will be exposed to the elements and so in
general you may not want to waste your time with decorating it....but
some people have used some great creative containers that add to the fun.

As far as what stamp to plant, sometimes it's an image that pertains
to that park or area, sometimes it's a play on the park's name, or
sometimes it's just something you like that you've found a good place
to plant.

I hope this answers your questions. You are welcome to ask questions
here, but you also may find a lot of help on the newboxers board on
yahoo. It's set up specifically as a place new boxers can ask lots of
questions!
http://groups.yahoo.com/group/newboxers/
