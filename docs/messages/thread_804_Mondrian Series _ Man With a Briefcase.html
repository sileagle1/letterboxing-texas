Mondrian Series & Man With a Briefcase

From: tx2chic (blhjrh@aol.com) | Date: 2005-04-18 18:56:03 UTC

I went looking for Art to stamp this morning in Ft Worth - first, to
the Kimbell to refind the Mondrian series (I love those stamps
that "build" a Mondrian print and I wanted to restamp it to send to my
sister, PuddleSplasher, in CT). Last time I had checked, most of the
boxes were missing, but they were all there today and in excellent
condition, although a little harder to find this time as Kantexan had
warned - not to have them go missing again anytime soon! I had figured
that no one would be around, since museums are closed on Mondays, and I
could crawl through the bushes as much as necessary without being
observed, but landscapers work on Mondays mowing the lawn and testing
sprinklers, which added to the challenge. Not so lucky finding Man
With a Briefcase today, though - a press conference was going on there
with TV crews and a small crowd, and landscapers were setting out new
summer annuals in the beds on the NE corner - right where I wanted to
search for the LB! I trust they didn't discover it! Thanks, Kantexan,
for my Monday morning Art Stamp experiences - and for the tip about the
Asel Art Supply Store (I found it, and they had exactly what I was
looking for! Now, I just have to learn how to use it.)

-TX2Chic,
The pleasure was all mine! I love carving and planting as much as I
love finding! Hopefully the groundscrew won't find the Man with

it will be safe.

This is kind of related to the subject of art stamps. Did y'all
read about the renegade artist who "plants" his own artwork in art
museums around the world? Usually the museums figure it out
immediately, but occasionally he gets away with it for a little
while. I can't remember his name but it was in the Star Telegram a
couple of months ago. He sounds like a Letterboxer at heart! If it
weren't for those pesky security cameras we could go nuts
planting! ;)lol

-Kantexan
