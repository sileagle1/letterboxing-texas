Where to go, where to go...

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-08-26 22:34:38 UTC-05:00
After not dying of heat exposure this past weekend, I've been inspired to
try to get out and go again next weekend, especially when I realized that
next weekend is LABOR DAY WEEKEND!

Wow! I can't believe how time flies. Labor Day marks four years officially
for us (but no, we aren't the ones who have that annoying reminder on LBNA)

Although I learned about letterboxing quite some time before actually
getting involved, I learned about it as a homeschooling activity and we
played it amongst ourselves as a sort of hide and seek with a twist on group
activities. I somehow figured out that there was letterboxing in the bigger
world too and started checking it out. It was about four years ago right
now that I was immersing myself in learning about it so that when we
actually made a trip out we wouldn't look like total newbs.

The problem with studying about letterboxing is that there is very limited
studying that can really be done. Back then, there was nothing but the Big
List in terms of talk lists. There was only LBNA as far as websites. And
somehow, studying on LBNA was feeding me just enough to whet my appetite,
but not enough to quench my thirst for knowledge. Being the sort of person
who wants to know everything about something before trying it, it was
terribly hard for me to just say "Okay guys, let's give it a go."

I can sort of remember the conversation going "Okay y'all, we're going
letterboxing." And the response being "What's that?" And the ensuing
attempt at explanation (how did YOU first explain it to someone???), ending
as I tapered off and said something like, "well, you know, it's sort of like
that...I think." The obvious response to my feeble explanation was "WHY?"
To which I used the rare "Because I want to". Now that particular line only
really works on my birthday and Mother's Day, and it also works around Labor
Day because Labor Day marks the time my dad died and they all knew that it
was best to get me out and busy rather than laying around the house and
slipping into a funk. Fearless Fox, already the LSS, thought it was a
hoax. Thought it was a trap. Thought it had to be some sort of gimicky
something. But figured it was wiser to humor me, so he told the kids we
were going letterboxing (I remember thinking "Didn't I just say that?") I
printed off all seven clues that we could possibly reach, find, and return
home from in one day's time. SEVEN! That was ALL! I read one clue, Baby
Bear's Tejas, that would take us down a path we had taken many, many times.
In fact, I could practically visualize where he was describing the box to
be. It took my breath away to think there was a box just a few feet from
where our feet had travelled. I hooked Fearless Fox by reading him the clue
and he was determined to disprove it. Such a cynic he was!

So off we went, stopping at Walmart to buy in cheap - three cheap
store-bought stamps (none for Fearless Fox, thank you) and a little flip
book of index cards. And a stamp pad. Black, of course, off the office
supplies aisle. We passed a couple of boxes in Rusk, one in Alto, then
passed one closer to the park entrance, in our determination to go find the
one that we knew where was. We followed the clues very closely, found
ourselves where we thought we would, and as we cautiously peeked, none of us
knowing what to expect, we saw the box. It blew us all away! Amazing!
Fearless Fox was still convinced there had to be a catch, but at least he
was a little interested.
