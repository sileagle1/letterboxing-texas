From: Vicki Stecher (stecher@runbox.com) | Date: 2008-03-22 16:47:29 UTC-05:00
Since I've been thinking about carving and planting boxes of late, I am wondering if you guys could weigh in on a topic. I know I am not ready to plant around until I find more boxes and learn more. But my heart is ready to carve! So here is my question...
 
We own a Christmas Tree Farm where we live. There is over 200 acres here and there are several family members who live on the farm (self included.) We are only open 12 days a year...the Friday, Saturday, and Sunday starting the weekend of Thanksgiving. We do not charge an entrance fee and folks that come are not required to purchase anything at all. We are 45 miles East of Dallas.
 
So here are my thoughts...Would there be any interest for anyone if I played letterboxer, carved a bunch of stamps and had my fun, and hid them around at our farm? I would post the directions on both websites and folks who wanted to could come and spend a day playing on the farm. Anyone who wanted to come could and find boxes during those 12 days.
 
It almost seems silly but I thought it would give me purpose to carve for, practice in hiding and writing clues, and I'd be right here of any maintenance is needed. I just don't know if there would be any interest.
 
Let me know what you guys think!
Ladybird Beetlebug
