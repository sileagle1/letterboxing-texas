From: Mike Olbrisch (mike-2007@elp.rr.com) | Date: 2008-05-01 05:31:05 UTC-06:00
Well - serving in combat changes you for life.  Assuming you live of course.  Hmmmm - I guess if you get killed that changes your life too.  Never thought of it that way......  Had this discussion with my son a few weeks ago.
 
I know those of you who watched that video were in awe (or terror) of the trail, the height, trail width and condition, what it took to cross some of it.
 
The first time I watched it I thought AMBUSH CITY!  "One good rifleman could shut that trail down, a whole squad or larger could be held at bay and need air support to get through".  I imagined trying to rush up the trail and find cover while under fire.  It wouldn't be easy.  There are some choke-points where the assaulting team has to slow down, perfect place to pick them off.
 
The second time I watched it I was able to pay more attention to the beauty of the area.  Sure is different from the hiking in the desert.  I would like hiking there I think.  Might crap my drawers over some of the holes in the sidewalk.  But that never stopped me.  There is a mountain like that in Japan that leads to a temple at the summit.  Tried to google it, but no luck.
 
I don't regret a single thing I did.  Neither does James.  But I know my life is way different for it.  I know things about myself that most people never learn about themselves.  And I see things much differently too.  I have to really LOOK to see the flowers, it isn't the first thing I see.
 
Thanks for sharing it.
 
I have a letter here from a friend, he includes me in all the stuff he sends to my son.  He has about a page of a story of a little town that adopted him and his crew in combat, and his years-later visit to that town.  Really moving.  If a few people express interest I will drop it in here.  It really is a nice story.  You would not believe what a few words of support mean to a soldier.  Especially from strangers.
 
Well - time for work.....
