maybe i'm just babbling

From: p_quest2005 (p_quest2005@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-01-19 03:47:11 UTC
i leave y'all alone for a few hours, and look what happens. reading
the last few posts, i've been trying to come up with a believable
excuse as to why i do the things i do...and that's pretty much where
i ended up, right there at the ellipses. maybe i don't get enough
excitement in my morning bowl of cheerio's, or--well, i'm not going
to hypothesize, because apparently i'm not alone in this contagion,
in this need (NOT in quotation marks, because i see it as a
legitimate necessity) for adventure. so the ABC's might be a little
bit off the charted territory for some, but by golly, if there's an
adventure to be had, i would guess that most of the members of this
site would be there, pens in hand, ready to sign up. that's our
common denominator, with letterboxing being the excuse.
but that's enough philosophy for tonight.
if it's more straightforward antics you're looking for, like jumping
fences or brick walls, being confronted by the authorities in a
state park after hours, being questioned by local police with their
hand on their firearm, walking across a nauseatingly long fallen
tree "bridge" at night with no flashlight, or just plain ol' being
scared out of your skin (again, in the middle of the night) in a
cemetery when a bird crashes through branches right over your head,
y'all might just have found your in-house rebel. if the stories are
posted here, then they are stories i've heard tell. if you want the
the truth, you can get me offline.
but back to the point: i listened to an audio recording of The Know-
It-All by A.J. Jacobs, and i heard a quote that i haven't been able
to track down since. something defining life as being all about new
experiences that push your personal envelope, or sense of comfort,
to new extremes. i'm not advocating the addiction to adrenaline
highs (more power to those junkies, though), just an encouragement
to always be willing to take yourself out of the daily grind and put
yourself INTO a situation where you have to swallow your heart back
down into your chest, whatever it takes to get there. so, go cross
that street when the little white man is not lit up, you daredevils!
ok, i'm really done this time.

p ?uest
