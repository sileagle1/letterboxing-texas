Mike and Moni with Parker and Justin. 12 hours.

Saturday - 5 Aug 2006 and Sun - 6 Aug 2006.

This is an extensive report. We took over 250 photos, far to many to upload
to you via E-mail. Moni made a short slideshow you can watch here.

http://www.photoshow.net/bonehenge/ Click on Flood damage.

Entered park - checked in with Danny and Robert. Robert asked for help in
assessing damage to the park and trails.

#1. Nature Walk.

The Nature Walk is about 50% destroyed. The bridge foundation is shakey and
obviously the pressure of the water rushing by has shifter the foundation.
In my opinion, it is not a danger, but will need repair in the future. The
bench area is in good shape, but the wash-out cut away an area to the west
of the podium. The left fork runs out in about 75 paces and falls off into
the wash-out. The trail is totally unmarked from that point. The right
fork runs along for a while in good shape. The area where it used to cross
the wash is now a bit wider. The trail can still be seen - and the stairs
are still sound. Once up on the high ground to the west, damage is minimal
until nearing the 1/2 way point there the wash-out had its way with the
trail. In my opinion, the nature walk will not be back the way it was for a
long time.

#2. End Loop Area.

The End Loop was not in real bad shape. The paved roads were fully passable
with any vehicle. The roads could stand a cleaning/sweeping, but no damage
noticed. The dirt/rock driving paths had mild to moderate erosion. Still,
it would be passable to all but the smallest of vehicles. There was some
erosion at the restroom sidewalk area. This might be a priority, not
because it is dangerous, but because if not repaired it may worsen and crack
the slab. There was moderate erosion at paths up to the large pavilion. It
would stop wheelchairs, but not hikers.

#3. Main Road.

Following the main road from End Loop to the Ranger Booth - we discovered
several places where erosion on the west side of the road was excessive. It
is clear that some of the pavement has crumbled into the wash-out. These
areas should be marked with warning cones until repaired. The worst was
near the Aztec Cave turn-off. In my opinion, this will take more than
filling in the holes and repairing the pavement. Doing this alone is only
asking for it to happen again.

#4. Aztec Cave Area and C-3-4-5.

The trailhead to Aztec Cave is not passable to any but the most stout of
off-road vehicle. Within 50 feet of the cable the wash-out is at least
knee-deep. In one area it was up to my waste. The wash is about 50 feet
across. Once beyond this, the trail has mild to moderate erosion, as do
C-3&4. We did not go beyond there, but it appeared that the trail might
have yet more damage in the upper wash-out. C-5 had mile to moderate
erosion, but was fully useable.

#5. C-1&2 area.

The road to C-1 and 2 is not passable. The cable post needs resetting, it
will fall over soon. Beyond the cable and into the first right turn there
is a huge wash-out to the left side. The edge of this wash-out is a shear
drop and crumbling. I would avoid vehicle traffic in this area. We parked
there and hiked in to C-1 and 2. Both C-1 and 2 showed mild erosion, but
nothing that would make them unusable except the need to back-pack gear up
to them.

#6. The parking area near the maintenance park.

This area showed little damage. Pavilion 13 had some minor erosion under
the concrete base. This will need to be repaired, but in my opinion is no
danger at present. The restroom door is coming lose at the upper hinge and
the door will not close properly. I secured the door forcefully, cut you
cannot expect visitors to do that and it will blow open and damage the door.
The pavement could use a cleaning/sweeping.

#7. Road to Cottonwood Springs Trailhead.

This road could use a cleaning/sweeping overall. In the dip beyond the
lower Sneeds Cory entrance there is significant pavement damage. In my
opinion, the water probably got under the pavement there and lifted it. It
is passable to all vehicles at this time, but will need some repairs or it
will get worse. Perhaps a culvert should be placed underneath it to stop it
from happening again. The parking area at the trailhead was OK. The west
side was covered in a layer of fist-sized rocks. I would not recommend
driving on it until swept clear. The socks may cut tires, or if pressed
into the pavement may cause damage to the pavement. Trail to upper Sneeds
OK. Picnic area above Sneeds OK. The road to that picnic area has mile do
moderate erosion, passable only with a high-profile vehicle. We did not
hike farther, but optically we can see that the bridge to Mundy's Gap is
GONE! We were not able to see where the bridge remains went to.

#8. Smugglers Pass West Fork.

The trail from Smuggler's Pass to the Elephant's Trunk is about 50%
destroyed. The trail starts west going down, then turns left. At the left
turn there was a MASSIVE wash-out. The trail is gone. The bench is there,
but covered in plant debris. Knowing this trail, I hugged the left wall of
the wash-out. I found the place where the trail used to climb out, because
there were two wood-pole stair steps still attached to the anchors, but no
trail under them. Parker enjoyed jumping over them. The trail was in good
shape for a while, but soon was washed out again. I was able to follow the
trail because I know it. Others light get lost. Where the trail turns
right and climbs the switchbacks up the red wall the severe damage ends.
The switchbacks up show mild to moderate erosion, but hikers will get
through. Above that on the ridge-line, the trail was in good shape overall
- all the way to the elephant's trunk.

#9. General assessment.

It looks like the park was hit hard. Moni and I put 12 hours total into
gathering this data. I (and Parker) are volunteers. But most of the time I
come out so does Moni, and she picks up trash etc too. Seems you should
call her a volunteer and count her hours too. Also - I removed 3
geocaches/letterboxes - and notified the owners of the situation. All is
well - they may be replaced once the park is back in operation.

My work-week will not allow me to participate in much through the week.
Perhaps we can do more next weekend. I will attempt to see if any of my
hiking friends will accompany me. Please let me know what areas you would
like me to check on next. You know my abilities, Ron Coleman - North
Franklin - Vinton Canyon - just let me know where you need my hiking and
observation skills.
