Rehiding - let's take this problem and make it a positive lesson!

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-07-31 10:21:20 UTC-07:00
I think we can all agree that the one and only
unfixable mistake a letterboxer can make is not
rehiding well and causing a box to disappear.

I think we can also agree that no matter how much we
talk about it, it will continue to be a problem.

However, I think we can work together and come up with
a tutorial of sorts on how to be a good finder, so
let's do it! Once we get it done, I'll be glad to add
it to the files section and set it as a file that will
automatically be sent to new members, and even sent to
the whole list once a month or so, if that's what the
list wants. I would hope that most finders WANT to be
responsible boxers, so let's operate from that
perspective for this purpose.

Let me throw out some questions we can answer
together. If you think of other questions, please
pose them as well:

What should the placer take into account when placing
a box, so that finders can be better rehiders?

What defines a good rehide?

What are tips for a finder to follow as they rehide?

Hopefully, that will get us started.

Lucy

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-07-31 10:57:23 UTC-07:00
My own answers to these questions:


> What should the placer take into account when
> placing a box, so that finders can be better
> rehiders?

*****A placer needs to think of what will be available
to use as cover material throughout the four seasons.

If the box is in a high traffic area, the placer needs
to consider the time it will take the finder to rehide
and choose a place to minimize the time needed.
Possibly suggest lower traffic times that would be
appropriate for a finder to look?


>
> What defines a good rehide?

*********After being rehidden, the box is not visible
from any angle and the cover doesn't appear contrived
or fake. Everything that came out of the box is back
in the box properly and there is no evidence in the
immediate area that someone has paused at the site.

>
> What are tips for a finder to follow as they rehide?
******Take the time to look at how the box is hidden
before removing it from its hiding place. Look around
to make sure you know what you will use to rehide with
(i.e. if a lot of the cover is leaves and pine straw,
consider that what is covering the box when you find
it may crumble away, so make sure you have more
available). If you're boxing in a group, make sure
one person is responsible for checking the box after
everyone moves away. Look at the site from all
angles.

From: JuneMcAllister (nfmoon@mindspring.com) | Date: 2005-07-31 14:36:32 UTC-04:00
It probably does go both ways. People (and I admit I blame it on the droves of newcomers that really do not GET this hobby) are remiss about not hiding stuff well, but there have to be placers who really don't take everything into consideration when they hide either. One rule of thumb for me is if I can hide it thoroughly,  given the location, pedestrian traffic, etc,  the person should be able to put it back the same way they found it without a problem. And the changing of the seasons :::is:::: a HUGE factor in some places.
 
Considering the pace of life that lots of people allow themselves to be subjected to these days, I think some people may just be in too big of a hurry when they box. This is not a hobby that can always be sandwiched between 37 other weekend activities.
 
missmoon

Re: [letterboxingtexas] Rehiding - let's take this problem and make it a positive lesson!

From: JOY (TeamTexUS@houston.rr.com) | Date: 2005-07-31 13:54:47 UTC-05:00
 Here is my .02:
 
1. What should the placer take into account when placing a box, so that finders can be better rehiders?
 
If the box is not urban, make sure there are several paths to the box, so that a "social" trail is not created by people going to and from the box. If you are looking for a box, and see a "social" trail, don't use it. Take another route. Often, you can detour by just a yard or two. Try not to trample plants and bushes.
 
If natural materials are used to hide the box, there should be a ready supply of those materials in the area surrounding the box- sticks, leaves, bark, stones, etc.
 
2. What defines a good rehide?
 
Walk in a circle around the box, insofar as is possible due to the location, to be sure that no portion of the box is visible.  In addition, the covering material must look natural, and not call attention to the location of the box (i.e., don't pile up a bunch of sticks and stones so that they are screaming "look here!).
 
3. What are tips for a finder to follow as they rehide?
 
Make sure to re-pack the box just as you found it, or better. Use all the baggies, and double-bag if possible. Use your first aid kit as needed- paper towel for the stamp, extra baggie, etc.
 
Make sure you have a good re-hide; see #2.
 
Happy Trails,
 
JOY
