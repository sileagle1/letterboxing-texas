Texas Hospitality

From: (eplace@frontiernet.net) | Date: 2015-04-25 08:47:30 UTC-07:00

We wanted to give a big SHOUT OUT and THANK YOU to Bell Motel for her gracious Texas hospitality on our recent visit to Austin.  Crocker and I were navigating somewhat hit or miss around Austin in a rental car, while our hosts ( my niece and nephew) were at work last week.  We had identified some interesting boxes to try, but the roads, traffic and heat were presenting something of a challenge for these Northern New York country gals.  We finally made our way from South Austin along Burnet Road to the Apothecary Shop where a clever and funny clue had  led us.  When we arrived at the shop we discovered the entryway and thus the hiding spot was all dug up.  We went inside and after a somewhat frustrating conversation with the pharmacist we got a phone number for Bell's Motel.  By now, we were HOT, sweaty and not the cleanest individuals.  I wanted to give up, but Crocker said, " Well let's call, maybe we can get these last two boxes and call it a day".


When we reached Bell Motel  on the phone,  she said, " oh, come to my house, I'll give you the boxes"   It is so far out of our character and comfort zone I still can't believe we went to a stranger's house in our disheveled state or in any state to be honest.  The lure of boxes is strong!!!


I can't even begin to describe what a gracious reception we got.  We were treated to ice tea, showered with boxes from a HH Hostel,  personal travelers, WOM box, exchange stamps,shown about the yard and finally got to walk along the trail near her house and get three fantastic carvings in the Posada's Satire series.


This is the perfect example of why the best part of letterboxing isn't always finding the stamp.  Again, thank you Bell Motel.  You gave us a great afternoon and fun story that we will share about our new friend in Austin.


Fondly, 

Apple girl and Crocker
