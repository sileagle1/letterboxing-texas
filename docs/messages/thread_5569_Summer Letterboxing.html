Since the only conversation has been about no conversations, let's talk about letterboxing (placing or finding) you have done this summer like Bell did a few weeks ago.  Even though it has been pretty hot out I'm sure some of you have some stories to tell.  Also, we have some new members that I would like to hear from, so I'll start things off.

Back in May I moved to Wimberley (southwest of Austin) and am still getting used to living somewhere new after 26 years in Sugar Land.  I placed two new boxes around town as further incentive for you to visit this pretty place.  Soon after the move I went to the Great Lakes Gathering which was hosted by the Michigan sisters who Baby Bear and I met during Live & Breathe XV in 2013.  These are the same ladies who pranked us during TALE 12 by placing two boxes (via Wisconsin Hiker & Martini Man) as a joke:  Everything’s Bigger & Don’t Mess With TX Eagles.  To avoid being pranked again, I signed up for the event under the assumed name of Argent Eagle, but they figured it out and when I arrived at my campsite late at night I found signs greeting me with “Silver Eagle Rules” and “Honk if You Love Texas”.    Of course I had to get even, so I placed two boxes in one called Letterboxer Pinups and wrote the clues as if the sisters had placed them.  The prank worked and everyone at the event thought they had carved these steamy stamps of themselves and a “TX Hunk”.  There is more fun to the story that I can tell during TALE 14, but needless to say I am a marked man.  After the Gathering my wife joined me for a trip around the Great Lakes and I placed boxes in 6 new states.  I also found the Cave Point Letterbox, which is the first one placed in Wisconsin and tied for oldest box with original contents (the other one is the Rolling Rock Letterbox in Vermont), and I have now found both.  The Great Lakes is a beautiful area of the country and I highly recommend visiting.


Three days after returning from this trip we were off to Europe for over 5 weeks.  My son joined us for a post-graduation vacation before starting his job and we visited 7 countries on 2 continents:  Portugal, Spain, France, Italy, Greece, Turkey & Montenegro.  We had a great time and of course I placed boxes in all of them, but it is good to be back in TX.


So what did y’all do this summer?


Silver Eagle (or Argent Eagle if Great Lakes folks are reading this)

Don’t think I’ve ever introduced myself to the group so at Silver Eagle’s suggestion I’ll take this time to do so.  I’m in the north central Texas area. Started ‘boxing in 2007. Started out as “mthr21” and that’s who I was up until a few months ago when I decided I should change my trail name to something a little more interesting. Carved a new signature stamp and now you’ll see me logged in as “Puddledunk”. (I know what you’re thinking, “That’s not much better than “mthr21”. But when you see my siggy stamp I think you’ll agree that the name fits the image really well!) I usually ‘box with my niece whose trail name was “Baby Dur”. She outgrew her signature stamp and trail name so she too has carved a new stamp and now goes by “Glow Worm”. 

When I first started out there were a ton of boxes in Grapevine. They were all over the place there! I found myself headed that direction every week-end for several weeks. After I found a few I became interested in carving and planting. That didn’t go too well. I was terrible at planting and those went missing pretty fast. After having so many boxes go missing back then I’ve re-assed and refined my process for hiding and as of late, I’ve been doing more carving/planting than finding. My planting skills have improved I think. The boxes are staying put a little longer now not because of fewer mishaps or muggles but because it seems there is no one searching for them which is a little disappointing. Hopefully, in time, that will take care of itself.

Since homeschooling is a passion of mine and is very near and dear to my heart that has impacted the direction my carves have taken. When my kiddo was little I saw everything as a challenge to incorporate into lesson plans and to use any and every avenue available to make each topic fun and memorable. Letterboxing was no different. I frequently searched out boxes that could be used to reinforce what he was learning. Even though he’s grown now seems I can’t get away from that mindset. Most of my carves are directed towards kids and each time I carve I find myself getting excited about how I would use this box if I were doing it all over again.

My biggest disappointment in ‘boxing:  Missing the Texas Rodeo the last time it was up for grabs by only an hour at the most! Someone please, please bring it back to the metroplex!

From: (justduckie123@att.net) | Date: 2016-08-17 10:52:56 UTC-07:00

Thanks for the shout-out, Bell Motel!  Yes, earlier this summer I reached out to the Austin Public Library Yarborough branch manager to talk about placing a series in the library, and she agreed, so long I agreed to hold a "Letterboxing 101" class in the library's public meeting room. The class was a couple weeks ago, and it was a blast, especially since Bell Motel very kindly agreed to co-present and brought along some cool items like LTCs and HIPS for folks to look at. Over the next few months, I plan to approach the managers at other APL branches to place more boxes, so stay tuned for more new listings.


Overall, this summer has been a busy one the letterboxing planting front. In June, I placed a half dozen boxes in the Corpus Christi/Portland/North Padre area, and in July I placed a series and a single box near Possum Kingdom Lake in Graford.  I've also been continuing to sprinkle a few boxes here and there in the Austin area as the whim strikes. I'm particularly proud of my "Taco Prayer" box - a year ago I wouldn't have even tried to attempt something that detailed. I also just got back from a day trip to Houston, where I planted a logbook/box-less "box" in Chinatown.  I'm wanting to experiment more with planting small magnetic stamps - see how they do in high-traffic, urban areas where hiding a plastic box isn't practical. 


On the finding front, I found about 15 boxes in North Carolina while on a trip, which was great, although it was extremely hot and humid, just like back home.  If anyone is out in the Boone area, I highly recommend the Blowing Rock Library box. 


Looking forward to TAFLE in the a few months, and TALE in the spring.  For TAFLE, I am bringing a letterboxing newbie - Letter Elle - hoping to convert her to a die-hard boxer too. :)


Thanks for starting the thread, Silver Eagle!  Origami Hen
