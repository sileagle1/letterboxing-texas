Yes, I'm going to vent. If you don't like rants, don't read this.

Let me preface this by saying I really appreciate that everyone who actively participates on this list seems to recognize how nice a little courtesy is between boxers.

I had a great time going after the tour bus, even though it was hot and even though I didn't get it. It was a serendipitous bit of letterboxing I wasn't expecting to do today. Any day letterboxing, even if unsuccessful, is better than a day of housework, which is what I had in mind for the afternoon otherwise. As I mentioned in my message a few minutes ago, I got home and checked my mail to see who got it and when and then I went on to read my other letterboxing email. I had another message, a note from a finder, waiting for me. In fact, had two - on the same box - one from AQ and the other from LBNA. And it''s those messages I want to vent about...

It seems someone went and found my box at the Gus Engeling Nature Preserve and it was wet. Because it's bad form to forward a message to a group without permission from the writer, and because I don't have any intention of asking permission, I'll paraphrase. "The box is wet. Someone else left a note that it was wet when they found it back in March 2009." That was through a contact on LBNA. The contact from the listing on AQ was to the effect of "Placer needs to make repairs.". Not a kind word. Nothing like "sorry to let you know this, but ..." Not a word about how much they liked the park. How the stamp was cute. How they enjoyed the trip. All the sorts of comments I've gotten from so many of the other finders of this box. In fact, this box has proven to be one of my favorites because of the cool notes I get from finders about it. My family had a really good time the day we placed that box and so many other folks seem to have enjoyed finding it too. That makes a good box even better - the experiences that accompany it.

Admittedly, I knew the box was wet but I had forgotten about it. I should have made a special trip over to fix the log, or I should have noted on the listing that it was wet, but I just simply forgot about it. Thing is - the stamp is still there and intact, so any finder is able to get an imprint for themselves - they just can't leave one unless they leave a piece of paper of their own in the box. One of the biggest reasons I'm not planting much right now is BECAUSE I don't have the time to do proper maintenance on the boxes I have. Thankfully most boxers are prepared to do a little maintenance as they go, and my boxes have reaped the benefit of their kindness. However, I'll take responsibility for not being as good a boxer as I should be with respect to maintaining in the last year.

Still though, it galls me that someone would send a message like that to me or any other placer. As I read it, I felt I was being accused of leaving a wet box out there on purpose and they had been inconvienced by having to find a box in that condition. I like feedback on my boxes, am glad to hear when there is a problem or when a box is potentially missing, but I really don't appreciate reading a message that makes me feel my effort is being graded and deemed substandard.

Maybe they are just bad at composing email. Maybe they aren't graced with social niceties. Maybe they were in a hurry. Maybe I was in a grouchy mood (although I was coming off a pleasant run and looking forward to checking in, so I don't THINK that's the case). Maybe I'm feeling guilty for not getting out there to do the maintenance that was needed. Or maybe they ARE just rude.

If it weren't for placers, there would be nothing for anyone to find. Am I wrong in thinking that if you can't at least show a little appreciation for the effort even if not for the present condition, you shouldn't bother writing at all? I'll be interested in hearing other thoughts, even if you disagree with me.

I have to confess that it was not one of my finer moments when I responded with a message that read something like "I'm so glad you enjoyed the park and had a good time looking for it anyway. Thanks for letting me know its condition." Probably shouldn't have done that...too late now though.
--

One of my favorite things about letterboxing is the people. By and large, the people are courteous to one another and they appreciate the effort others put into the game at whatever level. When finders find boxes in poor condition, they do whatever they can with what they have to correct it. If a box is missing, they let the placer know. If newbies come along and ask questions, they help them. I don't know what it is about letterboxing that seems to attract those sorts of people, people who are a cut or two above the general population, but I like it. But because that's the type of folks you find in letterboxing, the ones that aren't quite so much that way really stick out.

You are very kind to want to make sure I don't let it get under my skin, and I really appreciate that!
