The Original Texas Tour Bus

From: Carolyn Ramirez (danielcgarcia@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2007-03-21 04:14:30 UTC
Well after a long hibernation, the Texas Tour Bus took off this past
weekend. After fighting the traffic down I35 from the Spring
Breakers, the crew decided to stop off in Austin. After a quick
detour, the bus found itself in Barton Springs. While there to take
a quick dip in the 70 degree year round swimming hole, the guys
decided that they liked it here and decided to stay. As we went
about finding some of the boxes located here, we met a couple that
were doing the peas box, "The Barton Springs Salamander" and they
asked us if we were lb'ers. Guess the cammo'd box and the backpacks
gave it away. When we told them yes, we proceeded to find out that
they were too and were fairly newbies. So we went over some of the
finer points of lb'ing with them. They have only been doing it a
short while but they "Shoot for the Stars" were very intrigued with
it. As we sat in the beautiful sunshine and stamped we started to
tell them about the Texas Tour Bus. Having only found a couple of
the stamps they were very anxious to find it. So the bus detoured
home with them and is to be replanted shortly with new clues. With
it being such a nice day, the park was very crowded and we didn't
want to take a chance that someone would see us planting the box and
take off with it, so we figured it would be better if we knew a
lb'er had it and would replant it in the area locally. The TTB
didn't seem to mind so they are hanging out in Austin and will be
back on the road again with new clues shortly. Thanks to Shoot for
the Stars for relocating the TTB for us.
