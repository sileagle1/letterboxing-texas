From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-07-05 09:16:45 UTC-05:00
I mostly use LBNA, but I can take a stab at it until someone more knowledgeable on AQ will speak up.
 
1) The box doesn't show up in your search unless you meet the criteria for the box.
 
2) This isn't used much in Texas, and I don't think it is much used anywhere.  One big reason it IS used is if a box is in a sensitive area and subject to being found if not properly rehidden.  The placer might use this to try to assure that the finder has a little experience under their belts.  There are, however, boxers who have found 100 or more boxes and are still not very good and boxers who have found 1 box and are very diligent in rehiding.  Numbers are no guarantee of anything.
 
3) I have no idea if there is a "typical" restriction.
 
4) Nope.  You can't search to find out how many you can't see.
