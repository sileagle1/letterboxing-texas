Visitor from the Southeast: RECAP

From: StarSaels (steves_1701@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-05-06 04:39:01 UTC
Hey'all!

Just wanted to let all of you know that I had a great time in the
Houston area this week. I'm back home now, having recorded 10
"traditional" Finds and one HH (the Buffalo-Gourd Flower), which will
be planted in a Georgia box soon... perhaps this weekend!

Somebody once told me that Texas doesn't have trees... they don't know
what they were talking about, or they ain't been to Houston! One of
the boxes I went after said to look for a multi-trunked tree... and
everywhere I looked there was a multitude of multi-trunked oak trees!
The coolest thing was driving on some of the urban and suburban
residential streets and feeling like I was driving through tunnels
made of trees. Astounding!

Speaking of driving, it really got me, and not in a bad way. Nobody
told me about the wacky exit and on-ramp system (which actually works
pretty good, I think). And then those dedicated U-turn lanes under US
59... what imagination... But the kicker was that it seemed like
everyone was driving within 5 mph of the speed limit!! I live on the
far outskirts of the metro Atlanta area, and if I drove the speed
limit on any highway, freeway or street, I'd get an earful.

But back to the letterboxes: I think 10 boxes is pretty good for four
days in which I actually had limited time to get out there and 'box. I
missed four (that I actually got out of the car for). Three weren't
there (pretty sure, but not positive), and one was too overgrown with
poison ivy for me to risk it.

And there's always next time!

StarStaels
