Siterep Sunday and Monday

From: tigger32167 (tigger32167@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-04-10 12:32:17 UTC
Day 1 - Sunday
First of all I want to let everyone know that I am not much of a
writer, so bear with me on this :) Easter Sunday, my puggle
(pug/beagle mix 1 year old puppy) navigator, JR, and I had about 3
hours to kill, so we decided to take off and catch some local boxes
that we hadnt found yet. We started in Rusk at Hogg Park and found
the Native born Texas Governor Series box. It was a beautiful stamp
and the location was one of my favorite walks in Rusk. From here we
went to ID Fairchild Forest to pick up Baby Bear and Silver Eagle's
boxes. The directions were great and we found the boxes with only
one minor problem. I am an even five feet tall and Baby Bear
referenced in his clue to "hop" over a creek...the problem was that
the creek was about five feet wide..LOL..so we went upstream a
little and found a place we could cross and then proceeded to the
box with no problem. We still had a couple of hours, so we then
decided to drive to Palestine to the Davey Dogwood Park and find
Baby Bear, Silver Eagle, and Barefoot Lucy's boxes. The park was
beautiful, but we missed the blooming dogwoods. Thanks to SE and
BB's invaluable directions, we were able to find all 3 boxes. We
stil had some time to kill, and looked at the clues for the box in
Tennessee Colony by BL, but after reading about the possibility of
alligators, and not want to lose my puppy, I decided that it would
have to wait until I had someone else along with me to help keep an
eye on everything. So having looked for 6 boxes and finding 6
boxes, we decided to make it a day and then headed home.

Day 2 - Monday
I was off from work today, and got up early (5:30 am) and decided
that I would take off for Troup to find the Go Tigers box. When I
got there I realized that my great, great grandparents are buried in
this same cemetery, so it was really a thrill to find this box.

Day 2 - Monday
I was off from work today, and got up early (5:30 am) and decided
that I would take off for Troup to find the Go Tigers box. When I
got there I realized that my great, great grandparents are buried in
this same cemetery, so it was really a thrill to find this box.
From here I went to my dentist appointment..YUK. After having a
couple of cavities filled (1 1/2 hours), and the left side of my
face numb, I went back to Rusk to pick up my navigator again. We
headed off towards Athens. (side note...on the way we saw a little
feud going on between a couple of neighbors...one of them had a sign
in his front yard that read "My neighbor is a thief, just like
jeffrey dahmer, he kills pets."...i don't think any comment is
needed for that) We picked up the Texas Passport #1 box at the E Tx
Arboretum which is beautiful and then proceeded to the Cain Center
in and picked up Candi Cain (still not sure what disc golf is) Now
on to Gun Barrel City to City Hall (no I didnt fight city hall) I
knew I was in trouble when i re-read the clue and it said that the
box was in an awning above the door, did i mention i am only five
feet tall? well with some strong mcgyver skills, i managed to
retrieve the box, but then there was the matter of replacing it,
which was not easy either, but with JR on my shoulders he got it
back up there..LOL. While we were there we also retrieved Have Fun
Matte'
Our journey took us to Mabank next where I ran into couple of new
species that are kin to Pinus pretendis...they are Roadus pretendus
and Parkus pretendus...but still managed to use my letterboxing
skills to find the Boxing is a Circus box. The next stop was Kemp
to probably my favorite find of the trip...the Kemp Calaboose was a
really neat site and stamp to go along with it. From here we made
our way to Terrell to find Nana's Hummingbird (great directions and
beautiful stamp) and then on to Greenville. This was a neat
little "series" of boxes, and all 4 were placed in neat locations.
We didnt find the Forest Park box, but the other 3 were all right
where they should be. By now it was about 6:30 and we decided to
head home. When we got to Tyler, we still had about 30 minutes of
light left, so we did manage to find the Starrville Stagecoach box
in Winona...again really great stamp and location. So Day 2, we
attempted 13 boxes and found 12...so great trip. By the way, I
realized somewhere around Athens that JR was really good at reading
maps and at finding the locations of the boxes, but he had a lot of
difficulty actually reading the names of the towns and the clues, so
I guess I should teach him to read the next time he navigates...LOL
Also the bluebonnets along the way were phenomenal (my big word for
the day) Thanks to all the boxers who placed the above boxes for a
wonderful adventure
