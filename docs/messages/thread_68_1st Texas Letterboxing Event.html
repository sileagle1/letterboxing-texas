1st Texas Letterboxing Event

From: Wanda (wsthm@hotmail.com) | Date: 2004-03-08 13:21:52 UTC
Didn't it turn out great?! My granddaughter, Shabree, and I had a
terrific time. We rode up with Calico Rose on Friday and 'camped' at
the Best Western. Saturday, I woke up with one of my special
headaches,which lasted all day, so wasn't at my best, but still had
a lot of fun. It was wonderful meeting everyone. What an exceptional
group of people!

The boxes I/we found were DrooP's Skinny Pine, Baby Bear's Trail
Rider and Park Pest, and on the way home, the Mattke Family's TCM -
and on that one, enjoyed the symbolism of the 'item' placed near the
box. ;) I still need to look the background up on the internet to
understand more about the box subject....being probably the only
person around who is not very familiar with the subject this box
refers to.

Shabree really enjoyed playing with all the other children.

DrooP, TWO spider bites???? AND poison ivy???? YIKES!!!

Thank you all so much for making this a fantastic weekend. I am
already looking forward to the next one. A special thanks goes to
the organizers, too!

The 1st Texas Letterboxing Event was wonderful.  It was great to meet new people and visit such a wonderful park.  I wasn't able to find as many as Droop, but I did OK.  We didn't find Lost Loblolly...we went in at the wrong end of the trail.  We'll know better next time.

Droop, take care of those spider bites!!  They can be a mess.
 
Monkeytoes...I'm looking forward to your plants in Victoria.  Lots of great hiding places there.
 
Kissing Fishies:  Thanks so much for the compass & orienteering lesson.
 
Thanks Silver Eagle & everyone else for making the 1st Annual Texas Letterboxing Event such a great success.

Re: [letterboxingtexas] 1st Texas Letterboxing Event

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-03-08 05:51:56 UTC-08:00
The gathering was wonderful wasn't it? My family had
a great time - even my husband, who made a very brief
appearance, then retreated to our campsite (he's not a
big group person). He did, though, go out boxing with
us.

We found the 10th Mountain Infantry box, Skinny Pine,
Trail Rider, Lost Pines, and Park Pest. All were a
lot of fun, but I think we especially enjoyed the Lost
Pines. It was quite a trek!

We did have a bit of an adventure at Skinny Pine -
Alex was following his dad across the waterfall when
he stepped on slick mud instead of dry rock and went
sliding feet first on his belly! His feet were
hanging over the waterfall and he caught himself by
his fingertips. He pulled himself up a bit, then
hollered for my husband to hold the walking stick out
for him to grab. He grabbed hold and pulled himself a
little closer, then my husband pulled him on out. He
was a muddy mess! OH MY! It was a little tense for a
moment or so, but I was glad to see that he can think
on his feet if he has to - I've had my doubts at
times!

I loved meeting everyone and learning a little more
about all. The food was great - but the company was
even better! Thanks, Silver Eagle, for making all the
plans and getting us all together - it was memorable!

Lucy

P.S. I'd love to get Silver Eagle's chili recipe and
the Mattkes' Thai Chicken Wraps!

Re: [letterboxingtexas] 1st Texas Letterboxing Event

From: Drew Mattke (drew@justdrew.net) | Date: 2004-03-08 21:53:51 UTC-06:00
Thanks too, to Silver Eagle for organizing everything. It turned out terrific. A little more space might be needed next time- especially if the next gathering draws more folks (and if the weather isn't as cooperative as it was this weekend).
 
It was really nice to see some of the faces behind the stamps we've been seeing. We're all lucky such talented folks are active in the hobby so close to our stomping grounds.
 
We're working to get our latest box clues online. For those of you who took the drive to our TCM box but didn't completely get the reference or couldn't understand why we put a box at a seedy old gas station, email us and we'll fill you in on the details. BTW, Wanda- that 'item' was already there, we didn't plant it with the box. Creepy, eh?
 
A series of boxes is coming soon, themed around movies shot in and around Austin/CenTex. Plus a historical series we have been meaning to plant for over a year. It'll be worth the wait if you like a challenge in your letterbox quest.
 
The Thai Chicken wraps are part of the Mattke family camping staple diet. The recipe we copped from the Food Network's 30Minute meals- here's the link to the recipe. The trick to the peanut sauce is to start with peanut butter, but don't skimp on this one: that leftover half-empty jar of Skippy you've had in the cupboard for ("when did we buy that?") months isn't going to work :-}.
We found "Laura Scudder's" brand Old Fashioned Smooth to be the best (weird stuff you actually refrigerate), or you can try the local health-food store if they make the stuff fresh (try HEB Central Market if you're in Austin). Also- gotta do the *fresh* basil and mint. You can get it dried, but resist- resist! Go for the flavor, it really makes a difference.
 
At the next event, be prepared to be introduced to the subtle nuances of the Runza.
 
Happy hunting!
-The Mattke Family
http://mattkefamily.com
