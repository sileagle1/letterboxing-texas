Site Rep for the weekend

From: tigger32167 (tigger32167@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-04-25 12:03:26 UTC
Up bright and early Saturday morning; JBK picked up WagTime at 7am
to hit the road. First we headed over to Nichols Green II so Wag
could get JBK's March series (she's a little behind in ETX) and
her "The Magnolia & The Cowboy" set. We didn't think about the red
dirt paths being covered with dew, and little JR's paws and legs
were covered with red dirt glued on by the dew. We were filthy by
the time we got back in the car, even though JR got wiped down a
bit.

The next stop was Nichols Green I to get BFLucy's Girl Scout series
and Silver Eagle's "Barefoot Den Mother". We verified that
TexasLuvBug's "Life is a Picnic" is definitely not there. As he
always manages to do, JR got away from us on the trail, and although
we were afraid of not being able to catch him, had to laugh when he
picked up the handle of his own leash as if taking himself on a
walk. After that we headed over to the Homestead Hitchhiker Hotel
where WagTime got to do some stamping and JBK traded a new stamp for
a HH she liked.

Next on the list was the footbridge in Rusk so WagTime could get
BFLucy's "Friends are Friends Forever" stamp. The stamp was
beautiful and WagTime is thankful to Lucy for such a thoughtful
gesture. We found "Railway to Heaven" (fabulous stamp!) at the
Texas State Railroad Park, then went to Maydelle. Both JBK and
WagTime had missed opportunities to visit the winery and pick up the
box there, and both were anxious to try some East TX wines. We
sampled everything and got a bit of a buzz at 10:00 in the
morning! The LB had been misplaced, but the owner was sure it was
still there and promised to find it for us. We bought a case of
wine between us and headed on out. If you go, be sure to try the
lemon wine and visit the guard-cats, May and Dell.

We went by JBK's house for a fluid adjustment break and to figure
out where to go next. We visited the Killough Monument, but Lucy's
box couldn't be found. A road had been cut outside the barbed wire
fence and we found the remnants of a destroyed L&L box; we searched
around, but found no paper, baggies, etc. Lucy, we're sure sorry!

Disappointed but with more stops to make, we headed for Tyler.
After a quick drive-through lunch, we went out to Lindale. WagTime
planted a new box just inside Smith County in honor of her adopted
grandfather who passed away last year, and then we drove around
Mineola until we found a spot for WagTime's other memorial box.
Wood County has lost its virginity and has its first box. We tried
to find the Mineola Nature Preserve, but after we turned off the
highway at the big brown sign, there was a sudden shortage of signs
and we never could find the place.

We then headed north to Emory to find a home for a new box JBK had
put together; another county (Rains) has been de-flowered. We
backtracked down to Alba and cut across Quitman to get to Hawkins,
the pancake capital of Texas, where we planted another of JBK's
boxes; Wood County now has 2 boxes! We toured Jarvis Christian
College in while in town, and then happened across a bunch of kids
with a lemonade stand out in front of their house. In the back of
the house was a terrific tree house with a full staircase (not just
a ladder) going up to it. We were pretty jealous! The next stop
was Big Sandy in Upshur County to plant another box, but we couldn't
find a suitable spot so we drove toward Gladewater until we reached
our goal. We drove back through Big Sandy to Winona so WagTime
could get JKC's "Starville Stagecoach", then headed back to Tyler
around 6:30m, hoping that daylight would last long enough for us to
make a few more spots.

We found the Children's Park in Tyler and both got the stamp there
after wandering around enjoying all the memorials to the children;
if you haven't visited this park, please take the time to do so.
Next we hit Bergfield Park for Atoy Adventure's "Meow" and JR made a
new friend. Finally, really chasing the sun, we went home by way of
Troup to pick up Blue Butterfly's "Go Tigers". We got back to the
house around 8:15, exhausted, but having accomplished a lot.

We started again on Sunday around 9:00, having decided to stay in
Jacksonville since we didn't have a lot of time. First we went to
Lake Jacksonville for TexasLuvBug's "Big Bass Bonanza" and found it
just where it was supposed to be. Cary Lake was next on the list
for JBK's "Treasure at Cary Lake". Then we came back into town and
went to Buckner Park; we were batting a thousand now, verifying that
TexasLuvBug's "Sunflower Summer" and "Dragonfly Springs" are no
longer there, and also failed to find "Lightning's 11th Birthday
Box" although JBK felt sure we were in the right spot. We had
better luck finding Lucy's "Spring Hope", and then drove down the
farm roads to get JBK's "The Rose and the Tomato Farmer" set. While
in Afton Grove, we almost gave up but did find Lucy's "Flying
Again". That email talk about ants jinxed us; the box was pretty
much buried down in a hole and lots of ants were keeping it
company. JBK took it personallythat and the briar scratches she
got while she was digging.

Running out of time, we made quick work of another cemetery so
WagTime could get JBK's April series, then went over to Jarrett
Cemetery to get "Vogue" and find a spot for a new stamp WagTime had
planned in tribute for a second cousin who is buried there. We
pushed our curfew and WagTime was a little late getting back to pack
and head back to Dallas, but we had a blast and enjoyed the weekend
and the beautiful weather.

All in all, WagTime planted 3 boxes and found 40, and JBK planted 3
plus 3 HHs and found 5. Not bad for a weekend's work!
