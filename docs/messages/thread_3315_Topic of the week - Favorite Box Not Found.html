My favorite box not found was the original Multnomah Falls Letterbox,
placed by Der Mad Stamper in 1998 as part of his Columbia River Gorge
Waterfalls series near Portland, OR. It was in the fall of 2002,
just a few months after I started letterboxing, when I got to take a
Business trip to the Pacific Northwest. So far I had only found a
few boxes placed by Baby Bear (except for my first find in Spokane)
so I was pleased to be going to an area rich in letterboxes. After
my conference, I took a few days of vacation to enjoy the area and
letterbox. I worked my way down from Seattle, WA to the Portland, OR
area, finding every box I looked for and having a great time. The
scenery was beautiful, but nothing compared to the Columbia River
Gorge. I was getting spoiled by finding every box, so I was
confounded when I couldn't locate the Multnomah box. The clues call
for you to find Five trees together, two are twins from one trunk,
with Multnomah Falls at 125 degrees. I looked for hours with no
luck, but every time I tried a new location I would look up at the
falls at 125 degrees and just sigh with its beauty, so it was a
pleasure.

Well when I got home, I started hearing that everyone has trouble
finding that box and it became an obsession with me. I thought about
it off and on for a year until I finally had an inspiration. Luckily
I had another trip to the area in fall 2003, so I headed back to the
Gorge. This time I was successful and it was even sweeter after a
year thinking about it. So this box was a "not found the first
time", not a "never found" box.

Silver Eagle
