Hey Mike, that's great to hear! More boxes to find on my trip there
in December!  

Well - The Culberson County Letterbox will be relatively easy to get to, about 200 feet from the vehicle.  But it is over 100 miles from El Paso.  So unless you are coming in or leaving via US-62/180 through Guadalupe Pass, this one is a bit of a drive.

The Hudspeth County Letterbox will only be about 20 miles away from the Eastern city limits of El Paso, and only about 12 miles from the Hueco Tanks Letterbox.

 Hoping to plant a couple for you to find too. But I'm
not too sure about those 8-mile hikes you have planned for me. I'm
middle-aged, overweight, and out of shape.  

The most difficult Letterbox in El Paso is this one.  8 miles R/T and 3050 ft climb.  But worth it.

http://www.letterboxing.org/BoxView.php?boxnum=18163

http://www.localhikes.com/Hikes/N_Franklin_Mountain_2320.asp

And this is the next most difficult.  6 miles R/T but only 800 ft climb.

http://www.letterboxing.org/BoxView.php?boxnum=19238

http://www.localhikes.com/Hikes/FMSPTinMines_2320.asp

 I'm good for about 4 miles on a relatively flat surface, at sea-level. 8 miles in the mountains would probably kill me. LOL! 

Then comes this one.  About 2 miles R/T.

http://www.letterboxing.org/BoxView.php?boxnum=22454

http://www.localhikes.com/Hikes/ElephantsTrunk_2320.asp

The rest are EASY.  Well - easier anyway. 

Save Cattle on the Mesa for an evening out, and be HUNGRY when you get there!

Just remember - we are at 4000 feet+ here, and this is the DESERT!  I don't care if it is December.  BE PREPARED!  Rather dump water at the end of the hike than need water 1/2 way through the hike.  Everything sticks-stabs-bites-burns.  There isn't much shade, so wear a wide-brimmed hat.  It may be 20 F at 0600 and 70 F at 1600.  Sturdy shoes are a MUST - and trekking poles can help.

 Scared?   Heh heh heh - you WILL be.....

Come have a ball!

Mike.
