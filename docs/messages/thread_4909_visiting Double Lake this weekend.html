Re: visiting Double Lake this weekend

From: Boots Tex (redbarn@eastex.net) | Date: 2011-11-16 19:03:49 UTC
We live literally right around the corner from Double Lake. Unfortunately, we have a scrapbooking retreat at our place this weekend, so we'll be busy hosting and cooking for 20 ladies. There is only one small motel here, the San Jacinto Inn, but I wouldn't recommend it. Your best bet here in town is the Sunday Houses. They are a very good bargain and are new and clean. Here's a link:

http://www.coldspringsundayhouses.com/

They are right across the highway from us, so are only about 1/2 mile from Double Lake. There are only a few, so you might want to call. Your other option is to stay in Cleveland, where there is a nice motel right on Hwy. 59 at Hwy. 105, which is about 16 miles from Double Lake. There is a small barbecue place right across the road from the entrance to Double Lake (look for the big revolver bbq pit). There is also a hamburger place called The Hop, similar to Dairy Queen, a Subway, and a home style restaurant about a block from the courthouse. It was called Elaine's until a couple of weeks ago, when it was bought and the name changed to "Bubba's Too". We haven't eaten there since it changed hands. The lake is indeed dry. We had a good rain yesterday, but I doubt that it helped much. Despite the lack of water, the park is still very pretty and the leaves are turning. It's a nice place to hike. A few boxes are located on the trail around the "lake", including our very first boxes. Since the Lone Star Trail goes right through the park, most of the boxes are actually on the LST, although close to the park. Last time I counted there were about 32 letterboxes there and I think most of them are intact except for the few that are noted as missing. You are welcome to stop by and get our "Red Barn Retreat" stamp, but unfortunately, due to the retreat, I won't be able to offer you my cinnamon rolls.

Boots Tex
