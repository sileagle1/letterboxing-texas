How much fun can one have in one day?

From: jayceeinbedford (jayceeinbedford@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-09-03 23:56:06 UTC
We just got back from a day at the Cleburne State Park. We had a lot
of fun. It was a bit overcast, so it was beee-you-tee-full!

I would post the pictures, but my dog ate the cord that goes from the
camera to the computer. Bad dog!

I do believe we have to go back to the cemetery in Cleburne though.
Barefoot Lucy warned me, but I'm a little slow catching on.

Delivery Girl made us work for our stamps, but we perservered and
found what we were looking for.

I'm ready to start planning tomorrow, but I don't know if my family
can keep up with me.

If y'all want to have a gathering in Cleburne State Park, I'm ready
and willing. I didn't know Texas had such beautiful parks like that.
Without Letterboxing, I still wouldn't know. And, I would've wasted
this day at the apartment pool or worse, in front of the TV.

Enjoy the rest of the weekend everyone, I'm off again,
Janet, Bedford

From: Maribeth Jagger (cjagger405@earthlink.net) | Date: 2006-09-03 20:20:16 UTC-05:00
Have you been down to Cedar Hill State Park yet (just south of Dallas off I-20 between Grand Prairie and Dallas). It's a beautiful park also - well worth exploring! Silver Eagle and Baby Bear  have boxes (Texas Star and Campground Bandit) hidden down there. Viewfinder and I have several boxes hidden at Cedar Ridge Nature Preserve not too far from the state park, and the Cummins Clan have Duncanville Dragonfly in a park in the area also! Just another fun day of letterboxing adventures!
 
I know what you mean by having to make a return trip to the cemetery in Cleburne. Viewfinder and I realized we have to go back also...... after we got home!
