Siterep for Mission Tejas State Park

From: tigger32167 (tigger32167@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-07-13 22:12:30 UTC
Well I went out to Mission Tejas today with my navigator,(my puggle
JR). Even with it being Friday the 13th, we had a lot of success
and found 4 really great stamps. We started with the "shorter" walk
to find Will's World's "The Pipebangers:Fraggle Rock Series." He
says in the clues that it is a long walk and is "hilly". However,
they were long hills, which went up and down both ways. We did take
the "shorter" walk, but it still ended up being over a mile rt.
However the walk was worth it. It takes you to the CC Bathtubs
which is a really neat site in the middle of the forest. The stamp
was also really cool and ties in well with the site. After we
recouped from the walk, we went to the mission site. When I pulled
up the first thing I noticed was that the area where Silver
Eagle's "Spanish Flag Lb had previously been hidden was taped off
with yellow "Do Not Enter" tape and that they were clearing that
little area for a utility pole. So the log that "Spanish Flag" was
behind was no longer there. So being me, I looked around and didnt
see anyone, so I crossed the tape and went to look for the box. I
did find the box in a hole totally uncovered. So I took the box out
of the hole and found it a new hiding spot, which I will email
Silver Eagle the clues for. (Silver Eagle, if this is not
satisfactory, please let me know, and I can go pick it up and mail
it to you, or whatever you would like for me to do with it. I just
figured the best would be to find it a new home...the box and
contents were in perfect shape). I then went to find Boots
Tex's "Nabedache" box down a really nice trail and found it safe and
sound. I loved the stamp and location was great. Next, we went to
the playground area, and found Princess Aurora's "Tejas Bell"
resting nicely. The stamp and the story that goes along with it are
really great. Our next stop was the pond area and I did check on
Baby Bear's "Tejas" box which is safe and secure and then went to
find Lone Star Quilter's "Indian Trails -Quilt Block Series." The
box was safe and sound. I am always so impressed with the quilt
block series. They are such wonderful designs. So, besides Silver
Eagle's box being in jeopardy..it was a great day and four wonderful
boxes. (I had previously found Baby Bear and Silver Eagle's boxes,
but they are also wonderful) So much for the Friday the 13th
superstition...Thanks to all the wonderful letterboxers/carvers out
there for a great day.
JB Kokopelli


Re: Siterep for Mission Tejas State Park

From: Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter (janet@redbarnretreat.com) | Date: 2007-07-13 23:49:21 UTC
Thanks for the nice report, JB, and we're glad you liked our boxes.
That was a good thing you did for SE's Spanish Flag. That box has
been in that spot for over 4 years. When we were there 2 weeks ago,
the log was still there and the box was well hidden and in perfect
condition. I'm glad you could save it. That's a pretty little
park. When we went with our grandchildren (6, 4 and 8 months), they
took note at the office that we had children and one ranger came up
to our campsite and gave the kids little bug-catching kits with nets
and magnifying glasses. He was friendly and funny and the kids
loved him. He also has story-telling sessions around a campfire at
scheduled times, so if you go, check the schedule for this and other
programs.

Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter
