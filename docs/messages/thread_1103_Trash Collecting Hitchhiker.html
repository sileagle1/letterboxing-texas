Trash Collecting Hitchhiker

From: monkeytoes108 (cruschhaupt@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-07-24 23:33:03 UTC
Okay, I posted pictures of the completed Trash Collecting Hitchhiker
in the photo section (In the 'TCH' folder). It's two 35mm film
canisters glued end to end and secured with packing tape. A log
book and small stamp fit in one end and a Wal-Mart sized shopping
bag goes into the other. It's a tight fit, but you really can
squeeze that thing in there.

It works like this- You find it in a regular letterbox and treat it
like a regular Hitchhiker, making all the appropriate stampings.
Then you remove the bag and collect any trash you find along the way
back from the box. Dispose of the trash at your earliest
convenience and place a new bag into the Hitchhiker before placing
the whole thing in another Letterbox.

I stole the idea from a geocaching activity called Cache In/Trash
Out. They just leave a loaded film canister in the geocaches for
litter collection. It's a wonderfull idea that I wanted to bring to
the letterboxing community. It seemed to me that it would work
perfectly as a HH. Not oly are you prettying up the places we go
to, but you get a (F)ind out of it.

I've made two so far and will be placing them soon. If you're going
to make a hithhiker anyway, I ask that you give this a try.

-MonkeyToes

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-07-24 17:18:28 UTC-07:00
Oh, I LIKE this idea!

I'm going to begin teaching Leave No Trace next month
and preparing for it has caused me to have a crisis of
conscience about letterboxing. I've come to the point
that I can justify it because I am planting boxes in
durable areas and I'm checking them so that they
aren't abandoned. Also, through letterboxing, I'm
inviting folks into areas that heighten their
awareness of their natural surroundings and hopefully
through awareness we can foster stewardship.

I still can't say that letterboxing and LNT are
completely compatible, but I can live with the
difference if I change a few of my letterboxing habits
and this one plays right in.

I'm already looking toward making smaller boxes even
though I prefer carving larger stamps and I like
making a box big enough for someone to slip a HH into.


BUT-I'm definitely going to make my future HH's this
way, and I think I may start my boxes by leaving a
trash bag in them and writing it into my clues to
request that finders bring a trash bag with them in
case the one I place is missing (because if everyone
brings one, theoretically there would be no need to
take mine!)

Could we come up with our own designation for this
practice?

Lucy
