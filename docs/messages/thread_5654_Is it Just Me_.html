Is it Just Me?

From: (candj1969@yahoo.com) | Date: 2019-06-14 18:44:31 UTC

Is it just me, or does everyone have a box full of Letterboxing Stuff? (Click on the attachment to see the photo).  LSQ's having me clean out some shelves in the garage, so I opened this box to see if I could throw anything out.  

There are log books that were full.  I could spend a day looking through them, but I don't have time.  In one of them, I found the stamped image of a letterboxer from Taiwan.  There are a few retired letterboxes.  Maybe I'll replace them some day.  There is the 1998 issue of the Smithsonian that I've been intending to read cover to cover.  There are some stamps that were never used and a few that I re-carved to replace one that went missing, only to find that, after driving 200 miles, the missing box wasn't actually missing after all. There are a couple stories that would make interesting letterboxes.  Maybe I'll do that one day.  A bag of carving material, just bits and pieces, but maybe just what I need when I carve my next stamp.  A pathtag or two.  A hitchhiker that I mean to leave in a certain box, if I ever get out that way.  A bag that got too small for all my letterboxing stuff.  I think I'll put it all in a flatter box and slide it under the bed.  I might need some of it someday.  She'll never know.

I did the same thing a few months ago when I was told to move my letterbox stuff out of sight and tried to downsize (even my little letterbox work space is shrinking).  I have many damaged logs sent by kind letterboxers all over the country who replaced them for me, several first finder prizes, old maps of TX with counties grouped by region when I was helping get the LBNA TX map changed, old clues from GG when he used to only hand them out personally, old cipher clues that I solved and keep for future reference, and tons of pill bottles waiting to be turned into future letterboxes.  I got rid of very little since I am a nostalgic kind of guy, and just moved it all to a secret cubby hole. I'm glad to hear I am not the only one with this nice problem.
