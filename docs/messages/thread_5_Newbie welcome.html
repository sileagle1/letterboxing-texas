Hi Susan!

I guess I'm the last on this list to speak up with an
intro!

I've been letterboxing formally since last summer,
although we had played at it for awhile longer -
planting boxes as a kids' game basically. My family
boxes together - my husband, my 16 year old daughter,
and my almost 8 year old son. We've also introduced
our cub scout den to it, although as yet none of them
have jumped into it.

I'm in Northeast Texas myself, near Tyler. The few
boxes in our area are precious - they are few and far
between, as are boxers. I've been grateful to Silver
Eagle and BabyBear, since they have planted all the
boxes up here, but two people who live a few hours
away can't be expected to do all the work, so my
family is beginning to take on the challenge of
getting more boxes in up here - we planted our first
two a couple of weeks ago (just a hint - one of them
is in the Tyler Zoo, and the other is at Killough
Monument), and as soon as I get a chance I'll be
posting the clues on www.letterboxing.org. We have
several more ideas, so hopefully we will get them out
soon.

My family has big time commitments in soccer, scouts,
and my daughter's drill team activities, so we stay
very busy and don't get to go boxing very often. What
we've gotten from LB'ing is a means to get out and
away from phones, computers, and televisions by
ourselves as a family, get into the woods we love, and
then to have the fun of hunting boxes. I find that
when you are following someone else's clues to a box,
you see the surroundings for just a little while from
their perspective. That has really enhanced our
enjoyment of the woods we have hiked for so many
years. It blew me away the first time we went looking
for Silver Eagle's and BabyBear's boxes in Mission
Tejas - we've hiked all over there numerous times, and
to discover that someone else had been to "our" woods
and planted boxes along the very trails we visit two
or three times a year was amazing to me (this may not
strike you the way it did me, but I am easily
amazed!).

The area of letterboxing that has been most
challenging for me has been stamp carving - I'm not an
artist! I tried for the longest time to study and
think and work and design, and then to figure out
technique and method - before I ever set knife to
material. It just didn't work that way - I finally
just dove it and gave it a try. It's worth the
learning though - it really is exciting to see your
own stamp take shape.

Well, enough about me! Looking forward to meeting you
and everyone else at Bastrop!

BarefootLucy
