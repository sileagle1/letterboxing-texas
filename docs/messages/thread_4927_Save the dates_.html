Things that may help your search for Hill country state park:
Enchanted Rock - Not box friendly
Pedernales falls - Big park with lots of trails
McKinney Falls - Not box friendly
Blanco SP - To small, no trails
Guadelupe River SP - Lot of trails, but boxes seem to go missing
Inks Lake SP - Good trails and big park
Bastrop SP - TALE 10 will be held here in March of 2013, so may
be better not to double up.
Buescher SP - Not much trails
Longhorn Cavern - Good trails, but has a lot of boxes already
Hill Country SRA - Big park with tons of trail, but limited facilities
Kerrvile Park (use to be state park) - Great park, many trails and
good facilities.

Hope this helps.

From: Boots Tex (redbarn@eastex.net) | Date: 2012-01-13 00:58:58 UTC
McKinney Falls is actually a beautiful park, and we did lose two boxes that were planted there. In our case, the boxes were not found by park personnel but were picked up and turned in by a park visitor, or at least that's what we were told. They only lasted a couple of weeks. I personally don't think the park rangers search out letterboxes because they have better things to do. There are many ways for boxes to go missing, so anytime you place a box anywhere, you are taking your chances, and state parks (in my opinion) are no riskier than any other place.

Boots Tex

From: Boots Tex (redbarn@eastex.net) | Date: 2012-01-13 18:44:15 UTC
Okay, that does sound like that ranger doesn't like letterboxes. We just got an email that they had been picked up and we could come to the park and claim them, but we never did. My governor box at South Llano River State Park was found by the ranger when someone came to the office and asked for directions to the box. He contacted me by email and permitted the box and told me that they wouldn't mind having more letterboxes as they seemed to bring people to the park and he liked the history of the governor. What I'm saying is that they are all different.

Boots Tex

Things that may help your search for Hill country state park:
Enchanted Rock - Not box friendly
Pedernales falls - Big park with lots of trails
McKinney Falls - Not box friendly
Blanco SP - To small, no trails
Guadelupe River SP - Lot of trails, but boxes seem to go missing
Inks Lake SP - Good trails and big park
Bastrop SP - TALE 10 will be held here in March of 2013, so may
be better not to double up.
Buescher SP - Not much trails
Longhorn Cavern - Good trails, but has a lot of boxes already
Hill Country SRA - Big park with tons of trail, but limited facilities
Kerrvile Park (use to be state park) - Great park, many trails and
good facilities.

Hope this helps.
