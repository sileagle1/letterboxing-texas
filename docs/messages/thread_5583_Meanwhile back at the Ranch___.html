From: Phaedra Koonz (pekoonz@yahoo.com) | Date: 2017-01-09 16:47:15 UTC
PI Joe,
I used to live in El Paso. I never found any boxes around but I was very new to 'boxing at the time.  Near downtown, the Thousand Steps trail is pretty cool and a relatively easy hike.  The Tom Mays Unit has some awesome views and hikes. I intended to plant at the top of the Cottonwood Springs trail way up by the spring, but I got too gigantically pregnant to make the hike before I got the chance, then I left :D  A ways out of town, Hueco Tanks is an awesome park, but due to its historical site status and the fact that it is a sacred place for the Native Americans of the area, they would definitely not take kindly to a box within the park. (There are nearby rock outcroppings that are not on anyone's land, but they are frequented by kids being kids.)  The other direction from town, in NM there is a bunch of BLM land and a place called Aiden's Crater. Apparently a fossilized sloth was found there, and it's just cool looking. Plus a geocache series with cool metal sculptures out in the middle of nowhere nearby.

-rakoonz
