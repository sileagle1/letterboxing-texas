Hi,

I can't say that I've seen that phrase very often. In
letterboxing--and I may be speaking only for letterboxes around New
England--it usually means that you have to be FAR more than discreet
about finding and replacing the box... It may be practically "Mission:
Impossible" to locate the box without raising eyebrows.

Oleander Island is a tricky one, because it’s in the downtown area of Galveston near the Strand – I remember being a bit concerned when you wrote and told me you made a “spectacle” of yourself! (Yikes!) But you know what? If you’ve now picked up on the need for stealth with future boxing, then it’s all good!

There are some letterboxers who avoid urban boxes, just because of that extra need for finesse – doesn’t appeal to them. I currently have some health issues which rule out the over-a-mile-hike boxes – but hope to get back to them someday! To each his/her own!

Again, read thru the clues and decide whether you want to “sign on” for the requirements. Cheers to a not-green-anymore boxer!

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2010-04-15 07:15:41 UTC-05:00

My goodness! You ARE kidding, right?

So you went to the time and trouble of putting a box together, finding a location, writing the clues, and posting them, and a person comes along and finds the box and knocks the stamp? I'd say that takes a lot of nerve.

No one (or almost no one) starts out as a great carver. One of the things many boxers will tell you they enjoy about finding boxes by the same planter is seeing how the planter's boxes develop over time.

For myself, if you give me a nice walk, or take me to a cool location, or write clever clues that are fun to solve, the box is a bonus to the journey - the imprint of the stamp is the proof that I took that journey and made it to the end. This isn't to say I don't appreciate a great stamp, because I do, but it's secondary to the game for me.

I have no idea how good a carve you put in your box, but for another boxer to tell you they don't like it is rude, if you ask me. Sorry it happened...

My goodness!  You ARE kidding, right?

So you went to the time and trouble of putting a box together, finding a location, writing the clues, and posting them, and a person comes along and finds the box and knocks the stamp?  I'd say that takes a lot of nerve.

No one (or almost no one) starts out as a great carver.  One of the things many boxers will tell you they enjoy about finding boxes by the same planter is seeing how the planter's boxes develop over time. 

For myself, if you give me a nice walk, or take me to a cool location, or write clever clues that are fun to solve, the box is a bonus to the journey - the imprint of the stamp is the proof that I took that journey and made it to the end.  This isn't to say I don't appreciate a great stamp, because I do, but it's secondary to the game for me.

I have no idea how good a carve you put in your box, but for another boxer to tell you they don't like it is rude, if you ask me.  Sorry it happened...
