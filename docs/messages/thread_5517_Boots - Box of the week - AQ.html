Thank you, Baby Bear.  I'm glad you brought this up, because I am a little bewildered and a little embarrassed about this box being named Box of the Week.  While I do appreciate recognition for my efforts, I don't understand how my Wearing the Cinco Peso box would qualify for Box of the week for the following reason:  I placed the box at Mission Tejas State Park for the TALE event we had there in 2009, and most of the finds were during the event.  It was last found on June 6, 2009.  Open Space reported it missing in June 2013 when she filed her report on the state of boxes in that park.  So, a couple of weeks ago, when we went up there to check on all the boxes, this was one of the boxes we replaced.  So, it's not the original box, although the stamp is identical to the original, and it has a new logbook and a new box.  I just like the subject and wanted to leave boxes in the park to be found by others.  On Sept. 7, it was found by Cannibal69nsons.  So, it was only found once in the week in which it won the Box of the Week award.  This really doesn't make sense to me.  The finders must have given it a great rating!  I know this subject has been discussed on the AQ boards and Ryan has tried to explain the logic of the selection process, but I never understood how it works, except that it's done by a computer with many factors contributing to the final choice for Box of the Week.  Do all of the finds factor in, even if it went six years without being found?  I don't know.  The selection of my box makes the choice seem random and somewhat cheapens the honor for me.  That's just my two cents worth.
