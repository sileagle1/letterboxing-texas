Serious though - Question for the group
 
I will be planting some boxes for the upcoming Jamboree on the Trail.  The plants are initially intended for a group of Cub Scouts to find, but I have always intended that they would eventually be posted for the group down the line.  The problem is that the park I'm planning to plant in, and which the Cub Scouts will be doing Jamboree on the Trail in, is notorious for disappearing letterboxes.  Solutions?  Should I just leave it to chance and leave the boxes at said park (the Railroad Park in Palestine), or should I let the Cubbies do their thing, then plant the boxes in a different location at a later date.  The second solution seems like a good one, but another letterboxer that will be attending the same event would get the stamps that date and not be able to log them.  When I would eventually move them, she would not be able to get credit for a find, I presume, due to the stamp not being new.  Am I wrong?  What do you guys think.

Re: [letterboxingtexas] Boxes Galore/Plus a bonus

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-04-27 23:09:09 UTC-05:00
Hey Jim,
 
As the "other" letterboxer who will be at that event, don't worry about logging on my account.  I log my finds online because I know other folks like to be able to look online for who has found their boxes, but as far as my own records go, I keep my counts manually in my logbook.   My F counts on both LBNA and AQ are wayyyy off.
 
As far as not being able to count it later as a find, you are right - both the clues AND the stamp have to be different or it can't be counted as a find but once.  But again, don't worry about that on my account.  But that does make me think - that's the same situation as it was last year for all those boxes y'all found at DNL - I planted them temporarily at camp and then have been replanting them in the wild since then.  I think another option would be to plant them with an original date of DNL 07, then note in the clues that they were replanted in the wild after that date, so that anyone (y'all) who found those boxes at the event can log that date and by reading the clues you could tell that anyone who found them after that date found them in the newer location.  The same solution would work for your boxes, if you choose to move them.  Just a thought...
 
Lucy
