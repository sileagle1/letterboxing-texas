Freestone/Limestone Counties SitRep

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-12-29 19:53:31 UTC-08:00
Well, my son and I got out of the house again today
and this time we headed to Fairfield, Wortham, Mexia,
and Groesbeck. We just got back in and I'm really,
really tired - a lot tireder today than I was
yesterday! Lightning decided today that he wants to
go by Struck by Lightning, and I'm afraid to ask why
because I have a feeling it reflects on how he feels
after two days of breakneck letterboxing with good ol'
Mom.

I'm also making a note to compare my height to Baby
Bear's at the gathering, because although I didn't
think he was that much taller than me, I'm starting to
wonder because his steps are wayyyy longer than mine!
What was funny was watching Lightning try to step it
out since mine kept coming up short...like even
shorter legs were going to make it work out just
right? Thankfully, Baby Bear always gives really good
landmarks, so even if steps don't work out just right,
you can get close enough to spot his description of
the area.

We headed to Fairfield to snag Silver Eagle's and Baby
Bear's boxes they planted there last year. I suddenly
realized it really was only a little over a year ago
that I actually discovered a real log entry in one of
my boxes - Fairfield's Oh Deer! ... As slow as my
first year in letterboxing was, this past year has
been pretty eventful.

Well...we pulled in to the park and I went through my
clues to pull the two for Fairfield, only to discover
that my printer had stuttered out Silver Eagle's
Horton so badly that it was illegible. We really,
really need to come up with a letterboxing hotline so
that we can make a phone call and get those clues when
things like this happen. Regular people just don't
understand when you call them in a frenzy asking them
to log on to the website and painstakingly read you
the clues. In fact, today I couldn't even find anyone
to take my call and I'm thinking it is because they
knew what I was out doing, so they suspected why I was
calling!

So off we went to find Baby Bear's TX Tour - 12/4.
I'm fairly sure I found the right tree, but being only
5'5" myself, looking into the hole in the tree at 5'
didn't work too well and I didn't have a Miata in my
pocket to pull up to the tree. Unfortunately,
Lightning is still too short to be much good, even
when he stood on my knee to look in. This is what
cinched needing to compare heights with Baby Bear. No
problem here though - I've already figured out who I'm
going to drag - I mean invite - along on our next
foray to Fairfield, and he's 7' 6", so he should be
able to do the trick! It was, as Fairfield always is,
a nice hike. Lake is really low though.

We jumped in the car and made the short trip to
Wortham and checked in at T-Dog's Blind Lemon
Jefferson. Neat box. I'm going to buy a CD because I
love blues and I feel I should have a copy of one of
his CD's since I've now got the commemorative stamp
image. I was a little put off by the fact that T-Dog
led us right through the cemetery, traipsing around
the graves, but after the journey was over, I realized
I had developed a sense of history I didn't have
before. Having been a first grader in Baton Rouge
when desegregation was being enforced and coupled with
mandatory bussing, I've always felt I was sitting in a
very small seat on the front row watching the racial
issues of the 60's and sensing the profound unfairness
of the times, but my experience finding Blind Lemon
Jefferson gave me another layer of understanding I
hadn't previously considered.

Off we went to Mexia and on to the Confederate Reunion
Grounds. I still can't get over how many really cool
parks are day trips for me. We found Val Verde Cannon
and Confederate Flag and these were GREAT locations.
We actually took a little time to relax and kick back
here - boy was it a nice place for that! We also
found Something Fishy here and we'll be taking it and
dropping it off soon - what is the saying about guests
and fish? After three days they both start to stink?
We won't be keeping it too long.

Hi, I'm a new member to this board, I am the "T-Dog" who planted that
letterbox... (of course, I joined to see your sitrep!) I'm sorry to
have made you go traipsing around the graves in the cemetary, I
figured they were the best available landmarks. I tried to be
respectful while at the same time paying homage to a favorite
musician. I've seen clues for letterboxes in graveyards where they
have the box hidden in part of a monument before, and I definitely
did not want to do anything like that, so I put it discreetly on the
periphery of the graveyard. I really like visiting Lemon Jefferson
out there, it's a very peaceful spot. (Unless I bring my guitar
along--then you can hear Blind Lemon spinning in his grave!)

If you haven't bought a CD yet, I'd recommend this one:

http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/tg/detail/-
/B000000XW6/qid=1135974097/sr=8-3/ref=pd_bbs_3/103-6742792-5091059?
v=glance&s=music&n=507846

I've seen a few "best of" CD's of his, but this one truly does have
some of his "best" songs on it. (I have the complete boxed set,
myself.) If you get it check out "Matchbox Blues", "Pennitentiary
Blues", "Jack O' Diamonds," and his notorious "Black Snake Moan"
(called "black snake woman" on this one for some weird reason.) Since
he's one of the earliest recording artists, you're going to hear a
bunch of hiss and pop on any recording... I think it adds to the
character!

I've seen that place going to and from the site of my letterbox, I'll
have to go hunting for boxes there next time I'm out in that neck of
the woods!

Since this is my first post to the board, I should add that I
letterbox under the name "T-Dog" and have planted two boxes so far,
both near the graves of some legendary blues artists. I've found one
box so far, in Lakeway, TX, where I live. Hopefully, I'll be out
finding more soon!

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-12-30 14:41:59 UTC-08:00
T-Dog,

I hate that I left you with the idea that I didn't see
the value of visiting each of the gravestones. Yes,
while I was put off by it at first, by the time we
finished the box, I was totally impressed with the
selections you used to guide us through the cemetery
to Blind Lemon's headstone.

I like cemetery boxes - when they are well-done.
Usually, I don't like being encouraged to walk through
and among the headstones because it seems
disrespectful to the folks who are buried there and
who aren't the subject of the box. HOWEVER, in the
case of your box, you used some really good graves
that were interesting to read and pointed up the
culture of service and dedication to their country
that blacks felt, even when their country didn't feel
the same toward them. In the end, I thought yours was
an excellent cemetery box and it honored not only
Blind Lemon Jefferson, but also some folks I never
would've known before.

And I guess that points up something I've realized
since I started letterboxing. You gotta have an open
mind. If I had allowed my first impression to dictate
whether we went ahead and did the box or not, we
really would've missed out.

It is indeed a peaceful location and I'm always a
little surprised by the odd places we can find
tributes to people who have made a major contribution
to some element of our culture. Maybe you and I will
have to meet out there sometime with our guitars...

Thanks for the suggestion for a CD. I'll have to
check it out. I haven't bought a good blues CD in
ages!

Now that you've joined, I hope you'll stick around,
and again, nice job on the box!

Lucy

Heh, I don't have a problem with the cemetary letterboxes either...
the two I've done so far have both been in cemetaries. (Both honor
blues musicians who are no longer with us.) I'd feel weird looking
for a box that hidden somehow in someone's grave or marker, or if the
clues were somehow disrespectful, but other than that, I personally
feel it's OK.

The Blind Lemon Jefferson letterbox was my first box I've planted,
and, in hindsight, I could have done better on my clues. I probably
should have figured out a way to make a puzzle of it, instead, I gave
compass directions, counted paces, and used a few stones as landmarks.
