Don't be discouraged Stargazer, boxes do go missing.  It is an awful feeling, believe me, I know.  Sometimes they can be washed away, sometimes animals find them, sometimes they are found by accident, and who knows what else happens to them.  Just about everyone who has planted a box has also lost a box.  All you can do is try to find a more secure hiding spot the next time, but remember that boxes can disappear from even the perfect hiding spot.  I was so tired of losing boxes that I hid one so "well" that several veteran boxers couldn't find it...I wouldn't recommend that as a solution though!  Sorry for your box...I bet many on this list will blame it on the BEAs.  -Mother of Five

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-11-07 20:05:58 UTC-08:00
Unless you have specific reason to believe it was
stolen, it would probably help your feelings to assume
there wasn't malicious intent. Most of the time when
boxes go missing, they are victims of circumstance
rather than malice - possibly someone discovered it
and mistook it as trash, a mischievous raccoon found
it and took it as a treasure, or maybe someone went
looking for your box and replaced it badly. I realize
that doesn't make your box come back, but hopefully it
keeps you from feeling so discouraged about it.

Did you search the area well to make sure it wasn't
lying about somewhere nearby? Ask Ruby about her
Gryphon Bait box - it went missing and resurfaced very
mysteriously!

Hang in there - missing boxes DO happen, and most of
us have had it happen and know how badly it makes you
feel, but you just have to be philosophical!

Lucy

I have only been letterboxing a short while.  But I have been Geocaching a long time... long enough to have had a few caches stolen.
 
The first one, I was very angry.  Probably a good thing I couldn't figure out who did it.  But I got over it.  I looked at the on-line log, and saw how many people and FAMILIES came out to it and logged what a great time they had.  Especially the kids!  And that helped me get over it.
 
Now if something goes missing, I just tell myself that a lot of people enjoyed it, so I got my value from it.  Perhaps I replace it, and perhaps not.
