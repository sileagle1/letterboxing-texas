2 new boxes - Clear Lake area and Galveston

From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2005-06-26 02:28:26 UTC
We were able to hide two new boxes today--one down on the Island,
and one on our way back to town.

Kemp's ridley is a tribute to an endangered species that is slowly
making a comeback thanks to a lot of dedicated turtle friends. This
has been a record year for nesting turtles, with 6 on Galveston
alone. This box is at the Sandhill Crane Soccer Complex, primarily
because the National Marine Fishery Service on Galveston where we
wanted to place it near was spectacularly un-box-friendly.

Then, we placed Labyrinth at Sylvan Rodriguez Park on Clear Lake
City Blvd. I think it's technically City of Houston, but if map-
wizard Krohm wants to double check would seriously appreciate it.
We were there at sunset, and walking the labyrinth under the setting
sun was a magical experience. It was not the meditative walk that I
know labyrinths are for some people; more like an all-out sprint to
the center, but hey, they boys got something out of it at least!

Clues to be posted soon, when the brain starts to function again
after a day in the sun.
