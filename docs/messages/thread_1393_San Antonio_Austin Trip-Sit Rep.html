San Antonio/Austin Trip-Sit Rep

From: pkleingers (pam@kleingers.net) | Date: 2005-10-19 05:54:50 UTC-04:00

A HUGE thanks to everyone who was so friendly and helpful in suggesting boxes to visit on my trip to SA and Austin. I was able to convince the Sleeping Giant to box a couple of days.

 

Our first box was the beautiful San Marcos Salamander. First, a nice stamp and clever placement. Even better was the Wetlands Walk. We spent a long time admiring the clear river, spotting turtles and fish and herons and little lizards (quite the novelty for us Buckeyes!) What a lovely, peaceful way to start our trip—and a nice walk after the flight, for sure! The box is in great shape but could use a new outer bag if anyone passes by.

 

Friday we trucked out to Stonehenge II—yes, it met my definition of bizarre! Sleeping Giant contends there was alcohol involved J We enjoyed it, and found the box in very good condition, tho quite exposed. Looks like there is work going on in the cemetery, which is great. I would hate to see that lovely place left to despair. From what I can tell, the box should be fine where it is, providing not too much trash piles up around it.

 

Saturday was our trip to Austin and we found boxes. I am very impressed that your urban boxes stick around as long as they do. Says a lot about placement and about the boxers—we have so many here that are extremely careless that it is discouraging. Anyhow, I digress—we found Oakwood Cemetery (awesome placement!!), Moonlight tower, and Mexican Bat (Bold!)—even though we didn’t get to see the bats fly. Duck and Dog appears to be missing—we did find an empty, lidless round container in the general hiding spot. There was a lot of trash built up right there as well. Still, we went in and had a drink and watched some of the Texas game (after listening to it “live” during our time at Oakwood) so it certainly was not a wasted trip!

 

I will hopefully contact each of the placers individually in the next few days, time permitting, and will be signing off this list soon. I want to thank you all once again for your hospitality and hope to meet you on the trails someday.

 

Mama Stork

aka Pam in Cinci
