From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2009-12-11 22:41:08 UTC-06:00

Hi Rogue Artist!

Sounds like that box was a mess!

Asking on this list is a good way to find out the status of letterboxers. For some reason, I want to say I remember Kantexan moved away. Some of the DFW boxers might be better able to answer that question.

As far as whether you were right to remove that box, well - you hit on a hot topic!

First let me say that in MY opinion, you were right to pick up the remains. No stamp, no logbook=no letterbox. No box - no harm in collecting the leftover trash (because really, that's what it is when there's no stamp and no logbook). Did you look around really, really well to see if there was anything to salvage of the stamp and logbook? Assuming you did and there wasn't anything to be found.

HOWEVER, an occasion to move or remove a box is very rare indeed.

For the sake of discussion, let's ask the group a few hypothetical questions. What would YOU consider proper in the following circumstances?

1) You find the box with the stamp and the logbook, but the container is destroyed.

2) No sign of the box and the stamp, but you find the logbook. What do you do?

3) You find a stamp, but have no idea what box or boxer it might go to?

4) You find the box in perfect condition with contents intact, but there are bulldozers nearby.

How about it y'all?

From: Debbie Kotlarek (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2009-12-11 22:52:45 UTC-06:00

1) You find the box with the stamp and the logbook, but the container is destroyed. - We put the stamp & logbook in the spare container that we carry with us and leave everything there.

2) No sign of the box and the stamp, but you find the logbook.  What do you do? - Hmmm... put the logbook in the spare container we carry or only put it in a freezer grade baggie????

3) You find a stamp, but have no idea what box or boxer it might go to? - If we find a stamp near where we are looking for a box, we'd assume it was the one we were looking for.  We'd therefore put it in our spare container, add a new logbook (carry spares w/us) and feel good about re-creating a box.

4) You find the box in perfect condition with contents intact, but there are bulldozers nearby. - Would leave everything, but be sure to mention the bulldozers when we contacted the planters to thank them for planting.

1) You find the box with the stamp and the logbook, but the container is destroyed. 
 
I carry a few lock-N-lock containers in the dog-show van.  I would replace the container, and even put in a temporary log book if needed.  I have in fact done this before. 

2) No sign of the box and the stamp, but you find the logbook.  What do you do? 
 
After THOROUGHLY searching, and not finding it, I have a choice.  If I am in the dog-show van, and the log book is NOT damaged, I place it in the spare lock-N-lock container, rehide it where it should be, then notify the owner ASAP of the situation.

3) You find a stamp, but have no idea what box or boxer it might go to? 
 
In TEXAS?  After THOROUGHLY searching, and not finding anything, I would pick up the stamp,  take it home, and get on this list describing the stamp and asking where it came from.  Surely GG has seen it, and a scanned image of it could be posted.  Do the same on Atlas Quest after someone reminds me to check there too.  Then either mail it to the owner, or to some boxer near the area, or carry it where it belongs myself on the next trip that way.
 
In another state, dunno..... 

4) You find the box in perfect condition with contents intact, but there are bulldozers nearby. 
 
Ohhhhhhhhh.......  well - seek out the foreman and ask him for details on the construction project.  If it looks like the box is in no danger or no immediate danger, leave it and notify the owner of the potential.  If the box is in immanent danger, like 2 or 3 days - it depends on how talking to the foreman worked.  If he seemed trustworthy, I might tell him of the box and trust that he watch over it.  Or I could move it to a safer hiding place and notify the owner of what and why.

How about it y'all?

Heh...as you said, there are no real right answers - so the fact that you considered and answered, means you get an A+!

The answers so far point up that we should all always be prepared to do all but create a new box. Carry extra containers, extra baggies, extra logbooks. Tough to carry extra stamps, but I remember someone several years ago saying that they carried some sort of a placeholder stamp so that if they ran across a box without a stamp, they could put their temporary stamp in the box and let the placer know ASAP that the stamp was gone; however, any subsequent finders would have an image to take if they found the box before the placer could replace the stamp. I always kind of liked that idea.

Another things that comes to my mind is - know your fellow letterboxers. As an example, if I were boxing in my own neck of the woods, and I found that Puddle Splasher's box was needing a new logbook, I would go ahead and replace it. If the box belonged to JB Kokopelli, I wouldn't replace it, but let her know. Why? Because JBK lives close enough to get the job done, Puddle Splasher doesn't. Another consideration - how easy is it going to be for me to get back to the box if the placer asks for my help? Do I live 5 minutes away, or a half hour away, or four hours away? Do I know of other boxers in the area that could be called on to help? Is the placer someone who is real particular about their boxes, or is it someone who will be pretty laid back? As to the lost stamp, have I seen it in other logbooks? Does the image seem to correspond with a themed box?

Lots of things to consider, and these are only a few...

Good timing for this topic.

An out of state letterboxer was visiting Houston last week and found a box at Memorial Park laying in the middle of the trail. She thought it might be one of my Oz boxes, so we met and she gave me the box. It's not mine. It doesn't indicate the box name anywhere - not on the container, not on the logbook. The logbook shows that the stamp was carved by the Gillespie Tribe from California. The only two finders in the logbook are Boots Tex and Will's World from December '07. The image and the timeframe make me think it is an Oz box... possibly the stamp was contributed to Dewberry for the event. Based on description alone, Boots wasn't able to identify it, so I'll scan the image in tonight and send it to him and to Dewberry.

Not knowing the name of the box, and not knowing where it should be re-hidden, I think our out of town friend did the right thing in pulling the box and giving it to a local. Now if we can just figure out where it belongs. :)

Interesting to note... the container is a lock-n-lock, and although it's been in the wild for at least two years, everything was in perfect condition!
