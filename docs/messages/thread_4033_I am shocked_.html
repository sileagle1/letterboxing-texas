From: investigator_bacon (investigator_bacon@yahoo.com) | Date: 2009-04-09 16:56:25 UTC
Dear Texans,

Is this how you welcome fellow letterboxers into your great state? Accusations and slander abound!

Boots Tex and Barefoot Lucy: Investigator, Investigator, Investigator. If you say that 3 times (can you count), you might get it correct. I am an Investigator, not an Inspector.

Inspector:
1. A person employed to inspect something
2. A police officer who is in charge of usually several precincts

Investigator
1. A person not a member of a police force who is licensed to do detective work (as investigation of suspected wrongdoings or searching for missing persons or objects)

How can you possibly expect me to take you seriously if you can not get my title correct?

Corpus Christi to Amarillo is exactly 657 miles, and 10 hours driving time. I could have gone to San Angelo and looped up to Abilene which would have made it closer to your mileage. However, as comfortable as the brand new shiny silver Lincoln Towncar was, I preferred the shorter distance. Now, as I pointed out to Barefoot Lucy, I was in your great state of Texas way back in October of 2007. It is true, you have increased the number of your letterboxes since that time, to approximately 3,387. It is a disgrace that your mystery boxes have not increased proportionately. Even if you count the mysteries in the files, websites, and other mysterious places, the percentage of mysteries to traditionals is not that good. The question is; can you correct this situation?

Barefoot Lucy, your observations are amazing. Incorrect, but nevertheless amazing. My record stands on its own merit. The PZKut Caper and the Missing Store of Good Manners Case were both solved to the satisfaction of my clients. Can I be faulted for the unsavory conduct of those that call themselves Letteboxers? I think not!

Yes, I am familiar with the demise of poor IRA Boxer. Being an Investigator (notice that word) of somewhat moral conduct, I will state that I was not in the area when the crime occurred or when Detective Gandolph de Grey was detecting. Nor, might I add, was I in the area when your tour bus drove off without you. Perhaps you should have hired me to recover it.

Barefoot Lucy, if indeed the sock is yours (and I still harbor my doubts), you have me to thank for its return. After all, was it not I that you followed into the Gilgal Garden?

Puddle Splasher, I must agree with Barefoot Lucy on this one; you really should keep better tabs on current events.

Sincerely,

Investigator Francis Bacon
