I’m impressed, Kantexan!

You’re inspiring me to get off my duff and re-carve and replace our missing boxes! We (like you) had a real spell of several boxes going missing every weekend  until I think we had more missing than existing – it was really discouraging for a few months.

I finally gave up and figured there must be a rash of new boxers (forgive me, please, dear newbies!) or possibly a strange new streetgang ( the Encrypts?) – and decided we’d just wait a while before putting our efforts into vain attempts just to maintain status quo….let alone placing NEW boxes.

 

Thanks for the motivation- I love seeing your trail name around again!

Ruby,
 
I do hope you will eventually recarve the Shawnee Trail box!  That was an awesome box in a great location and a very nice hide, all wrapped up together.  When we took our Webelos to Dallas over the summer, we stopped off there just so they could see the exhibit (didn't show 'em the box, although I would've liked to have).  We had walked alot in 100 deg plus and thought alot and played alot, and that was the last stop of the day.  The boys moaned about not wanting to go, but I insisted they humor me and give me five minutes and a picture there.  It was well worth it I guess - they didn't want to leave when the five minutes was up!
 
I probably never would've known about it or spent much time there, if I hadn't had to linger there for awhile when SJZ and I went looking for your box.  We found it at night - and it was awesome walking among those sculptures in the dark!

Hi Ruby,
Good to see your trailname around again too!

I also had had waaaaay too many boxes go missing. In several of the
instances I had received emails from geocachers telling me they had
found my box by mistake while searching for a geocache in the same
area. I never search the geocache database so I never had any idea
there would be caches in the same location. Out of curiosity I went
on the geocache website and found there are probably 10 geocaches to
every 1 letterbox in our area! I think this is becoming a problem.
Don't get me wrong, I think geocaching has just as much of a right
to exist as much as letterboxing, but I am in a quandary as to how
to make sure my LBs are not placed right on top of geocaches and
vice versa! Anyone have any ideas!?

-Kantexan
