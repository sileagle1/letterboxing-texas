And now, Hiding Boxes

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-07-16 17:29:51 UTC-07:00
Someone a few days ago was asking about how to best
hide a box. Today as we traipsed around, and yes, as
I was frustrated by the lack of rehiding, I thought
about answers to that question. Here are a few of my
own thoughts, which are, of course, my opinion and
mine only. Others might suggest differently and have
perfectly good reasons for their own ideas:

1) Try to use containers with muted colors. Bright
shiny lids glint sunlight and attract attention. In
fact, I'm thinking that I may start brushing my boxes
with a wire brush to dull the finish.

2) Use the smallest container possible for the box.
You might make it a wee bit bigger in case someone
wants to slip a HH in, but don't give it a castle if
all it needs is a condo. Much easier to hide a small
box than a large one.

3) Don't hide boxes in areas that might flood. This
one is kind of hard to know. You just have to get a
feel for your altitude - I just know if I'm "higher"
or "lower" relative to my car, but you can watch for
wash lines and creeks. Near creeks is usually bad,
but you can normally tell how high the creek water
gets by where the wash line is on the creek bank.
Away from creeks, I kind of squint my eyes and imagine
a real gully-washer and try to visualize where the
water would flow.

4) Don't use foldover topped sandwich baggies. Better
to not use a baggie at all! Use ziplocks, and better
even, freezer bags.

5) Help your finder be a good re-hider by looking
around as you hide and making sure there will be an
ongoing supply of hiding material. Nice to use a big
rock or log or something cast casually over the top of
the box so that not much else is needed.

My .02,
Lucy

RE: [letterboxingtexas] And now, Hiding Boxes

From: celticlions (celticlions@comcast.net) | Date: 2005-07-17 12:48:57 UTC-05:00
We've been using camouflage duct tape for almost a year with pretty good
results - I stumbled on it as an idea when my D.I. team was buying every
conceivable kind of duct tape at a Home Depot one day!
I've recommended it to everyone I know and then been surprised when we've
hunted their "invisible" boxes! You could really be looking at a camouflaged
box and not see it!
I know folks in the North use camouflage burlap to wrap their boxes -
haven't seen it down here but I suspect it might disintegrate more quickly
than tape in the wilds.

I also have started labeling all the plastic baggies we use - somehow I keep
finding CelticLions logs (while doing maintenance) which folks have replaced
without any baggies at all. Not sure how that can happen, but I'm hoping
that the labels will prevent someone from accidentally walking off with a
plastic bag which was meant to protect the logbook from dampness.


Ruby T.
