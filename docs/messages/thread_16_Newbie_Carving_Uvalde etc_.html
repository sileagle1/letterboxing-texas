Hi Connie, welcome to letterboxing!

I agree with Barefoot Lucy.... Hobby Lobby is the best place to go
around my area for carving tools and carving medium. When I started,
I bought 'the pink stuff', and the small package I bought came with
a carving tool too. I didn't do all that great... made my signature
stamp (Busy Bee), which my kids laughed at and said it looks more
like 'Drunken Bee'. Actually, they were right, but I am still using
it anyway. ;)

Then I found the Staedter(sp?) Master Carve which says it 'cuts like
butter' - which it really does. That is what I prefer. But Hobby
Lobby (Calallen and Corpus Christi) has sold out (months ago) of all
of their stock except the very thick blocks which I hate to pay $7-
something for, when I want the not quite as thick square blocks I
used to get. A few days ago, I bought some new 'cream colored stuff'
made by the 'pink stuff' makers, which says it is new and improved
and easy to carve. I haven't tried it yet though....and I wish it
wasn't as thin and bendable as it is.

After awhile, I found that I prefered to use an exacto-knife for
stamp carving. And then use the carving tool only on the areas that
I want to look 'hand carved' in the stamped image.

I think I have heard of letterboxers using a dremel tool for some of
their stamp carving. Maybe do a search for 'dremel' on the LbNA list.

I'd be glad to get together with anyone to show them my materials,
share my tools and carve together. I'm in Skidmore though, and my
van is STILL in the shop getting a new engine. I do have my
husband's pick-up after about 7 each evening, and on most Fridays or
weekends. Would anyone like to get together?

Susan, I'd love to help you scout out the Uvalde area, if my
available time fits with your schedule. I have to be home each day
by about 3:15 to be here when our granddaughter gets home from
school. I do have Fridays free, IF my husband is not working, so he
can be here for her. And Saturday's all day... and Sunday
afternoons. I don't know if that is enough time, or if it fits with
the days you'd rather do it, though.

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-01-25 21:14:08 UTC-08:00
LOL Wanda, I've used the pink stuff, the mastercarve
and the cream-colored stuff too. It sounds like I did
the same thing you did - I got that set by speedball
with the single sheet of pink stuff and a few other
things in it. Then I found the blocks of mastercarve
and it it amazingly pliable. I found, though, and I'm
not sure if it is just because of my technique, that
it crumbles badly in detail work. I think I may be
cutting too deeply though.

The cream-stuff is kind of a happy medium, IMO, and no
pun intended - LOL. The pink stuff is very firm and I
find myself fighting it a bit (again, this may be due
to immature technique), the mastercarve crumbles, as I
mentioned, and the cream speedball stuff falls in
between. I did, though, just go back and carve a new
simple stamp this evening from the pink stuff, and I
felt a little better about it this time.

Needless to say, I'm not speaking from my vast
experience and great wisdom, but this is what I think
of the different media based on my limited experience
with them.

Lucy
