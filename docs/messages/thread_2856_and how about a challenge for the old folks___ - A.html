For me, I wish I had known how to hide boxes better so they would
last longer. When I first started letterboxing there weren't any
boxes nearby to find, so I had no guide as to what made a good hiding
place. Common sense was all I had, but sometimes that is too
limiting. I used to think if I hid a box and covered it well, nobody
would accidentally find it. What I found out was, the box rarely
would remain covered as well as I originally placed it, due to
animals, weather or careless boxers, so I started hiding them in
places that would keep it hidden even without much cover. For
example, if you want to hide a box by a tree, make sure to put it on
the back side away from the trail. Ideally, you should find several
boxes before placing one, since experience is the best teacher.

Camo paint helps.

SOP in letterboxing is "always rehide better than you found it."
There's a good reason for that; hides nearly always deteriorate for
one reason or another, the stuff you put over the box falls away, the
camo paint starts to peel, the many letterboxers visiting the hide
beat a path to it, etc., etc. Asking everyone to rehide better than
they found it is the only way you can hope that the hide remains
nearly as good as it started out.
