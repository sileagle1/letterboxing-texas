Mystery boxes

From: Boots Tex (redbarn@eastex.net) | Date: 2010-09-20 14:11:02 UTC
I have a series of five boxes in Houston that I didn't list as "Mystery" boxes, but the clues for box number five take some creative thinking to solve. Therefore, it seems, every other "finder" lists that box as "attempted". So, one person finds it, the next person "attempts" it, the next person finds it....so on. I don't mind that as long as everyone understands that the "attempt" was an attempt to solve the clue and not a "box missing" report. Should I change the box from a traditional to a mystery? Just wondering.

Boots Tex

Maybe you could put a disclaimer in the clues saying to ignore "Attempted" status.

Yes, the series I'm talking about is called NADA CLUE and is in Memorial Park. These boxes are a little different in that the listed clue only takes you to the first box, then each box has clues to take you to the next box, except for the last one, and there lies the mystery. How do you get to the last box? I assure you that the clues are available, you just have to figure them out on the fly so to speak. I know it must be frustrating to get that far and not be able to find the last box, but many people have found it, so it isn't impossible. I think I'll just make this series a "Mystery". There are mystery boxes out there that I have yet to solve, but I occasionally revisit them and maybe one day I'll have a breakthrough, who knows? Not everyone can solve every mystery. Do you really think the experience is ruined because you can't find the fifth box? I'm just curious, because I'd like to know what people think.

Re: [letterboxingtexas] Re: Mystery boxes

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2010-09-21 09:45:20 UTC-05:00

Well if you were to give a clue about the last clue, then the series couldn't be called NADA CLUE, could it?

I haven't been to Houston since Dewberry, TeamKing and MO5 did the book gathering so I can't speak specifically to your series, but I have to say I find it frustrating to get started but not be able to get to the end - thrillingly frustrating! I love a great challenge and if it means I have to come back a second time, so be it. But then, I'm one that loves the process of getting there as much or more than the actual finding.

If you have successful finders, then - as you said - you know it's doable. Beyond that, it is up to you to do as you please with the clues. Can't tell you how many times I've had people write to tell me my Spring Hope box isn't there, only to go and find it right in its place - the clues are deceptively simple and yet surprisingly elusive. I especially enjoy the people who come back for it three or four times, finally "get it" at the least expected moment and hurry back to find the box itself. THAT'S a placer's pleasure, if you ask me!

BarefootLucy
