I think dewberry's suggestions are all very good advice when you want to go with kids.  Especially slowing down and following the kids lead.  My oldest daughter enjoys it more than all the other kids, so a letterboxing outing for just the two of us is a reward for hard work she has done at school.  And anyone from a large family knows what a treat it is to go out with just one kid at a time!
 
You are lucky to be near Houston, because there is no shortage of boxes to find here! I would suggest you look through the clues, and pick boxes that sound interesting to your kids.  Telge park is full of Halloween themed boxes which appeal to the younger crowd.  The walks are not too long, and there is plenty of space to play.  That might be a good place to start.  Mommyaulait, how old are your kids?  Dewberry and I might be able to help you out picking some boxes around here to start, as we rarely ever go boxing without kids in tow.  Have fun!  Mother of Five

Growing up further out in the country I can completely understand the warning about spiders, snakes, scorpions and other things crawling around near boxes. So we are hoping to letterbox as a treat with the older kids while I'm out of commission with the younger kids over the next few months. We usually take a lot of walks and what we call Urban Hiking. So all of the kids are used to spending time out doors and enjoying nature.
Any help with finding a few good boxes would be appreciated. As I've said in other emails we are investigating the process so that we can do this as respectfully as possible.
