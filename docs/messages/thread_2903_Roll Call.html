From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-07-04 02:31:27 UTC
Late to the party....hope there's some of the good dip left!

I'm Dewberry, and YES, I letterbox with children (when they let me).
I live in Houston proper...I like mystery boxes and ciphers and
puzzles. I am an obsessive carver and can't possibly plant all that I
carve. I ship my stamps to other climes when people will let me.

We have a collective trail name of Gramatrick, and we all have our own
trail names and our own stamps and our own books (except for my
husband). I'm not crazy about dewberry, but by this point, it's sort
of stuck. My mailman's used to it, if nothing else.

We've been letterboxing for around three years, but it took about 6
months for it to stick. We had learned of it years before (when there
were just those 9 boxes Baby Bear mentioned in the whole state), and
thought, we'll that's something to do if we ever go to Connecticut.
Luckily, while planning a road trip, we looked to see if there were
any on the way, and not only were there some, but there was one a few
miles from our house.

I'm not all that great at keeping track, but we're close to 500 finds
and somewhere around 175 plants.

Interesting fact? Well, except for copperheads, I've seen every
species of venomous snake there is in Texas while letterboxing.

dewberry

From: Baileys Bunch (baileys_bunch@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-07-06 03:49:00 UTC
Bailey's Bunch here. Our group includes myself, husband, daughter
(11yrs), and Bailey (our four legged daughter) Bailey is a Mini
Australian Shepherd. We always ink our signature stamp in red
because she is a red-tri.
I found out about letterboxing in a round about way.I was looking
for a book to read. So this is how it went
A couple of months before a group of us girls went on a girl's
weekend. We do this every so offend to just get away.
We had just been to Jefferson, TX for a ghost huntSpooky!!! This is
a gem of a Texas small town. Look it up!!! There is a lot of
interesting things to do and SEE. We had toured "The Grove" This is
the home of Mitchel Whitington an author and great story teller. If
you get a chance read Uncle Bubba. It has some great recipes too.
After the tour we hung back and asked him and his wife some more
questions. They are a lovely couple and were interested in the
places we had been on our ghost hunting adventures. We shared some
of the places with them and they said they would have to check them
out. Anyway, back to letterboxing, I wouldn't want to scare
anyone.lol
As I said I was looking for a book to read and I thought about him.
I looked on his web site and found out they had gone to some of the
place we had mentioned to them. I looked at the pictures and read
the blog. He had a caption under a picture that said anytime they
take a trip they always do a little letterboxing? What on earth was
this? I clicked on the link he gave. This was beginning. I have to
admit I'm an addict now.
Thursday, October 12th 2006 I took my lunch break. Got stamp and
book. Run to the San Jacinto Battle grounds 15 min. away and did my
first. It was Silver Eagles' San Jacinto box. It wasn't enough so I
did another one. Texas Proud by Will's World, and then another. Two
hours later I came back to work, Late. My boss asked me what the
grim was about. "I haven't seen you this happy in a long time"
I had to tell him what I'd done. Was I going to be fired? Two hour
lunch, what was I thinking? (I know what I was thinking I was
thinking about getting more letterboxes) and no he didn't fire me.
He does it with me now. My boss in my husband!! And by the way, I
just let him think he's boss. lol.

Hello everyone!

I'm Sandy aka DragonFly Beauty, mom to Atoy
Adventurers, Pamela aka ButterFly Cutie and McKayla
aka Pretty Kitty. We have been letterboxing for a
year this past May. We were introduced to
letterboxing by our Girl Scout troop leader and have
been hooked ever since. We live in Cherokee County.
We are planning on doing a planting in the Tri-State
area of the ArkLaTex sometime soon. We need to gather
our carving supplies and brainstorm a way to tie all
three states together into a common theme.

One interesting thing about our letterboxing
adventurers aside from the almost daily occurence of
all three of us passing by a spot and then looking at
the other two nodding a silent that would be a perfect
spot for a letterbox!! would be the time that we were
boxing at a cemetery in Cherokee County, in search of
one of the Snow White series of letterboxes by
jbkokopelli and I discovered an ant bed quite by
accident. I came out of my pants screaming and a
squalling to the mortification of my two preteens.
Now I always use a stick to poke around the area
before stepping into it, once or maybe a few dozen
times bitten twice shy.

A suggestion that I would make to newboxers is to buy
your carving medium in bulk. We are constantly try to
hunt down the pink stuff in every town of any size
that we go to.

Is anyone ready for a get-together? We are getting
antsy for one!! We are considering hosting one once
the weather cools off or sooner if we can find an
indoor location to have one. We are open to
suggestions.

Happy Trails!!

Sandy

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-09-13 11:00:55 UTC-05:00
East Texas is packed with evacuees.  My church has one shelter holding 125 evacuees, and the church where we hold scouts has another with another 100 or more.  Yet another local church has a shelter much smaller.  As I understand, all of the shelters in Tyler are full also.  Most of the gas stations are out of gas along the way and the grocery stores and Walmarts are struggling to stay stocked.  I went to Walmart here in my town at 6 am this morning after I left the shelter, and it looked as if it had already been hit by a hurricane - sort of like Christmas Eve and Tax-Free Weekend multiplied.
 
I was working in the shelter when the hurricane made landfall and watched with about 20 evacuees as the hurricane moved through Galveston, on to Houston, and forward in our direction.  Most of our evacuees are from Beaumont.  It was humbling to sit through the night with those folks as they watched and waited to see what was happening in their town.  Mostly flooding.
 
We ourselves in Jacksonville are now experiencing lots of rain and winds of about 30 MPH.  They anticipate it getting up to around 50 MPH this afternoon, and gusting to 60 or more.  and just as I write this, our power is flickering.  Yep.  It's going RIGHT NOW!
 
See y'all - stay safe everyone else.  We're good here.
 
Lucy

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-09-14 20:04:38 UTC-05:00
Well - our yard looked like it was overgrown with lots of little shrubs by the time we lost lots of branches - nothing big, thank goodness.  Our power has now been out for 30 hours and it doesn't look like we'll get it back for another 24 or so.  One small leak in the roof.  Lots of flying debris.  All in all, we came out pretty good as far as I'm concerned.  Much better than our friends to the south.
 
Thankfully, the shelter was only without power for a short time, and during that time we ran generators to keep basic functions going.  The church had a large amount of water damage though and services today were cancelled for safety reasons.  We have a really big steeple - 10' at the base and 52' tall - and at one point yesterday, it was swaying noticeably.  I would imagine it had something to do with the water problems.  The evacuees are in a separate building.
 
We got out yesterday evening to go "observe", as my mother-in-law put it.  Hundreds of downed trees and countless downed power, cable, and phone lines.  We went to Henderson today for a 90th birthday party for my husband's grandmother and they had quite a lot of damage over there as well, and it seemed like about half the people at the party didn't have power either.
 
I haven't had a chance to talk to NacTrailCat, but I hear that Nac really got hit hard.  I'll try to call her this evening.
 
I probably won't be back online for a few days since it looks like "something" happened to my computer at home during several power surges that happened right before the power went out for good.  Jacksonville has no school tomorrow and my company is closed due to lack of power.
 
We in this area are certainly less than prepared for having a tropical storm come through.  It gave me a new appreciation for the gulf coast dwellers we know!
