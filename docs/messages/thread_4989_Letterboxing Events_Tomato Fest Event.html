Letterboxing Events/Tomato Fest Event

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2012-03-25 16:41:57 UTC-05:00
Since I posted the Tomato Fest event earlier today, I've had some new
folks to email and ask me to tell them more about what happens at
events. what they should expect, that sort of thing. Rather than
answer them each individually, I thought I would bring their questions
out here for everyone to answer, and I would briefly answer specifics
about the Tomato Fest.

In general, events and how they are "done" are as diverse as the
organizers who plan them. The only common features of pretty much any
gathering are 1) exchanging stamps with other boxers, 2) boxes to be
found, 3) an event box, and 4) food!

The Tomato Fest has sort of taken on a life of its own. It began six
years ago as a box I had at my booth I was manning at the Tomato Fest,
and I invited boxers to come and find me and find my box. The next
year I held a Pampered Chef kitchen party for my daughter, SJZ, and
invited letterboxers to attend that since our demonstrator was our
very own Maydelle Queen Bee. It was held the day of the city's Tomato
Fest and rather than giving directions to my house, I gave clues to
boxes which led boxers to my house. The third year I tried planting
boxes in the downtown area, so that boxers had to be stealthy in
crowds. The fourth year I was unable to host it and JB Kokopelli
graciously stepped in and put on a great event during which boxes were
hidden with her classic eye for the historical aspects of the
community. Last year, the theme was celebrity tomatoes and boxers
joined together in teams and had to accomplish objectives to earn
clues to boxes hidden away from the crowds. We have photos of
Gryzzled Gryphon in handcuffs as evidence of the good times from last
year!

In general, no matter what the premise, the event has turned into a
seat-of-the-pants, think-on-your-feet rolling good time!

This year? Well, as is customary with regard to the Tomato Fest
gathering, participants will know very little before the actual event.
The details will be cloaked in secrecy. From here on out, anything I
say on this list or on Facebook may or may not be an important clue to
what to expect. I guarantee only a few things: 1) the event is held
in conjunction with the city's Tomato Fest and you will have time to
enjoy the festival as well as the event. 2) The event will end with
food (less than $10 per person). 3) I will give you all the
information you need prior to the event with enough time to gather
what you need. 4) You will not have clues to the event boxes before
the event.

Finally, if you are a new boxer, don't be scared away by all the
mystery. It may sound a little intimidating to know so little going
in, but the name of the game for the day is FUN, FUN, FUN and I won't
have fun unless YOU have fun!
