General Sherman letterbox

From: Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter (janet@redbarnretreat.com) | Date: 2006-11-07 19:34:48 UTC
I thought I'd share a story about the adventures of my General
Sherman letterbox in Rusk State Park. It was first reported missing
by the Boxalots on June 29. I replaced the box with a new box and
stamp and a new location in early August after an unsuccessful
attempt at finding it. The old box was found by a new letteboxing
family on October 1, while attempting to find the new box. They
realized when they found the new one, exactly where it was supposed
to be, that they had found the old one. They graciously took the
old box, notified me, and mailed it to me. The logbook tells an
interesting story. The earliest entry, by other than known
letterboxers, was on May 12 when someone named Tim and Nick stamped
my General Sherman train stamp in with the marker I had provided in
the box and signed and dated it. On May 20, a family from
Duncanville did the same and put a note "Was great to find this on
our little walk". On June 4, the Richard family stamped in with my
stamp and left the note "Great end to our weekend trip". Next was
Dan B. and Brittney B. on June 10 "Leaving only footprints". June
17, Troop 1444 signed in (all of them) with a note "Thanks for the
fun". June 18, the Rhoads and McCaffertys. June 21, the Call
family from Marshall signed and stamed the book. June 25 found
Aymztx from Fort Worth signing in with a personal stamp. June 29 is
when the Boxalots attempted to find the box, but couldn't. July 2,
the Cains from New Caney stamped and wrote a note "This is cool!!".
On September 3, a family from Nevada, Texas signed in and on
September 21, a family from Crosby, Texas stamped and signed in.
That brings us to October 1, when the new boxers found the box lying
on the ground next to the railroad track.
I think it's pretty amazing that, for almost 5 months, this
letterbox just hung out in the state park. I guess visitors would
just find it and, out of curiosity, open it and sign it, then leave
it someplace where someone else could find it. Who says you have to
hide your boxes? Just leave 'em lying around and they'll be found.

Boots Tex

Sorry you couldn't find it when you were there, I guess it was
already making the rounds of the park by that time, chillin' out at
the picnic table or taking a ride on the train, who knows? I've
already replaced the box with a duplicate stamp and a new location,
similar to the old location, but farther on down the line. I might
place the old box with a new name in Harrisburg if I can find an
appropriate place. That's where the General Sherman (the first
railroad train in Texas) was located. When you come back through
Rusk, look for the new box, still listed as General Sherman on LbNA.

Boots Tex

Bears, lost boxes, snakes, scratches from the thorny bush that you were warned about. . .
