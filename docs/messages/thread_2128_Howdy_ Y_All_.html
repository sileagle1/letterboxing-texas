From: maliabarth (maliabarth@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-09-04 16:29:04 UTC
We are new to letterboxing, but are veteran geocachers, so we have
heard of letterboxing for several years. Recently we were in
Maryland and accidentally found a "missing" letterbox in Sandy Point
State Park (Bay Bridge/Sandy Point on lb-NA). That got us started,
and we are now letterboxing in the central Texas area.

We are using the same trail name as we do on geocaching, so watch
for "Moore9KSUcats" to appear in logbooks near you.

Originally a caching family of 6, there is now only one child left at
home, but he has carved his own stamp already. Our oldest daughter
is attending Johns Hopkins school of Public Health in Baltimore,
daughter #2 is an elementary ed major at Tarleton (in Stephenville),
daughter #3 lives in Round Rock and hopes to start college in the
spring, and our son is in the 9th grade here in Georgetown.

We just placed two new letterboxes in Georgetown for others to find
and enjoy. (Makin' Tracks #1, and Makin' Tracks #2, listed on both
AtlasQuest and LB-NA.) I know we will learn more tricks of the trade
as we continue to find them.

I would appreciate you sharing some of the tricks of the trade that
you have learned over the years, and will probably incorporate some
of our geocaching hide techniques here, too.

Thanks to all who have hidden boxes for us to find!
