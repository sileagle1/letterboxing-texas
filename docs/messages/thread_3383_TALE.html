From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-03-02 19:35:55 UTC-06:00
We just rolled in from Bastrop a few minutes ago - managed to unload the car and now the guys are settling in to a movie and I'm reading the 300+ emails I had.  Eesh...email.  That's what I hate about leaving, but that's what I like about leaving too...
 
I second the deep thanks to Silver Eagle and Baby Bear for such a great event!  I think it was the perfect mix of great weather, great company, and such a nice location.  And I wonder if I'm the only one that got an imprint of Baby Bear's wife's stamp????  Hmmm???
 
As always happens, we made great new friends and got to visit with old ones.  It's funny to me how people I see only once or twice a year can be such good friends, but it happens that way.  It also happens that new friends seem like old ones right away too, doesn't it?
 
I was especially glad to see Dewberry there, since she had previously thought she wouldn't be there.  It just doesn't seem like a gathering without Mother of Five (less three for the day) and Dewberry...
 
Thanks to Puddle Splasher and Dewberry also for the lovely tribute box to Ruby Tuesday.  I would've sworn Ruby herself carved the stamp!
 
Every year I'm amazed at how well-behaved letterboxing kids are - Lightning hit it off right away with WOWMOM's son and with TXPokey's and Frogkisses' boys.  They even went swimming!  Who'da thought you could swim in a lake on March 1st? (well okay, I used to swim in March when I was a kid, but I thought my kid was smarter than me!)
 
Then I have to wonder how I missed some folks - like Chrispy - who I was so looking forward to meeting?  I know there were lots of people, but I thought I had pretty much made the rounds. 
 
And then there was campfire at Silver Eagle's and Baby Bear's campsite - no logbooks, no stamps - just good conversation.  What a way to end the day!
 
Here's hoping all of us can meet again at the next gathering, and bring with us those who were unable to attend and those who don't yet know they want to attend!
 
--

From: SandiBox (sandibox@msn.com) | Date: 2008-03-03 04:57:41 UTC
I want to chime in with thanks to Silver Eagle and Baby Bear for
another great event! You did a fantastic job of putting everything
together - the location, the weather (I'll give you credit for that
too!), the food, and of course, the wonderful people.

We arrived Friday afternoon, checked into our little cabin, planted a
few letterboxes, then drove in a big loop around Bastrop looking for
the American Legion hall, only to find it was back at the beginning of
the loop! We joined Barefoot Lucy and her family for a fish fry, and I
had the pleasure of meeting Fearless Fox and Nac Trail Cat's husband
(never did catch his new trail name!) We got to talking about Lucy's
homemade Bailey's and Puddle Splasher's (or was that Viewfinder's?)
recipe for bourban balls... and I told them about Chocolate Cake shots.
Thus encouraged, we headed back into town after dinner for the
ingredients before settling down for the night.

Saturday was fabulous. It seems like I spent time doing all the things
that make for a memorable letterboxing event... seeing old friends,
doing exchanges and making new friends, solving personal travelers,
finding traditional letterboxes, eating some great chili, eating some
more great chili, and finding more boxes (and laughing at ourselves for
our remarkable lack of stealth).

Lucy, I think the reason that we connect so well when we see each other
in person is due in part to this talklist. The sense of community on
this list carries over to gatherings, so that we pick up right where we
left off, and even new friends seem like old buddies.

I also enjoyed the campfire chat last night. It was such a relaxing
end to an already great day. It was fun sharing stories of our
adventures - or MISadventures as the case may be, bidding farewell to
the Cranberry Cruiser, eating that scrumptious cobbler (thanks again
Fearless Fox and Lightening!), and sneaking in another chocolate cake
shot with Fearless Fox when no one else was looking. :D

But all good things must come to an end, so this morning we packed up,
said some final good byes and found a few more letterboxes before
leaving the park.

As the perfect ending to a perfect weekend, we stopped by Buescher
State Park and found the Lost Pines letterbox on our way home. It
seemed incredible that a letterbox could survive in the wild for 7-1/2
years, but when we found it we understood how this piece of Texas
letterboxing history has lasted so long. The trail was one of the most
beautiful I've seen, the hiding spot was carefully chosen to avoid
accidental detection, and the stamp well done and relavent to the
location. As much as I was in awe of the history behind the box, it
set the ideal of what a letterbox should be. Like I said, a perfect
ending to a perfect weekend.

Thanks to everyone who contributed, by planting a letterbox, by taking
time to talk, by contributing to the pot luck, or simply by your
presence. You are each a Texas Treasure!

SandiBox
