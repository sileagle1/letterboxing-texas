Question about regional lists

From: Ruby Tuesday (celticlions@comcast.net) | Date: 2006-12-03 15:45:07 UTC-06:00

I’ve been mulling over how to look at the question of Regional lists from different points of view.

Just out of curiousity, I took a look at the number of messages posted to the Texas list over the past year. You can find these numbers at the bottom of the group HOME page: http://groups.yahoo.com/group/letterboxingtexas/

 

The MetroBoxers list was formed on October 23rd. October was the month with the third highest number of posts, 353 – probably largely chatter about The Great Book Adventure. The next month, November, shows 175 messages. You could certainly deduce that the new Regional list drew some posts away from the main list as we were planning the Many Meese Mini-Meet.

But wait a minute - take a look at the rest of the year – May and June are pretty similar to November with 175 and 163 messages. It’s easy to guess why – the Rusk Gathering was over, all the chatter about it had died down and families were busy with outdoor sports, graduation, vacation and a zillion other things!

 

People and their lives ebb and flow – topics will catch their interest or not – lists will flourish if people want to exchange information on them and if they enjoy the forum.

Personally, it makes little difference to me whether I belong to 5 groups or 15 – I open all my messages (well, almost all of them ;-)!) and respond to many of them. If a group has more meaning to me at a particular time I tend to spend more time reading and answering that mail.

 

Groups of Letterboxers tend to develop a certain flavor (and no, I’m not talking about being out in the woods too long without a shower!) – the MetroBoxer group has been pretty exciting so far – but I’m guessing things will settle down! The Texas list is tried and true, but there are still only a very small percentage of members who actually post to that list. I’m hoping a smaller list will allow some new or less vocal boxers to express themselves in a smaller setting.

 

There is every reason why the “big” Texas list and any other regional lists can co-exist quite happily, and serve different needs at different times!

Let’s prove it!
