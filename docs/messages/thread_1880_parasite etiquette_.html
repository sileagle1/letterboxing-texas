Hi to all,

A regular hitchhiker does have its own stamp and
logbook.

But what about a parasite, the teeny tiny ones? Are
they just a loose stamp that travels willy-nilly,
stamping as they pass by? Or, do they also have their
own separate log book? We have only seen the evidence

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-05-11 21:31:07 UTC-05:00
It's pretty much a given that if there is a stamp, there is also a logbook.  Possibly the only exception to this is a cootie - a funsy stamp that is handed about at a gathering, usually a kids' game of secretly infesting someone with the stamp, the lucky victim stamps it into their logbook and then has to pass it along slyly.
 
A parasite is a HH that piggybacks with a HH.  If it only traveled with one HH for its entire life, I could see it wouldn't have to have a logbook, but some of the fun is removing it from one HH and planting it into another...and in that case, if I were its owner I would want it to have a logbook.  BUT - as with just about everything else to do with LB'ing, if YOU send a parasite off into the world and YOU don't want to send it with a logbook, it's yours and you can do with it as you please!
 
Lucy
