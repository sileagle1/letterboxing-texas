Watch for... What's Blooming?

From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2005-05-08 00:25:56 UTC
I love to garden. Not formal, fussy gardening, but more
unstructured splashes of color. My front yard features a large
butterfly garden. The back has a hummingbird-friendly section as
well as a cutting garden. I experiment with annuals and perennials
and the garden changes regularly. I thought it would be fun to
combine my hobbies with a letterbox featuring a stamp of whatever
was currently blooming in my yard.

I plan to change out the stamp regularly (every couple of months?)
based on what's blooming. I thought it might be fun if the location
of the box changed along with the stamp, so that it might be more
fun to find it again for those who are interested in the whole
series. So, look for the traveling What's Blooming? to land in
different spots all around the greater Houston Metropolitan area.
The log book will stay the same, but repeat finders are encouraged
to stamp into each flower's section if they find it again.

So, What's Blooming now? Right now it's foxglove, the first time
I've grown this great plant. The lavender spikes look particularly
nice next to the still-hanging on yellow pansies and the little miss
muffet daisies.

The stamp is carved and the box is ready to go, with the first
hiding place scouted out. The only catch is I have a badly strained
ankle. So Asterix (doing great after his knee surgery) has promised
to hide it for me. Look for clues to be posted tomorrow.

And, to all the mothers out there, may your day be filled with
flowers.

Dewberry
