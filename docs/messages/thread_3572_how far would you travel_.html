From: the carrascos (a.carrasco@earthlink.net) | Date: 2008-05-21 21:38:57 UTC-05:00
When I first started boxing the kids and I made a very memorable trip to Glen Rose by way of Waco and various stops along the way just to look for letterboxes.  I am still grateful to Silver Eagle for the fine itinerary he provided me when I had no idea what I was doing.
 
Now which far away boxes call my name?  If I could figure out how to swing it I would love to go to New Mexico to search out some Astro D and Lionsmane boxes.  And I know someday I will figure out how to make the trip to Midland for Boots Tex's "Homerun that Never Was", still the best baseball story I have ever read, and a box I would love to find.  MO5

From: sha.towers (TravelinTsOfTexas@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-05-22 14:44:35 UTC
there are many wonderful boxes in our state and many that I've yet to
visit, but when I think about how far I would travel for some good
boxes, I would definitely recommend visiting Santa Fe. it's worth the
trek! Some of the most amazing letterboxing experiences we've had were
in this city and many were thanks to Lionsmane and AstroD. Plus,
they'll be happy to meet you at their favorite local coffee house and
help you sort out the tons of boxes and help with your plan of attack!
This group of boxes has something for everyone...great urban stealth
adventures, magnificent sites, beautiful hikes, clever clues,
beautiful stamps and much more. It's worth the drive (even at today's
gas prices!) :-)

TravelinTs

TravelinTsOfTexas.gmail.com
