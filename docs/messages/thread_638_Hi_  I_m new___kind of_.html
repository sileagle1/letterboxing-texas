From: busybeesfamily (bbbndrider@msn.com) | Date: 2005-03-01 18:45:02 UTC

Hi!

This is my first time to post to this board, although I have been
silently "lurking" for a short time. My name is Brandi and myself,
my husband and 2 children are the BusyBeesFamily.

We were first introduced to letterboxing via a Girl Scout
orienteering event. They just said a small bit about other
activities using compass skills, and we were intrigued. We looked
into it a bit and thought it might be a good fit for the family!
Then, we got our neighbors hooked on it and now it's turning into a
great way for the neighborhood block to spend time together.

Our first find was SilverEagle's Bluebonnet box at Yegua Creek Park
(there for GS fishing event)! We have found 4 others around the
Austin area. We have also planted our very first box in San Gabriel
Park in Georgetown (If you plant it....). We do have plans and ideas
for a great many more boxes. We are hoping that if we plant more
boxes for others to find, that they will leave behind some boxes when
they come...hint, hint! There are many beautiful parks, lakes and
historic sites in and around Georgetown!

We are interested in coming to the event on March 19th. It would be
2 adults/2 children. I would also like to carve an oil rig
(Spindletop?) for your game. If I carve it today, could I mail it
tomorrow? I know this is last minute, but we were not sure if we
could make it to the event or not. Also, please remember that we are
not quite experts in the "carving" aspect of letterboxing, but I
would be happy to give it my best shot. :-) Please just let me know.

Thanks, Brandi (for BusyBeesFamily)
