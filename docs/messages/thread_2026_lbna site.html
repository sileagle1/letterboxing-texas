From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-08-06 01:15:58 UTC
This is one reason I think it is good to have your clues listed a
couple of places. If you post your clues on LBNA, but put an
Atlasquest link to them, AQ will save a "cached" copy of them, and
the clues will still be available when LBNA goes down. This works
very well, IMHO.

Or, you could do as Silver Eagle has done and host on your own
webpage.

You can search for a cached copy of specific clues using google, but
this is not working very well for newer clues. The wayback machine
(www.archive.org) will also help with older clues, but you sometimes
have to have very specific information to find what you're looking
for. It was a drag for me, because between the time I looked up the
clues in my hotel room upstairs and went downstairs to use the
printer (shooing away the gaggle of teeny myspace users), LBNA went
down and I was unable to find the boxes in Pensacola we were going
to find. However, that meant more beach time for the kids, so they
didn't complain.

This will all be very interesting. I have no problem paying a small
fee to keep LBNA running, after all, I'm a premium member at
Atlasquest because I appreciate how much work it is to run a website
like that and I want to support those efforts. As my husband says,
we should at least all contribute to the Choi beer fund as a thank
you for his HOURS of work.

But, there are people who will be ADAMENTLY opposed to even a
suggestion of paying. I hope cooler heads prevail, and I suggest we
just stay out of it!

DB

This is exactly what I've been thinking as well - glad I have most of mine in both places.  Sometimes AQ goes down too.  The likelihood they'll both ever be down at the same time is pretty slim, but it is remotely possible.  A couple boxes of mine aren't on AQ though.  I've also got a few of mine hosted myself on geocities, and I'll sure move toward getting them all over there, if not for public use at least for my own purposes.  The one thing I wish right now is that I had a copy saved also to my hard drive in a single Word file.
