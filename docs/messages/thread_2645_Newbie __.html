From: Amy (amjo0675@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-03-16 21:13:02 UTC
Hey guys!
I'm Amy and I'm the list owner for the Yahoo Group SETXPlaygroup.
http://groups.yahoo.com/group/SETXplaygroup/
Several of our moms are interested in letterboxing in the SETX area,
although none of us has ever actually doen it. We're doing a craft
day in a couple of weeks to make our stamps and talk about placing
some boxes in our area. I'm open to any and all suggestions and ideas
about either topic. We want the kids (preschool aged) to be able to
have easy to use materials for making their stamps. We will, of
course, be helping them, but I can't see us carving them. Is it
acceptable to use stamps cut from foam sheets and glued to some sort
of block? I don't want to offend any hardcore boxers by asking stupid
or uninformed questions, but I really don't have alot of places to
ask, so if I'm ignorant, please excuse me. :)
Also, are there any boxers from the SETX area on here? Most of us are
from the Beaumont area, but we're going to start out by trying to
find the boxes that are in Hardin, Jefferson, and Chambers counties
and we'd love company and/or experience with this area. :)
A little about me:
I'm 31 and have 3 kiddoes who'll be boxing with me. My youngest son
is 10 months old and I have a 3 yr old daughter and an 11 yr old son
(he's the MOST interested). I run the SETXPlaygroup in Beaumont and
have just started a home-based business making soft-structured baby
carriers (ring slings, mei tais, and wraparound carriers) and
boutique-style kids clothes out of bandanas. We are an attachment
parenting family who wear our baby and co-sleep and I breastfeed on
demand still and intend to until lil man decides to wean himself.
(Which I don't imagine will happen anytime soon.....lol ;)
I'm super excited about having found this group and would LOVE any
experience and advice you guys have to lend. :)

From: kelly04tx (kelly04tx@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2007-03-18 19:31:15 UTC
Hi! I am from the Beaumont area also. I have visited your playgroup
website and know a few people from there...I am currently doing
letterboxing myself here in this area and loving it. Unfortunately
there are not many boxes around here. I can give you some advice
about some that are listed that are not there anymore so you won't
waste your time. I really suggest making your own stamps. It sounds
very intimidating but really isn't. I have found it to be very
simple. If you email me offline I can suggest some simple things I
used. I even carved a bunch of stamps for some kids for our church
camping trip and it really can be very easy. Hand carved is the way
to go. Much more personal. Good luck and I would love to help.
