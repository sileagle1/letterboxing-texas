Re: Who had the spray adhesive idea?

From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2005-07-25 02:45:07 UTC
I buy my spray adhesive at a discount art supply store, but they
have them at Target too. A BIG can should last you through a
million projects! You won't believe all the uses you'll find for
it.

It'll be interesting to hear your finders' reactions to the sprayed
on twigs.

I tried using spray adhesive to mount stamps to foam. Didn't work,
and neither did hot glue. What worked best is Gorilla Glue (mucho
thanks to a very experienced and crazy letterboxer for that hint).
But you have to get a thin coat because it foams like Mad. I bet it
would work for twigs and other camouflage materials too--it's
waterproof.

DB

That's funny - I just saw Gorilla Glue at Office Depot
yesterday and I couldn't for the life of me remember
where I had heard of it before. NOWWW I remember...

I'm sure I'll be able to get through the can of spray
adhesive eventually. Between letterboxing and scouts,
I find myself gluing a LOT! The only bad thing I
found about the glue yesterday was - I took my son
with me and I promised him that as soon as we finished
boxing, we would stop at Eilenbarger's in Palestine
for cookies (make a note of that place for next time
y'all are in Palestine-they also make great
sandwiches!). Well, by the time I sprayed down three
