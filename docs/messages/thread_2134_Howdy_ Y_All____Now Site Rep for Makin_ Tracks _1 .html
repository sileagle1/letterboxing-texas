And I think the more people who do both, the better the
hobbies/sports/pasttimes understand each other.

I pretty much only go geocaching with a certain letterboxing pal,
mainly so I am not out there looking foolish walking in circles by
myself. We have seen some really creative and amazing hides by
geocachers. I'm not a fan of the mushrooming micro-geocaches
seemingly randomly dropped by the side of the road (or worse, way in
the woods), but I really appreciate those gcers who go to some effort
to provide scratch-your-head?-oh-doh!-right-there! entertainment. In
letterboxing and in geocaching, I like a thoughtful placement (and
that can mean lots of things).

So welcome to our little game. I'm glad to see you're joining right in!

Re: Howdy, Y'All!...Now Site Rep for Makin' Tracks #1 & #

From: maliabarth (maliabarth@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-09-05 03:17:38 UTC
We aren't fans of the urban micros, either... I don't mind a couple
now and then, especially when travelling (we are trying to get a
geocache in each state we travel through and micros are handy then),
and a local geocacher just put out 50 micros in my town in about 6
weeks! Ok, so what is so unique about the 5 postal boxes he put them
on? Or the Utility Boxes or road posts? Also, some of them appear to
be on private property (and I know some of them are...) and I really
doubt he got permission to hide those.. one of them I know for sure
that he didn't... but that is a different story. Yes, we have found
3 of them... but I don't really intend to look for the others, even
though it would greatly increase our count.

Enough of that rant... sorry.... Micros do have their place, but I
just don't see a need for 50 basically identical micros on lampposts
or utility boxes in a 10 mile radius...

Anyway, we have enjoyed bringing people to a park they might not have
known about, a pretty trail, and things like that... we like to see
our numbers increase, but I want to have each of those numbers
represent something.. that is what geocaching seemed to be about
when we first started (January 2002), and that is what we try to do
when we geocache.

If you get over to central Texas (Georgetown, especially) we can show
you some really fun geocaches, and hopefully our letterboxes will
stand up to the established standards... we sure will try, anyway.

Malia
