Congrats - and looking back.

From: Mike Olbrisch (mike-2007@elp.rr.com) | Date: 2007-08-31 14:28:59 UTC

Congrats to Silver Eagle and Baby Bear!!! 5 years - and you have done so
much for letterboxing in Texas.

I just pulled out my first logbook and looked..... Sad to say my first
letterbox was a Letterbox-Hybrid Geocache. I have been a Geocacher longer
than Silver Eagle and Baby Bear have been a Letterboxing. But still - it
was my first letterbox activity.

The really bad news is that it was what started me looking at Letterboxing.
And now all you good folks have to put up with my whining about no boxes in
El Paso.

My second box was a Silver Eagle Letterbox. I have come a long way since
then. I pretty much only Geocache and Letterbox while we are on the road to
Dog shows and stuff, so my counts are not real high. But - there isn't much
to pick from locally (whine whine). Couple that with the fact that when we
are on the road we don't have time to make the big hikes, so we miss a few.
On top of that, my dog is not able to hike anymore. Just getting too old.
He was along when I found the Silver Eagle box. With 5 obedience titles, he
is the best dog I have ever owned. I can't toss him aside for a new hiking
dog. http://www.vonbonehenge.com/parker.html

Today there are eight letterboxes in the El Paso area. One from Silver
Eagle, one from Sandibox, one from Dane Lady (my lovely wife of 30 years) -
the other 5 are all mine. I tried introducing a few folks to Letterboxing.
And I made most of my letterboxes Letterbox-Hybrids to try getting some
Geocachers interested. So far, it has not worked.

The high-point of my 2-years (on 18 Sept) Letterboxing is the day The
Grizzled Griffon blew through El Paso and snagged all my easy boxes. I got
a big head thinking my boxes were worthy of his visit. Then Dane Lady
explained that he was probably desperate for anything while driving through
to a real Letterboxing destination, and that was all there was.
Oh-well..... She is usually correct.

Now the good news. Yesterday was my birthday. I took the day off from work
today. The mission I was supposed to be on this weekend has been scrubbed.
We were to launch at 0800 this morning bound for a local mountaintop TV
station to replace a radio repeater for our small radio group. My job was
to climb the 200 ft tower and pull a 1" dia cable to the top for the antenna
connector. Yeah - I may be 52 now, but I can still put a hurt on young
soldiers! . Ask my son.

Access to the TV station is by hiking or an expensive tram ride. We were
going to take the tram - then live up there till we were finished rather
than pay for multiple rides. Two to maybe three days. Last minute details
killed the trip. NOT my fault! So, the wife and I decided to do a few
things around the local area today. Tomorrow we are going to Alamogordo NM
for a Hamfest - mostly to look at used radios etc. SUNDAY - our mission is
to PLACE a Letterbox in Culberson County, and PLAN a letterbox for Hudspeth
County on the return trip. So - with a little luck - and a lot of gas, one
more county colored in this weekend, and another to follow as soon as I can
design and order a stamp. Thinking about Cornudas Texas.

Letterboxing has been fun. You good folks on this list have enhanced the
fun. Thanks.

If I had one wish - it would be for Baby Bear to edit the name of his TOP of
TEXAS letterbox to add (missing) or something. There have been a number of
times when I logged on, and saw it, and thought it was a new active box.
Yeah - if I looked more often I would remember it!

Congrats Baby Bear and Silver Eagle. Thanks for your efforts.

Mike - KD9KC.
