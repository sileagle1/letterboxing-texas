From: Mike Olbrisch (mike-2007@elp.rr.com) | Date: 2010-08-30 09:07:46 UTC-06:00

Ahhhh – yes it is.  Thank you – thank you.  I had a great adventure yesterday for this year’s birthday party. 

 

How I spent my 55th birthday party.

 

I had my birthday present yesterday, a day early.  My son James and his girlfriend Monica arrived at 0700.  We loaded up in the dog-show van along with my portable ham radio set.  We drove 30 miles north to Las Cruces NM, then about 10 miles west, then another 10 miles north into the desert, to a mountain range called Rough and Ready Hills.  We climbed to the summit, where James and Monica helped me set up a 30 foot fiberglass mast, assembled my antenna and station, and put up a 12’ x 12’ canopy for shade.  The three of us had it all set up quickly.

 

I was 5403’ ASL, and the view was spectacular.  I pulled out my cell phone and sent a 1-line text message.  D QRPSPOTS KD9KC 14.306 1505UTC SOTA W5/RO-015.  What this meant was Data to www.QRPSPOTS.COM that KD9KC (me) was on 14.306 Mega Hertz at 1505 Universal Coordinated Time activating Summits on the Air summit number W5/RO-015.  QRPSPOTS is a place that many people watch to see who is on the air and from where. 

 

I turned on the radio and identified myself – KD9KC listening.  I was quickly called by my good friend Jeff in Jackson Mississippi.  We chatted a while – then closed.  I was immediately called by another good friend, Fred in Auburn California.  Fred has a special place in my heart.  Fred was Air Force in Viet Nam.  What he has for legs now isn’t much, but he doesn’t let it stop him.  He activates summits too, but only the ones he can drive within a mile of.  Fred was Air Force, but he should have been an Army Ranger for what he did.  He didn’t fly, he was on the ground and often behind the lines.  What a great guy.  James got to talk to Fred too, they are great buddies as Fred sent James a box of goodies to Iraq about every 2 weeks.

 

James and Monica then went off to go exploring while I finished talking to Fred.  For the next few hours I was alone on the summit overlooking a beautiful desert valley.  A while later I saw James and Monica going up the next hilltop to the west.  James had his handheld ham radio with him, so I called him.  They were just looking around and enjoying the scenery and each other. 

 

Secretly, I think they will get married in the future.  I am so happy for James.  He certainly saw hell in the gulf.  In 3 combat tours he was decorated for valor 3 times.  He carries a patch in his pocket that just says SPC SHAY.  SPC SHAY died in Iraq.  James’ EMT skills couldn’t save his life.  He couldn’t save Sgt Regulado either.  I suspect there are more, but he hasn’t spoken of others yet.  I know it hurts him inside.  I know all too well.  But James is home now, with a family that understands what he has been through, and a girlfriend that obviously loves him.   And better yet, she can keep up with him.  She never did any of this before meeting James.  But in the last 6 months she has learned to camp, hike, and shoot.  And she liked it all.  Even the big purple bruise my sniper rifle gave her.  She just had to try it, and she loved it.  They just got a new puppy, a German Short-hair Pointer.  A dog that when grown can keep up with them well.  And Monica has asked how to get a stamp for Letterboxing.  James better not let her get away!  <grin>.

 

After talking to James, my friend Michael in Alberta Canada called me.  We had a nice chat there too.  And so it went.  After a while I just started listening around on the radio randomly looking for people to talk to.  I discovered the 28 Mega Hertz band was open to South America, so I chatted with some stations in Brazil, Ecuador, and Bolivia.  My Spanish sucks, but their English was quite good.

 

About 1pm I saw James and Monica coming back across the flanks of the summit to the west of me.  I called him to see if they were returning.  They were, so I reluctantly shut down my radio and started packing it up.  I had it about 75% done when they arrived.  They were hot, the they got under my canopy out of the sun while I finished winding up the parachute cords that held my antenna up and such.

 

While doing this, I was attacked by an Africanized killer-bee.  That’s my story and I am sticking to it.  This bee came out of no-where, and began zooming around my head at high-speed, buzzing madly.  I tried to knock it away with my hands, and even hit it a few times.  But it wouldn’t let up.  It was extremely aggressive.  So I pulled off my hat and began trying to knock the bee away.  That is when it got me.  It never landed, it just brushed against my neck and I was stung.  Within the next few passes I got it in my hat and killed it.  James pulled the stinger out of my neck.  Within minutes ants were triumphantly eating the bee.  While annoying, I have had far worse than a bee sting.  So we packed up the canopy and started down the slope to the van.

 

The drive back to El Paso was uneventful.  We split up to clean up, but we met again at 6:30 pm.  We drove back to Las Cruces to a restaurant called La Posta in Old Mesilla New Mexico.  If you are ever passing through, you got to stop there.  Get the La Posta Grande.  And be hungry!

 

So that was my birthday party.

 

Runs for Chocolate – I hope you have as great a day as I did.  Happy Birthday to you also.

 

Mike - KD9KC.
El Paso, Tx.

There is a story behind that photo-show.

 

SOTA stands for Summits On The Air. The object is to climb summits of the appropriate height and use a radio from the summit to talk to other people. The summits have points assigned to them, based on the height ASL. The climber earns points for activating, the chaser earns points for the contact.

 

The project originates in Great Britain. A fellow ham op in Las Cruces NM started working to get the W5 area (Arkansas, Louisiana. Mississippi, New Mexico, Oklahoma and Texas) sanctioned by the management team (MT). Work took him from the task. I volunteered to do Texas. When the other fellow had to give it up, the MT asked me if I would do the whole W5 project. I said sure. I had no clue what was involved. It took me 6 months of cataloging summits, 1692 total, 447 in Texas and 1064 in New Mexico, the rest scattered in Oklahoma and Arkansas. But once I give my word I keep it.

 

On 1 July 2010 my documents were sanctioned. On 3 July we made the first activation. I made the first contact from the first activated summit.

 

That photo-show is the hike – the operation, and the day was just as perfect as it looked. It only reached 103 F on the way down.

 

The music….. let me see. First – On Day by Arlene Kole, next – What I Did On My Summer Vacation by Shane Jackman, last – Every Second of the Day by Arlene Kole.

 

If you would like to read the whole story of the operation, I will share it.
