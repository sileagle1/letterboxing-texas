From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2010-03-09 07:10:34 UTC-06:00


On Mon, Mar 8, 2010 at 11:18 PM, Boots Tex <redbarn@eastex.net> wrote:

This has been a good safety lesson for all of us.

*****************************************************************************************

Isn't that the truth! I think of all the times I've been out in pretty remote areas by myself and I wonder if I was always as safe as I should have been.

It's funny because as I was repacking my LB backpack to go to the gathering, I noticed that I had lost one of my gloves. I was grousing that I had lost my right glove. Fearless Fox thought it was odd I would think that was bad, since really, you only need one glove most the time and I am left-handed. The right glove is the one that has a big bloodstain on it from a time I got impatient while waiting for Draconis viridis to come back from the car with my gloves. I stuck my hand into a pile of brush and was immediately stabbed by something in it. I pulled my hand out and my finger was bleeding heavily. It took a bit to get it to slow down, but I stuck my hand into my glove and back into the pile of brush to get the box. Later, I realized my finger had bled through the glove. The bloody finger of the glove has been a reminder since that time to slow down and think before I do things.

Safety before all else, and fun will follow.
