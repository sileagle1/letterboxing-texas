Things I learned on Sunday...

From: kerry1194 (k_kellogg@hotmail.com) | Date: 2007-09-19 19:42:00 UTC
Sunday, DH had about 5 minutes of work to do at the office, so he
suggested that we have breakfast up there and then do some lbing. I
thought this was a great plan, mostly because I spent 4 hours on
Saturday playing a video game. Not sure what the guys did. This was
our first real lbing trip as a family. Boy, did I ever learn some
valuable lessons.

1- DH and I should NEVER attempt any home improvement project that
will take more than 3 hours. EVER. There is no way it will end with
two living adults.

2- There was a reason that we bought a car with a navigation system.

3- Letterboxing with navigation feels like cheating. But you are more
likely to have an intact family afterwards.

4- Even when you own a navigation system, make sure you print some
sort of map for the child in the car seat. It doesn't matter if he
can see exactly where you are on the navigation screen, he's still
going to badger you with questions. Budget rental car maps from out
of state work fine until they start reading.

5- If you are navigator, make sure you read EVERY line on the clue,
out loud, twice. Even if you accidently find yourself in the right
place, skipping 3 lines on the clue. Trust me on this one.

6- Avoid cemetery boxes when with DH. He gets the willies and gets
moody. Or at least make sure you don't go to three in a row.

7- Over plan your route. Just having a pile of clues in the same area
may work fine when you are in charge and lbing with a 5 year old, but
it's not going to work with a LSS. Know exactly where you want to go
next. If you can get exact directions (road, not clue) between boxes,
you could save a life.

8- Always make spouse program the address of the next box into the
navigation system BEFORE moving the car. Or you are going to hear
constant whining about not knowing where he is going for the next 5
minutes as you drive the two miles to the next park. (And we didn't
even turn!)

9- When faced with a number of boxes near each other that require
walking, make sure you don't start with the one near the nifty
playground. Because the 5 year old will barter boxes for playground time.

We actually had fun, though it was a little warm from us. We hit 5
locations and found 2 boxes. I will make very different choices about
any other treasure hunting adventures we plan with DH. More nature
walks, less headstones. He did take the camera out of the car a
couple of times, so it wasn't a complete loss for him. I do
understand why my mother printed 3 copies of all the boxes in VT.
Because there is no way my parents could survive a letterboxing
adventure sharing.

As a side note, DH did tell me that I am banned from printing clues.
I have a binder with local clues in it. After we find the box, I take
the clue out. Well, the binder is very full. If I want to print any
more, they have to be within 10 minutes of the house or school, we
have to be going on a trip out of DFW or I have to find about 15 boxes
and remove those clues. Z is viewing this as a change. We have a 1pm
dismissal on Friday and he wants to hit at least 2 boxes. Then there
is the 4 day weekend next week. He thinks we could easily pick up 110
in 4 days, because that is as high as he can count. :)

Happy boxing!
Kerry

From: kerry1194 (k_kellogg@hotmail.com) | Date: 2007-09-20 11:02:30 UTC
Don't get me wrong. I think navigation systems are WONDERFUL. DH can
be driving directly into the sun at 8am and swear that we are going
West. It just felt like cheating. Mostly because all the other boxes
I've done have been close by and I haven't needed a map to get to my
starting point.

But I was raised with a man that over over planned EVERYTHING with
maps. When I was looking at colleges, he spent 3 weeks planning the
route that we needed to take. He bought a map of New York and
highlighter the route for us, plus two side trips that we might want
to take. Of course, he wasn't going with us, so the first thing my
mother did after leaving the house was stop at her best friend's house
so we could ditch all the maps. We were gone for a week and had an
absolute blast. I think we used a little map on the back of a hotel
brochure once to get somewhere.

I agree that navigations systems are just another item that we can use
to keep us safe, but the first time using it made me feel like I was
missing something.

Kerry
