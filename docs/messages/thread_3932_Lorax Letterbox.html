I just received a note from a ranger at Lake Whitney SP telling me I
will have to fill out a geocaching permit for my Lorax box or it will
be confiscated. After looking at the restrictions and information I
would have to provide for the permit every year, I have decided not to
comply. I have changed the clues to require an email to me for the
clues, but if he already knows where it is located, I'm sure it will be
confiscated. Anyway, if you want to give this box a try, just email me
for the clues.

From: Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter (janet@redbarnretreat.com) | Date: 2009-01-29 01:23:04 UTC
That box has been there for a long time. I wonder why the ranger just
now discovered it. I think that having to renew the permit every year
is the real problem. That would be impossible for a lot of boxes, and
you can't develop a relationship with every park ranger. I don't know
the answer, other than to mark the box as "confiscated" and write it
off. I've done that to a couple of boxes. If the park personnel start
monitoring our websites and make hunting down letterboxes a priority,
there's nothing we can do about it (and maybe they don't have enough to
do).

Boots Tex
