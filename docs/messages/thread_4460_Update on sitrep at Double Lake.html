Update on sitrep at Double Lake

From: Boots Tex (redbarn@eastex.net) | Date: 2010-03-22 23:05:28 UTC
After a very wintery weekend, today was a warm and sunny day, so I went out to Double Lake Recreation Area to finish checking on the boxes there. Last week I checked out 17 and found 16 and today I checked out 18 and found 17. So, there are 33 letterboxes in that park. The only one I didn't find was "A Gift for Princess Aurora" by Barefoot Lucy. Sorry, Lucy, that was a favorite of mine because, as you know, Princess Aurora is my 9 year old granddaughter. The only box I found missing last week belonged to her, so she's not going to be very happy. The boxes that I did find and that, other than a couple of damp logbooks, were:

Four Legged Letterboxers by dewberry (a series of 5)
Best in Breeds by dewberry (another series of 5, although I found one of them last week)
A Dog-Gone Good Time by Lucy Locket
The Legend of the Dogwood by Baileys Bunch
Some Days You're the Dog.. By SandiBox
The LetterBOXER's Companion by SandiBox
Double Lake by Boots Tex
Flying Geese Quilt Block by Lone Star Quilter
It Is What It Is by El Lobo
You Ain't Nuthin' but a Hound Dog by Lone Star Quilter

The last box, You Ain't Nuthin' but a Hound Dog, has never been found and it's been out there for 2 years. The reason? I forgot to post the clues and only noticed that when I was printing out clues for all the boxes. Now, Lone Star Quilter doesn't know that I forgot to post the clues, so the only way she'll know is if you tell her. I'll get them posted tonight. Most of the 33 boxes there were found at the event in March 2008 and have only been found a couple of times, if at all, since then. It's really a shame because there are some very nice boxes out there. The 2 series above, by dewberry, consist of boxes made for the event by guest letterboxers: CW Sunseeker, MoWizLiz & Teeps;man, Flutterby, Fungus Woman, Sahalie, Triple T, Jabber & Jo Bob, SHH and JACKS. Where else are you going to find their boxes in Texas? Just throwing in a little plug for Coldspring. I hope this was winter's last blast so we can all go hunt some letterboxes. There are another 6 or 7 boxes on the Lone Star Trail close to Double Lake, so I'll check them out in a day or two.

Boots Tex

Let us know when you're coming, Mosaic Butterfly. As you can see by my report, there are a lot of boxes and Double Lake is a very pretty park. This is a good time to come. The weather has been just about perfect the last couple of days, the dogwoods are starting to bloom, there are no mosquitos, no spider webs across the trails with big yellow spiders and if you come on a weekday, you'll have the place mostly to yourself. I went back out today to check on a few boxes that are on the Lone Star Trail close to Double Lake, but not actually in the park. I picked up a couple of the boxes to dry out the logbook and they'll go back out tomorrow, so the letterboxes out there will all be accounted for and maintained. They are:

The Joker by Baby Bear
Quilted Boots By Silver Eagle
Muppets Take Texas Series: Gonzo by Pastry Princess
Shoolfly Quilt Block by Lone Star Quilter
Picoides Borealis by Boots Tex
Holy Anole by Wills World

And who knows, Princess Aurora's box, Mr. Bobbles, may be replaced by the time you are here.

Boots Tex
