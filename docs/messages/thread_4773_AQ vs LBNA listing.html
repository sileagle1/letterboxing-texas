From: (norwas111@aol.com) | Date: 2011-04-29 09:53:55 UTC-04:00
I don't think it is a waste of time to list the boxes in both places because if you don't you may miss sharing your boxes with all letterboxers. I look at both sites and just weed out the duplicates, I figure that would be better than missing something special.
 
However, I do have to agree I like AQ better than LBNA. And it has to do with the way it is organized. On LBNA there are about 100 listings for Houston, Harris County and you have to read each one to see where the box is, where as on AQ you can enter a location and the distance and get a better handle on which boxes are near where you want to go. LBNA may work better for rural counties because there are not as many boxes out there you can more easily see how many are in that given county.
 
The other thing that is a bit of a pet-peeve for me with LBNA is that some posters do not know where they are placing their boxes. For example, I went on a camping trip to Waller last year and could have sworn there were boxes in the park we were staying because I had seen them in the past. I search LBNA, Waller County and could not find them. Well a few weeks later I was looking for something else in Harris County and WOW there they were. The poster had listed the boxes as being in WALLER, HARRIS COUNTY, WALLER IS IN WALLER COUNTY!!!  There is another box that is the city of Houston listed in HOUSTON, HOUSTON COUNTY. I just think if you are going to place a box and post it, then you should post it correctly, if you don't no one will find it.
 
Sorry that just bothers me.

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2011-04-29 09:43:53 UTC-05:00

No matter which site you use, the information pulled by a finder is only as good as the information put in by a placer. Ive run into similar issues with AQ, where the placer put North instead of South in front of the street name and Ive wound up on the opposite side of town. On LBNA, there is a box in the middle of nowhere that is listed as being in a town 15 miles from the box, when there is a larger and better known town that is only 5 miles from the box. I never have understood that logic!

Dewberry and I had a similar discussion several years ago when she commented she liked AQ because she didnt have to use counties (been a long time, so thats a rough recollection of part of the discussion). And she was right because she is an urban boxer primarily, counties make NO sense for her! Saying Harris County for a Houston boxer is like saying somewhere West of here for me. For me, a rural boxer, using counties makes a lot more sense because it covers a broader area with lots of tiny communities I might not ever find if the county werent listed to help me narrow it down. Ive noticed that AQ doesnt handle unincorporated communities very well there are often three or four towns with the same name in the state and AQ points to one of them and it may not be the right one. Ryan has been gracious in helping to get them fixed, but its a problem that will never completely go away because tracking down every New Hope in the state of Texas is quite a challenge that he doesnt have time for!

As to all the pinpointing that AQ does, well thats the work I like to do for myself. Planning a day-long outing is a military maneuver in my house. I thoroughly enjoy studying maps, printing them and putting them all together, marking them up, studying clues in advance to see how close I can get to the boxs actual location. Quite often I learn a lot more about the area than just where the boxes are in the process. Thats a big part of letterboxing to me, and a part that gets my brain circulating and gets me out from in front of the computer. (It also drives Fearless Fox a little crazy when I get that paper flurry going and take over the kitchen table)

And so it comes back to the fact, that we thankfully have two sites with different focuses because if we only had one, some of us would not get what we want from letterboxing.

BarefootLucy
