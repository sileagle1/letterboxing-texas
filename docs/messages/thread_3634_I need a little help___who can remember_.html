Hmmm...  I have never been to a gathering - too far so far.  But I have two favorites I will share with you.  Lots of people have recipes for meat, so I will do the veggies!
 
===============================================
 
First favorite needs white onions, green chilies, jalapenos and butter.

Remove the dead dry skin to expose the fresh onion.  Cut the ends off the onion.  Set aside.
 
Cut the stem from the Jalapeno.  Cut into quarters lengthwise and remove the seeds and insides.  Set aside.
 
Roast fresh green Chilies.  Peal them, and remove the seeds and insides.  Try to keep the chilie intact in one big piece.  Set aside.
 
Now pay attention here, ALMOST cut the onion into quarter, but not quite all the way through.  It should have splits in it, but not fall into fourths.
 
Set the onion on a piece of aluminum foil large enough to wrap it fully closed.
 
Stuff slices of jalapeno into the split of the onion.
 
Place a pat of butter flat on top the onion. 
 
Wrap the onion with a Green chilie.
 
Wrap it all together in the aluminum foil and bake in the oven or campfire till the onion is soft and falls apart.  Timing depends on heat.  Unwrap and serve when done.
 
=================================================
 
Second favorite needs fresh corn on the cob still in the husk.
 
Cut the tip off the corn, exposing the end of the cob.
 
Soak on a bucket of water for several hours.  The idea here is to get the water down into the husk.
 
Throw the corn husk and all onto a grill with a hot fire.  Turn them over from time to time.  Roast until the outer layer of husk is burnt nearly black.
 
CAREFUL, they are HOT!  Remove from the grill, pull back the husk (makes a great handle) butter and salt, and serve it up.
 
==================================================
