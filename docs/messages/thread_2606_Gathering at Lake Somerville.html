Gathering at Lake Somerville

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-02-23 23:38:23 UTC-06:00
Here we are - a week before the big gathering!  I can hardly wait!  Here in East Texas the daffodils are blooming and the grass is greening up.  The air is fresh and clean and it is just nothing but Spring-y!  I can only imagine what it must be like at Somerville.
 
Let me encourage you to take a look at your schedule, and if you don't have next weekend booked, why don't you think of stretching yourself a bit and heading on down to Somerville?  I know there are quite a few time conflicts and if it had been a week or two or three earlier or later, I would've had a conflict too! 
 
BUT - there are some of you who DON'T have anything scheduled indelibly.  Maybe you're new and a little hesitant to attend a gathering?  Maybe you just don't know about going that far?  Maybe you think you've waited too late and can't get a campsite? 
 
Quit thinking of reasons you can't go and start thinking of ways you CAN go! 
 
For instance, it's going to be only Lightning and me in our campsite - if you have a tent and there are six or fewer of you, we would be more than happy to share our campsite. 
 
Not sure about meeting a bunch of strangers?  I can assure you - we won't be strangers for long, or at least we won't be unfamiliar for long - we may be just as strange as we were before you met us...
Going that far?  Why not?  It's only gas, and heck, a few short months ago it was 30 cents higher than it is right now...

The March gathering hosted by Silver Eagle and Baby Bear annually marks the anniversary of the first gathering (okay, not technically the first gathering, but the first gathering since letterboxing was reborn in Texas).  That first event was a gathering of folks who weren't sure what they were getting into, but not daring to not participate.  Sound familiar?  This will be the third anniversary of that first event and each year has seen the addition of new faces and the notable absences of old faces.  It's the marking and the making of letterboxing history in Texas.
 
SO - if your calendar allows, come on down - you'll be glad you did!
