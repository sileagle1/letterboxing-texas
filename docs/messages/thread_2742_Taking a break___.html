Taking a break...

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-04-26 22:40:11 UTC-05:00
Well after months of preparation, hours of carving, reams of paper, hole-punching, thinking, not-thinking, bandaids, questions, brilliant ideas, dumb ideas, and everything in between, this is the eve of the cub scout event I've been preparing for.
 
After five years of attending, Lightning is going to his first Cub Scout event as a staff member.  Fearless Fox is running the Archery Range, and I'm doing the letterboxing event.  Lightning is going to be my right hand guy (since he is the only other staffer that really has more than a general idea what it is we are doing!).   And poor Lightning has to keep reminding his dad that he WANTS to sleep in a tent this time instead of his hammock - after years of sleeping on the ground in nylon tents with scant ventilation, he wants his first shot at staff tents, the taj mahals of tent camping - old army tents with flies at each end to let the breeze (and the occasional critter) roll over you while you sleep in a bunk instead of on the ground, but don't bring food into those tents because the Pirtle Bears will invade and fight you for it - THEY know where the good campsite is - STAFF AREA! 
 
Fearless Fox is presently making archery awards out of leather rounders, so he has the wood burner going and the drill, so that we have the stench of charred dead flesh and leather sawdust all over the kitchen, mixing with rubber shavings and splotches of ink on the kitchen table.  And Lightning trying to make some small space to do homework of all things.
About 160 personal stamps down the road - and many thanks to letterboxers Corazon, GingerCat, TxDragonFly, MoWizLiz, PuddleSplasher, and JB Kokopelli for helping me hit that mark, along with my good friend, SheWhoReads, who felt guilty enough for throwing me in the deep end on this project that she learned to carve and came over several nights and carved with me while we laughed and lamented and had oh such a wonderful time! 
 
And the numerous staff stamps that HAD to be done, and a few cooties - of course! - and then there are the station stamps, all done up with nice logbooks - except the one last minute event change that I just learned of an hour ago.  And the event stamp and how on earth do you stamp it onto a patch?  Still working on that one...
 
Posters and materials, and 14 boxes hidden in places to avoid poison ivy (easier said than done!) and 2 snakes who created momentary excitement as we hid the boxes.  A torrential rain since The Day Of The Big Hide that very likely washed a few of said boxes - we'll know better tomorrow.  One box that is right in the line of fire on the sling shot range - oops, didn't realize they were using the shotgun range for that event!  Gotta move it tomorrow and hide a 15th box - they'll be bonuses for the guys who find the other 13. 
 
Busting Lightning out of school early tomorrow, or that was the plan, except that my counterpart at work has been off sick all week and who knows whether she'll be there tomorrow...I gotta bust myself out now too!
 
The fine dads in my own pack are graciously grilling brisket for the camp staff and the folks in their campsite - what a bunch of great guys they are! 
 
After all has been said and done (and since I'm taking my carving material, I guess I may NOT be done), more than 200 stamps have been carved for this thing, along with the requisite logbooks and ink sources and clue distribution - for my fellow staffers and 160 of our closest friends and their dads. 
 
I'm gonna have a GREAT weekend - how bout y'all???
--
Barefoot Lucy
"It's not about footwear, it's about philosophy"
