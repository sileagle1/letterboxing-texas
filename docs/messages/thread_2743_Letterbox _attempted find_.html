Just need a little advice from the group: one of my new Austin
boxes, "Two for the Price of One" has been reported as "attempted" on
AtlasQuest. The report was by a new letterboxer and, since my box was
new and had been found only a few days before by an experienced and
reliable boxer, I contacted the new person and asked the circumstances
(were my directions confusing? Are you sure you were in the right
spot and did it look like the box had been there, but removed?). It's
been several days and I have gotten no response. How do you handle a
situation like this? I don't want folks not to look for it until it's
loss has been confirmed, just because it's been attempted and not
found. LSQ's box, "Pinwheel - Quilt Block Series" is nearby, so if
you go to find it, I would appreciate if you would check on mine for
me. Thanks,

Boots Tex

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-04-29 17:51:07 UTC-05:00
Hey Boots!
 
For myself, I don't note a box as missing until I hear it from either a very reliable boxer or several folks (or verify it myself).  There have been too many times when someone (and not necessarily newbies) report my box as being missing, only to find it right where I left it.  And there have been a few occasions when I myself would have thought I had done a perfect job of searching for a box and would have sworn it was gone, only to see that someone found it right after me or the placer verified it was in place.
 
Since you aren't getting an answer from the one who reported it missing, try writing to the person who found it and ask them directly for feedback on the clues.  Was anything ambiguous?  Was something not as described?  Especially since they were a relatively experienced boxer, you should be able to take their feedback and decide what to do next.

LOL...you just have to watch out for that bear that knocks you INTO the hole! 
 
Whether there is a formal definition or not, there is an implied one:  If you actually go to search for the box and are unable to find it after following the directions TO the box, you have attempted it.   If you run into the bear or fall into the hole, that would be a "bad luck" category.
 
The problem with logging attempts is that someone else sees your attempt and thinks "WELL - Lucy didn't find the box, so it MUST be gone."  What they don't know is that maybe I have a pretty low find ratio of - say - 1 out of every 10, because I walk to where the box should be and if it doesn't wave and holler, I don't find it.
 
Although you can log attempts however you choose to do it, you should realize the implication to a box and its finder and perhaps think again whether you really should use the attempted label.  I know I went and searched for one of Lucy Locket's boxes, followed the clues perfectly and found no box.  I was absolutely positive.  I wrote her a couple of days later and she wrote back and said "Hmmm...my mother found it yesterday.  Are you SURE?"  I eventually went back to find it and lo and behold, there was a way to follow the clues that was MORE perfect than the way I had originally followed them.  I could still see why I thought I was on course the first time, but I realized I missed even more distinct landmarks.  That was a brand new box and if I had marked it Attempted, it's possible everyone would have assumed it went missing right away.

never log an attempted find as "attempted". Instead, I think I'll
notify the placer that I tried to find the box and explain the
circumstances and how I interpreted the clues, then leave it to him
to decide if the box is missing or not. Thanks, y'all.
