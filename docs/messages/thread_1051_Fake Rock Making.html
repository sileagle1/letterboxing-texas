Fake Rock Making

From: Wanda (wsthm@hotmail.com) | Date: 2005-07-14 23:01:04 UTC
Here are some instructions I copied and filed away a looong time
ago. This was from Sir Balthazar on the big list:

Fake Rock Making

My material of choice is Amaco Sculptamold Modeling Compound. Three
pounds of it is about $5 + tax. Three pounds makes lots of rocks! It
sets up in about 30 minutes and is very nicely hard after a day.

I am now mixing craft paint in with the wet material as I mold it so
the color goes through the entire "rock". Choose colors from your
area. Grey and black mottling can be very persuasive as just a
concrete lump.

I mold the material around a film canister or food container
(T*pperware). I put the lid side down on the container so it can be
opened later. Sometimes I have to scrape away a little of the
material if it has overlapped the lid. No problem, the stuff can be
carved and sanded.

After it is thoroughly dry, I spray the rock with at least two coats
of clear, matte-finish sealer.

I have recently purchased a product from the hardware store that
shows lots of promise: premixed "Flexall" flexible all-purpose
filler. It is water-proof and stays tacky for a couple hours. That
is long enough to dribble sand on the surface. The color is a slate
gray. It sets up pretty hard after a day. Not a bad thought to coat
the outside of the Amaco plaster stuff as a last sealant against
water.

More thoughts on this: Steve Rayvenhaus says he is experimenting
with Liquid Nails and twigs. I got some of the Liquid Nails and want
to get some pea-gravel to glue to the food containers, too.

I have also used oven-hardening clay. It is pretty much okay as it
is but I have sprayed it with a spackled rock paint and again, folks
look right past them. One is in a very high traffic downtown
location and has remained safe there, much to my surprise. Again,
waterproof these babies.

I think you would be surprised at how brazen one can be with a fake
rock. Folks see what they think should be there, not what is truly
there. Heck, with fake rocks, hollow rocks, Liquid Nails and twigs,
we could make whole centerpieces in which to hide letterboxes.

Now a small caveat: my Amaco rocks are pretty lightweight. They have
also not yet gone through a California winter. You might have to use
more sealant for your area, maybe even put some real rocks glued
together with it to give it weight. I don't know.

Sir Balthazar
