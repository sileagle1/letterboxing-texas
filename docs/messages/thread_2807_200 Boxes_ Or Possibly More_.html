200 Boxes, Or Possibly More?

From: Silver Eagle (sileagle@alltel.net) | Date: 2007-06-04 12:54:51 UTC
Thanks for the congrats on reaching 200 POSTED boxes .
I have been toying with an idea to keep some hidden until my demise,
just haven't worked out how I can post the clues from "the other
side". It would be great if I could come back as a letterboxing
angel, kind of like Clarence in It's A Wonderful Life. Every time
you hear a bell a new box is planted! Of course this makes the huge
assumption I end up north instead of south :)

As to placing a blueberry letterbox, I thought about it for several
years but there is no public place near there. I was going to ask
the owners if I could leave one with them, but they are always so
busy during picking season I did not think they would want to be
bothered. Plus the box would only be available for a few months.
Still, I might do it anyway.

BTW, I cracked up at the vision of everyone following me around like
the Sprint commercial and ducking behind trees whenever I turned
around. Then I laughed even harder when I replaced me with Gryzzled
Gryphon! Can you just see him trying to be his usual quiet, stealthy
self with all of us stampeding behind him?

Silver Eagle

Where I live, Brewster County (6,193 sqare miles) and the 4 adjacent
counties to Brewster County:
Pecos County (4,765 sqare miles)
Jeff Davis County (2,265 sqare miles)
Presidio County (3,856 sqare miles)
Terrell County (2,358 sqare miles)

have a combined land area of 19,437 sqare miles.

Slightly larger than the size of the combined area of Vermont (9,620
square miles) and New Hampshire (9,359 square miles) combined (18,979
sqare miles).[all land mass info from Wikipedia).

Within this area of West Texas there are (using both LBNA and AQ as
sources) a total of 11 letterboxes for me to hunt (there are also 2
of my own, bringing the grand total to 13). 3 of these boxes are
Silver Eagles, so he's provided 27.3% of my local hunting
opportunities. So, thanks again. (to be fair, El Lobo has 3 boxes in
the same space I mentioned).

From: (blhjrh@aol.com) | Date: 2007-06-04 19:22:47 UTC-04:00
You do realize, don't you, that most counties in Vermont and New Hampshire have more letterboxes than all of the area you described has in total?  But there are more letterboxers there, too.  If you plant them, eventually . . . they will come.  Sometime.  I have a couple of letterboxes planted in far away places that have only been visited once, to my knowledge.  Sometimes you just put one in a place because you want to plant one there, and know that if it survives long enough, someone else will want to find it there, too. 
    When I first discovered letterboxing (in New England), I was surprised to discover on my return to Texas that there were actually about 8-10 letterboxes scattered around the DFW area, and I immediately set out to find them all.  Didn't take long, either, and each was a real jewel, an interesting location, an original and thoughtfully-executed stamp, good clues or puzzles that were fun and a little bit challenging, and just a total great experience.   There were only about two local letterboxers, too, who were reponsible for all those boxes and who kept planting new ones, and then I added mine to the local mix, and there were others who appeared, and now we have quite a few DFW metroboxers and dozens and dozens of boxes - more than I can keep up with!  And visitors to the area find them and sometimes plant more, so it's really a sport/game/activity that feeds itself, once you plant a few good ones . . . and they will come!
