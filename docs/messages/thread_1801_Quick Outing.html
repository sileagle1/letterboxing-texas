Quick Outing

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-04-11 20:41:41 UTC-05:00

I had a very stressful day at work and was looking at
a stressful evening too.  I've got less than two weeks
until a district campout for 300 folks - give or take
100 - I am organizing goes off, and I feel like I am
about a month behind getting it together.  By the end
of the work day, I had a major headache and could only
look forward to going home to work on the campout.
About 3 p.m. I emailed my husband and suggested we leave
directly from work and head to Fairchild State Forest
and Rusk for the evening.  I guess he was a little
stressed too because he jumped on that idea and had
everything packed and ready to go, right down to
clothes for me to change in to, when I got off work.

We headed on to Fairchild and first off we found Lucy
Locket's new box there in the forest.  Cool stamp!
Besides Blue Butterfly (who I think was actually with
Lucy Locket when she planted the box), we were the
FF's on this one!  We went on down the trail and
checked on my Red-Cockaded Woodpecker and it was safe
and sound and tucked nicely away in its hiding place.

We drove on into Rusk and took a walk to find Baby
Bear's Lion King box - we still have several boxes
from the gathering we haven't found!  Lion King had
lots of stamps and was hidden pretty well - some trail
to get there! 

We were tentatively planning on getting the other
boxes there in Rusk State Park that we haven't yet
gotten, but our stomachs got the best of us and we
headed back to Maydelle to Big Bertha's for a burger.
She makes good burgers too!  By then it was getting a
little dark, so we took the back roads home and just
got in.  What a difference a couple of hours on the
trail makes - I'm ready to settle in and work on my
campout now and the rude customer I had to spend most
of the day dealing with is a vague memory.  Headache
is mostly gone too!

I do have to say - I think poison ivy must thrive on
drought.  It looks as if it is going to be worse than
usual this year.  I've had a case of it for 10 days
now, and after today's outing, I don't see being rid
of it for a week or two still.  It was pretty much
unavoidable!  When SJZ lived at home, I kept the pre-
and post-exposure treatments on hand, but I let her
take it all with her since she has the worst allergy
of any of us to PI.  Guess I'm going to have to
re-invest in it now!

Thanks to Baby Bear and to Lucy Locket for the great
boxes today.  We're planning on hitting the trails
again pretty soon to find the other Rusk boxes, and I
have a longer trip planned for the last week of the
month - after my campout is a done deal!
--
Barefoot Lucy
"It's not about footwear, it's about philosophy"

Yikes to all of the Poison Ivy out there!  I have seen lots of it on the running trails, too.  Not good! 
 
One way to box without fear of poison ivy is to do so in another state!  M-Dot and I were in Tempe, Arizona this past weekend for the Ironman Arizona.  M-Dot was a participant and did an awesome job at his second Ironman competition!
 
While there, I actually planted a box along Tempe Town Lake which was the running trail for the Ironman.  It is called Ironman Arizona ( I know...real original!)  Anyway, we were able to box some in the area and found seven letterboxes:  Papago Park Box #1 and Box #2,  Sun Devil Letterbox #1, Sun Devil Letterbox #2, ASU Memories #2 ( #1 was on top of a mountain near radio towers but the whole area around the trails were under some serious construction and we weren't able to get up the mountain), ASU Memories #3-4 were missing.  In Scottsdale, we found Happy Gecko at a really cute park with metal desert statues and then found On the Pond at another park in Scottsdale.
 
Back to the poison ivy- we didn't see ANY in Arizona!  :)  Maybe we could all take a road trip and letterbox in a PI free area.  Of course there are snakes and cactus and scorpions etc...
