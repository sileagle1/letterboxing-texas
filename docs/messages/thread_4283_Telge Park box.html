Telge Park box

From: iraboxer (iraboxer@yahoo.com) | Date: 2009-11-09 15:05:15 UTC
I received an email from a geocacher concerning a box he found at Telge Park. He assumed it was my box, "Box of Bones", but I'm not too sure because he describes the box as a cylinder, and I almost always use Lock 'n Lock boxes. Also, I don't believe my box had #1 written on it. He didn't say anything about a stamp. Does this sound familiar to anyone? Here is the message he sent:

"Hi, I'm not sure if this was yours or not, but while geocaching in this park, my nephew spotted what looked like a camo'd geocaching container floating in the water near the wooden bridge at the beginning of the trail. We fished it out, and opened it up... the contents were soaking wet but I was able to make out the website, letterboxing.org and on the baggie was written something about bones in magic marker (I didn't write anything down, so I don't recall if it said Box of Bones, but it did end in #1). Just in case I thought I'd contact you. We hid the container on the left side of the bridge on the creek end. It is a cylindrical container wrapped in camo tape.

Mike"

The Ghost of IRA Boxer
