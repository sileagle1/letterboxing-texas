I enjoy letterboxing and have found many boxes.  I'd like to ask, in this forum, the general  opinion and etiquite on planting. 
 
I have not planted any boxes in my experiences in letterboxing.  I have found in several instances that many boxes were planted on a vacation or have been abandon for other unexplained reasons.  It somehow does not seem fair to expect a local to tend to an abandon box, and in previous cases I have found the planter does not WANT a local tending to a box, preferring to keep it abandon or orphaned.  I can't say I understand this. 
 
As a part of a military family, we move frequently.  If I plant boxes, it seems wrong to leave them to someone else to care for or just let the box remain orphaned.  What is the prefered protocol or proper etiquite on this issue? 
 
I currently have 5 boxes ready to plant, but would like to know more about the right thing to do before planting, as we will likely move again in the spring. 

Thanks for reading this far and I look forward to hearing from anyone (regardless of expertise) on this.

FIRST – THANK YOU AND YOUR FAMILY – for protecting my freedom and making my retired military status possible.  Without you and those like you – life would be much different.

 

I do not mind looking after letterboxes left in my neighborhood.  Were it not for Silver Eagle leaving a single letterbox in El Paso, I would not be a letterboxer today.  PERIOD!  I check in on his now and then, and I don’t think he minds a bit.  I have gone out of my way to repair boxes when traveling.  I think we all have.

 

OTOH, while I have planted several around El Paso, I have never planted outside the area.  Well, not more than 100 miles or so anyway.  For the last two years, I have not had the time for letterboxing.  If I can’t maintain what I have locally, how can I justify adding more?

 

I don’t know where you are, but perhaps the boxing community of your area will adopt a few of your best (or maybe all) of your boxes when you leave.  Now might be a good time to start thinking and asking about it.

 

I am certain you will get a lot more and maybe better solutions – just wait….

From: sileagle1 (sileagle@windstream.net) | Date: 2010-11-08 16:14:08 UTC
In my opinion, as long as you are an active letterboxer and continue to update your clues with current status, your boxes are not abandoned or orphaned. I have planted many boxes all over the country (and elsewhere) and I see nothing wrong with it. If there is a problem with the box, I update the clues (e.g. full log) and the next letterboxer usually helps out. If the box is reported missing more than once, I update the clues to reflect that, and if it really is missing, there is no litter to worry about.

Silver Eagle

P.S. Thanks to all you letterboxers who have helped my boxes over the years!
