Mike,

Well, I have to agree with you - to an extent - LOL.
She got a lot of grief for a long time before we
trusted her with another vehicle. However, she's 18
now and gainfully employed, owns her own car and pays
her own insurance, so can't get too worked up over
it... and unfortunately, this particular wreck was a
bad case of wrong place, wrong time - she was leaving
work to go home when she was hit by a police car going
to a call and running well over the speed limit
without his red and blues on. Hit her squarely in the
driver door - they had to cut her out of the car. We
were camping at scout camp and got "the call". Made a
fast trip home (1 hour and ten minutes down to 45
minutes) to find her on a back board and in a neck
brace. After numerous x-rays and several CT scans and
being transferred to a bigger hospital 30 miles away

had no broken bones and only a moderate concussion,
but nothing like they first thought she would have. I
plan to thank every cow I see for a long while - my
kids have thrived on milk since they were little, and
with the exception of a fluky fracture she got three
months ago when she fell out of her room, she (my
athlete, risk-taker, always on the go, never on the
sidelines kiddo) has never broken a bone, even after
this wreck!

Lucy
(who is much grayer now)
