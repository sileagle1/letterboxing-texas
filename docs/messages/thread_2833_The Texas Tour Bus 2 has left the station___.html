The Texas Tour Bus 2 has left the station...

From: Dave (DavHow@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-06-15 21:36:00 UTC
... with two new passengers. Alright anybody west of San Antonio, or
anyone wanting a serious road trip.

We didn't want to make you folks drive all the way to Alpine, but we
wanted it close enough that we could check on it if no one comes to
get it for a while, so we drove it on up to the interstate (about 80
miles from here).

Kent, Tx. Kent is located in west Texas along I-10 about equal
distance from El Paso or Odessa - roughly 150 miles one-way from
either place... that's about 400 miles west of San Antonio on I-10
for any of you attendidn the meet and greet, that don't mind a day-
trip.

Just a note*** when I type in Kent, Tx on google maps, it points the
arrow to some other part of the state, so to be clear. Kent is at the
intersection of I-10, U.S. Hwy 80, State Hwy 118, & FM 2424, on the
Missouri Pacific Railroad 36 miles E of Van Horn in southeastern
Culberson County. It was founded before 1892 and was originally known
as Antelope.

Alrighty, so Clues already: Once in Kent locate the ruins of the old
schoolhouse on the south side of the interstate (the feeder roads are
2-way out here, so you can drive there easily).
