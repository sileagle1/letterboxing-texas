Trip to Alabama

From: Boots Tex (redbarn@eastex.net) | Date: 2011-08-30 20:27:26 UTC
We just got back last night from a trip to Alabama to visit relatives, do genealogy research and letterbox. Of course, letterboxing had to take a back seat to some other activies, but we managed to find a few and placed a couple. Other than a short trip to Italy 5 years ago, all our letterboxing had been confined to Texas, so other than that, we found our first out of state letterboxes and placed our first out of state. I did place one box in Louisiana that might as well be in Texas. It's near Logansport, which is almost in Texas and concerns a historic Texas figure known as "The Coward of the Alamo". The other two are in Huntsville, Alabama. I also placed a new governor box in San Augustine. We visited the Vicksburg National Military Site and the Shiloh Battlefield and found a letterbox in each (aren't they considered National Parks?). It was a fun trip and I can say that it made us appreciate Texas letterboxers. We did find at least one box in Louisiana, Mississippi and Alabama that were placed by Texans. By the way, Barefoot Lucy, we sat out on the patio at El Lobo's house yesterday in Huntsville and almost needed a jacket.

Boots Tex
