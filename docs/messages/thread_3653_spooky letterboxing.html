spooky letterboxing

From: M Duff (allyall@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-07-15 15:39:38 UTC
I was spooked out letterboxing last weekend in Ben Wheeler. We went
out to Edom to pick blueberries before the season was totally over
here and decided to find a few boxes out there as well. One of the
places we went was Bethlehem cemetery in Ben Wheeler.

Now I am totally fine in cemeteries. I go to them for genealogy stuff
all the time as well as letterboxing, but there is just something
about a cemetery/church in the middle of nowhere with waist high grass
and an abandoned feel to it that is a little spooky. Add to that the
possibility of snakes in the high grass and the big spiderwebs I
almost walked into and I got spooked out. I found one of the boxes in
the cemetery easily but when I didn't locate the other right away, I
decided to get out of there and come back in the winter (maybe). The
funny part was it was the middle of the day, I think if it had been
evening I would have been yelling and screaming and running out, if
I'd even had the courage to go in.

So that was a pretty memorable box hunt for me, and a story I thought
might be fun to share. Where were my kids who were with me? This was
box #5 so they were past done and were sitting in the nice cool a/c
truck while mom found "just one more box, it's on our way (kinda)".
Smart kids.

allyall

I was spooked out letterboxing last weekend in Ben Wheeler. We went
out to Edom to pick blueberries before the season was totally over
here and decided to find a few boxes out there as well. One of the
places we went was Bethlehem cemetery in Ben Wheeler.

Now I am totally fine in cemeteries. I go to them for genealogy stuff
all the time as well as letterboxing, but there is just something
about a cemetery/church in the middle of nowhere with waist high grass
and an abandoned feel to it that is a little spooky. Add to that the
possibility of snakes in the high grass and the big spiderwebs I
almost walked into and I got spooked out. I found one of the boxes in
the cemetery easily but when I didn't locate the other right away, I
decided to get out of there and come back in the winter (maybe). The
funny part was it was the middle of the day, I think if it had been
evening I would have been yelling and screaming and running out, if
I'd even had the courage to go in.

So that was a pretty memorable box hunt for me, and a story I thought
might be fun to share. Where were my kids who were with me? This was
box #5 so they were past done and were sitting in the nice cool a/c
truck while mom found "just one more box, it's on our way (kinda)".
Smart kids.

allyall
