From: Puddle Splasher (cjagger405@earthlink.net) | Date: 2008-05-21 21:43:27 UTC
I was thinking recently about some of the good boxes I've found over
the years, and the question came up, how far out of the way would you
travel to find a good box or a box hidden by someone who has a
reputation of hiding good boxes. I was recommending a couple of boxes
by Boots Tx and LSQ to a friend, and it occurred to me, that I would
drive a good distance to find their boxes. Their boxes always seem to
be interesting and often relevent to where they've been placed. I love
the fact that the box they hid in Farmersville, TX, was in the church
cemetery where his grandfather is buried. In fact, his grandfather was
the founder of that little church and the story is interesting and
historical. The day I found it, the wildflowers along the county roads
driving to the church were amazing, and when I went back a couple of
months later with Viewfinder, the huge pecan trees in the cemetery were
laden with pecans. I made a pecan tort for our family Christmas
gathering with the pecans I picked that day while Viewfinder was
stamping. What could be better? A beautiful spot, two great letterboxes
and stamps, a little history of the area and pecans!

So my question, how far out of the way would you travel (or have you
traveled) for a good box?

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-05-21 20:15:40 UTC-05:00
This is a really good question! 
 
Because of time constraints, I rarely get to venture far out of my home turf, so I guess I just can't quite fathom how far I would go.  But let's just say I am traveling to somewhere and there were two boxes along the way - one is right on the highway and the other one is, say, 15 or 20 miles off the highway by someone reputed to place outstanding boxes - lovely carvings, well-constructed boxes, known for their great hiding places, good clue-writing, and an experience to be had along the way.  My timeframe is such that I can get the close one and be 30 minutes early to my destination or go for the far one and be 30 minutes late, I'd very likely go for the far one and hope I could make up a little of the time on the road so I would only be 15 minutes late...
 
I probably wouldn't be the type who would go from Houston to Dallas via Austin though or leave the city to go out to, say, East Texas to grab a box that rightfully was someone else's to find first.
