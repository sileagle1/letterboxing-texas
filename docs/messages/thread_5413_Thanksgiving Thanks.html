From: (sileagle1@yahoo.com) | Date: 2014-12-01 06:37:57 UTC-08:00

Lately it seems the only time I get emails about my boxes is when there is a problem, like the box is missing or the stamp has been taken by Geocachers, and it has bummed me out to the point I wasn't sure placing any new ones was worth it.  However, in the last couple weeks I have gotten some nice emails that restored my spirit.  Blue Butterfly wrote to tell me she found my Tx Tomboy box and replaced the soaked log with an index card, saving me a long trip to do the maintenance.  Last week Mosaic Butterfly told me her and Corazon had fun finding some of my San Antonio boxes and appreciated me planting them.  Then over Thanksgiving I got an email from somebody in Arizona who had a great time finding my Disney Gone Wild box while visiting Orlando, Fl.  They went on to say "while we were stamping, a crane landed in a tree, then a 4 foot long young alligator came swimming up and we watched a cormorant dive in and come up with a sunfish speared on its beak ... thanks for the adventure!"  Needless to say this cheered me immensely and reaffirmed my reason for placing boxes, especially in new, fun places.  It also added one more thing to be thankful for during the Holiday weekend:  Letterboxers who still take the time to find boxes and share the joy with others.  Thanks to all of you who make letterboxing worth doing!
