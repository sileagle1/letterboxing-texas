From: Michael P. Olbrisch (mike2004@elp.rr.com) | Date: 2006-08-01 01:13:18 UTC
Heh heh heh....  when HARD ticks are first born, they have 6 legs.  After their first blood meal, they develop 2 more legs, for 8 total.  QUOTE:
 
Hard ticks have three distinct life stages. Larvae which emerge from the egg have six legs. After obtaining a blood meal from a vertebrate host, they molt to the nymphal stage and acquire eight legs.
 
SOFT ticks are born and forever have 6 legs.  QUOTE:
 
The life stages of soft ticks are not readily distinguishable. The first life stage to come out of the egg, a six legged larva, takes a blood meal from a host, and molts to the first nymphal stage. Unlike hard ticks, many soft ticks go through multiple nymphal stages, gradually increasing in size until the final molt to the adult stage.
 
More than you wanted to know here:
 
http://entomology.ucdavis.edu/faculty/rbkimsey/tickbio.html
 
Trivia over.
