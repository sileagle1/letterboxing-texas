Good Example of Clue Writing

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-07-11 15:10:35 UTC-07:00
There has been quite a lot of discussion on one of the
lists about good clue writing and I ran across a great
write-up last night.

TexasLuvBug and BlueButterfly have planted boxes in my
county in the last couple of weeks. That fact, and
Catpawz' amazing call to box last night has me worked
up to getting out in the heat and going boxing anyway!
You'd have to know me to know what an accomplishment
it is that I would even consider it!

Anyway, as I was reading the clues, this one struck my
fancy (well, they all did, but this one is really
nice!):

http://www.letterboxing.org/BoxView.php?boxnum=16401&boxname=A_Family_Affair

In these clues, TexasLuvBug leads us out through her
family land, the place of her personal history. Now
this would all mean very little to most of you, and it
doesn't mean much to me because my little part of the
county extended in a different direction (although
when she mentioned Mr. Walker - my heart paused
because he was a man I've known all MY life too,
although not well enough to get quarters from him
apparently!). However, as she describes different
points along the trip to the box and tells why each
spot is important, it is easy to visualize what must
have happened in those places in the past. It made me
wistful for my childhood and proud to be from Cherokee
County where we can grow up with roots that run deep
and strong. Knowing that this particularly road is a
gorgeous stretch of highway, I can't wait to go find
the box and I'm going to enjoy her imagery as I go.

At any rate, I really enjoyed just reading the clues
for this box, and I thought they were worthy of
mentioning as one style that makes good clues.

Lucy
