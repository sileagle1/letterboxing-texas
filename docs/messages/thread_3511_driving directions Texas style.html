From: the carrascos (a.carrasco@earthlink.net) | Date: 2008-04-23 09:09:55 UTC-05:00
I am not a regular reader of the big list.  This morning I began reading a thread because of Mark Pepe's clever hidden clue on his lost coin.  I noticed Silver Eagle was over there stirring up trouble over driving directions, and taking a few hits for his opinion.  So now I have something to say.  If it weren't for the precise directions on those boxes placed for us by Silver Eagle and Baby Bear so many years ago, I may never have stuck with letterboxing.  Trying to find a park in a big ol' place like Houston with 5 impatient kids in the car is no fun at all, and if I would have had to endure that for every one of those first boxes I found I might have given up.  I learned my way around this city thanks to their great directions, and I can't tell you how many times I have been lost on non letterboxing excursions and realized, "Hey!  I know where I am!  I found a box here once!"  So thanks Silver Eagle and Baby Bear for helping me find my way around.  -The directionally challenged Mother of Five

I'll echo that thought, in a D/FW sort of way!  If it weren't for the wonderful letterboxes around here - which pretty much all have had wonderful directions - it would have taken me FAR longer to learn my way around.  Until I started letterboxing, I had no idea where I was half of the time and I had been limiting myself to familiar destinations without branching out.  Letterboxing is such a wonderful way to get to know a place (what better way is there??) and I'm very grateful to all of the placers around here too.

Ya know, what was really getting me about all those comments is they were responding as though the lack of direction was someone being clever, and that wasn't at all what Silver Eagle was referring to (or I don't think it was). 
 
I still agree with Silver Eagle that the fun of letterboxing happens once your feet hit the ground in most cases (although I agree with Mark and Randy and all those folks who know that lack of driving directions CAN be a part of letterboxing) - not while you are driving around town trying to get your bearings in a broad way AND find a very specific place too.
 
I wondered at times during that discussion if the difference of perspective was that we are from a big state and they are all from little states.  You could cross three New England states in the distance between DFW and Houston - so maybe in Texas, inexact driving directions are a lot worse than they are in smaller states.
