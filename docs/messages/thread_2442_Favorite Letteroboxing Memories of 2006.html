Favorite Letteroboxing Memories of 2006

From: tx2chic (blhjrh@aol.com) | Date: 2007-01-02 22:11:59 UTC
Lots of good letterboxing moments this year, so here's a few:
1. Finding great letterboxes and wonderful views along Donner Pass
and around Lake Tahoe in California and NV;
2. Finally getting that elusive Rio Grande Gorge LB in New Mexico
the second try (this time without police interference!);
3. Finding Kokopelli at the Lowry Ruins at Hovenweep in Colorado,
and planting Howdy Man! at Canyon de Chelley in Arizona;
4. Taking the tiny Hickman Ferry across the mighty Mississippi River
from Kentucky to Missouri to a little bitty river town and being FF
for the Koffee Kup Kafe LB (and explaining letterboxing to the cafe
owner);
5. Letterboxing along the Natchez Trace through Tennesse and
Kentucky;
6. The Rusk Gathering last March, and getting to meet many
letterboxers I'd known previously only by stamp, logs, or emails (and
those beautiful dogwoods blooming in East Texas in the spring! And
finally finding Spring Hope, on the second try!);
7. The rapid increase in moose population around Lake Grapevine
thanks to the MMMM (which I missed, but I did get to make the Moose
Drool and met more local letterboxers, plus a few old friends);
8. The amazing explosion of new letterboxers - and new letterboxes! -
around the DFW metroplex, with so many great new boxes, clever clues,
wonderful stamps, and new places to discover right here in my own
backyard! Thanks, Metroboxers!
9. Personally: getting closer to F 400 (currently, P39, F380,
X23). Pretty sure that there are at least 20 new boxes around here
that I haven't yet looked for, so reaching 400 shouldn't be too hard.
10. Plans for 2007: Retire some of my original boxes and replace
with new series; several new Music Boxes in the works; just get out
there and letterbox! Looking forward to the gathering in March and
seeing everyone again (and sharing BL's Irish Cream).

Happy Letterboxing New Year, Everyone!
