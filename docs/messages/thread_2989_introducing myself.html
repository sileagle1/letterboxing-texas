Hey, Puddlesplasher,
 
We do feel incredibly blessed to live on this farm. It is the one I grew up on, my parents bought it in the early 60s. While only 11 acres of it are legally mine, it is almost 300 acres and there aren't any fences and it feels like one big farm. One of my brothers and his 7 kids live here as well, and my parents are still active on the farm. My brother and dad work together as farmers--they raise plants, nursery stock, pecans, and Christmas trees for the cut-your-own business. My husband (not a farmer) likes composting and gardening and is able to work from home on the computer. We are just so happy to be here. 
 
I have Americauna hens too. They can lay pink, green, or blue eggs. The fun thing is that you don't know what color they will lay, but whatever color it is, that is what they lay for life. So they don't lay a green one day and a blue the next. The Araucana chickens only lay blue...it is a very pretty light blue egg. The chickens look so similar that it is hard to tell them apart...lots of innerbreeding. They have these great ruff of feathers around their neck that make them look like beards almost. My niece (who is NOT a chicken person) was horrified that one of my hens that looked especially like Abe Lincoln was actually a GIRL. I also have Buff Orpingtons, Barred Rocks, Cochins, Wyandottes, Spitzhaubens, and Marans. I did have a great turkey until the local bobcat carried him off. I hope to have turkeys again, I LOVE listening to them. I'm thinking I may try to hatch out some in the spring. I also had guineas a few years ago, but they refused to nest in the coop and took to the trees. The owls picked them off one by one. Guineas are such strange birds...not just in looks but also in the brain dept. It is like they've all been clonked once really hard.
 
Well I'm new enough to letterboxing that it seems weird to refer to myself with my trail name. But I'll introduce my family that way. My husband hasn't thought of his yet as he hasn't gone with us yet, but my kids are Sneaky Cat, Aslan, and Stinky Monkey. Mine is Ladybird Beetlebug.
 
So speaking of letterboxing, I'd love someone on this list to point us in the right direction. I'd love to take the kids and go to some sites where I'd actually find a letterbox. Where are your favorite websites for finding directions? How do I really get started? I'm just kind of bummed that we've only had one out of three be where they were supposed to be.
 
Thanks everyone!
 
Vicki

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-08-07 22:22:15 UTC-05:00
Hey again Vicki!
 
I see by your trail names that you have at least one Chronicles of Narnia fan!  That series are my seven favorite books ever - but most especially, The Lion, The Witch, and The Wardrobe.  I wish I had kept count of how many times I've read that book both to myself and out loud, always from the only set I've ever owned, the one I got when I was about 10 years old - each of the books is marked 95 cents!  My two kids begged to hear it every couple of years or so, so that it is a family tradition to listen to Mom read LWW.  Nothing like all four of us laying on the living room floor and me holding the book over my head and starting it out - "Once there were four children whose names were Peter, Susan, Edmund, and Lucy".

My family lives in Cherokee County, which is the 3rd most box-populated county in Texas, based on listings from LBNA.  I was a little surprised to find it that close to the top, until I ran numbers a few minutes ago!  Harris County is by far the most populated with 323 listings, followed by Tarrant County with 142, then Cherokee with 85.  Cherokee County owes its high numbers in large part to JB Kokopelli's monthly holiday series - seven series in the county so far, with 5-6 boxes in each.
 
You are in a pretty good place for going in various directions for boxes.  Dallas, of course, has quite a few, spread nicely across the county.  You can go down I-45 fairly easily, where there are several pockets of boxes along the way (see sitreps by Amy and by JB Kokopelli in the past few days for reports on the I45 area).  Tyler has just gotten a fair number, and so has Athens as Lois pointed out.  And the Cedar Creek area, which Lois has been diligently planting lately.  Cherokee County with Rusk and Jacksonville both having lots of boxes is just south of Tyler.  North of I20 is pretty slim still. 
 
As far as sites go, there is www.letterboxing.org (LBNA) and www.atlasquest.com (AQ), both very good sites.  Lots of individuals have boxes on personal sites as well and if you read peoples' blogs and personal pages, you might find otherwise unlisted boxes.  I personally prefer letterboxing.org because it stays very simple, without a lot of bells and whistles - just boxes.  Atlasquest does all but wash the dishes, but it is very distracting to me and I spend way too much time getting ready to go boxing and not enough time actually boxing when I use AQ.  A lot of folks list their boxes on one or the other and a lot list their boxes on both.  LBNA is still a little bigger for the time being - 1644 listings on LBNA and 994 on AQ in Texas. 
 
Maybe we should just have a Christmas gathering at your cut-your-own place and all come get a tree and leave a box in your area!  Wouldn't that be fun?
 
BarefootLucy

Hey, Puddlesplasher,
 
We do feel incredibly blessed to live on this farm. It is the one I grew up on, my parents bought it in the early 60s. While only 11 acres of it are legally mine, it is almost 300 acres and there aren't any fences and it feels like one big farm. One of my brothers and his 7 kids live here as well, and my parents are still active on the farm. My brother and dad work together as farmers--they raise plants, nursery stock, pecans, and Christmas trees for the cut-your-own business. My husband (not a farmer) likes composting and gardening and is able to work from home on the computer. We are just so happy to be here. 
 
I have Americauna hens too. They can lay pink, green, or blue eggs. The fun thing is that you don't know what color they will lay, but whatever color it is, that is what they lay for life. So they don't lay a green one day and a blue the next. The Araucana chickens only lay blue...it is a very pretty light blue egg. The chickens look so similar that it is hard to tell them apart...lots of innerbreeding. They have these great ruff of feathers around their neck that make them look like beards almost. My niece (who is NOT a chicken person) was horrified that one of my hens that looked especially like Abe Lincoln was actually a GIRL. I also have Buff Orpingtons, Barred Rocks, Cochins, Wyandottes, Spitzhaubens, and Marans. I did have a great turkey until the local bobcat carried him off. I hope to have turkeys again, I LOVE listening to them. I'm thinking I may try to hatch out some in the spring. I also had guineas a few years ago, but they refused to nest in the coop and took to the trees. The owls picked them off one by one. Guineas are such strange birds...not just in looks but also in the brain dept. It is like they've all been clonked once really hard.
 
Well I'm new enough to letterboxing that it seems weird to refer to myself with my trail name. But I'll introduce my family that way. My husband hasn't thought of his yet as he hasn't gone with us yet, but my kids are Sneaky Cat, Aslan, and Stinky Monkey. Mine is Ladybird Beetlebug.
 
So speaking of letterboxing, I'd love someone on this list to point us in the right direction. I'd love to take the kids and go to some sites where I'd actually find a letterbox. Where are your favorite websites for finding directions? How do I really get started? I'm just kind of bummed that we've only had one out of three be where they were supposed to be.
 
Thanks everyone!
