I got cancelled. :-(

From: Mike Olbrisch (mike-2007@elp.rr.com) | Date: 2008-11-08 19:53:03 UTC-07:00

Howdy all.

The overnight hike I was going to make this weekend got cancelled. It
seems two of the hikers are doctors and were called to work in the hospital for
an emergency, and two others had a family emergency. Additionally a few others
begged off - probably their gear was not rated for freezing temps overnight. It
was 33.5F at my house at 0430 this morning. You just gotta KNOW it was in the
20s out on the mountain.

My sleeping bag is rated for -30F, but I must admit after doing that
kind of thing for 20+ years in the Army, getting out of the bag at 0-dark-30
when it's below freezing is tough. I can do it, but I was not looking forward
to THAT part of it. So now we are on for 22-23 November.

NOTE: If any of you want to come along - I will ask our guide if he has
room for more hikers on this trip. Yeah - right..... a warm day-hike -
maybe.... But a cold overnighter??? NOT! .

I weighed my pack this morning. 50 pounds. But I looked at my list and
couldn't think of a thing I didn't want to take. I could shed a pound here or
there, but I really WANT to take my handheld ham-radio up there. At 8000 feet I
can probably talk to people in Tucson and Albuquerque on 1-watt output. Looking
at the load-plan, 22 pounds is just water. So that means my entire gear is only
28 pounds. I mentioned that to the hike leader and he said his pack was about
50 pounds too. So I don't feel so bad. Moni offered to let me use her IPOD so
I could watch a movie up there. Naaaaa - rather play radio up there - I can
watch a movie any time.

Anyway - I am home. My gear is packed and ready. I did unpack some of
it, like the sleeping bag. They keep their fluff longer when stored unpacked.
