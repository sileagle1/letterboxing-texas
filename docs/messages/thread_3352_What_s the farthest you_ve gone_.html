From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-02-22 19:24:34 UTC-06:00
The furthest I've been with letterboxing as my primary motivation is Little Rock.  Shiloh had a gathering up there last year and I set my mind to go to it.  Fearless Fox took advantage of my determination and he and Lightning made a hiking weekend of it while I was at the gathering.
 
I also have a few other letterboxing trips I plan to make, come he** or high water.
 
First one - Connecticut.  My sis-in-law and I have a standing threat (promise?) to our respective husbands that one of these days we're both gonna disappear, and turn up in CT.  As long as our logbooks are gone too, they don't need to worry.
 
Another - Ohio.  Granny Butterfly keeps tempting me in that direction. 
 
And maybe MoWizLiz's Midwinter BBQ in Kentucky. 

And Stone Mountain sounds nice. 
 
And California...
 
Sheesh.  I guess I can stop since the question wasn't where do I WANT to go...otherwise, the list might go on forever.

Well, my letterboxing experiences initially started because I was looking for directions to a lake that is near where I grew up - Warner Lake near Moab, UT (but I hadn't been home in almost 20 years). When I finally found a good step-by-step instruction about how to get to that lake, it ended up actually being a LbNA clue to a box up there (KAT is the author and it is a great box!!!). So, I got my stuff together and looked for some in the Denton area to try it out.

Eventually I went on a road trip with my daughter, Pure Joy, and we letterboxed in five states on that trip -- Texas, New Mexico, Arizona, Utah, and Colorado. Overall, it was a 3000 mile trip by the time we got home 10 days later (we did hang out in Moab for a while - hours and hours of which was spent hiking and letterboxing in the Arches and Dead Horse Point parks, on the La Sal Mountains, and along the Colorado River). It was great fun.

The farthest I have been when I have found a letterbox is to Kauai, Hawaii. I have to admit my motivation was to have an aniversary trip with my wife, but the letterboxing was fun while we were there. I left "Aloha Scream" in Oahu as a matter of fact.

Happy Hunting!
