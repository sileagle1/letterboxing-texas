I would definitely recommend any of BootsTex's and Lone Star Quilter's boxes for sure. my other recommendation would be to use atlasquest's handy icons and hit blue diamond boxes and then assuming you're not planning to hit EVERY box in waco, use the icons to decide what kind of adventures you want to tackle (inside, hikes, drive-bys, etc.) i'd also recommend comparing AQ's results between a city search and a simple search specifying waco.

happy hunting!

I had a great few hours in Waco/West to spend boxing today. I was early to meet the seller for the 5 tires I was buying (how did that happen) so I stopped in to the Tx Ranger Museum and then the nearby Oakwood Cemetery. All was well with those 4 boxes. Then on to West. I had never stopped there before. Always just drove on by going to or from DFW from Austin. The food is great and West is a neat little town. The box there was wet, however. The logbook was only damp so I was able to salvage and repair a little. Great stamp! After buying my tires I headed back to Waco and visited the college campus for a couple more boxes including the current Box of the Week and another nearby. Then I was running short of time so I just drove around checking locations - Cameron Park looks nice.  I stopped in quick to the Dr Pepper Museum. That place could use a stamp! I'll have to talk to them, see if they are interested in one in the gift shop, behind the counter, maybe.  The last location I checked on my way home was a little roadside park just south of Belton. There is a commemorative marker there and three lonely picnic tables. The area has been closed for some time now and seemingly abandoned. The sign at the entrance says No Vehicles and they mean it since there are large concrete blocks blocking the entrance but that is the only warning sign and there is plenty of room to park outside and walk in. Interesting place.

PI Joe
