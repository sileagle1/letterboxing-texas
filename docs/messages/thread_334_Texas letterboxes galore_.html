*****Once it cools off just a little more, and once we
get past a mega-campout we're planning for our
scouting district, we'll be hitting the trails with
several boxes we've put together over the summer. We
tend to create in fits and starts, I've found. My
husband and son aren't too much into carving (although
they are great for ideas), so my daughter and I
usually do the carving. She and I both do our best
work when we work together, but I keep wondering how
that's going to work out when she graduates this year
and leaves for college. {{{sigh}}}
>
I had hoped to do a football mania series this
football season, but I'm getting a late start on it.
I thought to place a box in the town of each of our
out of town games, placing them before our games. I
may still do it, but I'm going to have to forget
placing them before games-too hectic. I thought it
would be cool to place one in every town we play, with
their mascot. I had considered making them a
combination of our mascot and theirs, but I think that
might be a little too tempting as possible targets for
disappearance if accidentally found - we've put up a
winning team for the past several years and they may
not care to honor the rivalry. These little East Texas
towns take their football very seriously (is there any
Texas town that doesn't???)!

I'm also needing to get some advance carving done for
some cub scout excursions that are coming up, since
our boys believe it is a requirement to plant a box
when we go somewhere (sure makes planting easier when
we can piggyback planting with another activity!). My
son's den will get their whittling chips this year, so
maybe they can even carve their own stamps and stamp
in!

Well, lots of plans, we'll see how they go!

Lucy
