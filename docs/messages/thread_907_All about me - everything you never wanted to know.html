From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-06-12 18:52:47 UTC-07:00
Since I just asked for intros from the new folks and
updates from the oldies, I thought I would go ahead
and throw in.

I've been involved in formal letterboxing for about
two years. I found it about three years ago through a
homeschooling group I was in and finally found my way
to the real game. I'm forever grateful to Silver
Eagle and BabyBear for being forward thinking and kind
enough to plant boxes in my area before there were
even any boxers here.

I started this list, but it isn't my list - it belongs
to the group. I've learned so much from the members
of this list and I look forward each day to logging on
to see what's going on with the folks here.

I live in Cherokee County and until recently was the
only boxer in this neck of the woods. I've been so
thankful that TexasLuvBug got involved! It also seems
we are getting traffic passing through these days, so
it isn't such a hopeless jaunt to check on a box
anymore - we almost always find new stamps in our
journals! YEA!

I have a daughter, ElmoLuvr, who just graduated from
high school and is itching to get out into the world.
Since we've been boxing, she's always been my carving
compadre and the official fisher-out of boxes in our
family. She's working at scout camp this summer
(she's using her carving skills to teach woodcarving!)
so I'm getting used to her not being around, but I
don't think I'm going to like it very much when she
really leaves. I asked her recently if she thought
she would keep boxing after she leaves home, and she
felt like she probably would. Guess we'll be seeing
her stamp solo sometime soon. I imagine she'll be
changing her stamp and trail name soon too.

I also have a son, formerly Wolf and now Lightning.
He's 9 years old and he has a firm grasp of
letterboxing. He's pretty good with clues, but he
won't go box-diving like my daughter does. He's had a
couple "interesting" boxing experiences (hanging by
his fingers over a small waterfall comes to mind, as
does taking an unexpected tumble down a long
embankment). He keeps on going though - my kind of
guy!

My husband doesn't have a trail name. He's not really
too much into letterboxing, although he is a big
hiker, so he never turns down a boxing trip. He's a
real trouper and never complains, so we love him for
indulging us. I do think we are inching him into a
commitment - he's been playing at a stamp lately and a
trail name will probably follow. It's probably still
gonna be awhile before he gets up in the morning and
says "Let's go letterboxing!"

I'm currently working on a family stamp based on 3 A's
and 1 M - the initials of our first (real) names.
It's gonna be cool.

We are also big into scouting. I don't get nearly as
many letterboxing outings as I would like, so we try
to combine our scouting jaunts with boxing. So far,
I've had real good luck introducing cub scouts to
boxing.

I guess that sums me up - next!

Lucy
