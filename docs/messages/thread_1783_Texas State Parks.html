Texas State Parks

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-03-26 16:32:31 UTC-08:00
As I was coming home from yet another enjoyable
weekend in one of our great state parks, I realized
I've spent the past three weekends in state parks.
Unfortunately, our parks system is beleaguered by
budget woes, which seems to be most evident at this
point through short staffing. I hate the thought that
our excellent parks system might eventually falter
facility-wise, because as many of you who have camped
out of state know, our state parks have, as a whole,
ranked highly on all points of quality for many years.

I would like to suggest that as you spend time in
state parks, you give feedback to them about your
experience. Hopefully our comments will eventually
have some influence on the funding status of state
parks.

Here is the link off the website for contacting TPWD.
There are other folks who could also be copied on such
a message, like the Comptroller's office (sorry, but
their website is down), your elected state officials,
Robert Cook, the Executive Director of TPWD, but at
the very least, bookmark the following link and use it
to leave feedback about your experience:

http://www.tpwd.state.tx.us/business/feedback/webcomment/?p=%2Findex.phtml

Lucy

I have to say that we were shocked at the fees for your state parks when we visited last November.  Much higher than anywhere we have traveled, and we travel  quite a bit.  It certainly doesn't seem that the public lands are accessible to the general public unless they are willing to pay the hefty fees.  We can afford them, but I've never been in another state that charged BY THE PERSON to enter a park.  It sounds like budget issues may be the source of the problem, but it is unfortunate that it may be limiting access to those with lower incomes.
 
Wisconsin Hiker
