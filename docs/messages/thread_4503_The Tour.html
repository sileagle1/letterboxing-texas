From: Vicki Bell (vicki@bellmotel.com) | Date: 2010-04-14 19:27:47 UTC-05:00
I know several circus who would trust me enough to plant a box with them, especially my cousins, but they have so much to worry about when moving an entire "village" from town to town, that I wouldn't expect them to want to keep up with a letterbox as well.  Moreover, circuses are notoriously secretive about their routes.  They like to surprise a town with flyers and coupons about a week before they show up.  (In the past, when one circus would learn of another's route, it would rush to play all the towns on that route a few days earlier, to "steal" all the crowds and force their competitor out of business.)  I suppose the clues to the box could read, "if you see the show in town, while you are buying your tickets, ask at the box office for the letterbox."  Then, of course, the box office personnel would change and no one would know what you were asking about!  It would be tough.  If I ever run away and join my cousin's circus, I'll carry a circus lb with me as my personal traveler, and do exchanges with any patron who asks!
-- Bell Motel
