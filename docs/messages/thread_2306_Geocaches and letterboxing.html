Geocaches and letterboxing

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-11-10 09:03:03 UTC-06:00
Viewfinder reported yesterday on the metroboxers list that someone put
a geocache literally right on top of her letterbox. It turned out to
have been done by a very new geocacher who had found 4 caches and
placed 1. Likely had no idea about anything like letterboxing!

It made me start thinking - we could easily do the same thing, and it
is no less rude or irresponsible for us to do it to them than for them
to do it to us.

If you haven't already, take a minute to sign up for an account at
www.geocaching.com and learn a bit about how to use the site. An
account there is free (like AQ, if you want premium services you can
buy a membership, but a basic free membership gets you a good bit of
information) and allows you to send communication to GC placers if you
ever need to.

If you go to Hide and Seek Cache (left hand column) and click, and
then scroll down, you can see a keyword search where you can enter
various keywords that might help you see what caches are in a
particular place. You can also do a Zip Code search. Those are
probably the easiest searches for someone without a GPS to do on the
website.

Judging from the sound of the cache description, this guy is not some
lamebrain, more likely just unaware, and any of us could easily be the
same way if we don't think a little more broadly than just
letterboxing!
