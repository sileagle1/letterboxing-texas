Free workshop in Katy, Tues Aug 2nd

From: Eibhlin/Aisling (eibhlinm@gmail.com) | Date: 2005-07-09 12:31:25 UTC-05:00
Hi,

The Maud Marks branch of the Katy (Harris Co.) public library is
delighted to let us use their community room on Tuesday evening August
2nd for a two-hour workshop in carving rubber stamps and making easy
handbound logbooks. It will be a beginner-level class.

The library is on Westgreen, which is the I-10 exit between Fry Road
and Mason Road, in Katy.

We'll start at 7 p.m. if that's okay with everyone. This is a free
class, and I'll need to know the approximate number of people who plan
to attend. If there will be fewer than 10, I'll probably open this to
some of my regular art students, but I want to give letterboxers
priority.

I'll put together a supply list in the upcoming week. I'll also make
up a class outline in PDF format for you to print ahead of time, so
it'll be easy to jot down notes as reminders for when you're creating
rubber stamps and logbooks at home.

We're going to keep this very simple and lots of fun, and you should
bring your regular letterboxing supplies, too. At the end of the
evening, there will be time for everyone to exchange stamps in each
others' logbooks.

Cheerfully,
Aisling
http://www.aisling.net/

Hi,

In time, I'm sure that I'll put a tutorial online, and the workshop
handouts will be in PDF format, even before the class... if I make
time for that, anyway. (If I don't, I'll have 'em with me on the 2nd,
and throw them into a PDF later.)

My art website, http://www.aisling.net/ is pretty much wall-to-wall
art instruction and tips, and I have fantasies of spinning off a
letterboxing how-to site as well. But, I have nearly a dozen other
websites in some level of development, and I need to get them current
first.

But anyway, while this is probably the only time that I'll teach this
as a freebie, I will do my best to put the info online as well.

On the other hand, I will be teaching in late 2006 in the Austin area,
at a national arts event. If I can squeeze in a freebie class while
I'm there, I'll let everyone know. That's the only other Texas
teaching gig on my current schedule.

Thanks for asking!

Cheerfully,
Aisling (pronounced ASH-ling... it's Irish for a word meaning "dream"
or "ideal vision," with a reference to epic Irish poetry.)
