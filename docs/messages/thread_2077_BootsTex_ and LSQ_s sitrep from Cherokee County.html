BootsTex' and LSQ's sitrep from Cherokee County

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-08-14 20:50:18 UTC-05:00
I told BootsTex I would go ahead and post a couple of notable items he mentioned to me from his and LSQ's boxing today.
 
They've gotten 14 boxes today, I guess between their home and Cherokee County...this is only a partial sitrep, the highlights, or in some cases, lowlights of the day.
 
General Sherman-BootsTex in Texas State Railroad park in Rusk - really, truly missing.  He says he'll replace it, but in a little different spot probably.  What a loss - I loved that hiding spot.
 
She Rocks!-BarefootLucy - Nichols Green in Jacksonville - as I mentioned in an earlier post, it has been disturbed.  They were kind enough to put it back together, but it will be moved soon to avoid it going missing.
 
Barefoot Den Mother-Silver Eagle-Nichols Green in Jacksonville - didn't find.  The woods are very overgrown where it is and the landmarks to look for are difficult to spot, but in the course of box checking Saturday, I did find it - but I knew where to look.
Scarlett and Rhett-j b kokopelli-Dialville - entire box was soaked.  A tree limb fell on the box and forced the lid open.  Baggies were either not resealed well or may have had damage from the tree falling, and water got inside them and soaked the logbook.  ****BIG NOTE - Boots Tex and LSQ took the logbook to their hotel and will try to dry it out tonight and will return it to the box tomorrow. ****
 
While this is technically a partial sitrep I'm posting on their behalf, I would like to add that while I was box-checking Saturday, I found the following box to be missing from the place I had previously found it and knew it to belong:
 
Life is a Picnic-Nichols Green in Jacksonville.  It was nowhere to be found, although the cover material was still in the tree.  I tried mimicking PuddleSplasher's ingenius tool improvisation skills and was only successful enough to determine there was nothing under the cover material.  Perhaps I would've been more successful with a red wig.  Either that or the tree ate the box.  Or the Tree Druids stole it.  Your pick.
 
And on a final note, I hope that BootsTex has left his Italian ticks and mosquitoes at home!
--
Barefoot Lucy
