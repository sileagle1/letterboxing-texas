stealthy box hiding

From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2005-07-12 22:03:35 UTC
Okay, I stand in the park and look around: what is slightly out of
the way? What doesn't get mowed? What area won't children be
climbing around? Try to imagine it with a birthday party of 30 kids
or a busload of Japanese tourists snapping photos and think, what
might survive that onslaught?

So, for that reason, ours are often on the perimeter of parks,
between a tree and a fence, for instance. Every boxer sort of has
their favorite ways to hide--I often look for a gap between the tree
roots. Others use flowerbeds and this can work, but not if the
place is regularly landscaped and/or mulched. I can never find
boxes once they get buried in mulch, and have reported a couple
missing that weren't actually. Crepe myrtles are favorites, because
they provide a natural cup for the microbox, but sometimes they are
agressively trimmed--depends on the park.

That said, we have a couple that are smack dab in the middle of a
park, just with a little nook that we thought might work. And, we
have the record of probably picking the worst possible letterbox
location--inside a plant in a little garden near Buffalo Bayou. The
letterbox didn't last a day and I am not sure how in the world I
missed the liquor bottles and drug paraphenalia when we placed the
box. I still feel like I owe another apology to the two
letterboxers who tried to find it--it was a yucky spot to say the
least. Oh well, you learn as you go. . .

All questions are welcome, don't worry about it! I would suggest
trying to find a few more before you plant one; experience does
help. Then, everywhere you go, you'll see great hiding spots!
