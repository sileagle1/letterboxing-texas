mini-event Nov. 10 - proposal

From: Ashby (ashby@ic.org) | Date: 2012-10-19 23:52:09 UTC-07:00
Hi everyone,

I am in the middle of making plans for the annual Reunion / Open House for the Southeast Texas Council of Camp Fire, a nationwide youth organization similar to Scouts in many ways.

As part of that, I was thinking I might have a letterboxing hour or mini-gathering or info session or something like that, maybe.  the entire event is very casual and this would be too - just a time to get together with people you already know, meet a few new people, have some exchanges, maybe introduce some of the new people to the game, and just casually hang around and visit, work on some carving or boxes that need fixing up, various things like that.

the camp is a nice place for hiking, and has a small pond for fishing, very low-key kind of place (bring your own fishing equipment, though)

maybe hot dogs or something easy on the fire for supper?

there are cabins you could rent, or bring your own tent, if you wanted to spend the night, too.  probably a small fee, maybe $10, I think, to do that?

come early to join in more with the reunion/open house event (it's at lunch)

come a little later in the day to just be there for the letterboxing part (closer to mid-afternoon, ending with supper)

is there anyone out there who thinks they might be available and interested to come to this sort of thing?

it would be on Saturday November 10, in the mid-afternoon, maybe have a table set up after lunch for getting things started, you can box around the local area a bit during the morning or afternoon, and come back and forth to the camp as you want.  there is one box there already, and we could put out a couple more, too, maybe.

you are invited for lunch, too, of course.  I believe that will be potluck,  I think.

iif you think you would or might come on this day, please let me know, so I can make some final decisions soon.  It would be great to see some of you again - it's been way too long!

here's a link to the site with more info about the camp in general, too

http://campniwana.org/

Thanks!

ashby
