Great day in the sort-of-country

From: (blhjrh@aol.com) | Date: 2007-11-13 14:15:27 UTC-05:00
Last week on one of those glorious weather days, I made the trek to McKinney for a day of letterboxing with PuddleSplasher, who has really peppered that area with some great new boxes!  Here's the situation report:
 
We began our day in Farmersville, where Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter have boxes in a pretty little country cemetery filled with pecan trees (while I found and stamped the boxes, PS picked up pecans).  Then, after coffee and a shopping sidetrip in Farmersville - PS bought yarn - we made our way to Sister Grove Park on Lake Lavon, where PS's Kids' Games series of 5 boxes is planted along a very nice hiking trail.  Now, you'd think letterboxing with the planter would make it easy, even though I had all the clues and followed them myself, but we had several challenges  when a trail marker here or there had disappeared and the "trail" had been reclaimed by prairie grass, but we persevered and found them all.   
 
After  lunch in a quaint country cafe in McKinney (really good, too!), we made a tour of about 5 more little country cemeteries in the general area north of McKinney and found the other boxes she's planted.   I found the boxes and stamped in while  she made notes for editing clues where we found something had changed here and there.  We wound up in McKinney on Town Lake Trail, finding one of her boxes and one by Wolf Pack 4 that had mysteriously moved on its own to a completely different place - I just happened to spot it in a big pile of brush.  This is the same letterbox that PS had rescued and dried and returned after recent floods, and no one had stamped in since, so it's odd that it seems to be moving around on its own!  Maybe it's those BEA's or Letterboxing Coyotes or 'Boxin' 'Dillos, or maybe it's just kids.  Who knows?  Anyway, the box is still there and safe and dry.
 
On my drive back toward the west, I ended my day in the old Zion cemetery on a hilltop between Frisco, Denton, and McKinney, watching a gorgeous sunset between the branches of a large V-shaped old cedar and was doubly rewarded by finding a HH in the final letterbox of the day.  That was very nice conclusion to a lovely day in the sort-of countryside.
 
Now, I don't normally like letterboxing marathons (which might be called Throw-N-Go), in which the goal is to find as many as possible in a single day, because I really like to stop and savor the place and enjoy reading the logbooks and the adventure.  But all of these locations are in pretty close proximity and many have more than one box .  We hiked two nature trails and took time to enjoy the scenery and found 7 boxes on those trails; we visited 6 cemeteries, all small, country, historic and interesting places and took time to explore the headstones and found 9 boxes there, because some of those little cemeteries have several boxes, plus my extra reward of a HH in the final box, making a total of 17 boxes found in that day.  And we also took time out for a good lunch in a country cafe in McKinney, coffee and shopping in an antiques and yarn shop in Farmersville, and a long leisurely drive to and from home for me with the top down in the Miata to make it a perfect day.   If you want to spend a nice day finding lots of really good letterboxes (as all of these boxes certainly are) and doing some interesting exploring, hiking, and maybe even manage to pick up a few pecans too, I highly recommend this as a very good day!
