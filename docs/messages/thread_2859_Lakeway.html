From: Kirbert (PalmK@nettally.com) | Date: 2007-06-22 11:00:15 UTC-04:00
I've got three boxes in the Lakeway/Bee Cave area, all of which have
been there for some time. I get almost no logs of finders; I think
one has been found two or three times and the other two perhaps once
each. Are things just dead in that area of Texas? Or are people
finding and not logging? Are my clues hopelessly obtuse or
something?

Somebody planted a geocache only about 30 feet from one of them, and
a geocacher promptly found my box by accident. He reported it in the
log, then the geocache owner came and found it. He volunteered to
keep an eye on my box for me. There's mention of the box on the
geocache listing. I'm sure that box has been found by a great many
more geocachers than letterboxers! I have that geocache on my watch
list and regularly get reports from geocachers there.

-- Kirbert

I scanned over those clues a few days ago and they didn't seem overtly obtuse. 
 
I would really think letterboxing would go right down Austin's alley, but for some reason it hasn't caught on heavily there.  There are quite a few boxes there, but very few dedicated boxers.  It's a shame too, because the boxers that ARE there could really incubate a pocket of boxers.
 
I'd be interested to see your logbook on that one, but I think it's great the cacher's offered to watch your box.  That's how the games should both be played - with respect for the other.
