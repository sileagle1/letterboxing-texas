Fanthorp Inn letterbox

From: Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter (janet@redbarnretreat.com) | Date: 2008-03-12 21:33:00 UTC
My "Fanthorp Inn" letterbox in Grimes County has been removed from its
place in the Fanthorp family cemetery near the Inn by the park manager
because "a couple of folks were observed disturbing the grounds inside
the cemetery". The "folks" haven't yet reported finding the box, so I
don't know what their side of the story is. The box has been retired.

Boots Tex

From: Maribeth Jagger (cjagger405@earthlink.net) | Date: 2008-03-12 17:09:00 UTC-05:00
What a shame!
 
Boots, remember when you hid your governor series letterbox at Sparkman Hillcrest Cemetery in Dallas, and they gave you an escort to the governor's grave? That's also the cemetery where I later planted "The Mick" letterbox and spent practically an hour wandering around the mausoleum looking for Mickey Mantle's memorial because after your experience, I was afraid to ask where it was!
 
That is also the cemetery where our dear friend Ruby Tuesday has been laid to rest. I know she'd love the fact that there are two letterboxes planted nearby! Yesterday, Viewfinder and I wanted to pay a visit to her gravesite, but I couldn't find it. Finally we had to go to the office and ask. We were actually happy we did, because not only did we get a ride right to her grave, but we also got a tour of the other "famous" gravesites in the cemetery. Our guide was extremely nice. Anyway, it brought a smile to my face as we were riding in the van around the cemetery, remembering your very similar experience.
Puddle Splasher
