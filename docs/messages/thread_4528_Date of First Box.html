From: Ashby (ashby@ic.org) | Date: 2010-04-28 04:54:51 UTC-07:00
My first box was 12-29-05, Naturally Musical, by Lucy Locket.
Actually, we found it a few days before that, for the first time.  We checked online, and found the clue that said the box was on a trail that we walk on frequently -- apparently we had already passed by it many times, just not knowing it was there, which we frankly found hard to believe.  So we checked, just to see if this letterboxing thing we were finding out about was really true or not, and sure enough, there it was! 

So off to the dollar store we went, to get a little notebook, (the cheapest they had, since we weren't sure if we were going to even like letterboxing yet) and we stamped it up a few days later.

Now, five year later, I have a swanky bound book with a decorative cover and quality pages, and a whole bag of tools and boxes-to-be-placed in various stages of readiness, and I buy things from the thrift store for no reason other than I think "that would make a cool box!".  So I've got the letterboxing bug pretty bad, as it turns out.  :)

the only thing lacking in my letterboxing life now is free time to do it!

From: Lighthouse Luvin Momma (pyle.of.adventurers@gmail.com) | Date: 2010-04-28 17:27:34 UTC-05:00
Actually I dont remember when we found our first box because it was an accident. The kids and I were out piddling around one day and ended up at the Spring in Brushy Creek (a mile from my parents house lol) and Dolphin Darlin (12) and I were walking up the trail and found a box with a hunk of rubber with the image of the methodist church on it and a book with a lot of little pictures in it. We thought someone had lost it or maybe it was something the church had done. We put it back where we got it which isnt where it was supposed to be lol and went on and forgot it.
 
Our first real box was December 23, 2009, it was the only warm day (63) stuck between 2 cold fronts...lol. We ended up finding 8 boxes that day, Our first 3 we looked for were missing but then we struck gold.. er box lol. We found jb kokopellis Things to avoid: Plants... It was funny to hear my kids all hootin and hollerin over that find... There was no stealth involved at all lol.
 
Oddly enough we still hadnt remembered the little box in Brushy Creek till one evening just after bedtime a couple of weeks laterDolphin Darlin came running back in here trying to get me to remember and how she bet thats what that was part of the Brushy Creek Series by chancelierdelbu! Well we got on AQ and looked it up and sure enough it was, Wasnt long we were planning another trip with our friends to go get more boxes...lol We are hopelessly addicted now. Some of the kids have started carving stamps this past week so I have a feeling I need to order carving material in bulk...lol
