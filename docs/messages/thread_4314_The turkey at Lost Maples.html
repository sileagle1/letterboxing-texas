The turkey at Lost Maples

From: iraboxer (iraboxer@yahoo.com) | Date: 2009-12-07 00:21:04 UTC
It has come to my attention that a certain young bear has placed a letterbox at Lost Maples SNA called, of all things, I.R.A. Turkey. Appropriately, it was placed on Thanksgiving day. After reading the libelous story outlined by the young bear, I have concluded that the turkey was not lost, but merely served its purpose and the pioneer family, as all turkeys have done and will do every Thanksgiving, before and since. To blame a hard-working trapper, who happened to be my ancestor, and a respected showman, LB Bozo, who just happened to be traveling through with his world-famous circus at the time, is simply irresponsible. I think the only true statement that the little bear made was the title of his letterbox. Undoubtedly a Freudian slip.

The Ghost of IRA Boxer
