assume that you might have dropped out of the game if several boxes
in widely spread out areas go missing and you're not able to run
right out and replace them.

*****This is one of my concerns too, Batty. And throw Silver Eagle
and Baby Bear in that mix - there are many, many of their boxes they
can't get back to soon or readily!

>
> Yes, thankfully, both LSQ and Boots Tex respond immediately to
email, but what if there was a family emergency and they weren't
available? What if they were on one of their long trips and didn't
have access to email?

******I don't think you could declare someone "out of the game" in
less than 3 months, more practically six months. And frankly, if
LSQ and BT, or SE and BB, or anyone else had to go that long without
contact, I think we would have to consider the possibility that they
ARE out of the game, at least temporarily. I know I was in what
could have been that situation last year - I had a family member who
was very ill and I was burning the candle at both ends managing to
work, take care of my family, and tend to her. I was off the list
and out of letterboxing for about six weeks, AND it happened
suddenly enough that I didn't have an opportunity to tell anyone or
plan ahead. If her health had continued to deteriorate, it would've
been longer, and y'all would have had to look at my boxes as
possibly abandoned. Of course, the option is always there for
someone to return and reclaim their boxes if they must take a
hiatus. I think declaring boxes as abandoned is not necessarily
punitive, but alerts the community that maybe they need to jump in
and take on extra duties. I know in my case, I check my own boxes
and the boxes of folks who live at a distance somewhat regularly,
but I don't check the boxes of locals. I don't check them because I
feel like the more often the box is uncovered, the greater the
likelihood is it will be at risk, and it should be the owner's
privilege to check the box. Had I realized sooner that TexasLuvBug
wasn't playing any more, I probably would've checked some of her
boxes earlier than I did and possibly could've saved some of them.
One in particular was a real shame to lose. The key, though, is not
pulling the trigger and declaring them to have quit too soon.
>


>I think the best way to declare any particular box as
actually "missing" is to have a local boxer that is in the area go
out and check on it. Not only will it go you a definite "Yes...it's
there," or "No...it's gone," answer (especially for an informal-type
list), but it will also foster goodwill and a sense of community, at
least among Texas boxers.

******While I agree with you - to a certain extent - this isn't
always practical or possible. There are still enough areas of Texas
that have very low concentrations of letterboxers that there may
well not be a local who can run check. And if the local hasn't
actually, personally found the box before, it could happen that they
are simply looking in the wrong place and they compound the missing
reports by offering a "confirmation".


>
> Now I know that this is already a good, close community...but if
we're going to actually consider declaring someone's
box "missing"...I think it's a good idea to verify by a boxer that's
already found it.

*****Ooops. You said it already. I think you are right - this is
absolutely the only way to positively confirm a box is missing if
the owner is out of the picture. I know I have folks who report a
box of mine missing all the time, and every time they report it, I
zip the few blocks to it to check up on it and find it happily in
place. I email them back and say "try again". Only after they find
it do they realize what they were doing wrong! Many of them,
however, would swear it was missing right up until they find it.

There IS a difference between declaring a box missing and declaring
it abandoned.

A box can only be declared missing if the owner says it is missing,
or in the absence of the owner, someone who has found it before says
it isn't where they found it before. I, and probably all of us,
have been guilty of insisting a box was missing because "I followed
the clues exactly and everything fit perfectly, but it wasn't
there!" - only to go back and realize there were other landmarks
that fit MORE perfectly and lo and behold - there IS the box! So of
course, it wouldn't do to rely solely on the report of a prospective
finder.

An abandoned box only means that the owner of the box is gone and
not playing any more and has left the box in the wild without anyone
to care for it. An abandoned box can function healthily for quite a
long time if it is well stocked and well-hidden when the owner goes
AWOL. Saying a box is abandoned warns that it is more at risk than
a box with parents. And I think that a box can be considered as
having parents if there is someone who checks it regularly, even if
it isn't the finder. I know I consider it a small token of my
appreciation to check on boxes in my area that were planted by folks
passing through - if it weren't for them, I would've had precious
little to find for quite a long time! And I know that many other
boxers do the same thing.

I think the confusion of terms comes about when someone can't find a
box so they email the owner and don't get a response. It's usually
only because there is a problem with the box that we find out the
owner has left the game.

I still don't see any really good answer to the problem, but I'm
really glad all of us are thinking about it.

I do believe the key is to go at it in a slow and reasoned manner
before calling anything or anyone missing or abandoned.

Nice post, Batty, and by the way, ditto your comments on LSQ and
BT. They ARE awesome!
