Hill Country Trip

From: Silver Eagle (sileagle@alltel.net) | Date: 2006-07-08 23:54:25 UTC
I just got back from our annual 4th of July trip to the
Hill Country and thought I would share some of the highlights.
Thanks to Dewberry posting her clues so fast from her trip
the weekend before I left, I was able to add a few boxes to
my list of ones to find along the way.

We started out going to Beason Park in Columbus so I could
place a box there. A few days before we left, I read that
Columbus has the oldest Independence Day celebration in TX.
This surprised me since there are older towns in TX, but I
decided it needed a box and I quickly created Old Time 4th.
There are now 2 boxes in that park. Next up was Dewberry's
Lulling In Luling, planted just 2 days before. It was a nice
stop and allowed the kids to play while I boxed. I then tried
to find Missing Sequoias in San Antonio without success, but
after emailing the owner I realize I was not looking in the
right place. There are now several I need to get in San Antonio.

After our obligatory stop at The Cider Mill in Medina for
everything apple, including awesome apple ice cream,
we arrived at our cabin in Vanderpool. It is right on the
Sabinal River and is only 1 mile from Lost Maples SP. It
is so nice to get away from the big city and enjoy the small
town life for a little while. Even the weather is nicer; hot
but not humid and no mosquitoes! For 3 days we swam in the
river, shot fireworks (legally), attended the fireworks show
in Utopia that is the biggest one I have ever seen for a small
town, and even found time to relax.

I also got to letterbox in Lost Maples while birding with
my wife. I found both the Hill Country Cowboy & Lost Steer
on a trail I had not been on before. I love this park, even
in summer when there is no colorful foliage, and it could
stand more boxes, especially on the East Trail. As extra
incentive to visit, I left a surprise in one of the boxes
for someone to find.

Too soon it was time to leave, but instead of heading home
we made our way to Austin via Fredericksburg. While passing
through Kerrville I was able to find Dewberry's Potted Cactus,
also planted just before I left. I had the kids with me and
my wife was with her parents, who had gone on to Fredericksburg
to shop. Since I had extra time, I stopped at the library in
Kerrville to get Internet access and see if any more boxes
had been placed along my route. The place was crowded, but
I saw a terminal with nobody at it, so I sat down and started
searching. I saw that Dewberry had placed 2 more boxes, but
they were Mystery with no City listed. I was just about to
copy the clues when a librarian came up and asked if my name
was Frank. I said no, and she said Frank was supposed to be
on that computer and I needed to put my name on a sign up list
before I could get online. Well the list was long and I
decided to leave, debating whether to change my name to Frank.

We headed to Fredericksburg where I was supposed to meet
the rest of the family at the town square. While waiting
I noticed the old building across the street was actually
a LIBRARY! It looked so old I was not sure it would even
have computers, but I decided to give it a try anyway. So
the kids and I walked in and were treated to a great old
fashioned library with modern conveniences, and I got on a
computer with no problem and without changing my name. I
quickly copied the clues and while I waited for the family
I figured out where the boxes must be. I don't want to give
away the location of the boxes, but I was able to find both
later that day.

After leaving Fredericksburg we went to a couple of wineries.
This area has really blossomed into the "Napa of TX", and we
really enjoy visiting every chance we get. We also love getting
TX peaches which are grown in this area, but this year's peach
crop was ruined and there were none to be found. Next stop
was Pedernales SP, where I found Shrek and, after much effort,
Ziggy Gifts. This box by Baby Bear has been out there for
over a year and has been found by only 1 person before me,
and I can see why. The clues call for you to walk 210
steps down the trail, but I discovered it is more than 300.
I email my brother and I think he will fix the clues so more
people have a chance to find this great stamp.

That night we stayed with relatives in Austin, then got up
with the intention of tubing the "Horseshoe" in New Braunfels.
It was cloudy and cool with strong chance of storms, so we
elected to go canoing on Town Lake instead. We rented them
from the shack at Zilker Park, close to where my USA Flag LB
was hidden. This gave me a chance to retrieve the old box
that had been vandalized and place a new one in a different
part of the park. USA Flag 2 is hidden in a great place
I had never been to called the Austin Nature & Science Center.
Kids will love it and its free. The box is considered a
new one since it has a different stamp and location, so even
if you got the old one, go for this one, too.

I also got to find Jack And Adam's LB and San Jacinto Hall LB
before leaving Austin for the drive home. It was a great
trip and I thank all placers, especially Dewberry, for making
it even more fun. I hope everyone else had a great 4th!
