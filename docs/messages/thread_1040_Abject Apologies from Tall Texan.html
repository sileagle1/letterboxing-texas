Abject Apologies from Tall Texan

From: gheningt2003 (gheningt2003@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-07-13 10:33:47 UTC
I have been engrossed at work and just read the responses to my post of a few days
ago. I am sorry that I stepped on so many toes with my comments. They were not
intended to be harmful. Every online group has its own invisible rules and clearly I
have been offensive-for this I am sorry. As was evident from the responses, the
reasons that we letterbox are myriad and personal. Clearly, a free market exists
around the issue of the style of placement of boxes and I would not have it any other
way. That was not the point. My comments were simply a call to standard regarding
the placement of boxes with regard to what you want the finder to experience. This is
a rich area with room for much self-improvement and experimentation.

Some time back I was mortified to receive an e-mail from a family that they had made
the lengthy trip to one of my boxes and found it missing. My thoughts were not along
the line "too bad that is the risk of letterboxing", but rather "what could I have done
to better conceal the box" any not put this family in the position of being
disappointed (we have all been there). I have a customer-focus when I place a box,
that's all.

I enjoy all that you do and please keep doing it - it has been a very interesting hobby.

Tall Texan

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-07-13 05:37:26 UTC-07:00
Oh my!

I don't think you so much stepped on toes as got us
going on a topic that comes up from time to time.
Hopefully I speak for everyone else, but I at least
speak for myself, that although I disagreed with your
comments regarding the merit of high traffic area
boxes, you certainly are entitled to vent a little
when you are frustrated by a box. Just remember as
you vent that 99% of the placers are very attached to
their creations and take comments personally.

Your attitude of "What could I have done to better
conceal..." is one I think we all struggle with. I
recently got a similar message from Catpawz, who had
tried to find my one and only "pay to get to" box -
one in a zoo, only to find it wasn't there. I felt
terrible! BUT...it really is just a part of
letterboxing. However, if placers place with the
attitude of "tough luck if you can't find it", it
makes the game not so great. So I guess placers have
to be conscientious and finders have to be
philosophical.

Please go back and read several of those messages in
which folks expressed their appreciation for your
contributions to the hobby so far - you are clearly a
part of the community already.

Lucy

It's always all right to express your opinions, but remember that just because a box doesn't give :::you:::: the kind of experience ::::you:::: want, doesn't make it one that isn't just as good as any other. Several people, including myself took the time to respond with thoughtful posts, and I for one would have appreciated some indication that you spent  time reading and digesting each one of them rather than the "mass mailing" response you gave. I still think you really don't get it. But that's just me.
 
I wrote three other responses and deleted them in fear that they were too harsh. I wasn't going to say anything at all, but then I got a little angry at myself for being afraid to express *my* opinions to you, when you obviously had no compulsions about expressing some pretty harsh ones of your own.
 
I'd rather have someone like you involved in boxing, who at least gives the hobby some thought instead of someone who doesn't care, but I will always disagree with your opinions of high traffic boxes.
