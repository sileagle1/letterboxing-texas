One Stoplight Town

From: corazon78121 (connie@lavernia.net) | Date: 2007-04-13 00:14:22 UTC
Thank you to Woowooshoe for alerting me that this box wasn't to be
found earlier this week.

Today after work I had a chance to check on One Stoplight Town.
Right in front of the hiding place was a truck, trailer and two men--
planting shrubs. After driving around the Dairy Queen to summon up
my courage, I stopped. As I got out of the Jeep I recognized one of
the men as having lived across the street from my grandmother. And
worse, he recognized me and called me by name. What to do, what to
do? I told them that some friends and I had been "playing a silly
treasure hunt game" and I had left something in the area that they
were digging and would like to claim it. They said they hadn't found
anything, but when I mentioned that it was a camo-covered film
canister that had been hidden behind the lightpole, the man with the
shovel started scratching in the the dirt. About an inch down, there
it was! He picked it up, handed it to me. I thanked them, smiled
sweetly and left.

I don't think any aspects of letterboxing were compromised: One of
the men is about 70 and there was at least one beer (and probably
more) being consumed, so the details of the encounter might be a bit
hazy. Tomorrow morning at the Shell station or Busy Bear over
coffee: That crazy Connie! What's she up to now? She said she was
playing some sort of game with friends. What!?! Connie has friends?

What this all boils down to is that "One Stoplight Town" will be
unavailable until the landscaping is finished, and even then I might
have to relocate it.

Corazon

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-04-12 20:50:16 UTC-05:00
You know, Rusk didn't have a stoplight for the longest time - it only had a flashing caution light at Hwy 84 and Hwy 69.  They eventually put in a four way flashing red light, and only last year (right before the gathering in fact) did they put in a bona fide red light/green light.  It has been hilarious to watch my husband treat it like the 4-way stop it was for years, and then to hear of all the other people who have had similar experiences with suddenly discovering they have to stop and sit for awhile.  I never realized how dramatically a stoplight can affect a community!
 
Definitely a letterbox waiting to happen BT...
