found boxes and new boxes

From: janetsredbarn (janetsredbarn@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-07-15 16:35:01 UTC
Lone Star Quilter and I just got back from a letterboxing trip to
Bell, Williamson, Coryell and Hamilton Counties. We found 10 boxes
and a hitchhiker and we placed five new boxes. The boxes we found
were "State Dance", "Batty Bat", "State Flying Mammal" and "Texas
Passport Series #5 - Florence", all in Williamson County, "Dana
Peak", "Play Ball" and the "Peace" and "Friendship" series, all in
Bell County, and "Double Feature" and "Neff Bat", both in Coryell
County. In Williamson County, LSQ placed "Square Dance - Quilt
Block Series" and I placed "To Hell and Back" and "Three Legged
Willie". We also placed the first two boxes in Hamilton County:
my "Brushy Bill aka Billy the Kid" and LSQ's "Crazy Nine Patch -
Quilt Block Series". None of our new boxes are posted yet.
Hopefully, I'll that done later on today. Probably the toughest
hike we've yet taken to find letterboxes was the Peace and
Friendship boxes at Miller Springs Nature Center in Belton. The
hike took us a little over two hours, down into ravines and back
out, down into a rocky creek bed (one notation in the logbook called
it the "Ravine of Death") and back up the other side because the
bridge was out. We saw a deer and a beautiful red fox that just sat
and looked at us. Okay, I admit it wasn't anything like Mike O.
does around El Paso, but for a couple of senior citizens, it was
challenging, but worthwhile. The box in Florence was interesting
after we finally found the cemetery (be persistent, the name of the
street in the clues is wrong). There was a young man in a
wheelchair (a paraplegic), alone in the cemetery. He was just
rolling along, apparantly practicing operating the chair. We were
having trouble finding the right tombstone (turns out the name was
wrong), so we were driving around and every time we turned a corner,
the young man was in front of us. But, we found the box and left
him guarding it for us. As a coincidence, out of the ten boxes we
found, 3 of them were bats (4 if you count "Play Ball").

Boots Tex
