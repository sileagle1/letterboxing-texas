Scouting's Jamboree on the Air is this weekend.  It's a really cool event where HAM operators set up, certain frequencies are set aside for scout air traffic, and scouts from all over the world talk to each other over the airwaves.  I've been to several of these and they are awesome.  I walk away a whole lot smarter every time I go!
 
I've had the pleasure of arranging for our local station this year and I'm reminded yet again that HAM operators are a whole lot like scout leaders, who are a whole lot like letterboxers.  All very passionate about their hobby. 
Unfortunately, in some parts of the country JOTA is not all that well publicized, so I mention it so that any of you who have scout kiddos might go out of your way to make sure to find a HAM station and sit down, grab a mic, and have a chat with someone in a distant place. 
 
Heck, if it tickles your interest and you AREN'T associated with scouting, you could probably still stop by your local setup and check it out.  I'm betting there are letterboxes close by too!
