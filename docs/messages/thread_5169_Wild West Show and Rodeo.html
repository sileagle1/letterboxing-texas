Wild West Show and Rodeo

From: Maribeth (cjagger405@earthlink.net) | Date: 2013-03-15 16:56:01 UTC-05:00
Well, this was fun! I learned some tricks and skills from the best Wild West Show & Rodeo performers out there! From sharp shooting, to rope tricks and trick riding. I didn’t dare attempt the bull riding, but really enjoyed watching the best of the best. I learned that barrel racing is not a sport for the faint of heart. As one racer explained to me: “3 barrels, 2 hearts, 1 dream”! You’ve got to love these people. But it finally had to come to and end as the show must move on. Be sure to catch them when they’re in your area!

• Next show: The Greenbelt, 8 miles east of Denton, Texas.
• From Dallas/Fort Worth, go North on I-35W to 380 (University DR) in Denton, Texas
• Turn Right (East), and go approximately 8 miles to trailhead clearly marked on North side of road.
• Park in parking lot. Register your car at the kiosk.
• Go north on the trail approximately one mile until the trail crosses under a railroad track
• Go right at the track to the second trestle and look under the SPOR for the box!

I heard that the Wild West Show, having just left McKinney, was hanging out in the Denton area camping under a railroad trestle, so I reckoned they were hoping to get on a little farther down southwest to Ft. Worth, aka Cowtown, City of Cowboys and Culture, to do a little sightseeing and visiting and enjoying the western heritage.  So I beat it up there to find the cowboys and cowgirls and invite them to stay with me a spell and let me host them while they check out some of the local western color: the National Cowgirl Hall of Fame, Will Rogers Arena (home of the Ft Worth Stock Show and Rodeo), and of course the Ft. Worth Stockyards to see the daily longhorn cattle drive on Exchange Avenue, Saturday night rodeo, Walk of Fame, and other Wild West attractions centered here.  I overheard them saying as how they are hoping to lure a famous local Wild West performer to join their show, so we’ll just see how that goes.   I’m a little concerned though at just what all they might be getting up to while they are here, since they seem to be a rowdy bunch given to ridin’, shootin’ and drinkin’ (not necessarily in that order), and who knows what else, so I’m hoping to move them right along as soon as they indicate a willingness to relocate once again, probably soon!  Keep your horses saddled and ready to go!

Re: Wild West Show and Rodeo

From: (blhjrh@aol.com) | Date: 2013-03-19 16:09:54 UTC-04:00
Those Wild West and Rodeo performers with their crazy buffalo, bulls and horses!  Try to keep a low profile with your neighbors entertaining these guys awhile!  They were eager to visit the National Cowgirl Hall of Fame, see the Russells and Remingtons at the Amon Carter Museum of Art, watch a few cutting horse shows at Will Rogers, and then head on over to the FW Stockyards for some whoopin’ and hollerin’ at the White Elephant Saloon, but pretty soon things just got out of hand when they hit Sundance Square.  Since they wanted to meet Tad Lucas, Rodeo’s First Lady, I took them out to see her at Rose Hill and while they were wandering around out there, I left them!  That’s where you can find them if you hurry, and along the way, you may want to stop and find The Cowgirls letterbox that I placed near Ms.Lucas' marker in her honor, too, so here are both sets of clues.
 
To find the Cowgirls Letterbox:  Ft. Worth, Shannon Rose Hill Cemetery,7301 E Lancaster, approx 2 mi E of Loop 820. Enter Main Cemetery entrance on East side of parking lot through main gate and continue uphill around stone wall.  Follow roads generally going to right and downhill toward East side of cemetery to cemetery road paralleling fence along Sandy Lane, then left (N) and continue towards back of cemetery along road that parallels fence.  Watch for set of short concrete steps on left going uphill, and then look for single stone marker about 4 ½  ft  tall on hill on left:  TAD LUCAS.  Park along here (If you reach Temple 23 on curb at curve in road, you’ve just passed it). After paying respects to Tad Lucas, look to right past second set of steps to find Huddleston marker. Cowgirls box is under boxwood shrub next to Herman Clyde. (NOTE: The Cowgirls letterbox is to remain in place, not travel with the Wild West Show.  I added a stamp for Ms. Lucas to the Wild West Show and Rodeo since she wanted to join the tour)

To find the Wild West Show and Rodeo:  From the Tad Lucas marker, continue to left around curve with old stone chapel up on hill ahead, toward maintenance barn. Just past Schmidt, park at the sidewalk on the right near a small lone cedar. Follow the walk N to Nguyen and turn left to end of walk at maintenance area fence.  Look under tall bushes next to fence where walk ends.
 
OR:  just head on back toward the N end of the cemetery toward the maintenance area, look for the fence, walk, bushes, and grab the WW box and go!
