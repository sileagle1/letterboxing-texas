Go Forth & Multiply

From: WagTime2007 (WagTime2007@tx.rr.com) | Date: 2009-02-04 20:06:45 UTC-06:00
It's that time of year again...almost time for JBKokopelli's and Wag Time's
annual Circle Tour spring break trip. In 2007, we circled through
Louisiana and Mississippi and back again. In 2008, we circled through the
Texas hill country. This year, after changing our minds several times,
we've settled on a tour through the Texas panhandle. Why?

Why? Because a new state map was recently uploaded and JBKokopelli
interpreted it as a challenge to populate the remaining panhandle counties
with letterboxes. We are now faced with the task of researching and
preparing at least forty boxes for this trip. Are we nuts? Absolutely...it
runs in our family. We were able to do some research last weekend and came
up with some great ideas, but we still have a long way to go.

This evening, JBK had the idea of inviting ya'll to help with this
undertaking and I agreed that it was a brilliant idea. If you would like to
prepare a box for the panhandle, we will take it along with us and plant it
during our trip. This plan will let more people be involved in the effort
to populate every Texas county without having to actually do the driving.
If you are interested, please email us off-list so we can coordinate who's
doing what/where and avoid overlap. You can then either mail your box to
one of us or bring it to TALE (as long as we get it by March 12th).

Love you all,

Wag Time

From: Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter (janet@redbarnretreat.com) | Date: 2009-02-05 19:55:34 UTC
Good for you! That's a worthwhile thing to do. LSQ and I went out
in that direction in December, although we never actually got into
the Panhandle. From our experience, you have your work cut out for
you. Most of the counties in the Panhandle, other than Potter and
Lubbock, have small popualations and miles and miles of nothing but
cotton fields and pastures and lots of sand, which moves around a lot
in March as a rule. Finding a good place to hide a box will indeed be
a challenge, but one I'm sure you are both up to. On the other hand,
the northern part of the Panhandle, the canyon lands, hold some of
the most spectacular scenery in all of Texas. Good luck and take
care.

Boots Tex
