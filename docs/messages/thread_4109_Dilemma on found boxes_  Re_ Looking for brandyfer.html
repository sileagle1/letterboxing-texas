From: beachbumtx09 (beachbumtx09@yahoo.com) | Date: 2009-05-25 16:01:29 UTC

Ok, here's my dilemma.  My daughter Pink Cookie and I went letterboxing on Sat. and found a lot of boxes that were in desperate need of maintenance.  I replaced bags on every box that we found needed them; thank goodness I had just put a new box of freezer bags in my backpack just for letterboxing because I used all most all of them.

Two of our "founds" had water in the boxes and the stamp mountings were coming apart and molding.  One log book was so waterlogged I was near crying at the loss of it for the planter.  The stamps themselves were still in good shape so I cleaned them up and removed the wood backing and wrapped them in a paper towels.  The log book that was totally soaked I didn't know what to do... leave it to mold in the box or take it home and try to save it for the planter.  So I thought what would I want someone to do if they found one that I planted in the same situation.  Well, I took it home and was able to save 15 pages of logs from the time it was planted until Jan. this year.  Some of the stamps are faint and some are still very good.  I noticed some names that I have seen on posts in the pages from 2007.  I left some blank index cards in the box for a temporary fix.

Another thing that I also noticed is that the last "finder" in April was not in any of the log books that we found  I wouldn't hope to think that they would stamp their log book and leave the boxes in the shape they were in.  Or did they just log the find falsely?

I emailed the planter brandyfern but she hasn't responded and I guess no one knows her because I didn't get a response from anyone on my previous post searching for her.

My dilemma is what do I do now with her log book pages?  I have them ready to mail to her  Any suggestions? 

Beach Bum TX & Pink Cookie
