From: janet clark (jayceeinbedford@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-11-27 00:25:04 UTC-08:00
It sounds like everyone had wonderful Thanksgiving weekends. That is great. I can live vicariously through all of you.
 
Last Monday night I was rappelling of a mountain (translation, walking down a hallway) I twisted my foot. For the first time in my career at this company, I actually had to work. So, Monday and Tuesday night I was on my feet quite a bit. On Wednesday morning my foot was hurting so bad that I couldn't take it anymore. I picked Cassie up and we headed toward the hospital.
 
On the way, we stopped for breakfast (a full child is a happy child). After breakfast we got to the hospital at about 10 am. By 1 pm I learned that my foot was sprained (what does that really mean?) and I wouldn't be able to put any weight on it for 3 days.
 
Any other time, this would not be a problem. But, my plans were to go back out to Grapevine lake and find all the Meese that were still wandering out there. And of course Thursday I was to bake a turkey, make stuffing, gravy and help Cassie bake some pumpkin pies (DH earned his LSS degree on this day, he ended up doing it all).
 
All you shoppers know where I was going to be Friday morning. Okay, so I still did the shopping thing. If you have ever had to shop on Black Friday in a wheelchair, you know it's not fun. There was nothing fun about it. First you have to find a motorized cart. This is after you had to park 3 blocks down the street and use crutches to get to the store. And everyone is nice, they all want to help. I told one lady that I appreciated your help, but what I really needed was a really strong man to carry me around. (If you've met me, you know he'd have to be really strong.) I made it to two stores and called it quits.
 
Saturday morning, I made it to Kohl's (they don't have motorized carts. They have a traditional wheelchair (I now have much more respect for the wheelchair bound than I ever did, and I had alot.) That's the only store I made it to that day.
 
I managed to buy 1 gift, where normally I would've spent my whole paycheck. See, there is a bright side to everything. Thanksgiving weekend is over and I still have money in the bank. And we all know there will be several more sales between now and Christmas.
 
Y'all have a wonderful week. I'll still be trying to mend a sore foot and see if I can spend a little bit of money in the process.
 
Janet, Blooming Flowers (who has a wonderful husband who will have a special place in heaven)

RFC, if you are in the Tuscany area or around Naples, be sure to
check for letterboxes. There was one in the entire country, but
Flash Dreamer planted two for us to find while we're here, so we
have found three so far. Also, we will be planting two before we
leave, so hopefully there will be 5 here when you come. By the way,
Barefoot Lucy, the Italian mosquitoes did show up. They are the
ones that planted the first and second boxes in Italy, and they are
really cool. Check them out: they're called "On the Rim" and "Old
World Charm" and are listed on LbNA (International).
