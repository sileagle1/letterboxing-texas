my only suggestion is to be careful and to be aware of your surroundings.  While I will not peg this area as "bad"  I will tell you that there are people in the area who will take advantage of people who are not aware of their surroundings.  I have not personally searched for boxes in that area but I have gone water skiing at the lake and found some of the "locals" to be a bit scary.  A much safer (at least in my opinion)  letterboxing location would be Mercer Arboretum..  It is within spittn' distance of Bush IAH , beautiful gardens lovely walking path that is quite long and at the end you are greeted with a beautiful tea house.  Mercer is divided by a road..there are boxes on both sides...gardens on one side playground traditional park on the other...
Just my suggestion...If I was in a strange town I would feel much safer at Mercer...OH heck I'll tell you the truth..I live in this town..and I would much rather take my kids to Mercer than Lake Houston...I was there with my Dh and 3 kids..and WE as a family that had skied here in the past...did not feel like Lake Houston was a safe place for us to go to anymore.  At the time (this was at least 2 years ago) it seemed that gang members were trying to take over the park.  And as we loaded our boat on the trailer we were literally stared down/scoped out by several young men who could only be described as gang members..we high-tailed it out of there and have no desire to go back.  There may be someone else who has had different experiences and if they have I hope they chime in..I hate to give off a bad vibe of Houston but I don't think this is the park you want to try out on your first trip to Houston.  
On the north side of town close to IAH I would try Mercer (which I know there are a lot of boxes) and Jesse H Jones park..I'll provide a link.
http://www.hcp4.net/jones/

I think Mercer will be the park you are looking for.

http://www.hcp4.net/mercer/

I Hope you have a good time...just be careful if you go to Lake H...

Carol

From: (redbarn@eastex.net) | Date: 2014-01-20 06:26:42 UTC-08:00

If you go to those four parks you might want to map them out, depending on where you start, to minimize the driving distance.  By the time you get here, we'll probably already be staying at Huntsville State Park, but I'd be glad to meet you and show you the eagle's nest.  We went out there yesterday and saw both eagles fly in and out of the nest, but it's been hit or miss.  I think they either have eggs or hatchlings and spend a lot of time down in their huge nest, so we don't always see them when we go out.  When you do, though, it's a rare and exciting experience.  On occasion, they have perched in a tree literally right overhead, so if they are there, they are very close.  Being from Wisconsin, eagle sightings may not be unusual for you, but in Texas it is.
