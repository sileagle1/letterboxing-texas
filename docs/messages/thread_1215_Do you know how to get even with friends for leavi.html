Do you know how to get even with friends for leaving their son with you at 6:30 a.m.?

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-08-20 14:46:21 UTC-07:00
I DO!

I finally got these boys (my son and our friends'
kiddo - I swapped them my husband for this kid at 6:30
a.m.) out of the house and on the trail with the
intention of at least moving Killough Monument today -
well not the whole monument, but my box that is there.

We've hiked a fair amount with our friends and when we
are with them and in a park where we know there are
boxes, they are kind enough to at least humor us and
let us box a little, and really, I think they kind of
enjoy it themselves. So when they dropped him off,
they left him with a store-bought stamp for him to use
while we were boxing. I had an extra little art book,
so I grabbed it before we left and made it into his
personal logbook.

We drove first out to Killough and the boys had a good
time climbing all over the monument while I tended to
the box. I then sat them down and told them the story
of the Killough Massacre. Somehow, boys just really
relate to that story! After the story I got them
started stamping in (my son hadn't stamped Killough
either) and then we started the other boy's logbook.
I set about finding a new home for it while they
played a bit more. I loaded them back up, and this
kid was ready to hit another box. I had already
planned to hit Love's Lookout, which I still haven't
gotten, so that was cool with me. We stopped at LL on
our way back in to town and at first they just wanted
to stamp and leave the finding to me. When it came
down to it though, they decided they wanted to look
too. So we hunted it down together and once again,
went through the whole stamping process (now I feel
for you moms who tow multiple youngers around with you
- I'm used to going with an older and a younger, but
two 9 year old boys is an --- experience!). By now,
the other boy (who decided to go by Scrappy) was gung
ho and didn't want to stop with that one. I
envisioned what was between us and my house, and
figured we could get two more. We went and they found
one of mine and one of TexasLuvBug's (Sunflower Summer
is still there and well hidden TLB). He STILL wasn't
ready to stop! I still have Spring Hope at home, so I
told him I would let him stamp that one and an as yet
unreleased stamp too. He finally decided that was
fine, as long as I told him where to find some other
ones he could get his mother to take him to. I agreed
and a deal was made.

Soooo...how do you get even with friends who leave
their kid at your house at only slightly after
daybreak? You take him and get him hooked on
letterboxing, then send him home armed with clues!
heheh....what a good friend I am!

Lucy
