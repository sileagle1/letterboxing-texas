Life is so good I almost need a twin to share it!  The new position within the company has a lot more responsibility, but I am doing exceedingly well.  It is important to note that I again have a great boss, nothing like the one that forced me to leave the power plant.  To a retired soldier, leadership is as important as the $$$$.

Our dog page : http://www.vonbonehenge.com/

Well - we have a 10 month old - she is gray and white.  The color is not acceptable in the show ring.  But structurally she is perfect.  she was the only female out of the litter, and the breeding was done with frozen semen from a champion dog who is no longer available to us.  So we had to keep her.

Her name - somewhat un-original, but still cute for a gray dog is - GRAY-CEE.

Gray-Cee's page: http://www.vonbonehenge.com/7weeks/7wksDSCN3432.JPG

The next liter was born Christmas Day.  This was a Champion mother, and the father was again frozen, from the 2005 National champion.  From this litter we got a beautiful Mantle girl, named Christmas Magic, or Maggie.  There were 7, and 4 will be competing in the show ring.

Maggie's picture:  http://www.vonbonehenge.com/newlitterpage.htm - on the far left.

My boy River is doing well.  Tomorrow we will visit an elementary school to teach proper pet care.

River's page:  http://www.vonbonehenge.com/River/River.html

Everyone else is well.  Son has a new daughter.  He has recovered from his 8 years and 3 combat tours... has a great job and a fine wife.  I couldn't be happier for him.

Daughter is doing so-so... she still doesn't get it that her life is the sum-total of her own decisions.  Still blaming the world.  <grin>.

We are going to Germany for 3 weeks to visit the in-laws.... I will be climbing in the Rhone mountains of Northern Bavaria.  Not so dangerous as the Alps.  <grin>.  I have many friends there from when I was a soldier, and I am looking forward to seeing many of them.

And to see a little bit of what I am up to:  www.qrz.com/db/kd9kc/   Still burning up electronic stuff!  <grin>.

I still read the mail here.  And I do wish you all a very safe and happy weekend.  Watch out for Jake the snake!

Hmmm...  Although I am the world’s worst at “clue” writing (I always write simple directions), I understand that letterboxing is a games of CLUES, not directions.  I have to admit that I get a huge sense of satisfaction out of realizing that I have found the beginning place of a clue that has been written as a clue, not as a direction.  I do enjoy the camaraderie of Events, where we share what clues we’ve deciphered that day with each other.  I am also a huge hypocrite, in that I would gladly take any directions to all the boxes in Memorial Park, but I wonder how planters who worded their clues as clues would feel about someone else posting directions to their boxes online.  I think I would have to check with each planter before posting directions online.  That being said, I truly appreciate that you have mapped out the boxes so we can check on them next weekend.  I’m interested in hearing what others think.  -- Bell Motel
