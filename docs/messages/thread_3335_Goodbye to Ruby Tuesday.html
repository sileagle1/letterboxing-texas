Goodbye to Ruby Tuesday

From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-02-11 12:19:32 UTC
We lost a great letterboxer last week when our friend Ruby Tuesday
(part of Celtic Lions) died from ovarian cancer after a five-year fight.

She was larger than life, a woman who commanded attention, sassy and
witty, but with a kind heart. She'd do anything for her friends.

Here's a link to her obituary, also in print today in the Dallas
Morning News. It mentions one of her hobbies as letterboxing.

http://www.legacy.com/DallasMorningNews/Obituaries.asp?Page=LifeStory&PersonId=103178387

I will miss her more than I can say, but this last bit has been
horrid, so I wish peace on her beloved family.

We are lucky to have some pieces of her still floating around in her
letterboxes. I hope that we will all commit to maintaining these as a
group effort, so that others may be inspired by her still.

dewberry

Re: [letterboxingtexas] Goodbye to Ruby Tuesday

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-02-11 08:11:18 UTC-06:00
If you know the lyrics to Goodbye Ruby Tuesday, you know Ruby pretty well.  She WAS bigger than life itself, but more than that, wanted everyone around her to be bigger than life too.  It was a privilege to know her, and if you missed the opportunity, I encourage you to find a way to go to one of her boxes and get to know her through her boxes. 
 
Sitting here trying to think of what I will remember most about her, and I think it might have to be how she combined her respect for each and every person with an understanding that people fall and fail, and because of and in spite of those things, finding a way to believe in her fellow man.
 
She was an amazing woman.

Re: Goodbye to Ruby Tuesday

From: Puddle Splasher (cjagger405@earthlink.net) | Date: 2008-02-11 14:18:43 UTC
To me, Ruby Tuesday was the definition of a letterboxer. Clever, fun,
unique, artistic, intelligent and creative. She believed strongly in
following the established guidelines of letterboxing. Even during her
illness, she had an enormous energy for the sport, as demonstrated in
her planning and execution of the Mini Meese Meet at Lake Grapevine
in November of 2006. I already miss her terribly, but take comfort in
knowing she is somewhere now... writing new clues to another
great "Scarlet Pimpernel" which hopefully someday I can look for in
the after-life.
Puddle Splasher

Re:Goodbye to Ruby Tuesday

From: (blhjrh@aol.com) | Date: 2008-02-12 11:08:57 UTC-05:00
I think anyone who ever met her or read her clues or discovered her clever, original boxes or read her witty postings must have funny stories and memories of Ruby Tuesday.  She was the first Texas letterboxer I actually met,  a thrill for me after finding all of her local boxes beginning with Wacky Tacky in Texas: Lenin's Statue behind the Goff's hamburger joint on Lover's Lane in Dallas (no longer there).  She loved irony!  Just looking at the list of those Celtic Lions boxes is enough to inspire anyone and bring a smile or a big laugh; finding them (and CactusLikker's great stamps) is the frosting on the cake.  She really was a Great Lady in every true sense of the word. 
Goodbye, Ruby Tuesday, and thanks.

Recently, I was reading a blog in which the author said, "you can
get to know a person through their clues and boxes." This was how I
got to know Ruby Tuesday. I was fortunate to meet her, but reading
her clues told me so much about her. The places she took you was
always an adventure. I traveled around White Rock Lake finding Lost
Keys, I imagined myself on the Shawnee Trail and felt sadness as I
read about the Cherokee Nations and their struggles.

Goodbye Ruby Tuesday,
Thanks for the memories.

I am just stunned that we lost Ruby Tuesday. As I write this I am
tearing up at the loss of such a wonderful person. I only met her

site so they can see what an impression she made on all of us.
