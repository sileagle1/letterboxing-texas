From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-12-30 19:53:10 UTC-08:00
I really like getting the mail too. That's one thing
I like about AtlasQuest - it's easy to send a short
note as well as log the find.

BUT-if I've been on a longer excursion and found
several boxes, I try to do a sitrep first and if the
placer reads it, they'll get the gist of my visit to
their box. However, if I find something I need to
tell them about, like needed maintenance or if I did
some maintenance, or if it looks like it might be
missing, I email the placer privately. In any case, I
like to send a message, but it may be a few days
before I get it done!

Lucy

From: Michael P. Olbrisch (mike2004@elp.rr.com) | Date: 2006-05-18 23:19:40 UTC

Good morning,

    I wanted to thank you for the cooperation you and your staff provided in the recovery of the lost Letterbox known to us as Hueco Tanks #1.  I sent an after action report to the Texas Letterboxing community about the search.  I was very clear in stating that without this cooperation, the successful conclusion of this search would not have been possible.  I got several short replies of thanks and gratitude.  One reply, from one whom I consider the foremost Letterboxer in Texas is particularly interesting.  This is all the information I have been able to gather on this Letterbox.  This is his reply.


        Mike, great job!  You have confirmed the existence of the oldest current letterbox in TX (Hueco Tanks #1). I don't know anything else about the boxes, just what is written in the clues, since they were placed before my letterboxing days.  I was not even sure you could get to the area described by Hueco #2 since it requires some rock climbing, so that is amazing you came so close.

        That will be great if you can rehide the #1 box, even if it is outside the park.  Do you think the rangers would be receptive to other boxes being placed in that park with their approval?

        Thanks again for going above and beyond the call of duty!

Silver Eagle

    Since the suspected location of the Hueco Tanks #2 Letterbox is on the North Mountain near the large oak tree, I would like to verify  with you that this is a place open to self-guided hikes.  If this is true, I would like to return to that spot in the near future for a more extensive search.  I would be accompanied by at least one other accomplished hiker/Letterboxer, so I would not be alone on the mountain.  This way it would pose no additional load to your staff, nor would it require Ken to put in yet another day acting as a guide. Possibly our wives would come along and set up a picnic while we hike and search.  It is unlikely either of the wives would follow us all the way in to the tree.

    I realize that should this trek be possible, we will need to make reservations the usual way.  I will work on that just as soon as I get an affirmative reply from you.

    I also hope we can work together in placing Hueco Tanks #1 in a "safe" TPWD approved location.  And should recovery of Hueco Tanks #2 be realized, I hope we can also work to re-plant that one.  I am willing to take responsibility for the maintenance of these boxes.  If you have any concerns about my sincerity, any of the Franklin Mountain Rangers or Danny Lee on your staff know me well enough to answer questions.

    Once again, thank you for your assistance.  So far - mainly because of your assistance - this has been a pleasure and an adventure.  I will be waiting to hear from you.  Have a great day.

 

Mike.  KD9KC

I think new people need to understand the game a little more, research the history.  Letterboxing is defined as being "mysterious" and we are cautioned to be "stealthy" so we won't be noticed.  I started letterboxing in 2005 as well, and I was immediately welcomed.  I have been meeting many of those folks at events now for going on 10 years and I don't even know the names of most of them.  Still, I consider them friends and some of my favorite people in the world.  This coming from an old man who, by nature, isn't very sociable and who may come across as "grouchy".  Apparently, even my advice to new boxers about the evils of "attempting" to find my boxes can be interpreted as "blistering" and "mean", which was not my intention at all.  Please forgive me if you feel like I've snubbed you.  If you've been around for a few years and still don't feel like you belong, maybe you should look for a more "social" hobby.  Not trying to be mean, just suggesting.
