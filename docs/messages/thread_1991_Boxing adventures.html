Lucy, I loved your story.  Funny how that happens when you're just out innocently pursuing a nice, recreational pastime.  So far, it's happened to me 3 times, or almost.... First was that Taos-area letterbox last January -- my husband and I detoured all the way around to the Rio Grande Gorge to find it, only to discover the entire vicinity blocked off by police helicopters, cars, and way too much activity, searching for an alleged murderer around the gorge.  Abandoned that letterbox pursuit (drat!).  Then in June, while we were in Reno, there was supposed to be one near the big Reno sign that arches across the street by all the casinos downtown, but as we drove through downtown (under that sign), again, streets all blocked off with lots of police activity: a sniper had just shot at a judge and they were searching all the area - didn't stop to look around for that one, either!  And again, two days before the 4th of July, my sister (Puddle Splasher) and I were out doing a little local letterboxing and were passing the FW Botanic Gardens.  One of her boxes in there I haven't yet looked for, so why not stop?  Pulled in - police cars blocking the quick route to the box, fencing all around the area.  No problem, we'd go the long way.  Parked, started off: blocked again with signs "By order of the Fire Marshall."  Of course!  The big concerts in the park followed by fireworks - just didn't realize it was right where the letterbox is and there was, again, no way to get to it, even though she did offer to create a diversion for me to go in and get it if I promised to take a picture of her arrest.  Decided against it.  So that makes 3, count 'em, 3 close encounters with police activity while just trying to find Tupperware in the great outdoors (or city).
