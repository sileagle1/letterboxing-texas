Box Seeding Idea

From: celticlions (celticlions@comcast.net) | Date: 2005-07-29 15:11:54 UTC-05:00
I've just had a thought - and it gets even more dangerous - I want to throw
it out for comments!

Last March we collected the most wonderful, creative and clever stamps from
about 20 Texas letterboxers (including honorary Texans from LA!) and put
together a crazy Wild West CLUE game to play at the Brazos gathering.
For those of you who missed the event, SilverEagle has posted a photo of the
clue stamps display in the group photo album under the caption TLRE.

After the gathering I had thought we would offer to share the collection
with other gatherings around the country - we had one group in California
very interested in borrowing them for their Goldrush event, but sadly the
organizer had a death in the family and plans fell through. I've offered the
collection of 35 stamps to a couple other groups for their gatherings, but
the "Wild West" theme seems to throw them! ( Go figure!!!)

Sooo...on the heels of Barefoot Lucy's "barn raising" idea, I want to
suggest that we send these delightful stamps back out into the state,
specifically to "seed" barren areas. For example, Catpawz had mentioned that
she travels to Lubbock once a month or so - perhaps she'd be willing to take
3 or 4 boxes to plant?

We would bring 4 or 5 boxes to Waxahachie to plant, of course!

I still owe two out-of-state boxers, Wanda (of Wanda&Pete) and Scarab (who
inspired the whole CLUE game and suggested most of the titles) a booklet of
the WildWestClues - somehow as soon as I remember that I need to do that, I
forget again....obvious senility! I'm copying them both on this note to
pressure myself into DOING it!

Any comments, thoughts or suggestions?

Thanks!
Ruby T.
