Boxing with a hungry non-boxer in the Hill Country

From: Sandy Milfeld (pugapalooza@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2009-05-23 22:40:58 UTC-05:00
We just returned from out trip through the hill country. I don't know if it's poor etiquette to name specific boxes in describing our adventures, so I'll refrain.

I had nine boxes on my list, from Ingram to Hamilton. My mate and I agreed that I could find one box for every horseshoe he bought. (He's a strong-man, and they bend horseshoes for fun.) He bought eight. I had nine on the list.

We couldn't find the first one. That happens. Not off to a good start, but still optimistic.

I was stamping in the second box on my list, in Ingram, while DH was reading the information sign. He overheard a man say, "I think there's a geocache here...right about where that woman is rooting around in those trees." Thankfully, I looked up and saw them looking at me (I was 150 yards away) and meandered away casually. I sealed up the box seconds before a torrential downpour started.
