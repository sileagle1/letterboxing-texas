Welcome to the first newsletter of 2010! 

Last year's January was downright balmy compared to the start of this New Year.  The cold has made the wildlife downright frisky on the lake.  Must be those northern migratory fowl coming in to warm up.  Speaking of warming up, our mild Texas winters will show us some sparkling days this month.  The warmth of the sun and the life vest can make for a very comfortable kayak outing, even in winter.  As always, we appreciate readers' comments and we like to hear how we're doing down here.  Thank you - Darla, Matt, Phaedra, and Danny. 
 Stepping Out in 2010
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
Perhaps some readers didn't get to kayak during their youth;  that's certainly my story.  There's no time like 2010 to remedy that, so, in addition to our committment to offering our weekly first-timer kayaker session each Saturday, we are dedicating Saturdays to welcome the older paddlers out there.  Come on down and we'll show you the ropes and put your photos up too!   The members of the book club that paddle here in the photo on the right will be glad to share the water with you.
 
And we appreciate your responses to our customer survey.   A variety of requests asked for us to add paddle boats and sit-in kayaks to our fleet.  We agree that these are splendid choices and we'll be making additions this spring.
 
 
January's T-shirt Contest
This month's puzzler is:  What birds have built these nests under Parmer Lane Bridge?   If you look up as you paddle under Parmer, these unusual nests appear.  I haven't a clue.  Reply with your answer and the first five respondents will receive a Row Paddle t-shirt.   Birders out there, help us out and tell us what you think they are.
 
Geocaching and Letter-boxing at Brushy Creek
 ~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
One of our winners in last month's T-Shirt Contest,  Paul A.,  volunteered the following global positioning coordinates to find the geocache locations at the lake by boat:
 
Kayaking Fun - # 1 (GC1PXR5) located at N 30° 30.233 W 097° 46.820
Brush Limbo (GC1W5J7) located at N 30° 30.249 W 097° 46.541 
Hobie One Canoe Me (GC1QZ1F) located at N 30° 30.311 W 097° 46.209
Button, Button, Who's Got the Button? (GC1WPKZ) located at N 30° 30.381 W 097° 46.292   
 
Check out these cache spots and discover more about your neighborhood.  And, something new to me is called letter-boxing that is also popular, according to our manager Matt.  Tell us more if you're familiar with this. 
 
The historic railroad trestle that is to be found further up the creek, past the Sports Park, will reveal even more geocache locations known to readers.  Hopefully, none require moving those huge pink granite boulders along the creek!
