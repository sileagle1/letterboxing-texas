Hi everyone - while in Washington earlier this summer, I met up with fellow letterboxer Soto4, who gave me three stamps & logbooks to bring back to Texas to plant.  The stamps are lovely reproductions of Japanese hanafuda flower-themed playing cards.  I'd like to plant them:


(a) as a series of three in the same general location

(b) somewhere that has a Japanese theme/connection

(c) somewhere within the greater Austin area (so I can get to them for maintenance as needed)


Does anyone have any suggestions?  I was considering the Japanese Gardens at Zilker Park, but that area is so frequently maintained I am afraid the boxes would be found and discarded before too long.


If you have any ideas, please let me know. Thanks!  -- Origami Hen

From: (mjrekedal@gmail.com) | Date: 2015-09-06 11:25:33 UTC-07:00

What a lovely encounter, Origami. It's what makes letter boxing so very special. Regarding Zilker Park, several years ago AnnaBee planted a beautifully carved stamp box name Sakura in the Japanese garden area. It went missing. She recarved and replanted. It also went missing. I would encourage you to avoid the Botanical Garden. It is indeed very populated and often cleaned and plants (of the botanical variety) are redone and moved about. Plants (of the rubber medium variety) are very vulnerable there, in my opinion. 

Wish I were more helpful.
Other Austin boxer opinions?
