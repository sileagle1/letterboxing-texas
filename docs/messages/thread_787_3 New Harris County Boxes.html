From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2005-04-10 01:02:06 UTC

We hid the Backwards Box (at Mercer Arboretum), Head of Bison (Deussen
Park) and I'm Sari; I Tigress (Eisenhower Park) recently.

Eisenhower, the park where I'm Sari; I Tigress is hidden is an
undeveloped gem in northeast Harris County on Lake Houston. Lots of
letterboxing potential there. The great thing is Deussen and
Eisenhower are minutes from each other.

I put Sheldon as the nearest town for both Deussen and Eisenhower, I
think it's unincorporated Harris County. If anyone knows this area
better than I do, tell me what the closest city is!

Hope everyone enjoys them.

Will link them to AQ after the kids go to sleep.

Re: [letterboxingtexas] 3 New Harris County Boxes Actually, Dewberry, my map(s) indicates that the city of Houston sticks out a thin flagellate-like appendage of city-boundary from the vicinity of somewhere around Texaco Country Club, and projects on a vaguely north-northeastern heading, along the east side of the Sheldon Reservoir, which then encircles most of Lake Houston, including the two parks you mentioned.  So, technically, it appears the closest city is the city that the parks are actually in, which is Houston.  Otherwise, the next closest village-community-thingy appears to be Magnolia Gardens, and then Sheldon.

Two references I highly recommend:
  1. “The Roads of Texas”, a great set of maps of Texas by the Cartographic Laboratory at Texas A&M.  Should be good for finding small communities and “the road less traveled.”  Not good at all for inter-city travel, but great for just getting around Texas.  Also has a   reference for ghost towns which I plan on exploring more soon.  I got mine at Barnes and Noble, so should be readily available.
  2. A new feature of Google... mapping, including a great tool with satellite images.   http://maps.google.com.

What a great pun...  “I’m Sari; I Tigress!”  

krohm
