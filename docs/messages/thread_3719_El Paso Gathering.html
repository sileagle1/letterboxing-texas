From: Mike Olbrisch (mike-2007@elp.rr.com) | Date: 2008-08-14 05:24:13 UTC-06:00
In all seriousness - the price of gas being what it is and the distances involved - it was fun to talk.  But it would be asking a lot of you-all to have a gathering out here. 
 
Just as most of my travels are to dog shows for Moni or rifle matches for me instead of letterboxing gatherings, you got to prioritize your travel and fuel usage too.  While it sounds nice, I feel a gathering closer to the bright lights of the east will get far more participants. 
 
Remember, El Paso is closer to San Diego than San Antonio.  Midland, Albuquerque and Tucson are all 5 hours from here.
 
There are 12 letterboxes in El Paso, and 1 at the restaurant in Fabens.  The way I plant letterboxes (and geocaches) is that I place one that needs an ARMY MAN HIKE to find - AND - I try to place one that is almost wheel chair accessible along with it.  That way everyone has something.  But even my easy ones are in nice places.
 
As for camping - there is a very nice state park (where I volunteer) just 10 road miles from the house.  It is a dry camp, there is no electricity or water.  There is room for about 8 trailers/motor homes, and any number of tents.  The trailer and Tent areas are separated.
 
To anyone coming this way, I will provide info on the best way to attack the boxes, hiking maps, the best camping spot, restaurants, stores, loan you my water jugs and generator, and act as a hike guide if you ask.  If you find yourself coming through El Paso, do plan on a full day or two.  It will be worth it.
 
When my son gets home on mid-tour there are two things he will do.  One is visit with Parker for a last time.  The other is to go to Fabens and have a 2-pound T-Bone.  I will think of most of you while I am digging into my steak!
 
And we will do a few ARMY-MAN hikes too.  After all - thats what he is.

Mike - KD9KC.
El Paso, Tx.

Re: El Paso Gathering

From: Silver Eagle (sileagle@alltel.net) | Date: 2008-08-14 14:13:01 UTC
A Gathering in El Paso hosted by Mike is a great idea and I would
love to come if I get the chance. Not only are there several boxes
to find, there would most likely be quite a few new boxes added,
making El Paso an even more desired location for other letterboxers
in the future. Also, since El Paso is closer to many western states,
you could probably draw a few boxers from there, too. I know
Lionsmane and Astro D of New Mexico have come at least as far as
Central TX, so West TX would be close for them. Mike, you could join
the Southwest LB Group:
http://groups.yahoo.com/group/LetterboxingSouthwest/ and even the
Southern California LB Group:
http://groups.yahoo.com/group/letterboxing-socal/ to generate
interest after picking the place and date.

As far as date goes, late fall sounds good. In fact, since most
schools are off the whole week of Thanksgiving, a good date for the
Event might be the weekend before Thanksgiving. This would give
people the time to attend the Event and then do other things in the
area before heading back home for Thanksgiving. And there are many
fun and interesting things to see and do out that way. I highly
recommend Guadalupe Mountains NP, Carlsbad Caverns NP, Davis
Mountains SP (great stargazing at McDonald Observatory nearby)and Big
Bend NP. Also, depending on where you are coming from, you could
visit many places on the way and back. Basically, you could combine
a Letterboxing Event with a mini-vacation.

Mike, I bet if you set it up, people will come. Go for it!

Silver Eagle

HOWEVER, I will do my best to support anyone coming through.  I just don't have the time to do a lot of planning on my own.
 
Anyone coming this way - be sure to arrange a reservation to Hueco Tanks for your trip.  Sometimes they are full for several weeks.
