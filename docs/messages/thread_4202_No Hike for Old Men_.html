From: Mike Olbrisch (mike-2007@elp.rr.com) | Date: 2009-07-31 22:19:42 UTC-06:00
Well - I have faced danger before - for a purpose.  Protecting my soldiers, and as a side to that my country...  but it is flat stupid to do dangerous things just because someone who doesn't know of what they speak tries to talk you into it.
 
When they outlined their idea for the hike, I told them it could be done, other people have done it, but I thought it unnecessarily dangerous and I did not want to be a part of it. 
 
The words in the article were an exact quote, but there was more.  I did also offer them maps and advice.  In the end, they decided to follow my suggestion.  As you read, they were glad they did once we got past the rougher areas.
 
Overall, they were great guys and I had a hell of a good time guiding them.  And best of all, they liked it too.  We have formed a loose friendship and share bits of info back and forth.  Laurence will be passing through El Paso in a week, and we are planning on doing a quick lunch somewhere off the freeway.
 
Thanks for the kind words......  now if we can just get Lucy and Janet on that trail.  <grin>.
