Adventures in Massachusetts

From: Christine Borgfeld (christine@borgfeld.net) | Date: 2013-09-03 20:25:14 UTC-05:00

Last Tuesday, Bell Motel, NLW, and I flew into Boston to start our letterboxing vacation adventure.  We stayed in Andover and boxed in Harold Parker State Forest for most of 3 days.  For those who don’t know, Harold Parker is home to the annual week long Grateful Letterboxing event held in July.  Boxers from around the country send amazing stamps and this large park is packed with hundreds of boxes.  We focused on the boxes from the most recent event this summer Eyes of the World.  This featured artwork by M.C. Escher, optical illusions, and hidden images.  We hiked through beautiful forests with rocky hills covered in pine trees.  We discovered old stone quarries, ponds filled with lily pads, vernal pools, streams, stone walls, and erratics (large boulders left behind by glaciers long ago).  And of course the boxes were equally beautiful and amazing, it’s a letterboxer’s paradise. 

 

On Thursday night we met up for dinner with Mosaic Butterfly and screamin meemie who were also boxing in Harold Parker before the Live & Breathe event.  We met for dinner at the Lobster Claw restaurant where Mosaic Butterfly organized an event to meet local boxers going to Live & Breathe.  We enjoyed heaping piles of fried seafood, lobster rolls (a local favorite), and stamped ourselves silly.  We later ran into many of our new friends at the main event over the weekend. 

 

Friday we stopped to pick up some boxes on the way to Groton, MA before the main event registration at 1 pm.  We picked up a series in another beautiful park with some unique sights such as beaver lodges and a great heron rookery.  Afterward we picked up Silver Eagle’s The Game of Letterboxer Life, grab some lunch and make it to registration.   We arrived a bit later than expected since someone’s box was a 2 ½ mile round trip hike!

 

And then we finally made it to Live & Breathe!  Wow, where to start…we received a package with a beautiful event patch and a glossy printed clue book with 168 clues (assuming I counted correctly) plus puzzles and hidden clues buried in the others!  Add to that 3 series of night boxes, piles of boxes planted under cars, at campsites and cabins, yarn bombed trees with stamps, HIPS everywhere you can imagine, and tables covered with event boxes.  There were more boxes than anyone could possibly get in a single weekend!  So we checked into our cabin at the very bottom of the hill by the lake and got started.  Did I mention the floating boxes you had to wade out to stamp?

 

We completed a 16 box series on British authors and then headed to the big BBQ dinner.  And there we started meeting all the people on Atlas Quest that I never really expected to meet in person.  We met Choi and Green Tortuga (who runs Atlas Quest), Amanda from Seattle and so many others I can’t count.  And then we found Silver Eagle and Baby Bear!  The Texans now numbered 7!  Everyone was incredibly friendly and just as crazy as we are (more on that later).  After dinner some went to a campfire or stayed around the dining hall for exchanges, but NLW and I chose to go after another series of boxes in the dark with one of our cabin roomies.  We completed a Month Python series and then finally crashed for the night. 

 

Saturday was a blur of boxing with breaks for meals.  The organizers planned numerous activities for those who could resist the lure of boxing – archery lessons, rock climbing, tie dye shirt making, and several others.  We even boxed through everything, only stopping to chat with other boxers we met on the trails.  Saturday afternoon we ran into Denise Rows who spent all day Friday stuck in the airport in Alaska.  Somehow managed overnight flights and made it to the event that afternoon.   We wrapped up the evening with night boxing and called it a day after 10 miles of hiking.  Then tragedy struck.  While walking through the parking lot, NLW stepped into a hole and sprained her ankle.  One of the event organizers drove her to the volunteer physician’s assistant on call for the weekend (talk about a good idea!).  While nothing was broken, NLW was out of commission for Sunday’s boxing frenzy.  I have to say Muddy Paws was amazingly helpful and made a bad situation much better.  

 

Sunday was filled with more boxing of course while NLW found as many of the indoor boxes as possible.  Bell Motel and I boxed through the raffle Saturday afternoon, but NLW and Baby Bear both won prizes there.  Meanwhile our boxing group barely made it back in time for dinner.  And then there was the Masquerade ball… while some people continued night boxing, others dressed up as their favorite letterbox.  Our Texas “boxes” were Sterling Seagull from Murder at the Arboretum, the Renegade Marshall, a Cat of a Different Color (it fit my costume!) and Message in a Shark in a Bottle.  The costumes were just as varied and creative as you can expect.  Green Tortuga was a through hiker walking through a civil war reenactment, there were letterboxing police, and strangely enough the winner of the costume contest was wearing disturbing underwear.  Hmmmm….

 

Great fun was had by all, but sadly we had to leave.  With early flights home Monday morning the three of us drove back to Boston to crash at a hotel near the airport.  I’m sure I’ve missed many things (like trolls hiding under bridges), and no one could enjoy all the event had to offer.  Hopefully others will share their own adventures as well.  I can only hope the organizers arrange the 20th anniversary Live & Breathe so we can do it again!
