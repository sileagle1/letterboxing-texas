Well, I finally did it! I planted my first box! Thank you all so much for your help!

I ended up carving against my better judgment. It's nothing special, but now I feel like people won't be going out of their way to avoid my box because of the stigma of it being store-bought.

I decided to use spray paint instead of duct tape. Thanks Silver Eagle for recommending the Krylon Fusion-it worked well!

On the down side, I wasn't able to find any lock-n-locks at WalMart or Big Lots, so it's a cheap-o box. I will try to keep a close eye on it and change out later as needed.

Here is the clue for those of you interested: http://www.atlasquest.com/boxes/clue/?gBoxId=170426

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2011-05-05 19:06:44 UTC-05:00

Rachel,

It's good he pointed that fact out to you, but really - it's not all that uncommon for boxes to live amongst Poison Ivy; in fact, I think they have a secret symbiotic relationship. If you are a letterboxer in the eastern half of Texas, you WILL buddy up with PI at one point or another.

So - that was hardly a rookie mistake! It's good that you now know what to look for, but as much so you know what to avoid as you look for other boxes as for when you plant your own.

It almost seems appropriate that many of us should add a boilerplate warning in our box clues - something like:

"There may or may not be poison ivy, poison oak, snakes, mosquitoes, spiders, lizards, frogs, fire ants, other kinds of ants, fungus, gooey stuff, mud bogs, quicksand, or man-eating vines in the general proximity of this box. The planter suggests that if you intend to search for this box, you should dress in long sleeves, long pants, and heavy leather gloves. Additionally, you should bring Benadryl, anti-malaria tablets, specimen jars, a machete, a loud whistle, and ID books for plants, reptiles, and insects. To prepare for your outing, you should begin taking allergy medication at least one week in advance. Further equip yourself by wearing Technu, getting a fresh tetanus shot, slathering yourself in Deep Woods Off, and pre-dialing 911 into your cell phone (in case you can actually get a signal). Verifying that your doctor is indeed in your network and that your health AND life insurance are paid up are good precautionary measures to take."

So you see - when you consider ALL the various risks, PI is nothing more than an itchy petunia.

Hope this makes you feel better!
--
Barefoot Lucy
