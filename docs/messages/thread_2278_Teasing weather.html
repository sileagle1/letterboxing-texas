Teasing weather

From: The Frye's (partyof7@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-11-01 16:09:30 UTC-06:00

Yesterday we had to make a road trip down 59 to Lufkin - great we thought - Livingston Park had been calling our name as well as Lufkin.   This weather is my absolute fav so what reason could we think of not to go lb.  After blowing off the dust off of our bags, we headed out early to Livingston State Park.  On the very first box we attempted, we were so swarmed with mosquitoes, we had to run out of the woods. - we couldnt even get the box.  It was awful - it reminded me of Galveston last summer.   The mosquiotes were actually landing on our clues.  I am not sure a bee keeper mask would have kept them away.

The only safe place we could find was a picnic table by the water where the breeze kept the bugs away.  It was gorgeous and the lake has really filled up compared to last year.

We did make a stop at the History Center in Diboll.  (We have a box there but hid it when the center was closed).  The place is great for kids as they let them climb all over the train outside.  It even has a bell and a real airhorn for them to ring.  Traffic on 59 may never be the same.  I kept waiting for a crash after the # of times the kids rang them horn.  It is really loud.  Anyway neat place. 

Hopefully our next adventure that way will be more successful but at least we were able to enjoy a gorgeous day!!

Frye Team
