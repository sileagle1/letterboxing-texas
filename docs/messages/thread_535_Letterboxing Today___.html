From: Wanda (wsthm@hotmail.com) | Date: 2005-01-05 20:12:25 UTC

My daughter-in-law, Amy (Ladybug), grandson, Mathieu, and I went
letterboxing this morning. We found Calico Rose's Dancing Ducks
letterbox, but it was full of stagnant water. The ziplock bag inside
was filled with water, too. The stamp was intact, but the booklet is
ruined. I am drying it out for you now, but I think only one stamp
image can be even faintly made out. I dried out the box, and put the
stamp with clean index cards inside of a fresh ziplock bag and put
it all back in the box. I didn't have an extra box with me, but the
one it's in should keep it safe until we have a heavy rain.

After that we went on to Calliham and looked for Baby Bear's
Southern Magnolia but failed to find it. It may have been the wrong
tree, but we looked around three different ones. I hope it is still
there and someone else can find it.

We saw lots of deer and birds in the park - my favorite being the
green jay! :) And with Mathieu along we stopped at several anthills
to watch the ants working dilligently. We liked all those birdhouses
and birdfeeders along the trail too. Mathieu and I were both really
intrigued by the huge ones that I'm guessing are meant for owls. One
was down on the ground so we stopped to look at it more closely,
first making sure that no one was home. ;)

Ladybug, who just decided yesterday that she wanted to start
letterboxing, placed her first box today - on the same birding trail
as Baby Bear's letterbox. Hopefully she can post the clues this
evening. It's a nice one, commemerating our foot of snowfall over
Christmas. ...Now why didn't I think of doing that...?

We have tentative plans tomorrow to go letterboxing in Sinton.
Ladybug hasn't found the boxes there, and the ones of mine there
need checking. Or we may go to Goliad instead. Either way we plan to
go to all the nearer places soon.

If anyone wants to go with us on our forays, you are welcome. I
don't know if anyone is near enough ....Calico Rose? Corazon?...but
if you don't mind having a 2 year old along, and having to drop
everything to be home by 3:15, you are welcome to join us.

Busy Bee
