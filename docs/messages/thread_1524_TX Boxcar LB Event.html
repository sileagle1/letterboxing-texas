RE: [letterboxingtexas] TX Boxcar LB Event

From: celticlions (celticlions@comcast.net) | Date: 2005-12-15 18:47:28 UTC-06:00

I really hope that you two can come to the Boxcar gathering!

 

Six out of seven of our CelticLIons crew came last year to Brazos and we had a blast! There was an incredible lightning storm and the roof of our RV caved in because the wind lifted up the awning and smashed it into the skylight above the bathroom, and the tent outside collapsed and flooded, so the 3 CelticLions in the tent had to come inside to sleep, so CactusLicker ended up on the floor with the 2 damp dogs and a CLUE letterbox was snatched by BEAs before the game even started and we had to keep dragging Vandal away from the gators and ….wait a minute, where was I ???

Oh yeah – it was GREAT!!!

Seriously – despite the crazy mishaps, we loved every minute! And you just can’t miss being part of Texas Letterboxing mythology – gatherings are where stories such as those about Beignet-Eating Aliens are cooked up!

 

If you go out on the LbNA site, you can look up boxes placed in Needville around March of 2005 ( or in Bastrop the previous year) and see how many boxes were placed in the area for the event – as I recall there were about 16. So not everyone creates a letterbox just for the event but it does add to the fun. Rusk already has some really cool boxes in the area which we recently enjoyed hunting, and we’ll certainly plan to bring at least a couple new ones with us.

 

Personal travelers are generally used more at gatherings than anywhere else – so it might be fun for you guys to carve one/two. This is usually a totally separate stamp from your usual “signature” stamp, and most folks have a requirement for the stamp image to be obtained by another boxer. I remember answering HAWK’s question to get her stamp while we eating beignets, for instance – and SilverEagle had previously emailed me the answer to mine, so I owed him the image when we met. HAWK had done unbelievable research and knew the answers to EVERYONE’s questions by heart – not surprising if you know her!

 

Up in New England, where boxers can stumble over each other on trails (!!!) I believe people carry their travelers with them all the time – maybe you guys in Houston do that, too? But here in the Metroplex, I’ve never met another boxer unless she was kind enough to arrange to meet for coffee!!!

 

Hope regisfilia’s schedule works out-

Looking forward to meeting you,

Ruby Tuesday
