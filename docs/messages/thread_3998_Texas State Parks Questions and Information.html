You've been an example in this, Mike, and have said before that we need to get on board if we hope to stay active in state parks.  I think the lack of consistency on the part of the parks has made it more difficult to try to decide how to step, and for my part, I've just avoided planting any more boxes in state parks while I mused over it. 
 
The real sticking point for me, and for many of us I think, has been the renewal thing.  Easy enough to apply for a permit - another story when it comes to doing the renewal.  If all parks are willing to honor renewals by email, it puts a whole different face on the issue.  Heck - Tyler is apparently even willing to do the permit application by email.  He may not be so willing for new boxes, but he surely was glad to handle it by email for my existing box.  What I recognized would be a problem was if I had to talk to HIM and only him because his staff wasn't up to speed.  That would limit the times I could go and get permitted if I had to do it face-to-face.  He saw the issue there as well and was willing to be flexible enough to make it work.  I guess it's just going to be a matter of figuring out which parks are going to be jerky and avoiding them and conversely, making a point to be friendly with the parks that welcome us. 
 
What I really never saw coming was that a geocacher would create such a problem and force the issue.  Not that it couldn't have just as easily been a nutcase letterboxer - we had one a couple of years ago that was pulling boxes because she didn't like the locations and I could easily see her using the policy to justify her actions.
