These Texas travelling boxes are never listed on lbna or on Atlasquest.  The clues are only found on this listserve, which makes the Texas Wild West Show & Rodeo sort of a “word-of-mouth” box (“word-of-listserve” box?).  The best clues are often quite funny, and don’t give explicit directions, only CLUES!  Since no one has claimed the box, I’m hoping it is still where BeachBumTX left it in June.  Here is a re-cap of her clue:
 
Whoahoo!! The Wild West Show was off to down town Cow Town but got 360'd before it got there they thought it was too hot and was wishing it was Christmas already so they Crossed over to the where the old Dobkin's Pioneer Farm use to be to cool off under the shade bush for a while. Annie is testing out her guns and making noise so you better get there quick!
 
I have to admit that I don’t know how to figure out where a Farm “used to be”!  So, I’m hoping someone in the area knows!  -- Bell Motel

From: Vicki Bell (vicki@bellmotel.com) | Date: 2012-09-28 12:07:03 UTC-05:00
I suppose I should also mention that it’s more fun if the box moves around as soon as possible, at least once a month.  If you don’t know about the wonderful history of the Texas travelling boxes, here’s the scoop:  When you find the box, you let the listserve know as soon as possible.  You stamp in all the wonderful characters who have been travelling with the box, and then carve another character to add to the crew.  Then you hide the box somewhere down the road, but always in Texas.  The box never leaves Texas.  Post your CLUES on the listserve for the next lucky finder!  The last travelling box, the Texas Travelling Circus, and its predecessor, the Texas Tour Bus, are too full of characters to travel anymore, and have been retired.  They are available at the annual TALE (Texas Annual Letterboxing Event), so folks who never had a chance to find them can stamp in all the wonderful, gorgeous stamps!  Time for someone to find the Texas Wild West Show & Rodeo!  -- Bell Motel

The Wild West Show & Rodeo is on the move. I had to call in the sheriff, but finally that gang has vacated my property. They are hoping to clear out of the metroplex, but will accept pretty much any available lodging. They are available for a night-time pick-up, but you will need a flash light.

You'll find them hanging out at the little Dobkins Family Cemetery in Euless. Just barely north of Airport Freeway/183 on Hwy 360, exit for Mid-Cities, immediately slow down and move all the way to the right. Before the first green road sign, turn onto the *tiny* gravel driveway. The short drive ends at the cemetery, which is fenced in on airport property. Park anywhere.

Go to the left, over or around the dirt pile. Dodge trees, twigs, vines, and phone books as you go. Look for a new-ish stump about a foot or two tall, then look to your right toward the fence. There is a pile of trees, branches, and debris leaning on the fence near a red and blue airport property sign. Camp has been set up behind and under that pile, covered by bark and small logs.
