Waco Sitrep from July 2011

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2012-04-20 11:03:32 UTC-05:00
I'm finally catching up on my Waco trip. It did NOT go as planned, but
we still had a good time. Trail Whale wasn't quite the letterboxer he
believed himself to be (or that I had hoped he would be) - was done
with it after the first box. I hassled with him for a little while in
Holy Cross Cemetery, trying to get him back on board, but I finally
gave up and we went to the dinosaur site, had ice cream and called it
good.

We did, however, find LSQ's Angel box. It was a VERY nice first stamp
for Jonah's first box in his logbook. I wanted to make sure that one
was really special, so I knew we would first go for either one of
yours or one of hers. There was a funeral going on in the other
cemetery, so I decided we would go for hers. As it turned out, when I
found the box it was out in plain view. There was a note in the
logbook from the groundskeeper where he had stamped the box's stamp
into the box's logbook, and made a note that he had trimmed the
bushes, essentially getting rid of the box's cover. Since his note
sounded as if he was cool with it, I cleaned out a little indention in
the ground and slid the box into it, then covered it with a little
local chaff - basically grass clippings- and put a rock on top of it
(without looking back at the clues, I think she had the box covered
with the rock anyway).

I'm really bummed we didn't get to do more boxing, but I was already
thinking that I really wished Fearless Fox and d'vir were with me,
most especially d'vir, because they would have loved the historical
nature of most the boxes in Waco. We'll probably head back that way
very soon.

Following is a note I wrote to someone who was bummed she didn't feel
more like "real" letterboxer because she doesn't get to box much...I
thought you would enjoy the "whole" story behind our Waco trip.

I took Trail Whale letterboxing with me to Waco the other day. He
considers himself to be a serious letterboxer. It's been five years
since I've been to Waco to box and virtually every box I had found is
gone and all new ones are there. I did my crazy trip planning that I
do (if you've never seen one of my maps, you're missing out!), then
stepped back and made myself prioritize the boxes there to come up
with a list of about 10-12 boxes that we could manage bearing in mind
that I was taking a young child with me. Since about six of those
boxes were in two bordering cemeteries, I figured it was very do-able.
I even had the good sense to plan for one box at a place I KNEW he
would enjoy - the Waco Mammoth Site. I woke him up at 6 am and he
happily got up and got dressed and we hit the road right on time. Got
to Waco a little more than two hours later, where we hit Hobby Lobby
to get me some new markers and him a logbook of his own (my own kids
didn't have logbooks until they could manage them themselves - what
was I thinking getting Jonah a logbook???). Got water and hit the
cemeteries (figured getting a bunch of boxes all at once would be a
good way to get him in a groove and he's enjoyed
cemetery boxing before). Found the first box fairly quickly and
unbuttoned it to begin stamping. "Grammy. I'm tired." Nice. Great.
He's tired. I invited him to sit next to me while we stamped in.
"Grammy. When are we gonna be done?" Sigh. And then my foolishness hit
me - I not only have to stamp my own logbook, but his too. And he's
tired of letterboxing, right when our count was ALMOST "1". So he
starts skipping around headstones. "Grammy. What does this one say?"
So I look up and read it for him. "Grammy. What does THIS one say?"
Again, I look up and read it for him. You get the idea. This went on
well beyond my normal level of patience, until finally I snapped. "You
SAID you are tired. Said you don't want to letterbox anymore. And yet
you won't let me finish this so we can move on. Read it yourself!".
"Grammy. You know I can't read!". Sigh. Finally finished stamping in
and buttoned the box up. I stood up to move on
to the next clue (because I am a confident Grammy who was quite
certain I could engage him again and keep him going, and because our
parenting style doesn't allow us to cave in to the first hint of
manipulation by the kid). I looked down - and saw that some of the
box's logbook pages had come out of the comb binding and were laying
there on the stone (and this after I noticed that there were no more
pages in the book and so had dug through my pack to find pages to
leave behind). I sat down and opened up the box while enduring an
attack of questions about why I was starting over. Finally got it
sorted out and got the next set of clues and started working them out.
Well - they had a lot of directions that required interpretation,
which means - in my case - brainwork. Meanwhile, Trail Whale had
spread his hopping circle a little larger and wanted me to read even
more tombstones, some of them from a distance that a rabbit couldn't
read, much less me. I reined him in a bit, which meant he was restless
at having to stay so close, so his questions intensified. The
brainwork was NOT working out. And I started getting mad at the placer
for making it so darned difficult. Finally, I took a breath, continued
to drip
sweat, and thought things out. First, these would have been the sort
of clue I enjoyed if I weren't so stinking distracted, so was it the
placers fault? Only if I blamed them for not putting a warning in the
clues against bringing active, talkative 3 year olds to the box.
Second, my stubborn nature kicked in and I steeled myself for MAKING
Trail Whale letterbox with me all day long, whether he liked it or
not. And finally, I realized that would not be fun for him, but it
would also not be fun for me and why were we in Waco? To have FUN!

I zipped up my bag, buckled him into the van, and drove out of the
cemetery thinking the whole time that I could hardly be a
self-respecting letterboxer if I came to a town with some twenty boxes
available and found only one. Oh well. I went and fed him barbecue and
ice cream, took him to the Waco Mammoth Site which we both enjoyed
thoroughly (although we forgot to get the box) and we came home to
Jacksonville with our one little find of the day. It WAS a fun day
after all was said and done, just wasn't the letterboxing day I had
envisioned or planned in detail. The kicker? He was looking through
his logbook yesterday and said "Grammy. Why didn't we get more
stamps?" Eesh...I wanted to yell at him that it was all his fault,
blah blah blah. Instead I said "Well Trail Whale, I got kind of tired
of letterboxing. Didn't you?" He said? "No. I wanted to find more
letterboxes." So yeah, sometimes life and letterboxing don't mix very
well...


--
Barefoot Lucy
