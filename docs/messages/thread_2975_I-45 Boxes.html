From: Amy (aymztx@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-08-05 13:38:53 UTC
Drove down to Huntsville from Fort Worth yesterday to drop off our
kids at my sister in laws house. We hit several boxes on the way down
and several on the way back, 8 total ... took us about 12 hours to
make the drive, but had a great time! If you ever get a chance, some
of these are exceptional. Alot of good history, locations and some
amazing stamps. Here are the boxes we hit:

Lancaster:
"Pokey"

Ennis:
"Pinky & The Brain"

Corsicana:
"Pitch In!"
"The People's Path - Texas Governor Series"

Madisonville:
"Sarah's Flag"
"Fiesta de Hongos"
"Madison Quilt Block - Quilt Block Series"

Huntsville:
"Raven, Texas Governor Series"

Thanks to all the owners of these boxes for giving us such a fun
filled day.

aymztx

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-08-05 22:44:02 UTC-05:00
It sounds like you were looking on AQ if Silver Eagle's box didn't come up.  There are still quite a few Texas letterboxers who only list their boxes on letterboxing.org (LBNA), and some who only list on AQ, so don't forget to look on both sites if you want to get ALL the boxes.  It sounds like you might still have some more to find around Lake Bardwell too!
 
Don't be discouraged by Scarlet Pimpernel, it took quite a few of us several months to solve.  Keep at it! 

You mention that some of the clues required you to be a paid member of LBNA, and I'm curious which ones.  To my knowledge, LBNA doesn't require membership, paid or otherwise, to find clues.
 
There are quite a few boxes in the area you are talking about going next weekend.  You can do a county search on LBNA or a city search on AQ.

Re: I-45 Boxes

From: Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter (janet@redbarnretreat.com) | Date: 2007-08-06 14:44:16 UTC
Those two boxes were placed by Norasta, who now lives in Canada.
She only asks that you contact her through the LbNA website, which
means you would have to be a member. She'll then send you the clues
through either LbNA or Atlas Quest, so you have to have a trail
name. We found that she answered very promptly with the clues.
Very nice boxes and worth the effort of asking for clues. Baby Bear
has a box there and I don't think he's had any problems.

Boots Tex
