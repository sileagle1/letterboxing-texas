Texas Tour Bus UPDATE

From: TeamKing (teamkingus@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-11-07 01:03:20 UTC-06:00
In May 2005, the Texas Tour Bus left the plant and started it's
journey at Stephen F. Austin State Park. From May 2005 until October
2006, the bus toured the state picking up passengers. The bus is now
at max capacity and is no longer accepting any more passengers. It
will continue to tour the state and enjoy visits with any
letterboxers who missed it on active duty.

Final tally: 39 total passengers plus the bus and bus driver stamps
for a total of 41 stamps

All stamps have been entered individually under the Texas Tour Bus
box listing in AQ. Be sure to update your finds!

Thanks for making this box such a great success!!


TeamKing


P.S. - Texas Tour Bus #2 is now in production and will start its
journey by the end of the year.

From: janet clark (jayceeinbedford@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-11-07 04:26:55 UTC-08:00
Hi Anna,
 
You and Mike are up pretty early. I am about to get off work and it always surprises me to see posts at this time of day. I don't know why it surprises me, back in the day I used to be getting to work at this time. Anyways:
 
When the bus departs from the depot it will be hidden and the clues will be posted on this group. The subject line will have Texas Tour Bus in it with the city name or something to identify the area it is in.
 
A rush of people will set out to find it. If you are lucky enough to find it in it's place, you actually take it with you. You post on this group that you found the box so that others won't be looking for it. Sometimes a person will go out and find it, but they won't take it (GG ) they stamp in and leave it where it was.
 
When you get the box home, you then stamp all the stamps into your logbook and add your sig stamp to the box's logbook. Now is the time for you to carve a stamp to add to the box. You stamp your own logbook with your new stamp and in the box's logbook.
 
After this is all done, you go out and hide the box in a new spot. When you get home you post the clue to the new location. The box is not supposed to leave Texas. And for Mike's sake I'll let everyone know here that El Paso is still in Texas.
 
Hope that helps explain it. Others that have more experience, please feel free to add, rephrase and correct me.
 
Janet (who is so ready to go home to bed but has to be here at 10 am for an insurance meeting)
