The First Texas Tour - Seeding Trip

From: Baby Bear (babybearlb2002@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-07-02 18:53:27 UTC
Hopefully this will not bore every to tears, but below are some
additional side notes of the early days of Texas Letterboxing, and a
history of Silver Eagle and my (Baby Bear) first trip to seed boxes
around the state.

* My first finds were all out of state letterboxes, in Washington,
New Mexico and Colorado.
* My oldest surviving "original" letter box is "Old 300" placed
12/08/02 at Stephen F. Austin State Historical Park.
* My first Placed box was actually put in LA, "Cypress Bend" - it is
gone.
* My second box, "Texas Birth" is my oldest named box still around,
but had to be replaced once. Originally placed 11/16/02, and replace
the next year.


The First Trip - 2 days (1/30/03 - 1/31/03)

Silver Eagle and I headed out from Cypress, up I-45 to Huntsville.
We stopped at the statue for Sam Houston, and I placed "The Raven".
This box is now gone (state prisoners clean the area, and was picked
up by guards worried about drugs being left for prisoners). We then
heading to Sam Houston's home, where Silver Eagle placed "Steamboat
Sam". After quit side trip to cemetery to see Sam's grave, we
headed up to Crockett and I place "Davy Crockett" box at Davy
Crockett Springs (box recently reported still in place). On to
Weches, and Mission Tejas S.P. We each placed boxes here, "Tejas"
and "Spanish Flag". On to Alto, and Caddoan Mounds SHP. I
placed "Stone Celt", which is now gone. Next was Rusk, and Jim Hogg
SHP, where I placed "Hogg", which is still in place. On to Rusk
S.P. and Silver Eagle placed "Texas State RR" (replaced since).
Next came Palestine S.P., and I placed Texas Dogwood (gone). East
to Old Fort Parker in Mexia, and I placed "Comanche" box (which I
had to replace later, and has gone missing again). On to
Confederate Reunion Grounds SHP and we each placed boxes, "Val Verde
Cannon" (has been replaced) and "Confederate Flag". Then to Waco,
and the Texas Ranger Hall of Fame, where we each placed boxes
("Texas Ranger #1" and "Baylor Bear" - both went missing, and were
replaced in other locations). North to Mother Neff S.P. and I
placed "Horned Owl", which went missing and has been replaced
by "Horned Owl #2". On to Belton, where we finally had a chance to
find boxes. We found "4ID" at Chalk Ridge Falls as it was getting
dark (yes we had done all of this on 1/30/03).

The next morning we found "Dana's Peak", "Mama Turtle", "Baby
Turtle", and "Patriotic Pug" all at Harker Heights. Now we went to
Bend, and Colorado Bend S.P. and found the 6 boxes ("Six Flags over
Texas") and I placed "Guadelupe Bass" (gone). On to Austin, where
after wasting an hour trying to figure out the clues we finally
found "Barbed wire & the Galopagos of Texas". Then back to Houston.

Not to many placing, as at that time we did not want to put boxes
anywhere close to another one of our boxes, and we were still fairly
new at carving stamps. We then began doing weekend trips to other
areas to place boxes in parks we liked.

Our 2003 Trip along the Lewis and Clark route from Missouri to North
Dakota is were we found and placed more than ever before.

Okay, that's enough for one day. Thanks for your interest in our
journeys!
