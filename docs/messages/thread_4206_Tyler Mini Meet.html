From: Heather (heathercarnes@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2009-08-09 03:37:35 UTC
First of all, I owe a thank you to everyone. You really made the day worthwhile for me. I got fed up with missing every meet because I'm in class (I'll be missing the next couple that have been announced, too) and decided to create one that I could attend. More than once I decided I was in over my head. I originally called it a Mini Meet in hopes that 2 or 3 others would show up with me, however this morning, I packed my book in my backpack because I was secretly afraid that no one would show up. I'm so thankful that you did.

I had met a few of you before, but it was a true honor to meet everyone else. I finally got to meet Gryzzled Gryphon (My daughter grabs the logbooks first when we 'box and has asked more than once how Gryzzled always finds the boxes before we do. Alas, that will remain a mystery!). It was great to find brand new boxers at the event (Rogue Artist and The Heart of Oz who made up their trailname on the way to Tyler this morning). We also were able to meet JB Kokopelli who is just so darn prolific with planting!

I think the thing that really defines my day can be related in this story. At one point this afternoon, I realized that I had smiled so much that either my cheek muscles were fatigued from overwork or they were just cramping. Thank you to everyone. Thank you, thank you, thank you.

Heather

From: Mike Olbrisch (mike-2007@elp.rr.com) | Date: 2009-08-09 07:15:44 UTC-06:00
A suggestion for Lucy and others.
 
In this day and age, when even cell phones have GPS units built inside them, why not consider giving the GPS coordinates to the meet in the notice.  Ham operators have been doing this for their meets for years.  It takes all the trouble out of finding the place.  A simple line like this...
 
Location:  N 31.23456  W 95.56789.
 
Is all it would take.  You don't even need to drive to the site to get the coords - you can get it from Google Earth or any number of mapping programs.
 
GPS has kept me from getting lost, mapped trails for the state of Texas, and helped me avoid traffic jams in all my travels, I can't imagine being without it when driving or hiking in new areas.  It's a long story, but in short, we were at a dog show in San Marcos.  We came upon a huge traffic jam.  I reached up and punched DETOUR in the GPS, and in 5 minutes we were on the freeway, avoiding the jam that surely would have held us up 30 minutes or more.
