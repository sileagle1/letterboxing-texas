Another Lesson Learned

From: cwenter1 (cwenter1@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-03-22 23:18:19 UTC
Well, I thought I was pretty slick yesterday when I went out to find
letterboxes in Alto, Weches, Brushy Creek, and Palestine. I was so
careful to check and double check the clues on AQ. While logging my
finds together on AQ and Letterboxing.org today I found out that I
missed 7 other letterboxes that are listed on Letterboxing.org. Not
only did I miss these 7 letterboxes but 2 of them are planted in the
same park I was searching for JBK's August Series AND 1 of the
letterboxes is about 2-3 feet away from where I found JBK's box. UGH!!!

Frogkisses and Texas Pokey have told me numerous times that I also need
to check on Letterboxing.org as well. I think I've learned my lesson.

Now I get to make another trip to Weches and maybe this time I'll be
able to find Brushy Creek as it eluded me yesterday.

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-03-22 20:11:26 UTC-05:00
Yep.  You just can't use one site or the other - you have to look on both or you will certainly miss some boxes.  At the moment, LBNA has a larger repository with 1855 listings to AQ's 1334.  Bear in mind that with both sites, these are listings counts - if one listing has more than one box, it is still only counted as 1.
 
To compound the problem, it isn't as easy as to say all the boxes on AQ are also on LBNA, because they aren't!  There are a few folks who only list on one or the other.  I tend to be one of those - I will always list my boxes on LBNA, but I'll list them on AQ if I get around to it.
