Missing stamps

From: Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter (janet@redbarnretreat.com) | Date: 2008-04-07 03:12:58 UTC
Has anyone been missing stamps from their boxes? In the last few days
LSQ has had reports of the stamps missing from two of her boxes. One
report was that her box at Rusk Railroad Park called Underground
Railroad was found lying on a bench with no stamp. The finder placed
the box back where it should have been. Today we got a report from
some letterboxers from San Diego, Cal. that they found her box at Cedar
Hill State Park, but that the stamp was missing. All they found in the
box were two small shells and the logbook (geocachers?).

Boots Tex


Re: [letterboxingtexas] Missing stamps

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-04-06 23:08:53 UTC-05:00
Eesh...this really disturbs me!  Jim found my Flying Again box open and exposed recently and someone else found Spring Hope in a similar condition recently.  The stamps were in place though.  I've had some concerns of a recurrence of a past problem, but nothing to go on yet. 
 
We seem to have really good geocachers in this area - my geocacher friend, Pearle, and his caching buddy, Fire2Water, are the two most prevalent placers around here and both are well aware of letterboxing and how it works.  They are very good about posting in their clues to be respectful of nearby letterboxes and I think they've had a positive impact on area geocachers because they've demonstrated they can respect letterboxes even if they don't participate (and Fire2Water does letterbox some).  Pearle and I seem to have a knack for finding the same spots and we've had several stashes in close proximity for approaching 2 years. 

All of this is to say that it sounds like we may have an individual problem going on in this area (not taking into account the Cedar Hill box of LSQ's), whether it is a careless letterboxer or a geocacher-gone-rampant. 
 
BT, I'll look up dates on my problems and if you can look up the dates of your reports, maybe we can do a little comparison and see if we see anything jump out at us.  I'll also email Pearle and see if he knows of any problems going on in their world.

Re: [letterboxingtexas] Re: Missing stamps

From: JimGregEricRachelKeli Chancellor (chancelier_de_lbu@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-04-07 05:39:25 UTC-07:00
We checked on two of our boxes yesterday and found both lying uncovered.  While we were there we checked on a friends box and found it uncovered as well.  Much worse, his box had the baggies open, potentially leaving the logbook exposed to the weather.  I have noted this (boxes laying exposed or not hidden) on several of our recent finds. 
 
This may require a self-evaluation of our letterboxing etiquette.  Are we really re-hiding as well as we should.  If you enjoy finding letterboxes, make sure you leave them in a condition so others might enjoy them as well. 
 
Jim

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-04-07 10:25:29 UTC-05:00

Hey Deb,

 

You are right that weather and storms can be a factor in displacing boxes, and hopefully planters keep that in mind when placing a box.  That's one reason why Puddle Splasher carries a rock with her to put on top of her newly planted boxes.  It's always a good idea to consider how water will flow through a given area when placing a box, so that you can know if it is in the path of run-off.  We sure learned that lesson last Spring with the 100 year deluge! 

 

Given the type of damage sustained by my two boxes here in Jacksonville and the locations they were planted in, I don't think the recent rains were a factor (in fact, I think they were both pre-rain, now that I think about it).  I don't think the rains would have affected LSQ's Underground Railroad or JBK's Treasure at Carey Lake either.  Not sure about the others...
 
Exposure is a problem that seems to run in spells - although this spate is apparently an odd one in that there are missing pieces out of boxes.  Boxes being left exposed or not hidden well is the bane of a planter's world, and this is the one point that this list always tries to stress to new boxers - the best way for a new boxer to be considered a treasure in the letterboxing community is to rehide well and to let placers know when something needs to be done for a box (or to let them know you took care of it already).  Most newbies get that idea and graciously do everything they can to abide by that. 
 
By addressing the poor rehiding issue amongst ourselves and reminding ourselves to be diligent, we minimize the exposure issues to only being the ones caused by weather and forces of nature and sometimes, just plain bad luck.

From: Maribeth Jagger (cjagger405@earthlink.net) | Date: 2008-04-08 22:59:02 UTC-05:00
I have many more boxers who find my boxes and stamp them, than those who actually log their finds on AQ or LBNA. You just can't go by the last person who logged their find and assume they are responsible. It always amazes me when I've only had about 8 people notify me that they've found my boxes, but suddenly someone tells me the logbook is almost full (I usually put about 36 pages in my local logbooks and more in ones that I have to travel a long distance to get to). When I actually check the logbook, I find that there are many stamps from people I've never heard of before. That actually makes it fun to check those logbooks and see who all has actually visited the box. My point is, don't ever assume if you were the last person listed to find a box, that you are responsible. There were probably several people who visited it after you who just don't log their finds. Those are generally the people who are unaware of the importance of taking good care of the boxes.
Puddle Splasher
