This is one of those gut instinct situations: 99% of all boxes are placed
without asking anyone's permission.
The exceptions probably fall mostly into a few categories:
1. Libraries
2. Museums
3. Historical Sites


However, I know of boxes covertly hidden in each of the above situations,
too!
I have been trying for a few months to get permission to place a box in our
public library - they haven't turned me down, but I haven't been given the
go-ahead. I'll keep trying to get their blessing, because I think my goals
might align very nicely with theirs!

A letterboxing buddy secretly planted a box in Old City Park, a Dallas park
which houses historical buildings and artifacts. The box was found during
some repairs, and to our surprise the powers-that-be are very excited about
replacing it with an official Old City Park letterbox! The original owner of
the box has carved a new stamp that symbolizes the park logo, another boxer
has created a magnificent wooden treasure cask to hold it & I'm making the
logbook. My Metroboxing buddy Kantexan is also helping to plan possible
future additions to the project.

Last year, our family planted a box in the Perry Homestead, which is a
museum/farmhouse - it is now missing, and I think if we recarve it, we'll
try to get permission to place it inside the home.

I used to volunteer as a docent for Old City Park, and the SMU Meadows
Museum and I think most docents would be more than happy to help maintain a
letterbox, given enough communication and opportunity for input.

Good luck!
