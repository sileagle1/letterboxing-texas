From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-07-30 07:50:49 UTC-05:00
You would have to know how this park is laid out - there's the train where the majority of their traffic comes from during certain seasons, then there's a very small camping area, a very small lake, and a short (less than a mile) trail.
 
Depending on their mindset, they may be keeping the camping area as a convenience to some train riders, but it might not be the focus of their business.  If that's the case, they might not bother with maintaining the trail.  In other words, they may be buying the train and getting a camping area, a small lake, and trail in the purchase, as opposed to buying a train, a camping area, a lake, and a trail.
 
This is the park where we had the third state gathering, so there are quite a few boxes on that short trail.  There are a couple geocaches in it as well, and it is also the site of a big event that was (I think) pretty successful last summer that crossed several interests, so there may be enough folks that don't care about the train who will speak up about the rest of the park.  I know I, for one, don't particularly care about the train - done it enough times that I only do it now with guests - but I DO really like the rest of the park.
