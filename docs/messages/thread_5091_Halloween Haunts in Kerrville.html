Hey PI Joe, I’m glad to see you got some boxing done.  It’s great to hear another box at Bastrop survived the fire.  I hope we have other survivors turn up! 

The event went amazingly well.  Wry Me and I arrived Thursday and I planted some last minute temporary boxes.  Most everyone else arrived Friday afternoon and started working on the clues planted for the event.  Many of the clues had a twist to them.  There were puzzles to solve, partial clues (one written on puzzle pieces where you had to find other teams to put the puzzle together), hidden clues, bonus boxes, camouflaged  boxes, and other sneaky goings on.  Mosaic Butterfly had a wonderful series of Jack O Lantern faces – you stamped a pumpkin 10 times and all of the face stamps were planted in little pumpkin gift bags hanging on surrounding trees. 

We had a good showing for dinner Friday night and everyone enjoyed some local burgers.  Afterward we found some great carves in Wry Me’s night boxing series.  Saturday was our potluck and  the boxers came and went from the dining hall all day as they solved clues and found the boxes around the event hall.  We had stamps attached to rodents and bats, clues hidden in surprise places, and stamps in buckets for the kids (which most of the adults enjoyed also).  There were also spooky decorations to explore at the campsites (those tombstones would have been a great place for a box you know…)  Amazingly enough, most people found everything by midafternoon and had time to take a break!   Saturday night wrapped things up with trick or treat night boxing (the boxes included candy for everyone), and the last stragglers ended up around our campfire.

We had several boxers attending their first event!  topcrop came from College Station, Basketcase1 and macman from Lewisville, and lastly young Kat Miss and MoonShine joined Wry Me on this trip.

So that’s the short version (hopefully others can fill in the details!) All in all it was good crazy fun and I can’t wait to do it again next year!  I’ll get pictures posted in the next few days. 

Open Space

PS – We had over 60 boxes planted for the event (not including event stamps or boxes others planted Saturday)!  All of the temporary boxes are pulled.  There will be over a dozen left as permanent plants.  I’ll try to keep as much of the creative clues in the permanent plants as possible, but many have to be modified to be listed as on AQ and found by one person.

I was quite disappointed when I found out I had a conflict this past weekend and could not attend Open Space's event in Kerrville but I managed to get some letterboxing in anyway.

Friday I visited a portion of the Barton Creek Greenbelt I was not familiar with where Mazy Days has started a letterboxing Pirate's Lair. There are now 5 pirate themed boxes there from 4 different carvers and there has already been someone who traveled all the way from Hawaii just to visit this hotbed of letterboxing activity. They did mention something about wanting to see the Austin City Limits Festival as well, whatever that is.

Saturday was family stuff.

Today I visited Bastrop State Park to try and place a couple more Bastrop Phoenix boxes. Unfortunately, this park has a long way to go before it will be close the the letterboxing, hiking, and orienteering mecca that has been before the fire of 2011. Only about half the trails (by mileage) are open yet and they are still forbidding anyone from leaving the trails and wandering around as we used to do.

One a positive note, I did find Baby Bear's Poison Ivy Box! It is just a couple feet off of a portion of the purple-blazed trail that is open. Since it is located in a rocky area and was shielded by several rocks in a little cubby hole it survived with only a slightly melted cover. That is the second letterbox I have found that survived the fire. The other one is called "A Mouse (microbox)".

After leaving Bastrop I headed for San Marcos to plant three boxes in some of the natural areas that Silver Eagle clued us all in to last spring.

PI Joe
