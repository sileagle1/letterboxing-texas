From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2009-01-25 08:44:54 UTC-06:00
I can't believe you did either!
 
In the event camping in Mission Tejas fills up, there is camping nearby at Ratcliff Recreation Area:
 
http://www.fs.fed.us/r8/texas/recreation/davy_crockett/ratcliff.shtml
 
Ratcliff is about 17 miles from Mission Tejas through the Davy Crockett National Forest.
I've camped many times at both places, and I like 'em both. 
 
Other lodging is available at Salmon Lake in Grapeland, about 18 miles away:
 
http://www.salmonlakepark.com/
 
For very primitive camping, you can camp along the 4C trail which runs a scant few miles (2-5 miles) from Mission Tejas.  The 4C basically runs from Mission Tejas to Ratcliff Recreation Area. 
 
http://www.ouachitamaps.com/picture_library/Four%20C/Four%20C%20Trail.jpg
http://www.ouachitamaps.com/Four%20C.html
 
 
On the 4C, you can find primitive car camping at Neches Bluff (4C-19 on the second ouachitamaps link above).  4C-10 has a camping shelter about 1/2 mile off the road, and 4C-12 has a good primitive camping spot about 1 mile off the road.  Neches Bluff is about 3 miles from Mission Tejas.  No clean water at any of these, so BYOW or bring a filter.
 
For hotels, look in Crockett, 18 miles away.  Rusk is just a little further.  Palestine, and Lufkin aren't too far away either, 38-40 miles, but they are harder drives because you are driving smaller roads.
