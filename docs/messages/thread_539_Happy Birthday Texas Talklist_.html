Happy Birthday Texas Talklist!

From: Silver Eagle (sileagle@alltel.net) | Date: 2005-01-07 18:13:35 UTC

This talklist is now over 1 year old and I want to give a
public "Thank You" to Barefoot Lucy for creating it and
functioning as its main Moderator. I have to admit that
at the time it was created I did not think Texas was ready
for a Talklist just for them, since there were only about
10 letterboxers in the whole state. Happily, I couldn't
have been more mistaken. Right after it was created, TX
letterboxing started taking off and this list has been a
big part of that. At a time when I was growing tired of
the fights on the Main Talklist and the fear that I might
post something "wrong", this list provided a comfortable
place to talk freely and I appreciate that. Not to mention
it provides a much better way to communicate things like
Events and box status to fellow TX boxers. Last year I
had to put together an email list of every boxer that had
emailed me about my boxes in order to notify them of the
first TX LB Event. Now it is so much easier. So Happy
Birthday TX Talklist, and here's to another great year!

Silver Eagle

Re: [letterboxingtexas] Happy Birthday Texas Talklist!

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-01-07 19:16:04 UTC-08:00
Well that's just nifty, isn't it? It's hard to
believe the list is already a year old! I thought
y'all might get a kick out of how I got around to
setting this list up...

I wish I could say that when I set the list up, I did
it with a strong sense of purpose and determination,
but the truth is - I was more pondering and playing
around than anything. I was fairly new to
letterboxing and (I know y'all will find this hard to
believe) there was a flame war in full swing when I
joined the big list - talk about intimidating! Yes, I
know, it's hard to imagine, but everyone was fighting
over there!

I was fascinated by boxing, and I really wanted a
better outlet for learning about the pasttime than the
big list, but I didn't know if any Texas boxers would
really be interested in helping little ol' me or if
they would even be interested in helping each other.
I knew who Silver Eagle and Baby Bear were because
they had planted the only boxes within reach of me,
and I knew BusyBee because she had welcomed me when I
posted my intro on the big list, but as far as I knew,
they were the only three "real" letterboxers in Texas
and I was faking it til I figured it out! Now this is
not meant to slight those other letterboxers who
really were out there too, that was just my perception
at the time, based on my very limited knowledge! I
guess I kind of had this image of them being three
letterboxing yodas - masters of the tupperware
force...

About the time I joined the big list, a few other
Texas newbies joined, so I suspected I might not be
the only one overwhelmed. I set the list up, then
hesitated to publicize it because I didn't want to get
shot down. Well, I let it simmer a bit, and almost
forgot about it until BusyBee stumbled upon it. She
requested to join, but I didn't get her request for a
few days because I wasn't tending the list. I really
only found her request because I was working on
another list I help moderate.

I remember gulping nervously when I saw her request,
then deciding "what the heck" - if the list fails,
who's gonna know it besides me and the others who
might want to join. I accepted her request, but I put
off publicizing the list. Finally, BusyBee kicked me
in the rear end (in her own nice way) and urged me to
announce the list on the big list. She also suggested
I get in touch with Silver Eagle and Baby Bear because
they were thinking of scheduling a gathering. Again,
I gulped, because these were the guys who were bigger
than life to me. I emailed them and sure enough they
joined my little list, and next thing I knew, so were
other folks! And then we started talking - wow! And
everyone was soooo nice - WOW! And I was learning by
leaps and bounds and everyone else seemed to be too -
WOOOOWWW!

I laugh now when I look back at how insecure I was
about this list. It's okay - y'all can laugh at me
too. It was silly, wasn't it? We are the warmest,
most friendly e-group I've ever belonged to. When
things get heated on the big list, this is a great
place to depressurize. I've noticed that we go quiet
sometimes, then suddenly everyone starts talking - I
like that. Everyone has a "go for it" attitude and
encourages one another, no one seems to feel
threatened by others. No one gets shot down because
they aren't as prolific as others. Newbies can take
the targets off their chests and relax here (I like
the newboxers list for that too!).

I want to thank Silver Eagle for agreeing to help
moderate this list. Once I knew this list was going
to be here to stay, I realized it would be a shame for
it to suffer if I were to ever lose my password or get
hit by a bus or run over by a letterboxing pteradactyl
(there ya go Hawk - howzabout that for a box?), so I
asked him to help make sure it keeps going by being
able to manage the list along with me. Not that y'all
are that hard to manage - far from it! In fact, I'm
sure he would agree that we are kind of like the
Maytag man - not much to do except approve new
members...

What else has happened in the last year? How about a
great gathering organized by Silver Eagle with an
awesome stamp carved by Baby Bear? And those great
t-shirts Ruby pulled together for all of us, and Jeff
of Birds of a Feather put together the most awesome
database yet known to letterboxing. Don't forget that
Silver Eagle revamped our map too.

And of course, every one of us who has labored to
plant a box in the last year has participated in
seeding the legacy...and everyone who has appreciated
the pursuit of a box has fueled the flame.

I feel truly honored to have been around Texas
letterboxing for the past year - it has come a long
way! And together we are a formidable force!

Lucy
