Ruby

From: the carrascos (a.carrasco@earthlink.net) | Date: 2008-02-11 08:39:28 UTC-06:00
You always knew something great was coming when you saw a post from the Celtic Lions.  It is amazing how someone you only met in person a few times could have such an impact on you, but Ruby was that sort of person.  Her humor and kindness were in a class all by themselves.  This is very sad news, but it seems to me Ruby wouldn't like people to be moping around.  So instead of being sad and missing her, I am going to think of her sitting in the car with Cactus Licker and a pair of binoculars keeping watch to see if "Gryphon Bait" drew any interesting visitors.  She will truly be missed.  MO5

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-02-11 08:52:07 UTC-06:00
LOL...can you imagine the number of wildly funny memories her family must have of her?  I doubt watching the Gryphon Bait box was the only over-the-top sort of thing she ever did with them!  I can hardly imagine they themselves can shed very many tears before they think of one of her antics and have to smile too.
 
You know, she always signed her emails "skidding in broadside".  Not too long ago, Fearless Fox ran across that particular line (without knowing of its association with Ruby) and started using it as his sign-out.  He uses the whole quote though, which is probably very appropriate to the moment:
 
 

Life is not a journey to the grave with the intention of arriving safely in a pretty and well preserved body, but rather to skid in broadside, thoroughly used up, totally worn out, and loudly proclaiming

WOW! WHAT A RIDE!

From: tigger32167 (tigger32167@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-02-12 12:51:47 UTC
I was also one of the unfortunate ones who never actually met her.
However when I announced on this group not long after I started
letterboxing, that my friend and letterboxing partner was diagnosed
with ovarian cancer, she sent me the sweetest personal email to let
me know of her struggles and how letterboxing had helped her to cope
with cancer. She became an inspiration to me with that email and it
was then that I truly realized what a wonderful group of people that
letterboxers are. I thank her...and only wish that I could have met
her in person and thanked her for that inspiring message.
JB Kokopelli
