I took a deep breath when I read the post about the stamp not being very artistic, but then the kids all got in a big arguement and I had to leave the computer and return to real life.  But then I thought about it for a while, and this is what I thought (for what its worth) Letterboxing is just like anything else, people are drawn to it for all sorts of reasons.  And letterboxers all have different strengths. Just like everyone's mothers used to say, "the world would be a pretty boring place if everyone were just the same".  I think anyone who takes the time to carve a stamp and hide a box deserves encouragement.  Some of the first stamps we put out there were pretty primitave, but if I had waited until I was a great carver I'd still be waiting to place a box.  It just seems to me that we are Texans, not New Yorkers (sorry miss moon!) and should conduct ourselves as such.  Now I have to go break up another kid fight:) -Mother of Five
