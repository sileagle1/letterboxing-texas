TeamKing's Year in Review

From: TeamKing (teamking@entouch.net) | Date: 2005-08-29 15:11:57 UTC-05:00
Last August, I read the Time magazine article describing this
interesting pastime called 'letterboxing'. It sounded intriguing. So,
I checked out the LBNA site and found that were boxes right here in
Houston! Alright! Off I went. My first box was Baby Bear's 'Park
Angel' in Telge Park. I was excited when I found it. Didn't really
know WHAT I was looking for and since the box was a micro, I was
surprised how tiny. Onward I went through the heat of August &
September getting boxes mostly by Hawk, Baby Bear, Silver Eagle, and
Tiger. Who WERE these people? Some I chatted with online. Others
didn't say too much. I never met any of them until Thanksgiving. By
then we finally ventured out of Houston and headed up to Dallas for
the weekend. I finally met my first real letterboxers - the Celtic
Lions. We hit our 100th box that weekend too - Celtic Lions' Shawnee
Trail box. Wee. 100 boxes! We finally felt like real boxers.

Little by little, more names and people started to appear. By March,
we were excited to be going to our first Texas gathering. A chance to
finally put some faces to the names. We had a great time that day.
Thanks to everyone who came out, and those who contributed boxes. And
who could ever forget meeting Wanda from R.I.?!

Since then, I have continued this 'hobby' with varying degrees of
interest - have often been first finder on the Houston boxes for a
while but have laid low recently. Planted boxes in Texas, Ohio,
Pennsylvania, England, and France. Spent 2 days in Dartmoor in July -
what a fabulous experience THAT was! And have continued my
involvement in postal letterboxing which I really enjoy.

I am really surprised at the amount of new people that have come in
this year. So many new boxes right here in Houston. Great thanks to
all of you for continuing to plant them for the rest of us. (Go
Gramatrick!) Also, around the rest of state. Areas that were hardly
mentioned a year ago seem to have grown in leaps and bounds. How
could you not enjoy traveling across Texas with the assortment of
boxes we now have available?!

Thanks for making letterboxing such a fun time for our whole family.
We love getting out and exploring, and finding all the neat little
parks, trails, and places we had never noticed before. We are
currently just over 500 finds and still going strong - one year later.

TeamKing
