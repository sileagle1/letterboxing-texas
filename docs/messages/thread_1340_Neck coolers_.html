First - I bought some 3-color desert camo material, the same stuff the uniforms were made of.  I do not remember where I got it, some surplus web site.  Seems to me it was here:
http://www.majorsurplusnsurvival.com/index.htm
 
I cut strips  5 inches wide by 27 inches long. 
 
I folded it color side in, and stitched it on one end and all along the 27 inches.
 
Then I turned it in-side-out, which means now the color is out.
 
On the end sewn shut, I stitched it again about 1 inch from the end, and put a 1 inch square of velcro HOOKS.
 
I poured some of the silica material into it.  I do not remember how much, but it seemed like it was not enough, that stuff expands.
 
Then I stitched it shut 4 inches from the open end, and right at the open end.
 
Last, I stitched a 4 inch by 1 inch piece of velcro LOOPS to that end.
 
It was a lot of work for me.  I do electronics, not sewing!  Most likely you can improve it, and do it better.
 
The stuff inside is a silica material we got at a garden store.  It is mixed in with the plant dirt.  It swells to hold water when wet, then slowly gives up the water as it dries.  Do not remember the name.
 
NOTE:  Mine were quite large.  4.5 or even 4 inches might be better.
 
EXPERIMENT!
 
Good luck.
 
Mike.
