Trip to Houston, and thanks to you all

From: (vicki@bellmotel.com) | Date: 2009-02-24 10:40:41 UTC-06:00
You are all very kind, but I am learning how to letterbox by observing all of you!
 
My husband, Whiz Gee, and I went on a grand letter-boxing spree in Houston last Thursday through Sunday, finding 62 boxes, mostly in three parks -- Mercer Arboretum, Memorial Park, and Telge Park.  Houston has an embarrassment of letterboxing riches!  I know the folks from Houston have been telling us about it, but seeing the destruction from Hurricane Ike up close and personal is overwhelming at times.  I remember one clue reading "Look for the fallen tree", and we were staring at a sea of fallen trees.  I wasn't prepared for how much rescue efforts would have been welcome on these boxes.  A fair number of boxes in Mercer Arboretum were resting out in the open, and though they had floated up in rising waters, and then settled back down on top of the debris.  We tucked those back into hiding places.  We only found one "hanging" box on the ground near a tree, and we hung it back up where it belonged.  I'm surprised more weren't blown to Louisiana.  I emailed dewberry that one tree in Memorial Park where her box had been tucked into roots, was now on it's side with the box still in the roots, but dangling up in the air!  We tucked it out of sight.
 
Most of the logbooks we found were damp.  I have been saving any large silica pillows I've come across, and I tucked those into baggies of damp logbooks to help dry them out.  But, I didn't bring nearly enough silica, and I only brought two logbooks to replace soggy ones with; so by the middle of the trip we were really feeling guilty that we couldn't help out more boxes.  Actually, there were very few boxes we couldn't find, some just took more effort at hacking in and around debris than others.  At Telge Park, we found the bottom half of a small round Lock-and-Lock that had been artfully painted in camouflage colors.  If you recognize that as your box, we can confirm that its contents are missing. 
 
We did stop in at Texas Art Supply on Montrose, and gleefully bought ink pads, bottles of re-inkers, and carving tools that I've never seen anywhere else.   What a treasure! 
 
So, I hope that more letterboxers make a trip to Houston, to find the myriads of boxes, and to help out with Hurricane Relief.  Bring lots of Hurricane Relief supplies when you come.
 
-- Bell Motel
