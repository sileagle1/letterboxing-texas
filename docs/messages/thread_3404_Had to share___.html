Had to share...

From: Melina Baker (mgb099@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-03-12 18:49:28 UTC-07:00
I feel a little silly here - I've been carving for what - four years?  And all that time I've used the pencil transfer method.  You know-trace the outline with a charcoal pencil, then turn it over on the rubber and rub the image from the paper to the rubber with your finger?
 
Well - as I mentioned earlier, I retreated to my garage work bench to carve for awhile.  Lightning called me in to listen to his trombone practice pieces (he sounds GOOD!) so while I'm in for a few minutes, I had to share.
 
That rubbing with the finger thing?  It works.  But BootsTex mentioned that he uses something hard to rub with, like a spoon or - something.  But something hard.  I had a pair of snub nosed scissors close by, so rather than using my finger I grabbed them and rubbed the image on with them.  It was positively AMAZING how much better an image I got by using the scissors!  WOW!
 
The other thing that was pretty cool was using my magnifying light along with my lighted table to trace the image in the first place.  I have a very small table-top table with a light beneath it to shine through and help with tracing...with the light under and the light over, and the image magnified, I was able to trace it with much more detail than usual.  I'm now using the magnifying light to carve. 
 
I can feel my carving moving to another level - so cool, so truly cool!  And now, to go back out to my little haven...

From: Maribeth Jagger (cjagger405@earthlink.net) | Date: 2008-03-12 23:50:18 UTC-05:00
I'm working on improving my technique also! I used to sit at my worktable and keep my carving material flat on my table while carving it. I used a magnifying glass which hung around my neck to see better. However, just recently I bought a table mounted lighted magnifying glass, and I've found that if I stand up and hold the carving material in one hand while carving it with the other (under the magnifying glass), I have a lot more control and can do a lot better job on the details. Strange how little things can make a difference, but that seems to work for me. (Recognizing that I still have a lot of room for improvement!)
Puddle Splasher
