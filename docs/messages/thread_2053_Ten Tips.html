Ten Tips

From: the carrascos (a.carrasco@earthlink.net) | Date: 2006-08-10 14:05:44 UTC-05:00
-Always contact the placer after finding (or attempting to find) a box.  This helps placers keep up with condition of boxes, alerts them to repairs that need to be made, and it is just plain thrilling to find out someone found your box!
 
-As a placer, always reply to someone who has notified about one of your boxes.  This helps build a sense of community, and helps everyone to become more connected to the letterboxing "experience".
 
I have made friends through letterboxing that I otherwise never would have met, if I had just stamped into their boxes (or they into mine) and gone about my business I would have never have had the opportunity to get to know some really great people!  MO5

The only problem with immediately putting a notification on the clue sheet that a box is missing, after someone notifies the placer that it is gone, is sometimes people report a box missing to the placer when it actually is NOT missing. I've had boxes reported missing before and when I went to check on them, they were right where they were supposed to be. The problem is that sometimes, I can't just immediately go check on a box when it is reported missing, but I hate to report it missing until I satisfy myself that it actually is.

From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-08-11 05:29:40 UTC
I have had lots of boxes reported missing that weren't.

Sometimes it's a question of needing to refine the clues, but more
likely it's that the finder has misinterpreted them. I wouldn't
automatically jump to the conclusion that they're missing. However,
if I do know that a box is missing, I try to change the status--either
archiving it on LBNA if I know I'm not going to replace it, adding
MISSING to the clue line if I am going to replace it, and changing the
status on AQ to missing.

As to donating, I used Paypal. It was easy. On his blog, Choi has
the address to use.

I have one box, Spring Hope, that gets reported missing frequently.  Fortunately it is about two minutes from my house, so every time it gets reported I go check it and there it sits, happily tucked away!  The clues to it are deliberately vague, so if someone emails me for a hint I tell them not to think too literally.  Almost everyone who logs the find reports that it was a challenge, but so many people have found it without any help that I know the clues work.  And besides, Stillwater told me to never change the clues because they were perfect.  Who am I to argue with Stillwater?

From: Janet or Charles (janetsredbarn@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-08-11 13:47:43 UTC
You wouldn't have to report it missing, just add a note that it has
dropped down and may be difficult to retrieve. Then, the finder
could decide whether he wanted to attempt it or not, or at least he
could be prepared with a "Grabber". We found a box in Martins
Spring Nature Center not long ago that had been attempted several
times, but not marked as missing. The clue said to look for a tree
reaching out over the creekbed and there was such a tree there.
There was also a dead, gray tree on the ground that probably reached
out over the creek at one time and sure enough, the box was at the
base of the dead tree. You had to visualize how it might have
looked when the box was placed.

Boots Tex
