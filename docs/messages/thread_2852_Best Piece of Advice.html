Best Piece of Advice

From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-06-21 18:27:54 UTC
My best advice is to understand that there may be hazards on the
trail, and to come prepared.

This means (so Lucy, am I going over my allotted 1 piece of advice by
expanding):
1. Know what poison ivy looks like (in all its forms and in all seasons)
2. Never stick your hand in a hole. (glove or stick)
3. Be aware of rising water & cliffs.
4. Carry enough water and some snacks so that if you misread a
direction and end up on a 6mile round trip hike instead of the 1 you
thought that you'll be okay.
5. If a situation doesn't seem right...follow your instincts and turn
around. Not every box is right for every family and it's okay to give
up on a box. You can always return a


Dewberry


Re: Best Piece of Advice

From: Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter (janet@redbarnretreat.com) | Date: 2007-06-21 20:00:02 UTC
In reference to Dewberry's No. 5: As a placer, try to find scenic
or interesting places to plant your own boxes. Ask yourself "If
this were someone else's box and I was coming to find it, would this
be an enjoyable experience?". If you want your box to be a
challenge, with that 6 mile hike, that's okay, but remember that not
everyone will choose to find it.

Boots Tex
