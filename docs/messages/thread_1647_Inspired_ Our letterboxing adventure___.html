Inspired! Our letterboxing adventure...

From: Christi Brogan (christi@broganfamily.org) | Date: 2006-01-28 10:22:03 UTC-06:00
Wow! Yesterday (dh only works a half day on Fridays) we went on our
very first letterboxing adventure. We went to a somewhat nearby park
(McAllister Park in San Antonio, which is about a 45 minute drive each
way for us)to start our adventure and we found a total of 5 letterboxes
as well as a live armadillo and his nest. Dh loved it (well, I think he
was super excited about photographing the armadillo and excited, but not
quite as, by the stamps ;) ) and I have to say, after seeing all of the
stamps in these letterboxes, I am totally in awe! I mean, to carve the
intricate details that are on some of these stamps, in such a small
space (eraser size and/or smaller) just blows me away. Anyway, I just
wanted to say thanks! I really had a great time and am looking forward
more now to planting my own boxes. I do have to tell you that being
my size (I'm a very "large and lovable" lady!) this trip is quite a
feat, but I'm thrilled to have discovered this activity and hope to go
on many more letterboxing adventures! Thank you also to those who plant
"easy" boxes so that until I can work up my stamina, I have some to
discover! I'm going to be going again this weekend at some point to hit
both Freidrick Park and Raymond Russell (maybe I'll get lucky and Angi's
boxes will have reappeared?) to see what's there... Anyway, thanks
again for the great fun and I'll be emailing status updates to the box
owners later on today :)
