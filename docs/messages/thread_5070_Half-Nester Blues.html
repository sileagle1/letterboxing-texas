Half-Nester Blues

From: sileagle1 (sileagle@windstream.net) | Date: 2012-09-12 14:28:31 UTC
With my son Clueless Collector going away to college this year I am now halfway to being an empty-nester, and I'm not handling it very well. Some people look forward to this time but I have been so involved with my kid's lives through school, sports and other activities that it is going to be quite an adjustment for me. Though they never fully embraced letterboxing, we did have some fun times together in the early days and I must admit I got a little misty-eyed when I found my son's sig stamp while helping him pack. He probably hasn't used it much since I carved it years ago, but just thinking about all he has done since then brought on a wave of nostalgia. Adding to this feeling was my 10 year anniversary of letterboxing last month, which gave me even more memories to reflect on. So, I thought I would share a few since I've known some of y'all almost that long and we are like family.

Finding my first box with Baby Bear in Spokane, WA on August 31, 2002.
Placing my first box at Zube park while the kids rode the train.
Finding our first box together at SFA Park, then finding it again 8 years later and seeing the comments from that visit.
Finding Eoghan's early boxes, especially Wraith, which took several attempts.
Taking my first letterbox trip with Baby Bear through TX during the Space Shuttle explosion in 2003.
Taking our next letterbox trip through the mid-west and finding Der Mad Stamper boxes in the middle of nowhere.
Attending my first letterbox event in Washington.
Hosting our first TX letterbox event in Bastrop SP with a young Clueless Collector helping.
Working to help improve LBNA, especially the map regions, which took over 2 years of gentle prodding.
Helping to populate those regions with letterboxes, then later all counties.
Trying to fit letterboxing into family vacations without too many complaints of "Are we stopping for letterboxes again?".
Meeting many wonderful people through the years via letterboxing.
Watching my kids grow from wide-eyed children to mature adults in the blink of an eye.

Silver Eagle
