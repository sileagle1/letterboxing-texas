After Action Report. Long.

From: Michael P. Olbrisch (mike2004@elp.rr.com) | Date: 2006-05-11 02:11:52 UTC

For my dog owning, ham radio, letterboxing, gun owning and geocaching
friends.

I promised an after action report on our Geocaching - Letterboxing -
Armadillo Ham Radio picnic and camping trip to Abilene State Park last
weekend. Sorry this took so long. In my defense I can only say that I get
up at 0400, in to work at 0500, off at 1530, spend two hours overhauling my
jeep motor every day after work, then come home at 1800 and clean the
camping stuff.

I took off from work a half day last week Thursday to get ready and pack.
We wanted to be on the road by 0500 Friday morning. We made it out the gate
on time, but drug our feet a bit getting gas. We needn't have worried. Our
friends Brad and Cherry were running even later. No matter - nobody's life
depended on it. And we drove slow while they caught up.

On the road we were in constant communication with each other on VHF, and
had the UHF radio on whatever DILLO radio frequency we were in range of.
Played a lot of radio on this trip. And the ICOM IC-901A never missed a
beat the whole trip. The repairs I did just two days earlier were just
fine.

We had five Geocaches planned for the trip out. Would you believe the only
letterbox on I-10 and I-20 from El Paso to Abilene is in Fabens Texas? Yes
- I am sure of it. I own it. So there were no letterboxes on the drive.
But we did find the 5 Geocaches. Three were just geocaches, nothing
special. These two were particularly interesting.
http://www.geocaching.com/seek/cache_details.aspx?guid=5e0944cb-217d-4983-a9
6d-31f1e7e5f26b
http://www.geocaching.com/seek/cache_details.aspx?guid=7aeea00c-db1a-4f93-ac
60-ebfcc6d1f8cf

We rolled into Buffalo Gap - Abilene state Park at about 4pm. Yep - 11
hours on the road. A few hours were spent Geocaching of course. The sky
was looking pretty grim, but we still had some sunlight too. We started
making camp. I was surprised. Our new 10 man tent went together as fast as
the 3 man tent our Brad and Cherry had. With the tent up and anchored, I
proceeded to move the gear into the tent. Just got that underway when the
storm struck.

Now - some will tell you this was the mother of all storms. I will
disagree. I have seen worse. I have been out in the desert in worse. But
not as a civilian. However, I will admit that it was a formidable storm,
and I was glad to be inside a good tent. The rainfall was about 2-3 inches/
hour, and hail the size of nickels. I turned on the CC Radio to get a
weather report.

http://www.ccrane.com/radios/am-fm-radios/ccradio-plus/index.aspx

This is perhaps the finest AM radio available for under $500. It also does
FM, TV audio, and weather band with alert. The weather report was kinda
grim. Still, we were on the outskirts of the storm. It was pretty much
over in about an hour, and the sun came out again. Briefly.

I called out to Brad to see if he was OK. They had flooded out. We were on
the high spot in the area. But somehow Brad was in the lowest point of the
high spot, and in a puddle. His tent was light, so we moved him a bit
higher. We moved out tent a bit higher too.

Some folks rolled in right during the storm and sat it out in the car. As
the storm cleared they got out, and started to set up. Something was
forgotten, and one of them left to find a Wal-mart. The other started
setting up camp. Uh-oh... A MAJOR part of the tent was also forgotten. And
the sky was clouding up again. We kept refining out set-up, and Moni made
dinner.

With dinner out of the way, we started making the beds etc. The sky was
getting VERY dark. Not sunset - but CLOUDS! The Wal-Mart run came back.
With a whole new tent. So we all pitched in and started getting it
together. Good thing too, because here comes the rain. We finished their
tent in a light rain, then went to our own tents to dig in. This storm
lasted about an hour too, and it was dark before it ended. We did a few
more things around camp and prepared for bed. I set the CC Radio for
weather alert. Good thing I did!

About mid-night the CC Radio weather alarm woke me. BAD storm coming in.
And it was headed right for us. We had about 20 minutes before it got to
us. There wasn't much to do but prepare to run for whatever shelter if the
tent went down. Big storm indeed. Wind. Rain. Lightning. Hail.
Fortunately for us, the tornadoes missed us completely. After a while the
storm passed us over, and I went back to sleep. Here is the weather map.
http://www.bbelppub.com/Abilene/Brads%20Camera/WeatherMap.jpg

Woke the next morning to the sounds of birds chirping. I also had a
crushing pain in my chest and could hardly breathe. So I shoved Parker off
me and sat up. It was just turning gray, so I lay back down between Moni
and Parker and listened to the birds chirping. After a while Brad and
Cherry stirred, so we got up and moving too. Brad and I assessed the camp
situation for storm damage while Moni and Cherry made breakfast.

With breakfast out of the way, we got our gear together for the
hiking/letterboxing/geocaching. There were 2 letterboxes in the park, and
about 12 geocaches. I wanted to do the letterboxes first so we would have
them even if we ran out of time.

http://www.letterboxing.org/BoxView.php?boxnum=6601 was the first one. The
clues were somewhat non-specific. There was two ways to work it, depending
on which way you came in. We picked one way. It was the wrong way. So we
started over. Found the box. It was destroyed! The log book... Well lets
just say it was WATER-LOGGED! We dried the stamp, stamped our
logbooks, then re-hid the box. There wasn't much we could do for
maintenance.

http://www.geocities.com/sileagle1/TX/BufGap/ was the next letterbox. This
one by Silver Eagle. It was the third one of his I have done. I have
learned that he is either very tall, or he under-estimates distance. This
one was no different! But we did find it, and stamped in. Then on
to the geocaches. We found 4 geocaches before it was time to return to the
picnic.

The picnic was fun. We learned that our camping partner of the night before
was Amy - W7AMY. She was interested in the geocaching we did and we ended
up teaching her about it. Visited with a number of the other Ham Radio ops
for a while. Slowly it started to break up. And we gathered around the
tents. Amy and her family went out to look for a few geocaches. We were
waiting. Waiting for dark. Waiting for this cache!
http://www.geocaching.com/seek/cache_details.aspx?guid=f82e6424-2660-4e49-91
9b-2c6f241fdfa1

This cache has to be done in the dark with a flashlight. The coordinates
leads you to the start point. From there you shine a flashlight around
looking for a clue. The clue was a very small reflector on a tree. Go to
it and sweep the light around. There is another one there..... And on and
on about a half mile in the deep dark woods. Good thing there were 7 of us,
it could get pretty scary in the woods at night. But Amy's 5 y/o daughter
did most of the flashlight work. And we found the cache. WHAT GREAT FUN it
was!!!!!

Uh-oh.... How do we get back? Did anyone lay in a track-log on the GPS? I
didn't. Well, nothing to do but start off and hope we can follow our
footprints. Shine the light down the trail and what's that? A reflector.
And another. What a thoughtful cache owner! Back out on the road in
minutes, and back to the tent. The weather was nothing like the night
before. It was pleasant, and we all sat out talking for a few hours before
bed. Then at bedtime, I set the CC Radio for weather alert again. And it
didn't go off all night.

The next morning I had that crushing pain in my chest and could hardly
breathe again. So I shoved Parker off me and sat up. Moni got up and
started making breakfast as I started slowly breaking camp. We wanted to
get home. But more importantly, we had about 10 geocaches on the list for
the trip home. Soooo..... Breakfast done - vehicles loaded, and on the
road.

Moni stopped a few times for photos. She is so lucky. There was a huge
longhorn sleeping in a corral that she wanted a photo of. So she stopped
the van and got out. And that longhorn got up and came to her, standing
just perfect for the photo. The photo doesn't do him justice!

http://pg.photos.yahoo.com/ph/olbrisch/detail?.dir=/d2c9re2&.dnm=9285scd.jpg
&.src=ph

Chatting on the radio, we decided to skip some of the caches to concentrate
on some other stuff. We grabbed one cache on the road, then ran to Midland
for lunch at Clear Springs restaurant. Great seafood.

After lunch we went to Odessa for a series of 5 similar geocaches. This was
the first of the group.
http://www.geocaching.com/seek/cache_details.aspx?guid=486570a3-6669-47c0-a6
16-489343f5d620
We did two other geocaches after the 5 in Odessa, then got home about
sunset. Unpacked the van, and settled in for the evening. Clean-up could
start the next day.

All I all - a wonderful weekend. We would do it again. Without the rain if
possible. If you missed it, you missed it!

Next Saturday Moni and I are going to Hueco Tanks to search for two
letterboxes. They are said to be the two oldest letterboxes in Texas. They
are reported as missing. We will see.
