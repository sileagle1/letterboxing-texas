Sitrep at Lake Grapevine and Irving (Las Colinas)

From: (blhjrh@aol.com) | Date: 2007-07-20 16:15:55 UTC-04:00
I made a trek out to Lake Grapevine today and over to Las Colinas in Irving to see about our flooded letterboxes.   Rockledge Park near the Lake Grapevine dam is closed, no access to anything on those trails, and anything on the lower trail near the lake is likely in the lake by now.  I was able to access a part of the trail from another area and can report that Merlin the Mooseterious is high and dry, as is White Sail  up on its hill (most likely, but I couldn't get to it to be positive).   The lower trail is still under the lake, so I wasn't able to check on the other boxes there, but my best guess is that The Lost Moose is definitely lost, into the lake and on its way to who knows where.  I can't really report on the other letterboxes along this trail since I wasn't able to get to that part of the trail for the high waters.
 
In Irving, the Trinity is still way over the banks along the Campion Trail near NW Hwy, and part of the trails are still underwater, too.  I'm pretty sure that anything on that trail is now somewhere way down the river heading towards the Gulf of Mexico.  I was able to get to the location of my Vivaldi! Four Seasons box, and it is definitely gone.  Maybe Vivaldi is trying to float back to Italy (now that would be a HH!).  
 
I pulled both my Southlake boxes near the south shore of Lake Grapevine at Bob Jones Park for maintenance.   Flooding has made the equestrian trails along the lake  inaccessible following the clues, but I took another route and found the locations of both Bat! and Roadrunner  high and dry.  Unfortunately, the boxes themselves weren't so lucky. The last few finders had reported finding them full of water, boxes and bags left open and the logbooks soaked.
 
I found the Bat!  letterbox lying open on the ground under the tree without the lid (which was lying nearby).  Both the original logbook (full) and a second logbook were in the box in plastic bags, but those bags were open, and of course, both logbooks are completely wet and the box full of water.  Roadrunner box was wedged in its tree where it should be, box sealed, but bags full of water and both original and second logbooks completely soaked.  Apparently this one, too, had been left open previously but the last finder must have sealed them up and put the box back where it went, well above the ground.  Bat!, too,  was supposed to be placed well up in a bushy cedar tree, wedged tightly into the branches and it had survived a number of strong wind and rainstorms in that tree.   
 
It's in a location that isn't likely to be discovered accidentally, since only equestrian trails go nearby and few walkers ever frequent that area.  I think that it had to be a letterboxer who is, sadly, a careless replacer, maybe someone who was in a hurry or let kids put it back or just didn't realize that we DO have terrible rains, when it does rain in Texas.   I'm not sure if either of these will be replaced.  First, I hope to salvage the soggy logbooks with my blowdryer and maybe some hot Texas sun - if we get some!  
 
I'm  sorry now that I didn't keep the original logbooks when they were full, when I added the second logbooks.  I remember our discussion on the Texas list that finders like to read the history of a letterbox as it is contained in the logs, but now I wish I'd kept that history for myself.  Then I'd only have lost part of it if it can't be dried.
 
What can we do other that ask finders to Recover Well to protect these logbooks? Maybe we need to add ReSEAL Tightly to our motto. 
 
~ Viewfinder (whose next letterbox might well be, Lady of the Lake).
