From: Mike Olbrisch (mike-2007@elp.rr.com) | Date: 2009-05-24 10:28:37 UTC-06:00

This is wizardry!

I went shopping with my wife this morning. This trip wasn't a "necessities"
shopping trip so much as it was a "stocking the pantry" shopping trip. The gets
these "$ale$ coupon" deals via E-mail somewhere. Then she makes a plan of
attack, and off she goes. Now, she says this is not a personal best, but close.
I admit some of this - like the "military discount" will not apply to some of
you. But most of the rest does or could apply.

The object is to collect coupons, buy items on sale, apply the coupons, and
stock up enough to last until the next sale.

Even I don't understand it all. BUT...... Watch how it works.....

We started off with a total bill of $123,61. I thought it wasn't a bad price -
for all of what she got. Lots of things that I know are expensive. Heh heh heh
- watch the wizard at work!

The stuff went past the scanner. BEEP - BEEP - BEEP - BEEP. Then she got -$10
for spending over $100. Then she got -10% military retiree discount. Then out
comes the magic wand (coupon book). BEEP - BEEP - BEEP - BEEP, each BEEP
taking something off the total.

When it was over, we paid $47.39 cash money to get out the door.

$123.61 - $47.39 = $76.22 in savings. Amazing!

The store claims she saves nearly $200 in total savings, this is the In-Store
specials, coupons, and other applied discounts. Not only that, but she has some
mail-in coupons that she claims will pay US $2.00 to eat the ice cream!

She found some deal on pork ribs. $6 a pound - but buy one and get 2 free! She
bought $75 worth of ribs, and paid $25, then applied the other discounts and
coupons on top of it.

This is just plain wizardry! And she packs a purple .357 Magnum too. No wonder
I married her!

I sit in awe at the feet of the master!
