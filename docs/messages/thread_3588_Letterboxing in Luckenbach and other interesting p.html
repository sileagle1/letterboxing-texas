Letterboxing in Luckenbach and other interesting places

From: Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter (janet@redbarnretreat.com) | Date: 2008-05-30 01:50:00 UTC
Over the recent holiday weekend, LSQ and I took a few days for a
family gathering and letterboxing trip to the Fredericksburg area.
We met El Lobo and Star Seeker (LSQ's brother and his wife), then
Will's World (our son) and Cattitude (LSQ's sister) met us there.
They're all family and all letterboxers, so how cool is that? We
rented a large ranch house on 250 acres of land for 3 days and it
actually cost less than staying in a motel, plus we had the house to
ourselves. In total, counting the trip there and back, we found 30
letterboxes and planted 8. The bad news is that we had to leave so
many boxes unfound, due to a lack of time. According to the ladies,
you can't go to Fredericksburg and not spend some time shopping.
According to me, that just takes away from letterboxing. And the
heat. And the chiggers! We had a great time, ate too much good food
and even tasted a little wine, thanks to Baby Bear. If you've never
been to the Old Tunnel to watch 3 million bats emerge, you need to do
that sometime. I've posted the 8 boxes on both LbNA and AtlasQuest.
They are: "Nimitz", "The Yellow Roads of Texas" and "Unbroken
Promises" in Fredericksburg, "Waylon & Willie" in Luckenbach, "The
Road to the White House - Quilt Block Series" in Stonewall, "Mr.
Texas - Texas Governors Series" in Junction, "The Lost Jim Bowie
Mine" in Menard (first box in Menard County) and "Mohair Sam" in
Rocksprings (first box in Edwards County). I expect Mohair Sam,
which is Lone Star Quilter's letterbox, to be pretty lonely for a
while. When you decide to go find him, please take another box to
plant so he'll have some company. I actually had another box ready
to plant at Enchanted Rock, but when we were on top of the rock
looking for another box, I discovered I had left mine in the car.
It'll have to wait.

Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter
