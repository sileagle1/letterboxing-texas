From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2010-03-10 11:36:25 UTC-06:00
I think the question should be "Who OWNS Fort Concho?" and that would answer your question. The short answer is that it is owned by the City of San Angelo, so you're probably good. However, given that the site is a NHL, they might be a little touchy about a box, so you may want to see if you can somehow confirm their position on stashing games on their property.
After reading your question, I did a little quick research. I found that National Historic Landmarks are designated by the Secretary of the Interior, who oversees theNational Parks Service. It appears the NPS is responsible for keeping the list of NHL's and for recommending new NHL sites, but that doesn't automatically mean they own the sites.
That said, I know that after a location is designated as a historic site, there are pretty strictlimitations on what can be done with the location in the future. It's possible there may be rules addressing our type of activity, but I didn't immediately find that.
FYI, here's some good information about National Historic Landmarks:
http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Historic_Landmark
