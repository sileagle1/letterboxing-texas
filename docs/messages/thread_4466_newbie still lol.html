From: Tasha Morris (keishkakeishka@yahoo.com) | Date: 2010-03-29 14:27:05 UTC-07:00
With very few exceptions, you will need to bring your own ink pads (or markers) with you when you go letterboxing.  Most boxes do not have ink pads in them, since they can dry out (or if the box gets wet, possibly ruin the box's logbook). 

You can use what ever you would like for your logbook.  Some people have acid-free books.  Some use index cards that they put in binders.  Some stamp the clue pages they have printed out.  Whatever works for you and makes you happy!  :-)

Good luck with your first finds this weekend!
Rogue Artist

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2010-03-29 22:19:48 UTC-05:00

Kim,

Keep it simple. Go with what you have as far as logook, stamp, ink. The finding is the fun. The rest of this stuff is moofah and you can change, update, modify as you go. You aren't stuck with what you started with. In fact, you will probably never have the "perfect" setup - you'll always look for the perfect logbook, work on carving a better stamp, finding the best carving tool, want a little different color ink, better markers, ideal carving material... That's another part of the fun of it, but until you actually get out and go hunting, you won't know what you like and dislike.

It's really easy to get bogged down in the details, but the big thing is - go and seek - and HAVE FUN! Worry about all that other stuff later...

Lucy
