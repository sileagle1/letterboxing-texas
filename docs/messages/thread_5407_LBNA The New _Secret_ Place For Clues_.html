LBNA The New "Secret" Place For Clues?

From: (sileagle1@yahoo.com) | Date: 2014-11-09 07:48:28 UTC-08:00

I just got an email from someone who found my Eyes Of Texas box in California who told me it was full of finders from many years ago, but nobody in the last 3 years.  This made me curious so I looked at status for some of my other boxes and the trend is similar for many of them across the US.  I know not everyone logs their finds online, but it still appears that not as many people are finding my boxes as in the past.  I know I don't get many emails from finders anymore, either.  This either means there are fewer new boxers or that fewer boxers are looking on LBNA for clues since I post there almost exclusively.  Of course, it could also mean people just don't want to look for boxes by Silver Eagle, but I choose to ignore that possibility.  Last year Baby Bear decided to post all his boxes on both LBNA and AQ, and he told me that he gets 10 times the email from finders on AQ than LBNA.  Also, when we went to Live & Breathe XV we both placed boxes in several states before and after the event and his have been found many more times than mine, even though our boxes are in the same parks, sometimes just yards away from each other.  All of this makes me realize that LBNA is no longer used by many people and has thus become almost a secret place to post clues, just like those hard to find clues from the early days of letterboxing.  So if you still use LBNA, don't feel like a dinosaur using antique technology, feel smug knowing you are potentially finding boxes that very few know about ;)

Silver Eagle
