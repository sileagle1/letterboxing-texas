From: (blhjrh@aol.com) | Date: 2005-03-08 10:36:05 UTC-05:00
OK, I've been a member of this group for several weeks (tx2chic) but I haven't yet introduced myself, and now my sister has joined and introduced herself, so I'm shamed into doing so too. 
    Hello, everyone!  I'm the sister of the CT-displaced Texan, and through her I discovered letterboxing and heard about all of her adventures hiking the forests there, so this fall I visited her and I earned my CT patch, too, and then I was completely hooked!  Since then, I've been in a LB frenzy, especially when I discovered so many fun LB's right in my area, the Fort Worth-Dallas area. 
    So far, I've located 63 letterboxes (yes, occasionally some are missing, but now that I know about the beignet-eating Aliens, it's completely understandable). Thanks to Ruby Tuesday for informing me of the TX Letterboxing group and website after finding some of the Celtic Lions boxes with their excellent stamps and clever clues.  I've found travellers, hitchhikers, and bonus boxes, and I love the surprises.  My husband has joined me on several LB adventures, and occasionally a granddaughter (there are 3 of them),  We sign Benny&Jim from Keller TX, and my stamp is a chickadee (gift from sister); his is Kokopelli.  My sister and I have plans to place a few letterboxes, and maybe drop a hitchhiker or traveller or two - watch for those chicks! 
    Here's what I've concluded about letterboxers so far:  they love the outdoors and discovering things; they're clever and literate (the clues!); they're artistic (the stamps), and they have a sense of humor.  My kind of group!

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-06-02 10:56:31 UTC-05:00
The Houston area is perhaps best served by www.atlasquest.com .  That site has a mapping function and the boxers in the Houston area have been pretty good about entering mapable locations.
 
BUT - www.letterboxing.org and www.atlasquest.com are the big two - it's a good idea to check both to make sure you find all the boxes listed in any given area.  Some folks post their clues to one and not the other.  And then there are personal websites for other greater Houstonians (who are also Great Houstonians) like Silver Eagle and Sandi Box and Dewberry, to name some, but certainly not all!

From: Charlotte (cpease@austin.rr.com) | Date: 2013-08-04 03:21:00 UTC
OK, so I just joined this group and have been strongly encouraged to introduce myself. :)

My trailname is Giant Eyeball! In our neighborhood are some large white spheres on a vacant lot. Whoever lives next to them lights them from within so that they glow in the dark. One day someone had painted pupils on them. So whenever we drive by, my kids shout "Look, Giant Eyeball!" It seemed like a good name for people looking for something.

I started looking for Letterboxes last fall and have had a lot of fun finding them since. I found out about it when I was working with my daughter on a Girl Scout badge. Once we tried it, I was totally sucked in. Our family has been doing it together but I have to admit I'm the one driving it. The others are not nearly as obsessive. I am a full-time Mama, a part-time Architect, and a lifelong "maker" of things. My husband is an elementary school teacher. We've got two young girls.

I recognize some of your names because I've found your boxes. I know I've found some by SilverEagle, BootsTex, & LoneStarQuilter (that weeping angel was amazing). I haven't planted any boxes yet but I've been doing a lot of carving lately! I have about half a dozen stamps ready to release and ideas for several more. There are a couple of things holding me back. One is that I can't find those Lock-and-Lock boxes anywhere. I've looked in several stores and on-line but they aren't available. What would be the next best (but affordable) container? The stamps I've done are too big to fit in a film canister.

I live in Austin but spend a lot of time in my hometown Houston, so expect to find most of my boxes in one of those two cities. Hopefully it won't be long before I get something posted. I'd love your advice/feedback.

Hi Giant Eyeball – good to see you joined the group! I’m glad we had a chance to meet at the library mini-meet.  Speaking of which…I promised you a link to the event in October:

 

http://www.atlasquest.com/showinfo.html?gEventId=2754

 

Most of us buy Lock N Lock’s online.  There used to be a dedicated store but it closed, so I just pick them up whenever I see a sale posted.  Usually people post on Atlas Quest forums when they find a sale.  This place has a sale on sets right now:

http://www.heritagemint.com/lock-n-lock-sets.html

Sterilite and others make some boxes similar to Lock-n-lock if you can’t find them. Look for boxes where the lid on each side snaps down independently.   

 

I look forward to seeing some of your stamps in the wild, and I hope you can make it to the October event!

Hey Charlotte, please let me add my welcome to you.  And answer some questions while I'm at it.

1. Please don't add the imprint of your stamp you are hiding.  It is considered a spoiler.  Personally, I would be afraid folks may not go after my stamp once they see the image.  :-D  I wouldn't worry about adding a photo at all unless it was part of the clue.

2.  An exchange is when you meet someone and exchange signature images.  Yes, you simply stamp into their logbook and they stamp into yours.  There is also something called "Personal Travelers."  These are additional carvings that letterboxers carry with them for additional fun.  Sometimes you have to answer a question to "earn" the PT, but most often you can just ask. Or just share if you have one.

For more information on these and other things you hadn't even thought to ask, AQ has a glossary:  http://www.atlasquest.com/about/glossary/

Again, welcome.  I hope to meet you soon.
