I don't want to bore everyone that has already heard my
"LB Beginning" story, so I will just give a brief account
of the events leading up to my first trip with Baby Bear
planned around letterboxing.

After reading about letterboxing in April, 2002, finding
my first box in August, then placing my first box in
September, Baby Bear and I started placing boxes around
Houston for each other to find (since nobody else was
participating at that time). We still had not found any
of the less than 10 boxes existing at that time in TX,
since the closest one was in Austin, so finding each other's
boxes was the next best thing. It's hard to imagine it now,
but posting clues for boxes back then was a long process,
fraught with pleading, begging and near-despair. You had to
submit your clues to the webmasters and wait for them to
post them on LBNA. Direct posting by letterboxers was over
a year away at that point. Well, since TX had seen so little
activity, no webmaster had been assigned to our state, and
our clues were not being published. This did not stop us,
since we just emailed each other our clues, but we worried
that other letterboxers would never come if no clues were out
there to draw them. So I started bugging the webmasters
for help, and after a month or so I got an email from a
webmaster named Wes saying that he had been assigned our state
(along with his others). He soon posted our clues and I
developed a good relationship with him, which helped me
get several other requests implemented to help the future
of TX letterboxing.

At that time, the map of TX on the cluepage only had a couple
of regions colored in, since those were the only areas with
boxes. I wanted the Southeast Region colored in, since we
had placed boxes there, so people would know at a glance
that boxes did exist around Houston. I had also noticed
that some counties were in the wrong region, so when my
clues were finally posted they were showing up in the wrong
place and I worried future boxers would miss them. Wes
colored in the SE region, then sent me the spreadsheet used
for TX regions and had me "fix" it. Well, TX has a LOT of
counties, and I had to go through every one and make sure
it matched up to the correct region. It was worth it though,
since once Wes implemented the updated spreadsheet, clues
were showing up in the correct place.

So, with the posting process finally under control, it was
time to start placing boxes. Baby Bear and I realized that
just placing boxes around Houston was not good enough if we
wanted more boxers to get interested, so we started planning
a Letterbox Planting trip. It was to be like Johnny Appleseed,
except our seeds would be letterboxes.

Stay tuned for the story about that trip .....

Silver Eagle

Recently on one of the lists the suggestion was made that this is
really a low-tech game we play and that it used to be played WITHOUT a
website, and implied that the websites have made us soft. I had no
idea you and Baby Bear lived that life, or I should say - I never
stopped to realize you must have lived that life!

What a challenge it would be to find another letterboxer in Texas (let
alone the whole country) in order to find another letterbox. It makes
me think of that Dr. Suess book "Are You My Mother?" I can just see
y'all peering at folks on trails saying "Are YOU a letterboxer???"

Just imagine if the two of you hadn't both become interested in
letterboxing. About the only way this could have ever taken off was
to have two or more people who wanted to play AND who knew each other
AND who were good at both hiding and finding.

It amazes me yet again how far we've come in a few short years...

Lucy
