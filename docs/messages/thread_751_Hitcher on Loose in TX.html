Hitcher on Loose in TX

From: wandaandpete (wandaandpete@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-03-28 21:41:19 UTC

Wanda's still boxing up a storm out there in Texas, and has collected a couple
hundred boxes since that Rodeo Event last weekend in Brazos Bend. She'll
probably be reaching F7000 sometime while I'm out there with her in the next few
days. In the meantime, though, before I could get any boxes to plant out to her, she
must have gotten awfully desperate to plant something, because she just planted a
hitchhiker all by itself! I hope someone out there will rescue this homeless hitcher
soon, and perhaps plant a more permanent home in its temporary resting place.

The hitcher is in the northern part of Panna Maria cemetery in the historic little
Texas town of that same name, founded by Polish immigrants in 1854 along Bahia
Trail between San Antonio and Goliad. Look for a statue of a little girl (age 4 and 1/
2) under a Live Oak tree. On the north side of the nearby cedar to the south, find
Harriet tucked into a little nest at about the height Elzbieta might have reached.

Looking forward to letterboxing in TX and OK myself.

Cheers,

Pete

From: Connie Terry (connie@lavernia.net) | Date: 2005-03-28 18:59:50 UTC-06:00

To make a long story short, as Pete hoped, Harriet’s Hitchhiker has indeed been rescued.

 

The long version:  Back in October, Lech Walensa, leader of the Solidarity movement and former Prime Minister of Poland visited Panna Maria to commemorate the town’s 150th anniversary. Because Lech Walensa is someone I have always admired I made a “Solidarnosc” letterbox in honor of his visit.  We had the box with us on the day of the festivities, but there was so much going on that it didn’t get hidden.  I was in Panna Maria once more with the box but I had wanted to leave it “in town” and didn’t get it hidden then either.  Well, Wanda’s visit shamed me into finally placing the box—if I had already planted it there would have been one more for her to find.  I came home at 4:00, told Navigator what was going on and by 4:30 he, SunSnake and I were Panna Maria bound.  The little hitchhiker has been moved into my Solidarnosc box. (Yes, I know it’s bad form to say where you put a hitchhiker, but since it’s not where Wanda left it, I thought it would be best to explain.)  I left a small slip of paper in the nest where Wanda left the hitchhiker directing anyone who comes after me where to look.  After I get supper made, I will get the clues posted for the new box.

 

Corazon
