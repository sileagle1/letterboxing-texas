Looking back at the last year or more

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-12-29 21:31:00 UTC-08:00
Being off this week and able to devote my time to
letterboxing, I've had some time to take a look back
and see that the last year has been good to me
letterboxing-wise.

When I started boxing in September 2003, there was a
scant few boxes to be found in our area. But we found
them, and we fed ourselves by going to the first
gathering in Bastrop, and finally we got up the
gumption to place a few. By the end of 2004 which was
a little more than 1 year in the game, our PFX count
was a whopping P5 F9 X23. Throw in a Postal box or
two (not my thing I learned - I can't depend on myself
to get them mailed out quickly enough to suit everyone
else) and a couple Virtuals (okay in a pinch, but too
- still - for me to really enjoy). I have to admit, I
was a little discouraged at that time because
letterboxing, while it was a lot of fun, was a whole
lot of work to get not much payout.

We had planted our first box in February 2004 and it
disappeared within 3 months or so. We planted another
shortly after that and it too was sucked into a black
hole. Our next box was Oh Deer! at Fairfield, planted
on May 22, 2004, and thankfully it has stuck around.

It was right at the end of 2004 that I finally had a
hit on one of my boxes, and as it turned out, I found
THREE hits when I checked it - GG was first, followed
by Silver Eagle and Baby Bear on their epic
letterboxing adventure last December. Finding those
log entries was exactly what I needed to keep the
faith and keep on letterboxing!

At the beginning of 2005 I was determined to seriously
up my F count but I realized it was going to mean some
serious traveling because there still were very, very
few boxes around my area and the only new ones were
ones I had planted.

A windfall hit in the form of an article in the local
paper and another article in Family Fun (I think that
was the one) and boxes and boxers began popping up
around me. I suddenly found lots of opportunity
(relatively speaking) to find boxes and it was like a
breath of fresh air! Eventually though, the pace
slowed down and about the only ones I haven't yet
found in my area are one box by TexasLuvBug right here
in my town that I guess I'm saving for a rainy day,
and the ones that Lucy Locket takes pleasure in
placing immediately after I've finished an area.
Still though, nice to be surrounded by boxes these
days, knowing that if I just need a fix, I could go
find one of those again just for kicks.

At the first of the year, I focused quite a bit on
placing instead of finding, and what I discovered with
that is that you then have to check them regularly if
you want to feel like letterboxing CAN live
harmoniously with Leave No Trace principles. And
actually, having boxes in the wild is pretty addictive
in itself. You pass close to an area and you wonder
how your box is doing, so you swing in to check up on
it. You suddenly realize you haven't heard anything
of a particular box in awhile, so you feel compelled
to go visit it. You want to read those logbooks. So
next thing I knew, my boxing time was occupied in part
with checking my plants, but I realized that was
perfectly okay with me because I enjoyed checking up
on them!

Toward the end of the year, I suddenly realized I
really hadn't done much for my F count. Even at the
gathering, I had been lazy in getting out and finding
boxes there. So I felt the urge to go on mad boxing
binges to get the count up. It's a lot harder to up
your count than you would think, especially if you are
trying so hard to do just that!

Finally, today as we were sitting at Confederate
Reunion Grounds, just kicking back and enjoying the
surroundings, I realized that when I was worrying
about counts and not about experiencing the world
around me, I wasn't enjoying letterboxing as much as I
could have been. Yes, it's nice to have a fatter
logbook, but not nearly as nice as knowing that I
walked the whole trail for every stamp I ever got
instead of bailing off the trail as soon as I got what
I came for. And not as nice as breathing deep the air
around me as we go hunting. And not as nice as those
odd little conversations that come up with my kids
especially when we're out in the woods - like "imagine
what the world must look like to an ant. Do they even
know we're here because we are so big and they are so
small? And is there a world around us much bigger than
we are that we are oblivious of because we are so
small and it is so big?"

Those wild trips in search of lots of boxes are loads
of fun, but for me, they aren't the meat of
letterboxing, and I'm glad I've tried a few of them so
I can realize that. And I'll probably indulge myself
from time to time again, but I won't forget that the
slow easy trips are something special to savor.

My letterboxing year has indeed been good to me, not
as much in quantity as I had hoped, but much, much
more in quality than I had bargained for!

Lucy

From: Maribeth Jagger (cjagger405@earthlink.net) | Date: 2005-12-30 00:50:14 UTC-06:00
Thank you Lucy for sharing your past letterboxing experiences with us! I so enjoy reading other folks approach to this adventure we share! Although I sometimes think of letterboxing as something that can be done alone (searching for a box) - the truth is, we are not at all alone in this endeavor! When I first started letterboxing, I was more aware of my hike, finding the box, stamping in, etc. with little thought about the folks out there that made those boxes available. After getting acquainted with so many great letterboxers in person or through their participation in our website, I am very aware that each box represents a lot of time, creativity and thought of some pretty interesting people. That added dimension makes this a very personal and special activity for me. I actually try not to count my PFXs because I've found in the past when I paid too much attention to my count, it would result in my trying to find as many boxes as possible as fast as I could, and it took away from the enjoyment and excitement of my experience. (I refer to this as my personal character flaw.) But every once in awhile curiosity gets the best of me and I do count the entries in my journal! Doing so just confirms my suspicion - I love letterboxing and all that it involves. So.... thank you to all you great boxers out there who participate by carving stamps, hiding boxes, writing clues, searching for each others boxes and encouraging each other. I can't wait to meet a big 'ol bunch of you at our gathering in March!
Puddle Splasher
