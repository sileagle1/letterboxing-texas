Just a few notes - as I see it.....

Geocaching promotes a program called

CACHE IN - TRASH OUT.

Or CITO.

Like any group of people - some are fanatical about it, some practice it in
moderation, and some ignore it. But the program is somewhat visible, and
many parks know about it. It seems to help get caches approved - knowing
that the cache owner and visitors practice CITO.

Here is something else for you-all to consider.

http://www.petcarecentral.com/kj-00580.html

This little item comes in several colors. If you fold carefully and pack it
tight, it will hold more than 30 Wal-Mart shopping bags. It is made to
strap to your dog leash for scooping after your dog. I have one of these
little carriers strapped to each of my packs. So whenever I am out, I have
a ready supply of trash bags at hand.

I don't like blue Wal-Mart bags, so I found a store using tan bags and I
begged them for some. When the manager heard what I wanted them for, he
donated a box of 1000 bags.

The rangers really appreciate seeing me come in from a hike hauling a bag of
trash. Every now and then I find a big pile of trash - too much to haul. I
bag it all, then mark the coordinates and send the rangers out to pick it
up. They appreciate the effort.

The larger ANY organization gets, the more it gets noticed.  For good or bad.  I believe it it better to be up front about it, and to prove your value BEFORE they discover you on their own. 
 
When a ranger walks in and says "Guess what I just found", and another ranger says "Yeah - nice people - picking up trash around the park too"  instead of "Who?  What?" - it makes a better impression.
 
I have always believed in getting permission before placing a cache (or letterbox).  Most parks are more than willing to work with you, but it gives them the opportunity to steer you away from protected areas that you might otherwise not know about.
 
There ARE regulations about Geocaching (TYPE) games (including letterboxing by style if not by name) in Texas state parks.  It is a long story....  but I can tell you WHY, and because of WHOM these rules were worked up.  The rules are not prohibitive, nor are they unfair.
 
Being up front about it has gotten Geocaching a very clean name in my area.  I am just getting them introduced to Letterboxing.  So far - the rangers approve.  And what the rangers approve rarely gets disapproved at HQ.
 
Mike.
