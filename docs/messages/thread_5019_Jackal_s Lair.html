> I guess great minds think alike. We were just talking this morning about the retirement of the Circus and starting a new traveling box. The first is that, in my opinion, there should be a size limit for the stamps. There are some really large Circus stamps and that just makes the box fill up faster. I know there were some published rules or guidelines for the first two buses, such as prompt notification when a box is found so that folks won't travel a great distance only to find that the box has already been picked up. I was considering a trip when Bell Motel reported her find, and I appreciated the notification, but that hasn't always been the case, so maybe we should revisit the rules. I know the traveling boxes have owners, but I think all of us feel like partial owners and feel the responsibility of ownership. One other point, and I'd like to hear other opinions on this, is that the first two boxes counted as one find for every stamp, so you could really pad your find stats by finding the box. I think that generated a lot of excitement and was a good reason to go out of your way to grab the box. For the circus box, all of those 42 stamps only count as one find. That's a lot of work for one find. What's your opinion?
