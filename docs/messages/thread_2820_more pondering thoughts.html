From: Susan Tashiro (stashiro@hughes.net) | Date: 2007-06-11 08:07:02 UTC-05:00
 
Things I love about Texas:  Max's Cafe in Tilden, the Nueces River (former Texas/Mexico border),  the fact that Texas once was a republic (maybe this is one reason why we are such proud Texans),  the Texas Flag can fly at the same height as the US flag, our State Capital is taller than the Nation's Capital, pump jacks, drilling rigs, sage brush (Cenizo), mesquite trees, mockingbirds, road runners, longhorns, Santa Gertrudis cattle developed on the King Ranch, the King Ranch, Padre Island, the multi cultural history, The King's Inn at Rivera, the vast spaces, white tail deer, wild ferral hogs, mountain lions, Choke Canyon Lake & State Park (awesome bass and catfishing), Zella, Crowther & Wentz (ghost towns near me), Guajilla honey, prickly pear blooms,  the missions at Goliad, the San Antonio missions, San Antonio, San Caja mountain, Corpus Christi (the T-heads, the marinas, walking along Shoreline Dr.),  Rockport, Fulton, Garner State Park,  Uvalde and the John Nancy "Cactus Jack" Garner (VP under Franklin Roosevelt) Museum, Con Can, the Frio River, tubing the Guadalupe River, Schlitterbahn, driving River Road along the Guadalupe, Gruene, the Grist Mill Restaurant at Gruene, cowboys in tight Wranglers, eating lunch in the cafe at the weekly livestock auction, rodeo's, ranch rodeo's, horses, the SA SPURS!, Bussy's Flea Market, Hwy. 16 Flea Market...
 
You know, when it comes down to it, there isn't much I don't like about Texas...except maybe the mosquitos and gnats.
 
CalicoRose
Proud Texan
