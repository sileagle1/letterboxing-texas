Howdy!
 
About the "Caduceus" letterbox - I feel better now that I know it's missing.  I just thought I had goofed in translating the Latin clues when I couldn't find the box.  Speaking of Latin, I look forward to reading the clues for the Aduceus-Cay box.
 
I've just started letterboxing.  I was directed to the letterboxing.org website from a link on the web page for the National Treasure movie.   (In the film Nicholas Gage's character finds one of his clues which is a hidden stamp inside a smoker's pipe.  He stamps an image of words onto paper - but since he has no ink, he cuts himself and uses his own blood to stamp the image instead.) 
 
Once I got to the letterboxing.org site, I was intrigued by everything I was reading.  I've tried finding 4 boxes so far but was only able to locate 1 box this past November before all the rain. That was exciting - so I'm glad to see the updates and hopefully will be able to find more this weekend!  Wish me luck!
 
I see that I really need to get a compass to use for some of the clues and I don't know the first thing about them.   Anyone have any recommendations for a rookie?  Where does one buy a compass?  Is digital better than the regular kind?
 
Miss Chris

From: Hawk (omkarmashanti@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-12-10 09:11:38 UTC-08:00
Glad to have another letterboxer in Texas!!!!

I plan to replace the boxes that are "missing", but it
may take me a couple of weeks. I am thinking about
moving the Caduceus box, as parking is a problem, and
the groundskeepers seem to be rather...diligent. It
looks as though the box disappeared during regular
grounds maintenance.

As far as compasses go, just go to an Academy store.
Get the regular kind of compass with a needle. They
aren't expensive, and they are very simple to work.
Instructions for using it are usually printed on the
back. If those instructions seem like Greek to you
(and they often will), just drop me a note and I'll
give you the Hawk version, which is simplified for
letterboxing. Or....perhaps we could meet and exchange
stamps, and I'll show you how to use it while we find
a box!

Hawk
