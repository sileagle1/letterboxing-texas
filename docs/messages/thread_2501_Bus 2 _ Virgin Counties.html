From: Wisconsin Hiker (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2007-01-13 18:39:15 UTC
Hi!

We encouraged a similar "populate the counties" project in Wisconsin
last year. I started publicizing the project mid-year. At that time
there were boxes in 42 counties and 30 counties without a box. By mid-
December there was a box in every WI county! (I know Texas is a MUCH
larger state, so it might take a bit longer!). We had some help from
people in neighboring Minnesota who planted boxes in WI counties
across the border from them.

Since the LBNA webmasters are busy with a variety of things, they
only update the maps periodically. I therefore created my own map
that actually showed the names of the counties and colored in the
ones that had boxes. It was a somewhat crudely hand-colored map, but
I could scan it and update it as soon as a new county was populated.
I think it kept up some awareness of the project. If you want to see
what it looked like before we had the last county populated, you can
see it here: http://letterboxing.homestead.com/WIcounties.html

Good luck!

Wisconsin Hiker

RE: [letterboxingtexas] Re: Bus 2 & Virgin Counties

From: Debbie Kotlarek (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2007-01-13 15:50:54 UTC-06:00
Well, that was an interesting question that required a little research. I'd say that there are probably only 40 fairly active boxers/groups in Wisconsin (meaning that they find and place boxes more than on a 1-shot basis).  Most of the boxers are in the southeast part of the state, generally in the Milwaukee area.  There are a few in the the NE and a few in the central part of WI.  The western part of the state doesn't seem to have had many boxers at all, but a few seem to be emerging recently. 
 
However when I recently picked up a full logbook it is obvious that there are others that have been out finding a few boxes - there are many names I've never seen on the message boards or as planters, so it is hard to really say how many people are boxing in the state. 
 
There's a lot of overlap of people in our Great Lakes region, with many of us boxing "over the border" and there has been very good turnout at recent gatherings in the region (80-120 people), so we generally think of ourselves as "Great Lakes" boxers rather than WI boxers.  People from bordering states will often also plant boxes across the border.  There are a lot more active boxers if we consider the whole region.
 
I'd say the growth has been somewhat steady over the past 3.5 years and we have a good variety of boxes.  There are several of us who enjoy some tricky clues and mystery boxes, we've placed some ourselves that are 4-9 mile hikes and there are some drive-bys.  I'd say that generally most involve at least a bit of a walk/hike, not simply jumping out of the car to retrieve the box.  Also not many truly urban boxes.
 
Most people in the Great Lakes region (WI, MN, IL, MI, IN, OH) seem to really enjoy participating in our regional message board (418 members).  Almost all list their clues on LBNA.  A few newer people participate regularly on the AtlasQuest boards and also post their boxes on that site.  It is rare in this area that a box in this region is posted ONLY on AQ (except when Ryan and Amanda from Seattle placed some boxes here recently).
 
So, that's a summary based on my knowledge of our part of the world!

Wisconsin Hiker

Well, MI was a little ahead of us in getting counties populated, but then they started a "friendly competition" with us to see who would have all the counties populated first.  They won by a few weeks, but I feel we were all winners since we all now have more boxes to find!  I'd do the same thing again - post a message whenever a new county got a box, with a link to the map and encouragement for more planting in the boxless counties if someone would be in the area.
 
IL has a similar situation as WI - most of the boxers are concentrated near the largest city in the state.  There has been some talk about another competition between IL and IN or MN to see who can get their counties filled.  I owe MN a few plants since they helped us out.
 
If anyone is interested in keeping a personal map of coloring TX counties, here are two links to named county maps:
 
http://ftp2.census.gov/geo/maps/general_ref/stco_outline/cen2k_pgsz/stco_TX.pdf
 
http://quickfacts.census.gov/qfd/maps/texas_map.html
 
I really think it is helpful to see a map colored in and see what counties are near each other.   I posted regular updates with the map, so people could decide if they were heading to an area, what counties were adjacent to each other.  That way it worked out that someone could cover 2-5 counties at a time.
 
I was curious, so I did a little more research.  TX is about 4 times as large as WI and has 3.5 times as many counties (254 TX counties vs. 72 WI counties).  You definitely have a lot bigger project to tackle, although I don't know what % are already populated.
 
Yeah, it can get cold up here, but we've been having a pretty mild winter so far.  Only snowed one day in early DEC and melted fairly quickly.  We've actually had abnormally warm temperatures  (but this doesn't mean it is actually WARM).  Luckily this winter I am working on a project that is in California, so will be spending a fair amount of time there JAN-MAR.  Enjoyed some temps in the 60s this week, not to mention palm trees and flowers!!
