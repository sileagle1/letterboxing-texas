List security

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-09-15 21:09:14 UTC-07:00
Here's a topic I've been pondering for quite awhile,
and I thought that as our list grows, it is a great
time to bring up the idea of list security. Let's
think of this as setting up a neighborhood watch.

I want you all to know that I don't intend or desire
to alarm anyone or make anyone paranoid, but I really,
truly do worry about personal safety and how a list
like this potentially affects safety. As the list
owner, I feel a certain amount of responsibility for
exercising as much alertness as I can so that the list
is "safe" for all the members.

First of all, I'm not going to get into the specific
ramifications of what could happen if bad guys were to
use our list. I frankly think the risk is relatively
low, although it IS there, so that I prefer not to
dwell too much on dramatizing the risk. If anyone
feels we need to speak those risks, we can open that
up in a different thread, but I would like to see any
discussion of specific risks stay constructive and
non-alarmist.

One of my biggest concerns with a list like this is
that we, by virtue of the fact that we are a
geographically-based group who share a
geographically-based interest, tell a little more
about ourselves and our locations than we would
typically share on the internet. Couple that fact
with the fact that letterboxing can, and often is, an
isolated activity, I worry that this list could be
targeted by someone with "non-letterboxing intent."
Fortunately, we are still small enough in number that
somebody knows somebody who knows or has met most
everyone here.

Do you wonder by now what we do to keep the list safe?

Well, first of all, I'm going to speak as "I" most of
the time, because I don't want to imply that what I
think is necessarily what Silver Eagle thinks. He
can, and I'm sure will, speak well enough for himself!

Silver Eagle and I look at each request to join this
list to see why the requestor wants to join.
Admittedly, we (or at least I) aren't terribly picky,
but I want to see that the reason given sounds
letterboxerish (letterboxerish, non-letterboxing
intent??? Don't y'all love these terms I'm coming up
with?). Also, when the member is accepted, they get a
welcome letter (most of y'all got it, I'm sure!)
asking them to post an introduction. Hopefully,
they'll get that intro out there, because that is the
list membership's opportunity to meet someone and size
them up and to know who is out there reading their
posts. Members are more than welcome to fade into
lurkerdom if they so desire, but I sure like to see
that intro at the very least.

I've considered whether we should bounce members who
don't post intros within, say, 30 days. I hate to do
that, but at the same time, I think I owe it to the
active members to help them know their co-list
members.

I would ask that if you invite someone to join our
list, you should email me or Silver Eagle and let us
know to expect your friend (or better yet, send us the
email address and we'll invite them!), or at least
tell your friend to use your name in their reason to
join (kind of like a secret knock!!!).

Do any of you have thoughts about all of this? Ideas
about how we, as moderators, can be more diligent in
our gatekeeping? Or how we all, as members, can be
more conscious of list security?

This is a little rambly, I know, but I wanted to get
y'all thinking about how you would like to see our
little neighborhood protected....

Lucy

LOL Monkeytoes, AMEN! Caffeine - it does a body good!

We certainly want anyone to be able to FIND the list,
and don't want to be exclusive by any means, except
that the members of the list need to have a general
interest in letterboxing, whether they are just
curious about it after reading an article, or are
serious letterboxers in Texas, or have relatives or
ties to Texas and letterbox elsewhere - we have all
kinds on this list, and I think that's great! DON'T
take the link to the list off your webpages!

I have no problems with lurkers-I have some lists I
belong to that I rarely speak up on - I glean a lot,
but contribute little. However, I don't think it is
inappropriate (and I lean strongly toward saying it is
good manners) to tell the group you are out there.
Think of it as a conference call - when you sign into
a big telephone meeting, you may never say a word
during the meeting, but when you join, you are at
least expected to say your name and "who you are".

The "reason for joining" is a relatively new yahoo
feature that just appeared out of nowhere not too long
ago. I love it! But no, you probably joined before
that feature was added.

Here's one of the reasons I think security is
something more than miniscule but less than gargantuan
to take into account: even though the list is closed
and you must theoretically join to see the posts, it
is possible to google a member's name and find their
activity on a list and even pull up posts. Those
searches seem to be pretty transient, since the same
google results I got on myself last week are not the
same ones I got just now (I truly am NOT this
egocentric, but I have an ongoing curiosity about how
it works) At any rate, it is very possible for
someone to stumble into our group without even a
slight interest in letterboxing.

Case in point, this very thing happened to me
personally. I had chatted casually with someone from
another interest of mine for a little while, but I had
a series of computer problems and wasn't able to
communicate with them for awhile and never got back to
it again. This person googled my screen name and
stumbled into the big list and subsequently joined
that list and this one. They sent me an email saying
"they knew I still used my email address because they
had observed my activity on the letterboxing lists."
I have never figured out who they are or even if they
are still on this list, but the extent they went to to
track me down was disproportionate to the nature of
our previous correspondence, and that's a little
unnerving!

Because talking about area boxes and talking about
possible plans is part of what makes a list like this
dynamic and lively and keeps people in the flow of
local letterboxing, I think that it would be difficult
to say "don't talk about who you are or where you live
or what you are up to". The key has to be in closely
monitoring the membership. Needless to say, list
security is only a start to maintaining personal
safety and each person has to be responsible for their
own wellbeing once they are in the field, but that job
would be easier if we can be more assured about who
knows what to begin with.

So there, that's a little more of where this line of
thought comes from for me. I want to stress still
that I don't see it as a monstrous problem, but I
think now is a good time for all of you as members to
really think about it and have some input into how you
want to see it addressed by your moderators. As the
list grows, it becomes more difficult to say "oh yeah,
I know MonkeyToes, he's that really energetic guy,
isn't he?"

Lucy (who is FINALLY finishing a cup of coffee)

That's my point though (obviously not well made!), I
wouldn't want the list to NOT be able to share
personal information! BUT, if everyone is to feel
comfortable sharing personal information, they should
be able to know who is listening to them.

I worry less about the lurker who has been on the list
for a few weeks than about the person who joined and
has never, in several months, even said "hello". I
also don't worry about the lurker who says "hello" and
then fades into the wallpaper, never to speak again.
Everyone has their own motivation for being on a list,
and even MOST of those who fit into the "never spoke,
been here awhile" category are not a problem. It's
those few, like the one I related in an earlier post,
that concern me.

I guess I kind of equate it to a community. What
would you think if you went to a gathering and some
guy showed up. Nobody knew who he was, he didn't
introduce himself, he queued up in the food line, but
nobody saw him doing the stamping frenzy. He never
introduced himself to anyone, never talked to anyone,
but he sure hung out with the group. Frankly, I
personally would be pretty leery by the end of the
day, and if I were to run into him in the woods, I
would have to worry a little. I'm betting some of you
would have an interesting way of dealing with that
situation, and I'd love to know what it is!

disagree with you, I do think I got more than .02
worth!
