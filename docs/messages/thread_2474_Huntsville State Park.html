From: Boots Tex (redbarn@eastex.net) | Date: 2009-11-09 17:21:06 UTC
At one time, not too long ago, Huntsville State Park had a reputation of being "Anti-Letterbox", when in reality, they were unaware of the existence of letterboxes. I have gone through a long and sometimes frustating process with them in the last 4 months and now they are aware that letterboxes exist in their park and have appointed a ranger to be their letterbox coordinator. I have permitted four boxes there. If you have a letterbox in Huntsville State Park and want to get it permitted, you can email them at:

Chrissy.Halpen@tpwd.state.tx.us

Tell her you want to permit your box and give her the name of the box. She'll email you the application and she'll go find the box to make sure it's in a safe place. She told me that they will not remove any box but will notify you if they become aware of its existence and ask you to permit it.

If you want to place a box there, ask for a letterbox permit application when you enter the park. If they say, "What's a letterbox", ask for Chrissy Halpen.

Boots Tex
