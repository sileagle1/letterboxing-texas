From: Mike Olbrisch (mike-2007@elp.rr.com) | Date: 2009-10-05 19:56:25 UTC-06:00

First - let me explain something. I got a few questions about this - so to help
you better understand.

We entered River and I at the Alamogordo Dog shows for PRACTICE. The idea was
to give River some experience in the ring, and to get me back into it too.
Since Parker couldn't compete the last 3 years I had him, I had lost my edge
also. And I didn't want to Break Parker's heart by training up another dog
while Parker was still alive. I have one dog - we are devoted to each other
until death.

I hoped that River would like being at the shows (he did) and would not be
afraid in the ring (he was not). I had a very small spark of hope that maybe by
day three we might get in a qualifying score - and with some practice maybe even
enter some other shows later in the year. What I did not expect was to have a
dog so sharp that he was qualifying right from the start. Not from a 1-year old
puppy - the youngest dog competing that weekend. It was supposed to be fun...
Just practice!

Having said all of this - I could not be prouder of River. Not only did he give
me two wins the first two days - he also earned his Rally Novice Title on the
third day. His first time in the ring - his first real entry - and we did 3
runs and finished.

The competition was very tough on Sunday. There were 8 in the class, some other
people showed up for Sunday's trials. And they were good. And River was not so
good. We started late, it was very windy, I didn't let River rest - I maybe
walked him too much, and I could go on making excuses. But the fact is, River
scored an 84. Yes - an 84. The very score he WON with on Friday didn't even
get in the running on Sunday. But an 84 is passing, and we did pick up a green
ribbon. The judge told me River did very well for such a young pup. Then when
she found out he completed his title already - she was shocked.

So - he is now.

Von Bonehenge white River of Dreams - RN, CGC, SP.

How can you not be proud of that???

I gotta add - he liked the shows so much that he left half of his breakfast in
the dish and raced outside to jump in the van. He sat in the van for 30 minutes
before we left. He didn't want to be left at home. Silly puppy, I want you
with me where-ever I can take you. It is us two together - until death. You
cudda finished your breakfast - I would have waited for you.

Watch for a DOG-BLOG in the coming week.
