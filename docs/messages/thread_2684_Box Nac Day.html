Box Nac Day

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-03-31 21:13:00 UTC-05:00
What dawned as an ugly day turned out beautifully! Thankfully, letterboxers
are hardy souls, so no one was put off by the stormy weather. While we had
clouds most of the day, we had only occasional very light showers and the
temperature was as perfect as we could have hoped.

Lightning and I hit the door about 8:45 after waiting out the rain for a bit
and checking out the weather. We met TrailCat at the Old North Church about
10:00. Lightning went and explored the big part of the cemetery while
TrailCat found Tres Cruces in the older part, which she had never realized
was there.

We went from there to Millard's Crossing, which was deserted for once, and
quickly found Little Church at Millard's Crossing with a very nice stamp by
Josephine. Lightning insisted on exploring some of the little buildings
there, so we did a bit of a history tour with him. That's a neat little
village, if you ever get a chance to go there while visiting Nacogdoches.

Re: Box Nac Day

From: SandiBox (sandibox@msn.com) | Date: 2007-04-02 00:47:16 UTC
We also had a wonderful day boxing in Nac. Thanks to Barefoot Lucy
for coordinating the not-a-gathering, and to all of the placers for
their great boxes!

The day was off to a late start when my 20-year old alarm clock
didn't go off at the set time of 5:15 a.m. This clock is so old that
it has fingernail grooves in the buttons to set the hour and minute.
And regardless of the set time, it now only seems to go off at 6:00
a.m. (my regular weekday wake-up time). Whether or not I actually
turn on the alarm, it goes off at 6:00 a.m. weekends and holidays
included.

When I woke up at 6:00 a.m. (the time I should have been leaving the
house), the first things to cross my consciousness were a loud clap
of thunder and bright flash of lightening. That couldn't be good. I
quickly turned on the TV and found a news report promising that,
although severe, the storm would move quickly through the area
leaving only an 80% chance of light rain for the rest of the day.
Like Lucy said, we letterboxers are a hardy bunch, so we decided that
the trip was still a go. After showering and dressing in record
time, I called Runs for Chocolate to let her know we were running
late. Unfortunately she was already on her way to our pre-arranged
meeting location, so she had to wait for almost an hour while I
navigated my way across Houston, past several weather-related traffic
accidents. Sorry RFC!

We finally headed toward Nacogdoches, with SoccerChik blissfully
sleeping in the back seat while RFC and I happily munched on her
amazing pumpkin-chocolate chip mini-muffins and tried to solve the
clues for the Marvels letterbox. We reached Nac around 10:00 and
being rather OCD in my boxing style, I had the day planned out we
would start with the out-lying areas, then go to the north side of
town and work our way south.

We woke up SoccerChik when we reached the SFA Experimental Forest.
This is a beautiful little park with a very nice trail system that
RFC thinks she'd like to revisit for a workout. Look for us in the
visitor's log. We enjoyed the natural beauty of the forest while
locating Lucy Locket's "Vivaldi's Winter", Baby Bear's "Letterboxing
So Easy a Caveman Can Do It", and Silver Eagle's "Sam-I-Am" (where
we picked up the Tickastic HH). We got a little wet from a very
brief sprinkling of rain, but then the sun came out and the rest of
the day was perfect letterboxing weather.

Next we headed over to Lake Nacogdoches and picked up Ashby's "Peace
Now" and "Dragon by the Lake" -- both very nice boxes with sweet
dedications to some important folks in her life. We took a few
minutes to enjoy the scenic overlook of the lake and take a couple of
photos (in spite of our having humidity inspired hair-do's in all of
them).

Our next stop was at the Old North Church where we convinced
SoccerChik to overcome her cemetery phobia and found Blue
Butterfly's "Tres Cruces" while learning some fascinating history
about the church and cemetery from the historical plaque and grave
markers. We briefly stopped at the Millard's Crossing historical
village, but were unable to retrieve the box because a tour had just
started. We decided to come back later and headed to Maroney Park to
find Ashby's "Krewe d'etat". Our only question: Do they publish an
instruction book for those exercise stations? LOL! Hoping enough
time had passed, we went back to the historical village to
attempt "Little Church at Millard's Crossing". The tour was just
finishing and we decided to grab the box and stamp-in in the car.
RFC ran over to the church, wrestled with a 5-pound chunk of
concrete, and tried to be discrete dashing back to the car with a
rather large plastic box in-hand, while SoccerChik and I howled with
laughter at her antics. I tried to snap a photo of her running to
the car, but was laughing too hard to hold the camera steady. Sorry
to laugh at your expense RFC, but it was priceless! Her efforts were
well worth it though, as the carving by Josephine was superb! As
much as we wanted to stay and explore the quaint little village, we
were on a mission. We swung by Pecan Park, and unsuccessfully tried
to find Atoy Adventurer's "Pecan Paradise", but enjoyed watching the
co-eds play Frisbee golf.

Our next stop was SFA main campus where we discovered the campus was
overrun with visitors and since we were unable to look for Atoy
Adventurers' "Charlotte's Web" or "SFA Flower Power" due to the
crowds we stopped for a quick lunch. Afterward we found the area for
Barefoot Lucy's "Go Jacks!" to be deserted, but alas, no box. At the
SFA Arboretum we were pleasantly surprised to discover such a
fabulous little sanctuary. We ooh'd and aah'd over the orange
honeysuckle that smelled just like Juicy Fruit gum and the banana
bush with a sweet heavenly fragrance that is so exquisite as to be
indescribable. We were afraid that we'd have to take RFC to the
emergency room for inhaling too many banana blossoms! Although the
park was busy, we were able to look for "Texas Blueberry Festival"
planted by Lucy Locket, but were not able to locate the box.

Since we still had a little time before meeting everyone else, we
drove to the Ruby M. Mize Azalea Garden only to find the parking lot
full, and to realize that we could have simply crossed a bridge from
the arboretum. We returned to the arboretum and entered the garden
where we were overwhelmed by the magnificent display of Texas'
largest azalea garden in full bloom. The dedication plaque aptly
exclaims "The kiss of the sun for pardon, the song of the birds for
mirth; One's nearer God's heart in a garden, than anywhere else on
earth." We wandered around for a while in awe of the incredible
variety of color before looking for the two boxes Enaj planted
there. When we arrived at the spot where we knew "Confetti" box was
located, there were a couple of women taking photos of two little
girls, but we were relieved to hear them say they had run out of
film. "Gee, that's too bad see ya!" But they really wanted a few
more shots. I suggested they run to the nearest drugstore for more
film. "See ya!" But they prefer to send their film to one of those
places that returns free film with their photos. Maybe they could go
buy a disposable camera? "See ya!" But they didn't trust those.
Sigh! What if I use my digital camera to take a couple of pictures
and email them to you? Great idea! I strategically positioned the
little girls and distracted the women away from the box while RFC and
SoccerChik retrieved it and stamped in. Yeah! Next we found the
tree where "Monkey Business" box was hidden. Fortunately there was
no one around, so SoccerChik and RFC took turns crawling under the
azalea bushes to look for the box while I kept lookout for muggles.
Neither one could find it, and since we couldn't come up with a
convincing cover story for WHY they were crawling under the bushes,
we decided to give up before someone came along.

By then it was time to meet everyone at Java Jack's. It was really
nice to meet those east Texas boxers whose names we've heard, but
haven't previously had the pleasure to meet: JBKokopelli, Ashby,
Josephine and Trail Cat. It was also nice to meet new boxer Dave and
see him take such an interest in learning about this hobby/obsession
of ours. And of course seeing Barefoot Lucy and Gryzzled Gryphon is
like seeing old friends again. Now Lucy's already told you about
some of the methods we employed in trying to get Green Shirt Guy to
leave, but she neglected to mention a couple of the suggestions that
were discarded: Instead of sending SoccerChik over to flirt with GS
Guy, we thought perhaps Mr. Gryphon should go flirt with him (GG
declined to participate in that particular suggestion). Or we could
have started throwing things around the room (food fight anyone?).
In the end we decided that even if GS Guy learned about letterboxing,
he would never make a very good boxer because he simply couldn't take
a subtle (or not so subtle) hint.

After leaving Java Jack's, we wanted to try the Lanana Creek Trail
letterboxes before it got too dark. We stopped by the soccer fields
for Trail Cat's "Happy Trails to You" and almost lost SoccerChik who
wanted to find a pick-up game. Then we went back to Liberty Hall
where a party was in full swing. The food smelled incredible, and we
tried to get SoccerChik to use her improving Spanish language skills
to get us invited to dinner, but she wasn't cooperating. So we
headed down the trail and quickly found "Naturally Musical"
and "Vivaldi's Summer" where we replaced the container and added a
new logbook for Lucy Locket. We continued down the trail to Zion
Hill Baptist Church cemetery where we found Ashby's "Number Four".
As we were stamping in, we heard what sounded like a child laughing,
but didn't see anyone around. I said maybe it was a ghost, since we
were at a cemetery. (Dumb of me, now I'll NEVER get SoccerChik to
another cemetery box!) It was rapidly approaching dusk, so we
finished up in a hurry and headed back toward the car. We were
walking side-by-side on the trail talking about the day, when
suddenly SoccerChik screamed! I looked at her and saw a man standing
RIGHT behind her I screamed Runs for Chocolate screamed. Turns out
it was just a college student out for his evening run (and since we
were blocking the entire trail he tapped SoccerChik on the shoulder
and said "excuse me"...causing the uproar). The poor guy apologized
all over himself "I'm sorry I'm SO sorry I'm REALLY sorry!"
After he went by we got the giggles. We were still laughing when a
few minutes later our friendly jogger had reached the end of the
trail and was returning in our direction. He was wearing a red
shirt, so we saw him from quite a distance, but even so he starting
calling out to us from about 50 yards away. "It's okay It's me
again I'm coming back by." Poor thing - we scared him as much as
he scared us.

Great weather Great boxes Great company... Great fun. We had such
a wonderful time in Nacogdoches we can't wait to return and explore
more of the town and hopefully more boxes will be planted by then.
Thanks again to everyone who contributed their time and talents for
our girl's day out!
