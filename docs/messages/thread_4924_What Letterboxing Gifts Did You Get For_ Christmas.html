I received the gift of friendship this year.  On the night before Christmas Eve, Cancan threw a wonderful Christmas letterboxing bash where at least 20 of us gathered to exchange Christmas stamp images into little booklets that fit into Christmas ornaments that Cancan had made for each of us.  In October, I was in Houston accompanying my Mom to MD Anderson because her cancer has returned.  I got away to Telge park, to pick up a few Wedding Guests, and had just begun to stamp in my first box when I heard my name called!  It was Dewberry and Mother of Five, who invited me to join them for lunch, where we talked and laughed about anything and everything but cancer – a wonderful surprise gift.  I got funny, clever emails all year long from clever, funny people.  I am realizing that one of the reasons I love this letterboxing “club” is that is doesn’t ask anything from me, it only gives.  I am not required to give the educational program “next month”, or single-handedly bring food for 40 to the next “meeting”.  I can just know that I belong to a great group of people who keep giving me gifts all year long.  Merry Christmas and Happy New Year to you all!  Love, Bell Motel

I laid out a survival plan; part of it included getting outdoors more. Letterboxing became a catalyst for that. There was a lot of solace to be found in a pretty trail or a good find.

Time has passed, and what a blessing to be where I am now in this journey. Enjoy the rest of the holidays with your friends and families - you may not realize it, but this community and the boxes you've placed have been part of a healing process for me. Needless to say, I'm grateful.
