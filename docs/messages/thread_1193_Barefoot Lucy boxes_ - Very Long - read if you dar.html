Some people fly to the Bahamas for a weekend in the sun - some folks fly to
the Cape for a little cooler seascape...
Stillwater, Vandal & I spent the weekend driving around East Texas in the
Jacksonville-Rusk area and you know what? I don't think the Bahamas or Cape
Cod can outdo that area for absolute charm. (Or maybe it's just that the
letterboxers and their boxes are charming and it swayed our judgment???)

About a year ago when we were very new to letterboxing, I read the clues to
barefoot Lucy's "Killough Monument" box - the story just hooked me - I
couldn't wait to go looking for it. And then it went missing, and I've been
checking on it from time to time ever since. So when I knew we were going to
be in the area, I wrote to Barefoot Lucy to ask if she had by any chance
replaced it yet...oh, okay...and I shamelessly hinted how cool it would be
if we could find it on Saturday!!!
Well, she did indeed replace it for us (and I can only imagine what she had
to shuffle in her schedule to do that - 8:00 am on a Saturday morning). It
was awesome in the literal sense of the word. Her story clues came alive for
me and I could picture everything that happened there. This is no ordinary
monument, in my opinion. The stamp, of course, was great - Lucy's always are
- and every time I look at that image, the poignant feelings of admiration
and grief for those settlers will come rushing back.
Another of the Footwear Philosopher's boxes has become Stillwater's absolute
favorite box - and he's pretty stingy with superlatives. This was "Spring
Hope", and he was STILL talking about it later that night when we had the
great good fortune to have a coffee & chat with Lucy at a new coffeehouse in
Jacksonville. Great coffee drinks, terrific live music and of course, the
company couldn't have been better! Makes me look forward to the Barn Raising
even more!
The following day we drove down to Rusk and found Lucy's "Starry, Starry
Night" - very lovely stamp & interesting spot. We spotted a HUGE toad and
gigantic butterflies along the trail.

Our plan had been to drive back home through Jacksonville because we had a
special box to plant in Barefoot Lucy's honor - and by that time we figured
we might have driven around enough locations to find an appropriate
spot...just as we were driving around the lake, a really terrifying storm
whipped up out of nowhere - I truly thought there might be tornadoes forming
- and the rain began pouring down in sheets. We couldn't afford to wait
around for the storm to pass because we had to get home to our youngest.

We could hardly believe that we had to bring Barefoot Lucy's box all the way
home again! So...we're hoping we can beg a favor of another Jacksonville
boxer to allow us to mail the box to her to place for us!

Ruby Tuesday
