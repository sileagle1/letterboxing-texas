Never Guess What I Found

From: (wry_me@yahoo.com) | Date: 2014-01-20 14:23:47 UTC-08:00

I found a trackable in one of my Challenger 7 boxes today.  What the hell is that doing there?  So I sent it spinning, end over end and glinting merrily in the beautiful balmy sunshine to land with a gratifying spe-lunk! in the pond.  What's that you say?  That's not how those are supposed to be handled?  Oh, dear.  What a stupid rule.  Well, I don't really agree with that rule, and even though it's not really MY trackable, well, I'm not following rules I personally think are pointless.  Right?  Right?


Oh, I suppose you're be relieved that instead I sent the last person who logged it a friendly email, once I figured out how to do that on geocaching.com, thanking them profusely for not buggering up one of the boxes I spent a year, off and on, researching, by swapping the stamp out.  I couldn't find the cache it SHOULD have been it (wasn't that nice of me?), though I've long known where it is.  It has gone missing.  Hence, my box, or yay, ANY box, clearly labeled "NOT A GEOCACHE" or not, will suffice. Nor can I figure out what they were doing 60+ feet from that location, because I am just not smart enough for that hobby, but my tolerance knows no bounds, so I notified them that I had it and asked for guidance on getting it back to them.  Now I primp around the house, egged on by my over-developed sense of swellness.  Sigh.  Isn't it wonderful?!!!!!  Seriously, though, it was kinda cool that the thing originated in Germany.  I have a soft spot for Germany.  Some people call it a beer belly, but that's not polite.
