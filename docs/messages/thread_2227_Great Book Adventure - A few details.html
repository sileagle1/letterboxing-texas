Well, your hostesses were out in Tomball again this morning, and one
thing to report is that our recent rains have brought the mosquitos
out in force! Bring spray.

The Pavilion is in the very back of the park, if you keep driving,
you'll find it easily. There are volleyball nets and an area for
recreation. If someone wants to bring balls and get a game going,
they are welcome to. There is also a catch and release small lake
(pond really) stocked with rainbow trout, we always see lots of kids
fishing out there. You are required to follow the TPWD fishing regs
if you want to fish.
http://www.hcp4.net/parks/burroughs/troutstocking.htm

To be honest, the trails are NOT very well marked, meaning that they
do not have individual names or colors. There are lots of signs that
direct you back to the parking area, so that if you were to become a
bit disoriented, you can easily make your way back.

Most of the trails are stroller friendly, there is one that you have
to go uphill through sand where a stroller would be tough. Good shoes
are a smart idea, as is an adequate amount of water.

So far, they're forecasting pretty good weather for next weekend. The
pavilion is rather large, so it will provide great shelter in the
event of a passing shower.

If someone wants to grill, there are barbecue pits.

I think that's all. They are hosting a big event out there this
weekend, Sunday in the Park, so we are crossing our fingers that the
boxes already hidden will be okay.

We can't wait to see you all! The stamps are truly amazing and I
think it's going to be great fun for all ages and abilities!
