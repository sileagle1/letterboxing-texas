From: Boots Tex (redbarn@eastex.net) | Date: 2010-08-11 22:20:38 UTC
That would be interesting, but would you have to carry a wrench to open it? :) Seriously, I've seen a lot of your boxes that were plastic jars with screw-on lids and they seem to work fine. But, they too require that the last boxer screw the lid on tightly. I have seen a couple of boxes (not yours) with screw-on lids that were placed upside down and therefore are wet inside. I think in those cases the water would run down around the threads and, over time, seep inside. If the same jar were placed upright, water would drip off the lid. I think you hang most of those from the lid for that reason. Of course, Galveston isn't a forest, so what works in the woods may not work in the sand dunes. Also, oleander is poisonous, and there's a lot of it in Galveston, so be careful of that.

Boots Tex
