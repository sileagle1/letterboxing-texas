From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-10-05 23:42:58 UTC
I loved the clues to this box and happily solved it months ago
(thought it was clever).

What you need to know is that in general, any boxes that aren't t
simple and straightforward, whether puzzle such as Marvels or one with
a longer hike get MUCH less traffic than those with straightforward
clues or a short walk. I have a mystery box not very far away from
another box. I've had to replace the logbook on the non-mystery; the
mystery has only had 5 finders. On another mystery box, people have
to walk past mine to get to another box. But plenty of finders don't
realize that and never stop at mine. I am okay with that, because I
like providing boxes for those who like a challenge. You know that if
you solve it, your reward will be a nice carve that will happily
remind you of your efforts (and it gives you something to do on
laundry day, right Mother of Five?)

If you keep planting mystery boxes, the finders in your area get more
sophisticated with their clue-solving. And, I find that people are
appreciative of the boxes they have to work for a bit, whether through
a hike or a intricate cipher.

But, puzzles and the like are just not for everyone. If you're not
okay with fewer finders, you should definitely change the clues. Each
box has its audience.

As an aside, I hope to plant yet another mystery box tomorrow for
Mystery Box Planting day so look for it--clues to debut on Saturday.

From: the carrascos (a.carrasco@earthlink.net) | Date: 2006-10-05 21:19:09 UTC-05:00
As someone who loves puzzle boxes I can say that when someone does find that box, the stamp will be one of their favorites, a reminder of all the work they went through to solve the clues.  I have stamped some of dewberry's more evil creations in my logbook on several pages, I was so happy to finally be holding the stamp in my hand!  There could be other reasons no one has found it yet, I have a stack of puzzle clues I have solved, but have yet to go and actually find the boxes.  School has started, schedules get busier, and time to go letterboxing becomes limited.  Don't give up on puzzle clues, but don't expect as many finders as on a more simple box.  I have one box that has had only one finder, and may never have another.  That is just fine with me, I placed the box with dewberry in mind, hoping to create a puzzle that would drive her as nuts as hers have done to me.  While as far as I know she is still sane, she did have to work a little bit on the clues, and it was sort of a thank you to her for all her amazing puzzles.  (The box is the "Tree Hugger"..come on Silver Eagle and Sea Dove, I know you two like puzzles too!)  Come on down to Houston and give us some of those great puzzles, we'll find 'em!  MO5

Hey Ashby,
 
I've had one eye on Marvels and I have it on my list of boxes next time I box Nac, but I have been swamped since it was first listed and haven't had a chance to sit down and work out the clues.  In fact, that's one reason I haven't yet gone back and cleaned up Nac (there are about 10 boxes down there now that I don't have) - I wanted to wait until I had a chance to work out Marvels so I could get it while I was getting the others.
 
Don't get discouraged though - as Dewberry and MO5 have told you, puzzle boxes are lower traffic, but higher reward.  The problem I have with puzzle boxes or mystery boxes, especially hard ones, is that once I solve them, the actual finding is kind of anti-climactic.  I solved SP several months ago but have yet to go log it. 
 
Lucy
