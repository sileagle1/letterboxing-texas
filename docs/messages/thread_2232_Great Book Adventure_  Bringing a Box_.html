So, trying to answer some questions from folks. This gets a bit long.
If you have other questions, feel free to email me or post them here!

The Literary Traveler aspect of the event seems to be going well, with
great participation. The easiest way to find the clues for these
travelers is to go to atlasquest.com and find the event (it's now on
the right hand side of the page). When you click on the event (or
follow this link):
http://www.atlasquest.com/events/event.html?gEventId=173
you'll see a list of all of the personal traveler boxes. You'll want
to study the clues for them before hand, because it depends on whether
you've read the book, or can remember reading the book, in question.

I've had some people ask what a traveler is, so a quick definition.
It is a stamp that you carry on your person that you offer to others
when you meet them. Sometimes, it's very secretive, sometimes you
have to say a code word or do something, sometimes all you have to do
is ask. This is a bit different than an exchange, which is usually
when two boxers trade signature stamps.

AQ and letterboxing.info both have good glossaries.

We will be preparing clue packets for all rsvp'ed attendees (or
really, for families) with a few extra. These are the boxes that
we've pre-hidden on the walks and in a special area for children that
we won't be hiding until that day. A few boxes, I've already listed
on AQ, but those boxes aren't in place and won't be until that day,
just the hiding places are set.

Now, if you're bringing a box to hide that day and haven't sent it to
me or Teamking, that's fabulous (but not required). You can feel free
to hide them, either permanently in the park or temporarily for that
day. Even though we've set up the walks, there is still a large area
in the back of the trails that would be great for more boxes.

If you do hide a box, I will have bulletin boards where you will be
able to pin up your clue. Others can copy the clue down. Low-tech,
but workable.

There will be fun surprises. Keep your eyes open. Probably no box on
Mother of Five's license plate for this one, but you never know.
