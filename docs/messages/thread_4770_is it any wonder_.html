is it any wonder?

From: Valerie (brebiz@yahoo.com) | Date: 2011-04-28 02:22:57 UTC
One of my first letterboxing experiences was not very pleasant and I had a tiff with the planter. I was NEW and learned a hard lesson. Now I have over 70 finds, 5 plants plus many postals and a few hitchhikers to my credit. Imagine my surprise when "the one" was in MY town today, finding MY boxes and my SO's single box PLUS he planted 2 new ones...one right in OUR town yet it's restricted so we can't look for it. Is it any wonder we are not as into letterboxing as we could be?

That's just rude. My GF has a premium membership & even she can't see the clues. How would you feel???? I have been geocaching over a year and I started out as a letterboxer but this just reinforces my feeling that at least in NM geocachers are more of a fun group and some of us are like family....that doesn't mean I won't try for the ones in El Paso or Mesilla or even Albuquerque if I happen to be there... but it sure makes me feel like some people think they are better than we are.....

I was going to plant some storebought stamps but why should I? HE can go look for someone elses boxes. There's only a few of us here in town anyways. My geocaches are being found almost every day and I have 40 total plants. I think I'll stick to postals and geocaches.

getting down off my soapbox now....

ladyval

From: Debbie Kotlarek (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2011-04-28 11:14:51 UTC-05:00

There are 2 separate issues.  One is why some people choose to restrict finds (and of course I disagree with many of the reasons). 

 

The second issue is "Seeing" a restricted clue.  If you are on AQ, you can't see the clue unless you meet the criteria, so you don't know it exists.  However when someone posts the link to a restricted clue on the LBNA site, you THINK there will be a clue, only to get to this taunting message.  I feel this is rude.  I therefore believe that if you have a restricted clue you should NOT list it on LBNA.

 

Wisconsin Hiker

From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2011-04-29 01:36:31 UTC
Hi, I don't post here very much. I still letterbox (had a wonderful outing with my friend the other day).

I don't think private tiffs should be aired on public boards. We don't have the whole story and we really don't want to take sides.

If you publicly offer clues, then you are saying that anyone is welcome to come find your boxes, even your "enemy" such as it is. You can't say "everyone but person XYZ." Being a planter can mean lots of things to different people--some people want as many finders as possible, some kind of want as few.

If you choose to restrict your clues, you may do so either through website apps, making them word of mouth (people have been doing this since the beginning of letterboxing) or by being diabolically eeeevil (my choice of restriction). Not every box is for every finder.

For me, when I choose to find boxes, I like a good location, fun clues, and then a great stamp. I won't find a storebought stamp unless there are other redeeming parts to the box (for me, based on my taste), and even then it'll be at the bottom of my list. Luckily for planters of boxes with store bought stamps, some people will happily find them--for them it may be more about number of finds. All this is good--different planters have different audiences for their boxes, if you will.

I don't care about numbers. I think I'm somewhere way north of 1000 finds, but I lost interest in counting long ago. For me, it's more about the experience. Some of my best times have been letterboxing, with my kids as they were tiny and as they've gotten bigger, with my wonderful friends, and even occasionally by myself.

It's not the hobby for everyone, but it is one that I still love and enjoy. I hope you find your own personal joy in it, or if letterboxing isn't for you, that you find that joy in something else, like geocaching.

Best,

Dewberry
