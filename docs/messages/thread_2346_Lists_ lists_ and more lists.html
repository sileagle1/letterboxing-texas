This is a difficult question that our dear Lucy poses to us tonight.  I have just a couple thoughts on the matter, and although they won't be nearly as eloquent as Lucy's, I'll try to make them make sense.
 
When I found my very first letterbox 3 years ago (Brazos Bend, by Silver Eagle) I wrote him and told him about what fun we had, and thanked him for placing the box for us to find.  In his gentlemanly way he wrote back, and asked me to join the Texas boxing list.  I had never been a member of any sort of a list before, but I wanted to learn all I could about letterboxing, so I joined.  Through the list I learned things that I never would have learned otherwise, met some folks with kooky names that now feel like old friends, and on more than one occasion was given wonderful travel itinerates for planned boxing trips with the kids.  In short, without the list I would not have felt so welcome, learned so much, or felt like part of a community.
 
I understand the appeal, and even the need for regional lists.  I happily joined the metroboxers list, mostly because I was missing laughs from posts by Ruby, PuddleSplasher, Viewfinder, and those other crazies up in Dallas.  I know there are other letterboxers in the Houston area, but there are very few who ever participate on the Texas list, so maybe a Houston list would draw them in.  That all being said, I hate for the big list to be so quiet.  How else will we stay connected and feel like "Texas Letterboxers"?  I worry that if regional lists take the place of the Texas list, new boxers my miss out on all that can be gained from hearing from Silver Eagle, Baby Bear, Ruby, Lucy, Teamking, Dewberry, etc.etc...letterboxers who are in it for the "long haul", and who are wonderful, kind mentors. I don't know what the solution to all this is, but I know that at least for me it is difficult to keep track of several lists (remember, I live in total chaos 24 hours a day, there is a reason for my disabilities) and I miss the posts from all the personalities that make up Letterboxing Texas. A Houston list and a Texas list I could keep up with, as long as everyone keeps the Texas list as their main form of communication...If I have to join 8 lists just to keep up with what you all are doing I know for a fact I will end up in the nut house:)  -Mother of Five
