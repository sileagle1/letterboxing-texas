Gorgeous day!

From: (blhjrh@aol.com) | Date: 2007-01-26 22:59:57 UTC-05:00
      After so many days of cold and wet and more cold, today's warm sunshine was an invitation to take a walk in the park.  So, after reading this morning that PuddleSplasher's newest Artist Series boxes had been placed, my kind spouse and I enjoyed a lovely walk on a pretty trail, finding all 4 beautiful letterboxes (with equally artistic logbooks), and had the added pleasure of stamping in while sitting in the sunshine on a pleasant park bench --  AND collecting those FF certificates!  Hooray!   
    Then, after enjoying lunch outside in the sunshine at Jason's Deli in Grapevine, I came home and recorded these finds in my own database and I discovered that I now have 401 finds!  Dali's Persistence of Time was #400, and Munch's The Scream is #401.  Whoo-Hoo!  This is a wonderful series, of course, since PS actually is an artist and her stamps and logbooks are always treasures to enjoy, but the added pleasures of the sunshine, the nice trail, actually being FF for these boxes, and reaching a letterboxing milestone added to our experience today.
    Which brings me back to the recent discussion of logbooks.   
    I consider that PuddleSplasher's logbooks are always excellent. She cuts the paper from sketchbooks so it stamps nicely, and the covers have either the imprint of the artisticly carved stamps or original artwork related to the letterbox, and even the bindings are unique, sometimes ribbon or twine or something appropriate to the overall effect.  I'll admit that my own aren't nearly that artistic, but I usually do like to create hand-made logbooks, cutting them to size and making the covers and sewing them together.  But sometimes, I just use what I can quickly put together if I don't have time to do create the logbook the way I'd really like to. 
    Some other logbooks I've found recently that I think are beautiful or unique include Stargazer's Safari Series and her Endangered Species of Texas series (only some of which I've found so far).  Each was hand-made and had her beautiful artwork on the cover - nice!  Also, CelticLions' Memoirs of a Meesha and the Adventures of a Mooshido Warrior mystery boxes had really clever, beautifully crafted shaped logbooks with unique details and folded covers.  Those added efforts make me smile and appreciate the time and trouble it takes to do something like that!  They must enjoy a great deal of personal satisfaction in creating them, and I certainly appreciate the results when I log in.
    The extra care and effort of the creator of the letterbox in carving the stamp and creating a hand-made logbook, not to mention putting it all together, finding the perfect location and planting the box, and then writing the clues (and some of those really clever clues - well, that's another discussion!), makes me feel even more responsible as a finder to carefully seal the plastic bags to protect their work or to replace torn bags with new ones if necessary, and to use caution and stealth if necessary to avoid being detected when finding and replacing the box, which is sometimes the most difficult part of letterboxing in urban locations and on busy trails -- and then to recover it very well and completely so all this artistic effort isn't lost to future finders! 
    Thanks to everyone who carves, creates, plants, and finds and recovers!  I really enjoyed the pleasant outing this morning and finding these boxes. And tomorrow, I hear the cold and wet is coming back for awhile. Oh well, time to start carving and cutting and sewing, I guess. 
    --  Viewfinder
