From: sandy redding (sandyreddingusa@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-04-20 01:42:27 UTC-07:00
Hello everyone!

I am a bit behind in reading my emails so forgive me
for being so late to jump on the bandwagon. About the
funniest thing that happened while letterboxing was
back in Feb. We went to find one of JBKokopelli's
letterboxes at a cemetery outside of Rusk. We
followed the clues to the letter or so I thought,
still have yet to find it although lots of others have
found it since. Anyway the cemetery is right next to
a farm that has a pasture full of cows who fortunately
were about the only audenience I had. There was a
antbed under the branches of the cedar tree where the
letterbox is planted. I was wearing knit pants that
were a bit loose and I kept feeling something sting me
on my calf. I pulled my pants leg up a bit to
discover that about half the colony of ants had
crawled up my pants leg and were climbing up further
and further! I screamed and came out of my pants
right then and there. The girls were mortified and
some of the cows I think were amused! I dislodged the
ants and pulled my pants back up and was very thankful
that it was a rather deserted location. Now the girls
are very quick to point out the ant beds and make sure
I am wearing jeans that aren't as easy to get off.

Okay, you can stop laughing now!

Happy Trails!!

Sandy
mom to Atoy Adventurers

From: SandiBox (sandibox@msn.com) | Date: 2007-04-20 21:01:14 UTC
That's too funny!

Not a LB story, but it reminds me of the time we took our cocker
spaniel to Matagora Island. Spaniels are bird dogs... and once he
saw the seagulls, he was a gonner. He chanced those birds for MILES
down the beach. We yelled, we bribed, we yelled some more, but we
couldn't get his attention either. He was completely focused on the
birds. If the seagulls dove into the water, the dog dove into the
water. If the seagulls flew a mile further down the beach, the dog
ran a mile further down the beach. We finally hopped into our Jeep
and took off after him. As we pulled close to the errant pup, my
husband told me to jump out (of the moving vehicle) and grab him.
Do WHAT?!!! I don't think so! So instead, hubby pulled slightly
ahead of the dog, threw the jeep into neutral, and HE jumped out of
the rolling vehicle and grabbed the dog by the curly hairs on his
stubby little tail. Meanwhile, I'm howling with laughter. To this
day, I don't know if hubby was more angry with the dog or me!

Thanks for sharing and bringing back memories!

SandiBox
