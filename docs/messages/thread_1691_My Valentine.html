My Valentine

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-02-14 18:41:07 UTC-08:00
I just got home from a nice Valentine's Day dinner
with my two favorite guys - Fearless Fox and
Lightning. SJZ was out of the picture for the day
since she headed up to Denton to spend Valentine's
with her valentine.

Valentine's Day has always been a "nice" holiday for
us, but nothing much more special than any other day
of the year. I suspected a few days ago that FF had
something up his sleeve because I wasn't allowed to go
into Michael's with him when we went to Tyler. I was
pretty sure jewelry wasn't involved since I'm not a
jewelry kind of girl, and the only thing that would've
really surprised me would've been a new car - which we
really need right now. That seemed pretty unlikely
though!

He cancelled lunch on me today, and then called to say
he would be a few minutes late picking me up from
work. I took the opportunity to plant a box near
where I work, and he and Lightning finally showed up.
Nothing glaringly obvious in the car, except that my
LB backpack was in the backseat and I knew I had left
it on the kitchen table, but he gave me a card and
wished me Happy Valentine's Day. I figured I must've
been wrong about the surprise since he would've waited
until he gave me the surprise to give me a card, so I
figured it was a normal V-Day. I opened the card - a
kids' card, hardly for an adult - not even signed.
But on the inside of the front of the card was a clue
- handwritten. I had to figure out the clue and tell
him where to take me. The clue began "go to the place
we first met"...bear in mind, we grew up in this
little town from the time I was 6 and he was 7, so we
have 36 years of history springing from this little
town. I reached deep back into my brain to recall -
did we meet first at school or at church? He
remembered though - it was at school! We went to the
school and walked around the campus as his clues
directed me from one point to another, and finally
ended right outside the window of the 3rd grade class
we shared when he moved to town the year after I did.
This, by the way, is the same school Lightning
attends! I dug around and found the box - with a pair
of stamps (store-bought, but hey, he IS an LSS and
only an avid LB'er because I am) and another set of
clues. I diligently stamped into my logbook, opening
to a place where I had two clean face-to-face pages to
allow me plenty of room to stamp for what would
evidently be a very special occasion.

The next set of clues were to the place where we
shared our first kiss - the home of a friend who had a
party way back when we were freshmen in high school.
We were basically parking in someone's driveway and
jotting off into the woods nearby to get this one. I
said a quick hello to the residents of the home who
were out in the yard (and although I know the woman
who owns the home, it was her daughter-in-law and
grandkids in the yard and they didn't know us!) and
snagged the box - thinking to myself that it was a
good thing we weren't worrying about replacing these
boxes! Again, there were a pair of stamps and another
set of clues. We left the driveway and went to a
parking lot nearby for me to stamp in, then proceeded
off as directed by the clues into the country to visit
my childhood home. When we were kids we spent
countless hours on the 50 acres of so that my parents
owned, rambling about and hunting bugs and conducting
snipe hunts and riding horses and whatever else seemed
to be the pasttime du jour. When FF and Lightning
planted the box, they introduced themselves to the
current owner of the house and explained what they
were doing, so they were able to place the box within
the boundaries of the property. I stamped in the new
pair of stamps while we looked around and took in the
changes that had been made - some good and some bad.
Finally, as we were leaving the owner and his dog came
out to wave goodbye.

The next set of clues directed me to the church where
we grew up and where we were married. The church is
now a private school and it's been many years since
I've set foot on the property, but it is special in so
many ways because FF and I actually helped build the
building and grew up in that church, then had a unique
marriage ceremony there. Odd to find myself digging
in the flower beds to find tupperware, but dig I did
and found the final box, again with a pair of stamps!


What makes this whole thing especially special is that
he is an LSS - he boxes because I do. He never
complains about it, always enjoys it and has even been
known to go on a mission to retrieve a box when I
couldn't go. But it's not what he would choose to do
with his own time. This was better than diamonds!

He mentioned rehiding the boxes in permanent
locations, and you never know - we may just do that!

Lucy
