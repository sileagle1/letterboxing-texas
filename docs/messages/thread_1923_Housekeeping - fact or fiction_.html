From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-06-10 22:45:38 UTC-05:00
OMG!!! I'm not the only one!!! I finally gave up on apologizing
for the constant mess/upheaval in our house and just came right out
and admitted the truth. We live our lives and don't clean our
house. (Not that it's dirty...just in a constant, never-ending
state of coming and going!) I finally had to give up the imaginary
picture in my head of what my house might look like if everything
were picked up and put away........now it's just my house......the
place where we sleep and get ready to go somewhere else to do
something else!

You cannot imagine how relieved I am to know that I'm not the only
one out there!

Batty B

*****LOL...I don't let my mother come over any more - it would make her crazy!  But I long ago gave up my neat freak habits, and I must say I've been happier ever since...
 
I wish I could remember who it was, but the matriarch of some famous football family was once talking about living among a household of athletes.  She said something about wishing she had a beautiful yard and being upset with her husband for insisting on playing football on the lawn with the boys and tearing up her grass and beds - until she realized a well-manicured lawn doesn't raise happy, healthy, active boys.  She decided then that she would have her lawn after the boys grew up and moved away, but she would be happy with it just the way it was until that time came, and she wouldn't wish it to come too soon. 
 
After reading that, I decided I could live with whatever condition my home is in.  It's not like we leave dirty dishes and and bags of trash and dog poop all over the house - it's just the clutter of the stuff we use to live our busy lives!

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-06-11 17:44:48 UTC-05:00
Well, after several moves and two kids, my furniture was on its last legs, so getting rid of it was almost a relief, but yes, I got rid of it with nothing to go in its place.  At the time, my daughter, SJZ, was on drill team and there is nothing better to practice dance on than hardwood floors!  Then Lightning started karate, so it made sense not to worry about it.  Karate, it turned out, wasn't for him but the look has stayed.  We've added back a couple pieces of furniture, but they are small and there for pure comfort and the entertainment center is still there, and I have a work table set up so I can do projects indoors instead of in the garage, but other than that, it is open for whatever is the project of the moment!
 
And it especially made sense when I realized that most of our adult company comes to the kitchen anyway (which has its share of clutter, but all our friends live in the same world as us, so who cares?!?)
