Re: [letterboxingtexas] Re: What's with all the new trail names planting boxes?

From: Kelli Oliver (kellioliver2004@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-08-12 10:49:19 UTC-07:00
Lock n Locks gets their name from the type of lid they have.  They are all plastic boxes with lids that lock on all four sides, creating almost a vaccum seal. When sealed properly - no moisture will get inside.  With Glad containters and other brands - the plastic lids just pop on allowing moisture to creep in and detroy the log book if the zip lock baggies inside are not zipped properly. 
Also with the twist/screw on lids (which I have a few placed), dirt can get in the groves allowing for an uneven closure.  Or if a letterboxer is in a hurry and doesn't twist/screw the lid on tight this will allow for moisture to creep in.  Luckily / Knock on wood, I have not had this problem.   I do keep a close eye on these boxes.  Well, my step daughter does since they are all in Alabama and not too far from her house.

Ta_Da is a regional North Texas letterboxing group (which stands for TArrant-DAllas counties, but includes many more such as Denton, Collin etc) which centers in the DFW area, but the list has people on it from everywhere who are just interested in keeping up with what's going on in this area. The website is http://groups.yahoo.com/group/TA_DA/ and anyone is welcomed to join the list. It's a place where we can discuss anything local which may not be of interest to the rest of the state, like local events, or requesting help or information on local boxes, etc. There is also a group for the Houston area and East Texas area. Last June, we had an event in Denton, so it's about time we start thinking about another one soon.
