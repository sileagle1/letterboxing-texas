From: Silver Eagle (sileagle@alltel.net) | Date: 2008-09-17 19:31:39 UTC
We finally got power late last night so I am just now catching up with
email and LB posts. We have minimal damage, but my wife's parent's
house near Galveston has major damage, so they will be staying with us
for many months. Baby Bear and wife are fine and so is Sea Dove and
his family. Glad to hear many of you are fine, even though a little
battered. Being without power was a pain, but I still consider us
lucky. It was actually kind of fun for a while, since I finally got to
play BOARD games with the kids, who never want to play non-electronic
games. We even played Hide & Go Seek outside with neighbors, so I
guess there is a silver lining to everything. Hang in there everyone!

Good hearing from you. I loved the part about playing Hide and Seek with neighbors. I attended a seminar once where the presenter made the point that the loss of our sense of neighborhoods was due to three elemental things: TV, Air Conditioners and Loss of Front Porches. TV and air conditioning drove everyone inside their homes with closed doors, and houses weren't built with front porches anymore where people used to sit and chat with passing neighbors. I guess that's being proven as true since the lack of TV and AC has driven everyone outside to "play" with their neighbors again.
