Hot time in the old town

From: Boots Tex (redbarn@eastex.net) | Date: 2011-08-09 14:09:35 UTC
Texas is hot! Has anyone else noticed or is it just me? :) We're contemplating a trip to northern Alabama in a couple of weeks (sounds cooler). We're going from here to San Augustine to place a governor box, then to Shreveport, east on I-20 to Birmingham, then north on I-65 to Huntsville. We're visiting El Lobo and Star Seeker, who have moved there and built a new house. Anyway, I've been searching for boxes along that route within easy access from the Interstate. We have never found a letterbox in the U.S. outside of Texas (amazing!), and I am struck by the fact that Louisiana and Mississippi have so few boxes in so few counties. We've picked out a couple that we want to stop and get, but was wondering if anyone could recommend some good ones along that route? We won't have time to get very far off-line and don't want to attempt urban boxes just because they take more time. Any help would be appreciated. I can't believe I didn't see any Silver Eagle or Baby Bear boxes!

Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter

Hi Boots! Just to let you know, my box is probably about 10-12 miles south of I-20. Last time I checked, it was still there, but it has been a while since I have been out there. (Vicksburg is my hometown) It's an easy drive by at a cemetery. Corazon's box "The Rock of Gilbraltor" is fairly easy to get because it is right off of I-20 just across the bridge and happens to be the very first box Lucy Locket and I stamped in. Although it is near a very busy playground. I hope you are able to get a few of Corazon's boxes, they are some of my favorites.
Have fun!

I have an easy box in Tatum, TX (The Family Tree) but that may not be on your route. 
 
JBK has a box in Gibsland, LA (about 10 miles south of I-20 to the box) - Ambush of Bonnie & Clyde.  It was last found in June of this year, so a safe bet that it's still there and it will be a quick find as well.
 
When we went through LA and MS, we found several boxes in Vicksburg that we really liked.  They were technically "urban", but were easy finds that didn't take much time or send us around in circles.  JBK also placed two in cemeteries in Vicksburg (Hats Off To Freedom, and The Price of Freedom).
 
Much fun and safe travels!
