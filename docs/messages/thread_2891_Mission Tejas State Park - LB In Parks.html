Nice job, BT & LSQ! Just curious, did they say they would check on
your boxes every once in a while and let you know of any problems?
Also, I wonder why they don't have to follow the permit rules
supposedly mandated for all State Parks? It would appear that each
park does have some leeway in how they interpret the rules. This is
good in some ways and bad in others. It means some parks like this
one currently allow boxes, but there is no guarantee the next set of
Rangers will continue this policy and the boxes will be confiscated.
I guess the best we can do is negotiate with current Rangers and hope
they stay a long time.

This was a great park to negotiate with since it was already known
that they were geocache-friendly. Parks that are against it will be
more difficult, but at least we would have nothing to lose. The
parks that have no defined policy with letterboxes currently in them
(Rangers probably unaware) are the ones I worry about. If you
discuss letterboxing with them, it might backfire and get all boxes
in the park confiscated. I guess we could ask about geocache policy
first to see how they feel about them, and if they are ok with that,
then ask about letterboxes. Hopefully we would not endanger the
geocaches in the park if they don't know about them, but I doubt many
Rangers aren't aware of geocaches by now. Either way, it will
require good communication skills and a lot of patience, which Boots
and LS Quilter obviously possess!

Silver Eagle

> place to hide LSQ's box, we found one of Atoy Adventurer's boxes
> (Pretty Kitty). She has four there, although we didn't realize it
> at the time since they're not listed on LbNA. The ranger gave me
> her card and asked if I would email her the website address
whenever
> I posted the clues. This is a lovely little park, but has some good
> trails and a restored 1828 log house,as well as the Mission Tejas,
> and is also kept very clean. This is now a letterbox-friendly park
> and if we all treat it with respect, we can keep it that way.

Re: Mission Tejas State Park - LB In Parks

From: Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter (janet@redbarnretreat.com) | Date: 2007-06-29 04:50:43 UTC
They didn't even ask me for the location of the boxes, although the
one lady ranger did ask me to email her the address of the website.
She said that she goes into geocaching.com and reads comments from
finders and enjoys hearing the compliments they get on the park. Of
course, I guess if they know the website they will be able to locate
the boxes if they choose to do so. I thought I'd send her the
AtlasQuest website address since our boxes are posted there. As far
as the mandate on "treasure hunting games", I don't know why they
would allow geocaching, but none of them had ever heard of
letterboxes. I can understand how you would be worried that we have
put your box and Baby Bear's box in jeopardy by discussing
letterboxing with them. I didn't tell them that there were any
other boxes there, but I guess they could find out now if they
really want to know. I think they would be greatly surprised to
learn that two letterboxes had been there for over four years. This
park may be small enough and remote enough that they just make up
their own rules as they go. I think this is the way we may have to
approach letterboxing in state parks in the future.

Boots Tex
