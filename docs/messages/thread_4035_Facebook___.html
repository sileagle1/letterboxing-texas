Facebook...

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2009-04-09 19:51:58 UTC-05:00

Okay, I've been sort of ignoring this whole Facebook thing until recently. I've had accounts on there, one in my real name and one in my given name, but they've just sort of sat out there.


Next thing I know, Fearless Fox comes home knowing all about what's going on with everyone we've ever known. When I finally realized he is usually the last to know anything, and now he knows everything before me, I started asking questions and I find he's gotten himself thoroughly networked on Facebook. Not one to get left behind, I've gotten myself up and going a little better.

So - a few of you here have linked to my facebook, but now I'm actually getting tuned in there, I realized there is a whole 'nother set of letterboxing groups there! I can't quite get my mind wrapped around how they are different than yahoo groups or AQ, but there they are.

And more interesting? I went through the largest of the letterboxing nextworks yesterday looking for Texas letterboxers, and all I found were a bunch of folks I didn't know! Admittedly, some of them were probably y'all in disguise, but since I have at least a vague awareness of many of your given names, I'm thinking there are folks letterboxing on Facebook that don't know about us!

Thoughts?

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2009-04-09 22:13:20 UTC-05:00

That's kind of my thing too - I won't overlap my personal FB with my LB FB. I don't feel very anonymous on this list anyway, but I DO feel like I have control over what anonymity I have here. It seems like an all-or-nothing thing on FB, unless you have two accounts and that is a pain in the rear!

The one thing I see as being an advantage to it for letterboxing purposes is being able to post to someone's wall when I find one of their boxes. I don't care for logging my finds online, yet I want to let placers know I've found their box, so a quick note on FB means I can fill them in relatively painlessly.
