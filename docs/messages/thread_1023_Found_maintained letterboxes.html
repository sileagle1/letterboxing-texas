Found/maintained letterboxes

From: Hawk (omkarmashanti@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-07-10 16:22:35 UTC-07:00
I finally got some time to do some letterboxing today!

Oleander Island: Finally! A chance to find a Celtic
Lions box! What a treat. If you haven't found this one
yet, you might want to make sure you get to Galveston
before the next big storm, as this box will probably
get blown all the way to the Celtic Lions den up in
DFW if we get a good strong storm. For now, it seems
happy living where it's hiding.

Sponge Bob Square Pants: I had to ask several guests
at the gathering I was attending before I found one
that knew about Sponge Bob. The box was wet, but I
dried everything out, put the log and stamp in new
baggies, and added some camo tape to the bottom of the
box.

Kemp's Ridley: Alive and well. Great hiding place.
Easy place to stamp in. Really nice stamp!

Sylvester: Yet another awesome stamp by Baby Bear! I
lived on Galveston Island for several years, and had
never been to this bird sanctuary!

Celtic Cross: Alive and well. I replaced the logbook,
so there is plenty of paper for more boxers to sign
in!

Sea Aggie: Thanks to Celtic Lions, this box is still
alive. I replaced baggies and box and put in a new
logbook. It's ready for the next finder!

Cavalla: Totally awesome stamp by Celtic Lions that
makes my original Cavalla stamp look like something
old and gray and formless! I counted this as a find,
even though I originally placed the box -- new box,
stamp, and pad, not my own doing. The box is under the
brick by the bush and doing well.

Labyrinth: If you want to see something really neat,
check out this labyrinth! Not only can you walk the
labyrinth, but check out the large stones to mark the
Solstice sun positions. Dewberry is right about the
brambles, though. Be sure to take a stick to "beat the
bushes" before you venture off the trail. This is one
of those boxes where you can see where the box is
located, but it takes a bit of work to figure out how
to get it! Oh yeah -- the restrooms are on the back
side of the shelter, and they were extremely clean for
public restrooms.

Thank you Celtic Lions, Mother of Five, Gramatrick,
and Baby Bear for placing these boxes and turning my
day into a wonderful afternoon of letterboxing!

Hawk
