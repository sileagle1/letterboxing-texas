Re-Hiding Boxes (forgive my grumpiness, but hear my message)

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-07-16 17:13:07 UTC-07:00
I just posted my sitrep for the day, but I have to
address an issue and I didn't want to do it while I
talked about what a fun day we had. It WAS a lot of
fun, but it wouldn't have been nearly as much fun if
some of the boxes we found had been missing.

Let me just say it:

REHIDE.

REHIDE WELL.

REHIDE WELL PLEASE.

I hate to say this, but every single box I found was
exposed. Every one. Two of them could be seen from a
distance of about 15 feet, and one of those two was so
clearly visible from the trail that I'm surprised
someone didn't pick it up and take it away as trash.
Someone had tried to rehide by laying three sticks
across it. Three sticks and nothing else across a
bright blue lid is like an elephant trying to hide
behind a lamppost. It doesn't work.

When you rehide, take a look from several different
angles to make sure you can't see any hint of the box.
Also, consider that if you are using small matter to
rehide, some of that material will slip away. And
don't go digging with a backhoe to get more material
to cover with - that only draws attention to the fact
that someone has been concealing something.

One of the boxes I hid today, Go Jacks!, was a very
difficult carving. Aside from being an intricate
piece of work, it is also a tribute to my deceased dad
who earned a forestry degree from SFA, and who was a
man bigger than life and as fine a person as anyone I
could ever hope to meet. And as if that isn't enough,
it is a nod to my own alma matre and the time I spent
at SFA. I put a lot of work into the stamp and it
turned out great! I'm very proud of it and I'm eager
to share it with my fellow letterboxers. I know that
I placed it in a high risk area, but it is exactly
where it needs to be, given what it means.

HOWEVER, I'm very discouraged after my run today - if
Go Jacks! isn't hidden back any better than the boxes
I found today, it won't last a month. Consequently,
I'm going to post it on LBNA, but the clues will only
be available by email from me. If it goes missing, it
goes missing, and I WILL be sad, but I don't want it
to go missing because someone didn't respect my effort
enough to take care of it for me.

For you finders who have never placed a box, I
encourage you to carve a stamp and place a box, so you
can better understand the position of a placer. In
most cases, placers are sharing a little bit of
themselves with future finders unknown. It's a
difficult idea to understand if you haven't been
there. It's a wonderful feeling to set a box free,
but it really is frustrating to know that it might
never be seen again.

I hope that I haven't offended anyone or hurt any
feelings by what I've said, but I say it for the good
of all of us, not just myself. And I am not trying to
point fingers at any person, but at a practice that
needs to be better. If it was a newbie who did the
poor rehiding, I don't want you to get discouraged and
quit because of what I've said, but I DO want you to
understand that it is up to YOU - the finder - to take
extra care to protect the boxes you find. The placer
should be diligent to put the box in a location that
is amenable to hiding and to provide a means to hide
it well, but that's as far as they can go. The rest
is up to all of us!

Thank you today because I know you'll try harder
tomorrow.

Lucy

From: Connie Terry (connie@lavernia.net) | Date: 2005-07-16 19:39:34 UTC-05:00

Well said Lucy.  Far, far too many times the container, blue lid or not, is showing.  I also have a concern about “camouflage” that looks too contrived.  Like 5-6 sticks placed parallel over the box, or a flat rock leaning against a tree and there are no other rocks in the vicinity. Many times it is easy find boxes just by looking for what looks “out of place”. 

 

However, on a happier note, sometimes I find my boxes hidden better than I left them.  One is “Bear with Me” in New Berlin.  I think it was Silver Eagle who contrived a way to conceal it better.  My favorite good rehide, however, is “Button Necklaces” in La Vernia.  Again, I think it was Silver Eagle who made the hiding spot better than it was, but since then when I have checked it there have been several subtle changes to the spot.  I think that a perhaps gardener has found the box and is looking after it.  I wish I knew who the Good Samaritan is!

 

Corazon
