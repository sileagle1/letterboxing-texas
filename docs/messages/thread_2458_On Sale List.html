From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-01-04 16:44:00 UTC
I want to encourage you (and others who haven't taken the plunge) to
try carving your own. It just takes practice, and if you've got lots
of cheap material, it won't be so scary.

If you do a negative image (where you draw or transfer the lines onto
the carving medium and then simply cut them out, so that the picture
is formed by the missing lines), you can get a cute little image
fairly easily. Or, start by carving an outline to a block-style stamp
(my first real carves were done this way).

As you work your way up in proficiency, you can tackle more difficult
types of carving styles. Really, you get better quickly.

There's lots of disagreement about store-bought stamps, and we
probably don't need to get into that. Personally, I'll find
store-bought stamps if the letterbox is somewhere really neat or a
place I'm going anyway, but I won't really go out of my way to find
them. To me, the stamp is an integral part of the whole package that
makes up the uniqueness that is a letterbox.

Dewberry

I just wonder where people get off saying one stamp or one box is any better than another.  You think they would be grateful that someone put something out somewhere.  I guess not.

Back to reloading ammo.  I have not shot a sanctioned rifle match (my REAL hobby) since July 10 2005.  I am shooting on Saturday.  Shooting - not carving!  Get it?
