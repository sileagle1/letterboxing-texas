OT-Thanks y'all, from the bottom of my heart

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-08-26 08:18:40 UTC-07:00
I have been through moderator hell the last few days
because of a local recycling list I have. It's a
fairly new list associated with an international
recycling initiative that is having some pretty
serious growing pains. Now my little local list is
made up of the nicest folks (kind of like y'all),
basically friends and neighbors to one another, but
the behind the scenes stuff of dealing with the bigger
group has been a big pain lately. I almost dread
logging onto my other email account, because I know
that it is going to be filled with yukky stuff - you
know how the Big List gets sometimes? Triple that! I
never knew how mean and spiteful people could be to
each other while they are all supposedly trying to
accomplish a similar objective.

You cannot begin to know how nice it is to log onto
this email account and know that our little corner of
the internet is going to be behaving nicely, enjoying
one another's company. As I read Ruby's post about
rounding on future orders and pooling the excess so we
can cover expenses at gatherings, it reminded me yet
again how well this list functions together and how
fortunate I am to know folks like you.

I had better get back to work, but I didn't want to
wait to let y'all know how much I appreciate each and
every member of this list for what you bring to Texas
Letterboxing!

Lucy

Re: [letterboxingtexas] OT-Thanks y'all, from the bottom of my heart

From: JOY (TeamTexUS@houston.rr.com) | Date: 2005-08-26 21:04:07 UTC-05:00
Well said, Lucy! 
 
I must admit that this is the first group I have joined, and I assumed that I was joining a group of people who had a common purpose- furthering the pursuit of letterboxing- and that the members would be supportive of and helpful to each other. This group is exactly that! No flames, no nasty bickering. When people disagree, they voice their opinions politely and are respectful towards those who disagree with them. Of course, this is how adults should behave. :-)
 
Several months after joining this list, I joined the Big List, and I am often appalled by the "goings on" on that list. Flames, nasty bickering, and the occasional blatantly suggestive post, like the one from Chuck that was posted today re "not at all funny log entry". Maybe it's a generational thing, but I was not amused.
 
I like our nice, happy group so much better! I look forward to checking my e-mail every day. I appreciate all you "old-timers" like Ruby T and Barefoot Lucy and Baby Bear and Silver Eagle, who host events and contribute so much through the organizational duties you voluntarily assume, your advice to newbies, and your awesome creativity.  If you are an "old-timer" and I didn't mention your name, please don't be offended- I appreciate you, too, but I'm old and can barely remember my own name, much less yours, lol!!!  I also appreciate all the newbies who bring a fresh perspective and help keep letterboxing so exciting- nothing better than finding or planting that first box!
 
I searched for years to find the right hobby. I tried all kinds of stuff- bonsai, quilting, all other kindsa needlwork, crafts, gardening, bicycling, etc. and this is the first thing that has fit just right!
 
Appreciatively,
 
JOY
