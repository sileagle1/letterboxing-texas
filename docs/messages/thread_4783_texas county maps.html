From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2011-05-10 22:51:13 UTC-05:00
I have a great atlas that shows counties and nearly all the roads you can imagine - it's really good in rural areas, not so great in urban areas, but usually maps aren't such an issue to find in urban areas. It's called The Roads of Texas and it's published by Mapsco.

It really is a letterboxer's book of maps because every little country road is shown, many cemeteries, it has write-ups of roadside attractions, ghost towns, Texas history, State and National Parks, and other items of interest, in addition to just the maps.

My copy is beat up from living in the car, going camping, getting rained on, written in, spilled on, dog-eared. At this point, I barely had enough cover left to be able to tell you the name of it, but thank goodness for duct tape - enough of the cover had been duct-taped back to the book (with camo tape, of course).

This is actually a map book called The Roads of Texas. I have an older version and it's great to carry in the car. No folding required. It costs about $20.00, but it has all the roads and county lines and a lot of information I haven't seen anywhere else. I highly recommend it.
