Having been misplaced in Connecticut when I first started letterboxing, the first thirty boxes I found were placed by the CT State Forestry Department in their 30 various state forests. Each hike was vastly different, educational, and entertaining. My first five Texas boxes were hidden by the Celticlions, and I quickly realized that boxing in Texas was going to be a very special treat. The first Silver Eagle box I found was north of San Diego, CA. I love that we have so many new boxers in the great state of Texas, and the talent of Texas boxers is amazing.

First of all - I have a confession to make! I've so enjoyed that
answers to this question that I "borrowed" the idea for our Newboxers
list.

Secondly - our answer to the question:

Sue and I found Jay Drew's "Zoom, Zoom, Zoom" in Essex, CT on June
16, 2002.

It was Sue's XXth birthday (that wasn't a typo!) and we had gone away
to a B&B as a surprise for her birthday. I even brought the clues for
several boxes in the vicinity.

Letterboxing was something Sue spotted many months previous in a
local newspaper and we had talked about trying but never did. I
brought our supplies with us that weekend and we spent the afternoon
doing several of Jay's boxes.

Little did we know that we'd become such great friends with Jay and
that we'd become so hooked - traveling around the country and to
Dartmoor in search of Tupperware!

Mark

I have to second that (although I've not yet been to Dartmoor)!

When we first started letterboxing, it was sort of a "thing" that I really liked that I felt like I would have to fit in to family time inobtrusively.   Consequently, I figured it to be a local, catch-as-catch-can activity.  It didn't take me long to figure out that if I was going to sit around and wait for more boxes in my area, I was going to have a LONNNNGGGG wait!

 

I started thinking of it on broader terms, realizing that with only a LITTLE extra effort, I could find boxes further afield.   And Lightning became particularly attached to letterboxing too, so now I had a compadre in the family.  And SJZ was cool with it, even though she was and is more of an opportunistic boxer.   And Fearless Fox, while he isn't much of a boxer, enjoys all the activities that go along with it and generously humors us.

 

So now I've found that I have a hobby that takes me places locally and more distant both by myself as the circumstances dictate, and with my family.   I keep telling FF not to be surprised when he finds a note to say I've headed to Connecticut, but as yet I haven't followed throughand of course, he plans on attending the World Jamboree in England in a couple years, and I've told him I would like to stop off at Dartmoor for a little boxing.   I'll tell him more what that would entail when the time gets closer.
