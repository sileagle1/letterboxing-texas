Just a reminder from your happy Webmasters: please do not plant letterboxes in Government Canyon Natural Area without proper permission and permits. Thanks, Jay in CT

<< (Government Canyon State Natural Area, or GCSNA.)As an SNA that harbors and
protects many federally-listed endangered species we have fairly strict land
management regulations and restrictions on the placement of anything within our
property. This includes geocaches and letterboxes. This letterbox was placed
without our knowledge or completion of necessaary permits to do so. I will be
removing it/them and attempting to contact the person(s) who placed it/them in
order for the "placer" to follow proper protocol and procedure. It is my hope
that we can simply have the person who placed it complete the necessary permits
and then we can re-place the letterbox(es.) Any assistance you can provide in
helping me contact the appropriate individuals is appreciated andnwill
cedrtainly help in our supporting this activity.>>>

The Government Canyon boxes have been archived.  Planters haven’t logged in since July 2011.
 
Sounds like Government Canyon (NW San Antonio, BTW) is welcoming letterboxes—as long as they have approval.  It is a huge park with lots of great hiking and some biking.  And you can use your state park pass.  The downside is that they are closed Tuesday-Thursday.  I always seem to forget that and haven’t been in the park for years because I always show up on the wrong days.
