From: carrascoruth2000 (a.carrasco@earthlink.net) | Date: 2004-04-15 18:48:09 UTC
Thanks to all who sent hog and javelina info...we have determined
that what we saw were baby javelinas. I didn't know they were found
that far north, but I am sure that is what they were.

We spent last weekend camping and letterboxing in Glen Rose. This is
a stunning park. We found Silver Eagles box, and planted another of
our own (Dino-Mite! placed by mother of four if you want the clues)
We also found the confederate flag box in Mexia, this is also a cool
place to visit. We looked for Baby Bear's val verde box, but I think
the steps to the building have been recently replaced, and the box
wasn't there. We stopped in Waco and found the don't mess with Texas
box (my favorite stamp so far!) at the suspension bridge. We were
with my four little ones, and they all enjoyed each of these spots.
Pouring rain and naptime kept us from finding any more boxes in Waco,
so we will save them for the next trip.

By the way, we stopped at Washington on the Brazos on the way up. We
looked for Baby Bears box, but if we were at the correct fallen tree
(and I think we were) it is covered with suspicious looking vines.
After we all stepped in ants (except the baby who was riding in the
backpack) we decided to call it quits for the day. We'll return when
the park isn't so full of people taking pictures in the bluebonnets!

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-01-29 22:19:20 UTC-06:00
I've had the opportunity to meet Mother's Love twice now, and I'm not a bit surprised she would do such a thing!  She and her daughters, Texas Pokey and Frogkisses, have proven to be three of the really good letterboxers we've recently gotten in East Texas. 
 
So on that note I would like to throw in a belated thanks for the same sort of thing - Texas Pokey and Frogkisses found my Signs of Spring box in Palestine last weekend and saw that the LNL container was buttoned up upside down and one of the flaps was broken.  They got the lid straightened out as best they could, then let me know of the problem.  I want to get back over to Palestine to fix that box and plant a little more (let me tell y'all - Palestine and Anderson County is a ripe area with many, many great planting opportunities).
 
I have been very much encouraged about boxing in East Texas recently with the influx of good boxers in the last several months.  It is so much fun to have new people to play with, who want to play to the best of their ability.  Thanks to you ET newbies for jumping in the game!
