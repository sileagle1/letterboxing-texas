end of summer outing

From: the carrascos (a.carrasco@earthlink.net) | Date: 2006-08-12 22:15:42 UTC-05:00
We just got back from a fabulous last day of summer vacation outing, and I want to advertise two spectacular spots.  Our first stop was Sea Center Texas, a couple of weeks ago the kids and I went to find Silver Eagle's "Gordon Grouper" box.  This is a "can't be missed" spot, especially if you have kids.  There is a great little FREE aquarium, with all sorts of displays on the Gulf, and super cool touch tanks containing very feisty crabs.  We went back today, because when we were replacing the microbox, I somehow managed to knock it down between two pilings holding up the boardwalk.  After many tries at retrieving the box, we had to give it up for lost.  If you all think it feels bad to find out one of your own boxes has gone missing, imagine the feeling of knowing you have lost a great box that has been in place for a LONG time.  But leave it to me and my crew to mess thins up.  Anyway, I recarved SE's original stamp, and added a little bonus "I'm Sorry" stamp from us, and put it all in a box too big to fit down any little cracks.  If you haven't yet visited Sea Center TX, make a point to go.  It is one of those great places we never would have found without letterboxing.  From there we went on down to Quintana ( a short drive down 288) to find Dewberry's "Hummer of a Summer" box.  It is located in a super little county park, with a great beach and an ANTI AIRCRAFT GUN (I think this falls under the "only in Texas" category)  A great spot for a picnic dinner (there is a Church's Chicken in Freeport, and it will stay warm until you reach the park!)  We had planned to go on to the bird sanctuary in Quintana and find the boxes there, but had way too much fun on the beach, so those will have to wait until next time.  I was thinking on the way home about what Dewberry said the other day about not being afraid to leave without a box, and she was right.  For me, the best part of letterboxing is all the great places we have found, and all the fun we have had out in search of those little plastic boxes.  When you slow down, especially if you are out with kids, they begin to enjoy it much more.  And leaving a box for "next time" means you have a reason to return to those great spots.  Thanks to Silver Eagle and Dewberry for a perfect end to a wonderful summer.  I just am not ready for school to start again.  MO5
