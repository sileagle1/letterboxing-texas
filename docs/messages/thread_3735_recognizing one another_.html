recognizing one another?

From: Ashby (ashby@ic.org) | Date: 2008-08-17 13:41:42 UTC-07:00
I've been wondering lately, is there any "code word" or phrase type thing, (secret handshake, maybe?) that boxers might use to help identify each other as letterboxers, if they meet by accident out on the trail, other than giving pointed glances at your backpack or plastic containers!?

not that I have ever met another boxer on the trail, not as far as I know, but in case I might some day, I want to be ready and know what to do, or what to look for!

thanks,

ashby







Re: [letterboxingtexas] recognizing one another?

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-08-17 16:36:20 UTC-05:00
Well - there IS one way that is pretty much accepted, but frankly I'm a little embarassed by it because it is a little cheesy.  You can always approach someone you suspect of being a letterboxer and ask them if they know which way is north.  If they point straight up to the sky, they are a letterboxer.  I would like to think we are a little smarter than that, so I opt for using the powers of observation.  Backpack, inky fingers, furtive glances, well chosen questions...that sort of thing.

I've never asked the "north" question either, because so few people know about it, I don't think it means someone is a letterboxer or not. Kind of funny.
