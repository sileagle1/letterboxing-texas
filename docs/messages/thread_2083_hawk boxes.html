Allright, time for a general consensus.

As much as we miss her and wish she'd return, it doesn't really look
like Hawk is going to return to letterboxing any time soon. But,
she's got more than 80 boxes out there, most of them in the Houston
area. Some of these have gone missing (some for as long as a year)
and there's no way to update them. So people continue to look for
them fruitlessly. That's just frustrating.

I can fix the problem on Atlasquest. Because she hasn't logged in
there in more than a year, I can adopt her boxes, change the status
and then reassign them back to her, (I'm not really interested in
being the owner long-term, I just want to change the status). I can't
change the clues on LBNA, so for these boxes you would need to check
status on AQ.

Does this sound workable?

If you know of a Hawk box that is definitely missing (or definitely
found within the last few months), let me know either by posting her
or sending my gmail account a mail. I'm not going to run around
checking on all of them (can't seem to keep up with my own maintenance
as quickly as I'd like), but if it could be more of a collaborative
effort, we could make it work.

I tried it out on Flight of Fancy, which I know is missing, and it
worked great.

Dewberry

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-08-18 12:32:26 UTC-05:00
I like your solution Dewberry.  I think that, even with potential pitfalls, it is the best solution we can hope for for the moment.  I really like that it all goes back to her name, so that a search on either site would yield the same information, except that AQ would have a current status.
 
My biggest issue with making a change on AQ that can't be made on LBNA is that LBNA is the standard still - the point where virtually all newbies start.  And they are the ones who will go looking for missing boxes because they haven't heard or seen the discussion about specific boxes.  And they are the ones that are most susceptible to frustration and discouragement that this particular problem poses.
 
It seems to me that LBNA is the sticking point - every solution I've thought of has come back to the record being stuck in LBNA without updated information.  How to fix that...
