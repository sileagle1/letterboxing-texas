From: Michael P. Olbrisch (mike2004@elp.rr.com) | Date: 2005-12-04 23:08:59 UTC

The information included here was shared with someone else on the list
previously. Fortunately - I didn't delete it, so I will post it to the
whole list. The information is my opinions and experiences, and not the
final word.

First - everyone knows I am about 1000% patriotic. This means I will not
support the french. And this also means Magellan is OUT! Magellan is owned
by Thales. Thales is a french aerospace company. Sooooo..... I have no
knowledge of any of the latest magellan stuff. Don't WANT to know either!
And they will not get a penny from me. I am not saying do not get one....
I AM saying I cannot help you there.

I use, and HIGHLY recommend a Garmin. Specifically, I use a GPS-map-60CS.
And I LOVE IT! The best recommendation I can give it is that I have had
this one nearly 2 years now, and I have not found something I would replace
it with yet.

Now - I ended up with the 60CS as my fourth GPS. I outgrew each of the
first three for various reasons, as follows. NOTE: these had an accuracy of
12-18 feet because of the patch antenna.

Garmin E-Trex Yellow. (Cheap, under $100). Small display, no pixels, no
memory, limited waypoint names (6 char) Small - lightweight-easy to use.
Lasted 6 months. Gave it to my son. He took it to Iraq. Destroyed in
combat. Thrown in trash.
http://www.garmin.com/products/etrex/

Garmin E-Trex Venture. (under $150) Better display. More pixels. Very
little memory, limited waypoint names (7 char) Small - lightweight-easy to
use. Lasted 18 months. Sent it to son in Iraq to replace E-Trex Yellow.
Destroyed in combat. Sent destroyed unit to Garmin - repaired. Now my
spare unit.
http://www.garmin.com/products/etrexVenture/

Garmin E-Trex Vista. (under $300) Better display. More pixels. Some
memory, longer waypoint names (8 char) Small - lightweight-easy to use.
Lasted 18 months. EATS BATTERIES!!! Like one set every 10 hours! Sold to
local hiker.
http://www.garmin.com/products/etrexVista/

Garmin GPSMAP 60CS. (under $400) Large detailed color display. Quad Helix
antenna (much better (more sensitive) than E-Trex patch antenna). External
antenna available. Large memory capacity. Loadable maps (streets and/or
TOPO). Easy to use, lots of functions, plus a real altimeter and a magnetic
compass built in. If you look at that feature alone - it replaces your
normal compass too. Best of all, more than 24 (I get nearly 30) hours of
operation on one set (2) Ni-MH rechargeable batteries. GPS weight 5.4 oz.
7.5 oz with batteries installed. Typical accuracy for me is 7-9 feet. Fits
in the hand nicely. On and On and On.... Hope I am convincing you to NOT
make the same mistakes I did. Not much I don't like about it.
http://www.garmin.com/products/gpsmap60cs/

I have dealt with this company. Great service, best price at the time.
Shop around, perhaps you can beat it, but it is a good starting price.
$363.00.
http://www.getfeetwet.com/ProductInfo.aspx?productid=010-00322-30

I put a lot of effort into this - hope it is helpful. Lemme know if I can
do more.

Mike.
