From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2010-02-12 21:23:52 UTC-06:00

Very good question BootsTex.

I have a few thoughts (don't be shocked now, okay?)

When the list first began, there were far fewer of us and letterboxing was new to everyone and we were ALL learning. We were also all getting to know one another, so there was a lot of chatter, a lot of questions, a lot of excitement, many great new ideas, a lot of idle chat and a mountain of inane and entirely silly humor (remember Ruby's endless puns?).

As the list matured, the charter members had become comfortable with each other and so we didn't have to ask whether Puddle Splasher was a scurrilous sneak who would steal a box out from under someone's nose given half an opportunity. We all knew it.

The Newboxers listed started shortly after this list did, and it has become a well-established forum for asking "dumb newbie questions." I can't tell you how many times the question "Do I have to carve my own stamp?" question has been answered there, and answered well and with great patience. This list used to be where Texas newbies asked newbie questions. This list has much the same environment as Newboxers, but it seems most of those questions go there instead of here now. That's fine - it's a GREAT list (Mark Pepe is an incredible leader on that list and I'm proud to say I'm a moderator on it for him). However, sometimes I wish Texas newbies would ask their questions here because they would get the location-specific customs rather than the global answer. Also, asking questions is a great way to spark conversation with folks who are essentially your neighbors.

I think alot of the newer members got a sense that we were a tight-knit group of friends and felt something like outsiders. I don't think we originals intended to be exclusive, but I know some new boxers did feel that we were (because they said so). For my part, I felt that we were indeed a tight-knit group, but we had a "more the merrier" feeling about us and anyone who wanted to could break into our cluster But that attitude didn't always convey itself. Admittedly though, at times we were going so hard and fast that it would have been hard for a newbie to jump in and get in the mix.

We have always allowed a wide latitude of discussion on this list - essentially, if you are a letterboxer you can join and talk about whatever you want to talk about (i.e. Blockbuster) as long as it is respectful. I know that some folks became offended by the opinions expressed by others, and quit participating. Sometimes they felt the line of respect was crossed - I can't say I always agreed that it was, but yeah - it was crossed on occasion. But when you have a bunch of folks talking together, somebody is gonna say something that hurts someone else's feelings every now and then, or offends them. I consider many of the offenders and the offendees to be highly intelligent, interesting and caring folks who have strong opinions about their respective positions. But then - I LOVE a good debate and have been known to play devil's advocate to generate interesting discussion myself and have gotten myself into hot water on an occasion or two because of that.

When the list started, there were essentially two overriding precepts and one rule. The rule - Don't plant in national parks. The precepts - civility in all things and a letterbox is a letterbox if it has clues, a stamp, and a logbook. In my opinion, this brilliant lack of boundaries (except the national park one) led to creativity and thought-provoking ideas that promoted great discussion and idea-bouncing. As the game has progressed, everything has to have a name so it can be logged just right or listed just right, so that it has become pretty formula-driven. What fun is it to talk about THAT?

And I think the creativity level of a large number of boxes has gone down. How much discussion can a box generate whose clue reads "Go to the Walmart in Jacksonville and park in the Garden Center parking area. Look under the cover at the base of the light by the shopping cart return."? On the other hand, may I just say Scarlet Pimpernel - or Murder in the Arboretum?

And folks don't post sitreps. They used to generate a LOT of discussion!

I have a few other thoughts that I haven't entirely formulated, but I'm looking forward to seeing what other folks have to say.

Very good question BootsTex.

I have a few thoughts (don't be shocked now, okay?)

When the list first began, there were far fewer of us and letterboxing was new to everyone and we were ALL learning.  We were also all getting to know one another, so there was a lot of chatter, a lot of questions, a lot of excitement, many great new ideas, a lot of idle chat and a mountain of inane and entirely silly humor (remember Ruby's endless puns?). 

As the list matured, the charter members had become comfortable with each other and so we didn't have to ask whether Puddle Splasher was a scurrilous sneak who would steal a box out from under someone's nose given half an opportunity.  We all knew it. 

The Newboxers listed started shortly after this list did, and it has become a well-established forum for asking "dumb newbie questions."  I can't tell you how many times the question "Do I have to carve my own stamp?" question has been answered there, and answered well and with great patience.  This list used to be where Texas newbies asked newbie questions.  This list has much the same environment as Newboxers, but it seems most of those questions go there instead of here now.  That's fine - it's a GREAT list (Mark Pepe is an incredible leader on that list and I'm proud to say I'm a moderator on it for him).  However, sometimes I wish Texas newbies would ask their questions here because they would get the location-specific customs rather than the global answer.  Also, asking questions is a great way to spark conversation with folks who are essentially your neighbors.

I think alot of the newer members got a sense that we were a tight-knit group of friends and felt something like outsiders.  I don't think we originals intended to be exclusive, but I know some new boxers did feel that we were (because they said so).  For my part, I felt that we were indeed a tight-knit group, but we had a "more the merrier" feeling about us and anyone who wanted to could break into our cluster  But that attitude didn't always convey itself. Admittedly though, at times we were going so hard and fast that it would have been hard for a newbie to jump in and get in the mix. 

We have always allowed a wide latitude of discussion on this list - essentially, if you are a letterboxer you can join and talk about whatever you want to talk about (i.e. Blockbuster) as long as it is respectful.  I know that some folks became offended by the opinions expressed by others, and quit participating.  Sometimes they felt the line of respect was crossed - I can't say I always agreed that it was, but yeah - it was crossed on occasion.  But when you have a bunch of folks talking together, somebody is gonna say something that hurts someone else's feelings every now and then, or offends them.  I consider many of the offenders and the offendees to be highly intelligent, interesting and caring folks who have strong opinions about their respective positions.  But then - I LOVE a good debate and have been known to play devil's advocate to generate interesting discussion myself and have gotten myself into hot water on an occasion or two because of that.

When the list started, there were essentially two overriding precepts and one rule.  The rule - Don't plant in national parks.  The precepts - civility in all things and a letterbox is a letterbox if it has clues, a stamp, and a logbook.  In my opinion, this brilliant lack of boundaries (except the national park one) led to creativity and thought-provoking ideas that promoted great discussion and idea-bouncing.  As the game has progressed, everything has to have a name so it can be logged just right or listed just right, so that it has become pretty formula-driven.  What fun is it to talk about THAT?

And I think the creativity level of a large number of boxes has gone down.  How much discussion can a box generate whose clue reads "Go to the Walmart in Jacksonville and park in the Garden Center parking area.  Look under the cover at the base of the light by the shopping cart return."?  On the other hand, may I just say Scarlet Pimpernel - or Murder in the Arboretum?

And folks don't post sitreps.  They used to generate a LOT of discussion!

I have a few other thoughts that I haven't entirely formulated, but I'm looking forward to seeing what other folks have to say.
