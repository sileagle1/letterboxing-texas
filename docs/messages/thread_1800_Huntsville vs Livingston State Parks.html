What are your opinions on Hunstville and Livingston State Parks?

 We stopped by Hunstville State Park this past weekend while camping in Cagle.  It was $8 to get in and the place was packed solid.   Is it just this time of year that makes it packed?   We had a couple of boxes to plant there byut decided against it as it seemed a little high to get in.

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-04-12 16:11:36 UTC-05:00
I haven't been to Huntsville in ages, but I'm guessing that one big difference is the time of year.  Only the hardcore camp and visit state parks in winter months, but once it starts brightening up, lots of folks pour out!  And the only time worse than spring is summer - crowdwise...
 
The cost to get in can add up and I can imagine it was frustrating to pay the fee and then not be able to do what you went to do!  We usually get the Texas Passport, which is $60 for one card and $75 for two.  The main benefit is that everyone in the car enters the park for free (you know, the daily per person fee?).  We decided this year that it probably wasn't worth it to us to renew, until we started having to pay those fees again.  For about six weeks straight, I and some combination of folks were in state parks together, so that we could've save the entire amount in less than two months...I'm a believer again in that Passport!
 
Lucy
