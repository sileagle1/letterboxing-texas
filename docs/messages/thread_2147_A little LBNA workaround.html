A little LBNA workaround

From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-09-10 16:06:47 UTC
Choi posted to the big list that some of the maps on LBNA are messed
up. That's true right now of Texas, as some of the counties aren't
showing up right now and there are some Georgia listings under Texas.

You can use the search function to find what you're looking for. The
webmasters have done a great job with the search function. My
youngest wants to go letterboxing and on a picnic today, but I have a
bad cold, so don't feel up to much hiking. I wanted to look for a
couple of the boxes in the Woodlands we hadn't found, so I did a
county search for Montgomery County and voila! there they all were.

dewberry

From: _the_eidolon_ (the_eidolon@hotmail.com) | Date: 2006-09-13 01:29:30 UTC
It was a minor mess up, it just LOOKED major since it took a little
while to fix up. :>

Choi was resorting Georgia into some subgroups I had sent him and
accidently sorted _ALL_ counties by a specific name, not just the ones
in Georgia. So for instance, Pierce county WA and GA were both in GA as
were Chatham SC and GA, Cook IL and GA (suddenly we had a ton of boxes;
I guess adding Chicago to a county with one box listed will do that)
etc. Then when he undid that sorting, he apparently put all the GA ones
back into the "other" states temporarily until he reresorted back out
the GA only counties.

Luckily I don't anticipate sending him any more resort requests after
having done NC and GA in recent weeks.
