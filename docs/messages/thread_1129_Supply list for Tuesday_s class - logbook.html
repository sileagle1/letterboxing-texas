Hi,

If you're taking my free class on Tuesday night and want to make a
small logbook, I'll teach a couple of very basic techniques for that.
Frankly, they're things that you can figure out on your own, but I'll
show a few "artsy" options, too.

If you're planning to make a logbook, you'll need paper. ANY paper is
fine. Five sheets of 8 1/2" x 11" paper will make a few very small
logbooks.

If you want a heavier cover on your logbook, bring something for that.
Cardboard works fine, but if you want a very flexible cover, you may
want fabric or even UltraSuede.

If you know someone who sews, ask if he or she has leftover scraps for
this. Or, the remnants bin at the fabric store may have what you
want, for cheap. The sale aisles at the fabric store are also a good
resource.

Or, any thrift shop that has cheapo stained clothing and household
items... you can cut around the stain and use the rest of the fabric
or leather or... whatever.

You'll also want embroidery floss, string, or other thick-ish thread.
You can even use yarn or cording, if you want.

You'll also need a nice fat embroidery needle... the
tapestry/upholstery kind work the best because you can punch the holes
with them AND sew with them.

Any store that carries sewing needles--including some Wal-Marts--will
have these needles. Cheap ones are fine... you only need one. Just
be sure that your thread or yarn can fit through the eye of the
needle.

If you like to use a thimble when you sew, bring one. This will also
help push the needle through the paper if you're punching holes that
way.

You will need scissors. Anything that will cut paper and your thread
(and fabric, if you opt for a fabric cover) is fine.

If your pages are going to get a lot of wear, you'll want cellophane
tape. Not the "magic" disappearing tape, but the old fashioned shiny
stuff. One roll will be plenty for the entire class to use.

If you're going to get fancy about this, bring an awl or ice pick to
push holes into your paper. I'll bring a few wooden blocks and a
hammer for anyone who needs to work with a lot of paper at once. I'll
also have my own awl.

See you on Tuesday night!
