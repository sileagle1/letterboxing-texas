SitRep from yesterday's trip out

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-04-30 15:58:06 UTC-05:00
Lightning and I got up bright and early and got my sister-in-law Texas Flutterby yesterday and we all hit the trail with full intentions of hitting the federal areas down in Polk and San Jacinto counties. 
 
I had to pick her up at our in-law's house, and they live out in the boonies.  JB kokopelli's Scarlet and Rhett was right on the way between the in-law's and Highway 69.  It seemed foolish to pass that one by in our hurry to get on down to Coldspring, and since I hadn't yet actually found any of jbk's boxes, we were intrigued.  As I expected, we were FF's, so Lightning and I let TX-Flutterby stamp in first to have the official title.  Turns out - it is in the same cemetery as a GC I tried desperately to find for over an hour, and all I had to show for it was my FIRST case of PI for the season (if anyone besides me is counting - I'm now on my THIRD bout of PI!).  Very cool cemetery though - it has some amazing old headstones and yet is still a working church cemetery.  This little country church obviously loves their property - it is well tended and looks to be used by young and old alike.  And I've now visited there four times and three of those times, I had to dodge other visitors.
We made our way on down to Lufkin, stopping long enough to get something to drink and check out the Forestry Museum, mainly to see if they have the forest maps for sale - they don't.  I could tell Flutterby wanted to go ahead and snag the boxes along the Urban Wildscape Trail, but heartless me told her we had to go!  It was raining anyway...
 
By the time we got to Diboll, I was feeling guilty for not going ahead and getting the Wildscape boxes in Lufkin for her, so we stopped and let her get the Crawlin Down 59 box. 
 
We made our way on down to Livingston then and got the one at the Scout Hut there, Treasure, by ashby.  There were well over a dozen squirrels around there and they ignored us while we stamped in.  I'm just hoping they weren't muggle squirrels that went and unearthed that box after we left... 
 
We were still running early so we went on to Lake Livingston State Park and started working on those there for her benefit (I had found several of them when I visited in March).  I just love the boardwalk trail through that park.  It had apparently rained quite a lot before we got there (it was still raining sporadically while we were there).  There was a lot of water standing around the boardwalk.  We decided we would go ahead and try to find BabyBear's Gone Fishin' box since Will's World had reported finding it after it had been reported missing.  Now of course we knew better than to stick our bare hands behind that step - at first.  We slogged around with a stick, then with our hands after we either scared anything hiding away or made it mad.  We couldn't find the box anywhere, but we did have a little company!  Not 8 feet from us was a water moccasin moving through the water.  We decided that IF that box was there, we were content to let him enjoy it by himself!  We went on and visited the Duck Pond, where we were treated to more snake sightings.  There were two more water moccasins in the water, and one started swimming toward us.  Flutterby grabbed Lightning's stick and slapped the water, thinking it would scare the snake away, but he came on toward us anyway (now really - we were up on the pier and he was in the water, but he did seem a little intent on getting to know us better!).  She slapped the water again and yet he still came on.  When he got within stick's reach, he reared his head up about six inches out of the water and looked her squarely in the eye - I swear he did!  She smacked him directly this time, striking him in the head and he disappeared.  We decided it was time for us to move on too, in case he was gathering his snake army (I guess they wouldn't have foot soldiers, would they?) to come and meet us.  Now bear in mind, I am not particularly afraid of snakes (I respect 'em, mind you, but I can live in peace and harmony with them as long as the poisonous ones stay out of my yard), but that was a freaky experience.  I had a momentary visioin of that snake wrapping around the stick and her slinging him back high over our heads - UGH!
 
We went on to find The Jig, which was still playing host to Chillin' At Tonkawa.  We picked up Tonkawa at Frye Team's request, so it is no longer there as of yesterday. We also got Silver Eagle's Livingston Largemouth, Lucy Locket's Hank Hill, and Baby Bear's Udder Madness.
 
We were able to find all the boxes in LLSP - except one.  The trail that is home to State Mammal by Quetzel was being deluged by under sixers.  My goodness!  I have no idea what the appeal was on that trail, but there were more rugrats on that trail than I think I've ever seen in total in state parks.  Weird.  We didn't even try it because we were a little intimidated at the thought of being so overwhelmingly outnumbered by munchkins, and I was wearing shorts and didn't feel my ankles were properly protected anyway.
 
Unfortunately, we had to cut our trip short after LLSP - I had had a headache all morning, and it eventually became a migraine.  We wound up having to head on home, but ironically we had to stop in Lufkin for a bit so I could get a reprieve from the motion of the car.  Wound up Lightning and Flutterby went and got the boxes on the Wildscape Trail.  Next time?  I'll let her get 'em when she wants to - it wasn't worth the headache in the end!
 
In the end, Flutterby came away with something like 13 finds.  I found a few I hadn't yet gotten, we released one HH, retrieved a box for Frye Team, and rescued a box that was soaked.  I sent Flutterby home with a HH and a box I had made but hadn't gotten around to planting, so look for something new in the Navarro County area. 
 
We are hoping to resume our trip in the near future, hopefully spending the weekend camping down that direction somewhere so we can really go to work!

--
Barefoot Lucy
"It's not about footwear, it's about philosophy"

You take too long!
 
I could do all the Letterboxes I am eligible for in El Paso County in 30 minutes.
 
If I include the one in the Franklin Mountains - that makes 1 (ONE) letterbox.  <grin>
 
I have actually held that letterbox in my hand and looked at the stamp.  But I didn't log it, I wanted to save it for a rainy day.
 
I have 5 letterboxes out, I have one almost ready to place - and I have an idea for a 7th.  I am so hoping that someone else starts putting out a few for me.  We have nearly 300 Geocaches within 100 miles of El Paso.  I hope some day we have Letterboxes like that too.
 
So today we went to a Hamfest.  I spent a little $$$ on some cable connectors for the antennas on the wife's dog show van.  I also got some DC power connectors.  That was about it.  The dogs had a great time socializing, and their friend Krystal (A Great Dane from Deming NM) was there too.  They had a Chili cook-off, so we had lunch, then came back home.
 
NO LETTERBOXES TODAY!   But.....  TWO next week at Buffalo Gap when we camp with the Ham Radio group.
 
I envy you the plethora of Letterboxes in your area.
 
Mike.
El Paso, TX.

When you come out of the State Park, turn right, then right again on
FM 1988 (left would take you back to Livingston), then right again
on FM 3278. This takes you across the Trinity River right in front
of the dam. FM 3278 deadends into FM 222 at Camilla. Take another
right. FM 222 deadends into SH 150. Turn right again and you will
be in Coldspring. Just keep turning right!

When you come out of the State Park, turn right, then right again on
FM 1988 (left would take you back to Livingston), then right again
on FM 3278.  This takes you across the Trinity River right in front
of the dam. FM 3278 deadends into FM 222 at Camilla.  Take another

be in Coldspring.  Just keep turning right!
