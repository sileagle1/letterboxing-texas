Hmmmmm....  it is tempting.
 
How old is he?  Good eyesight?  Not afraid of trying new things?  Not afraid of loud noises (with proper hearing protection of course)?  Likes dogs?  Likes to travel. explore, hike, camp?
 
If he can listen to a radio drama-show and/or read books and use his imagination, thats a plus.  If he likes to sit in front of the TV, thats a minus.  Computers are OK, but within reason.  He must learn how to think for himself, and sometimes quickly.  He can't allow himself to be brainwashed by one source of information, but rather to look at it all and form his own opinion.  For everything.
 
I must warn you though...  he will likely turn out like my son.  James is a Cavalry Scout - Sniper - Paramedic in the 3d Cavalry.  He likes what he does, and he is good at it too. 
 
In my circles, he is thought of as a good young man.  The advantage is when he is home, he never has to pay for a beer.  The down-side is he is never home enough.  Just about the time he started getting really interesting - he went off to defend the country.  I am proud of him, but I miss him too.  The sad part is that if the army would have me back, I would go join him.  Two snipers, a lefty and a righty, sharing the same foxhole.  What a combo.
 
Dunno - who would want to make their kid turn out like that?  <grin>
 
Mike.
