Re: Thank-you!

From: Boots Tex (redbarn@eastex.net) | Date: 2011-10-24 16:26:32 UTC
Roarin' Lion was a good letterboxing companion, he did all the work and was a fun and polite young gentleman, it was our pleasure. And, yes, thanks to Open Space and her family for all the work and expense it must have taken to put on this great event. It will be part of tomorrow's Letterboxing Legacy! There were certainly a lot of new letterboxers and their boxing and carving skills are amazing! If you weren't there, you really need to go find all of those new letterboxes. And, of course, there were old friends we hadn't seen for a while, and the chance to catch up on all their exploits. We certainly missed those "legends" who couldn't make it, like Silver Eagle and Baby Bear, dewberry and Mother of Five, Barefoot Lucy. Many people were disappointed that dewberry and Mother of Five weren't there because it's been way too long since we've seen you ladies! MO5, you would have loved to meet our newest Texas transplants from Michigan, DSMA, who now call The Woodlands home. Oh, and sorry about those Detroit Tigers, they had a great year. We won't mention the Astros.

Boots Tex

Hey!!! The TEXAS Rangers aren't exactly dog meat! Since we've never won a World Series, this is pretty exciting stuff for us up here in the north part of Texas. Speaking of Thank-yous….  I’d like to give a special thanks to some other letterboxers for all their help before and during the event.  NLW was my partner in crime.  She helped come up with ideas for activities, created the trivia question list, embroidered all the cool raffle prizes (with her mom’s help!), and helped set things up before the event.  Mosaic Butterfly designed the new Texas patches, planted the wonderful Monty Python night boxing series, organized the dinner Friday, and encouraged me the whole way.  Wry Me helped me with some last minute preparations and kept me entertained with her usual humor. 

 

Anyway, I’m so glad we had such a big turnout and everyone enjoyed it.
