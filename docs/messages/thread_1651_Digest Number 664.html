Of course.  In fact, I recommend it.  I have found that if you involve the rangers they usually try to meet you more than 1/2 way.  Geocaching (and now letterboxing) have been received by the rangers quite well in El Paso.
 
If you think about it, most rangers are after the same thing we are after.  To preserve the area for others to enjoy.  By placing the object of our game in the area, we change the area.  There are things that happen in parks that we are not privy to.  Mating areas that are protected, spots where a certain plant is trying to come back.... whatever.  Asking permission to place an object in those areas is the only way you will know if it is safe or will cause some damage. 
 
In El Paso, we have had only one geocache rejected.  It was too near a cliff.
 
Of course, you could always run into a jerk of a ranger who says no to everything.  But I take that chance - would rather not hide something in a place where it may cause harm.
 
My opinion only - YMMV!
 
Mike.  El Paso.
