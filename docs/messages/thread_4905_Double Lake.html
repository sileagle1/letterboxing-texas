The town where we live was named "Cold Springs" because of the several spring-fed creeks in the area. (It's now Coldspring). One of those creeks, called Double Creek, (actually two creeks very close together), were dammed up in the 1930's by the CCC to form a 28 acre lake called Double Lake, and for 75 years, it has been a nice little fishing and camping lake, made into a recration area by the surrounding National Forest. When I saw Mr. Gryzzled Gryphon at the event the other day, he mentioned that they might need to rename that lake "Double Pond". Although it's very close to our house, we hadn't been over there all summer, so I was surprised. Today was a beautiful day to walk and there was a new letterbox to find and one to check on, so I went over to Double Lake and I have to say I disagree with the Gryzzled one. It should now be called "Double Desert" because it is completely dry. I don't know if those springs had ever gone dry before, but if they did, it was a long time ago. It was a sad sight to see. The letterboxes seem to be fine except for Lone Star Quilter's March Santa box that had been hiding behind a large leaning pine tree, which is now a large fallen pine tree. Although I assume the box is still under the tree (way under the tree), I'm confident that Santa had already departed for the North Pole, so he should be fine. When he returns next March, we'll find him a new hiding place.

Boots Tex
