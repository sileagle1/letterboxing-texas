From: Mike - RR (mike-2007@elp.rr.com) | Date: 2006-12-26 01:46:38 UTC
OK - I too have the 60CS.  It is a great hikers GPS.  I have loaded it with TOPO maps - I use a laptop for driving maps.
 
I am kinda bad about this - but I will have NOTHING to do with magellan - or any other company that puts our soldiers in greater danger.
 
Have you turned the compass ON?  Check your manual - page 15.  Press and hold the PAGE button a few seconds - you should get an ICON in the upper left showing a compass rose when it is on.  Site-N-Go should work then also. 
 
Notice that when you hold the compass level to your eye you should see the white dots at the top and bottom of the screen lining up.  Site them to your target and hit enter to activate the site-n-go. 
 
That should get you going.  BUT - a word of warning.  The electronic Compass uses up energy quickly.  You should keep it off except for when you use it.

Don't worry - we will work through this.
 
OK - lets back up.  How many satellites are you getting locked in?  You must have at least a 2-D fix before you can site-n-go.  This requires at least 4 satellites with a steady lock.  If you are working indoors and cannot get at least 3 satellites to lock, it will not work.
 
Also, the compass page will not show you a compass arrow unless you have some navigation function working.  This means you must have selected a waypoint to "GOTO" before you get an arrow, or invoke a site-n-go to get an arrow.
 
I just pulled mine out and tried it.  It would do nothing without at least 3 satellites locked.  Once I got a 2-D fix, site-n-go works.
