Lucy, while we're waiting for Brother Eagle to swallow his S'mores and for
Brother Bear to finish stirring the campfire, I'd love to tell you all
something neat I found digging around in the archives:

Perhaps the very first Texas letterboxer was a woman named Julie who lived
in Garland, just outside of Dallas. From her posts she sounds warm, witty
and spirited!
She organized the first gathering of letterboxers in Texas: "The First
Letterboxing Congress and Muffaletta Fest" was held in Dallas in late
October of 1998. I wish she mentioned WHERE they had their muffalettas, but
I'm willing to wager it may have been at Crescent City Cafe!

She planted 4 boxes along Duck Creek in that Garland, but unfortunately none
of them exist today- however, with a very little sleuthing ( and an original
tip-off from Silver Eagle) I've unearthed 3 out of 4 sets of clues and we
plan to re-create the series as a tribute to Julie.

If anyone is still in contact with her, I'd love to let her know or plans.
Her original email address is no longer active!

CelticLions

I thought that was really interesting about the first
gathering - that has my interest really piqued!

I've not been able to help myself - I'm well down a
rabbit hole at the moment - I've delved into the
history and have been reading and running inquiries
off LBNA to see how the influx began and when folks
started really getting involved. Of course, all I can
get from that is planting history, but folks can't
find 'em if they aren't planted so I figured that was
the place to start anyway. I haven't even begun to
look in the basement, but I feel the draw and I'm
gonna have to go there - probably tonight. I find it
interesting that the earliest active boxes on LBNA are
Eoghan's planted in 2000/2001. No record or reference
even to anything prior to that - all in the basement
at this point, I'm sure. Soooo - I have to wonder how
many boxers were at that fateful 1998 gathering. Was
there a world of boxers we don't know about?

Anyway, for those of you who know much about Boy
Scouts, I attended Wood Badge this Spring and am now
in the process of completing my tickets. I've really
got to get one done this summer - writing a resource
guide for local packs - and I am high centered on it
at the moment! Consequently I've diverted my
attention from that to this very interesting
self-imposed history project for the moment...lol.
