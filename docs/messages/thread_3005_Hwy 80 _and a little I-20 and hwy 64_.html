From: Amy (aymztx@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-08-13 00:04:29 UTC
We went to visit the mother-in-law this weekend to pick up our kids
and we hit several on the way down and on the way back. Here's a list
of the ones we found:

Terrell:

Nana's Hummingbird - Nice cemetery, and nicely carved stamp. Saw some
really large creepy garden spiders while we were there. (not much of
a spider fan here! haha)

Wills Point/Lake Tawakoni area:

Shelob's Lair - She warned that if the spiders didn't get you, then
something would. I think it was the heat and the mosquitos for us.
We did enjoy the trails and the views though!

Fairy At Work - took us a couple of tries to navigate the splits,
initially went too far. Glad we found this one though. We stopped on
the way to get the kids, and when they saw the stamp later, they were
jealous!

Mineola:

Grandpa's Pocket Watch - Another neat historical cemetery. Enjoyed
the little story about "Candy Man" and the pocket watch. Sounds like
a really special man.

Canton:

Splash Kingdom box for Kami - Was unable to find. Sent her some
pictures of where we looked. Hoping we looked in the wrong spot or
something.. otherwise it may be missing... if we looked in the wrong
spot, she may still need another person to stop by and look again and
rehide.

Anyhow, these are the ones we hit this weekend. Thanks to those who
planted these, we really enjoyed finding them!

aymztx (and redoggy.. and katiefrog and blue squirrel (I can't get
them to make accounts on letterboxing or atlas..))

Well, you can't do it right on the web site, but if you have either MS Word or Excel, you can go to a regional list of boxes on LBNA (or any box list) and highlight the rows of box names, placers, etc.  Use Edit, Copy (or Ctrl C) to copy the list, then open Word or Excel and use Edit, Paste (or Ctrl P) to paste the info.  You can sort the table by the Box Name column.
