About group security

From: remymom (remymom@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-09-17 12:45:51 UTC
Hello all- Bunnybee from Amarillo here! :-)

I have checked out myself on search engines... and I am surprised at
some of the things that get pulled up. Sometimes it's something
fairly personal (like an address) but most of the time, I'm finding
info about someone else (with the same name) or I'm not even found.
But yeah- it IS scary just what people can find out about you if they
really know where and how to dig. The internet is quite the open door!

Anyway, about letterboxing and this forum... I think it should be
required that people offer some personal info if they want to join.
Sometimes that is enough to discourage the ones who really aren't
interested. And to send an email to those who have joined but never
introduced themselves... and if they don't respond, then go ahead and
delete them. They aren't active here anyway- so what would that
matter? And if they decide to introduce themselves, then that's all
the better- maybe they'll get active and jump in, too!

And as for actual going-out-and-finding-boxes kind of security... I'd
suggest not posting on here where you're headed for the weekend, just
in case we do have unsavory lurkers looking for an opportunity to be
unsavory. I don't like to get paranoid (or to get anyone else here
that way) but I do believe in internet safety -so much that you never
announce to a group online when and where you will be. Especially
with something like letterboxing- they could get directions to the
place just by clicking on a link. So yeah- come and report what boxes
you've found AFTER you've been there- but don't announce it
beforehand. Or if you're headed somewhere and have a question to ask
first, at least leave it open to several days when you might be
there, and be vague on any specifics.

I think once we've figured out who is here and who's who... that
won't be such a factor- but for now, I'd rather not announce my plans
ahead of time. Thankfully, I am relieved we've never heard of anyone
using letterboxing or geocaching clues to do any criminal acts or as
a way of finding victims. I know- my head works in weird ways- but I
hope everyone will realize just how safe letterboxing is and has been-
just be aware of what you're doing at all times anyway.

~Bunnybee
