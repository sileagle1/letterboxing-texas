Image Transfers

From: babybearlb2002 (babybearlb2002@yahoo.com) | Date: 2013-09-11 19:16:06 UTC
While at L&B XV, the Michigan group told me of another way to transfer images to your medium. Many of you may already be doing this, but I seem to lag behind in learning these helpful aids, so thought I would share in case anyone else does not know about this. I tried it this week and what a difference (hopefully you will see that difference in upcoming carves from me). Here it is:

You need a laser printer and Acetone. Print the image you want using a laser printer. Place image face down on your medium (pink stuff). Rub acetone on the back of the image to transfer to medium (you can experiment with how much needed and how hard to rub, as I'm still working on that). I used cotton ear swab to do it, and worked well.

Anyway, may not be new to anyone, but wanted to share just in case.

From: Debbie Kotlarek (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2013-09-11 14:42:06 UTC-05:00

That's the way I've always done it, except I "pat/push" on the back of the paper, rather than "rub". Rubbing can sometimes smear the image.  Another trick is to first lightly rub the carving media w/acetone before laying the image on top of it, then pat the back with acetone on an old (clean) rag.

From: Christine Borgfeld (christine@borgfeld.net) | Date: 2013-09-11 17:47:40 UTC-05:00

I’m still experimenting with the laser transfers and it really depends on the printer.  The old printers/copiers (like they tend to have a public libraries) take very little force (patting).  I recently got a laser printer for home and it does take some rubbing to get a clean transfer.  The laser printer where I work won’t make transfers at all.  Transfers also work best with freshly printed images.  If you wait a couple of months they don’t work so well. 

 

Also – for those using pink stuff to carve, the material has changed recently.  This has been discussed to death on the forums but there are two batches out right now that have issues. One is crumbly, and the other is much softer than normal.  If you have some of the soft material, be very careful putting acetone directly on the stamp.  Even light rubbing with a cotton ball will ruin the surface.  I learned this the hard way while trying to clean off a bad transfer.  Hopefully the manufacturer will get this sorted out soon (several people on AQ are working with them directly and say a solution is in the works).

From: Wry Me (wry_me@yahoo.com) | Date: 2013-09-11 18:25:25 UTC-07:00
You can enhance either solvent (xylene, acetone, wintergreen oil) transfers with heat -- I use an iron at the highest setting before it makes steam, and cover the transfer image w/ paper (after rubbing it w/ xylene) so I don't ruin my iron.  Use less heat with the crappy pink stuff.  You can also use heat alone.  Or solvent alone.
 
And you're right about the various printers as well.  I've successfully transferred images that were months old (did one last night, in fact) and it worked just fine. But, as you know, the older one I got from you did squat.
