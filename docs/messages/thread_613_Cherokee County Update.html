Cherokee County Update

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-02-14 14:26:57 UTC-08:00
I had a really busy weekend planned, but through a
series of quirky events I found myself whisked away at
1 a.m. Saturday morning to go camping with Cub Scouts
(long story - but basically, a couple of leaders were
taken ill and they needed my credentials, not me).
Luckily, the campout happened to be happening in a
park where there are two letterboxes, so I checked
them out.

One of them, Crowin Bout the Railroad, I had never
found. This box was created by one of the younger
Birds of a Feather flock, and it was a cool box.
Scarcely a word to the clues, but oh so clever! Silly
me - I had a preconceived idea of what the stamp was
going to be, so it took me a minute or two to finally
figure out why it was named what it was. DUHHHH! The
other DUH thing I did was to grab the wrong stamp out
of our kit, so I got to the box and went to stamp in,
only to find that I had my son's stamp instead. I
stamped in anyway, and made a note to the effect that
it was me stamping in disguise. I'll probably get
back there fairly soon and be able to stamp in
properly. I'm going to chalk that up to being
disturbed in the middle of the night!

The other box in the park was Texas RR by Silver
Eagle. It had been reported missing by Birds of a
Feather, but since I had previously found that box and
knew exactly where it was, I decided to check in on it
anyway. It doesn't look good - I couldn't find it
either. There had been a lot of disturbance in the
area. I'm going to report what I found to Silver
Eagle so he can do what he wants about it, so check
the clues before you go hunting for it!

Lucy
