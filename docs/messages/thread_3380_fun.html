I say - take the test and get it back.  Quality handheld radios are very much available at good prices.  Mobile radios too.  Especially good used units.  I have such fun talking to people as we travel to and from dog shows.  And did you know there is a "National Radio System of Texas"?  Sitting in El Paso with a pocket-sized radio, I was able to talk to my son at Ft. Hood while he was on his sail-boat.  http://www.armadillo.org/
 
The test has only gotten easier.  They simplified the license structure, there are only 3 classes of license now.  Technician is the beginners class.  General, just what it sounds like.  And extra, you get it all.
 
Anyone who has ever looked at my backpack has seen the 24 inch antenna sticking out of it.  I never hit the trail without a radio.
 
Let me toss out this offer to you, and anyone else interested.  I can give you the URL for the study manual.  And if several are interested, we could answer questions via E-mail.  If there are several, perhaps even using the list just so long as the message was marked RADIO or something like that.  I would be most pleased to help anyone get started.
 
Remember this.  Some year all the bad things I did to my body are going to come back to me.  I won't be able to see the sights on my guns.  My legs won't be able to take me down the trails anymore.  My back will be too bad to stand riding in my jeep anymore.  Training dogs would get me jerked to the ground.
 
BUT - I can lay in bed and use a radio.  I can still participate in things outside my home through others.  I can stay on the air till my last minutes...... not many hobbies can give you that.
 
Lemme know, I will look up the study info and post it.
