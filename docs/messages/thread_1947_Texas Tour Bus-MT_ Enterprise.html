Texas Tour Bus-MT. Enterprise

From: hellbrunn (hellbrunn@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-06-26 19:10:15 UTC
The following is an excerpt from a police officer's surveillance
log.

Officer J. Smith Reporting

21 June 2006
I've followed a camouflage bus from south east Texas to a small town
called Nacogdoches. The bus pulled in this evening to a forest,
which is apparently operated by the local college.

22 June 2006
I watched the people on the bus last night. There seem to be all
kinds of people...politicians, athletes, musicians, and others. I'm
not exactly sure why all of these people are traveling together. It
must be some sort of illegal activity.

This morning, the bus moved to a residential area in Nacogdoches. I
reported my findings to the Chief, and he said to continue my
observations.

26 June 2006
The riders of the bus got on again this morning, but it looks like
they've added two people. They appear to be Tex Ritter and
Frederick Fennell. Once again, I have no idea what these people
have in common.

I followed the cammo bus out of Nacogdoches on 259. They are headed
north. When they got to Mt. Enterprise, they turned East onto 84.
My guess was they are headed for another secluded area. I was
right. They went about two miles, then they pulled off on the side
of the road. This bus must have four wheel drive, because they went
right into the woods! When I realized where they pulled in, I
stopped at the head of the Griff Ross trail and decided to leave my
car at the small parking area.

I walked along the trail, and when I came to a T, I followed the bus
tracks to the right. I walked along, and when I came to a sign that
said WHITE OAK, I stopped and pretended to read the sign. Actually,
I was listening for the noise of this rowdy bunch. Sure enough, I
could hear them. I went 15 steps from the White Oak sign, and
looked left. I could see the camo bus. It is behind a pine tree
about 8 steps off the trail. I'll find a place to camp out and see
what happens next.
