Letterboxing History

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-01-01 21:20:42 UTC-06:00
As many of you know, Mark and Sue Pepe do interviews with letterboxing pioneers from time to time.  The one they just posted today is outstanding (but they all are) and I think many members of our group would find it fascinating.  It's about one of the very first letterboxers who eventually left the limelight of the hobby due to controversy about the direction the hobby should take.  Here a year or so ago (yep, right about a year ago, because the annual gathering was approaching) I did a lot of research about the beginning of letterboxing in Texas.  My research took me to the first posts on the Big List and a world opened up before my eyes.  This man, Dan Servatius, played a big role in the "internetization" (<===New word - hehe) of letterboxing.  He was also instrumental in setting the direction of letterboxing in our country.  And Mark (I know you're lurking, Mark, so WAY TO GO!) scored an interview with Dan.  This is a great read and I think it will help us all, and especially newbies, get a really cool perspective on the hobby.  
 
  http://danservatius.blogspot.com/
