From: Rachel Stout (prazehm878@yahoo.com) | Date: 2012-08-03 01:27:04 UTC
I love it when I find boxes that have been planted quite some time ago! I think the boxes listed on LBNA are a little more "protected" than those on the AQ because they are not quite as advertised-but perhaps that's just my opinion. The one I found today was planted back in 2005, and was still in great shape-still the first logbook-and still has plenty of room for more stamps. LBNA shows the planter, Catpawz2004, as being inactive, but because the stamp is hidden in such a great location, it has been safe all these years.
http://lifeasitistous.blogspot.com/2012/08/a-passport-to-italy.html

What factors do you think contribute to the longevity of boxes?
LBNA vs Atlasquest?
Locations that are not visited often?
What else?

Elwood Fan



Re: Found an old box today

From: Boots Tex (redbarn@eastex.net) | Date: 2012-08-03 17:03:35 UTC
I remember Italy! It was cool to see that the box is still there and my original sig stamp to prove that I was there six years ago. We were making a sweep of boxes up into East Texas and down to Waco. It took us two days and we found 47 boxes. The temperature was 104 degrees that day, kind of like it is now up in that area. I think that good locations help, but I can think of a lot of instances where that didn't work. The amount of care taken by the finder in avoiding detection and replacing the box well certainly is a factor. Locations that are not visited often helps, because that means fewer finders. Unfortunately, many letterboxers don't go to out of the way places or find boxes that require a long walk. Sometimes, it's just luck, I think. Thanks for bringing back those memories, Elwood Fan.

Boots Tex
