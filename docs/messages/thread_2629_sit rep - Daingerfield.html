Sounds like everyone had a great time at the gathering. Wish I
could have been there - hopefully next year!

I went to the Daingerfield State Park on Sunday, to hike and
search for the 9 boxes that JKC put there.

First I have to say that it was was a very enjoyable series.
The park is nice and we really liked the trail around the lake. The
walk was an easy one, relatively flat ground, nice path, and I liked
that there was also a path going back to the area of your car. The
stamps were great. And the smoldering ground, smoke, and soot make
things all that more interesting.

I believe that the park did a controlled burn, since some of the
trees had the needles, etc pulled away from their trunks and the
charred areas didn't cross the paths... But I didn't get to ask at
the desk because they were closed when we were leaving. Half of the
park was burned and a lot of areas still had places that were
smoldering.

Sadly, some of the boxes were hit by this. Danger, Heron, Bass,
and Grasshopper were fine (even though Grasshopper was in a burned
area). Rattler and Armadillo were fine as far as the stamp and log.
The boxes were slightly melted on one side. They got new bags and
will have to be opened a little carefully. Deer and Lizard were
definately melted. I did not find Frog. They had cleared around the
tree where it was hidden. It might have been picked up or could be
in the area in what they raked - but that area was still smoldering
pretty badly, so I couldn't look very well. GG will remain first AND
only finder on those :) - unless they get replaced...

So, in addition to marker fingers, I had some soot smudges here
and there, and looked like I really had a good time doing something
or had a bad time doing something, depending on what anyone may have
thought that I had been doing. :) I think the soot on my backpack
might be there a while.

I hope some others get the opportunity to enjoy this park and
find these boxes. JKC did a good job with them and I feel bad that
they had problems so quickly. You might want to call ahead for a
fire report before making the drive out there, if this is something
the park does annually or something. If we had gotten there a little
sooner, I don't think we would have been allowed in the area.

If this was a controlled burn, I wonder how often other parks do
this...?

((A side note - for anyone who likes crawfish or cajun food - there
is a neat little place you can stop on your way out there if you are
going up from the south. It is the Crawfish Shack. It is in Big
Sandy on 155, after you cross over the Sabine bridge and the bridge
right after that. It's on the right, across from the Industrial
Park. They are currently open 7 days a week. It is a little stand
like they have at fairs, where you walk up to the window. You can
get the food to go, or you can walk around the back, where they have
it tented in with picnic tables, heaters this time of year, a sink to
wash up in,... There was a truck parked there also, that said Circle
M crawfish and had the number 903-360-8946.))
