From: (blhjrh@aol.com) | Date: 2005-06-28 15:29:21 UTC-04:00
I like both LBNA and AQ, and they do have some differences.  LBNA is the more complete list; not everyone has posted their clues on AQ.  But AQ allows for specific searches by distance from a particular city, along a highway route, etc.  And you can post your clues on AQ and link them automatically to LBNA so they show up on both sites.  They each have some distinct features to manage your own LB count, too, and message boards. 

Re: Atlas Quest

From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2005-06-28 20:35:48 UTC
Atlas Quest is less than a year old, so it was designed once
letterboxing community was already firmly established. LBNA has
evolved and changed many times over the years. The webmasters do a
good job at both sites of responding to people's needs and changing
the sites as technology improves. As someone else pointed out, LBNA
is the mother ship and has the majority of the clues, although there
are a few on AQ that aren't listed anywhere else. The two sites are
not competitive with each other. Most people who use AQ also use LBNA
and cross-list boxes; it is more a question of personal preference.

Personally, I prefer AQ. I like the way their boards are set up. But
I still use LBNA all the time and derive a lot of benefit from it too.

Now for a city like Houston that is so huge, neither of those sites do
a great job of helping you figure out where in the city/county the
letterboxes are hidden. For that, I much prefer Birds of a Feather
Texas Letterboxing Atlas.

Hope that helps.
