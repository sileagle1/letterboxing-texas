Good morning,

 

Glad you enjoyed your visit to El Paso. Also glad we were able to stumble on an exchange with you. The food there is superb; we try to visit that restaurant about once a quarter. A fine restaurant like that AND a letterbox, what more could someone ask for?

 

Of the other boxes, that one box was put there for exactly that reason, to amuse the rangers. And it has on a number of occasions. The others are just normal boxes, hidden in normal ways, nothing special or fancy. But I do try to put them in unique and nice places. I am so pleased you thought they were good.

 

Most of my hides are listed as letterboxes AND geocaches. I think both hobbies can co-exist, and putting some of them together will make others more aware of the other hobby. Might even get a few people interested in Letterboxing. And the ones who see it and take the initiative to learn more on their own are likely to be better boxers than those recruited but not really interested. So far, it has not been successful.

 

So glad you made it out here. And you placed another letterbox. Thank you. I am tempted to gear up and go after it right now. But that would take away the only “unfound” box I would have within 100 miles. So I think I will save it for another day.

 

BTW – did you get to check on your other box? I hope you approved of the place I moved it to.

 

Y-all take care.

Geocaching and Letterboxing together (was Re: [letterboxingtexas] : el paso)

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-01-06 18:23:35 UTC-06:00


On Jan 6, 2008 9:49 AM, Mike Olbrisch <mike-2007@elp.rr.com> wrote:
 
Most of my hides are listed as letterboxes AND geocaches.  I think both hobbies can co-exist, and putting some of them together will make others more aware of the other hobby.  Might even get a few people interested in Letterboxing.  And the ones who see it and take the initiative to learn more on their own are likely to be better boxers than those recruited but not really interested.  So far, it has not been successful.--
**************************************************
 
We have the GC vs LB discussion every now and then, and about a year ago I reported that a GC'er had placed a cache very near my Spring Hope box (one of my personal faves, FYI).  This particular GC'er is also a friend of mine from high school and then later from soccer, and our paths crossed again as we each participated in these two hobbies.
 
It just happened that we discovered we had stashes close together and we agreed to monitor them to see how they do together, and to make a note in each of our clues that there is another stash of the other type nearby and also noting what to do if you find the other one.  He agreed to move his cache if the proximity caused problems since my box had been there first.
 
It has worked out very well!  Since that time the duplication has happened by accident several more times (I guess when you grow prowling the same places, you're going to have similar ideas for hides) and in each case we repeat what we did with the Spring Hope location.  We're to the point that we tip each other on cool locations and we send each other links to respective hides. 
 
He has a caching buddy (in no way related to the "boxing buddy" fad we are having right now - but a real life person he caches with) who I had met through my few forays into gc'ing and that guy now carries a stamp and logbook in case he happens across a cache.
 
I've also noticed that cachers who find boxes in our area are, for the most part, familiar with our hobby and know what to do, and they do it.  I'm sure these two guys (pearle and Fire2Water, if you want to know) have been helpful in that respect, since they seem to be the top GC dogs around here.
 
I hope that other of us are having similar experiences with Geocachers.  I know there are those rude individuals GC'ers (and, I'm sure, LB'ers) who don't or won't get with the program, but maybe we are all making inroads together.
 
 
Barefoot Lucy
