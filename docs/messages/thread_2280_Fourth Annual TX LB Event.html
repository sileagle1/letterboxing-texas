Fourth Annual TX LB Event

From: Silver Eagle (sileagle@alltel.net) | Date: 2006-11-03 03:07:59 UTC
It's time to start thinking about our next Event. I am thinking
about having it on the first Saturday in March (3/3/2007), but not
sure where. In the past, we have rotated it through different
regions of TX; first in Central, then in Southeast, then Northeast.
I propose that we have it in Central again (and possibly from now
on) so that most letterboxers in TX have a somewhat equal chance of
attending. We could continue to have other Gatherings in other
regions (like Great Book Adventure in Southeast and the upcoming
Mini-Meese in North), but the annual one would be centrally located.

I've looked at several State Parks in the Central Region and come up
with a few suggestions. Unfortunately, some of my original choices
(Pedernales, Guadalupe) don't have pavilions, so won't work. Inks
Lake has lots of camping, but pavilion only holds 25 people.
Mckinney Falls has over 80 campsites, but only has a dining hall
that is pretty expensive. Palmetto has about 40 sites, but also has
an expensive dining hall. Mother Neff only has about 20 campsites,
but has a great rock pavilion that holds 250 people and is
moderately priced. I only looked at State Parks, so there might be
other choices out there I'm not aware of.

Let me know what you think about my ideas and let's discuss options.
Thanks,

Silver Eagle

From: Baby Bear (babybearlb2002@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-11-03 20:40:39 UTC
I am still researching, but think these will be our best options for
the gathering, and some notes. We will need to check details on
each of these parks.

1) Palmetto - Great stone pavillion. 3 miles of hiking trails (but
low flood area for most). 41 campsites. Hotels - not real close.
2) McKinney Falls - Dinning hall (Have not seen). 8 miles of hiking
trails. 84 campsites. Hotels about 10 miles.
3) Fort Parker - Dinning Hall (have not seen). 4 miles of hiking
trails. 25 campsites. 10 shelters. No local hotels.
4) Lake Somerville-Birch Creek - 2 Group picnic areas (have not
seen). 8 miles of hiking trails (and 13 mile trail to Nails Creek
unit). 103 campsites. Hotels in Brenham.
5) Enchanted Rock - Group Pavillion (have not seen). Miles of
hiking trials (but is a State Natural Area, so some concern about
boxes getting into trouble once they find out). 46 campsites(but
books up early). Hotels in Fredericksburg....wineries...
6) Lake Livingston - Recreation Hall and Group Pavillion (have not
seen). 8 miles of hiking trails. 147 campsites. Shelters. Hotels
in Livingston. Park has a store. If not this event, this would be
a great location.

* Bastrop - Can't see doing there again (yet). Bastrop Lake is
okay, but still think it is to close to other event location.
* There are other Corp of Eng. parks around Lake Somerville that may
be worth a look.

I think the top 3 are: Lake Livingston, McKinney Falls, and Lake
Somerville. I also agree that costs should not stop us (unless we
are talking $250 or more) from going to the best option. I will
discuss with Silver Eagle this weekend, and would love more of
everyone's input (best location and why).

Baby Bear
