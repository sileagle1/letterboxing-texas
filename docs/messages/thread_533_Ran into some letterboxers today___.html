Ran into some letterboxers today...

From: Wanda (wsthm@hotmail.com) | Date: 2005-01-04 02:30:25 UTC

Today turned into an interesting day. First of all, I got an email
that my Lost and Found Leaf Micro-box was open, scattered, damp and
the stamp was missing.

Then when my grandson Zach...uh, Cabaio Dieblo, uh...just plain
Caballo(?) and I went out to check on The Beehive Hitchhiker Hostel,
we ran into a grandmother, mom, and two children (ages 10 & 7)
stamping in to 'Watchful Birds'. They were the group who had emailed
this morning about 'Lost and Found Leaf'. The mom and kids are
visiting from Colorado, and will be returning tomorrow, but they
have been letterboxing all over the area - Rockport, Goose Island
and other places. Actually, the mom and grandmother are not
letterboxers. The daughter learned about it from her teacher, who
introduced it to the class and had them all carve stamps. So the two
children are actually the letterboxers, with the support of their
mom and the cheerful accompaniment of their grandmother. :) The
grandmother, who lives in Beeville is planning to 'spread the word'
to a history group, I think she said. So we may get some more
letterboxers in the area. :)

Anyway, I let the kids take the only hitchhiker currently laying
over in the hostel and then I hurried home to replace it with
another. Half-Hearted Hitchhiker is now in residence, as is 'Magic
Marble' - one that Caballo carved while we were getting ready to
return to the hostel. I also have the replacement 'Lost and Found
Leaf' ready to place again tomorrow.

We had a great time visiting with the group, and exchanging stamps
with 'Huey Bear' and 'Emily Bear'.

Busy Bee
