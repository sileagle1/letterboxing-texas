Personal travelers are cool, in my opinion.

It is a stamp you carry, different from your signature stamp, that you
offer to people when you run into them--on the trail or at a
gathering. It started from the fact that people didn't want to give
away their "signature" stamp, so they devised another one for
exchanges. Now, most people do use their signature stamp for
exchanges, so the personal travelers are an extra--kind of fun--little
bit of nonsense.

To get them, you often need to solve a riddle, give a bit of
information or perform a little trick. Sometimes you can just ask for
them. Clues to how to get a personal traveler are located on LBNA
(often as mystery boxes), in the archives of this list and Atlas
Quest--if you go to the TX gathering page for instance on AQ you will
see a list of those people who have RSVP'd for the gathering who have
listed their personal travelers on AQ. Then, just click on clue.
There are others on LBNA, so you may need to search for them as well.

My boys each have their own personal travelers--to get Gpillar's you
have to tell a knock-knock joke. To get Batrick's you have to tell
what 0-0 is in chess notation. They will probably both get new
travelers for the Texas gathering, depending on their interests this
year--and I'll post those to this list. I just listed a new traveler
for myself and listed it on AQ. It's my best carving yet, and I made
it a PT because I couldn't bear to part with it.

Some people get humongously sneaky about their travelers, carrying
them in a special pocket so they can slip them surreptiously to the
person with the correct answer. They are very emphatic about no
hints, and that you have to quietly whisper them the correct answer.

We will also be introducing more cooties at this year's gathering, as
that is something fun for the kids. Adults, please act unsuspecting
as the children try to sneak cooties onto your person! Here's the
definition of cooties from AQ:

cooties
A relatively new type of letterbox where one tries to sneak around
hiding the box on another personperhaps in an open backpack or jacket
pocket. Once you 'catch a cootie', you stamp in and try to pawn it off
onto another unsuspecting letterboxer. Cooties are often signed into
with the stamp of your thumb or fingers that you draw on to embellish
instead of the traditional signature stamp, though many people use
signature stamps as well.


And finally, the Texas Tour Bus is a cool Letterbox that TeamKing
started. Think of it as a bus traveling through Texas, picking up
passengers as it goes. Clues for it are to be published only on this
list. If you collect it, you have to carve a stamp to put inside it
and then rehide it, offering the clues on this list. I didn't get it
when it was down here at the beginning, and now I can't wait for it to
make its way back to SE texas.
