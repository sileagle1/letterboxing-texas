From: Silver Eagle (sileagle@alltel.net) | Date: 2004-06-23 02:54:02 UTC
I know what you mean about the guard across from where Brown
Bomber LB is located. Once when my wife and I were there
birdwatching, he came over and warned us to move further away
from the Coast Guard entrance since we had binoculers!
As far as stamping discreetly, it is a challenge, especially
with kids. I try to do the actual stamping when nobody is
looking, but don't worry too much about all the stuff spread
all over. If somebody asks what I am doing (and most don't),
I just say I am making notes about birds or plants and they
usually let it go at that. It did backfire on me once when
a lady turned out to be an avid birdwatcher and started asking
me all kinds of questions about what I had seen. Hope I'm
not punished for the whoppers I told that day! Sometimes I
debate whether to just tell the truth and introduce people
to the hobby, but it just doesn't feel right.
Another thing I say if I am with my kids is that we are
playing a treasure hunt game, which isn't too far from the truth.
I wouldn't worry too much about being seen while stamping in,
but I would take care not to be seen finding and replacing the
box. That seems more important to me. Have fun and maybe
start thinking about placing some of your own.

Silver Eagle

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-06-23 08:19:00 UTC-07:00
I think the key is to have something else to spread
out with your letterbox stuff...a map, a notebook,
lunch, whatever and to know what you will say or do if
you have to deal with strangers.

It seems like we've gotten better at doing our
business quickly and keeping things compact as we've
done a few boxes though. I hadn't really thought
about it until you posed the question, but one habit
we've fallen into is that we each have a job we do. I
open the box up and take out the stamp and book while
my daughter gets our own log book ready and my son
gets our stamps out and lines them up. Whichever of
us is done first gets the stamps out and gets ready to
stamp in. My husband watches out and creates a cover
if we need it. After we've stamped in, we do the
whole process in reverse.

Unless we know we are on a secluded trail, we take the
box away from its hiding place to stamp in, and even
in a secluded spot we have a cover ready. Once we
find a good place to sit and stamp in, we spread the
box out, along with anything else that might create
cover. I figure that even if I have an interstate map
lying out and over the stamp stuff, if we are away
from the hiding place and the letterbox and stamps are
covered, anyone who wanders by will probably not stop
and inquire, so they won't possibly see what we're
really doing, and at worst, we've made them wonder
what kind of nuts look at an interstate map in the
middle of the woods!

Sometimes people do stop to see what's going on
though. The other day my kids and I (hubby was
elsewhere) were planting a box. We had our stuff
spread out, and I had laid a compass close by for a
quick cover. We had good visibility up and down the
trail, so we didn't worry about having something to
lay over the box stuff. A group of teenagers
approached from up the trail, so the kids quickly
grabbed the box stuff and dropped it in my backpack
and I stood up and started playing with the compass.
By the time the teenagers got close enough, we were
all engrossed in a compass lesson. My kids were so
"interested" in what I was doing that these kids
stopped to see what we had going on (it didn't help
that these were mostly boys and I was standing in the
middle of the woods with a pretty 16 year old girl).
I wound up giving them a compass lesson also, all the
while my kids were glancing at me and obviously
wondering how we were going to get rid of the group.
They suddenly remembered they had to get back to their
campsite and took off, and we were able to get back to
our business.

I think, if you have a plan in case you have to deal
with strangers, then keep your head about you if you
encounter someone, all will be fine, but I think part
of the fun of letterboxing is the sneakiness!

Lucy

I realized after I reread my post and remembered I had
referred to having "lunch" spread out while stamping
in - I should have mentioned one caveat to spreading
food about when you are letterboxing. Be diligent in
keeping your hands clean while you are handling the
box. Even slight food smells may attract animals, and
especially animals like raccoons that are not only
sensitive to smell but are curious and dextrous - a
recipe for disaster for an otherwise well-hidden
letterbox! I take a vinegar-soaked paper towel in a
baggie with me to wipe down anything I may need to
wipe before replacing a box. Vinegar isn't a pleasant
smell for animals and neutralizes most smells. Other
folks have other ideas which, I'm sure, work just as
well.
