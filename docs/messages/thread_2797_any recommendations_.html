From: Maribeth Jagger (cjagger405@earthlink.net) | Date: 2007-05-31 20:11:29 UTC-05:00
Sheba,
Oh there are so many good boxes in the metroplex area - I just wish you had more time! I assume you will be driving east on I-20. When you get to Weatherford, I-20 and I-30 join for about 15 miles until you get to Ft Worth. One box I'm fond of is Badge of Friendship by fourfitzs in Weatherford (Parker County). Also as you get into Ft Worth on I-30, you will pass right by the Botanical Gardens where there are quite a few boxes by different placers (Tarrant County). Let me know if you want more specific information about boxes. I just moved from the Bedford area (between Ft Worth and Dallas) to McKinney (about ten or fifteen miles north of the Dallas/Garland area). Hope you have a great trip!

Yep.  Pawtrekker it is. 
 
She is a Girl Scout who got interested in letterboxing through her troop.  Her younger brother is a Cub Scout in my pack.  As y'all well know, we did letterboxing as the focus of our last district campout, and she carried with her one of the boxes from that event.  Consequently, the logbook is hardly fresh and new - I left all the boys' log entries in the back of the book and added some fresh pages to the front, and added a cub scout patch to the box as a FTRF prize.  Those boxes are all going out as GWP Series, and that one is called Texas Tea.  I think the stamp is reasonably good, but hopefully the placement will make it stellar!
