Heh heh heh - - - Yeah - I am bad!
 
Truth be known - From Austin, Gulfport Mississippi is closer than El Paso is.
 
For me - Blythe California is closer to me than Austin is.
 
Still - we are not barbarians out here.  We do have enough letterboxes out to make for an interesting weekend.  We have the best steaks in Texas - out on a real ranch.  We have the largest urban park, 37 square miles.  We have as much history as anywhere else in Texas too.  Maybe more, as the REAL first thanksgiving happened right here on the banks of the Rio Grande with Don Juan de Onate, about 12 years before the pilgrims say Plymouth Rock.  There is a huge bronze marker on the border just south of me.
 
And fall is the very best time to be in El Paso.
 
I say this every time.  ANYONE contemplating a trip to El Paso and wanting to hunt for the Hueco Tanks Letterbox, make your Hueco Tanks State Park reservations ASAP!  That park stays full all late-fall to early-spring.
 
Y-all come for a visit.
