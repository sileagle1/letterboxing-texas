Yes, you'll definitely have to do a return trip, then!  The Mesa Verde ruins are spectacular, as is Canyon de Chelley.  But at Mesa Verde you can get much closer to the ruins, right there inside them!  Grand Junction is right there on the edge of the Colorado Monument and the Grand Mesa, two more amazing places with really great views.  And for some truly jaw-dropping scenery, the Grand Canyon and Monument Valley in AZ and Utah, of course, and also Monument Valley in AZ-Utah, and Fisher Towers and the Arches near Moab, Utah (which puts you right there in the middle of every car commerical and all the John Ford movies), and Sedona, AZ - oh, my, so many incredible places to go in the west!  Another place not to be missed for sightseeing and hikes and colorful rocks and spires and towers in Bryce Canyon, Utah.  Yes, you'll need several more trips!  How long before that daughter can share the driving?
