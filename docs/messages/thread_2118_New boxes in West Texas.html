We've placed seven new boxes in West Texas; four in Ector County
(Odessa) and three in Midland County. They are called "Horny
Toad", "From Russia With Love", "Comanche War Trail", "Desert
Flower", "Quilt Block Series - Birds in the Air", "The Pliska
Aeroplane" and "Wascally Wabbit". Could there be more in the near
future?

Re: New boxes in West Texas

From: Brandi J Rider (brandi@busybeesfamily.com) | Date: 2006-09-02 08:03:17 UTC-07:00
Hey Lone Star Quilter & Boots Tex,
 
Wow!  Just have to speak up and say "You both are amazing!"  What great, prolific boxers the pair of you are....I know that we in Georgetown are grateful for your interest in our town and the incredible boxes that you have planted here.  :-)
 
And...since I grew up in Midland, I can't wait until our next visit home so I can run around and find all those new boxes!!!
 
Actually, I've been thinking about LSQ & Boots Tex a lot during the group's discussions about "abandoned" boxes and boxers that have "dropped out".  You both travel so much and are so willing to plant along the way...I think it could be very easy for someone to assume that you might have dropped out of the game if several boxes in widely spread out areas go missing and you're not able to run right out and replace them. 
 
Yes, thankfully, both LSQ and Boots Tex respond immediately to email, but what if there was a family emergency and they weren't available?  What if they were on one of their long trips and didn't have access to email?
 
They are definitely there and "in the game".  I think the best way to declare any particular box as actually "missing" is to have a local boxer that is in the area go out and check on it.  Not only will it go you a definite "Yes...it's there," or "No...it's gone," answer (especially for an informal-type list), but it will also foster goodwill and a sense of community, at least among Texas boxers.
 
Now I know that this is already a good, close community...but if we're going to actually consider declaring someone's box "missing"...I think it's a good idea to verify by a boxer that's already found it.
 
Don't want to get any flames fanned in the fire, but just had to throw my hat into the ring.
 
Thanks, Batty B of the BusyBeesFamily
