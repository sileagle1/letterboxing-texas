From: Silver Eagle (sileagle@alltel.net) | Date: 2008-04-24 03:47:56 UTC
You are exactly right. My point was that if the box is not a Mystery
box and the clues are straight-forward, then having no directions
just seems lazy to me. It doesn't mean I think every box should have
explicit directions, but it can sure ruin an outing if I spend all my
time driving around looking for a park I thought I had mapped out but
isn't where I thought it was.

As MOF said, most of my clues give detailed directions, and that is
because in the early days when there were few boxers I wanted to make
sure any new boxer would have no trouble finding my boxes. Now that
there are plenty of boxers and boxes, other types of clues work great
because there is something for everyone. But old habits die hard and
I still usually give details.

I do have to say that I'm all for hidden clues, like the one Mark
just did on the Big List. But you have to be careful, as Mark found
out, because if you ask for help as part of your clues, some people
will take you literally and want to help. It is the nature of
letterboxers to help and I wouldn't want to jeopardize this by
appearing to Cry Wolf. The next person that really needs help might
not get it.

Silver Eagle

From: Abbie Beck (abbiembeck@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2008-04-24 06:00:16 UTC-07:00
As a newer letterboxer, I have really appreciated the directions given in the clues.  Usually, when I'm hunting down boxes in another town (we are in San Antonio), I will take several at once.  I will most likely have all five of our children with me, and the directions make the difference between a fun day and a day of frustration!! 
 
Recently, I printed off the clues for La Vernia, and found a couple of the clues written in code.  This was a lot of fun!  (no sarcasm intended LOL!!)  Once decoded, the clues were very precise, but the mental work of decoding gave me something to work on as I prepare for our actual outing.

So clues and wolf cries aside, I really, really did lose one highly prized and dearly held lapiz earring last week while Amazin Racer and I were looking for Heaven Box 1 and Box 2. We were well on the road home when I realized it was gone. Amazin quickly pulled out her camera, remembering we had taken a photo in the bluebonnets between box finds. And sure enough there it was in the photo, which helped establish when I'd lost it. Knowing I was driving back in the area in a few days, we opted not to drive back that night. And I was also wondering how I could put out an alert without being mistaken for a hidden clue....(One of our greater l'boxing conundrums, she puzzled.)
But lo and behold, two days later, stopping at the sight, there was the lost jewel, clinging to a leaf awaiting rescue.
Just glad I didn't have to sing I'm a Little Teapot to get it back...
Bloomin Gramma Jo on the road with Bird Dog
