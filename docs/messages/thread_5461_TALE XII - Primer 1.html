2 weeks to go!!!!     Just some thoughts to help you get ready for the event.   McKinney Falls SP has in the past not been friendly to letterboxes.  Major issue Rangers had was boxes being placed in "historic structures".    This would include the rough rock wall that runs through the park.   So, please do not hide boxes in any historic buildings or this wall.   Also, do not discuss with Rangers that you are at the park to "Letterbox", or at a Letterboxing event.   While on the trails finding boxes, please be cautious to not draw to much attention to what you are doing (okay, do you best but still have fun).  Do not leave clues behind.


With the rains this week, creek may be up some.  If so, some boxes might be placed on far side on extensive trails over there.   I seem to remember that you can cross that creek in 3 ways.

1) below falls (safest).   short crossing through shallow water to far side, but then either must climb high wall or skirt downstream and scramble up bank.

2) Falls cliff (Most dangerous) - shortest path, but will require stepping in water about 3 times

3) Above Falls (Slick) - Shallow water, and longest crossing.   Very slick.  Best to actually walk on grassy parts which are less slick.

**** or you can swim!!!!


Saturday - Event Start - Remember, we do not get hall until I believe 9am.   That does not mean Hall will be open at 9am.   We must check in with Ranger, then get all our gear.   I would not show up until 9:30am at EARLIEST.  Silver Eagle, if you know different time on this please inform.


Bikes are a good choice for getting around the park.  FYI.

One tip I can give to first timers is to not worry about finding all the letterboxes.  I think meeting and talking with your peers should be your main goal since you can always come back to find the letterboxes you miss later.  I do, however, recommend finding any boxes that will only be there during the Event (e.g. Event boxes, night boxes) and keep your eyes open for hidden boxes nearby.

As a reminder to all attendees, no pets are allowed in the Dining Hall and everyone should bring their own drinks (or cups for water).

Some ideas:

1) keep a list of who you exchanged with (it will get confusing).

2) be sure you have plenty of log space for exchanges and found boxes.

3) Be sure to get all the clues from the copy table (and check back to see if more added later).

4) We have a raffle each year to offset the costs of the dinning hall, so if you want to participate the tickets are $1 each, or if buy $20 worth you get 25 tickets.   Great......okay, interesting prizes.

5) Schedule usually is  .....arrive after 9:30am...meet and exchange....lunch at noonish.....go find boxes....

       return to hall anytime.....raffle about 6pm .....eat leftovers....talk and tell stories.
