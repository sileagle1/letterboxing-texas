Went Letterboxing and Sightseeing Saturday...

From: Wanda (wsthm@hotmail.com) | Date: 2004-02-02 17:35:44 UTC
Calico Rose and I went on a grand letterboxing excursion Saturday.
We left early, about 8:30, and headed to Rockport, making a quick
stop in Papalote so she could find my Lonesome Luke letterbox....
and so I could check on it again, since I don't have very good luck
with keeping letterboxes at that site. It was alive and well.

We drove to Goose Island, where we found Silver Eagle's 'Texas
Bonefish'. There were some really interesting bushes scattered all
along the trail that had gorgeous bright red berries. Really pretty.

Then we went to the Big Tree so Susan could find the 'Big Tree'
letterbox (Silver Eagle), and to the old Lamar Cemetery where Susan
found my 'Rest in Peace #3'. Leaving the cemetery, we met a couple
from WI who we talked to for a few minutes. Susan asked if they were
winter Texans, and the woman asked how she knew. Susan said it was
her accent. The woman thought she didn't have an accent - LOL! Susan
and I assured her she did! ;) Then Susan and I pointed the finger at
each other for being able to hear a strong 'Texas twang' in each
other. :)

In/Near Rockport, we searched for WOW MOM's two letterboxes, 'I Love
You' and 'X Marks the Spot', but couldn't find either one. I knew
there had been trouble with 'X', but didn't find out until later
when I emailed WOWMOM that they were both gone.

We did find Baby Bear's Gulf Wader though, in great shape, and
enjoyed looking around the area, making a note to visit there again
in the late spring or in summer when the flowers, plants, vines and
trees as well as wildlife ought to be very bountiful. There were
some of the less flashy wildflowers starting to bloom... some yellow
daisies and baby blue eyes.
