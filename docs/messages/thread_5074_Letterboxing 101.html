From: Boots Tex (redbarn@eastex.net) | Date: 2012-10-09 19:34:39 UTC
I think that geocaching became so pervasive in the parks that they had to do something and this is a case of TPWD embracing the game so they could enforce the rules. Sort of like making some drugs legal as a way of controlling them. Letterboxing, on the other hand, and I was told this "off the record" by an appointed representative of TPWD, virtually doesn't exist, as far as they are concerned, since it is not a problem. This person had never actually heard of letterboxing until he was appointed to meet with me to address "letterboxes in Texas State Parks". The problems we cause are not worth the effort and expense of establishing a program for us separate from geocaching. I guess someone at Caprock Canyons SP sees it a little differently, or someone on the staff has gotten involved in letterboxing.

Boots Tex
