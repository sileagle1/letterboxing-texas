Our first Letterbox trip was in January, 2003, and we made a big loop
including Mission Tejas SP in the east, Confederate Reunion SP in
central, and Mother Neff SP in west central, plus a few places in
between. We didn't place boxes in every county, just enough to give
everyone in those areas a box within a hundred miles or so. Of
course nobody else was letterboxing at that time, but we were hoping
our seeds would start it growing, and it sure did!

That summer we took our letterbox seeds out of state as we followed
the Lewis & Clark trail. We ended up putting boxes in OK, AR, MO, KS
IA, NE, SD & ND. We actually got to find a few on this trip and it
was a lot of fun, so we decided to do a letterbox trip every year.

The next few years we mostly just found boxes and did not place that
many. We went backpacking in Montana twice and put boxes in WA, OR,
ID, WY & MT. We also went to the Dallas area twice and East TX once.
We missed our trip last year so we were long overdue. Since
populating virgin counties has become our new obsession, we decided
to get back to our roots and go on a letterbox seeding trip just like
the first one, only this time it would involve many more boxes and
much more travel. It also required a lot of research to come up with
good subjects for all these counties and places to put the boxes.
Since Boots & LSQ had just finished the North Region with their LB
trip, we decided to go South and try to finish that region, along
with Central.

Normally I like to place boxes in places that I want people to visit,
but in this case we sometimes had to place our boxes any place we
could find, usually cemeteries, and I'm sorry about that. Most of
the boxes are about interesting things, though, and many of the forts
are fun to visit, so maybe somebody will go after them, or maybe a
new boxer from one of the areas will get hooked.

There were several high notes and a few low notes on this trip. A
few of the low ones:

1. The bleak countryside - nothing but scrub oak, thorn bushes and
cactus everywhere made me appreciate what the early travelers had to
deal with. We are still picking thorns and cactus needles out of our
skin!

2. Trashy towns - not sure if it is because people are poor in that
part of the state or what, but many of the towns we visited were not
very nice. Even some of the cemeteries had trash.

Some high notes:

1. Good wildlife - we saw and heard many birds, including several
Mexican Eagles right after I placed my box of the same name.

2. Interesting history - we saw a rattlesnake taller than me,
Popeye, the cannon that started the TX Revolution, a town that was
under 7 flags instead of just 6, a town where O. Henry lived, and
several great forts.

3. Interesting stamps - we did manage to find a few boxes and some
were memorable, especially Lasso This, which contained a stamp with
great curves, er I mean carves, that helped keep our tent warm at
night.

There is much more, but I will save it for the campfire at TALE6.

Silver Eagle
