Call from James.

From: Mike Olbrisch (mike-2007@elp.rr.com) | Date: 2008-01-08 21:38:14 UTC-07:00


For those of you who knew James while he was at home or at Ft. Hood, we heard
from him today. He said to say HI to everyone.

He is in Mosul, in the northern mountains, the Kurdish region. He says it is
colder and has more snow than Ft. Carson (Colorado Springs).

You-all may find this amusing. He has been a ham operator for all of 19 months
now, although he has listened to Short Wave since he was a small boy. Anyway -
he has a new job. Instead of being a Scout-Sniper-Paramedic as a part of a
Bradley crew, he is now in the HQ, running the COMMO shop. Yup - his "VAST"
radio experience as a ham operator got him into the commo shop. He says he
doesn't think it will last long, maybe a few months. His favorite quote - "Its
a Push To Talk button, not a push to think button." At least he is warm.

I started to lecture him about getting soft, letting his guard down. I
mentioned the recent attack in Mosul as an example. I told him I saw it on the
news, and he should keep ready - condition yellow to orange all the time. He
said he didn't need to wait for the news to see it, he was there. Two
insurgents were killed immediately, one got out the door before the dropped him.

Anyway - he is well, and he misses Ft. Hood and the warm Texas weather. I
expect about Summer he will be wishing for the snow.

He should return to Ft, Hood/Killeen in the spring of 2009. Maybe Lucy will
make another Welcome Home box for him that forces me to come back for too.

Y-all have a great evening.

Mike - KD9KC.
