Snake Phenomena (was [letterboxingtexas] Spring must be close...)

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2009-02-15 08:51:01 UTC-06:00
Trading snakes for frogs - Now THAT is a very good idea.  Unfortunately, I left my magic wand in my other backpack, so I'm stuck with snakes and bugs, I guess!  Unless I use Granny Butterfly's snow shovel to whack them both...
 
Seriously - I got home from work Thursday and all was fine on the front porch.  Walked back out fifteen minutes later and there were two (count 'em - two) very small snakes on my porch.  Both dead, both headless.  I'm guessing - one for each cat.  AND - it happened again yesterday...two snakes sans heads.  Same kind, same size. 
 
The first two I pushed off the edge of the porch into the flower bed.  I decided with the second two that perhaps it would be better to push them to the side of the porch to let the cats know that while we appreciated the gifts, we didn't want to walk on them and risk squashing their handiwork (or would that be paw-work???).  So - there they stayed most of the day.  Thankfully I'm not squeamish about snakes, because it was a little weird walking in and out the door and seeing those two snakes laying nearby.
 
She Who Volunteers Me For Everything's son was over yesterday hanging out with Lightning.  That boy is a joker.  He's something else.  When he wanted to take one of our dead snakes home with him to give to his mom as a Valentine's Day gift, I readily agreed and gave him a Walmart bag to carry his booty in.  He was very excited about the prospect of scaring his mom and made Lightning and I go in the house to watch when I took him home.  I began to feel a little guilty about feeding into the kid's desire to scare his mom, my friend.  Still though, I was also curious about why a little dead snake would scare her - they have three dogs, two cats, a cage full of finches, a cockatiel, a guinea pig, and - yes - a snake (a BIG snake).   But she did jump - didn't shriek like he was hoping though.  To assuage my guilt over conspiring with him, I apologized profusely. 
 
I got home and I debated kicking the remaining snake over the edge into the flower bed, but decided to leave it and see if the number of snakes we get is a function of how many cats we own or if there is a snake quota in my neighborhood and our quota is two, so that the prank snake would be replaced by one snake.  It's pretty chilly out this morning, so I'm thinking we won't know the answer until this afternoon at the earliest.  I find I'm looking forward to finding out...
 
If it turns out that the number of snakes we get is in direct correlation to the number of cats we have, I can see that the next step of this experiement is to reduce the number of cats and see if it also reduces the number of snakes...anybody want a cat?  The calico is especially sweet, and a hunter too!
