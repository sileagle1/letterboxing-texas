Transferring Images

From: BeachBum (beachbumtx09@yahoo.com) | Date: 2011-07-07 01:32:13 UTC-05:00

Hi Everyone,
I just wanted to share a discovery that I had tonight. I usually do the trace and rub method but tonight I was trying the iron method of transferring a image over and I goofed it.. So I cleaned my surface with some Stazon All Purpose Stamp Cleaner that I use when I'm testing while carving. Then I tried the iron on method again and oh my goodness I had a perfect image! Thinking it was just a quirk I tried it again and again and it works! I just cleaned the surface a little leaving it damp and then placed my image and ran my little iron over it. I'm guessing the cleaner dampened the copied image just enough for the heat to make it easier to transfer.

Now I've always heard that you learn by your mistakes.. Well this goof up has made my night! :)

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2011-07-07 23:13:50 UTC-05:00

I've recently tried heat transfers of laser (not inkjet - they didn't work at all for me) printed images. The image transferred beautifully, but either the heat or the ink made the surface of the rubber un-inkable. The ink sat on top of the rubber, so that it wouldn't collect evenly on the surface and wouldn't imprint uniformly. It was not a big problem when I carved an outlined image, but if I wanted large inked surfaces, it pretty much stunk! I did really, really like it for doing line drawings - I got a crisp clear line to work with, but I did find I had to set the iron a little lower than I had thought I would. I did, by the way, start out by cleaning the rubber with soap and water. I've always used the pencil transfer too...

Lucy
