Ashby heard from Fugichip

From: Ashby (ashby@ic.org) | Date: 2006-12-21 18:27:11 UTC-08:00
Hi all,

Since many of you seemed interested in the Adazing
Treasure box report, I thought I should send an
update. I did hear back from Fugichip today and will
be mailing their box to them tomorrow. Thank you for
all your interest and concern.

I have learned a lot about stamping in from this
experience, because a FEW of the stamps or notes are
as clear as if they were written just this morning,
but MOST of them are washed away completely...I guess
the difference between different kinds of inks, in
both stamp pads or writing pens. I am going to find
out more about this and make sure I am using something
that will not wash away. I don't know if what I am
using right now is waterproof or not, so this
situation has been very informative for me.

ashby

So it sounds like the Adazing Treasure box will be out of commission for awhile.  Did he happen to mention if he still lives in the area or not?  Just wondering how quickly he will return it, or if he will.
 
The subject of ink comes up periodically on other lists, mainly newboxers.  Pigment inks take longer to dry, but are more colorfast.  Dye inks are quick dry, quick to run.  I think kids' markers are a mistake because they are intended (for the most part) to be washable - washable means runnable. 
 
It really is funny though, when you see a logbook that has been waterlogged, to see some stamps that are plain as day and others that look like Picasso in pastel.

Concerning ink (since I'm a professional stamper :) ), my ink of choice
for boxing is a hybrid ink - all the benefits of a quick drying dye ink
but the waterfast properties of a pigment. Many varieties are
available and can be found at your local craft store. I use the
Ancient Page and/or Versafine brands but there are others that work
just as well.
ThimbleCat
