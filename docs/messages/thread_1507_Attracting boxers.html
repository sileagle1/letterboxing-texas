One of the greatest thing about this hobby is how different it is for
each of us. . .Sandy's comment has fostered an interesting discussion
because there is no one right way. Each of us has a point of view
that is valid.

I personally don't care how many people find my boxes. My biggest
hope is that the ones who do are respectful--of the work the box
represents and its contents and more importantly in my eyes, the
overall environment (avoid social trails, please--that's how I find
all my geocaches without a GPS). FOR ME, quality beats quantity. I
love to carve a great stamp, find the perfect spot for a box, write a
challenging clue, or whatever. That motivates me more than the people
who find it (although there are times I plant boxes with specific
finders in mind).

That said, we have planted boxes appropriate for all kinds of
letterboxers. Some people are very casual boxers, and that's okay.
They tend toward the easier boxes and there's nothing wrong with that.
I personally like it when people take an extra effort and solve the
more mysterious ones--you'd be surprised how few that is. And, for
those of you who have enjoyed some of our boxes, I am hoping to plant
some that are past difficult into downright diabolical. And, as the
kids get a little older, we hope to plant some on much longer hikes
than we have in the past--again, that won't be for everyone.

Does that make us elitist? I don't think so. It's more about how we
personally choose to play this game.

I think you'll find some homeschoolers will be great boxers, some will
be horrible. It's hard to know what's really going to spark the love
in someone. But it's great to find others who enjoy the same
hobby/sport/craft/activity/whatever--I am not in general a joiner, so
joining this forum was a real departure for me. However, I have
gotten tons out of it, including some very rewarding friendships.
That has been a true surprise bonus beyond my log book full of memories.

Dewberry
