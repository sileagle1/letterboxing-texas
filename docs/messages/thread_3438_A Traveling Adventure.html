A Traveling Adventure

From: cwenter1 (cwenter1@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-03-23 00:02:08 UTC
On Friday, March 14th, I flew from California to Nashville, Tennessee
to attend a conference for 5 days. I had researched clues for a
number of letterboxes in the Nashville area and found that there was
one planted in the Gaylord Opryland Hotel where the conference was
being held and where I was also staying. I had made arrangements
with the planter of the box to meet for an exchange. She actually
works at the hotel but is only there Monday through Friday so I had
to wait until Monday to meet her. In the meantime I tried to find
the letterbox and I knew I was in the right area of the garden but
there was no box so on Monday I hunted her down in the area she was
working and she told me that one of her co-workers had removed it
from it's hiding place temporarily. She told her co-worker to put it
back because she had some people that were going to be looking for
it. It was put back that morning so off I went to try again and sure
enough, it was there. Also, I had checked AQ the day before for any
updates since I had a problem locating the letterbox over the weekend
and I found out that the planter had added a second letterbox in the
garden and that the clues to find it were written in the back of the
logbook of the 1st letterbox. After I found the 1st box I began to
wander around to find a secluded area in the garden to stamp in when
a woman came up behind me and asked what I was doing. She caught me
off guard and all I could say was ah, ah, ah. She then told me she
had just stamped in and she was searching for the 2nd box. What a
surprise to find another letterboxer in the hotel garden looking for
letterboxes too. Not only is she a letterboxer but she was also
attending the same conference that I was attending. We did an
exchange. I stamped in the 1st letterbox and put it back in it's
hiding place, and my new friend and I went off in search of the 2nd
box. We were successful. YEAH!

The next day I had some time to go off site in search of more
Nashville letterboxes. I went to a park that was supposed to have 2
boxes hidden. I found the 1st box quite easily. The hardest part
was finding the park. After stamping in I continued down the
concrete path towards the 2nd box which was hidden off of the
concrete path and down a muddy dirt path. When I reached the
location of the letterbox I found the dryest area I could and laid
everything out thinking that this off the main trail mud road would
be safe. WRONG!! I was right in the middle of stamping in when
along comes a Mother and her 4 sons down the mud trail. The Mother
walks straight up to me and says "hello". I said hello back and she
then tells me that she just put a Hitchhiker in the box. What a
small world. I've heard other letterboxers tell stories of running
into other boxers out on the trail but I really never expected it to
happen to me. I now have 3 new letterboxing friends. Actually, it's
4 as I also met the husband of "twokitties".

Here's a shout out to "twokitties", "DS", and "The Fielder Family".
It was great to meet all of you and I hope we meet up again on the
letterboxing trails and thank you for making my trip to Nashville so
memorable.

Mothers Love
