New Year's at Enchanted Rock

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2009-01-16 22:30:25 UTC-06:00
Well I told y'all we were going, but I never got around to telling you we
went!

I was worried to death our trip to Enchanted Rock would fall through at the
last minute. That seemed to be the watch phrase of our year last year - "We
ALMOST got to go!" Well - it didn't fall through, despite
circumstances conspiring against us.

Fearless Fox works in life and health insurance, and anyone who knows much
about that, or who is old enough to have medicare benefits, knows that the
last two months of each calendar year is time for medicare recipients to
change their drug card coverage. It gets CRAZY! He and the women in his
office worked the last two weeks of the year from 8 am to 8 pm, and only
took Christmas Day off. I had the whole week before Christmas off, which
was good since he was out for the count as far as shopping went. Still
though, it was a bummer because we couldn't do anything fun that
week! Anyway, Christmas came and went (well, for the most part, we still
haven't celebrated Christmas with two parts of our family!), and I had to go
back to work the next week. Fearless Fox had to work right up to New Year's
Eve, as I did, but we both managed to take off January 2nd for a long
weekend to Enchanted Rock.

Now - for Christmas I had asked for a 2-3 day trip to do nothing but
letterbox. Well - my original thought had been for Lightning and I to go
and leave Fearless Fox at home since he doesn' t much care for boxing, but
Fearless Fox got enthusiastic about going too. And he assumed I meant that
we should camp. That was not what I meant at all. I wanted to go lazy and
stay in a hotel and let someone else fix my meals and make my bed, and know
that I had a warm dry room at night with internet access in the evenings so
I could log finds and look up boxes and with a television to watch until I
fell asleep. I wanted to go to Austin. He wanted to go to Enchanted Rock.
I started swelling up about him changing my idea of what I wanted for
Christmas, but then realized I didn't actually get this as a Christmas gift
- it was being planned as a family getaway. So I quit puffing up quite as
much and took a look at where he wanted to go. I figured I could live with
it, and we had gotten a lot of new camping equipment so it would be fun to
try it all out too. So that's how the Enchanted Rock trip was born, and our
work schedules nearly killed it on more than one occasion.

We managed to get ourselves all packed into our own backpacks in the few
days before New Year's Eve. We debated when to leave and decided it would
be best to go New Year's Eve and leave early enough to avoid the drunks on
the road. We actually managed to leave town at 5:30, for what should be
about a 5.5 hour drive. Well - that would be 5.5 hours if I were driving!
I'm like my mother, NacTrailCat, in that once I get on the road, I want to
GOOOOO! I don't want to stop until I absolutely have to. Fearless Fox is
NOT that way. We go for an hour, then stop to get a drink. Drive another
hour and stop to go to the bathroom. Another hour and we have to eat. You
see the pattern... It used to drive me crazy, but I've learned to accept it
and use it to my own benefit. Rather than packing all the perishables and
food and buying all the incidentals before we leave, we use one of our stops
along the way to grocery shop, then we get back on the road and think of all
the things we forgot to get, and one of our later stops are to do Walmart
again.

And it was thus that we happened upon the stroke of midnight on New Year's
Even. SIX and a HALF hours after leaving home for a 5.5 hour trip, we found
ourselves in a convenience store in Llano at the magic hour. I found it odd
that we, who usually spend New Year's Eve happily snoozing the hour by, were
in a convenience store in the middle of nowhere with ourselves, two clerks,
and two strangers who were also in the store. I tried to get Lightning to
get one of them to take our picture with all the others, but he thought it
was a stupid idea. I think he will regret that in later life - he missed
out on documenting yet another of his mom's weirdo encounters, and on a
special moment too. One of the clerks was half asleep (or not too bright -
couldn't tell which, so I'll give him the benefit of the doubt) and the
other seemed to think she was slumming amongst the rest of us at that time.
She kept saying she ALWAYS spends New Year's in New York. I was sorely
tempted to say "Well - not ALWAYS...", but thought that would be rude. The
two strangers wandered out and Fearless Fox and Lightning paid and went back
to the truck while I went to the bathroom. Apparently the New York refugee
forgot that I was in the bathroom, because as I emerged I was treated to a
weird sort of shrieking jig that was apparently intended to be some sort of
welcome ritual for the New Year. Apparently the New Year missed what I
saw, or it would have been scared back to wherever it came from.
We were really not that far from Enchanted Rock when we were in Llano, but
we had figured out that we had absolutely no concept of space in the dark in
the mountains, so we refrained from trying to predict how far out we were.
We didn't want to be depressed if it was longer than we estimated. We
rolled in to Enchanted Rock SNA at 12:30 and had to set about to find
ourselves a camping spot without waking up the other campers. Our
headlights and flashlights just didn't do the area justice, so we did the
best we could. We finally conceded that we were not there to camp, but to
stay while we letterboxed - we would have been a whole lot more particular
about getting away from other campers and finding the perfectly perfect
site, complete with view and privacy, but it WAS after midnight and we
didn't plan to spend much time in our site anyway. With that in mind, we
opted for a site that would be easy to load and unload from (no pull-ins at
ER) and close to the bathrooms - convenient. We set up camp by the lights
of our headlamps (we got new headlamps for Christmas - as we do every year -
and these newest ones are really GOOD!), and Lightning turned in to his
hammock around 1 a.m. and Fearless Fox and I retired to our tent.

Daylight rolled in somewhere around 7:30 am and I managed to keep my eyes
tightly closed until 8:30. Finally, I rolled out of my sleeping bag and
climbed out of the tent to find Lightning roaming about getting camp
organized (otherwise known as waiting for mom to get up and declare what
breakfast would be). We explored a little and looked up at The Rock to see
a light trickle of hikers heading up already, then I set water to boiling in
my Jetboil, a Christmas present I had been wanting for three years (
http://www.jetboil.com/products/cookingsystems/personalcookingsystem). I
had coffee and hot chocolate whipped up for all in no time and got oatmeal
under way too. Breakfast was done and I was ready to hit the ground, but
Fearless Fox had slept in later than Lightning and I, so we had to wait for
him to get social niceties like toothbrushing out of the way.

Now - we knew climbing Enchanted Rock would be tough since we hadn't done
much in the last couple of months, so our plan had been to letterbox on New
Year's Day and save The Rock for Friday after we had toned up a bit on
Thursday. We were getting a really late start though, and the later it
got, the more we waffled We were still tired from the trip, so we decided
we should just stay in camp and do The Rock later in the day on New Year's
Day. Fearless Fox and I put our hammocks up and we almost managed to talk
ourselves out of doing anything at all that day, and just lay around in our
hammocks and read, but then I realized it would be a real bummer not to go
up ER on New Year's Day. It seems to always hold true that what we do on
New Year's, we do more off the rest of the year, and we would really like to
do a whole lot more hiking this year. Well that thought hit a chord and we
stuck with the idea of going up the rock later in the afternoon. After a
little more laying around, I couldn't stand it anymore and I rallied them
out of their hammocks and insisted we get up and go - I didn't want to think
about it anymore. I wanted to do it. I was motivated, inspired, and ready
- right up until we hit the first hard incline. Then I was ready to go back
and lay in my hammock. I knew better than to even mention that though,
since Fearless Fox was perfectly happy in his hammock and wouldn't
appreciate me calling it off. So up I went - stop and go fashion - rest a
bit, go a bit. Check my phone signal to see if 9-1-1 was an option. And
grumbled the whole stinking way up. To myself mind you. Meanwhile
surrounded by throngs of people making the same trek. And some kids RUNNING
up the hill. Some of them ran close by me. I wanted to stick my foot out
and trip them - teach them to run UP the hill near ME! And finally,
finally, we got close enough to the top that it was senseless really to turn
around, and then it was easy to go. And I kept thinking about MO5 making
the trek with all her kids - and youngest one in a kangaroo pouch. I KNEW
she was nuts anyway - I am CERTAIN of it now!

But - I made it to the top. I really did. I even have pictures to prove
it. Lightning took pics with his phone the whole way up and kept texting
them to NacTrailCat and SJZ. SJZ says she finally texted him back to put
his phone down and enjoy the scenery! Now - SJZ - that's another ER story.
She and Fearless Fox went to ER on a lark when she was about 13. She had
never really been hiking, but he declared her to be his hiking partner about
that time. They got to ER sometime around 8 pm and he determined that they
would camp in the primitive camping on the other side of the rock. Somehow
or another they got off on the wrong trail and wound up going down the side
of Little Rock and going back into the main camping area. They set out
again and this time came upon a hiker who had hiked across the mountain to
go to the bathroom (that's another story I wish I knew!) and he agreed to
show them to the top since he was going that way anyway. Now this was about
11 pm. They got to the top, and parted company with the hiker dude, and
they stopped to look around. My poor girl, he tells about her little voice
asking him "Please Daddy - please, can't we stop? I'm really tired!" She
has always had him wrapped around her little finger, and who could resist a
cry for help like that anyway? He found a recessed area in the rock and
laid down a tarp for them and they slept until the morning, waking right
before the first visitors of the day. For the record - you aren't supposed
to camp on ER. Oh well. When you're 13 and you're tired, you stop. She's
a trooper these days and would outdo anyone who challenged her.

ANYWAY - the top of the rock. As soon as I reached the top and could look
out and around, I couldn't remember why I grumbled so much getting up
there. The view is astounding and the natural world atop it is
breathtaking. Enchanted Rock is a living example of geology in flux. You
can literally see how changes are happening. It was positively beautiful.
Before we left home, we had researched the eco-vironment of the rock and
learned that the vernal pools represent whole ecosystems in themselves.
Unfortunately, Lightning forgot and crushed an ecosystem or two before he
got used to walking around them instead of through them. We spent an hour
or so on top before we decided to venture back down. The way down is much
easier than the way up, except that I have a bad knee that I had thwacked
real good on the bumper hitch on the way to Enchanted Rock the night
before. I had hit it hard enough that I knocked it out of place (a cool
trick it does at inappropriate moments). The down trek was much harder on
my knee than the up trek had been, but even so, we made it down relatively
easily, except that my knee really hurt for the whole rest of the trip.

We got back into camp and fell into our hammocks, watching the climbers
going up and down the Rock like ants. I expected I would take a nap, but I
found it was hard to get still and rest after the initial recovery time, and
so did Fearless Fox and Lightning. We decided that rather than the camp
rations we had brought with us, we would go in to Fredericksburg and get a
hot meal. We hit town and realized precious few restaurants were open on
New Years' Day. We finally spotted a German restaurant and Fearless Fox put
me out to find out what the wait time was while he circled the block. The
hostess told me we would have to wait a half hour if we wanted to sit inside
or we could sit outdoors right away. I stepped aside and called Fearless
Fox with the information and he decided he could live with eating outside.
Just as they got parked and I was about to step up to the hostess podium to
tell her we wanted to sit outside, the owner of the restaurant came and
turned their sign to Closed - they were running out of food because they had
been so busy. He told her she could seat those whose names were on the
list, but to take no more. ARGH! I went up to her and begged her to seat
us, reminding her that I HAD just asked her about seating. She agreed that
"technically" I could have been on the waiting list, so she seated us.
YES! It was going to be McD's otherwise, and camp rations sounded pretty
good compared to that! I have never been much on German food, but I decided
that if I was going to like it, it would be at this place. I ordered a
Reuben because I had seen NacTrailCat eat them and figured it was safe. Had
I considered that she talked me into gulping down some mineral water in
Manitou Springs CO and a raw oyster in some restaurant somewhere - all for
the entertainment value - I woulda reconsidered the Reuben, but it turned
out to be GOOD - sauerkraut and all. We wound up going back to that
restaurant another couple times!

Now - let me stop here to answer the question you are almost certainly
asking. What's all this got to do with letterboxing? Well - not much so
far. And it won't have much to do with it in the next while either. It
seems the ER area and Fredericksburg are very hard on boxes. BootsTex and
Silver Eagle and Can Can had all told me in private messages that quite a
few in the area were missing, so I was expecting to have a high miss rate,
but what I expected was low even compared to what I had been told by them -
I don't think I've ever taken a trip with more misses than finds. The theme
of our weekend was "maybe it'll be there, maybe it won't". And hopefully
this will be a lesson to new boxers who struggle with not being able to find
boxes. Sometimes they just aren't where they are supposed to be, or maybe
they are, but we can't find them. BUT - we can still have fun! And make no
mistake, it WAS a fun trip, even if we didn't find all that we went for.

Anyway, the ER boxes are - for the most part - not there. Or we didn't find
them. We looked for some and were unable to find them, didn't get a chance
to look for others. I don't want to leave the idea that they are definitely
not there, so I won't mention the ones we looked for and couldn't find. We
didn't find any at all in the Enchanted Rock SNA. I will say, though, that
we didn't get a chance to look for Cancan's Extreme Turkey Trot or Silver
Eagle's Turkey Peak and those were two I think I specifically said we would
try for.

We swapped out our intended Thursday for Friday, so Friday was Luckenbach,
Grapetown, and the Old Tunnel WMA. Off we set after breakfast in
Fredericksburg to go to Luckenbach first. Somehow or another, I managed to
get us way off course, so we corrected by going first to Grapetown. I
somehow missed seeing Can Can's Raisins in the Sun box listing, so we didn't
even look for it, but we DID find The Golden Throne! What a cute box and a
neat location. Wouldn't you know - we were stamping in on the front steps
when another family came poking through, exploring the school. We were well
away from the hiding spot, so we made no attempt to cover up or explain what
we were doing, but they certainly gave us several odd looks. We offered to
move so they could peak in behind where we were, but they declined. It then
became a waiting game, but we outlasted them and hid the box back after they
drove off. I thought it was interesting the amount of traffic that came and
went through the big closed gate beyond the school. It was like grand
central station. I wanted to drive up to the gate to see if they would let
us in, but Fearless Fox wouldn't do it.

On we went to Old Tunnel WMA and since I had messed us up on Luckenbach,
Fearless Fox had lost confidence in my navigational skills (which were, I
must say, on the whole pretty darned good through the whole trip!). He
simply did not believe that Old Tunnel could be as far down the road as it
was and nearly turned around several times. We finally came upon it, and as
I read the clues for the box there, I doubted myself briefly , thinking we
might have found a different entrance to it. After reading more and
exploring it a bit, I got my groove back and knew we were cool. We ventured
down the trail a distance, checked out the tunnel, smelled the evidence of
the bat and finally went after the box. Nice box, cool hiding spot. Neat
place all the way around. I'd love to go back when the bats are flying.
I'll bet they really smell then!

By then, we realized we really weren't too far from Comfort, a town we had
ruled out because we thought it was a little far out of range for this
trip. Feeling like we had forever, we ventured on down there to see what we
could find or not find. As was the case, we had some misses, but we had
some very cool finds too! Another difference between Fearless Fox's and my
travel styles is that I go strictly by instructions and by the map. He gets
a feel for what direction to go and finds his way to where we are going. I
don't understand it, but it works. We decided we would go out to Singing
Waters Vineyard for a glass of wine and Baby Bear's letterbox. I gave very
explicit directions based on Baby Bear's clues and he half listened and took
off. I rolled my eyes, trying to keep track of where we were so I could
tell him where he messed up. Somehow or another we wound up at the Vineyard
and drove in. We were the only visitors, and there were some men working
outside, so we nonchalantly ventured about and looked at the tree in
question. We found the box, sneaked it back to the car, then went in and
sampled. The ladies were very nice there and we bought a bottle of a
semi-sweet Merlot named Sweet Lupe after the dog. We managed to stamp in
and get the box back to its hiding place, but interestingly, the box was
planted in April and except for Silver Eagle who found it a few weeks after
it was planted, there had been no visitors to the box before us.

We had in mind visiting Corazon's Where's Hermann? box next, so I carefully
backtracked directions to the point we had to go to get on track to find
it. And Fearless Fox again barreled off cross-country, knowing how to
connect without backtracking so far. I tried to direct him, but he knew
what he was doing, and once again we found ourselves exactly where we needed
to be. Corazon's box brought back memories for me - reading what she wrote
about her years of summer camp there at Hermann Sons reminded me of my own
several years of camp at Pine Cove near Tyler. And - because the box is
hidden at the site of a memorial to campers from a nearby camp who perished
in floodwaters - it reminded me of that fateful day in 1987 when that camp
bus washed off the road. Fearless Fox, Nac Trail Cat, my dad and I were
rafting on the Guadalupe River that same day just a few miles downriver.
The torrential rains were overwhelming and we were forced to take cover off
the river ourselves that day. I was 8 months pregnant with SJZ and it was a
dicey situation for awhile, although I don't think any of us really realized
that until later. It was sobering later that day after our outfitter had
picked us up, to hear the news of those kids on that bus. Fearless Fox and
I appreciated the opportunity to stand and remember them.
