Replacing missing boxes

From: Boots Tex (redbarn@eastex.net) | Date: 2011-06-29 16:05:18 UTC
Over the weekend, I received an "attempt" on my Texas Governor box in Huntsville called Raven, so Monday I went over to check on it and it was indeed missing after 4 1/2 years in the same spot. I try to keep the governors going, so I have carved a new stamp and will replace the box this week. I didn't look up the old image before carving the stamp, but when I finished the new stamp, I compared the two and found that my carving skills have improved considerably over that time. This brings up a question: Since many, if not most, active letterboxers have already found this box and probably won't go back just to stamp the new image, should I rename the box "Raven 2" and re-list it as a new box? If I do that, must I hide it in a different place? I have only renamed one of my replaced governor boxes, but that was because I moved it about 150 miles in the process. I'd like your opinion. What would you do?

Boots Tex

From: dforsdale (dforsdale68@comcast.net) | Date: 2011-06-30 14:00:14 UTC
My water lilies box in San Angelo went missing after a few years and I recently replaced it - made a new and improved stamp with a different image, gave the box a new name, but hid it in the same spot since I still thought it was the best hiding spot in the park. I debated making it a "new" box too but decided to since it had a new image. I think normally, I just recarve the old image and replace the original box. But in your case, since someone who had the old image might want the new one as well, I'd probably rename it and make a new box.
