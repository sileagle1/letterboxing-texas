Runs for Chocolate and I went out this morning and placed some boxes-
-(including her very first!) at a wonderful park south of downtown
Houston, ER and Ann Taylor Park.

However, the fact that it has rained every day for the last three
weeks was very evident in the mosquito population. There were quite
literally clouds of them following us. So, my boxes got planted
closer to the entrance than I would have liked. I envisioned a
leisurely stroll through the park investigating all the
possibilities. NO!!! This was a "that looks like a good spot, now
let's get out of here" operation.

I do think that you will love this park. Everytime we've been,
we've seen different species of birds and butterflies--it is a
lovely patch of wilderness with a rich history. It is a perfect
letterboxing spot (shady and pretty and terrific if you're a birder)
that I was waiting for some worthy boxes to hide. Just bathe in
DEET first or wait 'til the skeeter population drops off a little!
I may plant another box out there in the Time Traveler Series, but
it won't be until fall.

My bug repellant did work, but last I saw, Runs For Chocolate was
pretty itchy.

Dewberry
