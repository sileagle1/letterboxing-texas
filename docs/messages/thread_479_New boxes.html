New boxes

From: Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter (janet@redbarnretreat.com) | Date: 2006-10-20 02:36:55 UTC
Hi, boxers,

We just wanted y'all to know that we planted some new letterboxes
today, so check the clues before you leave for the Great Book
Gathering this weekend. We placed two in Burroughs Park. They're
called "America's Storyteller" and "Elm Creek Quilts - Quilt Block
Series" (yes, it's based on a series of books, well known in the
quilting world).

While we were in that neck of the woods, we also placed a couple in
Telge Park just for fun. They're called "Legend of Sleepy Hollow"
and "Stingy Jack".

All four boxes are listed on Atlas Quest and LbNA. By the way,
Burroughs Park is in very good condition considering all the rain,
but Telge Park is WET! In some places the paths are underwater and
difficult to get through unless you don't mind wading. This was our
first time in the park. Is there a trail next to the creek on the
park side? Some of the box clues seemed to indicate that. If so,
it is under several feet of water.

If you are familiar with the location of the Frye Team's box "A True
Goblin", the water in the creek was within a couple of feet of the
three pine trees. The box was safe and dry, but exposed and about
to slip down the slope to the creek.

We also saw what we hope was a King snake. There are no Coral
snakes in this area, right? I forgot how to tell the difference.

Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter

From: Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter (janet@redbarnretreat.com) | Date: 2007-02-08 02:20:43 UTC
Seems everyone is getting out in the beautiful weather and getting
the letterboxing fever. We were no different, except that we had a
couple of boxes to place and, where we went, none to find. I'd like
to thank Puddle Splasher for placing one of my "Texas Governor"
boxes for me in Collin County. That's just where it had to be and,
since I couldn't make it up right away, she volunteered to hide it
for me. Very generous, and I thank her very much. The box, by the
way, is called "Old Leathercoat", as was the Texas governor that the
box honors. The box is posted, so you can look it up if you want to
know the gentleman's name. We headed east from where we live to a
spot east of Livingston, where we placed two new boxes. Mine is
called "The Alabama-Coushatta" for lack of a better name and honors
the Indian Tribe of that name. Lone Star Quilter placed a box
called "Indian Chief - Quilt Block Series", and it has one of her
lovely little quilt blocks in the box waiting for the first finder.
I intended to plant another governor box in Tyler County, which
desperately needs more boxes, but couldn't find an appropriate
place, so decided to do more research. This gentleman, like many

Good job! I have updated the map in the Files section but will
probably wait to notify the webmasters about updating the TX Clue map
since they just updated it recently.

For those that don't know, a couple of years ago the TX Clue map on
LBNA was updated by the webmasters to show counties with letterboxes
colored in green. We took up the challenge to populate the "virgin"
counties, thus coloring the whole state green. I created a map
showing our progress and put it in the Files section of this list.
When letterboxers place boxes in virgin counties, they let me know
(or post to the List) and I update the map. Then every 6 months or
so I send an updated map to the webmasters so they can update the TX
clue map on LBNA. Right now the TX Clue map accurately reflects
counties that have boxes, except for these 4 new ones. However, I
noticed that 4 counties that used to have boxes are no longer colored
because those boxes were removed from LBNA. They are: Foard in
North Region, Lee in Central Region, Live Oak in South Region, and
Dawson in Panhandle. Even though I show them colored in my map, they
will have to be repopulated before they can be colored on the
official TX Clue map.

As you can see, we have come a long way since we started this goal
back in December, 2006, and hopefully we can complete the Central
Region before the end of this year. If someone can repopulate Lee
county, I hope to get to Kinney in a month or so. Keep those boxes
coming!
