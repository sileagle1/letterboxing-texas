I know this has been discussed before, but I don't remember the
consensus. How do the Texas Tour Busses work? When you find a TTB,
is it supposed to count as dozens of finds (toward your F count) when
it is really only one find? I've sort of ignored the busses because
I couldn't, in good conscience, log 40 finds for one box. And I
understand that many boxers aren't concerned about F counts at all,
and that's totally cool. I happen to be proud of my F count, as
lowly as it is. And when someone asks how many boxes I've found, I
don't want to have to qualify my answer with, "but 40 of those finds
were in one box." So, can you find the tour bus and have it only
count as one find? And how does everyone else feel about it? (I'm
just curious if I'm the only one that would feel weird about it.)

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-10-17 22:17:07 UTC-05:00
I think when TeamKing set the original up on AQ, she entered each of the stamps individually, so that it wound up that every stamp wound up being a find.  When she set up the second one, she set it up as only one box.
 
I agree with you that I don't want to count every stamp as a find when I only made the effort to find it once.  Once that is, as long as you don't count the once when I managed to convince my LSS Fearless Fox to go out and try to get it because I had to go to work so he took Lightning and recruited his bro-in-law and our nephew and drove all the way to Martin Creek only to be robbed by a certain - ahem - "unnamed" letterboxer who left her house in the middle of the night to come venture into my neck of the woods to steal the bus right out from under Fearless Fox's et al's noses.  Ya know, I'M find with that, I really am, but Fearless Fox?  He's another story.  But I digress...
 
Yeah, I think it oughta be counted only once.  One effort - one find.  As far as I'm concerned though, the only count that matters is the one in my book, so I have them all logged as finds on AQ so that I can quickly know which ones I haven't stamped in yet, in the event the TTB1 ever resurfaces.  And I have hope for it, just as I have hope for Steve Fossett. 
 
But my personal opinion on counting finds is that you should only count as finds the ones that you really put the effort into.  HH's, Bonus Boxes, Cooties, passengers on buses, those are pleasant little niceties, surprises that make letterboxing that much more fun, but they aren't really finds because I didn't work for them - they came to me. 
 
But being a good accountant, I understand that different people can count the same things and come up with different totals.  And that's why the IRS continues to exist...

Re: F counts and the TTB

From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-10-18 12:32:08 UTC
I never really worry about adding up my finds. Most of our boxes are
registered as finds on AQ, but that's more for my boys than for me.
They like to know when they are approaching milestones. There are
still a lot of boxes we've found missing from our on-line logbook,
but it's good enough. Unlike Lucy, I am NOT an accountant, and
reaonable proximity is good enough in my book.

So if I were counting, I would count the TTB 1 as one find (and I
think that's how the TTB2 is set up--somewhere else TeamKing
acknowledged that's how she should have set up TTB1, but she didn't
want to change it at that date). On AQ, that means I only registered
one of the stamps as a find. But, I don't get my knickers in a twist
if someone else does it differently.

The traditional way to count a find is that you put your image in the
box's log. There are some people who won't count a box in a series
that doesn't have its own log. Others are less stringent.

I count a bonus box where I've followed clues to the bonus box. To
me that's a second find.

On Silent Doug's page, he says to count HHs in your F count, but I
don't. Like Lucy, that doesn't seem like you did anything to find
the box.

I think find a system that you're comfortable with and use that.
There's probably no one right answer.

dewberry

From: Maribeth Jagger (cjagger405@earthlink.net) | Date: 2007-10-18 14:48:02 UTC-05:00
I haven't joined this discussion because I really don't have a sense of how people need to do their counts. That's probably because I'm obviously kind of sloppy when it comes to recording my finds. I rarely record a HH as a find, although I definitely email the owner, and I've never recorded a cootie. I think I probably did record each stamp on the TT1 as a find since that's how it was set up on AQ. I use the counts primarily to mark the boxes I've already located. Although I'm a Premium Member of AQ, and have used the function of recording finds that are not boxes listed on AQ, I find that there are many that I have not recorded. My thinking is that my list is basically there for me, and it probably doesn't matter too much to anyone else how many or which finds I have recorded. I was excited when I passed a big milestone (500), but I use my actual count from my logbook instead of LBNA or AQ for that. Some people who list boxes on AQ have used the option of making some of their boxes available only to boxers who have a minimum number of finds, so it is helpful to list my finds on AQ for that reason.
 
Having said that, I consider myself to be a "traditionalist" when it comes to the "code of conduct" of letterboxing. I hope every new member who becomes interested in letterboxing will check out these two sites, before ever searching for or placing their first box. http://www.atlasquest.com/aboutlb/rules/ and http://www.letterboxing.org/faq/faq.html
 
Of course there are gray areas when it comes to certain questions, such as how closely is appropriate to place your box to an existing box (ie: is it really necessary to get permission from someone who has placed a box on the Appalachian Trail if you'd also like to place on there). Common sense is difficult to regulate, so I guess the old "Golden Rule" of treating others (and their boxes) as you would like them to treat you, is a good guide.
 
I'd just like to say that I really admire Stargazer for asking these questions, because it is a good reminder to each of us that we need to be respectful of each others boxes as well as locations.
