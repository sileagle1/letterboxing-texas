From: rachelincolorado (rachelincolorado@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-01-26 01:57:26 UTC
Wow! It was exciting to see this list posted. I found it today
after looking up RMLetterboxing (Rocky Mountain Letterboxing). YES,
I will be a lurker from Colorado but am TEXAN by birth and proud to
be from Texas! I grew up in South Texas - "Odem" to be exact and
I'm very familiar with that area - so hearing about towns from there
in your posts is very familiar to me!

We moved to Colorado almost two years ago in May! I lived in Austin
since 1989 and my husband since 1987. We discovered letterboxing in
Austin and hid some there as well. We have 5 hidden in Bastrop
State Park, our favoritie SP in Texas AND my husband proposed to me
with a letterbox (not hidden) in Bastrop SP! So when I read about
the Bastrop event I was so envious! I'm going to work very hard to
convince my husband, also a letterboxer - hopefully he will post
soon, that we should go to this event!!!! I know it's a longshot
but I really want to go.

Look forward to the news, ideas, events and meeting new people!
Thanks for putting this group together!

Ciao,
Rachel Carlson aka the Pumpkin Princess
P8F19X16

From: Silver Eagle (sileagle@alltel.net) | Date: 2004-01-26 04:17:44 UTC
Welcome to the group! Just want to point out to the rest
of the group how special Pumpkin Princess and her husband
Eoghan are to Texas Letterboxing. They were one of the
first letterboxers in Texas and currently have some of the
oldest, if not the oldest letterboxes still alive. When
I first started boxing, only theirs, a few in Dallas,
Brownsville, Hueco Tanks and Franklin Mountains existed.
Now, the original ones in Dallas (which I think were the
first ever in Texas), are gone and I'm not sure about the
others. It must have been tough waiting all those years
for the rest of us to start letterboxing and find your boxes.
It would almost be a crime if ya'll weren't at the First
Texas Letterboxing Event!

From: rachelincolorado (rachelincolorado@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-01-26 16:12:39 UTC
Ha! Thanks Silver Eagle! Saves me from tooting my own horn, tee,
hee! I found my first letterbox (Lost Pines) Buescher back in
2000! And it was Eoghan's box! That's the funny part, we were
hiding boxes for each other to find at that time. Sad but true. We
did find the one in Dallas, which was a series but only 'one' was to
be found - I don't think those are around anymore. So you can see
how desperate we were for other people to start letterboxing at that
time! It was still so new. We even made sure to look boxes up if
we were taking a road trip.

I will work hard to convince Eoghan that we should go to Bastrop.
It would be a shame if we didn't. We LOVE the cabins there but they
book so quickly! We shall see!

Take care all of you and have a great week!

Ciao,
Pumpkin Princess
