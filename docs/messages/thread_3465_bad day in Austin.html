bad day in Austin

From: Sandy Milfeld (pugapalooza@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2008-04-04 02:33:15 UTC-05:00
Okay, do you ever have one of those really bad letterboxing days?
And does it ever occur when you are out of town? I am just getting
back into boxing after my annual winter hibernation and I'm off to a
sour start.

We went to Austin yesterday. I decided to hit a few boxes as we were
heading back to Fort Worth this evening. I missed the first two
because I was too late getting to the cemetery. (Closes at 6pm.)
The next box on my list was in a facility (private property) where
there is a guard station. The clues tell you that you may be stopped
and they are specific about what you are supposed to say. Well, the
guard stopped us, questioned us, didn't like our answer or the way we
looked or something, and then proceeded to search our car! Then he
got very belligerent. So I explained letterboxing and what we were
looking for. His response: "There is a WAR going on, in case you
didn't know, and you are telling me you are looking for hidden
boxes!? I should call the authorities!" Then he ripped the clues
out of my hand and demanded that I give him the contact number for
the person responsible for this act of terrorism. (My words, but
that's the way he made it sound.) I almost lost it. I was so mad I
was about to cry. Do you ever get that mad? My eight year old son
was in the back of the car about to cry and THAT got me really
steamed. I grabbed my clues out of his hand and said, "This is MY
property and you don't have the authority to confiscate it." He
tried to grab it back! Then things got really ugly and I probably
gave letterboxers a very bad name, but I felt like a mother bear
protecting her cubs and her right to hunt, so I felt justified.

By now the sun was going down and I still hadn't found a box. So I
stopped for one last try at a box hidden at some stables (not giving
anything away there, it's in the clues). I stepped in horse dookie,
and there was a rather large (but harmless) snake right on top of the
box that scared my husband to death, but golly darn-it I got that box
and made friends with a nice mare to boot. So I felt that the day
wasn't an entire loss. But if you happen to go searching for a box
in Austin that requires you to stop at a guard station, be ready for
the inquisition.

I hope y'all are doing better in your searches. I think tomorrow I
will go for a nice eventless hunt in the mid-cities area.

Black Widow
