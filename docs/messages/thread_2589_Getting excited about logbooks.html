Getting excited about logbooks

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-02-17 21:12:04 UTC-06:00
My dear-friend-who-drags-me-in-to-everything-she-does and I went shopping today for letterboxing stuff for the Cub Scout event coming up in April.  We bought bunches of stamp pads, but even better, we shopped PAPER!
 
For ease of talking about her (because dear-friend-who-drags-me-in-to-everything-she-does is simply too long, even if I initialize it), I will call her by what will be her trailname if I ever get her into letterboxing - She Who Reads.  That's her name under some other venue, and she has promised me she won't consider using anything else.  So she is - She Who Reads.
 
Hobby Lobby is a wondrous place.  We walked in the store and the air seemed fresher and the light a little brighter.  Of course, it could've been the powder fresh scented air freshener that squirted just as we walked in, and the lack of lampshades on the nearby display of lamps, but I prefer to think of it as fresher and brighter.  I LOVE that place!
I had not ever shopped paper there before, but I am now thoroughly enthused about the potential for making cool logbooks!  We are going to stay fairly simple, but we are going to use Mat Paper for the backs of the book, a nice grade of cardstock - no gloss - for the pages, and a lightly textured colored cardstock for the front cover.  Probably going to use a comb binder to punch holes, then let the boys weave through the holes using hemp, then decorate the covers with natural material.  So many ideas - and what a springboard for starting to make my own books!
 
She Who Reads shamelessly pulled me in on this event by making letterboxing the vehicle for teaching other things to the boys, but she's starting to perk her ears up to it herself!   Hehe...
--
Barefoot Lucy
"It's not about footwear, it's about philosophy"

Re: Getting excited about logbooks

From: SandiBox (sandibox@msn.com) | Date: 2007-02-18 04:17:12 UTC
Personally I'm getting a little nervous about logbooks! Since I've
committed to demonstrating how to make a logbook at the gathering...
which is only two weeks away... gulp!... I'm getting a little
anxious. Trying to put together instructions is not as easy as I'd
imagined. It's kind of like my DH who can find his way home easily
enough, but can't give directions on how to reach our house, even
though we've lived here for 20 years.

On a different note, we had a wonderful letterboxing day. The
weather was beautiful - warm in the sun, cool in the shade. We went
to Brazos Bend State Park and found several letterboxes from the
March '05 gathering. If you've never been letterboxing there, I
highly recommend it! The boxes displayed the wonderful creativity
I've come to expect from Texas Letterboxers. The teens were excited
to see 3 alligators up close. I got a kick out of looking through
the logbook in a box that has been in place for couple years and
seeing then "Mother of Four" had stamped in (seems the singing
letterboxing baby hadn't yet made her appearance at that time!). We
finished off the day with SoccerChik planting her first ever
letterbox, Wish Upon a Star (see AQ for clues). Her idea, her
carving, her hand-made logbook, her choice of location, her clues.
Makes a letterboxing Momma very proud!

SandiBox

Barefoot Lucy wrote: Okay - for you paper-holics - do you have a paper rack? 
 
 
Yet another reason I need a new house! When I had my graphic design business, I always wanted an oak architect's file to keep my artwork in. I searched for years, but the cheapest ones I could ever find (oak or metal) was well over a couple hundred dollars - used, and many were close to a thousand. After I moved to Connecticut and had my antique business, I spent many hours wandering through resale shops, etc. One day while cruising the aisles of my favorite Goodwill, I stumbled upon a gorgeous huge, oak, five drawer architect's flat file in perfect condition for $25. It was so big and heavy, we had to go home first and take the seat out of the Jeep and the top off just to get it home, and it took two big men to lift it. Right now, it's sitting out in my garage just waiting for a space to put it. I can't wait to get it IN the house and organize my art, paper, etc in it.
 
I also found a music file (narrow open cabinet with about 12 slots) which was in "not so good" condition at a garage sale for $7. I brought it home and added legs, fixed it up, painted it and use it to hold my shallow baskets of paints, canvas, palettes, etc. Love it! I need to put casters on it so I can easily roll it around beside my easel (but I've been meaning to do that for at least four years).
