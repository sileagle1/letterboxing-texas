Weekend in the East Texas Piney Woods

From: CelticLions (celticlions@comcast.net) | Date: 2004-09-06 22:22:35 UTC-05:00

What a great weekend! Beautiful weather, gorgeous pine woods and
terrific letterboxes placed in interesting locations!
We found "My Heart Belongs in Texas" with a lovely HH - "Swans". Both
were in good condition and we enjoyed the easy hike and clever
placement. (Meanwhile, Cactus Licker enjoyed catching a bass well over a
foot long in the clear water of Tyler State Park - made his day!)

In Lufkin we had fun wandering around the Texas Forestry Museum nature
trail, and found "Lufkin Lookout" & "Road Runner". "Roadrunner" was a
little damp, but we dried it out & the logbook looks fine. We had looked
forward to the Stone Celt - Caddo Burial Mound box, but we arrived at
one minute past 4:00, and the barrier was down- a good reminder to us to
double-check opening & closing times for museums and historic sites!

We made our way up to Rusk where Cactus Licker had a blast casting into
giant lily pads on the lake while we found Texas RR box - in great shape
and containing a HH (Kokopelli) which must have been placed there just
before us. Our pathetic acting skills were called into play, because we
could not outwait a family nearby. I pretended to spill my pack and then
scrambled around, blocking the view, as Stillwater retrieved the
letterbox. The family didn't even turn around to admire our performance!

Our entrance into Mission Tejas Park was enlivened by the Park Ranger
who is quite a trickster, and snared CL as a likely audience - it's
worth going, so I won't spoil it for you all! We loved the mission, and
the box - cleverly hidden. Had to outwait a family of sightseers, but it
was worth it. Unfortunately, the Tejas box at another location in the
park was waterlogged. I'll write to the owner with more details.

Wish we'd had more time at the Hogg Home site - we zipped thru the
nature trail (Cactus Licker surprised a snake) stamped in & out and had
to leave.

Tried to get into the Tiger Creek preserve twice - both times the
barrier was across the road - we'll have to go back for that one!

Thank you so much to all the creators of the boxes mentioned - you gave
us a delightful and educational weekend!

Ruby Tuesday
CelticLions
