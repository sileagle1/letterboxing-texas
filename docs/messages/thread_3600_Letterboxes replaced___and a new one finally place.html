Letterboxes replaced...and a new one finally placed.

From: Wanda (wsthm@hotmail.com) | Date: 2008-06-01 18:12:52 UTC
All of the letterboxes that I had removed last week are now replaced.
Also, I added a new one - Adam and Eve - that I am really happy with.
I carved the stamp and got the box ready 4 years ago! So I am really
glad that it is finally placed. Maybe I should start a Bible Story
series while I seem to be on a roll. ;)

Oh - and I know I am losing my mind, but I just had that confirmed
again... The three-part stamp for the Bee Hive Hitchhiker Hostel,
that I thought had two of the parts missing, was all there. I had
carved all three stamp images on one piece of 'rubber'. Even looking
at it, I had not noticed, and forgotten all about that little fact.
The stamp just has to be flipped and then turned on end to get all
three images. Sheesh!

Busy Bee (Wanda)


Wanda wrote:
Hi everyone! It's been so long, y'all probably don't remember me,
but I wanted to drop by and give an update on a few of my
letterboxes.
> In Evergreen Cemetery, near Skidmore, I have pulled three of my
boxes. The Bottino Family emailed me recently telling me that the
books were full and damaged. When I went to check on them, the boxes
on two of> them were cracked too, so they will be replaced as
well. 'Watchful Birds' and 'Rest in Peace #1' will be returned very
soon, but 'The Bee Hive Hitchhiker Hostel' is missing 2 parts of the
3-part box stamp. So I am going to have to do some recarving there.
Apparently someone mistook those two stamps (a bee and a hexagon(?)
shape) for hitchhikers! There are several nice hitchhikers in the
hostel, but I am taking good care of them until the whole thing is re-
placed.
I did get a report that the Lost and Found Leaf outside of a
cemetery in Beeville is alive and well - except for needing a new
book. I think it had been reported as missing before. So, I will get
back to that one with a new booklet as soon as I can get around to
it.
Happy boxing,
Busy Bee (Wanda)
