Carving and planting (Was Another Little Rant)

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-07-31 13:11:05 UTC-07:00
There will always be some finders who insist on nice
art. It's an ongoing debate that seems to come up on
one LB list or another periodically - are we in it for
the art or are we in it for the experience?

I look back at my first stamps and I'm amazed at how
much my carving has developed. Do I regret planting
those first boxes? Not at all. I'm glad they are out
there and I don't apologize for them. I'm sure there
are some folks who find them and think "Wow...what was
she thinking when she carved THAT?" Who cares. I
enjoyed carving those stamps and planting those boxes
and they were the best I could do at the time. I
didn't promise Rembrandt in my clues after all!

I think one of the best ways for a new boxer to become
a good boxer is to plant a box. When you plant, you
get the full extent of the experience. You learn what
it is like to create a box and set it free. You
almost certainly find out what it is like to lose a
box. You find out how nice it is to get an email
saying "found your box". Becoming a planter makes you
a better finder.

Most folks will be very encouraging to you as they go
and find your boxes. But if creating and planting the
box gave you pleasure, it was worthwhile to you
anyway. Artwork is subjective and if one person
doesn't like your stamp, someone else will come along
who does.

Hang in there!
Lucy
