Gosh I hope you're right - all the areas we will be going appear to be under federal ownership and all within Sam Houston National Forest.  Very likely four different areas, so we could be looking at $20 a person if it isn't that way - oh and, we'll be visiting a couple of state parks too!  It makes more sense to make it one fee (well, one for the state facilities and one for the feds), but sense doesn't always strike the guy taking the money the same way it hits the guy giving it up!

Just out of curiosity, if Hueco Tanks allows access for multiple days on one fee, how much is that fee?
 
I have a funny story about those day use fees - about 17 years ago, we found ourselves homeless for about five months.  We wound up camping in a state park in California until we could get back on our feet.  We could barely afford the very cheapest of sites - one without electricity for $7 a night.  There were no per person day use fees, but you did have to pay an extra $1 if you had more than one vehicle.  We had two vehicles and the park would give you a folded up receipt to put in the window that showed in bold marker the last day of the month the vehicle would be in the park ( i.e. it would have a 19 if the vehicle was to leave after the 19th).  We just could not afford that $1 but we had to pay it because the extra vehicle was, at times, the only shelter my infant daughter and I had from the elements during the day while the other vehicle was gone with my husband to work.  Well after a week or two of paying it and realizing this was going to be more than a passing situation, we realized we could keep the receipts and reuse them from month to month and as long as we laid pretty low, the park wouldn't realize it.  We reused those receipts for four months and never got caught.  It was a great feeling not to have to keep track of them once we got going again, and here about 5 years ago we sent the California park where we had stayed a check for $150 to cover all those fees we avoided.  I've always wondered what they thought when they got that money, but they DID cash the check!
 
Lucy

Wolf Creek park is run by the Trinity River Authority. They charge
$4.00 per car and $2.00 per person. We wanted to put a letterbox
there, but we would have had to pay $8.00 to get in (just the two of
us) and we thought that would be unreasonable. We have a Golden Age
pass (sometimes being old has advantages) and Cagle charges us half
price, but Double Lake gave us a permanent day pass, so we get in
there free. Stubblefield works on the honor system (no attendant),
so I used my Cagle receipt and just placed it on the dash. If you
visit state parks often, it really helps to get the annual pass.
