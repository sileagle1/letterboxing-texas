> Hi all! With the coming of spring, the Webmasters at LbNA are seeing lots of
> notes with questions about missing boxes and retired letterbox clues. Here's
> a brief note about how to manage your own retired and missing letterbox
> clues.
>
> The LbNA FAQ on the topic says:
> I had to remove my actual letterbox. Can I delete my clues?
> You have the option of archiving your letterbox or deleting it completely.
> Archiving it removes it from the standard letterbox lists, but you can still
> see it in grey in your list of letterboxes in Member Services and in the
> LbNA Basement. You can activate one of your archived letterboxes from the
> same list later. To delete the letterbox, click archive then click the link
> to delete the box.
>
> So the best thing to do with a retired letterbox clue is to "archive" it
> into the LbNA Basement. That way the clues still stay in your account for
> future reference, and other boxers who have logged it as "found" still keep
> their count.
>
> There are some folks that put the word "retired" into the clue title (or the
> body of the clues) instead of archiving the box. This does work, but the
> preferred method is to archive the clue.
>
> If you search for a letterbox and are convinced that it is missing, the
> first things to do are to use the "contact the placer" button to let them
> know, plus mark the box as attempted in the box status area. If you don't
> receive a reply from the placer, please feel free to contact the LbNA
> Webmasters (webmasters-lbna at yahoogroups dot com) and we will be glad to
> help.
>
> Happy boxing, Jay in CT
