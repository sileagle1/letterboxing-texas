The TPWD needs help.

From: Mike Olbrisch (mike-2007@elp.rr.com) | Date: 2008-03-10 21:35:17 UTC-06:00

Good evening.

Many of you use and enjoy our Texas State Parks. I certainly do. Some
of you have cultivated a relationship with your local park rangers, such as I
have. That is good, it helps us Letterboxers, Geocachers, Hikers, and it helps
the TPWD too. Well, some of us may be in a position to help TPWD some more.
This job was particularly suited to me, but perhaps others will want to jump in.
So I thought I would ask here.

Many of you know I hike trails with a GPS, download the track log into
my computer, then upload it to localhikes.com for others to enjoy. I have 51
hikes posted to date. Well, TPWD is trying to do nearly the same thing. There
is a group of people in Austin working on mapping the state park trails. This
will lead to other things in the future. But for now, they need trail maps.

As it turns out, the work I did for localhikes.com is about 75-% of the
job. The rest if filling out a short form for each trail, and changing the
track log format from .GPX to .SHP. They even give you a free program to do it
with. If you have a GPS, can walk a trail, and are willing to do it, you can
help too.

If anyone here on the list is interested in GPS, mapping trails, and
helping TPWD with this project, I would be most pleased to put you in contact
with the Austin folks. I am not sure how many parks need help, but you folks in
the eastern areas could surely find some park in need of assistance.

It doesn't hurt to give a little back. And it might even help in the
future. Maybe more than you think right now. You know how to contact me if
there is any interest.

Mike - KD9KC.
