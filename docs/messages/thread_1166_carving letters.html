Hi,

Are you trying to carve the letters into the stamp, or leave them
raised and carve everything around them? The latter usually works
better.

If you are having difficulty deciding what will look like a letter,
how about using an existing alphabet stamp kit and stamping the
letter/s onto your rubber. Use that as your guide for cutting.

Worst case, you can always flatten the area appropriately and glue on
ready-made letters cut out from kiddie alphabet rubber stamp kits.
(Toys R Us, Wal-Mart, other stores sometimes carry these all-rubber
kits for a few dollars.)

Does any of this answer your questions?
