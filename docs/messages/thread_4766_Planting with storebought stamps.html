From: tko1235 (TKO1235@rocketmail.com) | Date: 2011-04-23 13:34:04 UTC
4 of 5 in my first series planted had storebought stamps. It doesn't appear that they get any less traffic than the handcarved boxes I've planted. However where they are hidden is a nice park area that would be family/kid friendly to seek. I think it is more important where you hide the boxes and if the clues are well written. I prefer to find hand carved stamps because I like to see them when I reach the destination... however, if the plant is close by or a place that's fun to go to and I can take my dog - I'll still go find a plant with a storebought stamp.

The only time I probably get annoyed is if the plant is done carelessly (just tossed somewhere in the middle of PI, poor clues, etc) or done to the extreme (2 mile hike and bush wacking) and then find a storebought stamp. If you go to the trouble to hide a box in extreme terrain or distance with difficult clues - it better be a box worth seeking in my opinion.

I now only hide hand carved stamps. I find it less expensive, more creative, and fun to do. Carving to me is relaxing and a great way to wind down after work.

In these types of games, there are the standard rules and personal ones for each player. Don't let those who's personal rules prohibit them from looking for storebought stamps, keep you from playing the game either. If you want to hide a box with a storebought stamp, go for it. My only wish for you is that even though you have no desire to carve, you at least try it once.

Good luck,

From: Boots Tex (redbarn@eastex.net) | Date: 2011-04-23 15:26:53 UTC
I think there should be another category for personally-designed stamps because, to my way of thinking, they are equal to hand carved stamps in creativity and take as much talent, and are far superior to store-bought stamps, like those you would pick up at Michaels. Also, hand-carved stamps are not all created equal, so you never know what you're getting unless you know the reputation of the placer and personally-designed stamps will always be unique and well-executed. While I don't exclude store-bought stamps, they would tend to be at the bottom of my find list when I'm on a hunting trip. In other words, if they are right there and I have time, I'll stamp in, but if I have to go out of my way or take too much time, I'll tend to skip them. If that's elitist, I'm sorry, there are too many boxes available to me and I know I can't find them all. May I ask why, if you are not interested in carving, you feel the need to place a letterboxbox? Maybe you would be satisfied just being a finder or a geocacher (I'm just sayin'). In the end, there are not many rules. If you want to place a box and don't want to carve, then use a store-bought stamp. That's your choice. As has been said before, though, if you choose to use store-bought stamps, your boxes need to be superior in other ways; interesting story, interesting location, good clues. And, make the box fit the stamp. If you establish that kind of reputation, the fact that the stamp is store-bought won't matter as much. There is one prominent letterboxer in Texas that uses some store-bought stamps and his boxes are deservedly sought-after for the reasons stated above. Just my opinion.

Boots Tex

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2011-04-24 12:45:51 UTC-05:00
There's a big difference between saying store-boughts are a no-no and saying that hand-carveds are preferred.

If planting store-boughts gives you the planting satisfaction you want, plant store-boughts. Those who don't like store-boughts won't hunt them, but plenty of others will. You won't ever make everyone happy, because everyone has their own preferences. Consider this - some would not look for your box if it were a beautiful hand-carved stamp but it wasn't a mystery. Others might not stop on a street corner to find a perfect hand-carved drive-by because they only like hikes. There are all kinds of boxes for all kinds of boxers and on the whole, plenty enough of each for all of us, even if we do have to drive a little for them.

If you want to plant letterboxes, figure out who your target finder is and plant boxes that make them - and you - happy. And if you prefer geocaching or postals over letterboxing, that's okay too, but that's reason enough to say "I like geocaching better" or "I like postals better than letterboxing".
