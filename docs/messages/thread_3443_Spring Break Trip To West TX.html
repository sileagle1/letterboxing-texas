Spring Break Trip To West TX

From: Silver Eagle (sileagle@alltel.net) | Date: 2008-03-24 17:22:17 UTC
I'm back from my family's Spring Break trip to West TX and wanted to
give some of the highlights to entice you to visit this part of our
state. I managed to place 15 boxes, 8 in new counties, to serve as
a "breadcrumb trail" leading you there and back. I also moved my
Displaced Donkey microbox to Balmorhea SP, which was crying for a
box, and placed Paisano Pete in Fort Stockton where the Donkey used
to be.

We left Saturday, 3/15/08, about 12:30 after my son's merit badge
class and headed for Junction, where we camped at South Llano River
SP. I was impressed by this park and it looked like a fun place to
tube in the summer, so I placed Junction Boys microbox there. The
next day we headed for the Davis Mountains area of TX where we stayed
2 nights at the Limpia hotel in Fort Davis. On the way I placed
Sonora Butterfly at a rest area off I10 near Sonora and Paisano Pete
in Fort Stockton. I picked up Displaced Donkey and moved it to
Balmorhea SP, a great place to cool off in the summer. For those of
you who already visited the Paisano Pete statue to stamp the Donkey
box, you can visit again to stamp the new box dedicated to it.

While in Fort Davis I placed another box (Vanguard 1) in Davis Mts
SP, but on a different trail than my McDonald Observatory microbox.
Normally I don't like to place more than 1 box at a site, but I
needed to place this box on March 17 and that is where I found myself
on that date. The date marks the 50th anniversary of the launch of
Vanguard 1 satellite, the oldest human-made object still in space.
Not many people remember it, including several amateur astronomers at
McDonald Observatory, but I think it is quite an accomplishment and
deserves to be remembered at least through letterboxing.

Speaking of McDonald Observatory, that is still one of my favorite
places to go see objects in space at one of their Star Parties. I
always see or learn something new, and this time was no different.
As we sat listening to the astronomer talk, he casually mentioned
that in a few minutes we would be able to see an Iridium Flare. I
had never heard of it before and he explained that it is one of
several telecommunication satellites in low Earth orbit operated by
the Iridium Corporation. Each has antennas with highly reflective
surfaces that can be seen by ground observers when the angle with the
Sun is just right, and it just so happened that it was going to
happen that night. At the appointed time we saw the satellite grow
brighter until it made a flash almost like a camera. It was awesome!

The next day was cold and cloudy as we headed through the mountains
on our way to El Paso. The temperature was about 34 degrees and we
actually came across snow on the ground! Of course I stopped and we
had a great snowball fight, then we built a small snowman on the hood
of the car against the windshield that lasted quite a ways down I10.
Once in El Paso, I talked the family into letting me letterbox at
Franklin Mountains SP and I managed to find a few, including my first
one by Desert Warrior. I also left him a new box called Glory Road,
near UTEP campus.

The next morning we headed to Hueco Tanks SP for our 10:30 rock art
tour. We got there early so I was able to find the Hueco Tanks
letterbox that Mike just placed. Both the box and the tour were
great, and I highly recommend going to this park. I see why Mike has
so much fun climbing around on all those rocks!

Now we started heading back home, but via a different route. We went
past Guadalupe Mountains NP, where I found Mike's box at the rest
area. I was the first letterboxer to find it, but surprisingly a
group of kids had accidentally found it several months earlier, even
though the box is under several large rocks. We then drove to Pecos,
where I placed my Pecos Cantaloupe microbox, and on to Monahans
Sandhills SP where we camped for the night. The next morning I
placed my Sand Surfing microbox, then we rented disks and surfed the
dunes. It was a lot of fun, but hard work climbing back up the giant
dunes.

Next we headed to Odessa and Midland, where I found a few boxes and
placed my Friday Night Lights minibox. I did not have the time to
find all the boxes in that area so will have to come back some day.
I was able to go to the Meteor Crater, though, and it was very
interesting.

We continued east through Glasscock & Sterling counties, where I
placed Gone To Texas and Windmill City microboxes. The next stop was
San Angelo SP where I planned to camp, but the family was anxious to
get home so I just placed my Fort Concho minibox and we drove on. We
drove through Eden, where I placed Cow Patty Bingo, then found a
motel in Brady for the night.

It turned out to be a good thing that we stayed in Brady instead of
camping at San Angelo, because I found a great little BBQ place that
I would never have gone to. It is called Hard 8 BBQ, and they have
all kinds of meat that you order outside where the cook removes it
right from the grill onto your plate. Now this town is known for its
Goat Cook-Off every year, and in fact that is the name of the
microbox I placed there the next morning, so you can also order
Cabrito. Unfortunately, they only offer it on Fri-Sun, and it was a
Thursday when I was there. Oh well, I got brisket and sausage and
headed inside to pay and eat. The lady told us there was a
complimentary pot of beans, along with pickles, onions and sauce by
the tables, then asked what we wanted to drink. I asked what they
had and she rattled off several soft drinks, then said "and
complimentary beer". I did a double-take and asked if she just said
FREE beer? She said yes and explained that the owner feels so
strongly about having beer available for BBQ eaters that he offers it
free since he can't sell it in that city. She then said in an
apologetic tone that all they had that night was Shiner Bock. I
thought of Mother Of Five, smiled to myself, and said "that'll be
fine".

The next morning we drove through Mason, home of Fred Gipson, the
author of Old Yeller, and I placed a box dedicated to him. Then it
was on to Inks Lake SP where I found a couple of boxes and placed
Devil's Waterhole microbox on a nice hike. It was here that I first
saw that JB Kokopelli was nearby since she had stamped in that very
day. I ended up following her to Longhorn Cavern SP to find the
boxes there but never ran into her.

After that we went straight home and started getting ready for Easter
company. It was a lot of driving (almost 2000 miles) but also a lot
of fun and I highly recommend a similar trip. I hope y'all had a
great Easter!

Silver Eagle
