Okay...this one I've definitely got $0.02 cents to toss around. A WOM
box is, as Janet guessed, a Word of Mouth box.

IMHO, these boxes are usually boxes that the placer has put a ginormous
amount of time and effort into and does not want them to go missing.
It may also be a box that is in a more delicate environmental area and
the placer doesn't want the most casual of boxers to go trampling
through the area. They seem to be boxes that are placed for only the
most experienced of boxers (assuming that experienced boxers are more
stealthy, environmentally aware and always careful to RECOVER WELL!)

In my case, when I did my Cookie Caper series in the Spring, my boxes
were all part of a series and you had to find them ALL to win the prize
(a box of GS cookies). I didn't want casual boxers to hunt for them
and risk one of them being discovered and "lost", and then compromise
the series for those boxers that might come after them. Therefore, I
only made the email link available to those boxers who took the time
and trouble to email me first and request the clues. Though perhaps
not TECHNICALLY a WOM box...the clues were NOT made public to just any
Johnny or Susie boxer.

BTW, the series went well and all boxes stayed safely hidden through
the end of the "cookie season". (I do plan to repeat the series again
this year, but to get the clue link...you'll have to answer a riddle
a'la the BusyBeesFamily's Personal Traveler!)

As far as sharing WOM clues...I think it's a definite "no-no" unless
you specifically ask the placer for permission. I kinda think of WOM
clues as a "secret knock" to let you into the "secret club", and other
boxers should be invited by the placer.

If you need hints (as I did once), I do think it's okay to ask other
boxers that you KNOW have had the clues before you. However, when I
asked for hints (via the Txletterboxers group)...I tried to do so
subtly...without listing the WOM clues directly and without listing the
placer's name. (I am still working on those clues, by the way! Pretty
much got them figured out, just need to set out on a trip!)

That's all I've got on WOM boxes. Anybody else?

From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-09-24 17:12:04 UTC
Okay, I've got some cents to throw around on this.

I do have some WOM boxes. I also have some that would be considered
"Hidden Clues." Your imagination is free to take flight on what that
means. You never know where a clue might be lurking. . .it's one of
my goals to really explore this idea and continue to add complexity to
my clue-ing.

For instance, I really want to do a Flame War box, and if you were the
innocent recipient of a big ole Internet Flame, the clue would just
drop in your email inbox. Kind of like a heal your wounds WOM box.
Doesn't that sound fun? And a good way to poke fun at the whole
touchiness of people on Internet chat lists?

Clearly, I personally love the tricky clues. To me an obscured clue
is part of the fun--like having a present arrive unexpectedly, not for
your birthday or Valentines' or some Hallmark-created holiday, but
because it's Thursday and the giver was simply thinking of you. When
you look at something and you realize there's another way to see,
well, to me that's amazing.

Finally, when I distribute clues, I may mention that you are free to
share the clue with others who might appreciate it. I personally do
not like it if without permission you share WOM clues or hidden clues
or the answers to my mystery boxes with others. I won't publicly flay
you, but I will be much less likely to make you the recipient of the
next one.

Dewberry

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-09-24 13:40:00 UTC-05:00
I have four (I think) sets of clues that require a potential finder to contact me for the clues and there are three different reasons why I have them as WOM boxes.  However, none of mine are meant to be exclusive - they are WOM because of special considerations.
 
Two of them are series I placed for scouts and I want to be contacted so I can know what scout groups in my area are letterboxing and so I can help them learn to box in such a way as to not other boxers' boxes at risk because of poor habits.  And I readily give them to letterboxers I know as well.  Also, because they are geared toward kids, I want to know there is a gatekeeper (me) between my boxes and someone who may mean ill will toward my targeted finders.  I have no illusion that this makes them completely safe, but it adds a layer of safety. 
 
One of mine is in a very high risk location AND it is sentimentally very close to my heart.  Not a good combination, I know, but the place it is is where it has to be.  By requiring that someone contact me for the clues, I can reiterate to them the need to be stealthy and re-cover well and I know who is looking for it also.
 
The third is a box I planted in honor of another boxer.  To get the clues, the finder has to email me the EXACT phrase that would be found on the stamp if I weren't such a bad carver (long story...).  Hopefully in the course of researching it, they learn a little something about the subject as well.  It's also a bit of a risky box though, and frankly, if a finder can't be diligent enough to get the exact phrase, why should I think they will be diligent enough to recover my box as it should be?  So that box has a multiple reason for being WOM.
 
Three different reasons for placing them as WOMs, but the key is that I want to be contacted by the finder to get the clues.  The understanding is that clues to a WOM box, or the solution to a puzzle in a WOM or mystery box, should only be passed on with the placer's permission, unless the placer has expressly said "share my clues with the world".  And certainly if you get clues to an unpublished WOM clue (one that is not listed on the sites and is passed directly between boxers, it's a big no-no to share!
