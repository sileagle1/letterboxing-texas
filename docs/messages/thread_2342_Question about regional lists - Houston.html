Question about regional lists - Houston

From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-12-02 22:18:20 UTC
So the metro boxers have a successful and thriving list. That makes
me want to throw the topic of a Houston/Galveston SE Texas yahoo group
out there.

There is an existing Houston list, but it is not actively moderated,
meaning membership is wide open. As a result, it usually is just spam
posts. I never really felt comfortable there, so I unjoined a while
ago. Since that time, we've had lots of new boxers in this area, to
the point that we have a pretty active group. Enough to support a
list if we chose.

I think there is real value and a necessity of joining and
participating in the Texas list. It's fun to share ideas and to get
to know the people across the state. I really like reading about
Barefoot Lucy's adventures, Mike's rescue of the Hueco Tanks box, and
Viewfinder's run-in with a tree. It also helps in planning statewide
gatherings. And we're all busy and it can be hard to keep up with
multiple lists. I would hate to see the regional lists negatively
impact that sense of community we share here.

That said, there are some local issues that are not applicable
state-wide, like issues with local city and county parks, or mini
gathers or just the uniqueness of boxing in this area. I think that
people might feel more comfortable with silly "chatter" about local
issues on a more local list.

So, I'm throwing it out there to SE Texas boxers. Do you want our own
list? Or would that be one too many? And if you do want one, what
should we call ourselves?

I'll start and co-moderate if there's interest. If there's not, we
can revisit the issue in 6 months or a year. Just because there's not
a need now, doesn't mean there won't be one in the future. Things are
constantly changing and evolving in this game.

Dewberry


Re: [letterboxingtexas] Question about regional lists - Houston

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-12-02 19:39:53 UTC-06:00
I will ask that y'all read my post from start to finish, or not at all.
 
I'm a member of the DFW list and have been pleased to see how well received it has been!  I was also a member of the Houston list that never really took off.  Personally, I think there are two key differences as to why one has been successful and the other wasn't:  1) an active involved moderator that relates well to others is critical to the success of the list, especially in the beginning, and 2) the list has to be structured properly to make it a safe place. 
 
I have some personal thoughts about regional lists:
 
1) While I like to read about topics of purely local interest for areas not my own, I quite often wind up deleting them because my inbox seems to stay full and I have to prioritize what I read.  The only way I know of to manage localized postings on the Texas list would be to start dictating that if you are going to post about a region, you need to put the region in the heading, but "dictating" is purely unacceptable for letterboxers and that would be an intuitively bad move.
 
2) I truly believe that we in Texas have developed a base of respect in the letterboxing world and I believe that is primarily because we have thrown in together and earned that respect through the cohesion that has happened in large part due to the Texas list.  I believe that we put that base at risk if we splinter into smaller groups (note that I say we RISK - I don't say we DOOM).
 
3) On a purely personal basis, I really hate to see local lists pop up because I really don't "belong" to any local area.  There are a lot of other folks in the same boat - and some of those might not feel comfortable crashing regional lists if they don't live in the region.  I mean, we could start a list for the Northeast Region and the six or ten of us that live here (most of whom are very quiet, listwise) could join it, but the Northeast region really has no commonalities other than geography, unlike the more urban areas.  Now I know y'all will all say "you can join our list", and I know I and anyone else would be welcome on them and I would join them, but I always feel more like a nosey neighbor than a part of the group on regional lists.
 
Now realize - I am NOT saying I am opposed to regional/local lists.  That is absolutely NOT the case.  I think Ruby has been a great moderator and I think Dewberry would do an excellent job for the Houston group, and they both have my full support and goodwill in getting groups underway; however, I think we need to take a hard look at what we would want from both regional/local lists and from the state list.  I would hate for the state list to founder as the local lists develop IF there is a continued need for the state list (and I believe there is), but that is a very real possibility as people pour their energy into the local lists to the neglect of the state list.  
 
I believe that we have a significant enough member base to support BOTH local and the state list.  
 
I believe that we can best serve our new Texas letterboxers by having both the state and local lists - they can use the local lists to ask questions about local boxes or customs, and the state list to glean from the broad basis of wisdom from folks all over the state (and other parts of the country).
 
I started this Texas list because I, as a new boxer with not a soul in my area and wanting to feel more like a letterboxer, saw a need.  I hoped I wouldn't find myself sitting on the floor of a big empty list wondering if anyone would show up, and I was not disappointed.  I knew if it had a decent bit of management, the list would grow, so I asked Silver Eagle to help out because I knew I could manage the list but we needed his greater wisdom in letterboxing, and it did grow - more than I ever suspected it would.  And I know that the list has grown strong enough that it will remain viable until there is no further need for it.  And that will be okay if that ever happens too, as long as it happens because the list has completed its purpose. 
 
I guess my greatest fear is that regional lists will unintentionally become a replacement for the Texas list for individuals, and that we as a community will suffer some unintended consequences if we don't consider benefits that we have because of our strong state-wide presence.
 
I remind you of what I said in the very beginning of the Texas list - this list is yours.  And I have seen Ruby say the same thing about the metro list, and Dewberry, I'm sure, is saying the same thing as she asks for your opinion.   So my question is - what do YOU (the broad YOU-not the Dewberry YOU - so all the rest of y'all speak up!) hope to gain from regional lists?  What would you expect from the Texas list?  How would you propose that they work together to give Texas letterboxers the best of all worlds?
--
Barefoot Lucy

My off-the-cuff reaction to your email is that you have raised some very good concerns.  I know what you mean by having the feeling you would be crashing someone else's party by participating in a list that is not really representative of your area. When I first read Dewberry's post re: a Houston list, I thought, I want to join that one too so I don't miss out on anything, but like you said, I may not feel too comfortable about posting messages on it since it's not really my area.... although I really don't care who post messages on our metroboxers list.
 
 I think the best way to insure these various lists best serve the purposes for which they were originally established is to make sure when you make a post to one of these lists, you consider which list would benefit most from it. ie: if you are sending a post about a local/regional situation, send it to the local list; if the list is more general (such as your boxing adventures) or a Texas gathering etc, share it with the entire Texas lists. I love reading posts about boxing in the hill country, Houston area, or Austin etc, although I don't get down that way as often as I would like. It would make me sad not to hear the stories of all the Texas boxers I have met in person or on the Texas list. Wasn't it fun to hear from Boots and Lone Star Quilter in Italy? Those posts certainly belongs on the full Texas list. On the other hand, if your post is personal, it's probably best to send it directly to the person you are wanting to contact.
 
It does seem that we should keep the Texas list as our primary source of messages and use the local lists more for local concerns. Texas is a big ol' state though, so having regional lists do serve a purpose.

From: Ruby Tuesday (celticlions@comcast.net) | Date: 2006-12-02 21:18:40 UTC-06:00

As usual, our Lucy has written a well-thought out and eloquent post, and I promise that I did read it from start-to-finish!

 

When we started a MetroBoxers group for the DFW area, it was somewhat driven by the need to try to get our plans together for the Many Meese Mini-Meet. We were determined to pull something together for November 18th, because Silver Eagle was going to be in the area! Our past tries at getting a mini-meet together around here had fizzled, and we just felt that the time was right to give it another go: lots of enthusiastic new Boxers in the area, a reason to celebrate, and a fun and funny theme!

But we were a little concerned that discussions about who was bringing the chocolate moosicles might really begin to irritate the “bigger” Texas list…after all, if you’re in El Paso, with no chance of attending the Many Meese Mini-Meet, do you really CARE who brings what for potluck??? Some people are very skilled in using the delete button, but others really get annoyed at chatter about what might be considered off-topic stuff. On a little local list, we could chatter to our hearts’ content about the small stuff!

My own personal idea of a local list would include planning mini-meets, discussing local issues that impact local boxes, and creating a smaller, “cozier” feeling to encourage new boxers to get to know other local boxers. Some folks might be intimidated by posting a message to a list of a few hundred, but will venture out into a group of thirty or so ;-).

 

If Dewberry creates a Houston/Galveston list, I’ll join – we drive through Houston on the way to Galveston at least once every 6 weeks, so I’d love to keep up on that area news. (And I personally enjoy the heck out of Dewberry, so I’m guessing it would be a fun group!)

 

In the past, I’ve joined a regional group somewhere around the country when I thought we’d be visiting the area – just to get to know a few local boxers or to pick up on the general “feel” of the region. I hope all of you will free to come and go on the MetroBoxer list if you plan to visit the DFW area – or any time!

 

My guess is that the regional lists, like our MetroBoxers, will become very active around planning events and projects and less active as the event/project is completed. Right now we’re discussing creating a logo for patches, hiking stick medallions, or other letterboxing gear, representing the DFW area. That’s a conversation in which the rest of Texas would not be much vested, so it’s best to keep it local! (Although if other areas want to create a patch or medallion, it would be fun to trade them at the big annual gathering!)

 

Although I understand Lucy’s concern about the local lists drawing away some participation from the Texas list, I think it will all settle out eventually. There will always be a need for a statewide group – I love hearing about everyone’s adventures in different parts of this great state!

Local lists will either flourish or fade, depending on whether they are needed and serve their community.

Only time will tell!

 

Ruby Tuesday

From: Silver Eagle (sileagle@alltel.net) | Date: 2006-12-03 14:05:49 UTC
I have to admit, my first thought when this subject came up was "Oh
no, not another List". I have many of the same concerns that have
already been mentioned, namely that the TX List will suffer if too
many Local Lists crop up. My own experience with the Big List shows
how the evolution can go (though not necessarily). Before the TX
List, all my posts were on the Big List with TX in the subject line.
Since I figured most people would be bored with any post from TX,
which was not a big player in the game yet, I did not post often. I
did it mainly to point out boxes not found or in need of help, just
in case any TX boxers were out there that I did not know about.
Luckily this lead to finding others like Wanda (Busy Bee), Corazon
and Barefoot Lucy. Lucy then had the brilliant idea of creating this
List, which really helped TX Letterboxing take off. And I must admit
that my first reaction to this List was "There are not enough TX
boxers to justify it", which is why I never created one. Boy was I
wrong! It has grown to over 200 people and is the heart and soul of
TX Letterboxing. So much so that I hardly ever post to the Big List
now. But the funny thing is, I don't really miss posting to the Big
List since it was never intimate, just a source of information.
However, it did raise the issue of when to post to TX List vs Big
List, which could be a similar issue with Local Lists within TX. Do
I post information about local boxes only on the Local List? And if
so, what about people traveling from out of town that might be
interested? I tried posting to both the TX and Big List, but I
didn't like doing that, so I just starting posting here, which I
think is the right thing to do.

All that being said, it does not necessarily mean that people will
evolve into only using Local Lists instead of the TX List. I think
of the TX List as a big family, and Local Lists as immediate family,
whereas the Big List is just a collection of peers. Local Lists
might entice shy boxers to post more and become a bigger part of the
game, and maybe even start posting on the TX List. Hopefully, the
intimacy and history that we share on the TX List will be enough to
sustain us if Local Lists become popular.

So, this was a long-winded way of saying I don't know if we should
have a Houston-area List or not. Since many people don't like to
speak publically, maybe we should have a poll to see the level of
interest. No matter what is decided, I will support it, at least
initially.

One other thing I will say is that if most of the people that
currently post on the TX List end up joining the Local Lists, then
what is the point? I guess it could be argued that people that don't
post might not want to see local issues discussed on the TX List, but
I'm not sure. Maybe the poll would entice non-posters to voice an
opinion. Thanks for listening and for being a great family!

Silver Eagle

List, which is one of the few lists I have time to look at, and
catch up with what everyone is doing. I look at Texas as a whole,
not as regional groups. As many of you may have gathered, I'm not
as social as my brother, but when I do decide to post to a list
(National, Rocky Mountain, Texas, etc), it doen't bother me how big
the list is. Thus I don't believe we will lead more people to post
messages by creating smaller regional lists. I quit posting to the
National list only because I did not agree with many of the comments
that were being posted on that list. That has never been an issue on
the Texas list, at least for me. I think email groups would be a
better way to handle regional issues which concern event planning
and such. Anyway, that my two cents on the subject. Thanks to
all!!

Baby Bear
