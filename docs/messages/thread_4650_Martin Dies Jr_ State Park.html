Martin Dies Jr. State Park

From: Boots Tex (redbarn@eastex.net) | Date: 2010-10-21 19:35:04 UTC
Lone Star Quilter and I decided to go for a drive yesterday afternoon to take advantage of the beautiful weather and we ended up in Jasper County and Martin Dies Jr. State Park. The last time we were there was just after Hurricane Rita had dealt the park a major blow. Many huge trees were snapped off or ripped out of the ground and scattered everywhere and most of the trails were closed. The transformation is remarkable. The park has been restored beautifully. We hardly recognized some of the places that we had seen before, and the trails are numerous and beautiful. We were only prepared to look for one letterbox and we found it, although it was damaged. I took the opportunity to talk to the Rangers about letterboxing and, as usual, they were very familiar with geocaching but had never heard of letterboxing. However, after describing letterboxing to them and showing them a letterbox that I just happened to have with me, they became enthusiastic about it. They were surprised that letterboxes had ever existed in the park but said that they would like to have more placed there. They said that the current climate at TPWD was that, anything that attracted visitors was good and they felt that letterboxing should be welcome in State Parks. They gave me a contact name in Austin who is in charge of family activities, including geocaching, so I sent him an email, although I haven't yet heard from him. Of course, you would still have to go through the permitting process, but I encourage you to try it. I don't know if any of the old boxes are still there, but we are going to place a couple of boxes there in the next couple of weeks and, when we do, we'll look for all the old ones that might have survived the storm. I asked about the old boxes and was told that they would, in this park at least, consider them "grandfathered" and wouldn't require a permit since it wasn't necessary when they were placed. So this park will be open to new boxes if you ask at the desk for a permit to place a letterbox.

Boots Tex

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2010-10-22 08:41:00 UTC-05:00
Great news about that park - my family has only visited it once in our pre-letterboxing days, but we have a great memory of it and I have always wanted to go back.

We stopped there on a late afternoon on our way home from the Beaumont area to stretch and check out the park. SQZ was 11-ish and Dvir was a baby (so this has been well over 10 years ago). We decided to take a quick walk around what I believe must have been the Island Trail. While we were out there on the trail, we found ourselves in the middle of an amazing display of fireflies - they were everywhere around us! We were all spellbound and must have stood there watching them for about 10 minutes. There were so many of them that they almost lit up the area around us. Finally, they drifted on by and we continued down the trail. As often happens in the woods, the daylight was shorter than we had expected when we got out of our car. We found ourselves on that trail in the dark pushing a stroller with a baby and a walking child and no flashlight - on an island with one trail in and out. It got VERY dark out there and a couple of times we walked into the edges of the water. We missed the trail off the island and made an extra pass around. Thankfully most especially for Fearless Fox, who can make just about anything fun, and we laughed our way back to the car finally.
