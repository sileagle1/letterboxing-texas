Newbie . . . and a confession

From: Jane Almquist (almquist_jane@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-01-14 15:58:45 UTC
Hi all,

I've been a nominal member of this list for several months but have
never posted, just because I can't access yahoogroups at work, and I
have teenagers who hog my pc at home.

Anyway, I thought it might be time to just say hi, virtually, since
I did meet several of you at the Great Book Adventure in October.

And . . . I have a confession. Reading over posts from the last
couple of months, I learn that I have probably committed a cardinal
sin or two. Late last month, my DH and I found a letterbox that was
scattered all over the place so we found all the pieces, took it
home, cleaned the stamp, provided a new box and logbook, and
replaced it, including the old logbook in case the planter wanted
it. We notified the planter that we were doing this. Now I find that
may have been the WRONG thing to do.

Yesterday we did something even worse. We found a microbox that had
apparently been waterlogged for a long time, to the point that we
couldn't even get the stamp out of the container because it was
crumbling. So we took it with us and immediately emailed the placer
to offer to mail it back to them in case they would like to dry out
the logbook and keep it if they wanted to. We can't repair it and
it's over an hour from home so we can't replace it any time soon. I
guess this was the WRONG thing to do again but I guess we just did
what we'd like someone to do for one of our boxes.

This isn't to dredge up old flame wars, it's just to say that if we
breached protocol we're sorry--we enjoy letterboxing but
aren't "pros" at it.

On a brighter note, just wanted to mention that we've been boxing
for about seven months and have thoroughly enjoyed seeing new places
and things--kind of like why we enjoy volksmarching, except easier
to do spontaneously and closer to home. THANKS to Thimble Cat, for
offering an adult Girl Scout training at Crazy Quilt last year, and
getting us (okay, me, but DH is willing to be dragged along, with or
without the dogs)interested in letterboxing.

If you have time, our boxing blog is at
http://daisyandonslow.blogspot.com.

Best to all in the new year,
"Daisy"
(Jane)
