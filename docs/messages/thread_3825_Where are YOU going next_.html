From: Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter (janet@redbarnretreat.com) | Date: 2008-10-30 04:48:12 UTC
Last week we took a trip to the "Cross Timbers" area of Texas,
roughly the area between Abilene and Waco. The price of gasoline had
already gone down a little, so that helped. We found a few
letterboxes, but there weren't many we hadn't already found, so we
placed a box in each of the following counties: Erath, Comanche,
Eastland and Brown, none of which previously had letterboxes. We are
planning another trip after Thanksgiving, and this time we are taking
our travel trailer and plan to spend a couple of weeks visiting a few
state parks. We haven't decided which ones yet, because it may
depend on the weather. We would like to go into the panhandle
(Copper Breaks, Caprock Canyons, Palo Duro Canyon), but the weather
can be unpredictable in December. When we lived in Midland, we went
to the Davis Mountains occasionally, but haven't been there in more
than 20 years, so that may be a possibility. Both areas are
very "letterbox challenged", so we would probably concentrate on
placing more than finding. I guess I should start carving now.

Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter

From: Silver Eagle (sileagle@alltel.net) | Date: 2008-10-30 14:09:31 UTC
I'm planning on taking the kids camping and letterboxing in south-
central TX the week of Thanksgiving. I hope to repopulate Live Oak
County and place a few more along the way. I'm also still hoping to
go with Baby Bear on our long overdue annual TX LB trip in a couple
of months.

BTW Boots, both the panhandle and west Tx are great locations, but I
am partial to Davis Mts. There is a great hike from Davis Mts SP to
Fort Davis NHS if you don't have to hike back up (leave second car at
fort), and the star party at McDonald Observatory is great. Plus
there are several boxes in the area. Beware, if you get that close
to El Paso, Mike will draw you in! Of course, then you could go to
Hueco Tanks SP, Guadalupe NP and even Calsbad Caverns NP. Or there
is always Big Bend NP to the south. I'm envious!

Silver Eagle
