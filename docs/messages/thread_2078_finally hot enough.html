From: Maribeth Jagger (cjagger405@earthlink.net) | Date: 2006-08-15 15:14:20 UTC-05:00

It's finally gotten hot enough for Viewfinder and me to go letterboxing this week. We went to Cedar Hill State Park and found Silver Eagle's Texas Star Microbox and Baby Bear's Campground Bandit - both alive and well. (Great park and fun boxes!) I remember last time we went searching for their boxes at Lake Bardwell and ran into those letterboxing coyotes. Well... once again today, just as we were leaving the park, lo and behold there was a huge coyote standing off to the side of the road! All I can say is.... we did "recover well"!
 
Also, we planted a couple of new boxes today and hope to announce them tonight! Happy hunting!
 
Puddle Splasher

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-08-15 17:09:45 UTC-05:00
Finally hot enough?  Y'all are just flat NUTS!  I did a little figuring myself today, and I came to the conclusion that we are about halfway through the unbearable part of the year.  For me it starts about the first of July and ends about the end of September, give or take a little.  My little calculation made it all seem more bearable today, especially as I HAD to venture out at Noon to pick up my box BootsTex and Lone Star Quilter recovered yesterday.  Finally hot enough....hmph.
 
As to Cedar Hill State Park - I wasn't expecting much since that park is so close to Dallas - boy, was I pleasantly surprised!  That's a great park with nice views at every turn.  Some nice little trails - SJZ and I contemplated night boxing in that park and we probably could've.
