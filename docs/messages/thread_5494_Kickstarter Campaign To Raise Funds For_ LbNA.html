I just heard about this campaign started by Choi (webmaster of LBNA) to raise funds to move LBNA to a more reliable server.  As many of you know, LBNA has been down a lot lately and this would fix that problem, and provide funds for future support.  As extra incentive, he is offering rewards for different levels of giving, including a chance to be part of an LBNA Advisory Board that will discuss future developments (which I signed up for).  So if you have ideas that would make LBNA better (but not too much like AQ), this would be your chance to express them.  If you can't give that much ($75) just give what you can and I will capture your ideas to bring up at the meetings for you.  I'll probably set up a file on this group that everyone can add to.

I know many of you prefer AQ and might not care about LBNA, but I think it is worth saving if for no other reason than it was the first letterboxing website and we should preserve our history (not to mention all the old clues).  Also important to me, it is still a non-profit website run by non-paid people who care more about letterboxing than making money off of it.  Thanks for your support!
