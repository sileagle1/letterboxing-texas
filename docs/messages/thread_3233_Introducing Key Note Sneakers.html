Introducing Key Note Sneakers

From: KeyNote Sneakers (keynotesneakers@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-12-23 20:54:50 UTC-08:00
Per the moderator:

<quote>I do ask, though, that you post an introduction of yourself so the other members know that you are out there, even if you just intend on lurking (and that's okay!).  Just let us know a little something about yourself, how you got involved in letterboxing, and what your interest is in Texas letterboxing, what your favorite color is - whatever you think is important!<\quote>

We are the Key Note Sneakers of the Houston Area.  We learned about LB by doing some research that was prompted by a relative sending an email suggesting geocaching.  After reading about GC, we thought that
   1) the high tech element conflicted with the natural environment we hoped to spend more time in
   2) we didn't want to buy a GPS for a new hobby not knowing how long we'd be involved with it
   3) technology changes so fast that a GPS purchased today is obsolete in 6 months (we think)
   4) there is nothing worse than being out in the woods trying to do something with high tech gear and running into a dead battery or other tech glitch which ruins your outing (dewberry can vouch for this one (see below))

So, after learning more about GC on Wikipedia and such, we stumbled upon LB which seemed to be a better match for us.  We read about it and within a couple days carved a stamp, went out for our first box and never looked back.

That was over 50 finds ago already (since Oct. 24th) which we are excited about (not quite 2 months).

Our first encounter with other 'boxers on the trail was pretty funny (we thought) if you're interested, you can read about it on AQ here:

http://www.atlasquest.com/boards/message.html?gMsgId=156554;gAuthorId=15819

Since then, we bumped in to dewberry and were experiencing a technical glitch (you know, the kind we were trying to avoid by not getting a GPS?)

We had just started downloading clues to our PDA so that we could stop creating such a mess of paper and due to a skinware error (read that to mean "idiot user problem") we got out to the park without any clues on the PDA.  The good news was that dewberry's party was kind enough to pass along the pages they had printed and were no longer using.  Also, it only takes one experience like that to make sure that doesn't happen again.

So, we'll give you all a chance to breath now and give this thing a rest.

By for now,

Key Note Sneakers
(a conglomeration of many, many little people who box together mostly as a family unit but rarely use their individual trail names)
