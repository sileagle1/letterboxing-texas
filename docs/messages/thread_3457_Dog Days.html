Dog Days

From: the carrascos (a.carrasco@earthlink.net) | Date: 2008-03-31 08:47:53 UTC-05:00
Thank you so much to Boots Tex, LSQ, and dewberry for a GREAT time yesterday!  Daddy of Five said he thought it was the best yet (though I suspect this is because I didn't have to arrive early to help set up leaving him on kid duty.)  Double Lake is such a pretty spot, we are planning a fall camping trip there, we just didn't have enough time to really get out on the trails.
 
On the way home yesterday I was thinking how lucky I am to have stumbled on letterboxing.  It has given me the chance to meet so many great people whom I otherwise would have never had the chance to know.  It is amazing how many creative people are out there lurking behind funny names.
 
As I sit here with my coffee I am dreaming of a Red Barn cinnamon roll, I know what Boots Tex will have to bring to all future gatherings!  Thanks again for such a great day out!  MO5

Dog Days

From: Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter (janet@redbarnretreat.com) | Date: 2008-04-01 00:52:35 UTC
Thanks to everyone who came to the Dog Days at Double Lake event
yesterday. A big thanks to dewberry for all the hard work and the
great dog boxes she gathered from all over for our letterboxing
pleasure, and the fun boxes she carved and made available at the
pavilion. If you didn't get those out on the Lone Star Trail, you
missed out. Thanks also to those of you who came by the Red Barn to
partake in Cinnamon Rolls and coffee. (Does this sound like an
acceptance speech?). Anyway, everyone seemed to have a great time
and that's the whole purpose, isn't it? Today, after our family left
to make their way back home, we had a relaxing time at Double Lake,
stamping into the 19 boxes we didn't already have. It was a
beautiful day and we saw not a single other person. We did see a few
deer and spent about five minutes watching a lovely Pileated
Woodpecker working for his dinner on a dead log about 10 feet away
from us. We need to get an accurate count, but offhand, we figure
there are about 32 letterboxes in the park and on the Lone Star Trail
within 1/2 mile of the park. There are 5 more if you don't mind
walking a little farther down the trail, so it's a great letterboxing
destination and a beautiful place. Y'all come back and see us
sometime.

Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter and The Texians
