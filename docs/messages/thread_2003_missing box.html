From: janetsredbarn (janetsredbarn@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-07-21 01:18:10 UTC
Texas Gal, we attempted to find your Heritage Park box last Thursday.
It was our last box of the day after having been to Georgetown and to
Dana Peak, so it was about dusk. This park was pretty busy, even at
that hour, with the ball fields close by and walkers on the path
nearby. We found the tree with the "scar", which was a good-size hole
in the tree and assumed that's where the box was located. I felt
inside the hole, which was full of that corky stuff that usually
inhabits holes in trees, and I couldn't find anything. On the left
side, the hole started getting pretty deep, so, being dark in there,
and not knowing how deep the hole was, I gave up on the box. The
height of the hole (about 4 to 4 1/2 feet off the ground?) seemed to
be just the right height for a kid to look in, just because he's a
kid, and maybe one of them found the box. You might want to find a
place a little more out of the way (Martin Springs Nature Center could
use a few more). From the observation deck, the hole in the tree is
pretty prominent. I know this is frustrating as we have lost three
boxes in the last month, and I thought they were pretty well hidden.
We have found almost 300 boxes, so we are relatively "seasoned"
boxers, I would say. Who knows what happens to these boxes? You
can't let it bother you, it doesn't happen often, just plant two more
to replace that one.

Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter
