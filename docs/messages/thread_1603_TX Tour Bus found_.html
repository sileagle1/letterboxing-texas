From: p_quest2005 (p_quest2005@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-01-17 22:56:04 UTC
to the victorious (one of the) four fitzs:
i just don't know how you or viewfinder or puddle splasher had the
courage to go on that trail on foot. those mountain bikers are
serious about their sport and fly along those narrow, narrow
trails. then there was me, on my borrowed bike with leaky, non-
treaded tires and with nothing but the front brakes being
operational, trying to keep up the pace set by those fanatics. i,
however, was not in full body spandex attire. no, i was sporting
the ever in-style blue jeans and t-shirt (and my brand new running
shoes for good luck).
in any case, the trail can change instantly from quiet nature spot
along a nice lake full of sail boats to the loud rush of multiple
bikers bearing down on you quicker than makes you comfortable. and
then they have the gall to yell at you. now, that being my first
time, i had no idea there was appropriate lingo to be used to inform
other trail users to beware of, say, someone on a bike about to run
you over. i do know that i--with my cat-like reaction time--was
able to respond quickly to my first 'near road kill' experience with
an apt expression that let them know i was a force to be reckoned
with, one that let the oncomer know i was there and that i wasn't
going to budge. i'm not sure if i remember word for word, but it
approximated to:
him: "BIKE UP!! ONE BACK!!"
me: "OH GOD, PLEASE DON'T HURT ME!"
i think it might have intimidated him...well, i mean, he gave me a
weird look as he went by.
in the end, viewfinder has given me perspective: i can happily wait
for the bus to reappear with even more passengers on it. maybe,
when i eventually get it, i'll put it three hours out of town to see
who else has the fever.

p ?uest

P Quest - love your story!  My first near-death experience with an unexpected biker from nowhere on that trail was in one of those places where the trail deeps suddenly and steeply and you can't see them coming over the hill until they're on you - the biker yelled his warning and I jumped (off the trail thankfully) and in my surprise yelled something referring to the Deity, which probably surprised the biker even more.  He probably wasn't used to being called something like Lord, only not exactly that.  But lots of people DO walk that trail, with or without dogs, kids, even carrying picnics sometimes, but you have to watch out for the bikes.  Weekends are busiest, though, but during the week there's often almost no one out there.  Nice and quiet, can hear the waves and the birds.
