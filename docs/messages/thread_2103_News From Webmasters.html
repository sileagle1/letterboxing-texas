News From Webmasters

From: Silver Eagle (sileagle@alltel.net) | Date: 2006-08-27 19:28:09 UTC
I just heard from Choi this morning about the requests I made
recently. First of all, the suggestion I made about how to handle
orphan boxes was not acceptable to the webmasters, mainly because of
the difficulty in establishing whether or not a person no longer
participates. I agree that this is a problem, along with others that
Lucy brought up, so this solution will not be implemented. I think
the best way to handle it for now is to look at the status field of a
box and see if it says "attempted" instead of "found" by the last
seeker. Of course, this assumes everyone updates this field,
especially when a box is not found. It does not mean the box is
necessarily missing, but at least gives you a warning, especially if
several letterboxers have posted "attempted".

Now for the exciting news. After over 2 years of waiting it looks
like the TX Regions will be changed soon! Choi sent me a sample map
to look at for errors and it looked good. For those that may not
know, a few years ago we decided that the current regions of TX
displayed on LBNA did not make sense geographically (Brownsville is
in the same region as Beaumont) and reached a consensus on what they
should look like. You can see what we came up with in the Files
section (txregions.jpg). I then rewrote the spreadsheet used by LBNA
to put counties in the new regions and sent it and the graphic file
to Wes, the webmaster at that time. Evidently, changing the graphic
was more work than he had time for so we waited. When Choi became
Webmaster I sent him the same information, then waited. I did not
pressure him too much since it was not high priority compared to
other things he was working on, but I did occasionally nag him.
Maybe this squeaky wheel is finally getting his oil. Of course,
boxes will only show up in correct regions if everyone puts the
correct county on their clues. I have seen some that are not correct
and everyone should take this time to review their boxes and correct
any mistakes. When the new regions take effect, it is still possible
some boxes won't be in the correct region since I might have mistyped
a county in the spreadsheet. I tried to be careful but you never
know. Hopefully everything will work correctly and we will have a
more usable map for our clues.

Silver Eagle
