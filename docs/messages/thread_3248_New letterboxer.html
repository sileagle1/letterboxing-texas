RE: [letterboxingtexas] New letterboxer

From: Maribeth Jagger (cjagger405@earthlink.net) | Date: 2008-01-05 00:51:28 UTC-06:00
Welcome, allyall! (Is that like Ally All or All Ya'll?)
 
My first three logbooks have been journals with unlined pages from Barnes and Noble, but many boxers use the spiral bound sketchbooks like you find at Michaels or Hobby Lobby. I really like those because they lay flat when they're open, and the smooth paper seems to give a sharp image to the stamp, so my next logbook is going to be a nice sketchbook.
 
By the way, there is a local website for letterboxers who are interested in what's happening in the letterboxing community in the DFW area. You can check it out at: http://groups.yahoo.com/group/TA_DA/ The purpose of that group is to discuss anything that pertains to letterboxing in the north central area of Texas, like local gatherings, information on local boxes and a whole bunch of "whatever". Hopefully, you'll enjoy the discussions on the Texas list as well as the DFW list (TaDa - for Tarrant/Dallas) and will jump right in.
I'm in McKinney, so it's always good to have a new boxer in the Dallas area! Enjoy!
Puddle Splasher

From: Carolyn Nelson (cancan1959@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-01-05 08:19:07 UTC-06:00

Welcome Allyall and family!

My logbook with unlined pages came from Borders.  It is 4x6 and has a decorative plastic front and back ($4.95).  I like the size for hiking. I started using it last Oct when I attended the Houston event and it is about half full.

Recently I (finally) started journaling and including little pictures after running into Robots with Dreams on the trail and seeing their gorgeous books (I think they got theirs at Hobby Lobby and they were slightly larger then mine). Borders also had a variety of other sizes (they are mixed both lined and unlined, colored paper and plain) and I have seen other boxers use hardcover artist sketch books which I purchased for less than $10 at Borders for each one of my kids "doodle diary."

I would imagine Barnes & Noble might also have a selection.

If you can join us March 1st in Bastrop for our annual letterboxing event that would be awesome.

From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-01-05 16:44:57 UTC
The sketch books are what my kids (and I) use. We get ours at Texas
Art Supply (like I get all my crafty stuff). I'm not sure what the
equivalent in the DFW area would be, but if you get stuck, you can
order online from them.

I need a new logbook. I like the way that the spiral bound ones open
up flat, but I don't like the way the wire can get caught on stuff or
bent. I'll probably go with another wire-bound one, because I have
found that on the trail, I much prefer being able to open the book all
the way while stamping.

The small sketch pads often make good logbooks for planting. I like
to use the larger ones for on the trail, because I like to have a
day's letterboxing on the same page. Occasionally, especially at
gatherings, you run across a stamp too large for a 4x6 piece of paper.

dewberry
