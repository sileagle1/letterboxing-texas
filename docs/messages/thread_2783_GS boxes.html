Hi fellow GS letterboxers. I'm busy planting away at GS camps too.
My girls love it and would love to have more boxes too find. In fact,
at our bridging ceremony last week, many of them announced that
letterboxing was their favorite activity from the past year. It even
beat horseback riding and learning to sew!

If you are in the San Jacinto GS council, I would love for you to
post your boxes to the files section of the new group I started right
before the training in March.
http://groups.yahoo.com/group/letterboxingGSSJC/
The council still hasn't made any progress toward putting the boxes
on their website and I don't want to post them on LBNA since they are
not available to anyone but scouts. I thought this might be the
best option (for the time being anyway). And the message board is
the easiest way to keep up with box status - like we do here.
Spread the word to your fellow GS letterboxers too.

ThimbleCat

doing it in my free time (haha) just ain't happening these days)

Hey ThimbleCat!

 

I thought I might throw out a little food for thought here.

 

If you go ahead and list your scout camp boxes, the girls who like to log can still log their finds.  You don't have to include the clues just write a note to the effect that these boxes are on private property and are for the use of girl scouts only and encourage them to write you for further information.   If they choose to write you, you can either tell them how to get to the yahoo group where the clues are or you can thank them for their interest but again emphasize that they are intended for GS use.

 

The other advantage to doing that is that you have a better chance of reaching GS folks who may not have heard of your yahoo group or of your planting endeavor.  As I'm sure you are well aware, it seems there is always a  stray leader or two who gets sidetracked and finally rejoins the tour after the vital information has been given.

 

Just a thought or two, but with inflation these days

BarefootLucy
