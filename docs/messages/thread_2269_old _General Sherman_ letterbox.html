old "General Sherman" letterbox

From: Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter (janet@redbarnretreat.com) | Date: 2006-10-24 20:51:17 UTC
I got a very interesting email today from some new letterboxers who
were in Rusk State Park to find their first letterboxes. They had
the clues to my box "General Sherman" and were on the way to find it
when they saw a plastic box laying beside the railroad track. It
was "General Sherman". They picked it up and stamped it and went to
put it back where the clues said it should be, and there was already
a box there. They had found the old "missing" box and the
replacement box was still in place right where it should have been.
They stamped into the new box as well, and emailed me to offer to
mail the old box to me. I have a feeling this family will make
great letterboxers. When I answered their email to thank them, this
is the note I got back:

"This was actually our first experience trying to find any
letterboxes. We were camping at Rusk state park and 2 of my kids and
myself tried to find two different boxes near the depot the night
before and had no luck. My husband and I were up early the next
morning and I decided to look for your box before the kids got up as
I didn't want them to be disappointed again. Since we were already
in the camp ground I figured we could pick up the trail somewhere in
the middle of your clues (turn left onto the tracks at the crossing)
so we were walking along the tracks and passed the first crossing, I
was busy looking to the left and my husband (who had no clue what we
were looking for and was seeming to be having a hard time grasping
the concept of letterboxing) said "what is that over there." The box
was just lying beside the track on the right not too far before the
second crossing. I was going to dismiss his find as that could not
possibly be the box we were looking for but he went and picked it
up. Sure enough it was a letterbox. We decided to try to put it
where it belonged and went to where the nature trail enters the
campground and followed the directions. When my husband went to put
it under the rail he couldn't and thus we found the new box. It was
exciting to actually find a box! When the kids got up we followed
the directions and let them find the box. We told them our story and
they decided we should stamp both books so we did. I am happy to
mail your box to you on me as a thank you for the time and effort
you take to make the world a fun place!"

I thought that was pretty neat.
