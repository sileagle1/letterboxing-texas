From: adamstrails (adamstrails@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-10-20 13:59:43 UTC
I got an email yesterday from a homeschooler mom. She had went on
Letterboxing.org and sent it in reguards to my letterbox in Mabank
called The Blue Daisy. She said that she and some other homeschooler
Moms were at the park and one of the kids found my box. She told me how
interesting it was and then said that she told (told) the child to go
put it back. I checked on it today and it is no where in the area. The
child must have decided to take it home. I guess it was just too cute
to resist the temptation. So until I replace it, don't look for it. I
will email the mom back and tell her the box is missing. I hope she is
cooperative. Here is a copy of her email to me.

Message:
hi we are the lake area christian homeshoolers in Mabak tx. Our kids
were playing in the park and found your box. The Blue Daisy.... We
all
didn't know what it was, until one of our mothers said it was a
letterbox. The kids didn't know what to do with ti, so we had them
just put
it back where they found it. I think this is the neatest idea!!!!

From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-10-20 18:05:19 UTC
Another possibility is that if it's in an area where one group of kids
found it while playing, someone else found it too. It's sometimes
hard to tell how much use an area of a park gets. Boxes hidden in
good climbing trees or places where kids play hide and seek could
easily be discovered.

Sometimes, what seems like a cool hiding place is actually in the
middle of a lot of traffic.

dewberry

When you said "she told the child to go put it back", that's all I had to read to know it was a gonner.Most likely the child failed to "put it back" properly, didn't cover it well, and it was found by a muggle. I never let my kids replace boxes in the beginning. Later on I taught them the fine art of re-hiding. Now I let them put one retrieve and replace occasionally, but I always double check. My son hides them WAY better than he found them. Sometimes I have to remove some of the "camouflage."
