Nooooooo, oh no! We WEREN'T the first! Not at all!

I was thrilled to see that others have been
letterboxing through our area! YIPPEE!!!

I guess I should've written down who had been there,
but I didn't. I did take down the only email address
listed, for Mama Huey, and I'm going to email her to
tell her about this list and about the gathering. The
chances are fairly good that she's from Texas.

It actually looked like there had been three groups
through, the most recent in November. It was one very
sweet little girl's 8th box to be found, the first for
one couple. It was hidden very well - we walked right
up to it, and if I hadn't seen several of yours hidden
by now, I would've sworn there was no way there was a
box where your clues led us. The couple, who was the
last to visit, did a very nice job of rehiding.

As it turned out, what was supposed to be a sunny day
in the 60's turned out to be a cloudy day in the 40's.
Our scouts didn't turn out, so we skipped Jim Hogg
and went on to Texas State RR. We were just going to
roam a bit (although it isn't a real big park, so we
weren't going to roam much!), find your box and leave.
WELL, it was Kid's Fish For Trout day there at the
park, so it was overrun with people. We had to be
supersneaks to get to the box! After we saw all the
stamps in it, I really wanted to run on over to Hogg
and see if there were more stamps there too, but we
ran out of time. Since the RR is somewhat of a
tourist attraction (as much as we have in Cherokee
County - LOL), I wonder if it has more stamps because
of that, or if there are legitimately more people
making the rounds. I really wish we could've gotten
over to Hogg to get an idea of the answer. It isn't
that far from me, and I have to run down there soon
and talk to the ranger about a conservation project
for our scouts, so I'll check it when I go and let you
know.

Lucy
