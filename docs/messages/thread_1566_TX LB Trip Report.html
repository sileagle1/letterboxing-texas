TX LB Trip Report

From: Silver Eagle (sileagle@alltel.net) | Date: 2006-01-02 02:49:22 UTC
Baby Bear and I just got back late yesterday from another
whirlwind letterboxing trip lasting 5 days and 4 nights.
This has become an annual winter affair, starting in early
2003 with our wide sweep through East, North and Central TX
planting our "seeds", then in 2004 with our Dallas trip.
For 2005, we wanted to go Northeast for all the new boxes
planted there in the last year, but decided to do it before
the Event next March. That left Dallas again since so many
new boxes were planted since our last visit, but finding
the time to go was tough. We pushed it back until after
Christmas and lucked out with the weather; it was perfect!

Our plan was to leave early Tuesday, 12/27, and when I say
early, I mean EARLY. Baby Bear wanted me at his house by
5:00 am, so I had to get up at 4:00 am. Never mind that it
does not get light until 7:00 am, he just said "That is what
flashlights are for". So our first few boxes in Brenham area
were in the dark, which was nothing new to us, but still a
challenge. We had a new wrinkle this time though, since the
first box was in a cemetery. I like boxing in cemeteries in
the daytime, but at night it is just a little too creepy. I
have to admit it added spice to the hunt, though.

We worked our way northwest through Somerville, Waco, Mother
Neff SP and eventually Cleburne SP where we camped for the
night. It was dark when we pulled in so had to find the 2
boxes there in the dark. We also placed 2 in the dark, which
is even harder in a way. One of the boxes (Hot Chicks) was
near the trailhead, so we figured we would just find it and
bring it back to the car to stamp. When we went to its location
we found a ziplock with log and stamp just laying out in the
open, so assumed the box was missing. Once we got back to the
car we realized it was a Hitchhiker and not the letterbox at all.
So back we went and sure enough, the box was nearby safe and
sound. We think the last letterboxer must have placed the HH
with the box but not in it, since there was no room, and then
an animal scattered both. We ended up taking the HH and placing
it somewhere else on our travels, so it worked out well.

The next day we broke camp and were on the road before 6:00 am.
Our first box was again in the dark and the weather was decidedly
colder, so our headlamps could barely pierce the fog coming
from our breaths. We wondered, not for the first or last time,
what we must look like to anyone crazy enough to be out watching
us. Boxing in the dark can be fun since you get to see some
nocturnal creatures as you hike, but I would not do it alone
(especially in a cemetery). We spent most of the day in the
Fort Worth area and ended up camping at Cedar Hill SP, the same
park we camped at last year. It would be our home for the
next 3 nights as we combed the Dallas area for more boxes.
Since we were always up before light and home after dark, I
still have never seen the park in the daytime, but it seems
like a nice place. As Barefoot Lucy had told us, the South
part of the park was closed, but even so the park is still huge.
We did check out the Group Picnic Pavilion and it looks nice
(at least in the dark), so that might be a choice when the
Event comes North.

I have to say that we were very impressed with the parks in
the towns around Dallas. It seems every creek had a Greenbelt
park along it with a wide hike/bike path extending for miles.
Most of these places only have 1 box, so many opportunities
await for those wishing to plant when they visit. I just wish
we could have spent more time exploring them. I struggle with
the choice between going fast to visit as many boxes as possible
vs. going slow to enjoy the park more, and we did try to go
a little slower than previous trips and "smell the roses" more.
But since neither of us gets to box much with our nonboxing
families, we decided to go to as many places as we could and
maybe go back with family some day to enjoy the parks we
really liked. Even going fast we still enjoyed hiking and
talking together as we let the Metroplex Letterboxers show
us their home through their boxes. They did a great job!

The last day found us boxing in Waxahachie at 6:00 am in
29 degree weather. Now we know how northern letterboxers
feel, though they would probably laugh at our thin skin. If
I needed any reminders of why I live in the South, this was
enough. Give me heat any day! We eventually made it to
the sight of the JEN gathering and wished again that we could
have attended. Even though it was too late for the Event, we
both placed boxes in the park for future visitors. We then
worked our way south to Mexia area and noticed Barefoot Lucy
had stamped in just 2 days before. I felt almost the same way
she must have felt last year when she found I had stamped into
her box the same day. We finally made it home (you guessed it,
after dark) ready to relax, only to be told we had New Year's
Eve parties to attend and we darn well better stay awake. I
managed and I assume Baby Bear did, too, but today was a very
lazy day.

We ended up finding almost 100 boxes, with about 15 not found,
and I placed 4 while Baby Bear placed 6 new boxes. There are
too many for me to send everyone an email, but I will try to
notify those that require maintenance or were not found. We
really enjoyed the places your boxes took us to, and we thank
you for the opportunity to be guests in your home.

Silver Eagle

P.S. We both reached personal milestones during the trip,
both occurring at the same box (Baby Bear's new Northern
Exposure letterbox). It was my F1000 and Baby Bear's
P200.
