From: Silver Eagle (sileagle@alltel.net) | Date: 2007-07-22 14:09:03 UTC
In my opinion there is no need for an apology, and I want to publicly
thank BL for handling this situation the best way I know of. I was
gone most of the day, so BL had to deal with this on her own, and I
think she did fine.

Just to let ya'll know, being the owner/moderator for a List can
sometimes be a trying experience. You have to occasionally make
judgment calls about when a person is "crossing the line" and then
decide how to handle it. In this case, BL was sent many emails from
some of ya'll complaining about Kirbert and his style of discourse.
(BTW, it would be better if you sent complaints to the Owners group
that she posted instead of just to her) He was put on Moderation for
a while so we could monitor his posts, but then it got worse
yesterday. She sent him a private email that was very well written
and tactful explaining why some of his posts were rubbing people the
wrong way and gave suggestions for how to make them better. She did
not tell him to stop posting or to no longer disagree with people,
she just suggested doing it in a more positive manner. When this did
not work and he kept posting the same way, she publicly reprimanded
him so all of you could see that his posts were not acceptable. If I
had been here I would have been the one to post the public message.
The next step would have probably been a final private warning to
improve his posts or be kicked off our List, but he unsubscribed on
his own. Though this may make many people happy or relieved, it
makes me sad. I must admit it makes things easier from a Moderator
point of view, but it also means I failed to keep a member on this
List who quite often contributed good discussion.

Just to let ya'll know, I view this List as a family, but just
because BL and I are the Moderator/Owners, I don't feel like we are
your parents. I don't want you to feel like you always have to agree
with everything people say or to feel scared to express a contrary
opinion. I just want you to do it in a manner that would be
acceptable to you from another person. Also, if you don't like the
tone of someone's post, try writing them privately first before
responding publicly or complaining to BL and me. Explain how you
interpreted their message and try to resolve it. If that fails, then
we will deal with it. Overall, I prefer to be less restrictive on
what people post since I don't want to suppress free speech, but if
many people are upset then I will act.

Now, hopefully we can get back to good discussions. I think many of
our recent ones have been interesting and timely. Thanks for being
such a great group!

Silver Eagle
