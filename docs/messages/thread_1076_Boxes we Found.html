Boxes we Found

From: Anita (atina3819@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-07-17 17:47:27 UTC
Yesterday we spent the day in Galveston doing the dino - thing at
Moody Gardens. I have to admit, I could not wait to finish Moody
Gardens so we can find all our stamps we had planned. LOL Then we
found several stamps on the island:

Kemp's Ridley
Sea Aggies - thanks for the warning of mosquito's, even though the
rain did not help matters
Celtic Cross - that cementary is really amazing, it started to rain
so we could not look around as much as we wanted to -- loved the
Historical markers, very interested, we found another one who fought
in the war of 1812. Willis mausoleum was unbelievable
craftsmanship!!!!!
Sylvester - we actually found this one in the rain, and where this
box is at the trees were are umbrellas - but boy did the mosquito's
love us!!!!! I don't think any amount of bug spray would of stopped
that many!!! Nice place, I wish we could of spent more time. Then
after all the snake stories, I thought for sure a snake would slither
out in front of us.

Also found was Topaz Memorial Box - we found this on the way down at
10 AM, good thing because we came back by to find Bravery at a park
down the street - the dog racing was beginning to start - we would of
never been able to stamp it. I was amazed by your great stamp
carving - actually all the stamps we did yesterday were great. I
would be interested in what kind of tools you use to stamp - and do
you draw freehand or what.

Well because of all these neat letterboxing we had a very enjoyable
day with great family memories -- THANKS ANITA
