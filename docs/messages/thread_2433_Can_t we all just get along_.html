From: (blhjrh@aol.com) | Date: 2006-12-31 14:38:11 UTC-05:00
In the spirit of harmony and cooperation that defines the Texas Letterboxing group, I want to to aplogize if anything I said about not taking other people's letterboxes was offensive.  It certainly wasn't meant to be!  While I strongly believe that only the owner (that would be the carver, creator, placer) of the letterbox can decide to remove a letterbox, I'll admit that if I were boxing and saw a prairie fire about to swoop across the area, I'd grab that letterbox and take it with me as I ran.  Other than that (or a similarly drastic scenario), I'd rather try to perform whatever emergency rescue maintenance I could, and contact the placer.  I'd never arbitrarily take even a damaged box and then decide to turn it into a HH or cootie if the owners hadn't responded to me within a couple of weeks.  But that's just me.  We all have to use our own judgment to decide how best to respond to every situation (and we would all like to think everyone will use GOOD judgment if it's our own letterbox that is in question!).  I didn't intend to upset anyone or hurt anyone's feelings or abscribe malice to anyone's actions, which is why my message to Kim was sent privately and offline, rather than posted publicly on any list, since not everyone was aware of all the details of the issues anyway.  But since it became a public issue, I'm apologizing and wishing everyone a Happy and Prosperous New Year of Letterboxing Adventures!  -- Viewfinder
