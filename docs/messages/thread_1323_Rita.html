From: Cindy Harland (droopwv@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-09-26 18:57:50 UTC-07:00
Hi again,
Sending a quick message while I have dial up working. Just able to see tv tonight and not a word about what is happening in this part of Texas and W. La. from what I can see. Did hear more about New Orleans. We don't have any deaths that we know of (except for the generator thing) and water is not up to the roofs of houses, we are not as bad off as Katrina victims by ANY MEANS but things are not good here either. My family was well prepared and we are ok. But there is no electricity anywhere and no water either (we have water because we have a well) There are trees down everywhere and under all the trees are electric lines. It will be a long time before things are back to normal. The heat index was 110 today and people are scavaging for food too. There is no ice to keep it cold. So there are problems here for some folks. Another issue is that there is no work and for people who live from paycheck to paycheck their funds will be gone,I would think, by the end of the week from evacuating and paying for things while they are away so the financial impact on some will be huge. They will get back and not have money for food and rent and gas and they wont have paychecks waiting for them from some employers I expect.
As I said my family is doing great, today we took my daughter and son back to her apartment in Houston (yes, we evacuated her TO the storm LOL) so that they can have comforts and my former husband and I are cleaning up his yard and taking care of the dogs. I actually live in Nederland now ( I am staying at my former husbands house in Vidor)  and they are not letting people in but I did check on my townhouse the day after the storm and all was well. The hospital I work for had roof damage and I don't know when it will re-open. My former husband works for Exxon Mobil and is waiting for them to call him back. So we basically have no troubles that time wont fix. Thanks for all the concern and prayers. I will send another mail later that tells the story of the storm in more detail. I always wanted to be here for a hurricane so I guess I got my wish.
More later....
