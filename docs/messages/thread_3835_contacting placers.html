I have really been following this discussion (I'm a logbook lover, by the way) and listening to what all of you have to say.  I think Boots is right with this point, but I think there is something else to consider.  If you notify a placer about finding their box, and receive no reply, you may be less likely to notify the placer on your next find.  I will never forget when I found my first box, wrote Silver Eagle to tell him about the find, and received a very nice note back thanking me for letting him know about his box.  When I started placing boxes, I thought that was what you were "supposed" to do, so I followed his lead, and try to always write back when someone takes the time to let me know they have found (or tried to find) one of my boxes.  Seems like since there are so many new boxers starting these days we might all like to consider sending a simple note of thanks on a found box to encourage finders to notify placers.  Now back to unloading the dishwasher....MO5
