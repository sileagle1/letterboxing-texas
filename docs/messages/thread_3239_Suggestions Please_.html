Hi! I'm going to have to give the restaurant query a little thought, but there is a good BBQ place we went to recently in Coppell on Bass Pro Road just east of Grapevine Mills Mall. I'll have to get the name and address for you later today. Also, if you like Mexican, Uncle Julios on Hwy. 114 and Tate in Grapevine is excellent. You might check the internet to see if Sneaky Pete's is open at the marina at Lake Grapevine... good place for breakfast.
 
Last I heard, all my boxes were still in place on the northern side of Lake Grapevine (Flower Mound, Grapevine area). The Moose series is a pretty good hike, so take plenty of water: http://www.atlasquest.com/lboxes/showboxinfo.html?gBoxId=14115. I am planning to go up there next week to do maintenance on my boxes, but like I said, I've been told they are all still there after the flood receded. All four boxes in my Artist Series Two http://www.atlasquest.com/lboxes/showboxinfo.html?gBoxId=30263 was also found last week. It's a short walk (about a mile round trip on a cement sidewalk), but it's a pretty walk on the south side of the lake in Grapevine. I haven't checked on Rather Be Paddlin or Three Blind Moose (on the north side of the lake closer to the Moose Series), but both were found since the flood so I'm assuming they're still there too.
 
I'll get back to you on the restaurant suggestions. Oh, there is a Colter's BBQ over by Grapevine Mills too.

From: (blhjrh@aol.com) | Date: 2007-12-27 14:13:19 UTC-05:00
Good barbecue in Lewisville/Flower Mound, hmmm.... anything with Spring Creek on the sign is good, in my opinion - hard to beat their ribs.  And they're pretty much all over the area - one is in Grapevine on Hwy 114 in the middle of a restaurant row, just north of the airport.  Of course, if you get down to the FW Stockyards area, the options are even better - try Riscky's in the Stockyards (heck, try anything in the stockyards!  All good!).
    One of my boxes along the Lake Grapevine trail is gone with the flood:  The Lost Moose (now permanently a lost little moose!). However, White Sail is still in place, happily, as is Merlin the Mooseterious.  Both are accessible from Rockledge Park, about a mile hike from the trailhead through some pretty wooded areas above the lakeshore.  Also on this same trail is PuddleSplasher's Three Blind Meese.  It's a good little hike with nice lakeviews, too.
    Of course, there are lots and lots of others in the Grapevine, Keller, Southlake areas, most of which involve fairly short hikes in some natural areas.  Batty Girl has several in old historic country cemeteries mostly south of Denton and roughly along I35W (the branch that goes to FW, not I35E, the branch between Denton and Dallas).  And there are a number of interesting boxes in Denton, too.  And there's a really pretty Audubon Society nature preserve just southwest of Dallas near Cedar Hill. where both PuddleSplasher and I have some boxes - lots of good hiking trails through that area along a limestone fossil ridge above a lake.  Look up clues for PS's Wren box and my Ludwig's Letterbox and Swing! (two of my music box series) if you plan to go to that area.   The boxes along the Campion Trail in Irving are all gone, washed away in the flooded Trinity, but I think they've all been marked as unavailable.
    Whereever you go, you're sure to find lots of good, interesting places tucked away with good hiking possibilities, good scenic views, and good letterboxes!  Have a good time!
    Viewfinder
