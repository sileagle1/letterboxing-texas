Yesterday at Southshore Park (just me jabbering)

From: Connie Terry (connie@lavernia.net) | Date: 2008-02-24 20:55:41 UTC-06:00

I was just passing through Bastrop on Saturday and for reasons that don’t merit explaining, I decided to cancel the rest of my trip and spend the day in the park.  Of course, it wasn’t quite that simple.  While I was there I wanted to do the early bird thing and find some of the boxes already placed there. [[Flashback]]  “SunSnake, look in my letterboxing bag and see if there is a stack of about 10 pages stapled together.”  Shuffle, shuffle. “Yes, mom, there is”  [[End flashback]]  Of course, the only paper in the letterboxing bag is a single sheet of paper folded into quarters.  Off to the public library to look up clues, by a way of a trip to the Bastrop Fire Station for directions. (In case you have never done so, a fire department is the best place to go for directions.  They know the streets of their area, have maps to show you, and you don’t have to buy anything while you are there!).  At the library I found the clues I wanted found some clip art to make a box to leave and searched to see if Bastrop had a Hobby Lobby or Michaels so I could get some carving supplies.  Nope.  Off to Wal-Mart to get pink pearl erasers, an exacto knife, lock n lock boxes camo tape and lunch.   Over lunch of a deli sandwich and chips I looked at clues and found that although the boxes were placed, most the actual clues were withheld. Oh well.  I went on to carve a stamp with the supplies from Wal-Mart. Let me tell you that I gained a new respect for Barefoot Lucy and all the rest who carve with an exacto knife.  Carving with a gouge is child’s play compared to using the exacto knife.  Then the knife skidded over the top of the eraser and cut the %$#@! out of my thumb. Ouch!  Glad I had a first aid kit in my jeep.  Got that puppy cleaned up and covered up and continued carving….very carefully. Got a lock & lock covered and found a logbook of the appropriate size.  I packed up my gear and drove to the trail that starts closest to campsite #1.  As I was securing the jeep a lady comes off the trail.  I eye her suspiciously to see if she is one of us.  Hmm..she got a towel and wiped her fingers…that’s a good sign, but not enough for me to approach her.  I turn to walk towards the trail and I hear, “Ma’am! Ma’am! Are you a letterboxer?”  As it turns out, I’m glad I didn’t ask her “Which way is North?” because she is new to the game and wouldn’t have known the significance of the question.  She’s been boxing only about 2 weeks.  Grammy Pat, if you’re out there listening, this lady isn’t on this list.  Can you help her find us?  We exchanged stamps and she gave me her impressions of the hike and clues for a box that I didn’t have.  I found that box, and a second box but the third box was in an area with far too many people to retrieve a box unnoticed, so I passed on that one.  I also placed my box…somewhere.  I’ll have clues on Saturday.  I do wish I had more to put in the South Shore park, maybe something industrious will happen during the course of this week. 

 

If you’re still reading, good night and I’ll see you Friday night (if SunSnake doesn’t make the Jr.Hi track team) or on Saturday morning if she does.

 

Corazon

I’m so glad you asked!  This is a challenge/response to find out if someone else is a letterboxer without giving yourself away in the process. 

 

The first person asks, “Which way is north?”   If the second person knows the significance of the question he/she indicates so by pointing towards the sky.  If the second person gets out a compass or looks to the sun for direction they do not have the correct response.  (Of course, they may indeed be a letterboxer, just one who doesn’t know the challenge/response.)  

 

If you point up when someone asks you which way is north, you have indicated that you are a letterboxer to another letterboxer.  If the person asking which way is north is just looking for directions, he/she goes away thinking that you are directionally challenged and is none the wiser.

 

Hope this helps,

Corazon
