From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2005-07-22 00:17:53 UTC
Welcome.

Letterboxing.org is the mother ship, the main site where clues are
distributed. I also like and use often atlasquest.com. It doesn't
have as many clues as letterboxing.org, but it has some nice
features and is a great community of folks.

Clues can also be distributed in other ways. Sometimes people
maintain their own websites, as does Silver Eagle (although each
clue on his site is linked to through letterboxing.org). Sometimes
clues are available solely through word of mouth (WOM), or sometimes
clues are hidden inside another box (that's a cuckoo clue or a bonus
box). There are a few other letterboxing websites out there, too,
with very helpful information like Silent Doug's site,
letterboxing.info.

Another group that might be of interest are the new boxers group on
yahoo (http://groups.yahoo.com/group/newboxers) as they are very
friendly and always willing to answer questions. And I would be
remiss if I didn't mention the Texas Letterboxing Atlas maintained
by our own Birds of a Feather
(http://www.geocities.com/birdsofafeather_lbox/). This is hugely
helpful when planning an outing.

Glad you could join us. As far as trail names, I think the
anonymous boxer sounds great. . .

Dewberry of
gramatrick (in Houston)
