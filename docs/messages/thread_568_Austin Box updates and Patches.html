Austin Box updates and Patches

From: monkeytoes108 (cruschhaupt@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-01-16 23:42:06 UTC

Hello all! I just got back from a cold weekend in Austin and
happened to find a couple of boxes at the Barton Creek Greenbelt.
My Step-father has lived in Austin all his life and prides himself
on knowing the ins and outs of the town. He couldn't believe that
he had driven past the entrance to this park hundreds of times and
never realized it was there. He loved it! He's going back this
week to explore it some more.
BARTON CREEK GREENBELT LETTERBOX- Found in perfect condition.
HIKING PUG LETTERBOX- Box was glaringly visible from the trail.
There was even a log from a geocacher who noticed it from the trail
and investigated. The insides were perfect and there were a lot of
interesting logs from around the globe. We placed it back in its
spot with a rather large rock wedged into the opening. I hope the
Pug Lovers don't mind.

We also searched for the AUSTIN HIGHLIGHTS 1 LETTERBOX at the
arboretum. It's not there. I'm certain of the location, but this
is a very popular place for the kiddies and I think it may have been
victimized.

I also found out were to look for the Box of Ants, Austin Anteater,
and 360 Bridge boxes, but those will have to wait till next trip.
Thanks to all the placers. Me and the family had a great time.

The entrance is actually on the northbound side of loop 360. I
guess if you are going SW on 1, take the 360 exit
and go SSE. Then there is a light you can turn at, but I don't
remember the street name or if there even was one. You can see the
park entrance sign to your left. There are no roadsigns for
directions. If you get to Lamar, you've gone too far. You can get
the two that I did, and if you're in the mood for a long walk or
bike ride, there are at least two other boxes in this park and one
in Zilker Park, which adjoins the Barton Greenbelt.
