I've been looking at photos from past events in an effort to get an idea for a stamp and I saw the photo of the Circus Poster at Mission Tejas in 2009. Time flies. It also reminded me that there is a new traveling box out there called something like "Wild West Show and Rodeo". Does anybody know where that box is? It seems to me that these boxes are not moving along as rapidly as they used to do. I know that there are extenuating circumstances sometimes, but there should be something we could do so that more people get a chance to see them and contribute to them. I'd love to get a chance to find this one. I remember jumping in the car and driving a hundred miles or more to grab the Tour Bus.

Boots Tex

From: (wry_me@yahoo.com) | Date: 2013-12-17 19:43:30 UTC-08:00

We added 3 new acts to the show and were happily  moving south when we were waylaid by hostile natives at an Indian camp and powwow south of Alvin.  The weather finally cleared and we sent a messenger to smuggle our plea for rescue.  Once through the gates, follow the road to the outskirts of the camp.  Don't cross over into Indian territory unless you want to be an overnight "guest!" You'll have to flank 'em on the right. Tie the horses at the entrance to the mission.  You'll have to sneak past the Tiguas, Kiowas, Caddos and Atakapan.  All the minor trails converge at a small parking lot.  Pass the Kickapoo and the swimmin' hole.  Take the left fork pass the privy.  Go past the backstop to a dead tree on the right.  From there, count 53 steps along the wide cattle trail, avoiding the bear trap, to a dead oak on the left.  Go six more steps and look for a pine hidden behind sapling oaks.  It's the fattest skinny pine in a group of skinny pines, at the base to the back, behind the saplings.  Send the cavalry.
