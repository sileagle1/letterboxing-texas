I must warn you - of the 5 letterboxes I have out - 3 are easy to get to.  One is a 30 mile drive outside of town, the other two are in a state park. None requires more than a 1/2 mile walk round trip. 
 
Of the other two......  one (tin mines) is a 6 mile round trip with an 880 foot elevation gain.
http://www.localhikes.com/Hikes/FMSPTinMines_2320.asp
 
The second (N. Franklin Mountain) is 7.9 miles round trip and 3050 feet elevation gain. 
http://www.localhikes.com/Hikes/N_Franklin_Mountain_2320.asp
 
Both are really beautiful desert hikes.  Franklin Mountain is a beautiful hike, I have seen deer up there.
 
The newest one (30 mile drive) - cattle on the mesa - is going to be interesting.  The clues will get you within 25 feet of it, but it will be deceptively difficult to find.
 
There are two other letterboxes out here, so it makes a total of 7.  The two that are not mine are easy - a short walk.
 
One is along this trail:
http://www.localhikes.com/Hikes/Naturewalk_2320.asp
 
The other is about 1/4 mile from the start of this trail:
http://www.localhikes.com/Hikes/ElephantsTrunk_2320.asp
 
The state park DOES charge $4 per person to enter - but this gets you 5 letterboxes within the park.
 
Well - I am feeling sick today - but not so bad that I won't go to work.  I pretty much work alone - so not likely to get others sick.  If I am going to be miserable - I may as well get paid for it!  And carry 90 hours of vacation over to next year.....
