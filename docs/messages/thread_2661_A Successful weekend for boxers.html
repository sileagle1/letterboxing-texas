A Successful weekend for boxers

From: Carolyn Ramirez (danielcgarcia@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2007-03-21 04:42:12 UTC
Having just caught up on the postings from everyone from this past
weekend, I just wanted to share my little adventure with the group.
First, PomPom Bear (my 12 y/o daughter) had just spent all last week
down in San Antonio, with family and I went down on Friday to pick
her up and bring her home with the intentions of finding a few boxes
while I was down there. WEll....it didn't start out so well. I
figured, I needed to pick up some Rx's and I needed to get the oil
changed before I set out on the road so why not kill 2 birds with
one stone and do both at Wal Mart. So when I pulled up the guy comes
out to greet me and I asked how long it would take to get my oil
changed. To which he promplty replies 30 - 45 minutes , an hour
tops. There are 2 cars in front of you. Well, tell me a girl that
can't kill an hour in Wal Mart so I tell him ok, go ahead, I have a
few things to do anyways in here. So I go in and get my RX's and
mull around, checking everything out, picked up a very unsatisfying
lunch at McD's and without even realizing it, managed to kill 2HRS
in there. As I hurried back to the automotive section, I tell the ,
couldn't be more than 18, gum chewing, smart alek, with a bad
attitude, cashier that I needed to pick up my vehicle. To which she
promptly tells me, its not ready and that they haven't even started
on it! This wasn't what I wanted to hear as it was already quickly
approaching 2:00 and I didn't want to get stuck in downtown Austin
traffic on a friday afternoon. I told her I didn't have anymore time
to wait and that I was told 1 hr. She told me there was nothing she
could do and if I wanted to leave I had to go get my keys from one
of the technicians. Not seeing a sole in sight, I walked over to my
car to find it sitting there with the windows rolled down, the keys
in the ignition, and all of my stuff in the backseat. This made me
just a little on the mad side and so I jumped in my car (after
making sure everything was still there) and took. I stopped at an
oil change place and had my car done in 5 min. So finally on the
road and after 2 hrs delay in traffic in downtown Austin (does I35
ever not have traffic?) I finally made it to SA. Saturday was much
better and after getting lost several times in SA I was able to find
some great lb. I wanted to do more, but ran out of time. A very
interesting thing happened to me though. I had gone and done the
Espada Aquaduct by Tall Texas (which is a very interesting place to
visit) and then proceeded to the Mission Espada to find the next lb
there. After having carefully followed the directions several times,
there was no lb to be found. We searched behing every tree and I'm
sure people thought we were crazy. But to no avail. Now, PomPom bear
tends to get easily upset if she doesn't find what she is looking
for. So as I accept defeat and tell her we have to go without
finding it, she stomps off through the middle of the grassy area
located inside the mission. After a prompt attitude check she
returned and we were walking on the sidewalk around the mission to
the info area and the parking lot. As we walked and talked about not
being able to find this box, I felt something tell me to stop. I'm
not sure why and I'm not sure why I chose that precise moment to
turn and look to my right, but I did. As we walked, I looked over
and what do I see but the lb that we had been searching for laying
there in the grass next to a newly planted tree. This was not where
it should have been and I can only assume that someone found it and
just threw it down there. Everything was in it and it was still in
good condition. I guess its true that God can lead us and things
happen for a reason. PomPom Bear was so excited and it this was a
very small lb so I could have easily walked right by it but I
honestly feel that he led me to this spot to recover this and
although I couldn't replace it because there were too many people in
the area it was to be hidden in and also because the park was
closing in 5 min so I was forced to take it with me. I have notified
Tall TExan and plan on mailing him back his lb as soon as he sends
me his address. I felt bad in taking it with me, but I didn't want
it to end up disappearing again and there were too many people to
put it back where it should have been. Sunday, it was off to tackle
the traffic back up I35. After a quick stop in Austin to drop off
the TTB and to find a few LB in Barton Springs (great area) we
decided that we needed to hit the road. From Round Rock to the I35
split at Hillsboro, the traffic just crawled...and crawled...and
crawled. We didn't get home until 8 Sunday evening. The construction
that was being done and then the extra traffic from people returning
from Spring BREak made everything horribly slow. The one bright spot
was a quick stop off in West to stretch our legs and to find
Stargazer's beautiful stamp The Czech stop. Very detailed and would
highly recommend anyone that doesn't have it to find it. I loved
that I could sit at the gazebo and stamp in my book. Nothing like
having a nice place to sit and stamp. So other than bad timing and
traffic, it was a great weekend. Have to make another trip to do all
the ones I wanted to do and didn't have time for. Sounds like
Barefoot Lucy and Viewfinder as well as others had great weeks with
successful finds as well. Good hunting to everyone.
