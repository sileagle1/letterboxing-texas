From: TeamKing (teamking@entouch.net) | Date: 2006-05-09 13:29:12 UTC-05:00
After traveling around the state for one year this month, the tour
bus box came home for a little maintenance after the gathering. I
think I had the only non-Texan passenger, so she stepped off and I
replaced. Now the bus is ready for it's second year moving around the
state.

The box currently contains: 2 box stamps and 18 passenger stamps

To find the box:

Visit Telge Park in Cypress. Once through the entrance gate, walk
straight back to the wooden bridge. You'll see a new wooden scout
marker here. From the marker, walk left along the woods line until
you come to the gray electrical box sticking out of the ground. From
here continue walking along the woods line, but take the first right
in the woods. Just down on the left is a large pine all by itself.
The tree is approx 54 steps from the gray box. Box is slightly behind
and to the right of the tree. It's a big tupperware so you shouldn't
miss it!

Have fun!

Don't forget this box is listed on AQ if you want to log it.

From: ryber3 (ryber3@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-08-27 17:24:52 UTC
strawberry6703 and I recently picked up the bus in Mansfield. It has
since made its way a bit north to Flower Mound, TX after picking up a
new passenger: the important, if somewhat eccentric singer/musician
Gibby Haynes. The Bus has made its next stop at Murrell Park on
Grapevine Lake.

To find the box head west on FM 3040 (Roundgrove Rd.) from I-35E into
Flower Mound, TX. Continue west on 3040 until you come to McKamy Creek
Rd. Turn onto McKamy Creek (you can only turn one way presently) and
keep going until you reach Simmons. Take a left on Simmons and
continue until you reach Murrell Park. Follow the road as it curves to
the right. After the picnic areas you will turn left into Fishing
Trail #2. Pull to the left of the grass covered circle and past the
first parking area. At the second parking area on your left park in
the South Eastern most spot. At the corner of the large wooden poles
you will see a small trail heading off toward the lake. Follow the
trail and soon you will come to a clearing. Directly in front of you
will be a nice Cedar Tree. Just past this tree and to the left will be
a larger Cedar. The Tour Bus is hidden at the base of this tree on the
lake side of the trunk.

Best wishes,
