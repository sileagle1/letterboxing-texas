From: Tracy Valdez (gtvaldez@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2008-02-05 09:31:51 UTC-06:00

Mineral Wells State Park.

 

There is a place to swim if it gets warm. You can rent paddle boats for pretty cheap and boat or fish.

There is a wonderful place to walk to the bottom of a man-made natural stone staircase and explore the base of a rock climbing area. Very safe at the bottom – lots of supervision needed at the top where you park.

There is one letterbox in the park. We had two others but they’ve disappeared. (We checked for sure a couple weeks ago).

There are wonderful hiking trails – horse trails as well.

 

We love that park.

 

Cedar Hill State Park is another awesome park with some campgrounds with a playground right next to it. They were doing some water plumbing construction a couple of months ago so you may want to get a campground farther away from that but since it is huge, you would be fine. Cedar Hill has Penn Farm which is fun to explore – it is an old working oldest running farm (I think) farm in Texas. They have old farm machines to explore and the original buildings and it is really cool. I think Cedar Hill has a perch pond, too. We go start parking so much they blend after a while. It is a very calm quite beautiful pond to fish from that is very kid friendly. More turtles than fish so nibblers on your pole are incredibly exciting for small kids.

 

HTH!

Tracy

Chrispy, the Texas Parks and Wildlife site is, for a state-run
website, really remarkable: http://www.tpwd.state.tx.us/spdest/
(that's the parks portion, main site is at
http://www.tpwd.state.tx.us/).

There's no substitute for getting personal recommendations, that's for
sure, but at the TPWD site you'll be able to check out campground
maps, amenities, and so on, and--though it's down right now--it is
possible to book your campground reservations online.

Here's a page that might be helpful for your DFW location:
http://www.tpwd.state.tx.us/publications/parkguide/?page=dfw⟨=en

Have fun this weekend!

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-02-05 09:59:24 UTC-06:00
It's tough to go wrong with Texas State Parks!
 
Cedar Hill is beautiful and it is just south of Dallas. 
  
Dinosaur Valley is cool and the kids would LOVE it.
 
For future reference, Mother Neff is an excellent park, lots to explore.  Some nice caves.  BUT it is closed right now due to flooding.
 
A good summertime park is Purtis Creek State Park near Athens - it has some nice trails with a few boxes and a great swimming area.  They also do a good campfire program and the rangers really like their visitors.
 
All the above parks have at least three boxes.
