My Intro

From: rrayven (rrayven@charter.net) | Date: 2006-03-19 13:50:55 UTC
Hi ya'll! My trail name is The Rayven and I'm from Fort Worth. I got
started in letterboxing when a friend from Virginia came for a visit a
few months back. She's an avid letterboxer and she made stamps for my
whole family, so needless to say, we all headed out to letterbox while
she was here! All it took was that one marathon search out at the
Fort Worth Nature Center and we're all hooked! Now I just have to
remember to check the list of local letterboxes before we head out for
a family outing - we went to the Botanical Gardens the other day, not
realizing there are several letterboxes hidden there!

I'm looking forward to getting more into this cool new hobby and to
making our first boxes to hide!

The Rayven

From: (blhjrh@aol.com) | Date: 2006-03-20 18:51:45 UTC-05:00
Hey, Rayven! 
Welcome to Texas Letterboxing, especially the Fort Worth area!  Only a little over a year ago when I first discovered letterboxing, there were just a few boxes (and letterboxers) near here, but now, there are lots of really good ones, good stamps, good locations.  I hope you'll enjoy them all and I especially hope you'll enjoy finding all of mine!  But please please please be sure to exercise stealth when finding them so they aren't accidentally discovered, please please please reseal all the bags and boxes tightly so the rain doesn't destroy the logbooks, and please please please pretty-please always recover them well, so they aren't destroyed!  It's a sad thing to carve a stamp, make a logbook, create a letterbox in a great location, and then have it disappear.  Only today I discovered another one of my boxes sitting right out in the open clearly visible to anyone playing on the creekbank nearby (which kids frequently do!), and I'm trying to believe that our rains last night just washed away the covering bark and stuff.  But, having said all that, have a great time finding all the wonderful local boxes!
