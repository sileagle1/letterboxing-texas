Well, I may be the only one who does this.  When I find a letterbox and its stamp has been muggled, I find something else to stamp in with, and draw the original image around it.  Objects have included a spent shotgun shell (which became an ant), a small mum (which became a fountain), an interesting rock (which was used to “build” a tower), an interesting piece of bark (which became a signature), and an interesting piece of moss (which became a butterfly).  Then I log the find, because I did find the letterbox, even if I was creative with my stamped image.  Of course, when I log the find, I let the owner know that the stamp has gone missing.  I’ve never checked the rules on this, so ya’ll can all now tell me if I am doing it wrong!  -- Bell Motel

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2011-03-20 09:00:37 UTC-05:00

Using " something" in the spot to memorialize the find is a nice way to accommodate your logbook so you can keep moving.

As far as logging the find online, its far more courteous to contact the finder before you do anything and give them an opportunity to replace the stamp. In fact I think many boxers would be willing to send you an imprint of the stamp for the sake of your time and effort since you let them know the box was there but the stamp was gone.

Recording it as an attempt just pushes that box into the abyss of boxes that some boxers wont try because the last log was an attempt, when all that was really needed was a litle maintenance.

My. 02,
Lucy
