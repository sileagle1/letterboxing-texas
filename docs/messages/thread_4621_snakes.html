I'm not sure if this was a true story or my dad just trying to warn us on the dangers of the creek. It didn't keep us from going there to play everyday but it did make me keep an eye out for water snakes.
 
I recently moved more into the country and I live right next to a small lake and snakes are in my yard all the time eating the toads and baby rabbits and whatever other things around here. I never thought living next to water would bring so much nature along with it. Rats, mice, sfinks (sp?), horny looking lizards, geckos, camillions, one lined striped water snakes, grass snakes, skunks, raccoons, rabbits, possum, cows, horses. lol Ok the last two weren't wild. But the nature is wonderful around here.
 
Oh another snake story. My ex-husbands best friend is a plumber and he was going to light a water heater in this basement, about a month ago, and he reached down behind him to get balance. His hand and all his weight landed on a 2 foot coral snakes head and killed it. He saw the blood on his hand and the snake and thought he was bit. lol It was the snakes blood. He sure was lucky to have survived and got rid of the snake in the Lady's house. She let him keep it and he tried to scare me with it. I thought it was a toy and picked it right up. Goes to show how stupid I am. As soon as it squished in my fingers I threw it down and asked him if it was dead. He ran around the rest of the day scaring people. We have snakes everywhere. One could be in your house right now...
Oh oh oh another snake story! lol I have plenty. When I was little there was a snake sleeping next to the toilet in my house. My dad said it must have crawled out of the toilet! OMG talk about checking the toilet for years after that one. It was really big too! Well at least to little me it was.
