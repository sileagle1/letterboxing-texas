sad story of the little bird

From: (blhjrh@aol.com) | Date: 2007-03-05 17:28:37 UTC-05:00
Read my sad tale of woe and heed the moral of the story:
 
You may recall a few months ago I reported that a geocache had been placed right on top of one of my letterboxes, In the Tree: Meghan's Bird.  This letterbox is one of 3 I'd carved and placed with my granddaughters, and was a special box in a very pretty park in Grapevine where it enjoyed many visitors.  The geocache is a great big honking blue-lid box that really doesn't fit the hole. 
 
I attempted to communicate with the geocacher to see if he realized my little letterbox was already in that same location and would be willing to move his geocache.  He finally did respond, but refused to move the geocache, since he like the spot, too.  Well, today I discovered that the geocache has consumed the letterbox.  When I looked for the box, it was gone, but when I looked inside the huge geocache, there was my box, but inside was only a plastic letter K.  No stamp.  Rummaging through the contents of the geocache I found the logbook (hooray!), sort of rumpled but intact, and even my plastic bag that protected both the stamp and logbook was in there among the toys, although empty and somewhat the worse for wear.  I reclaimed the empty letterbox, the logbook and bag, leaving the geocache to call that hole in the tree home even though the little bird had been there for two years, happily and safely, until the geocache moved in on it.
 
The little bird isn't gone for good. The other letterbox I had in the same park, In the Creek: Lauren's Turtle 2, had also gone missing, as has In the Pond: Allie's Duck from the Bedford Boys Ranch Park.  All 3 letterboxes will soon be replaced with new stamps, new logbooks, new boxcs, and a new home.  But it's a sad story, even so, that the geocache consumed the tiny bird.  They just couldn't get along in the same home, apparently. 
 
Moral:  Don't trust a Great Honking Geocache to share a hole in a tree with your tiny bird letterbox.
-- Viewfinder, Sadder but Wiser
