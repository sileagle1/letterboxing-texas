The LBJ State Historical Park is located west of Austin, 2 miles east
of Stonewall, 14 miles west of Johnson City on Hwy 290. Turn north on
Park Road 52, then go right at first intersection (left goes to the
National Park Visitor Center). Continue past the living history center
and park in the small parking lot east of the living history center
and west of the restrooms and picnic area (you'll be looking at a
large grapevine tangle). Head due north to the trailhead and into the
woods. Follow the path across the bridge marking the Visitor
Center/Texas Wildlife. Immediately after the bridge turn right.
follow the clues to the Texas Peach Letterbox. After stamping the
Texas Peach, take a compass heading of 310 degrees and walk 7 steps.
TTB2 is located under a cluster of several medium sized logs (abt 6-8
inches in diameter).

There’s a new passenger on the bus and it is now located in Johnson City.

 

Pedernales State Park is off of US 290 and RR 3232. From the Austin area, go west on 290 about 30 miles to 3232, then north for about 6 miles until you see the entrance of the park.

 

Once you see the sign you will need to drive several miles to the front gate. There is a small shack where you can pay your park fees ($5 per person, unless you have a park pass). If no one is at the gate, there is a small house located just past the gate where you can check in, get a map and pay your entrance fee.

 

Once you’ve finished up there, drive around the back of the house taking the only road that leads down the hill. Follow this road to the end without turning off anywhere. It is about 2.5 miles to the end. You will be in a circular parking lot with a trail head leading to the falls.

 

Take the trail about 350 yards to the stone steps that lead to the falls. There is a split about of the way down, but stay to the left on the main trail. You will soon be at the stone steps leading to the falls. Just a short way down the steps is a fabulous view of the water and rocks. It is not too treacherous and you can easily get down to the water by taking the steps. There are many caution signs around because the area can literally flood within minutes, but I was told today that there is little danger at this moment but you might read the warning signs for more information about what to look for – apparently sunny conditions at the park are no guarantee – stormy weather up stream can cause flooding in literally minutes.

 

After you’ve enjoyed your time at the falls, head back up the stone steps to the top to the Red and White Warning sign. As you are looking at the sign (facing the water), to your left there is a small trail heading upstream. Follow this trail to the sign warning you about trespassing on the other side of the river. From this sign, go about 35 steps. On the left side of the trail look for a small pile of slightly charred dead logs (about 2’-4’ in length) pointing to the left.

 

Follow where they are pointing another 35 steps directly into the woods. (You should notice two trees grown together in the shape of a “U” about 10 feet off the trail. If you see that you are headed in the right direction) If you stop after 35 steps and are very quiet, you may hear some lively discourse coming from the bus. The newest passenger is not known for being shy and should have plenty to say to the other passengers. If the sound of the falls is drowning them out and you can’t hear a thing, look to your right for a small cluster of cedar trees with a pile of dead wood at their base. You’ve found the bus!
