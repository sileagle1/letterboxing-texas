Just so Dewberry and the other Texans and honorary Texans - especially
that rowdy Metroplex bunch :p - don't feel left out on the rush to
April Fools. . .here's a challenge for you. . .

I will have an event only / traveling box at the gathering called
'Tribute to England' (in relation to my box series of the same name in
Houston). This box will NOT just be a stamp. However, I am going to
make you work to find it and you will be running around the park (okay,
well, walk, bike, drive. . .whatever). Anyway, I am going to give you
an option to get out of the runaround bit and go right for the box. All
you have to do is give me a name. . .here's how it works. . .

I recently tried a cache challenge that sounded pretty cool. Find a
Titantic survivor - alive or their gravestone/burial place. (my
grandmother was on the Olympic (Titantic's sister ship) in 1913 so I
have always had an interest in this) I assumed the odds of finding
someone alive were about nil, but I thought there might be a good
chance someone was buried here - and maybe even in Houston. So, a
little digging around the internet and YES, I found someone in Houston.

This man was on part of the deck crew on the Titantic. He made it onto
a boat with 11 others (about half crew and half 1st class passengers -
he was one of 2 of the deck sea crew and probably was the one who
handled the boat) and was picked up by the Carpathia and dropped off
in NYC. Eventually he made his way to the Houston area where he died in
a nursing home in 1962 and is buried in an area cemetary. Who is he?

Note: There may be others in Houston. . .I don't know, I didn't get
that far. But if you come up with proof of someone else, I'll accept
it. I did see the name of a woman who might be here, but didn't
continue researching it.

Are you up for the challenge? Good luck.
