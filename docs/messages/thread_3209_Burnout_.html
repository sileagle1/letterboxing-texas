Burnout?

From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-12-01 04:53:03 UTC
All this talk of burnout and slowing down is intriguing. If you stick
with anything for a long time, you're going to have natural peaks and
valleys in your participation. Depending on other things in your
life, your time and interest in letterboxing will wax and wane.

I have become somewhat disillusioned with a lot of urban letterboxing.
Too often, placements are rather haphazard, with no compelling reason
for the box. And personally, I can't believe how often the City of
Houston seems to re-landscape some of these parks! As most of my more
urban boxes go missing, they probably won't be replaced unless there's
a very compelling reason for them to be in a certain spot.

The boxes that I enjoy best involve a nice walk. Being outside and
sharing our love of hiking with our kids is one reason why we got
started letterboxing in the first place.

We did some hiking over Thanksgiving break where I didn't even bring
letterbox clues (shocking, I know), and we did a bit of letterboxing
too, but the focus was on enjoying our break, our time together, and
having a great time outdoors (as much as the weather allowed). The
way it works best for my family is to find balance and do a little
something that each of us wants to do. And that definitely means
fewer boxes are on the to-do list than perhaps once would have been.

And that we spent an hour skipping stones & climbing boulders at a
creek in Pflugerville rather than going on to find more boxes in the
next town.

But, when we do go letterboxing, we usually have a good time. We had
the best time as a family finding Mother of Five's latest...Which came
first? I definitely recommend that one as a boredom-buster!
