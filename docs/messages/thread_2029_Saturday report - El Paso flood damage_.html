From: Michael P. Olbrisch (mike2004@elp.rr.com) | Date: 2006-08-05 22:26:23 UTC

I just did a 6 hour inspection of the Franklin Mountains. I didn't need to
go down the trails too far to see damage and destruction. The floods off
the mountain were terrible. Franklin Mountain State Park is in bad shape.

Two of my letterbox-Hybrid Geocache combinations are removed. Not because
they were damaged. But because the approach to them is in need of serious
maintenance. They will be replaced once the park is again in shape for it.

Mr Silver Eagle sir. I removed your letterbox on the Nature Walk. The
nature Walk is more than 50% destroyed. When you take the left fork, in 75
paces you hit the wash-out. The trail is totally gone until the high area
just before you get to your box. The rock had settled on your box and
deformed it. NO WATER DAMAGE. You will need to re-write the clues once
things are back to normal. I will hold you letterbox safe here - unless you
have other ideas. Let me know what you would like me to do.

Dear SandiBox. Your letterbox is in GREAT shape. I did not remove it. The
clues essentially still work. If you can find your way to the trail. I
did, cause I know the trail. The trail starts down, makes a slight bend to
the left, and DISAPPEARS! If you hug the left side of the wash-out,
eventually you find the trail, then lose it and find it again. By the time
you get to the brown trail sign and the rock of the old placement, the clues
work. Beyond that less then 100 yards the trail is shot again. That trail
is gone until you hit the switchbacks on the rhyolite wall. But your box is
OK and the clues still work.

I guess the wife and I can put together a slideshow of what we saw. OTOH,
we are gearing up to go and inspect several trails tomorrow too. Perhaps we
will wait till later.

Silver Eagle and SandiBox, please let me know you got this.
