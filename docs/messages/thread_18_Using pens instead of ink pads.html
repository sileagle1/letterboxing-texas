This is pointed toward Silver Eagle, but I'd like to
hear from anyone:

Silver Eagle,

In your Mission Tejas box, you have a red felt tip pen
instead of an ink pad. I was suprised by how well it
inked, but it works really well.

I have a stamp I'm finishing right now that will
really need two colors to make it look great, so I
figure the best way to do it will be to place two pens
in the box with directions (it's kind of a duh thing,
but I figure directions won't hurt).

My question is - what kind of pen is that? I've tried
a number of different felt tips, but none of them are
really wet enough, so that they dry before I finish
inking the stamp.

Thanks!
Lucy

From: Silver Eagle (sileagle@alltel.net) | Date: 2004-01-26 14:13:18 UTC
I think it was just a marker from an art set that my
kids have. I have tried several different types of
markers and I know what you mean about most of them
being too dry. The Dry Erase kind don't work very
well and Sharpies aren't that great, either. What
I use now for multi-color stamps are the small raised
ink pads shaped like a cat-eye. Unless you need
the precision of a marker, they seem to work best.
I got them at Michaels. Looking forward to finding
your boxes next time I'm up that way.

Silver Eagle

Re: [letterboxingtexas] Using pens instead of ink pads

From: Susan Tashiro (ssmith043@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-01-26 06:29:30 UTC-08:00
Lucy,
As a long time rubberstamper, I have a bit of input on this one.  You need to use a water-based marker.  The ones that rubberstampers use for the most part are "Tombow" brand markers.  They usually come in a bullet tip and brush tip making them useful for detail and not-so-detailed work.  You can buy these at your arts/craft stores, scrapbooking stores and rubberstamping stores.  At a lot of these places, they can be bought singly or in sets.  There are other brands as well, just check and make sure they are water based or dye ink.  "Magic Markers", "Sharpies", "Prismacolor", etc markers are alcohol based and will not work.  There are oodles of rubberstamping and scrapbooking sources on the net.  Just do a search!
HTH,
Susan
