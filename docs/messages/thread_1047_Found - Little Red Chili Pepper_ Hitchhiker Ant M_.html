Found - Little Red Chili Pepper, Hitchhiker Ant M, and a "bonus"

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-07-14 07:18:49 UTC-07:00
You fine folks have managed to push me out the door to
get out and box in spite of the heat. Y'all have just
made it too good to resist!

Was going to go out last night and find several, but
there were storms brewing overhead and we decided to
stay in. My husband, the LSS, kept calling us
chickens and wimps until we verrrry patiently (NOT)
explained to him that it wasnt ourselves we were
worried about, but the log books. Sheeshthe things
we have to explain to the unconvinced. LOL

Anyway, skies were clear this morning but work loomed,
so I managed to get out of the house really early,
stopped by Jack in the Box and grabbed something for
breakfast so I could hit a box, Little Red Chili
Pepper, between my house and my office in a neat
little rest area. I landed myself at the rest area 20
minutes before I had to be at work and work was less
than a minute away plenty of time! I stepped out of
the car and heard the all-too-familiar cry of a
stranded kitten (my daughter, Elmo, is a stray animal
magnet). Sure enough, out trotted a small abandoned
kitten and I began telling myself Im not gonna take
it ANYWHERE! like a mantra. I had my doubts.

I got myself to the box with kitten underfoot and I
dug it (the box not the kitten) out of a very nice
little hiding spot. Carried it to the nearby picnic
table and began unbuttoning it, only to find a
hitchhiker - Ant M. YEA! I love finding HHs like
who doesnt, right?

Ive figured out that TexasLuvBug (it was her box with
the kitten) carves nice stamps that need color, so I
had brought my markers with me. Well, the kitten had
very kindly been going through my markers while I was
going through the box and most of my colors were on
the ground. As I got down on my hands and knees to
pick up my markers, the kitten began crawling up on my
back and down my arms, like I was monkey bars. Hmph.

Finally, finally, I had everything I needed gathered
up to begin stamping in. I started coloring my own
stamp so I could get the boxs logbook put away before
the kitten tracked its dirty little paws all over the
book (yes the same little paws it had been using to
climb all over my back). I had to put the kitten on
the ground no less than 30 times in the stamping in
process and in the meantime, it was clawing its way up
my pants legs. Finally got the boxs book stamped in
and put away, then I started on the boxs stamp in my
own logbook and after another 30 or 40 times of
putting the kitten (who by the way, had eaten at least
half my breakfast by this point!) on the ground I
finally managed to finish stamping in! I wasnt
having to chant my mantra any longer, because I knew
without a doubt that kitten wasnt going anywhere with
me!

So nothing left but to hide the box back. After the
stamping process, hiding should be easy. Yes? Right.
I went back to the nice little hiding spot and began
sewing the box back in. As I did, my hair hung down
in front of me and the kitten began using it to get up
close to my face climbing my hair like it would
climb a tree. Now that really was a little much! I
at last got it hidden back in well enough, and Ill
have to go back later today after work to make it
right.
Finally, I stood up and took a look at myself a
hundred dirty little kitty tracks all over my light
khaki pants and my pale green shirt (not counting the
ones on my back AND I was now running late. I
brushed off as best I could (especially since the
kitten was still trying to climb my leg) and jumped in
my car to get on to work. I got there one minute
late! Fell in the door and my punctual co-workers
thought I mustve been chased by thieves or had a car
accident or something because I was so bedraggled
looking. Who knew a tiny kitten could do so much
damage?

Two things Ive learned from this experience: First
and most important for all of us to know, apparently
TexasLuvBug and her boxes have an affinity to animals.
First the one with the armadillo and now this one
with a kitten. Im almost afraid to go hunt her next
one for fear Ill run into a rare East Texas Giant
Gorilla or something!

Secondly, letterboxing before work is great!
However, I suggest taking along a change of clothes

Lucy
