I just wanted to remind everyone that there are several Mystery
Letterboxes in Texas listed on LBNA and most of them are not difficult
to figure out. So why do people create mystery boxes? Sometimes it is
to make the hunt more challenging, but many times the location of the
box is made a mystery to keep it from being confiscated by park
rangers. So don't forget to check them out, there might be one near
you.

Silver Eagle

Re: Mystery Letterboxes

From: Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter (janet@redbarnretreat.com) | Date: 2008-10-27 14:01:45 UTC
I tend to forget to check the mystery boxes when I'm collecting clues
for an upcoming trip, so this is a good reminder. I wasn't aware
that Silver Eagle had a box in a particular park, but I contacted him
to ask his opinion on the park since it was one that had a reputation
as being "anti-letterbox". In offering me his insight, he hinted
that he had a box there, so I looked for the clues and found them
among the mystery boxes. It was one of those "well, duh!, why didn't
I look there before?" moments. On a related note, I placed a box in
Huntsville State Park in June called "Texas Trash". Now, Huntsville
SP also has a history of being "anti-letterbox" so I require that
potential finders contact me for the clues and promise to pick up
trash on the way to finding the box. Then, if they let me know that
they found the box and give me a report on the trash, I send them a
recipe for "Texas Trash". Only 3 times has anyone asked for the
clues and out of those 3, only 1 reported back (they found no trash
to pick up) and asked for the recipe. So, as an experiment, that one
hasn't been too successful. On the other hand, my box "Snipe
Hunting" has been in that park for almost a year, has been found a
few times and, as far as I know is alive and well. I don't think the
park gets many visitors because of its reputation. Thanks for the
heads-up on mystery boxes, SE.

Boots Tex
