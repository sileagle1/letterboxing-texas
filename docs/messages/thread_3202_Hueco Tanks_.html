Well - that is not a bad place to hike.  You will have a few things to overcome.  First, most of the area is small mountains.  There isn't a lot of level ground.  You do not need to go on the steep stuff, but it is uphill.  Next. you will be at about 4200 feet ASL.  This will make breathing more difficult.  But - with those two things in mind, just go slow and you will be fine.
 
The crawling part doesn't need to be done like we did it.  Tying our gear to our feet and dragging it through was a bit extreme.  But we were not sure we would return the same way.  And there was no other way through.  We couldn't fit with the packs on our backs.  We were searching for a lost letterbox.  Being the kind of people we are, we didn't want to miss any possibility no matter how remote.  So we did some crawling.  Knowing we were going to some pretty remote areas, we also over packed.  You will likely not need nor want to carry that much gear.  But we didn't expect to see anyone else where we were headed, we needed to be self-sufficient.
 
That was really two hikes combined into one.  Hiking to the paintings would have been a short hike.  It was the letterbox search that took the time.  But that was also the most enjoyable part to me.  I guess I like a good hike.  You will need to crawl a bit to get to the paintings.  But it isn't anything difficult.
 
Last - I am a state park volunteer.  I patrol trails, watch out for the park, and clean up trash.  We were in a very remote area, one not often visited.  There is no telling how the trash got in there.  Blown in, washed in, or someone left it... does it really matter.  Yes, leaving it behind would have been easy.  But my military career was not built on doing what was easy.  And my son was with me.  He was already 25 and a Cavalry Scout by then, but what example would I have set if I did the easy thing and left the trash?  That is just not me.  Doing what is right, even when no one else is looking, that's me.  Likely your daughter won't get it.  That's ok, but my friends do.  Maybe that is why they are my friends..... they know we can count on each other and little things like that are the example. 
 
For Dinner - at the airport?  Well - if you insist.  But I would recommend that for dinner you go get the Cattle on the Mesa Letterbox.  You would be hard-pressed to find a better steak anywhere.  Don't take my word for it, come and try it.  And yes- they have Margaritas!  Get there about an hour before sunset.  Find the letterbox.  Then sit on the veranda and enjoy the sunset.  When you are ready for your meal, beg them to seat you where you can see the coyotes come in.  They scatter raw trimmings on the side of the restaurant and after dark the coyotes come in to eat.
 
Hopefully by spring that will get you three letterboxes.  Hueco Tanks, Hudspeth County, and Cattle on the Mesa.
 
Well - enough.  Got ammo to reload today.  For competition rifle ammo I need to weigh each powder charge, 24.8 gr of powder in each shell.  There are 7000 grains per pound.  I will use about two pounds of powder today.  I will be busy.
 
If you make it, let us know.  Perhaps we can get away to have a steak with you.
