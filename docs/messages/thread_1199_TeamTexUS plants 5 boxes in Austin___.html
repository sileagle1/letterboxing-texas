OK, it's been a really rough day............let me try this again!
 
HI, everyone! We have planted our first 5 boxes. They are not listed on LbNA yet, but for those of you planning your weekend, I thought I'd get them out:
 
Boxes 1 and 2, planted by The Texan:
 
Go to Centennial Park and park on the street across from the Denton A. Cooley Pavillion. Go up the steps and bear left on the sidewalk for 20 steps. Sight the metal fence post at 320 degrees at the tree line. The Longhorn Microbox is to the left, under a loose rock in the wall.
 
Go back to the sidewalk and find the water fountain. With your back to the fountain, sight 320degrees and walk 52 steps to the tree line. The Predator Box is under a chunk or concrete/rock at the fence line.
 
Boxes 3 and 4, planted by The Patriot:
 
Go to Waterloo Park and enter the park at the gravel path at 14th and Trinity. Take the path to the bridge, bearing left to go under the bridge and through the tunnel. Cross the waterfall to the big tree weeping over the path. The Predator Microbox is in the crotch of the tree about 6 feet up.
 
Continue on the path, bearing left, so that you cross the stream and go back under the bridge on the other side of the stream. Find the stairs on your right. From the stairs, go 55 steps further along the path. The Spider Sword Box is up under the top ledge of the wall on your right.
 
Box 5 planted by JOY
 
Go to the place where Guy Clark likes to drink Mad Dog Margaritas. Go inside and enjoy. I did- WOO HOO! Find the sign that tells you how far it is to Bandera. Count the birds on the mural. Count the number of stars on Uncle Sam's hat on the happy hour poster. Add these numbers together to get the *magic number*. Multiply the *magic number* by 2 and add 15. Go out the front door, turn left and go that number of steps. Go left again and walk the *magic number* of steps. Go up to this historic old building and read the plaque. Then, go to the far left of the front porch. The Mad Dog Margarita Box is hidden behind the drainpipe, covered by rock and concrete. 
  
As always, please be discrete, re-hide well, and follow the usual precautions. Happy Trails!
