DIY Feeding (was Re: [letterboxingtexas] Re: Lock'n'Lock boxes-Round ones specifically)

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-04-08 22:44:09 UTC-05:00
Entirely NOT letterboxing, but...
 
A few years ago Lightning and I really got into birdwatching from our study, which has a window out to the front porch and the big tree where our bird feeder hangs.  We kept our bird feed in a trash can with a flip lid on the front porch and happily filled our feeder up with regularity. 
 
Until the squirrels got wind of what was going on.  It wasn't that we didn't want the squirrels around, but they made a horrible mess as they swung from the feeder and slung feed everywhere.  Rather than trying to run them off, we put a baffle on the bird feeder and began feeding the squirrels on the ground and the birds, the squirrels, and the people were happy. 
 
Until the squirrels realized the food source was sitting on the front porch.  Despite the fact they had food on the ground, the squirrels became preoccupied with raiding the motherload.  We would look out the window and see them peeking over the edge of the porch to see if we were peeking out the window, and they eventually got brave enough that it didn't matter if we were watching - they would step right on up on the porch and try to be nonchalant about checking out the trash can.  And then they started going in.  Lightning got it in his head that he was going to trap one in the trash can, although I warned him he would have his hands full if it actually happened.  He very methodically began trying different techniques for rigging the latch on the trash can so the squirrel could slip in but would have more difficulty getting out, but every time he began to approach the can while the squirrel was inside, the squirrel would hear him coming and explode out of the can, sometimes knocking it over, always startling the heck out of Lightning, and invariably escaping.  I have this perpetual vision of Lightning flying off his feet, the squirrel flying out of the can, and feed basically flying everywhere.  I wonder what the birds thought as they watched it going on...
