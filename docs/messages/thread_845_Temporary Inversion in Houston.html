Temporary Inversion in Houston

From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2005-05-21 20:11:41 UTC
We weren't really planning on this one, but have created a temporary
box called Inversion. It's to go along with this great installation
that the Art League is featuring near the intersection of Montrose
and West Gray. Two artists have reconstructed two wooden bungalows
into a vortex. For more information,

www.artleaguehouston.org

To encourage or reward you for visiting, we have created a letterbox
for the spot. It's a microbox and doesn't have all the bells and
whistles, just a simple hand-carved stamp and a log book, no ff
certificate. We have posted the clues on LBNA. If people want,
we'll post them on Atlas Quest as well.

The boys really enjoyed it, both when we came across it driving up
Montrose earlier this week and today, when we took the time to
explore. If you want, you can buy a cool t-shirt in the gallery to
support the project.

The houses are slated to be torn down the first week of June, so if
you want to see something neat rather than a pile of rubble, I'd go
before then.

Dewberry of
Gramatrick

From: Hawk (omkarmashanti@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-05-23 10:37:33 UTC-07:00
The vortex is waaaaaay cool! I had seen it but had not
stopped to explore until I had the excuse of finding a
letterbox. I crawled all the way through the vortex.
If you do this, be prepared to get down on all fours,
then on your belly. You know I always have a camera
hanging around my neck, so I must have been quite a
sight! Such an undignified position for a hawk! I'm
sure Krohm got several photos of the spectacle...

We didn't see anyone collecting any money for
admission. I think it's just there for people to
enjoy.

Be sure to go into the house from the side and see the
vortex worming through the house.

Hawk
