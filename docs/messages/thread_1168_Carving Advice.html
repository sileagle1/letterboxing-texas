Carving Advice

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-08-07 12:11:55 UTC-07:00
Don't cut yourself. (just kidding - but really, don't
cut yourself!)

How about this? When you are cutting the medium, move
the medium not the knife. You will have a certain
amount of knife movement, but if you move the medium
around, it works out a little better. Also, as you
are outlining, keep the blade of the knife turned away
from the area of the image you are wanting to keep -
at about a 45 degree angle. That way, you don't cut
up under the line you want to cut away from and create
weak edges of the stamp. And keeping that angle away
is a big reason why you move the medium not the knife.

And of course, if Aisling comes on and tells you
something completely different, go with what she says.

Lucy

But anyway, Lucy's advice is excellent! I tell people to give their
cuts "strong trunks," cutting away from the line, not undercutting
which will weaken it. I also remind them that you don't usually need
to cut nearly as deep as you think, to make a line visible.
