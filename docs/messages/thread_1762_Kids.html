I have a question: Our 5 year old granddaughter wants to make and
hide a letterbox. I went into the LbNA site with the idea that I
would sign her up for an account with her own trail name, but I wanted
to use my email address and I was denied for that reason. How do you
sign up a kid who doesn't have their own email address?

Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter





Re: [letterboxingtexas] Kids

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-03-19 14:06:37 UTC-08:00
I would just set up an email account for purposes of
the child's use because apparently LBNA won't let you
record one email address for two accounts. If you do
it right, you can set it up so that the secondary
account feeds into your main account, so you don't
have to check both accounts regularly. It's been a
LONG time since I've done that though, so I don't
recall which services allow that to be done...

I'll do a little research. I'm really in the same
boat, since I first logged SJZ's early boxes in under
my address (and actually, while she carved the stamp,
hiding was a group effort, so I could justify it
then). Looking back, I would like my kids to have
their own account so they will eventually have their
own history of boxes listed as they get old enough to
venture off on their own, as SJZ has.

Lucy
