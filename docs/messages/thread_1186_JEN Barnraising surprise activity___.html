From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-08-13 14:57:37 UTC-07:00
Okay, I'm back for awhile now - won't run away again!

That IS hilarious Semalee - and do you know, I was out
and about early this morning and realized I was right
in the same area as a box I hadn't yet found. My
first thought was exactly that - call the house and
get them to read me the clues. Well - yeah, right -
6:30 a.m. - any dedicated boxer in my house woulda
been with me, since I was out placing a box as it was.
Instead, they were all snoozing away like they had
nothing to do all day! And even assuming they were
awake, they certainly weren't gonna be alert enough
for me to walk them through getting to LBNA, getting
to the clues, and finally, reading them. Sooo...I
quickly thought of a Plan B. I started examining my
cell phone to learn how to get internet access using
it - well, after a good while looking, I still
couldn't figure it out, and figured it was probably
one of those features that I would have to have turned
on by my carrier before I could use it, so I gave up
on THAT. Soooo...Plan C. My Palm Pilot - and
suddenly I realized I should've paid much better
attention when everyone was discussing how to download
clues to Palm Pilots and Blackberries and such. At
the time I was thinking that I would NEVER use my Palm
for that, so I deleted all those messages....and by
that time, I had to be home anyway, so I had to give
up and plan to come back later.

Sooo...on to Who Had Visited My Boxes: well of course
you all know! Gryzzled Gryphon! He visited my new
box, Flying Again, on August 10th, and also my sort of
a mystery box, Spring Hope, that same day. He had
missed Spring Hope when he made his pass through last
month, but I was glad to see he got it this time!

And now, for the "surprise event" - and I have to
figure out how to explain it. Awhile back, we were
talking on the list about what a shame it was that the
cookbook PLB had apparently been lost. Ruby and I got
to talking about how it would be cool to be prepared
to exchange recipes at the JEN gathering since the
lunch is going to be potluck. So how about if
everyone were to bring their recipes on cards, with
plenty of cards to go around, and on the card with the
recipe we would stamp our own stamp, so that it would
be a combination recipe exchange/stamp exchange. If
your whole family is attending and wants to exchange
this way, let each person bring their favorite recipe.
Then, for those who aren't able to attend but want to
participate, you could send your favorite recipes
ahead of time stamped and in sufficient quantities to
pass around (except don't send the prepared food -
that would be yukky by the time it got here!) and we
could do a drop off/pick up of them and send them back
to you. Kind of like a valentine exchange in school!
As a further option, after the event is over, I could
scan them into a file, combine them and create a PDF
version of a recipe book for anyone who wants it.

Lucy
