From: Maribeth Jagger (cjagger405@earthlink.net) | Date: 2006-01-25 10:33:44 UTC-06:00

Viewfinder and I spent the day yesterday letterboxing in Johnson and Ellis counties. When I got home, I had this email (below) from someone I surmise had stumbled upon one of my letterboxes in Waxahatchie! Yikes! I'm impressed that he emailed me, but I replied to him trying to give him a crash course on letterboxing and asking that he please return the original stamp to the box, but I haven't heard back! I guess when he saw all the stamps inside the logbook, he figured that's what you do, take the stamp and leave another behind? Now I need to drive 50 miles back to Waxahatchie and check on the box to make sure it was rehidden properly!
 
> I found your letter box at the park in Waxahachie in the stump in the tree. I did not remove or destoy it, but I did take the stamp and put a stamp in there. I would have written my name on it also, but I didn't have a pen and there was not a pen in the bag. You can e-mail me at cjamesjennings@yahoo.com if you have any questions. Calvan
