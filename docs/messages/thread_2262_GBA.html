From: the carrascos (a.carrasco@earthlink.net) | Date: 2006-10-23 13:08:19 UTC-05:00
Now that I have finally cleaned out my van from the weekend's festivities I thought I would put in my two cents on the gathering.  I think it was great to see everyone from far and near, my only regret is that there was not more time to visit with each one of you!  Thank you to everyone who came, I hope you enjoyed your visit to the Houston area, we love our city, and hope you did too!
 
The best part of the gathering, for me, was to see families together having so much fun.  I think the storybook walk was a great success (due, I am sure, mostly to the huge pair of underwear that housed Captain Underpants!) and I loved watching the kids squeal as they found each box.  My 4 & 5 year olds are the ones least likely to cheer when I say we are going letterboxing, but they came home from the gathering looking at their stamps and talking about all the things they had found.  I also think there is a lot to be said on the power of reading to children, I had many conversations on the Storybook walk with grownups that started out, "I LOVED this book when I was little!" 
 
The best moment of the day (except for when it was Lightning, and NOT one of my kids, who fell in the water) was hearing my little boot boy bragging about how he had met "The Real" Silver Eagle and Baby Bear.  Even daddy of five was pretty impressed with seeing them in person.  It is funny how these trail names that started out to be so mysterious three years ago when we found our first letterbox have now become household names, and wonderful how all those "letterboxing people" have now become friends.  I hope everyone had as much fun as we did, and if you know anyone who could use some XXXXXXXXL underpants, just let me know!  MO5
