From: Mike - KD9KC (mike-2007@elp.rr.com) | Date: 2007-04-08 15:11:12 UTC
We have a Garmin Nuvi 350 in the van.  What a wonderful tool.
 
When we are planning a trip, and we are picking letterboxes to go after, we read the clues and work them on a mapping program.  Once we reach the "end of the road" on the map we mark a waypoint.  When we get that done for all the letterboxes on the trip, we upload the waypoints into the Nuvi.  Then while we are driving around we just ask it to find the closest.  It gives us turn-by-turn driving directions to the parking place.
 
You would be surprised at how nice it makes it, not having to read clues or deal with maps while driving. 
 
Very often I will write the "walking part" of the clues into my Garmin GPS-MAP60CS.  It also has a compass built in, and makes it easy to go without paper blowing in the wind.
 
Last - most of you know I geocache and letterbox.  <GASP!>  And many of you have realized that where there is a letterbox, there is also a geocache, and usually for the same reasons.  Since there are fewer letterboxes in the world, we go for the letterbox first.  Then we will grab a few of the nearest geocaches too.
 
The only ones I feel sorry for are the dog show people who finish at the show and sit in the motel room watching TV the rest of the day.  We take our dogs out exploring, and we have a great time.  TV makes you old.
 
Of course, there was the trip to Amarillo where we did the Cadillac Ranch, and while looking for it the beautiful WHITE show dog ran through cow-poopie, then sat in it, and flung it all over while running through it.  Probably tasted good too.  But, as luck would have it, the next letterbox (Helium Monument) had a lot of goose-poop.  And snow.  So after the goose-poop, we took everyone to the snow.  We rubbed the white dog down with snow to clean her as best possible.  Yeah - she was shivering.  Tough!  I cleaned my dog's feet.  And we walked in the snow to clean our shoes.
 
Sooo...  tools can add to the enjoyment.  And I can't imagine doing this without the dogs!
