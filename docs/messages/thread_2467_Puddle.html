I have already been carving. I'm in a ton of PLB's. No I'm not the
best at it....lol But I do very much get happy or kinnda pat myself
on the back when I do carve. The thing of it was when I went to Hobby
Lobby there stamps are so beautiful but cost so much and there ink
also. I would love to have all colors of ink and other stuff they
have there. When I saw a box containing all I could dream of getting,
on this item 75% off I caved....lol. So when I got it home I put all
of the ink and the markers up I looked at 58 stamps and thought to
myself oh my goodness what to do with all these stamps. So before I
made up a ton of LB I thought maybe I will ask to see if there is
more leaning towards one way or the other. So before I left to plant
the reaction I got was that some would like them no matter what and
some would like it better if they were carved so I still had the
problem of what to do with all of the stamps....lol. So I just
thought I would do some one way and some the other so when I list
them as either hand carved or store bought people could make up their
own minds. So thats all I wanted was just to see before I hammered
out all of those boxes. I hope everybody will like them no matter
what. That's all I want is to continue to help plant more boxes and
tell a little story about why that stamp means something to me. I
hope we all have fun and remember .....just box!

With love to all,
Kim

From: Maribeth Jagger (cjagger405@earthlink.net) | Date: 2007-01-04 22:16:22 UTC-06:00
I have a stash of store bought stamps too. Last summer I kept coming across so many geocaches while scouting places to hide my letterboxes. Sometimes I would stamp into their logbooks and leave a message when I'd come across one, but wished I'd had something to leave in their box. Then it occurred to me that I could leave one of my little store bought stamps in them, so I started carrying them with me in my backpack for such occasions. You're very ambitious. I didn't get the nerve to place my first letterbox until I had been boxing for a year and a half! Have fun with your new supplies!
