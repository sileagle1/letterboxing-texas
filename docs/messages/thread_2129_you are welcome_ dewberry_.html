When I read dewberry's thank you this morning it made me think about how meeting other letterboxers adds a whole new dimension to the pastime.  Anyone who has had to try to motivate hot, tired, thirsty kids just a little farther down the trail knows that letterboxing with kids isn't always what it appears to be in Family Fun articles.  Then you have a great morning like today...I hauled all the kids over to Edith Moore to find Dewberry's "Mia's Grasshopper".  It was COOL out, and we had enough snacks with us, and everyone was in a good mood, we had a delightful morning.  The real Mia (my 3 year old) was very happy with her grasshopper.  Dewberry has planted several boxes with my kids in mind, as I have tried to do for her, and it makes it so much more fun when the kids are looking for a box that has clues or a stamp or a place special for them.  I encourage everyone to try to come to the Book Gathering in October, Texas letterboxers are a great bunch of people to know!  MO5
