From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-12-28 16:19:32 UTC-06:00
Hi 5monkeys,
 
I'm really not sure what you are finding on stamps, although it still sounds like ink.  If it is gold, it may well look like an acrylic paint.  I think a good way to tell would be to look in the logbook next time you see that gold on a stamp, and see if the last finder used that color to log in with.  Theoretically, someone who uses an unusual color like that should clean the stamp before they go, but that doesn't always happen.
 
As far as applying a protective coating, that's a huge NO!  Anything I can think of that you would apply for protection will also keep the stamp from taking ink.  The best bet for protecting a stamp is a gentle cleaning with water - nothing with an odor.
 
Someone else may have other thoughts?

From: Maribeth Jagger (cjagger405@earthlink.net) | Date: 2008-12-28 18:13:06 UTC-06:00
Because of the metallic properties of gold (silver, copper, etc) inks, they do look like paint. Sometimes it's hard for the next couple of people (following someone who uses a metallic ink) to get a good coverage of their ink on the stamp, so it really is nice when the person using the metallic ink cleans the stamp after they've stamped it.
