Testing the Mystery Waters

From: janet clark (jayceeinbedford@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-04-23 15:18:02 UTC-07:00

Earlier this week, my friend and coworker Wermbo was back at the nurses station working on some virtuals. She was having problems with an especially difficult one. I said I would go back to my desk and work on it. I attempted to solve it FOR AN HOUR. I gave up (for now) and looked at some other virtuals. I solved a couple. Then, I got trapped into another one FOR OVER 15 MINUTES. At that point, I walked away from my computer.

Last night, pretty much the same scenario. But, this time, some chewing out to Wermbo. I actually had a headache on a couple of those virtuals.

All the virtuals I had done in the past were simple. Read clue, google answer, plug in answer, solve. I actually thought all virtuals were that easy. Yeah, right.

So, today as I was thinking about those two unsolved virtuals I had a duh moment. It happens.

If I can do virtuals (besides those two nasty ones) I can probably attempt mystery clues. So, I'm going to dive in. What's the worst that can happen? I could actually solve some or I could go crazy and get put in a loony bin. Is that so bad? I hear you get fed 3 times a day and you get to wear your pajamas all day.

So, seeing as how nothing catastrophic could go wrong. I'm going to do it.

Wish me luck,
Janet, BF
