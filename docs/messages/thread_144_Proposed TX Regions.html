Proposed TX Regions

From: Silver Eagle (sileagle@alltel.net) | Date: 2004-05-21 04:35:04 UTC
I made a picture of what I propose for the new Regions of
Texas and put it in the Files section (txregions.jpg).
Please take a look at it and post to the list what you
think. I ended up with 7 Regions: Panhandle, North,
Northeast, Southeast, South, Central & West. I made
some hard decisions where to draw the lines: Del Rio
& San Angelo are in West, Waco is in North, Lufkin is
in Northeast. Once we agree on this I will email Wes
for the spreadsheet to fill out (thanks for the info.
from Oregon). Not sure if any of this will come to
pass but it doesn't hurt to ask. If nothing else, the
map will give you a good reference for counties in Texas.
Also, in answer to the question "Why Regions?", I do
like having groups of counties grouped into Regions
since Texas is so big, but I also would like to be
able to search for boxes by county, so I will ask for
that feature, too. Thanks for your help on this.

Silver Eagle

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-05-21 06:42:22 UTC-07:00
Silver Eagle,

Yes! What you did was exactly what I was envisioning!
It makes much more sense in a stomping-ground kind of
mentality. I think what you propose is probably what
we all consider to be more of a natural division. As
an example, the original map appears to have split
Midland and Odessa into different regions, but they
are sister cities and should definitely be together.
I don't see anything I would change about your
suggestion, although I would be mainly familiar with
your Region 3, the Northeast Region.

To the "Why Regions" question, I would love to see a
different type of search (although if the regions were
divided differently, I wouldn't necessarily feel that
way). However, whenever I try to think of what I
would prefer, I always come back to regions. Texas
has so many counties, it's hard to know more than
about four counties in any given direction from home
where different counties are. I would be more
inclined toward a town search, but then it becomes
difficult to find anything in nearby towns unless you
know specifically what towns are close by. For me,
being in a low box population area, I have to search
farther afield to go finding, so I don't necessarily
know clusters of towns. Soooo...back I come to
regions!

I admire Wes for his willingness to take on the
monumfental task of cataloging and mapping all the
boxes in the country, but I sure am glad he has opened
up the floor to address needed changes. And thanks to
you, Silver Eagle, for being willing to think on a big
scale on our behalf!

Lucy

Any Waco-ans on the list? I'm not sure I agree with the division of
North TX and Central...the line of counties along w/ McLennon
(Coryell, Mills) seem pretty well known as part of Central Texas.
Almost everything you see associated with Waco says "central Texas"
(news stations, businesses, etc). I know I wouldn't think to look
for McLennon county in north Tx.

But that's just nitpicking, feedback that you can do with what you
will! As long as I can search by county, I'm sure I can still find
what I'm looking for! It's a dilemma in this state, because even
broken into 7 regions many regions are bigger than just a day trip
from one end to the other. It's not practical to divide it into
infinite small regions, yet it's hard to remember the names of
counties in a particular region you want to visit. I think you did
a fine job with this difficult task!

Cristy
