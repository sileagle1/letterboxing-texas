The Boxalots

From: barefootlucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-06-30 01:10:31 UTC
Blue Butterfly, Lucy Locket, and I (along with their Stinky Monkey
and Roaring Lion, and my Lightning) had the opportunity to meet and
exchange with the absolutely wonderful Boxalot family as they passed
through East Texas today. Honour's mother, Linda, came along with
them to observe, and she fit right in!

It is great fun to take opportunities to meet other letterboxers,
especially those that aren't regulars in our area. The Boxalots
come back to Louisiana to visit family fairly regularly it seems, so
hopefully others will have the chance to meet them. In the
meantime, if you make it to Northwestern Louisiana, take a minute to
plant a box for them to find on a return visit - they've planted
several in the Shreveport/Bossier City area on their visits.

It's funny to me how at gatherings and even small meet-ups like this
one everyone falls in like old friends and speaks the same language
and has a high level of mutual understanding in spite of diverse
backgrounds. Even the kids - throw them together and they play like
old friends.

I'm really glad I played hookie from work for the last hour of the
day and went to Rusk to meet up with The Boxalots. What a nice
break to the summer ho-hums! I say we make 'em honorary Texans!

Letterboxers are cool!

BarefootLucy

From: The Zucco Family (gadzuke@flash.net) | Date: 2006-06-30 08:03:01 UTC-07:00
Thank all of you so much for planting these wonderful boxes in Rusk for us to find!  And for the Texas-sized hospitality!  I lurk on several area lists - and the Texas group is so active and clever.  I love to see what you all come up with - and it was a treat to realize that BarefootLucy (of the FBI) was in possession of the infamous TX Tour Bus and I could lay eyes on it.  You are all so very creative and terrific carvers....and terrific writers! 
 
On the way home last night we were all talking about how the highlight of the day was meeting BarefootLucy, LucyLocket and Blue Butterfly and their children.  Our daughter Smart Cookie was so excited to meet "other letterboxing kids!" and loved the playtime at the park. 
 
I loved hearing the stories you all shared and look forward to hearing more in the future!  It seemed like we could talk for hours.  We would love to make it to the October gathering....we'll see once we get settled in DC.  We would love to become Honorary Texans and will represent you well in the big LB world! 
 
We were humbled that you all took time off to come meet us - it was truly the highlight of our day!  We are planning on several other boxes for the Shreveport/Bossier area in the future - come visit!
 
Take care, The Boxalots
 
PS - we escaped East Texas with no snake sightings, no poison oak or ivy and 20 stamps!  A Perfect Day!!  Thanks again!
