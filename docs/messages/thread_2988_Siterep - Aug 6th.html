Siterep - Aug 6th

From: tigger32167 (tigger32167@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-08-07 04:23:05 UTC
My navigator, JR and I headed home around 7:30 am down Hwy 175 from
Dallas. Our first stop was Seagoville to pick up the GWP Series: Oh
Give Me a Home box in C.O. Bruce Central Park. The box was
safe and sound. As we were leaving the park, we saw a mommy duck
going to the pond with about 10 baby ducks all in a line. Our next
stop was the Veteran's Memorial in Kaufman where we picked up the
Celebrate Our Military Day box. We then went to Athens to pick up
some of the new boxes there. The first stop was Athens City
Cemetery where we picked up the United We Stand box and the We
Remember box which had a HH in it. Next was the Kiwanis Park where
we found the Dont Rocket til Youve Tried It box at a really neat
arbor. The playground at the park has a big rocket that the kids
can climb in which reminded me of a park in Jacksonville that used
to have a rocket that I played in when I was a kid. Our next stop
was Central Park in Athens to find the TVCC Cardinal park which we
found with no problem. We then went to the TVCC campus to find The
Cardette box which has a neat history. We then went to the
Cain Center and picked up the Texas Longhorn box. We then went back
downtown in Athens to Ginger's Park and found The Old Fiddler box.
This is a neat pocket park and a really neat stamp. We then went to
the Railroad Park and picked up Black Eyed Uncle Fletcher. I really
liked this double stamp and the park was very cute. The next stop
today was at the Oaklawn Cemetery in Athens to find the Just Call
Him Jess box in tribute to "The Law East of the Trinity." This had
a neat history to it and was a nice stamp. I then stopped at Mt
Olive Cemetery in Frankston and placed a new box for Wag Time. Then
headed home after 6 days of marathon letterboxing...at the end of
the day I had found 70 boxes and 3 hitchhikers. Not a bad week!!!
