Actually, that isn't strictly true, at least not yet.   SOME national forests have regulations regarding geocaching/letterboxing and others have specifically allowed it, even without a permit.  However, most national forests simply don't address it and it falls under the general use requirements for the forest - i.e. no activities allowed that cause harm, no commercial use, - blah, blah, blah. 
 
It seems like the only consistently consistent guideline is that gc's and lb's aren't allowed in wilderness areas of national forests, which makes perfect sense to me.
