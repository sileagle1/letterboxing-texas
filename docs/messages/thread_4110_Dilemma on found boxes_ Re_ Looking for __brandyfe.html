Interesting. I decided I would do a little digging about brandyfern to see what I could find.

First off, the last time she logged on to AQ was February of this year and the last time she logged on to LBNA was April of 07. They joined this list in April of 07, but have never posted and are set to nomail, so they aren't receiving these messages either.

I'm guessing you tried to contact brandyfern through AQ. They have a different address on LBNA, so you could try contacting them through their box listings there.

Three months isn't a terribly long time for someone not to log on to one of the websites. And a week isn't a real long time for someone not to respond to an email. It appears they are a teacher in Dallas, so I wouldn't be surprised if they don't get more responsive after school is out.

If it were me, I would try to contact them through LBNA and again through AQ. Set the book aside. Then try them again in a month or so. If you don't get a response then, do what you want (but personally, I wouldn't mind just setting it off to the side for a few months).

The standard time before a box is considered abandoned is one year on both AQ and LBNA. If brandyfern doesn't log on in that length or respond to emails, you might consider adopting the boxex. In the meantime, you've done a good deed and you should feel really good about that!

Beach Bum,

You know, I see where you are coming from about someone thinking you had stolen the logbook, but I don't think that should be a real worry. One thing I have noticed over the years is that there is a basic assumption of trustworthiness among letterboxers. Of course, there are exceptions to every rule, but by and large, letterboxers (I'm talking about folks who stick with it, not necessarily those who try it for a day or two and lose interest - I don't think they get a firm enough grasp to understand the forward impact of their actions) care what they are doing and try to do right.

So much of what happens while we're out boxing is judgment and the best we can do is put ourselves in the shoes of the placer, and to a lesser extent of future finders, and ask ourselves what the best course of action would be.

Say, I see you are signed up for the Tomato Fest Challenge - I'm looking forward to meeting you!

BarefootLucy
