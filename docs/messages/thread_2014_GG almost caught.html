GG almost caught

From: raininknd42 (raininkatzndogs@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2006-07-29 04:41:04 UTC
I am fairly new to boxing and to this group but have managed to
introduce this pasttime to a neighbor and coworker. After losing my
first planted box I was hesitant to try again. But try again I did.
First I planted it and left it out for a week and checked back on it
twice (once following a severe storm) to see if it would remain prior
to posting the clues. I finally post the clues and advised my
neighbors and coworkers of the plant. It seemed the race was on to
see which of the new boxers could get to it before Grizzled Gryphon.
Of course the neighbors managed to get there first because the
coworkers had to work. I was off to Florence and Killeen to find
those boxes with my frequent trail mates (Tx Dragonfly and Crazy bout
dragons)with the plan that tx dragonfly would go find my new box on
the way home. The coworker is stuck at work and chomping at the bit
to get out there. So after work she sets out with another coworker
to see if it can be located. This same coworker was the one that
notified me of my lost Heritage Park box via cell phone. Well
shortly after work she begins her trek and shortly after that I get a
call from a cell phone saying that once again my box is missing. I
told her she was jinxing my boxes because it was there just a short
time ago as Tx dragonfly had just found it. About 15 minutes later
the phone rings again and she informs me that upon returning to her
vehicle there is a note posted stating "That which you seek will be
returned to its location in 30 minutes". Well I get excited because
who else would it be but the legendary GG. The coworker goes off to
find a different box with the goal of coming back shortly. I also
cautioned them that they were not being secretive enough and needed
to be much more discreet. A short time later I get another phone
call and all they could say was "It was Him!!, It was Him!!". Now I
am inspired to plant more boxes and see if we can lure the legendary
GG out of his lair.
