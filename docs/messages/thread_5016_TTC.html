There is nothing so sad as an out-of-work clown.  When the Texas Traveling Circus played the Bell Motel the first time,  the direction I sent them in left them out of work for weeks, until I took pity on them and moved the show to a grassier lot.  I couldn’t let that happen again, so I booked them for a return engagement at the Bell Motel.  However, this time, I wish I had a team of Percherons with me to help me move the show.  The cast has expanded dramatically!  There are now 42 performers on this circus, and it has outgrown it’s original quarters.  The show may not need to retire, but the cast is certainly plenty large.  Shall we limit the show to its current cast of characters?  Should it continue to tour, or should it become an Event Box?  Let’s hear from the Peanut Gallery.
So, how about a Texas Rodeo?  Do you think we could come with enough ideas to keep a rodeo tour going?
-- Bell Motel
 
P.S.  I did pay tribute to the Jackal with Pieces of Eight, in order to ransom the Circus.

1. The box comes to the next TALE where anyone who wants to sit at the table and round-robin the stamps can do that, thereby making me, -- er them -- happy.
2. Whomever has it last (Mosaic Butterfly?) takes it, thereby stamping it at her leisure, and afterward, possibly with assistance, she or whomever she designates, plants each of the stamps separately, preferably in one geographic location, thereby "seeding" a deserving barren area with a Circus Retirement and Assisted Living Settlement, thereby giving the carver credit on the listing and thereby making the one stamp=one box folks happy. Clues are made WOM and are given only to those who agree to box maintenance, including logbook & container replacement.
3. Willing participants volunteer either to help plant, or contribute a nicely camo'd l'n'l or 2 to facilitate the process. Um. That could even be a gathering, right?
4. Alternately, after attending TALE (pleeeeeze!), the stamps could be divvied up and planted all over the place, with dibs going to the folks who carved them in the first place.
5. Things continue as they are, with no new stamps allowed, only instead of finding the box's hiding place, it's handed off directly, from person-to-person.
6. It could show up at a pre-gathering dinner event. Preferably someplace with beer. Just sayin'
