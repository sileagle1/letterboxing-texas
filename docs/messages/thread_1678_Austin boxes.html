Clues for Eoghan's boxes in Bastrop and Buescher State Parks.

Note: O-Knot #2 is gone as it was destroyed by fire. O-Knot #3 and 4
were there in September 2005 as well as Lost Pines.

Lost Pines Letterbox
---------------------------------------------------------------------
-----------

Treasure Type: Letterbox
Location: Buescher State Park
Near: Smithville, TX
Placed on: 11/04/00
Terrain difficulty: ( for shortcut)
Overall difficulty:

Directions: Begin the hike on the Buescher Hiking Trail, across from
the Lakeview walk-in camping area. After 2.2 miles, take the left
path towards Park Road 1C (to the Pine Gulch trail). You can also
begin the hike here by parking your car on the side of the road. The
trail crosses the road at 0.3 miles past the UT Cancer Research
Center entrance. Approximately a third of a mile after the trail
crosses the road, there is a small wooden foot bridge over an
arroyo. From the bridge go 30 to 35 steps back down the trail to the
south. The letterbox is located approximately 30 feet up the slope
of the hill beneath a small pile of rocks.


O-Knots
---------------------------------------------------------------------
-----------

Treasure Type: Letterbox series
Placed on: 11/10/01
State: Texas
County: Bastrop
Nearest Town: Bastrop
Park: Bastrop State Park
Terrain difficulty:
Overall difficulty:

Clues:
To locate the four O-knots hidden in this park you will need to
purchase ($3) an orienteering map from the park headquarters when
you enter the park. There are two maps available; buy the one that
shows the numbered locations of the permanent control points. Each
of the letterboxes contains a circular ('O') celtic knot stamp and
is located near one of the permanent orienteering ('O') control
markers shown on the map. (One of the markers is shown in the
picture to the right.) Each box is in the base of a tree trunk at
the bearing and distance from the control marker shown on the table
below. Also indicated is the type of tree or other clue that will
help you locate each box.

Knot # Control # Bearing Distance Location
1 87 240 90' in base of dead pine tree
2 29 74 85' in base of small oak tree
3 71 180 50' in east end of rotting log
4 91 346 20' double-trunk oak at top of ditch,
beneath rocks

From: Carolyn Nelson (cancan1959@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-10-17 21:27:08 UTC-05:00

Downtown Austin Boxes:

1) The Mexican Bat-Retrieve during the day (too too crowded to get in the early eve) but go back at night to see the bats. Major construction going on right now in the immediate vicinity.

Ask at the Marriott about the free Dillo bus and get a map from them or the Capital Metro website (http://www.capmetro.org/riding/routes_400.asp)

2) San Jacinto Hall
3) Hook' em
On the UT Campus-Check out the wonderful exhibits at the Harry Ransom Center and the Cactus Cafe for music in the evening.  Visit the beautiful capital and grounds (free tours) on the way to or from.

An absolute must is a visit to Zilker Park (http://www.ci.austin.tx.us/zilker/) accessible via the dillo..swim in Barton Springs, take the mini Zilker Zephyr train, visit Umlauf Sculpture Gardens, Zilker Botanical Gardens or the Austin Nature & Science Center. 
4) Mr. Secretary: Texas Governor Series
5) Endangered Species Series of Texas: Barton Springs Salamander
6) Endangered Species Series of Texas: Tooth Cave Trio
7) USA Flag 2
8) City Streets: Quilt Block Series

BTW: Make sure you eat at Shady Grove located on the right side of Barton Springs just before you get to Zilker...the best onion rings and Bloody Mary's.  Eat outside; this IS how Austinites like to relax.

Enjoy Austin..I've only lived here two years, but it is a great city.  We are the "Live Music Capital of the World"

Kind Regards,
Cancan
