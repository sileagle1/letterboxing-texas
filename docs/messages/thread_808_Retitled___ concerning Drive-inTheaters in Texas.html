From: Carol Lemons (lemonscl@houston.rr.com) | Date: 2005-04-19 20:14:32 UTC-05:00
And, if there were no books on Drive-Ins, I would have called the librarian, or other fount of information, in every county in the state, to get the information. Anyone know how many counties there are in Texas? Yellow Pages and White Business Pages would also have to be checked. Then, because word-of-mouth and phone books can be very unreliable, I would have had to travel to each site to verify the information.
 
Remember time before personal computers? I bought one of the first Apples off the line, in 1976 or 1977....... so long ago now, I can't remember. Anyone remember punching cards to run programs? The card reader spitting the cards out all mangled and mixed up? One t.v. in the house, which your parents forbid you to touch? A hi-fi instead of a stereo? Convenience foods were new and viewed with distrust (what self-respecting woman would serve her family cake made from a box?!) and drive-through food was almost nonexistent. Girls were not permitted to take shop; they had to take typing and home ec. There were almost no FM radio stations, only 3 tv stations, no cds, no dvds, even 8-track tapes hadn't been invented yet. Man, how did we survive?!
 
JOY

Remember having a party line? And your exchange was
not numbers, but a word. My phone number was "Temple
8-3026", or "TE8-3026", which later morphed into the
common: "838-3026". My grandmothers didn't even have
to dial the exchange to talk to anyone in the county.
They just dialed the last four digits! I didn't know
anyone that had more than one phone in their house.

I still have and use an old rotary-dial phone. It's
the most reliable phone in the house.

Yes, and we always had to be careful not to tie-up the line or my mother would have kittens!  And calling long-distance?!
