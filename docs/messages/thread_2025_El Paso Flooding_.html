El Paso Flooding.

From: Michael P. Olbrisch (mike2004@elp.rr.com) | Date: 2006-08-04 22:49:56 UTC

I guess a lot of you have heard the news about the flooding in El Paso.  Yeah - its true.  Some of it was bad.  Surprisingly - the worst of it seems to have been on the mountain side, NOT in the low areas.

We are living on an island.  The water in out street would have needed to get 2 ft deeper to have come in the house.  The back yard was a puddle, but drained well into the street.  1/2 mile away the roads were impassible.  I saw about 6 inches of the top of one of the orange traffic barrels sticking up in what was supposed to be the middle of the street.  I went a different way.

I left the jeep in the garage and used the van.  It sits higher than most pick-ups, so it fords well, and has a real clear view of the road ahead.  One thing people never consider when fording it your fan.  When it hits the water, it will pull itself forward into your radiator.  This is why electric fans are so popular in jeeps - you can turn it off for fording.  The van has the IC-901A on 144, 220, 440, and 1200 - so it has good commo with the house all around town.

We picked up about 50 sandbags - just in case - to block the doors etc if we need to.

The newest danger in is Juarez.  There is a dam there that was near to bursting.  The Army corp. of engineers went in to Mexico to assess it.  Not good.  The city implemented mandatory evacuation for the areas down-stream of it.

Bottom line - we are safe.  Other than a few leaks discovered in the roof, nothing to cry about.  We have a pretty complete bug-out kit for disasters, and it is ready to go.  Load it and the dogs, and hit the road.
