Hmmm.  Well.  I will say that owning a gun isnt for everyone.  It is a very serious step in so many ways.

#1.  It doesnt make you invincible.  It only gives you an equal chance against similarly armed bad-guys.  It doesnt mean you will win or even survive.  It just gives you a chance.

#2.  It puts a very serious responsibility on your shoulders.  You must think about every moment.  Sure if family is threatened you will act.  But what about if you are sitting in a restaurant and some guy walks in waving a gun and shouting, but isnt a direct threat to you or your family?

#3.  You must be aware that the very moment you add a gun to your daily routine, some other things become more difficult.  Like going into school to get the kids.  Entering the post office.  You must be very aware of the law.

#4.  You MUST understand that ANY confrontation you enter into can escalate into a deadly encounter.  You must make every effort do stay disengaged.  Leave rather than argue.  Argue rather than fight.  Fight rather than shoot.  But once fully engaged you must be willing to use your gun.  Unarmed, you might get your butt kicked.  If you pull a pistol and dont use it, it might get taken from you and used anyway.  Things you should do anyway become a must.  Like looking for all the exits in a restaurant so you can escape without problems etc

#5.  If you use your pistol for any reason, no matter how lawful and justified.  You WILL be arrested.  Think of this.  If a cop were around, you would not have needed to defend yourself.  So you shot someone trying to beat your kid or whatever.  The cops arrive 7 minutes later.  There is a dead body on the floor, and you are standing over it with a smoking pistol in your hands.  You will be arrested and handcuffed on the spot.  Your pistol will be confiscated as evidence, and you may or may not be taken to jail depending on the stories of the witnesses.  Most cops are not dumb, and many support armed citizens.  Still, you and your family will have months of stress, lots of $$$$ spent, and although the grand jury may not indict you, the family of the one you shot may bring civil charges on you that you have to defend against after the criminal charged are dropped.

I am not trying to scare you.  But you do need to consider it all the way through.

That said, I rarely leave home without my pistol.  It is better to be tried by 12 than carried by 6.  The stores I frequent - most have signs in the windows saying that gun owners are welcome.  The owners get it that no guns allowed signs tell the criminals that they will have the upper hand in that store.  I do not go to my favorite Starbucks as much as I used to.  The new manager knows about us, and he insists on giving us our beverages FREE while we sit there and talk he feels safer knowing we are there.  I feel guilty taking free coffee.

In over 10 years of carrying legally I have only drawn my pistol twice.  Both times were hiking.  Once in the high-forests of New Mexico (I was hiking alone) I saw a bear right on the trail as I came around a big rock.  The sun was in my eyes and the bear was in a deep shadow.  The bear was standing, upper paws outstretched.  I drew my pistol and started backing up.  The bear didnt move.  So I looked closer.  It was not a bear it was an 8 ft tall burned out tree.  It took a while before my heart got back to normal on that one.

Another time we were miles up a trail and I and my handgun instructor encountered 4 guys coming down.  They had no gear.  They demanded our water.  Had they asked, we would have shared a little.  But they demanded.  There were 4 of them and two of us, and they began surrounding us as though they were going to take the water.  Not.  Larry pulled his 1911 .45, and I pulled my Titanium .357.  I explained to them that they might get the water, but it wouldnt stay in.  Whey shouted all sorts of obscenities, but they went down the trail.  Once they were gone and we were certain we were secure, we cell-phoned the rangers to pick them up at the bottom.  At four against two, we would have been justified had it come to a fight.

Very often (they say 95% of the time) just drawing your weapon stops the confrontation.  But you cannot count on that.

Your number one defense is awareness.  If you see something that doesnt feel right, go the other way.  Avoiding trouble is the best defense.

Wow you got me started.  <Grin>. Hope I didnt scare you.  If you do decide to get a pistol, take the classes.  Learn the law.  And learn to be aware and avoid trouble.  It is more a lifestyle change than anything else.  Let me know if I can help.

Understood.  A gun isn’t for everyone.

 

On a lesser scale, you might consider MACE brand pepper FOAM.  In Texas that is considered simple assault.  If you would be justified in using your hands to defend yourself you would be equally justified in using pepper foam.

 

I like the foam because it squirts out like a shaving cream, not a mist that can blow in the wind.  And like shaving cream, it expands behind glasses etc.  It also stains the target with a UV dye for later police identification.

 

I actually carry pepper foam too, and if there is time to decide and it is appropriate, that would be my defensive choice of first response.  The pepper foam is on my weak side, the pistol on my strong side.  Reaching for one does not prevent me from reaching for the other is you get my meaning.

 

I dislike contact weapons such as stun guns and knives.  You have to make contact for them to work.  If they are in range, so are you.  Stand-off defense is a better choice.

 

NOTE – I have never tried the pepper foam on a snake.  I wouldn’t do that to Rocky.
