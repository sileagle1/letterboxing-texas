I was trying to restrain myself, but since PuddleSplasher brought it up anyway... I still burn over that geocache-letterbox incident to which she referred.  My little letterbox had been well-hidden in its location for a couple of years at the time (a very small and personally-meaningful letterbox in a really good location, by the way, not that it matters).. and I discovered that great honkin' geocache planted right on top of my little letterbox -- and I do mean great honkin' -- it was about the size of a double-shoebox, bright plastic, highy visible even from a distance, nothing subtle about it nor its placement).  I did contact the placer and let him know I already had a small letterbox in that exact place and asked him to please move his letterbox at least a short distance away, not in the precise same location as my letterbox, and the response was: move your letterbox; my geocache stays.  And while I fumed about it, the Great Honker consumed my little letterbox, literally swallowed it right up (I guess Great Honkers eat Lesser Honkers).  Well, that is, when I did go to move the letterbox to protect it, I discovered my logbook inside the geocache box, but of course the stamp was gone, apparently as a "prize" for a geocacher.  So that little box was never replaced.  And I still burn to think of the absolute rudeness of the geocacher, who clearly didn't know how to play his game either.  Good to hear your experience was at least a little more friendly even if it was still (in the words of Tommy Lee Jones) "what I consider Rude Behavior!"
