From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-12-28 20:30:47 UTC-08:00
As I mentioned in my post I sent a few moments ago
(chiming in on the "safe bats" thing), Lightning and I
spent our day in Rusk checking on boxes there and
planting our scout series and a couple of the festival
series that I should've gotten planted long ago.

First of all, we were not able to check the two boxes
at The Footbridge - well, we probably could've, but I
know they've been doing maintenance on the bridge so
there were workers around. We found those a couple of
months ago and they were in great shape, so we'll wait
until after the work is done before we check on them
again.

Information on specific boxes follows.

Our first stop was at Jim Hogg State Historical Site,
where we checked two boxes and planted our six box
Scout Training Series. I'll probably drop the
"training" out of that title, and I'm going to post
that series a little differently than a standard box.
It's geared toward Cub Scouts, although Girl Scouts
and Boy Scouts will enjoy it also, as will plain ol'
boxers. I'm going to provide a packet of information
to leaders who want to take their dens boxing and
include in it the clues and instructions for leaders
to cover as well as things for them to watch for as
they take their boys boxing. My son stepped off the
steps so that they will be done in boy steps (in other
words, watch out grown ups - the step counts will be
really short compared to yours!) and I've included
compass readings, visual landmarks, steps, and one box
that is just plain easy but it will require stealth.
I'll be posting clues for the series somewhere other
than LBNA, but I'll put an announcement and an
invitation to request additional information on LBNA.
Once I have it all sorted out, I'll post a link here
and I'll also request input on the information I'll be
providing to scout leaders. I hope to hand stuff out
on the series at Roundtable next Thursday, so
hopefully I'll get all this done in the next day or
two.

After our encounter with the creepy dude and our
ambitious box-planting foray at Jim Hogg, we didn't
get to spend as much time as we wanted at Rusk State
Park. We got over there about 4:00 p.m. and went
right over to check on Silver Eagle's Texas RR 2 and
then planted one of the festival boxes I had in my
possession. We cruised around the camping area and
tried to figure out where our group pavilion will be,
but weren't sure exactly. If it's where I think it
is, there is a lot of group camping around it that
would be cool for anyone who wants to group up with
other letterboxers. I'll check that next time I make
it down that way. It was getting late fast, so we
headed to the nature trail and planted another
festival box we had, and meanwhile checked up on Birds
of a Feathers' Crowin' bout the Railroad. I love
those clues! We were stamping into the logbook and
checking out the book when suddenly it was as though
someone started shutting off the lights. We buttoned
the box back up and replaced it, mindful we needed to
get back to the road before it got real dark - and we
ALMOST made it!

The good news is - every single box we checked on was
rehidden well. And every box except my own was in
very good condition. Sure was a nice day and we got
in lots of good hiking, him with his new backpack and
me with new hiking shoes. Oh yeah, and we were amazed
by all the PURPLE leaves! Pretty...

Lucy

SPECIFIC BOX STATUS:
HOGG-We found Hogg with no problem - it's in great
shape and has had quite a few visitors. Box was nice
and dry and the stamp is still in great condition,
even being nearly three years old! While this was one
of our first boxes we ever found, I took an imprint
anyway just because I wanted to see how a three year
old stamp carved on a cheap eraser would stamp, and it
looks great!

STARRY, STARRY NIGHT -this box was pretty soggy. I've
been migrating to better containers and this one is a
testament to why that's a good idea. Although the box
was closed well, it had a small crack in the edge of
the lid and apparently that was sufficient to let a
lot of water in. It was also one of the last boxes I
left an ink pad in and I am now a firm believer in
leaving ink out of boxes! Fortunately, the pad was
separately bagged, so all I had to do was lift the
nasty mess out of the box in its bag, and drop it into
another bag in my backpack. The stamp was also really
wet, but I dried it off as best I could and I'm going
to watch the wood backing to see how badly it warps.
The logbook, thankfully, was dry as a bone. I'll
replace the container and possibly the stamp backing
in March at gathering time, if not before, so need for
anyone to worry about it if they find that box before
I get back to it.

TEXAS RR 2 - Box is in great shape. It's seen a fair
number of visitors, so I bet the logbook is enough for
any traffic between now and the gathering, but it
won't last through the gathering.

CROWIN' ABOUT THE RAILROAD-We caught this one as the
sun was rapidly dropping on us! I still LOVE the
clues for this one and was glad to find the box in
very good condition. Quite a few visitors so far.
The first time I visited it, I had grabbed the wrong
sig stamp, so I corrected it today.
