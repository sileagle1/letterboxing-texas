strange letterboxing day

From: Sandy Milfeld (pugapalooza@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2009-05-23 22:12:33 UTC-05:00
We had a really weird letterboxing day last week. We went out to find a box near Eagle Mountain Lake. We were walking down the trail, and saw this little deer standing in the path.   I started walking up to him slowly, expecting him to bolt, but he started trotting right toward us!  He walked right up to me and started rubbing his head on me!  We petted him for a while and he followed us all the way to the box location. There was a couple sitting nearby, so we left, with the intention of hitting another box and coming back.
 
We headed up to New Fairview to find Howling, a box that hadn't been found in almost two years, and only once (By Gryzzled Gryphon, of course).  I expected it to be missing, but it was right where it was suppose to be, in pristine condition.  (Y'all get up there and find this lonely box!!)  While we were there, this little cotton tail just hopped right past us...not scared at all. 
 
When we got back to Eagle Mountain Lake and found the first box, the deer was at the entrance of the park.  We sat with him for a while, and then headed to the box.  We found it, after some trouble. I picked it up and it started sloshing, so I knew something wasn't right. Sure enough, it was FULL of water. The logbook was soaked, the felt around the stamp was soaked...it was bad. I sat at the table to evaluate the situation, and looked down to see that someone had written a tiny message into the table; "A restless wind blows through a letterbox." Much to my chagrin, my husband wrote back, "and now it's full of calm waters."
I normally don't condone vandalism, but it was poignant.
