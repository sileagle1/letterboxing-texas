Here come a bunch of ifs. . .

If the letterboxes are listed on AtlasQuest and
if the listers have filled in the address (an option that only became
available 6 months ago), you can search by a street address and get
the closest ones.
Atlasquest is now linked to Google maps, so after performing a search
request, you can clink on the maps link and see where the lbs are.

On the other hand,
if the letterboxers have given the okay to birds of a feather, they'll
be graphically represented on his Texas Letterboxing Atlas.

This of course doesn't include mystery boxes. You'll want to figure
those out. I see reports all the time of people who have been VERY
close to one of my boxes, but not aware of it.

Dewberry covered boxes on AQ, but as far as LBNA is concerned, there is one main IF:
 
IF the park name is listed in the box clues (which it should be unless it is a mystery box), you can search on LBNA by (word/phrase in clue) and put the park name in the blank by that name.  However, this is a long and laborious search if you don't narrow it down a bit by state and city.
