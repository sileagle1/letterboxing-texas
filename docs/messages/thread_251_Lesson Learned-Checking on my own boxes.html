Lesson Learned-Checking on my own boxes

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-08-08 10:03:50 UTC-07:00
I made a spur of the moment decision to go hiking at
Fairfield State Park last Sunday. I couldn't convince
anyone else in the family to go, so I just jumped in
the car to go hike a little and check on my Fairfield
box before I changed my mind.

Well, I got to Fairfield and got out of the car at the
trail head. It was HOT!!! I hiked on in, determined
to ignore the heat. About halfway to Fairfield, I
realized I had forgotten my clues. Well, it was my
own box, so I figured I would surely remember where I
put it! HA!

I put on my best hot face and hiked on in. We had
severe storms since I was there in May, so the terrain
looked much different! Lots of downed trees across
the path, for one thing. The trail was also overgrown
with summer foliage in some places for another. I
realized, as I hiked through knee-high weeds next to
the lake by myself (translate that to hoping I didn't
get bit by a snake), that I had never been to
Fairfield in the hot summer. I got to the bench I had
planted the box near and turned to look in the
direction it would be - only to see lots of debris. I
knew I had hidden it near a fallen log, but which of
the dozen or so logs in sight was it? I searched
around for a bit, with no luck. Of course, not having
my clues I realized I could well have been wrong on
any of several points, OR the box was gone, OR ...

As it turns out, I was in the complete wrong place -
the wrong bench. I had remembered the clues
incorrectly, so I searched in vain for about a half an
hour.

It was a nice idea that I would remember where I
planted my box, but obviously that was a bad
assumption, so next time I will definitely remember to
take my own clues! Now I wonder if I would've
recognized my kids when they were babies, if I had had
to pick them out of a group of babies??? So, does
this make me a bad box mom or just a wiser boxer today
than I was last week?

Anyway, I'll throw that out for anyone else who may
consider checking their own boxes without clues. May
you be the better finder than I was if you try it!

Lucy
