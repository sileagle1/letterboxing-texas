Big Bend Road Trip

From: (blhjrh@aol.com) | Date: 2007-03-20 13:49:45 UTC-04:00

I've just returned from a road trip to far, far West Texas during Spring Break last week with my LSS, during which I did manage just a little letterboxing.   We drove to Marfa, a very long drive west from Ft. Worth, and stayed at the historic and lovely old Hotel Paisano, built in the 1930’s and beautifully restored.  This hotel was the headquarters for the cast of the 1956 movie, Giant, but I’m pretty sure our small historic room wasn’t the same room that either Elizabeth Taylor, Rock Hudson, or James Dean stayed in then! Still, it inspired my newest letterbox:  Giant.

 

We had expected it to be really hot out there, so only took light jackets, T shirts, shorts and jeans and hiking boots, but no sweat shirt or long sleeves – mistake!  The weather was very chilly our first day there, which we spent driving the scenic drive and a little hiking in the Davis Mts  State Park and at the McDonald Observatory.  I know my LSS’s letterboxing limit, but still, we just happened to find ourselves at the top of the mountain on Skyline Drive, where, after a short and scenic hike along the ridge, Silver Eagle's McDonald Observatory letterbox is located.  Great location, nice stamp.  Thanks, SE.

 

Then a little later, we just happened to drive right by the roadside pullout near the McDonald Observatory where Star Seeker’s beautiful Jupiter letterbox is located, an easy drive-by with an absolutely beautiful stamp. Thanks, Star Seeker! Love those easy finds and great stamps when I’m on vacation with my LSS.   

 

We made it back to Marfa that evening and drove out of town a few miles to the spot where people gather to look across the flats toward the mountains hoping to see the mystery lights, and we got out there with our pizza and cokes well before sunset.  No one else was out there yet, so I easily found the Astro D’s Marfa letterbox, too!  I mean, it was just there, and we were just there, so it was another easy find and a fun stamp.  I was pretty excited about it: 3 LBs on the first day, but decided to cool it on the LBing, not wanting to push the LSS’s letterboxing limit . 

 

Just after dusk, we actually SAW the Marfa mystery lights - incredible!  Big points of light just popping up, moving around, changing colors, disappearing and reappearing across the desert and mountains: pale and bright, yellow, orange, blue, small and large.  The first one I saw, sort of faint, brought me right up on my toes, and we stood transfixed for about an hour, wrapped in a quilt to keep from freezing and watching the lights appear and dance around and disappear and reappear in different places in the desert. Fantastic! 

 

The next day was sunny and hot with brilliant blue skies and enormous puffy clouds when we drove down to Big Bend and the Chisos Mts. basin We stopped at a roadside pullout to take pictures of the Rio Grande and the canyons (later realized that was the location of another one of Silver Eagle's LBs, but didn’t realize it at the time – darn!), and then we happened upon the Contrabando movie set along the river - took lots of pictures, explored, watched the river rafters floating by beneath the towering mountains.  Just beautiful – the river, canyons, cholla, ocotillo and prickly pear, old buildings that looked like the background of every old western.  I could just see Tommy Lee Jones and Robert Duvall there by the adobe church or saloon. 

 

In Chisos Basin, we hiked a short hike to photograph The Window - the opening in the basin with a long-distance view across the desert, then drove back the way we came, along the Rio Grande River Road to Lajitas, now an expensive upscale resort, stopping for more pictures, through Terlingua and Presidio and back to Marfa.  Saw lots and lots of pronghorn antelope, some bighorn sheep, several roadrunners, donkeys in the desert, goats and sheep, deer, some camels - and lots and lots of blooming yucca! 

 

One definite shortcoming in Marfa is the lack of places open for dinner on a Monday or Tuesday night, other than the lovely Jett Rink Bar and Grill in the Paisano (where we did enjoy a long slow dinner Sunday night), but after our long drive to and from Big Bend, we didn't want to wait until 8:30 or 9:00 for dinner that evening, so it was the DQ.  Those chili cheese dogs were good, too!  And the stars above us were amazing.

 

The next morning after breakfast at Alice's restaurant, about the only place open for breakfast in Marfa, we located a site for my Giant letterbox- not easy to do in Marfa because the entire city is so clean and neat, all the cedars are nicely trimmed and clipped, the historic buildings – now mostly art galleries – are exceptionally well maintained, but we found a good place. We then went back up to Ft. Davis and hiked around on the trails above the old historic fort (after making dinner reservations for that night so we'd have a choice besides DQ).  We climbed very steep rocky trails to the top of the hills above the fort (we were passed by a troop of boy scouts from San Antonio when I had to stop to breathe), hiked the mountain trail, walked all over the fort – great desert air and exercise! Then back to Marfa for dinner at the Blue Javelina (great place, very hip, modern art contemporary, incredible food, SLLLLLOOOOOOWWWWW kitchen! But definitely worth the wait). 

           

Thursday we left Marfa and drove to Alpine for breakfast and then on toward home.   We saw many more pronghorn antelope - even saw a big coyote chasing one but lost sight of them in a little brushy draw so we’ll never know who won; lots of sheep, goats, donkeys, even zebra and game antelope and bison on what was apparently a game ranch. In Ft. Stockton we detoured to find Paisano Pete’s statue and take his picture, and drove around the historic district and the fort, and then realized later, Silver Eagle had letterboxes right there, too, which again I didn’t realize at the time.  Still, I'd found 3 good ones and planted one of my own, so I was satisfied and my LSS wasn't burned out on letterboxing this trip.   It was a great road trip, relaxing, good exercise, good food, wildlife and scenery, history, and 3 cool letterboxes!
