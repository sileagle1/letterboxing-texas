From: celticlions (celticlions@comcast.net) | Date: 2005-08-01 08:56:49 UTC-05:00
To my amazement, we have already received orders for 30 patches, so ordering
100 patches will not be as much of a stretch as I thought!
TIME: I am corresponding right now with two companies, both of whom say they
can turn the finalized order around in 14 days.
SIZE: One company recommends enlarging the patch from 2 inches to 2 1/2
inches, so the lettering will be clearer.
EMBROIDERY: Both companies state we need to use at least 75 % embroidery
(rather than the 50% I first received quotes on). But in checking archives,
I see that Lucy may have been pursuing 100% embroidered patches. This means
that everything, including the orange background would be embroidery.
BACKING: I'm thinking about dropping the option of self-stick backing, since
the patches don't really self-stick permanently, and those of us who have
done scouting remember that it doesn't take a whole lot of skill to sew on a
patch!

Bottom line: if anyone has strong opinions on any of the above decisions
about the patches, please email me offlist at:
celticlions@comcast.net

Otherwise, I'm going to just try to figure out an acceptable patch for well
under $2.00 apiece and get them ordered!!!
BTW I think these will sell like the proverbial hotcakes if I bring them to
our barn-raising, so if you want to reserve patches, please send me an order
now offlist.
Thanks!

RT
