Bunny,
Here is my two cents (on a soapbox) re: the subject of advanced
stamps.

If you are like some here there is joy in the journey and there is
excitement in the discovery. Don't worry if your stamps aren't a
work of art to you. You made the, it is a creation and there is
reason behind the creation.

How exciting it must have been for the four teenagers who accidently
found the cave paintings at Lascaux in France.

I'm sure the artist who painted the dead man was criticized that the
head on the stick body was not just like a human. The picture is a
representation and this scene has an emotional impact even these many
years later.

Though most of the pictures and engravings are of animals it is the
only human figure mentioned that is in the cave. Thank goodness this
inclusion was made - Otherwise we'd have people saying aliens planted
the paintings and engravings.

If you explore the site you will also see "quadrangular signs",
or "blazons". Very much like a nine patch quilt block that early
americans used. Not much to them other than a 3 x 3 block pattern.
The beauty was created when they were patterend and repated and
colored. The early American quilters repeated this pattern of a much
earlier era, with great impact, particulary the Amish quilters. Just
maybe this pattern is a part of the collected unconcious that lies
whitin all of us.

So my advice is carve and don't worry about critics, someone will be
just as excited to find your stamp as the scientists were glad the
teenages shared their discovery with the rest of the world.

To see the Lascaux cave painting of the dead man: Cut and paste the
following link to your web browser.

http://www.culture.gouv.fr/culture/arcnat/lascaux/en/

As far as Art Critics - An early scene in Mel Brooks "History of the
World Part 1" - I'm sure the same result will not happen to your
stamp creation. I hope we a more advanced than that!

Regards,
tombned1
