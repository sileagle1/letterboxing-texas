From: (blhjrh@aol.com) | Date: 2007-06-27 18:26:58 UTC-04:00
Yep, that's the one.  I was with 2 of my 3 little granddaughters, both wearing their Hello Kitty pink backpacks for the adventure, hopping across the creek on the big stones, tripping along the walls near the creek, having a wonderful time.  Found the box OK, took it away to a nearby location to stamp in, then when we went to return the box, a maintanince man on a big mowing machine apparently saw us replacing it under its hidey-place.  So he picked it up and turned it in to the UTA campus police as a suspicious package., possibly terrorist-related   The police read the notes insides, looked it all up on the website and read about letterboxing, thought it was really cool, contacted the placer, and told him he could put it back with their permission.  The placer contacted me and we laughed about the Hello Kitty Terrorist Cell operating near the UTA campus: a grandmother and two little girls in pink Hello Kitty gear.  Pretty scary, all right!  I'm even more careful now to be stealthy in finding and replacing letterboxes!
