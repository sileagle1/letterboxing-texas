Damaged boxes: what would you say?

From: (candj1969@yahoo.com) | Date: 2015-02-13 10:15:57 UTC-08:00

Suppose you get a find report from a new letterboxer that says nice things about your box and stamp, but the logbook was soaked, so they put it in a new baggie, maybe even double-bagged it.  They've only found a few boxes and are excited about the adventure, so I don't want to make them feel bad by saying that was not the right thing to do.  An experienced boxer might take the logbook and try to dry it out or replace it with a temporary one and ask if they could mail the old one to you, but obviously, the new person doesn't know what to do, and are usually not prepared to do anything, even if they had the time.  So, what would you say and what would you do if it were you?

From: Christine Borgfeld (christine@borgfeld.net) | Date: 2015-02-14 08:08:39 UTC-06:00

If a logbook is completely ruined, I just remove the logbook and toss it.  If it’s salvageable, my response will depend on who planted it – for active boxers who I know, I’ll take it and contact them.  If they are not active, I usually just leave it in the box outside the bag and hope for a dry spell to dry it out.  

 

Boots, I’d suggest you thank them for their kind comments on your box and then suggest that if they come across that situation in the future, please remove the book and contact you so that it doesn’t mold in the box from the moisture.

I believe as a responsible hider of letterboxes, I would thank the newbie finders for the heads up, and as stated before me, give advice on handling future boxes with similar issues, then go and do maintenance on my box. If I was unable to I believe there are some experienced boxers who if contacted and asked nicely and are close by would do me the favor and "fix" my box. I have had to archive several of my letterboxes that were beyond repair or the stamp was missing, it happens, it's all apart of the joys and heartbreaks of letterboxing.
