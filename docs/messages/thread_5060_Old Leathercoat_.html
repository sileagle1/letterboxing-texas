Old Leathercoat!

From: (cjagger405@earthlink.net) | Date: 2012-08-16 12:24:12 UTC-05:00
Why U, Wry Me, but not Me? Why is it that I've been to Pecan Cemetery many times over the past five years, checking on our boxes there, but on your first visit, you get a visit from the Ghost of Maine and I never have? Not fair.
However, I am beyond happy to report that Boots Tex's Old Leathercoat is just where it's supposed to be waiting for its next visitor. In fact, Wry Me did such an outstanding job of rehiding it, at first I thought it wasn't there! I LOVE that! There were no markings on the trees as described by the last seeker, so I'm guessing she was at the wrong place. In fairness, they do have the main entrance to the Cemetery closed due to road construction (looks like it will open again within days), so you have to go around the corner to enter the cemetery, then work back to the Main Gate to start to follow the clues, however, that's LETTERBOXING, my friends!

Funny story: about five years ago, Baby Bear had hidden a box down by Trinity River in Irving (between Dallas and Ft Worth). Viewfinder and I also had boxes in that area, so we had a preconceived idea of just where it was. We drove out there and searched and searched the spot where we just KNEW it had to be, but no luck. In fact, we were pretty masterful in forcing his clues to match what we needed them to be. I got home that night, and re-read his clues and the light bulb went on: we were actually on the wrong trail. I went back the next morning and well, lets just say it's a lot easier to make sense of the clues when you're actually in the right place!

Puddle Splasher

Wry Me....  I feel this way about it. 

 

Where you were born is not your fault - although some will try to hold it against you anyway.  Where you truly want to grow your roots says a whole lot more.

 

Some mean it in fun, and some do not.  I can grin at the fun.  But I also take my position in Texas seriously.  On this list - I suspect it is in fun.

 

My situation is simple.  I was not born in Texas, but I got here as quick as I could.  I bought a home here.  I became a part of the community.  We participate, as park volunteers, as therapy dog owners, we teach in schools.  More importantly to me, I know the history of Texas as if it were my own.  I visited the Alamo, several times.  Being a retired soldier, I truly understand the bravery and sacrifice of those Texans.  And I promise to any Texan that would challenge me, my allegiance is to Texas, and I only hope if called upon I could live up to the standard set before me.  I wonder how many that was born here would make a statement to match mine? 

 

So am I a damn Yankee?  To some - the answer is probably still yes.  but I wait to see where they stand if truly called.  My wife and I are coming up on 28 years in Texas.  More of my life has been here than anywhere else.  And over half of my military career was in Texas.  I have two children.  My son was born in Fox Army Hospital in Alabama.  But if asked he would express the same feelings I just did.  And he is a true warrior - decorated for valor 6 times in Iraq, the last time for saving lives as an EMT rather than taking them.  I would like to think he is the kind of person Texas wants.  I certainly want him on my team!  My daughter was born in William Beaumont Army Medical Center - Fort Bliss.  She has yet to make a mark - her time will come.

 

BTW - my wife IS German.  Fraulein Ringleman aus Schweinfurt.

 

El Paso is my city, Texas is my state, and the United States is my country.  But in the present state of our nation, if Texas split, I would still be a Texan.

 

You cannot be faulted for where your parents were when you were born.  And you showed good sense in picking your place in the world.  Those are my words.  FWIW.
