Howdy. Hope you-all are well.

We are having a BLAST here. I hit it right off with the brother-in-law. Since he is a ham, most of our outings are ham-radio related. Haven't even thought of a letterbox.

Much of what I remember is still the same. Mush more is different. The base I was posted onn is still there. and still looks the same. The building I was assigned to is now the post office.

Crossing the former East German border is interesting. I spent 7-1/2 years of my live watching over this border waiting for the Russians to come boiling through the Meiningen Gap. I was in the states when Ronald Reagan and Margret Thatcher broke through the wall. Touring the area and actually going through the pld check-stations was interesting. But the most interesting was attending a ham radio meeting in Meiningen (formerly enemy territory) and having the owner of the gasthaus (restaurant) shake my hand and thank me for his freedom.

I (as usual) am keeping careful notes for a good story later. Although none of it will be boxing related, I will post it if anyone is interested.

The days are flying by - this will all be over too soon.

Y-all take care.
Mike.
Strahlungen, Bavaria, Germany.
