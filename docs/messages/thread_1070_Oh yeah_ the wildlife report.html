Oh yeah, the wildlife report

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-07-16 17:41:34 UTC-07:00
New armadillo story: We got to see a mama armadillo
nesting! We saw her well ahead of us and just off the
trail and she was hopping backward and oddly. We
thought it was very strange, until we got closer and
realized she was trying to carry a patch of forest
floor material with her. As we were approaching, she
managed to get it where she was going - to her hole in
some tree roots by a creek! She realized we were
approaching, so she hid well away in her hole, but we
had seen her go in, so we went and stood right over
where she was. She was rooting away and rearranging
material in her home very quietly. We finally crossed
a bridge to the other side of the creek, where we
could easily see her. She decided we had left so she
came out of her home and began shoveling bedding
material around and into her home - it was fascinating
to watch a mama of an odd species behave with such
care for her family.

At another juncture, we were treated to a small flock
of red and black butterflies interspersed with small
bright blue dragonflies. They swarmed up all around
us!

And at yet another spot (right by a box in fact), we
saw a young snake slip away...but that was nothing
compared to the water moccasin we watched sunning from
our safe vantage point on a bridge above him! He
appeared to be sunning and waiting for some minnows to
get close enough for him to eat, but they never did.
We even tried spitting in the water to draw them
closer, but while they would go to the spit far enough
away from him, they weren't being suckered in when it
was floating close by him! So either water moccasins
like minnows and they know it, or water moccasins
don't care much for minnows, but minnows don't like
the way water moccasins smell!

A good day for wildlife observation!
Lucy
