Box Exchange Poll

From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2005-07-25 20:16:33 UTC
Hi all!

Just a reminder that the Box Exchange Poll runs through this Friday.
Currently, Texas Festivals is the leader, but others are gaining in
popularity. Anyone on this list can vote, and if you've already voted
but want to change your vote, you can easily do that by visiting the
poll section. So if your first choice is a sure-fire loser, you may
switch to your second choice to build momentum for that choice. Hey,
I've switched my vote 5 times already!

After the polls close, I will create a database where you may sign up
to create and/or receive a box. Creators are responsible for the
stamp, the log book and the box to hold it all. Receivers will be
responsible for planting the box, writing, posting and hosting the
clues and doing any routine maintenance on the box itself. I will
conduct a slightly geographically-rigged lottery to hook up the
exchangees.

Remember, this is a good will exchange so that boxers from one part of
the state (or country, as in Miss Moon's case) can see the creations
of letterboxers whose boxes they might not routinely be able to find.
If you're on this list, but you don't live in Texas, you are still
welcome to participate. All are welcome--newbies and experienced
carvers alike.

Hopefully, everyone will get into the spirit and have lots of fun with
it. Target mail date is September 15th.

Dewberry

From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2005-07-26 04:07:26 UTC
I think that will be much harder to coordinate with that approach.
Plus, as these are stamps that we are essentially caretaking for
another boxer, they need to be placed relatively near to the
receiver's home for maintenance. I think I would be more careful
with someone else's box that I was entrusted with than I would be
with one of my own, some of which I have never even checked on. I
wouldn't necessarily want to get a box for a festival that's near
Houston, but for a community that I don't know very well and don't
really know the hiding spots in. I'd rather hide the Oldenberg
HammerFest Box or the Muleshoe Mule Days box in good spots in
Houston that I thought would be secure. Plus, to me, the oddly
named ones are the more entertaining ones!

Will of course go along with the group, as that's why there's a poll
and it's a group exchange and effort, but those are my thoughts.
