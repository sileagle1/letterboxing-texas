From: Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter (janet@redbarnretreat.com) | Date: 2007-04-07 03:14:59 UTC
Just a short note to thank Silver Eagle and Baby Bear for finding all
our letterboxes last week and for their kind words. And thanks for
the new ones you planted as well. We've been in Austin all week
staying at McKinney Falls SP and have had only limited Internet
access (had to go to Denny's to find a Wi-Fi hot spot), so have been
out of touch mostly. We met El Lobo and Star Seeker there and we
letterboxed together all week. I'll write more when we get home. I
enjoyed Silver Eagle's story about going down the dirt road to the
cemetery to find Sarah's Flag letterbox. Gryzzled Gryphon not only
negotiated that road in his Cranberry Cruiser, but he did it in the
dark. He parked the CC at the cattle guard in Madison County and
walked to the cemetery in Grimes County. I also wanted everyone to
know that we found the Lost Pines letterbox by Eoghan yesterday
morning. It's in perfect condition. I found the clues on the
Wayback Machine as suggested by Silver Eagle at the Wildflower Event,
and it was the highlight of our week. We found 29 letterboxes in 5
days, or about the daily average for Silver Eagle and Baby Bear. We
also planted 12 new boxes in the area, including 2 each at McKinney
Falls State Park and McKinney Roughs Nature Area, both great parks.
I hope Dewberry made it to McKinney Roughs to find our boxes (we hid
some clues for her) and the two that were already there. I'll be
posting the clues this weekend when we get home. Y'all have a Happy
Easter.

Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter
