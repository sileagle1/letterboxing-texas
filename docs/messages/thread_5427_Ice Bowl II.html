From: (sileagle1@yahoo.com) | Date: 2015-01-11 05:49:03 UTC-08:00

Since we have at least 2 members from Wisconsin in this group (Wisconsin Hiker & Martini Man), I just have to mention the football game today between Dallas & Green Bay.  Many of you are probably too young to remember the last time these two met in a championship game back in 1967, but it is considered one of the greatest games in NFL history.  It was called the Ice Bowl because the weather was so bad, and I remember being amazed they could play in such conditions.  In the last seconds, Bart Starr scored on a quarterback sneak with the temperature at -20 degrees and the Packers won 20-17.  I don't think the weather will be as bad today, but hopefully it will be a good game no matter who wins.

From: Debbie Kotlarek (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2015-01-11 21:46:45 UTC-06:00

Although we both are also old enough to remember the Ice Bowl, we didn't watch the Ice Bowl II game today.  We had sub-zero temperatures every day this past week in Wisconsin.  So we were excited that it was going to be "warm" today (that means mid-20s).  Our letterboxing friends Tiptoe & Tonto were in town, so we all headed out on the trail so they could find our "ICE Cube Adventures" series.  After that we headed south into Illinois (woo-hoo, even warmer at 30 degrees!!) and met more IL friends (Acorn & Golden Boy).  The 6 of us enjoyed a hike to find a series based on the movie "FROZEN".  So I guess we had our own "Ice Bowl" on the trails in the Great Lakes region! 

 

But we are definitely looking forward to escaping the frozen tundra and spending some time in Arizona & Texas soon.  We are excited to be attending another TALE event and hope that this year we will be able to participate in the evening socializing instead of spending our time in the emergency room.
