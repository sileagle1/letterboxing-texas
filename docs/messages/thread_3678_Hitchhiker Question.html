Aren't hitchhiker's supposed to be stamps also? We found a logbook
for a hitchhiker in a box day before yesterday and it was wrapped
around some sort of a pendant like a surfer "dude" would wear. There

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-08-01 13:06:00 UTC-05:00
Sounds like you found a geocaching travel bug instead of a hitchhiker.  But yes, a hitchhiker should have a stamp and logbook.  The TB would have a logbook, but it would have a trinket to travel with it.  Don't know what the dollar was for - lol!
 
I'm betting if you lookon geocaching.com, you'll be able to figure out that there is a GC nearby.  If you don't know how to do that, email me the name of the box you found and I'll help you figure it out.  Do it offlist though so we don't accidentally spoil anything for anyone!  My email is barefootlucy@gmail.com

From: Kelli Oliver (kellioliver2004@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-08-01 11:29:00 UTC-07:00
Yes.  Hitchhikers have their own stamps and logbooks inside their own baggie.
some one must  have taken the stamp.  Couldn't tell you why the dollar or pendant was there.  If the logbook has the placer's info in it, I would contact them for information regarding the stamp and at least let them know the stamp is missing.
I recently came upon a HH and the logbook was replaced due to destruction over time.
That will happen unfortunately, but for the stamp to come up missing is odd. 
Sadly, maybe the logbook will need to be sent home to the placer and the HH be retired if the stamp is missing.

Yes.  A hitchhiker will always have a stamp.  A HH isn't a HH without a stamp - it's just a notebook/logbook that got stuck in a box otherwise.
 
BUT - that could happen for a number of reasons!  Perhaps the logbook and the stamp got separated by someone who didn't know how to handle a HH, or maybe someone who didn't know how to make a HH.
