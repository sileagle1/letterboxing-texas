Murphy's Laws Of Letterboxing

From: Silver Eagle (sileagle@alltel.net) | Date: 2006-01-03 16:28:08 UTC
The original Murphy's Law states: "Anything that can
go wrong, will go wrong", and I have noticed similar
laws while letterboxing. On our recent trip, Baby Bear
and I experienced a few and I'd like to share them:

1. "No matter how remote a box is, someone will come by
just as you are retrieving or replacing it".

This happened to us way more than we thought normal, and
it resulted in some hard-to-explain situations. We made
sure nobody ever saw the box, but I'm sure they wondered
why 2 guys were going into the woods together. It's not
the first time my brother and I have been suspected of
being more than we are.

2. "The better a box is hidden, the more likely it will
be missing".

I know this sounds backwards, but it was amazing how many
times we would not find a box that was off-trail and in a
secluded area, yet find boxes totally uncovered in busy
areas. One box that was in a downtown alley park in a
well-maintained planter had Christmas light cord running
right over it for lights in the garden. Go figure.

3. "If there is more than one entrance to a park, you
will always go to the wrong one".

Several clues did not give directions to parks, so we
just picked a street on the map that looked correct. More
times than not, we had to drive around looking for another
part of the same park to match the clues.


It's possible these laws only apply to Baby Bear and I, but
if you have some I would love to hear them. BTW, these laws
in no way stopped us from enjoying ourselves.

Silver Eagle

Baby Bear's Laws

1) Wear shirt that says "He is my brother" in the future.
2) Printing mapquest directions to a park isn't good enough (some
parts of Dallas are not good to be lost in, as well as any large
city).
3) Realize it does not get light until 6:30am, and dark at 6pm
(letterboxing in the dark is interesting).
4) Always plan the trip, thus you are the navigator and avoid
driving!!!
5) Plan bathroom breaks!
6) When you pick your trail name, put some thought into it. Enough
said.

Can't wait for the event to hear more stories!!!
Take care,
Baby Bear
