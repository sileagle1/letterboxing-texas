From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2009-08-14 13:57:32 UTC-06:00
My experience with gc'ers has been far to the positive with a few negatives thrown in . In my area, they and we do a pretty good job of understanding both games, so they are good about communicating with "issues" or suggestions or questions and vice versa. BUT - just as there are careless or thoughtless letterboxers, there are also careless or thoughtless gc'ers.
Their forums are far less friendly, in my opinion, than any of ours are, so unless you get an in with a gc'er personally, it's hard to break in and understand what they have going on.
They have a limit of 1/10 of a mile between caches (so I think they misinformed you - perhaps your cache was 321' from another and that's what they meant to say, butthe limit is 528' between caches). Personally, I think it's a good practice so that an area isn't littered with caches, but it's easy for them to monitor that because they can tell by overlaying coords to determine proximity. Here's the link to the part of their guidelines that address this:
http://www.geocaching.com/about/guidelines.aspx#sat
If, by chance, that doesn't go through right, look at their guidelines and go to the part about Cache Saturation.
I tried geocaching for awhile and didn't get hooked. It was fun, but it didn't scratch my creative itch. When my GPSr's cratered, I wasn't driven to replace them. If I can find software to make my phone a gc'able GPS (it works great for roads), I'll try it again, but I probably won't spend the money again for one until we get into some serious back country backpacking. I did make one really good friend through geocaching and got reacquainted with an old school mate because of it.
I think it's great when folks either do both or have at least tried both. It makes us all more understanding of the other side of the fence.

Re: Can I ask a geocaching question?

From: Puddle Splasher (cjagger405@earthlink.net) | Date: 2009-08-14 22:06:05 UTC
There are no hard rules for letterboxing, but rather it's all based on respect. In fact, the subject is outlined on AQ under: "Respect"
http://www.atlasquest.com/about/rules/respect.html
It does suggest that you not hide your letterbox near another box. It's been widely accepted in letterboxing that you should contact the placer of nearby box before placing your box to make sure they don't object to having a new box placed nearby. Personally, I don't mind having other boxes located in the same general area as mine, as long as they aren't basically side by side. Last year at the TALE, Lucy Lockett and I hid our boxes extremely close to each other without realizing it. (Neither of our clues had been published when we hid our boxes.) That's pretty easy to do at a big event. However, when we realized it, we both agreed that we liked having a "neighbor" for our boxes and were happy to leave them where they were. Also, it's sometimes easy to place a box near a mystery box and never know it's there if you haven't solved the mystery. When this happens, the placer of the mystery box may feel their box might be compromised and contact you and ask if you would mind moving yours. You definitely should! Like the AQ topic states, it's all about respect.
Puddle Splasher
