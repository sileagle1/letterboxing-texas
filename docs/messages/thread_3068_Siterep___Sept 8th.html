Siterep...Sept 8th

From: tigger32167 (tigger32167@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-09-09 18:13:49 UTC
OK, Wagtime and I were both in Kaufman/Athens/Eustace on Sept 1st
and all of the boxes that I found today were planted after that (by
the way that is not a complaint, one can never have enough boxes on
their list to find). So here goes with the events of the day:

My navigator, JR, and I started out the day in Baxter Community,
near Athens. Found the Texas Schoolhouse Series, Old Baxter
Schoolhouse. Next we went to Coleman Park in Athens to pick up the
Hornet's Hive box. Both of these boxes were to set the standard for
the rest of the day. Both were really nice stamps and a great
story/history to go with it. Our next stop was at the J. Pinckney
Henderson Park in Athens. I had seen this park before, but so glad
I finally had the opportunity to read the historical markers..they
were really interesting. JR and I found the Caddoan Pottery and the
Another Brick in the Wall boxes at this location..again great stamps
and great finds. Next we attempted to find the Meredith Campground
box, but my navigator was sleeping instead of watching for the road
signs, and we missed the road. However, we did finally find the
road and did manage to find the campground/cemetery/tabernacle.
This was a neat spot, and after I got the box (nice stamp) I found
myself just thinking about all the people who have camped in that
spot...it makes you stop and think. Our next stop was Big
Rock Shelter, which we found with no problem..it really makes me
wonder why the owners don't open this place up to visitors. The
shelter can be seen from the road, but it just leaves you with
enough taste to want to see more. Again, Monarchtrailer did a
wonderful job on the stamp, placement, and history. Next we went to
Purtis Creek State Park to place 3 boxes for the Fishin' 4
Letterboxes event, by Bailey's Bunch. We got those placed and on
we went. Next we drove to Kaufman and picked up the No Shoes box.
What a neat sight...it is unbelieveable that the poor house is still
in existence..I really enjoyed looking at the old buildings. Great
find. Our next stop was Pioneer Cemetery in Kaufman to find Love's
Enduring Journey. I found the cemetery sign with no problem, but
had a hard time figuring out to get to it. So I re-read the clue
and realized to just drive across the grass. This was a neat story,
and great stamp. Next we found the All Hail the King box in
Kaufman...another great stamp and great history. My navigator and I
decided to conclude our day in Brownsboro. We made it to Brownsboro
and I have to admit that ignoring the No tresspassing sign to get to
Jennings Mountain Cemetery made me a little nervous, but
Monarchtrailer insured me that it would be ok in her clues. So
onward I went. When I got to the top of the mountain, there was a
definite eerieness (not sure if that is a word, but you get the
picture). I kind of felt like I was in some horror movie, but it
was still a great find. Our last stop was the Norwegian Lutheran
Cemetery in Brownsboro to pick up the Normandy Norweigan Settlement
box. Nice stamp. This is where our adventure ended for the day.

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-09-09 15:37:05 UTC-05:00
You'll see both siterep and sitrep - it stands for Situation Report.  It's something that hails back to the beginning of letterboxing - it was almost an expectation that you would write up a report of what you found while you were out.  Thankfully, we have quite a few boxers in Texas that continue that tradition, which is falling by the wayside in some places!
