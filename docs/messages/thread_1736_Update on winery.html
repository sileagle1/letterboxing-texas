Update on winery

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-03-11 19:59:54 UTC-08:00
Some of you are thinking of visiting the winery near
the State Park while you are at the gathering, so I
thought I would update you on what I learned today.

Ironically, the proprietor is leaving Friday evening
to go to Spring for a festival there. He will be at
the winery until closing Friday, which is 6:00 p.m.
and his assistant will be there the next day all day.
Although the assistant is capable, I'm sure, I would
urge y'all to go by there Friday evening if you
possibly can because the winemaker himself really
rounds out the experience.

This little winery is a nod to the rural spirit of
East Texas. Don't expect to compare Chardonnays or
discuss vintages - their wines are purely fun and
untraditional, yet tasty all the same. Expect stories
galore as he pours for you. He considers his winery
to be a family place - so he has root beer on tap for
the kiddos and has been known to slip a little
chocolate their way if he has it on hand.

His assistant also makes candles in wine glasses and
wine glass charms and she also has bath oils for sale.


The building is humble, but you can detect the pride
they feel as they talk about their product. I've been
there now a few times and I always feel good when I
leave (and that's even when I DON'T sample!).
