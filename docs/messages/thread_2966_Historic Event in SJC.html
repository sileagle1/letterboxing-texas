Historic Event in SJC

From: Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter (janet@redbarnretreat.com) | Date: 2007-07-31 14:34:49 UTC
Today is a historic one in San Jacinto County. Our first traffic
control light has been turned on. Oh, we have a couple of flashing
red lights, but this one has red, yellow and green! Welcome to the
21st century, SJC! The light is at the intersection of Highways 150
and 156 in Coldspring. We really did need one...I've seen as many as
seven or eight cars backed up at that intersection. Sometimes it
takes you a couple of minutes to get through, so this is a real
improvement. Y'all be careful now when you come through here. I
wonder how many Texas counties are left without a traffic light?
Probably Loving County at least. Just wondering.

Boots Tex

I may have told this before, but when we lived in Connecticut, the "city" installed a new stop sign at one of the intersections. Now... not too much happened in our town and sometimes the local newspaper (which had been in publication for a couple hundred years or so) was desperate for news. It's amazing how educational letterboxing can be. I
googled "inventor of traffic signal" and found out that Garret
Augustus Morgan, the grandson of a former slave and a Confederate
general, John Hunt Morgan, not only invented the traffic signal, but
also was a sewing machine repairman who invented the zigzag
attachment for sewing machines. He was also the first black man in
Cleveland (Ohio, not Texas) to own a car. Also, he invented the gas
mask which saved a lot of lives in World War I. Google is a
wonderful thing.

Boots Tex

From: Dave (DavHow@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-08-03 04:41:48 UTC
I can't answer how many Texas counties are left without a traffic
light, but I'll chime in and say that Brewster county still doesn't
have any. We have 2 flashing red lights in Alpine, and I believe that's
it for the whole county. Presidio County has a couple of flashing reds
too, but no proper trafic signal, anf Jeff Davis County is devoid of
any that I can recall. I think you're probably right about Loving
county too. Terrel county doesn't have ANY traffic lights (flashing or
otherwise), and I don't think Hudspeth county has any either. Pecos
county is off the list because I'm pretty sure there is one traffic
signal in Fort Stockton. I seem t remember waiting on a light in the
city of Pecos so Reeves county is off the list, and I might remember
one light in Van Horn, so maybe Culberson is off the list too. Until
you get to El Paso County the entire Trans-Pecos region is pretty much
traffic-signal free (and traffic free for that matter). I'm from
Houston, so I have to say that I don't miss the traffic one bit!

Warm Front
