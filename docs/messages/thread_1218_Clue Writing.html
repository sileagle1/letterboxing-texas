Clue Writing

From: hellbrunn (hellbrunn@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-08-21 20:06:49 UTC
Some things I try to remember as I write clues:

1. There are people who may come to find my box who have never been
to this town or even this area. Giving STREET NAMES will help them
get around easier.

2. If the trail I'm hiding my box on is used by people other than
hikers (for example, bicycles, motorcycles, or 4 wheelers), I try to
mention that. Being on a trail and hearing/seeing motorized
vehicles zoom by you can be frightening.

3. Always be SPECIFIC and use landmarks that stick out or are
unusual. For example, "look towards the power lines and find three
trails...take the one in the middle" may be clear to the clue
writer, however, to someone who is trying to follow your clues it
can be more difficult to decipher. I see power lines that are
covering a distance of about 50 yards. Where do I begin to look for
the three trails?

Thanks,

Lucy Locket

I just wanted to share something with all of you.  I had shared that the Chandler box went missing a week after its placement.  It was our first box and was very discouraging.  Everyone was kind enough to show me some sympathy and let me pout for a few days.  Well, good news!!  I received an email through the lbna website from someone who claimed to have my box.  He said he was going to rehide it somewhere new.
I panicked!!!  I emailed him back(practically begging) to please either rehide it in the park or let me pick it up.  I sent my phone number hoping he would call.  After a few nailbitting hours, he called me.  Come to find out he lives almost exactly right behind me.  I have successfuly retrieved THE CHANDLER stamp!!!  It should count as a double find for me!!  I am so happy, and now I can stop talking bad about all the neighborhood kids, aka, letterbox thievs.  Every kid we saw at the park we secretly blamed.  Now, I have to find a good spot to hide it!
