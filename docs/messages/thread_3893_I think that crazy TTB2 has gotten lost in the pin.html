I think that crazy TTB2 has gotten lost in the pines!

From: SandiBox (sandibox@msn.com) | Date: 2009-01-03 04:29:10 UTC
After loitering at my house for several weeks, those crazy passengers
of the Texas Tour Bus 2 took off this morning in a big ole hurry! I
thought I heard the bus driver say something about going to Austin,
but wasn't sure I heard correctly. MeMa of 7 is still visiting from
Baltimore, so she, SoccerChik and I jumped in my car to to give chase.

They headed west on I-10 for 40 miles or so, and made a quick side
trip to San Felipe. I think the Ghost of Davy Crockett wanted to say
hello to some Old friends of his. Next, they veered back onto I-10
and continued west to Columbus where they stopped at a regional LCRA
park to feed the kitties.

We chased the bus as it turned northwest on Hwy 71, but lost it when
we stopped in Ellinger for some to-die-for kolaches at Hruska's Store
& Bakery, then swung into Rosemary's Vineyard (MeMa's first name is
Rosemary and thought it would be cool to take home a bottle of Merlot
with "her" label).

We thought we'd lost them for good, but as we neared LaGrange, we
heard the unmistakable sounds of a home-grown Texas band
(Uh...huh..huh..huh) and caught a glimps of the passengers downing
some brewskies. Before we could even park the car, the were off
again! I thought they made a wrong turn when they stopped at another
LCRA park just outside LaGrange to whistle at some scantily dressed
ladies, but they only tossed something out the window and made their
way back onto Hwy 71.

We didn't know where in the heck that bus driver was headed! About
35 miles southeast of Austin, they stopped at Kendra Macon's
Roadhouse for some of the best burgers in the county. Between bites
of a HUGE bacon cheeseburger, Willie mumbled something about Houston
Toads. I think that boy's been smokin' some funny stuff! After our
long chase we were hungry too, so we rested for a spell on the patio
and enjoyed our grub.

Next thing we knew, those crazy passengers ran out and jumped back
onto the bus, zipping across the road into a park, and leaving me
stuck with their tab! After settling up with Kendra, we tried to
follow their path of destruction. Seems they almost mowed down some
guys in a golf cart and ran through the entrance gate. The ranger
was still shaking his fist at them as we came through. The way I hear
it, only two wheels of that bus were touching the ground as they made
a quick left onto Park Road 1A.

As we made the corner, we saw some kids on bicycles pointing up the
road toward an overlook in the northeast corner of the park. We
followed - always keeping right on Park Road 1A. We parked at the
overlook, and although we enjoyed the view, we didn't see that darned
bus anywhere!

As we returned to our car, SoccerChik noticed a sign across the road
indicating that this park was the largest remaining habitat of the
Houston Toad. Maybe Willie was onto to something after all. And
guess what?!! There were tire tracks heading east past the sign, and
down the trail. We hurried down the trail ourselves, and soon came
to a trail post declaring no fires or bikes allowed... it didn't
forbid buses, which is good, cause those tire tracks were still going
east on the trail, down a hill. We continued for another 58 steps,
passing two reflective trail markers nailed to trees. We were nearly
at the bottom of the hill and saw another of those reflective trail
markers nailed to a tall pine tree on the left. We stopped there to
catch our breath for a second. That's when we noticed the tire tracks
veered left into the forest. They seemed headed straight toward a
pair of pine trees about 15 steps from the trail, but the tracks
disappeared at the pine on the right, under the boughs of a large
fallen pine tree that rested against it.

I fear that crazy TTB2 has gotten lost in the loblolly pines! We
were exhausted and decided that someone else would have to rescue the
passengers from their folly. We returned to our car and headed home
to Houston.

Hope ya'll had a festive New Year's too!

SandiBox
