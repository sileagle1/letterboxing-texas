Rant and rave

From: celticlions (celticlions@comcast.net) | Date: 2005-07-30 19:05:18 UTC-05:00
Stillwater, Vandal and I drove around today in gorgeous weather, checking on
previously planted boxes and planting a brand new one ( more on that later).
I usually make it a hard & fast rule to wait a cooling-off 24 hours before I
post a cranky email, but I'm just really discouraged.
After finding our boxes with lids not replaced tightly or bags not resealed
(just wadded up in the box like litter), I finally just pulled two of our
boxes and may or may not replace them. In each instance I know who the last
to log in was, and I'm toying with writing to request that this letterboxer
just stops looking for our boxes.
If you are in too much of a hurry to reseal plastic bags, please don't
letterbox. If you don't have time to wrap the stamp back up in paper towel
to protect it, please don't letterbox. If you can't take an extra few
seconds to make sure nothing is sticking out the sides of the box and that
the lid is firmly resealed, please don't letterbox.

Please don't let your kids replace letterboxes - letterboxing with children
is WONDERFUL, but I don't know any under 10 year olds who can really check
that bags and boxes are truly re-sealed and will survive a rainstorm. I
don't know whether this particular person was boxing with kids, but I'm
going to give them the benefit of the doubt...because I hate to believe an
adult would be so careless and irresponsible and let's face it, unkind.

Very bummed out,
Ruby T.

Probably half of the boxes I find are not re-sealed or hidden to my standards. I am very fussy, and check the location from numerous angle to make sure the box is completely out of sight before I leave. I often have to gather new bark, stones, etc. to properly re-hide the box. It irritates me no end to come up on the site and see the box in plain sight, or see a corner sticking out.
 
If I were you, and I'm glad I'm not, because giving advice is soooo much easier than following it, I would contact the last finder and explain how you found the box, and *nicely* give some tips for re-hiding. Maybe this person is totally unaware of the problem and will fix it if asked nicely. If not, I suggest you conact the BEAs and see if they need a subject for experimentation.

From: pkleingers (pam@kleingers.net) | Date: 2005-07-31 00:45:16 UTC-04:00

This is probably not a popular opinion but:  Kids aren’t the problem, even if they are the ones rehiding.  It is the adults supervising them (or not) that are!

 

I box with my kids often.  We have rules.  Frequently, they retrieve the box—under my supervision so I can see how it was hidden. Sometimes they open the box, again under my supervision so I can see how it was packed.  More often, I open it to maintain some sort of order to the chaos of stamping in with several people.  They often replace the box as well—again, under my supervision to insure the box is properly hidden.  My kids do a great job—hide the box as well as or better than when we find it—because I teach them how!   And when they do not, I am right there explaining what needs to be done differently, showing them how to evaluate what they did.

 

As a new boxer, I was not always as careful as I should have been—and I am thankful that no boxes went missing after my visits (to my knowledge)  I would have appreciated a  note regarding my less than careful rehiding.  I learned a lot by reading posts such as Ruby T’s and have become even more careful when boxing with the kids.

 

 

Careless adults = careless children.  Can’t blame them for not knowing better if they have never been taught.  I get so annoyed because it is parents like these that give MY kids a bad reputation.  And I will spare you all my soap box discourse on the state of the world….

 

.

Mama Stork

aka Pam in Cinci
