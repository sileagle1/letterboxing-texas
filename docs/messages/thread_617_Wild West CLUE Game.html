Hi everyone!

Sorry to be confusing - I had posted earlier today a list of suggestions for
CLUE game stamp carvings, some from Scarab & some from Team King. My
preference would be to stick to a Western/Texan theme as much as possible.
For example, my Miss Scarlet stamp will be a belle from the old West - not a
contemporary vixen!
TeamKing's rope will be a traditional clue weapon, but I think it will have
a Western look to it? Catpawz has chosen the gun and it sounds as though it
might be a shotgun (old West) rather than revolver (contemporary).
But I know I'm suggesting this game rather late - we only have a couple of
weeks before I'm asking for the stamps to be mailed to me (March 1st). So I
am more interested in making sure that we receive enough stamps to play with
than to make this stressful for anyone.

Although it would be cool to have a unified theme for our stamps, we could
still play the game of CLUE with a mixture...for example, Colonel Mustard
could be a military man traveling through Amarillo who stops in a saloon and
meets a tragic end due to a bottle of poison snake oil...;-)
Miss Scarlet could be a dance hall girl who has an unfortunate encounter
with a rattlesnake near the Chuck Wagon...

I'm going to add a word doc to this group's files section, so that everyone
can take a look at TeamKing & Scarab's suggestions. Let's have fun with
this!

To answer the size question:

As you've probably all gathered, I'm not much of a rules person – (as if anyone with the trail name Ruby Tuesday would be!!!) So my first instinct is to tell everyone to make the stamps whatever size they choose.

However, we probably need an outer limit, don’t we? I believe BusyBee’s stamp is going to be 2.5 x 3.5 – can we agree to keep the stamps smaller than that?

When I first thought about doing this, I was envisioning making "scorecard" booklets, so that when you receive a CLUE from someone, they would stamp it onto your scorecard.

However, some of you may prefer to have the stamps put into your logbooks - or perhaps enter in both places - scorecard & logbooks. I’m going to experiment with creating some scorecard/logs and see if I can make enough of them cheaply & quickly!

They might make fun keepsakes of our Brazos Gathering.
