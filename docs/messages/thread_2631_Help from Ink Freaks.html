From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-03-08 18:49:10 UTC-06:00
As y'all know, I've got this mambo stamp project going with Cub Scouts for our district event at the end of April.  Someone came up with this workable idea, IF I can find an indelible stamp ink.
 
Rather than having a patch designed for the event (which I had already spent umpteen hours designing, only to find out it couldn't be done), we are going to have patches embroidered with the event name and leave them blank so the boys can stamp the camp stamp and their personal stamp on the patch. 
NOW - these patches wind up sewn onto the pockets of scout uniforms everywhere, so the ink really needs to be indelible.  Any thoughts on how to accomplish this?  I plan to make several copies of the camp stamp, and they will only be used that one day, so that if I have to use an ink that will damage the stamp, that will be fine as long as the stamps last the day.
 
Thoughts?

From: ThimbleCat (inklaura@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2007-03-09 14:29:32 UTC
You need an ink that will be waterproof of course. I'd go with a
pigment ink if possible (the kind with the foamy pad as opposed to the
firm pad). You will want to test it on one patch to make sure it will
actually take the ink and that the ink will dry. Pigment ink sometimes
won't dry on all surfaces. You might want to heat set it with an
embossing gun or iron (if possible - cover with something protective
first (muslin is usually fine) and test first of course).

Another option is a hybrid ink - part pigment and part dye. Dries fast
and is waterproof. This is what I use for boxing.

Then there are the Staz-On ink pads. These are solvent ink (kinda like
Sharpie ink in a pad form). This actually may be your best option.
Whomever is in charge of that station for the day may be high from the
smell by the end but they should be pretty permanent. They are great
for most unusual surfaces, but as always, it's good to test one first.

Good luck - what a huge undertaking!
ThimbleCat

From: Brandi J Rider (brandi@busybeesfamily.com) | Date: 2007-03-09 07:58:26 UTC-08:00
Hi everyone!
 
Hate to have missed the gathering, but have been in chemo and not felt well. Had to chime in on this topic as a GS leader and Cub SCout leader. My advice is to use regular fabric paint and use a foam brush to spread on stamp and then press onto fabric of the patches. Any other type of ink will not give you a distinct enough and bright enough impression and who knows how it will stand up to the many, many, many washings these uniforms tend to get?  A fabric paint is designed especially to withstand washing and will show up brightly enough to be seen from a distance.  They are also readily available at any craft store and come iin a large variety of colors. Only difficulty I can see is making sure that paint is not spread too thick on stamp so it will not make a squishy mess.  only need a thin layer.  Maybe squirt fabric paint onto a paper plate and have boys stamp into the plate, test on paper and then sstamp onto patches?
 
JMHO, Batty B (still hanging in there!)

I have used Staz-on, you can buy them at the art store, and they work
very well. A second option is fabric ink pads, which you can buy at
Michaels or Hobby Lobby. They are designed to work on fabric. These
often have to be heat-set, but are permanent after that.
