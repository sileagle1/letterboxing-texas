Letterboxing adventures

From: Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter (janet@redbarnretreat.com) | Date: 2006-12-13 23:09:04 UTC
We just got back from a trip to West Texas (for a family wedding),
but had to take the opportunity to do some letterboxing on the way
back. But first, I posted some pictures on this site of our
letterboxing in Italy trip. For more, better pictures of that trip,
go to the following blog:

http://beckn32pics.my-expressions.com/archives/4252_1032542792/196941

This site belongs to our daughter, known in letterboxing as "Flash
Dreamer", who lives in Naples. She recently placed two boxes of her
own in Italy called "On the Rim" and "Old World Charm". Check them
out. She is a great photographer and gives letterboxing a nice plug
on her blog.

We picked a few of El Lobo's boxes in the outer reaches of West
Texas, then went to San Angelo for the two boxes placed there by
Runs For Chocolate. Then, we went to the Hill Country and found the
boxes in Kerrville, Hunt and Lost Maples. We got the one
called "Here Everything's Better", then went to Uvalde to get that
one there and spend the night. We got up and headed for Garner
State Park, only to find that it is closed for hunting. What a
bummer that was! I let the poor ranger know that I was not a happy
camper (couldn't have camped if I had wanted to), for all the good
it did. There are about six active boxes there, but that's a long
way to go to find the park closed, so if you are heading that way,
you should probably check first. Lost Maples is spectacular, even
if it's a little late for the turning of the leaves. We made the
entire 4.9 mile trek up the side of the mountain, and it was worth
it. We replaced a couple of boxes along the way and a couple of
others have condition problems, which I'll pass on to the owners of
the box.

Be sure to check out Flash Dreamer's blog. You'll have to copy and
paste since the link isn't actove for some reason.

Happy Letterboxing, Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter
