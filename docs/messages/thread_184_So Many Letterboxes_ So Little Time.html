So Many Letterboxes, So Little Time

From: Silver Eagle (sileagle@alltel.net) | Date: 2004-06-18 17:16:15 UTC
I never thought I would say this about TEXAS letterboxes,
but thanks to all of you placers, there are more boxes than
I have time to look for. This means that I am having to
organize the clues so I can find them in the future, and I
wanted to let you know how I am doing it in case you are
having the same problem (its a good problem). I try to
look at What's New every so often and print out any new
Texas clues. I assume all new clues go there, so if some
don't I could be missing some. I then put them in a 3-ring
binder organized with dividers for each of the 7 new regions
we agreed upon. Even though it may take Wes a while to
change our regions, it is easier for me to organize the
clues this way. I also put a copy of the Texas Map with
counties and regions at the front of the binder to help
me know which region a clue goes into. When I am ready to
use a clue, I look at it online to make sure nothing has
changed and print out a new copy if it has. Now if I can just
find the time to actually use the clues! Thanks to everyone
for giving me this "problem".

Silver Eagle

From: Connie Terry (connie@lavernia.net) | Date: 2004-06-18 13:35:15 UTC-05:00

Decisions, decisions, decisions…

do I organize a binder (so my collection of clues isn’t so scary)

carve stamps, assemble log books and hide boxes (if you plant them, they will come!)

or catch up on bills, laundry, correspondence, etc (see below)

 

Stamp carving came in very useful yesterday—my oldest daughter called me from Hermann Sons Camp yesterday and just happened to mention that she hadn’t gotten any mail this week.  Since she is a second year counselor, mom has been lax in the mail department.  I verified that she was still in the dorm they call “Jackrabbit”, went to Google and found a suitable image, and carved her a jackrabbit stamp.  Then I located a stamp pad I could spare and packaged it all up in an Express Mail box.  Instant “personalized” camp gift!

 

Corazon (waiting for the phone to ring so I know she got the package)
