Stuck at home with a sick kid today, instead of finding TeamKing's The
Birds as planned, so I am pondering what I carry around. Some of this
is in the car, some is in the pack:

A full first aid kit, always. You don't know how many times I've been
the one at the park who is able to produce an automatic ice kit, bee
sting medicine, bandaid, etc. My husband laughs hysterically at me
because I believe you can never have too many first aid kits--I have a
big one at home, a little one in the pack and a medium one in the car.
To the usual, I have to add a poison ivy wash called tecnu. The more
I letterbox, the less tolerant I get of PI. All for emergency people
repair.

A car emergency kit with cables, etc. For minor emergency car repair
(the big stuff I have to call for help)

A box in the car with extra containers, baggies, logbooks, etc. for
emergency box repair.

Water, trail mix, raisins, crayons, pencils, paper, maze & puzzle
books, and goldfish for emergency whiny kid repair.

So, I've been contemplating changing my trail name. Maybe I should be
the Repair Lady (but then people might think I could fix stuff, which
is far, far, far from the truth).

Add to that maps, maps and more maps! I love maps! But I couldn't
fit it into the repair motif.
Grins,

Dewberry

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-01-24 15:53:06 UTC-08:00
We love Tecnu! My daughter is terribly, terribly
allergic to PI, and (as you mentioned) the more she is
exposed to it the less tolerant she has become. Tecnu
makes it possible for her to get off the sidewalk and
have fun. We are considering buying stock in the
company!

I carry the same first aid kit - always and forever.
My clan and my extended clan (cub scouts) have an
ongoing need for first aid stuff.

I just finally put all my LB stuff into a back pack
with three big pockets and several smaller outer
pockets. In one pocket I carry ink of all kinds and
my GPS. In the smallest big pocket I carry our stamps
and the ink that we use with them and I also have an
emergency contact list in that pocket. In the largest
big pocket, I carry my logbook and 3 large baggies: 1)
full of baggies of all shapes and sizes sorted and
bagged within the big baggie, paper towels, and wet
paper towels, 2) sheets of carving material and
pencils, blades and gouges, and drawing pad, 3)
journal stuff - precut pages, a hole punch, and rings.
In the bottom of the biggest pocket I carry an
all-in-one tool, my nifty new headlamp, a pair of
leather gloves, a roll of duct tape, an extra
container, and miscellaneous other stuff like snacks
(which hopefully, but not always, get rotated fairly
frequently - ewwww!). On the straps of my pack I have
a compass on one shoulder and a whistle on the other.
I also strap a nalgene bottle to the bottom of the
pack. My clue book and my walking stick goes
separately.

I wind up carrying about 12 pounds with me by having
all this, but I can literally stop any time, any where
and make up a box or fix a box. I also like carrying
the extra weight because it keeps me able to go at a
faster pace when we go out with scouts. It's been
really nice having it all together because my mom has
been in the hospital for the last week and I've been
able to carry my pack around and drop in a corner on
the floor of the hospital if I've needed to and draw
and carve and generally pass the time.

So far, I haven't found I needed anything else. If I
do, I'm going to have to get a bigger backpack!

Lucy
