From: (brandi@busybeesfamily.com) | Date: 2007-01-09 03:03:55 UTC
Hi Little Dove!

Welcome to letterboxing and the letterboxingtexas group!

Most carvers don't actually use erasers to carve their own stamps. I've actually only met two or there boxers who use erasers.

I use orange or white pz-cut, mars staedler, or speedy cut (the pink stuff). Pz-cut has to be ordered from Stampeze, but the others can be found at Michael's or Hobby Lobby.

You can also find the speedball carving tools at your local craft stores. You'll need to look for them around the fine set supplies. I usually find them somewhere near the calligraphy stuff.

If you choose to order from Stampeze, you can order the speedball cutting tool, or the mars staedler version of cutters.

Most importantly, don't give up on carving your own stamps. Practice makes perfect and letterboxers will love ALL of your creations!

Hope this helps! Batty B of the Busybeesfamily

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-01-08 21:12:09 UTC-06:00
The best place to look in hobby stores like Hobby Lobby and Michael's is in either the calligraphy section or the lino block printing areas.  Otherwise, you can order PZKut from www.stampeaz.com or you can order carving block from Dick Blick or other art material companies.
 
As to tools, my preferred tool is an Exacto knife.  I'm learning to carve with a Dremel right now though!  Also, a lot of folks use gouges, which are also found with the carving block.

Re: [letterboxingtexas] New to the group

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-01-09 06:59:39 UTC-06:00
Janet referred to pink stuff.  Pink stuff isn't it's real name (like BarefootLucy isn't mine), but that's what letterboxers call it.  You'll know it when you see it.  It's rubber and - well - it's pink.  I like it myself, although I like PZKut a little better.  HOWEVER, the same company that makes pink stuff (Speedball) makes some stuff that is a buff color.  Don't buy it.  Don't even be tempted.  It's yukky.  You'll be tempted, but don't.  It seems to be universally acknowledged by letterboxers that it just isn't good for what we do.  It must have a purpose, but it isn't for stamps that will be seeing some wear and tear. 
 
This concludes our public service announcement.

From: Lauren LaFleur (laurenlafleur@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-01-09 07:15:07 UTC-08:00
Just my 2 cents:

Went I shopped for some supplies at the Hobby Lobby
near me, it was on the aisle with craft lamps,
drafting supplies and something else odd (can't
remember).

If you abandon all hope of finding it yourself, ask
for help from someone working near the fine arts
supplies and tell them you're looking for screen
printing supplies -- ink, brayers, etc. The section
these supplies are in is devoted to printing. (By the
way, the buff stu8ff Barefoot Lucy was referring to is
used by artists for printing. They carve their works
of art into it, then print it -- these works don't get
a lot of use like your stamp will, though.)

By the way, supplies are also available on the Hobby
Lobby Web site, for those not into the hassle of
hunting it in the store or who don't live near a
location.

Also, I carved my first stamp using Master Carve
yesterday. I'm in love with how easy it is to work
with. Remains to be seen how I'll like it out and in
use =)
