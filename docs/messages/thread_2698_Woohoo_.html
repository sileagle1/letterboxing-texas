Im impressed.

See you out there.

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-04-07 09:22:36 UTC-05:00
Squished -
 
That's really cool y'all are enjoying it!
 
There have been a precious few letterboxers around SA for a good while, a few have given up along the way, but some of them have kept the faith that others would eventually join them.  You will have even more fun as you help build the area and suddenly, one day, y'all will all look back and realize there are bunches of boxes in your area!
 
I say this because my area - northeast Texas (Cherokee County specifically, in my case) - was like that when I started three years ago.  Seven boxes within two hours of me.  Now, thanks to all the letterboxers who have come along in the meantime, most of us can go out on any given day and find new boxes within an hour or so.  I was thinking about that yesterday when I took a couple hours off from carving to go boxing and realized that wayyy back then I would've just been getting to any new boxes instead of pulling back up into my driveway!

From: Amber (Cooper) Lujan (ladida@rocketmail.com) | Date: 2007-04-09 17:04:11 UTC
Okay, I did the research because all this talk about Earl Abels being
closed was confusing since I just ate there in February, and it didn't
look to be closing at that time.

Now, I'd never been to the original location, so I don't know if the
atmosphere has changed, but here's the story. The old owners sold the
building and closed the business supposedly because the building was
getting old and they were offered a good deal of money for it(by
people who wanted to build a high-rise). But, some old customers, and
smart investors, bought the business, and it is relocated on Austin
Highway.

The neat thing is that the new owners brought all the old fixtures
(including "Curly" the waiter statue and the outdoor sign) to the new
location. Not only that, they rehired the entire staff of the old
Earl Abels. So, really all that's changed is the location and the
owners. The food's the same. The cooks are the same. The waitstaff
is the same.

So, when you come to S.A., you can still have breakfast at Earl Abels.

Here's a news story about it all:
http://sanantonio.bizjournals.com/sanantonio/stories/2006/03/13/daily22.html

Okay, I did the research because all this talk about Earl Abels being
closed was confusing since I just ate there in February, and it didn't
look to be closing at that time.

Now, I'd never been to the original location, so I don't know if the
atmosphere has changed, but here's the story. The old owners sold the
building and closed the business supposedly because the building was
getting old and they were offered a good deal of money for it(by
people who wanted to build a high-rise). But, some old customers, and
smart investors, bought the business, and it is relocated on Austin
Highway.

The neat thing is that the new owners brought all the old fixtures
(including "Curly" the waiter statue and the outdoor sign) to the new
location. Not only that, they rehired the entire staff of the old
Earl Abels. So, really all that's changed is the location and the
owners. The food's the same. The cooks are the same. The waitstaff
is the same.

So, when you come to S.A., you can still have breakfast at Earl Abels.
