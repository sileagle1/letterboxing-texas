From: Kirbert (PalmK@nettally.com) | Date: 2007-06-26 11:05:58 UTC-04:00
jessarms wrote:

> I am a new letterboxer and really want to hide my first box. I have
> found 10 boxes AND a hitchhiker so far. Making a stamp seems like a
> daunting task and will probably keep me from hiding many boxes. Can I
> have a stamp custom made to match the location or does it have to be
> homemade?

You can just use a store-bought stamp, it's just that most boxers
would prefer that you didn't. If you just happen to find one that
*perfectly* fits the location, it might even be considered
acceptable.

Custom made stamps are just great. The problem is they're expensive.
As a general rule, people interested in a hobby such as this one are
more interested in doing things for themselves than paying someone
else to do it.

On AQ right now, Gillespie Tribe is putting together a scheme by
which people who enjoy carving more than planting can be matched up
with people who want to plant but don't want to carve. I'm in the
first group, preparing to send out several stamps.

I would recommend that you try carving, simply because you may find
you enjoy it! If you try it and find it's not your thing, by all
means either buy custom made stamps or arrange to plant someone
else's stamps.

Try this web site:

http://www.nettally.com/palmk/Rubber_Stamp_Carving_Tips.html

That might make carving a bit less daunting -- or a bit more, I
dunno.

-- Kirbert
