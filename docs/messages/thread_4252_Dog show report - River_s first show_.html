Dog show report - River's first show.

From: Mike Olbrisch (mike-2007@elp.rr.com) | Date: 2009-10-03 21:00:10 UTC-06:00

Good evening.

Dog show report - 2 and 3 Oct 2009.

2 Oct 2009. River and I are entered in Rally Novice B. Rally because
it is fun, and easy for beginners. Novice because River is a Novice dog. B -
because I put a title on another dog - Parker. Were River my first show dog, I
would be in the A class.

Every team (1 competitor and 1 dog is a team) starts out with 100
points, and points are deducted for errors. The number of points deducted
depends on the severity of the error. Some errors can get you disqualified
(D-Qed). Example - Pooping in the ring = D-Q!!! You must score a 70 or above
to pass, but each and every team scoring a 70 or above is "Qualified" and gets
one point towards the title and a green qualifying ribbon. Three points
(qualifying scores) and you earn the title. The top 4 scoring teams also get a
ribbon for first through fourth place.

Now - many of you know River and I have been training together almost
from his birth. A solid year of training - as he is one year old now. That
said - he is still VERY MUCH a puppy, and he has a small attention span.
Sometimes working with him is difficult, and it is better to quit than to get
mad. So you have to understand - while River is a good pup, and the future
looks bright, right now I would be happy if he just held it together long enough
to make a score of 70.

There were 6 competitors entered in my class on Friday. When I went to
pick up my number and the course map, I learned that I was number 30 and would
be the last one in the ring. Some say they save the best for last. Yeah -
right. I took the course map back to out canopy and River and I did some
practice. We did OK. But things have a way of going wrong when you get in the
ring.

One competitor didn't show up. So 5 stood ready to go into the ring at
1030. The first competitor had a really good dog, the dog did it all and did it
right. The owner mis-read a sign and performed the second stage backward. She
and her dog were D-Qed. The second, third and fourth competitors all had dogs
much like River, sorta ready for this, but maybe not so much. Each team had
their troubles, but all made it without being disqualified. With only 4 dogs in
the running, unless River and I got D-Qed, we were assured 2 ribbons. One for
qualifying, and one for first through fourth place, whatever we earned.

Finally came our turn. We went in, River gave me a nice sit, and the
judge asked if I was ready. Was I ready? NO! Well - I was... Sorta. I was
not real sure about River. His attention was drifting all over the place. But
it was too late to back out now. So I gave him a quick kiss on his head and
whispered in his ear "OK - Lets have some fun". Then I said "Yes - sir, we're
ready".

The Judge said "FORWARD", and we started off. First stage was a heeling
weave around the orange cones. It was tight, the cones were close to the ring
side and River is a big dog. But we made it. Stages 2 through 11 were not a
problem for us. Stage 12 is where we lost it. Call Front, 1-2-3 steps back,
forward.

Here is how it should work. While heeling along - I must abruptly stop
and take a step back while calling "RIVER - FRONT". River needs to pass me,
turn right 180, then sit in front of me looking up at my face. He did that.
Perfect. Then I take one step back while calling him to "FRONT". River must
step forward and sit in front of me looking up at my face again at the same time
I move. Then two steps back, then finally three steps back. Then I move
forward and River falls into heel position and we continue.

On the 3-steps, River was slow coming off his sit to follow, so he made
up for it by leaping past me and sitting to my right looking behind me. He was
distracted watching the dog in the next ring doing a retrieve. So -3 points for
the slow move and -10 points for an incorrectly completed stage. We lost
another 3 points for minor errors, and came out of the ring with an 84.

Now - I was very happy about this. An 84 is a qualifying score. Not
great - but qualifying. The judge tried to explain to me where we messed up. I
didn't hear him, I was just too happy we were still above 70. We left the ring
and I gave River a big hug and a thank-you. I was happy. Moni and some of the
other competitors said River did good. Well - they always say that. I tell
them that too.

The judge finished up the paperwork, wrote the scores on the
score-board, and called the 4 qualified teams into the ring for awards. As I
came in the ring I was SHOCKED to see that 84 was the high score. River and I
got First place - the BLUE RIBBON! Sure, we messed up. But I guess the others
messed up even more. As the judge handed me my green and blue ribbons, he
told me River was a fine dog and had a great future. Shoot - we know that. I
thanked him and accepted the ribbons. 1 down, two to go.

3 Oct 2009. Today's show was another surprise - but in a different way.
It rained all day. We were wet and cold. I did my very best to see that River
and Daisy were sheltered. I should have worn long pants too, but I had two
light jackets, so I was OK. At about 0830 I went to the ring to get my number
and a course map. First off, all the competitors showed up. So there were 6 of
us. I was certainly not expecting a repeat of Friday. I was again just hoping
to keep River's attention long enough to get in and out of the ring with at
least 70 points left. This course had a stage where River was expected to get
"DOWN" on his belly for me. Hmmmm - never tried that in the wet grass before.
We better practice. Well - he wasn't happy doing it, but he did it.

At 1015 6 competitors stood ready to enter the ring. All were doing
good. No DQs today, so I expected we were certainly out of the winner's circle.
Still, I had hopes of qualifying. The others made little mistakes. Just little
things. They all looked so good. Then it was our turn. Well - the pressure to
place was off, the others did such a fine job. So we just needed to get a 70
and another green ribbon.

Now, you have to understand... I never see River work. I see his nose
and maybe part of his head while we are heeling. But I do not see what he is
doing. He is there, or he isn't. In the ring is too late to be looking back.
Win or lose, my job on the team is to walk the course, read the signs, and give
the commands. River's job is to heel and follow the commands. I hope.

Well - the judge called for team 30. River and I entered the ring. The
judge looked small hunched in her rain coat. She asked if we were ready. Well
- yes - better than yesterday because there was no pressure. We just wanted our
70 points and get out of the rain. She said "FORWARD" and off we went. On the
second stage River had to come front and sit, then swing to my left back into
heel position when told to do so. When I called "SWING" River leapt in the air
and almost spun in flight to land next to me. I almost lost it there, Parker
used to do it just like that. But I kept moving. It seemed like just seconds
later we were finished. It went by so quickly, and I couldn't remember River
doing any major errors. He had one sit that was off a bit sideways. I didn't
make any mistakes either. As we left the ring everyone was applauding us. I
went over to Moni, and she told me that River was having a blast in the ring,
and the judge was smiling the whole time.

Another competitor came to me and said I needed to check the score
board. I wonder what could be wrong - we couldn't be D-Qed. WOW - 99 points!
First place again. We entered the ring for the awards. And yes - River earned
a second blue ribbon for us. I am happy.

Two down, one to go. I can't wait to see what we do on Sunday.

Mike - KD9KC.
El Paso, Tx.

> up for it by leaping past me and sitting to my right looking behind me. He was
> distracted watching the dog in the next ring doing a retrieve. So -3 points for

> another 3 points for minor errors, and came out of the ring with an 84.
>
> Now - I was very happy about this. An 84 is a qualifying score. Not
> great - but qualifying. The judge tried to explain to me where we messed up. I

> them that too.

> 3 Oct 2009. Today's show was another surprise - but in a different way.

> us. I was certainly not expecting a repeat of Friday. I was again just hoping

> At 1015 6 competitors stood ready to enter the ring. All were doing
> good. No DQs today, so I expected we were certainly out of the winner's circle.

>
> Now, you have to understand... I never see River work. I see his nose

> 70 points and get out of the rain. She said "FORWARD" and off we went. On the
> second stage River had to come front and sit, then swing to my left back into
> heel position when told to do so. When I called "SWING" River leapt in the air
> and almost spun in flight to land next to me. I almost lost it there, Parker
> used to do it just like that. But I kept moving. It seemed like just seconds
> later we were finished. It went by so quickly, and I couldn't remember River
> doing any major errors. He had one sit that was off a bit sideways. I didn't
> make any mistakes either. As we left the ring everyone was applauding us. I

>
