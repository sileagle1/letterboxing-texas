Texas Trip Report

From: (dlkotlarek@gmail.com) | Date: 2019-03-16 03:47:21 UTC

Martini Man & I have been to letterboxing events in TX three times before and decided to venture over again this year to attend another TALE event.  We left Tucson on Thursday FEB 28 and stopped in El Paso to visit the Chamizal National Memorial (we’re trying to visit all 400+ National Park sites).  After spending the night in Van Horn, we started the next morning by finding the “Dragon Ring” box.  Then we drove to Kerrville, finding the “Your Local Grocer”.  We appreciated the interesting background info about this chain.  That evening we enjoyed reconnecting with some of our TX letterboxing friends and meeting some new people at the Pint & Plow.


It was a dreary, misty day on Saturday so when we arrived at the event I stayed in our van for a bit to solve Grizzled Griffin’s devious logic puzzle.  After we got the other info needed, we headed out on the trail to find the first container with some of the missing info.  We were pleased that it was indeed where I thought!  We had initially thought we would just find the first one and then return to the event HQ, but we decided to just keep going.  And going…. and going……..  Eventually we found the final box and stamp and returned for a late lunch several hours after we had started our journey. It was long hike but we had the thrill of being the first finder.  Live long and prosper GG!


After enjoying some of your fantastic potluck food (who made the delicious shredded Buffalo Chicken?) we headed out into the mist again.  Our total box count for the day was 23 boxes found – some new for the event and some of the older ones in the park.  We returned for another round of eating, chatting with some folks we hadn’t met before and the entertaining Bingo game. GG & FM are one lucky couple!


The weather on Sunday wasn’t much better, but we tromped along the trails in the chilly damp weather and found 31 more boxes before we hit the road for Cedar Creek.   Even though the weather wasn’t very good over the weekend, it was a nice park and we enjoyed finding so many great stamps and spending time with the TX boxing community.


We had hoped to start boxing at McKinney Roughs on Monday, but it was much too cold for us (we live in AZ now and can’t tolerate the cold anymore).  Instead we headed into Austin and enjoyed spending the day at the fabulous Bullock Texas History Museum.  The sun did appear for a bit on Tuesday, but again the temperatures were too cold for us to be out on the trails.  So we toured the Texas State Capitol & the Visitor Center, admired the Driskill Hotel, had the famous burgers at Casino El Camino and stopped in at The Contemporary Art museum.


We were staying at an AirBnB rental in Cedar Creek, so while were in the area we checked out the famous Buc-ee’s, the Southside BBQ , the H-E-B grocery store and Blue Bell ice cream.  Lots of tasty stuff! 


On Wednesday it was FINALLY warm enough to go to the park to do some boxing (wearing all our layers)!  We got warmed up a bit going down and up the hills under the zip lines and it was fun to start finding some of the boxes planted for the L&B event in October.  The temperatures improved every day after that, but it was overcast almost every day.  Of our 11 days in TX, it was sunny 1.5 days.


We were able to spend 4 full, VERY BUSY days at McKinney Roughs and were impressed with so many of the excellent carvings.  We also saw the dorms and group buildings and it looked like the perfect place for a large event.  We also liked the many different trails in the park.  What we did NOT like was all the stinging nettles in some areas of the park! 


Speaking of those types of plants - along one trail I saw an odd flat flower and on another trail we saw a strange orange substance on a vine.  After we got home and did some research we found out the flower was a Sundew and the orange stuff was Orange Slime Mold.  Carnivorous plants, slime mold and stinging nettles – what’s not to like about Texas?!?!  At least we didn’t see any snakes this year!

With the upcoming summer camps at the park, we were a bit worried about the longevity of some of the other boxes that are very close to the trails.


We were sorry we couldn’t stay longer to look for the remaining boxes, but were very happy to have added 192 images to our logbooks from the 20th anniversary event.   Congratulations to all of you that hosted the big event!


We left Cedar Creek on Sunday morning, after a quick stop to purchase some pecans and find “Ms. Pearl’s Letterbox”.  Later that day we stopped at LBJ’s Boyhood Home and his Ranch.  We always enjoy learning more about the people featured at all the Natl Park sites and the LBJ sites were well done.  The weather was overcast, but the rain held off so we were able to stroll outside to see the various buildings as well as spend time inside.  We spent the night in Fort Stockton.  We originally planned to head down to the Fort Davis site, but the forecast called for rain, so we postponed that and instead drove all the way back to our home in the Tucson area, making our first stop ever at a What-a-Burger since we had learned it was a TX-born chain.


Thanks for your Texas hospitality and all the great letterboxes!
