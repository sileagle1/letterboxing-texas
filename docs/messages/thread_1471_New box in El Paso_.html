Good morning,
 
    In about 1 hour I will be on my way out to place my 4th letterbox.  It will be called The El Paso Tin Mines.  To get to it will take a 3 mile hike with an 880 foot elevation gain.  It is an unknown fact that the only active TIN mines in the continental United States were located in El Paso, on the eastern side of the Franklin Mountains.  The stamp (like all the stamps I have done) is representative of the area.
 
    So to date, I have 4 letterboxes out.  The fires 3 are:
 
#1. The High Point of El Paso is on top of N. Franklin Mountain, the highest point in the area.  It is 4 miles and 3050 feet up to get to the letterbox.  The views from up there are spectacular.
 
#2.  Make the Rangers Smile a PARK-N-GRAB situation... EXCEPT - it is very well camouflaged.  One letterboxer has already reported a NO-FIND....  but it is there right in plain sight!
 
#3.  The Nature Walk.  This is a 2-FOR-1 letterbox.  If you are going to find this one, also look up Silver Eagle's MOGOLLON MASK.  They are a short walk from each other.  These are not quite PARK-N-GRAB boxes, but not much worse either.
 
#4.  This will be the Tin Mines - which will be in place by this afternoon.
 
#5   This one will be 60 miles from my home.  It will be a very short hike in a pleasant area, but again VERY WELL hidden.  Frustratingly so in fact.  But it will be worth the trip - especially if you want one of the finest steaks in Texas.
 
There is one other active box in the area.  It is simply called Franklin Mountains.  I know EXACTLY which rock it is under.  But it is the only one left for me to hunt....  so I am saving it.
 
There are two others that are abandoned in the area.  I hope to investigate them tomorrow if the rangers can fit me in, they are in a place that has been closed off.
 
So with luck, there will be 8 boxes in El Paso.  I hope it is enough of a start to get something going here.
 
Y-all enjoy your day.
 
Mike.
