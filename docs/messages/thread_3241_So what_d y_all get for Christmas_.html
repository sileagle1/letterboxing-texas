So what'd y'all get for Christmas?

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-12-26 19:34:36 UTC-06:00
Letterboxing-wise (or otherwise, if you got something you really want to brag about!):
 
I finally, FINALLY got a magnifying light, and it works BE-utifully. 
 
I got a mapbook of Texas with every little country road listed on it (presumably) - organized like a Thomas Bros map, but for the whole state and with urban areas de-emphasized (easy enough to get detail maps of cities).. 
 
I didn't yet get the maps of the National Forests in Texas that I've asked for for three years, but I finally decided I'm going to just get them myself. 
 
I also got a new set of X-acto knives - my old ones were pretty much shot - did anyone else not know you could wear out X-acto knives?
AND - I've got five solid days of letterboxing ahead of me!
 
And perhaps best of all, I got a new crock pot so that I can set supper on in the morning before we leave, and go letterboxing and come home to supper ready to serve!
 
I must say - I think this is the best Christmas we've had in a really, really long time.  Rather than getting caught up in the "must-do's" and the trappings of traditions, we did only what we love about Christmas and spent lots of time among family and friends.  As the day rocketed in on us we still had not decorated, but instead of cracking the whip and getting the tree up and decorated and all the other things we are "supposed" to do, we pulled only those decorations out that we really love - my Christmas village and our collections of angels and Santas and snowmen.  We dragged out the Christmas dishes and cleaned (yes, we cleaned!) the living room and the kitchen so we could enjoy the company of the family as they stopped by.  It seemed we had lots of visitors this season and we also made it a point to make treats and share them with our shut-in neighbors so that we got to visit with them as well. 
 
I hope I can remember next year what made this year so wonderful - gifts were great, but the fellowship and time spent lingering on the season was the best!

--
Barefoot Lucy
"It's not about footwear, it's about philosophy"

I got a really nice set of Calphalon cooking pots (do I sound like I’m from the country?—that should be cookware!) that I have been eyeing for a couple of years.  As I was salivating over them in Linen & Things this summer, SunSnake used her cell phone camera to surreptitiously snap a picture of them. Then, when she and Navigator went shopping, they knew exactly what to get.  Gotta love those two!

 

Letterboxing related, and even more exciting than cooking pots:  I got an email today that my stamp for Gibraltar of the Confederacy in Vicksburg, MS, which had previously been reported missing from its box, had turned up in Oregon.  What a thrill!  It was a large, intricate stamp and I had dreaded recarving it. Don’t go running straight over there to find it, it will be a while before I get to Vicksburg to put it back.
