Heh heh heh – today I am going to do JUST THAT! Get out and enjoy the weather. But not the way you think.

Out in the living room piled on the coffee table is my shooting gear. Within an hour I will be arriving at a friend’s house. I will toss my gear into his truck, and we will drive to the Hueco Tanks Gun Range. There I will spend a few very enjoyable hours testing my lately un-used skills of hitting a very small black dot from 200 yards standing, 200 yards sitting, 300 yards prone and 600 yards prone. 22 shots at each position. Oh – did I say – NO SCOPES? Iron sights only, just using a standard battle-rifle.

Today I will be shooting a beautifully restored WW-II relic. It is a genuine M-1 Garand, re-barreled in .308 caliber, and glass-bedded in a laminate stock. If I do my part, the rifle is fully capable of the task. During WW-II these fine rifles were used successfully by our fathers and grandfathers to kill German and Japanese soldiers at ranges up to 1000 yards. Their only "flaw" if you want to call it that, is that they only hold 8 shots at a time. But they reload quicker than an M-14 or M-16, and they handle better with no magazine sticking out the bottom. JMHO of course.

I have no chance of winning the match today. One of my friends shooting today just took 4th place in the nation at Camp Perry in August. This is the national finals held every year in Ohio. If I shoot my best score ever, and he does really poorly, he would still likely win. But like letterboxing, it isn’t about the numbers. Oh - he is also a Ham Operator, so we really are good friends on and off the range.

When I get home, I will clean and put away the rifle and gear. Then it is back to the kitchen, where we have discovered that I need to install yet another 120 vac outlet for the new range. With the cabinets coming in starting tomorrow – I don’t have a lot of time left to finish my part.

Oh – in between somewhere I need to help the son-in-law trouble-shoot his pick-up truck. He has something terribly wrong with the wiring. It almost caught fire last night, the wires were smoking. It melted the negative battery clamp. It is parked in front of my house, we drove them home last night.

Those letterboxes will be there tomorrow. There is only one long-range match a month. And I have not shot beyond 400 yards in over a year. I couldn't rightly call myself a sniper any more, although I am probably still much better than the average soldier. Glad my son isn't shooting today, or the best I could hope for is third.

I hope your day is as fun as mine! And it WILL BE FUN!

Y-all take care.
