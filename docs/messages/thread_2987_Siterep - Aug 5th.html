Siterep - Aug 5th

From: tigger32167 (tigger32167@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-08-07 03:49:37 UTC
We started at 6:45 am. Our first stop was River Legacy Trails in
Arlington so that Wag Time could place a neat series called Four
Legged Friends which is dedicated to her beloved pets both past and
present. We then went to Randol Mil Park in Arlington and found A
Gecko's Tale. Nice stamp, but didnt find any geckos. We then went
to find Lake Chase #1 at Lake Arlington. The location of the box
has numerous newly downed trees and part of the area where the box
should be is under water. We then went to find Wassily and Joan at
Play in Gene Schrikland (sp?) park in Arlington. Instead of the
letterbox, we found a geocache..but no letterbox. We then went to
Elkins Park in Dalworthington Gardens to find Man's Best Friend.
The clues were very confusing and we didnt find the box. We did
find A Tree Grew in the same park. Our next stop was FJ "Red" Kane
Park in Arlington and picked up the Pepper box. The logbook was
soaked and did not have a logbook to replace it with. Our next stop
was Cliff Nelson Recreation Center and found the Jazz Quartet Bass
Guitar box. We then attempted the Gir Box at the Putt Putt place in
Haltam City, but did not find it. As we left this location on Hwy
820 I noticed a guy on a motorcycle who actually had shoulder pads
and a back pad on along with a helmet. As I was watching him in my
rear view mirror, I saw him pop a wheelie. A couple of minutes
later he passed us going about 70 mph and as he passed he poppled
another wheelie in the fast lane. He then proceeded to stand on the
motorcycle (not on the seat) and then when he sat down he poppled
another wheelie...strange things happen in the Dallas area. We then
went to North Park and found the Texas State Symbols - Armadillo.
Great stamp. We dropped off the Sgt HH in this box. Went to look
for What's in a Name at Fossil Creek Park in Richland Hills, but did
not find the box. We then went to find the Catch the Wind box in
Bedford, but there was no pile of rocks. It looks as though the
limbs had recently been cut, so it may have been discovered. This
was still a neat site. We then went to Bedford Cemetery to find the
Bedford Settlers Reunion box. The 3 trunk tree that it was in had
lost one of its trunks, which had fallen. The rocks were scattered
all over, and there was no sign of a letterbox. We then went to
Grapevine Cemetery in Grapevine and found the Lone Star State box.
Next we went to Heritage Park in Grapevine to look for Moose-ic Man
and it appeared that it must have been washed away. Our last box
for the day was at Delaney Vineyards in Grapevine. The Delaney
Vineyard box was right where it should have been. Back home we went.
JB Kokopelli

PS On Saturday we also did find the Time Warp box at the Brazos
Drive in Theatre in Granbury.
