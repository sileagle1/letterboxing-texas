TALE 8 Thanks

From: sileagle1 (sileagle@windstream.net) | Date: 2011-03-07 16:24:11 UTC
Well, another TALE has come and gone and I hope everyone enjoyed it; I know I did. Thankfully, nobody went to the hospital this year and I hope everyone made it home safely. I want to thank everyone for helping make it a success, but especially the following: Open Space for going to the Ranger Office and booking the warm Activity Center for us to use, Sandibox for donating the Smithsonian Magazine for the raffle, Boots Tex & Lone Star Quilter for bringing the copy machine and several nice table boxes, and Blue Butterfly & Lucy Lockett for another creative way of passing on the TX Traveling Circus. I also want to thank everyone that donated raffle prizes. We hope to do it again and bring even better prizes next year. Also thanks to Bell Motel for making eagle & bear treats. They were great to look at and even better to eat, though I felt like a cannibal :)

I have posted pictures from the Native Feast Event dinner and 8th TX Event in the Photos section of this list:

http://groups.yahoo.com/group/letterboxingtexas/photos/album/1953303838/pic/list

http://groups.yahoo.com/group/letterboxingtexas/photos/album/1937929927/pic/list

Feel free to post your pictures, especially of GG modeling his "prize"!

Silver Eagle

From: Vicki Bell (vicki@bellmotel.com) | Date: 2011-03-09 12:56:25 UTC-06:00
SandiBox, I want you to know that I am now the proud owner of the April 1998 Smithsonian Magazine which you so generously donated to the TALE8 raffle.  My husband, Whiz Gee, gallantly bought it for me from the raffle winner, a startled Pocahontas (daughter of Spyker and KerrMark), who had no idea that a grownup would pay that much for an old magazine!  (Then again, someone just paid $1.1 million for a comic book.)  I treasure it.  Since I have never had a personal traveler stamp, I am considering carving the cover for my personal traveler, with the clue being, “What did my husband purchase for me after the TALE8 raffle?”  Wish you could have been there!  And thanks for the treasure!  -- Bell Motel
