Jefferson area

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-01-23 07:14:50 UTC-06:00
I thought I would point out to anyone interested that "Mitch and Tami" are on the plant again.  They planted two boxes almost three years ago in Jefferson, but never anything else.  I contacted them about eight months ago and they consider themselves to be active, and sure enough, they've planted a couple new boxes in the last month or so!  I don't believe they are members of this list, nor do they communicate with any other boxers as far as I know, but they are up there and sticking with it.  Although I have not yet gotten to Jefferson to find their (or Silver Eagle's) boxes, their clues are intriguing and I'm hoping to find them when I make a run to Shreveport. 
 
That far northeast corner of the state (i.e. east of Tyler and north of I-20) is far behind the rest of our LBNA-defined region, so I sure hope that them planting will help bring that area around.

From: Baileys Bunch (baileys_bunch@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-01-23 14:38:11 UTC



Funny you mentioned these two.
They do not know this but they are the reason for my boxing obsession.
One of my other hobbies is visiting haunted locations. Jefferson, TX
is full of them.
My girlfriends and I were there a couple of years ago and did several
ghost tour in this quaint little town. "The Grove" was one of the
places on the list. This is Mitch & Tami's home.
He gives the tours. And yes their house is haunted.
At the end of the tour he tells of his writing. Some of his books are
about haunted places he's been and some are normal reading. I bought
one of his books and he even signed it for me. (They are such down to
earth people)
Anyway, after getting home and reading my new book (another hobby-reading)
I wanted to find other books he had done. I logged on the computer and
looked him up.
I bounced on to his site that had some of his resent vacations. I
decided to read on, and to
my surprise he had gone to some of the places he had asked my group
about. He had learned from us about some of the locations we had been
to in Mississippi.
They had posted pictures of these places and had one snap shot that
said something about taking a little time out to do some letterboxing.
What on earth is letterboxing I wondered?
He had a link to LNA. The more I read on the more excited I got. This
really sounded like fun. I sat on pins & needles until Hobby Lobby
opened up the next morning to get a stamp & logbook. Then I had to go
onto work. (Bummer) But, there was lunch. So when 12 o'clock rolled
around I was off in search of the first box.
And this is how one hobby can snowball into another.
Bailey's Bunch
