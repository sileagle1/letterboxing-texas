Runaway Circus Series

From: tombned1 (tombned1@hotmail.com) | Date: 2005-08-17 01:32:40 UTC
Last Thursday I went out to find the Runaway Circus series. Found
all in the series but 1st stamp "Zuberi" eluded me. - I'm not going
to say that its missing, as this is my first time boxing in the wilds
an I certainly am not the best at my lefts and rights. I found the
directions to be "spot on" if I took bigger than normal for me steps,
but I'm short.

Very well placed and described. Did I say I was short, but I got "Li
Mei". I was warned it was 7 feet off the ground so I brought a reach
tool.

"Chima the Giraffe" had a small juvinile snake located near it.
Unknown variety as it slithered away rather quickly and I was using
my prod to disturb the trail before me.

As I was stamping in at Circus wagon City of Houston worker came by
to tell me he was getting ready to lock up, and that the hours for
the park were from dawn to dusk. Even though the sun was still about
1 1/2 hours from setting. I asked for three minutes and he was
agreeable to that he drove to the entrance to wait for my exit.
Circus wagon was re-hid and I was on my way.

You will need more than the deepwoods variety of OFF repelant to go
after this series. After about 20 minutes I no longer noticed the
mosquitos, although they still were biting.

Lots of large orb weaving spiders along the trail - be watchful for
webs!

Had Ivy Block on so I didn't contract P.I. it was everywhere but I
also covered up. Who knew Mosquitoes have no problem biting knees
through denim?

Thanks Birds of a feather family for the wonderful series of fun and
beautiful stamps!

Now that I've had a taste of wilderness boxes maybe I'll be hitting
the wild trails more often.
