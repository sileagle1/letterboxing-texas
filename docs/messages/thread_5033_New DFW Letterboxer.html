From: ChrisC (cmcalverley@yahoo.com) | Date: 2012-05-31 16:13:23 UTC
Hi Everyone!

I thought that I would pop in and introduce myself. I just discovered letterboxing about a week ago through a friend's posting of her adventure on Facebook. I have already dragged my hubby out twice and found 7 boxes. The first one was especially exciting! I am totally hooked. I went right out and bought the carving kit and carved 3 stamps. Still need LOTS of practice.

We live in Lewisville, just north of Dallas and Fort Worth. My children are grown and married. When I first saw my friends post I thought that this was a great activity for families. But now I think it is even better for just the "grownups". I keep thinking that to traipse my younger children through all of that bush in the heat would have left us all miserable. I would definitely have had to choose my adventures more carefully. I am hoping to convince my daughter to join me soon.

Hubby is a work-from-home IT geek and I mostly volunteer and also have an online job that is quite flexible. Hubby's job has him working every weekend, so I have a lot of free weekend time and would love to get together with other letterboxers in the area.

From: Bloomin Gramma Jo (mjrekedal@gmail.com) | Date: 2012-06-01 12:30:39 UTC
Personal stamps, event stamps, exchanges. You are asking the right questions, ChrisC. From my limited experience, most folks have just one personal stamp, and once you exchange when meeting a person, that is it. Some people, however, have one stamp they use for exchanges, and a separate stamp for stamping in on letterbox logbooks.

Also, many l'boxers carry any number of Personal Travelers. A PT is an additional stamp that requires the seeker to ask for. Sometimes there is some sort of riddle which must be solved before the holder will reveal his/her traveler. PTs are all listed on AQ at the event site.
Then there are special event stamps carved by someone. It commemorates the event itself, and is only available at the event. Just ask for it. Also look around for boxes hidden in plain sight.

I'm sure you will have many more questions along the way. There is a l'box list for newbies, but I don't know the address. Anyone else out there to that info?

Happy 'boxing, Chris. I've done little boxing in SanAntonio and would love to meet you there too.

BGJo
