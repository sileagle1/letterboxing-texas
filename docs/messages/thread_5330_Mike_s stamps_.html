I know a lot of people frowned at that.  But if you're really interested, I think I have an E-mail saved of the exact information and I will post it if you like.

From: (sileagle1@yahoo.com) | Date: 2014-02-25 06:19:25 UTC-08:00

For the record, nobody I know "frowns upon" stamps created for boxers by professionals.  Gryzzled Gryphon does it all the time and we love his boxes.  The only thing frowned upon is just buying a stamp in the store that has no meaning.  If you put time and effort into creating the image, then placing the box in an interesting place with good clues, it will be a welcomed box no matter who does the carving.  That said, I do think carving your own stamps is worth trying if for no other reason than it can be fun, and who doesn't have time for a little fun!  Another thing I have found is that carving reduces stress; at least once you get good enough to be satisfied with it.  I used to run a lot to stay in shape and reduce stress, but with aging knees I'm relying more on carving than running these days.  It's not for everyone, but you will never know until you try.  Believe me, I'm no artist and never would have believed I could carve, let alone enjoy it.  So if I can, you can.

Silver Eagle

From: Christine Borgfeld (christine@borgfeld.net) | Date: 2014-02-25 19:13:58 UTC-06:00

If anyone wants a reference, I’ve used rubberstamps.net in the past.  They have a variety of different sizes and mounting options.  You can have them wood mounted, or order them unmounted (you just get the red rubber) and can mount them on thick foam or on wood yourself.  I was happy with the quality – these are the stamps in the last box for the Dartmoor series in Stephen F Austin park.
