TALE6 Recap

From: Silver Eagle (sileagle@windstream.net) | Date: 2009-03-09 14:27:34 UTC
Well, another TX Annual LB Event has come and gone, and I must say it was one of my favorites. The weather was good, the people were fun, and the place was beautiful and welcoming. Even though our planned Demos fell through, I don't think anyone became bored. In fact, the relaxed pace with no scheduled activities was kind of nice and let people visit or letterbox more. The only dark spot was not having Barefoot Lucy and her family. They had to leave early Saturday to help a friend who had a death in the family. You were missed, BL!

As usual, the post-event "campfire" (burn ban prevented an actual fire) was a highlight for me. Wanda & Pete showed up early to present Baby Bear and I with our Lifetime Achievement Letterboxing Awards and it was fun revisiting with these icons of American Letterboxing before they left to go night boxing. As others began arriving the mood became jovial (helped with "special" hot chocolate) as many stories were swapped. I especially enjoyed hearing Gryzzled Gryphon's version of his "coming out". I just wish we could have continued all night.

I'd like to thank Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter for bringing the copy machine, making jambalaya, and just for being there. You epitomize everything good about TX Letterboxing and I can't imagine an Event without you. Special kudos for your creative circus poster clue, too, and to Pacifickle for figuring it out so soon.

I'd also like to thank all the out-of-state visitors, too. Lionsmane & Astro D came again from New Mexico, JBanana from Louisiana, Goofy Girl from Oklahoma, Granny Butterfly from Ohio, and Wanda & Pete from Rhode Island. I guess our Event has become too big even for Texas!

And of course I have to thank all of you TX boxers for making it such a success with all your great food, conversation and creativity. You are the best!

Just before leaving the park, I talked with a Ranger who was very nice (he is the one that gives talks about the park history) and I praised his park and its acceptance of us letterboxers. Y'all should feel proud that you reinforced the good will we have at this park by your demeanor this weekend. He mentioned that more trails were due to be opened in the next few years, so we might just have to revisit.

I hope to post some pictures soon and if any of you have some, feel free to post them, too. Thanks again for a great time!

Silver Eagle

From: Puddle Splasher (cjagger405@earthlink.net) | Date: 2009-03-09 16:38:14 UTC
I definitely vote for a "revisit" to Mission Tejas. There were so many trails that I hadn't even discovered until the event was over - all crying out for letterboxes! Next time, we need to invite Ranger John to come to our campfire and tell us some of his great stories about the history of the area. He was passing by Viewfinder and me on Sunday morning as we were getting ready to hit the trails again. He pulled over and rolled down the window and asked if we'd remembered to change our clocks for daylight savings. We told him we'd decided to ignore the clock until the weekend was over. He also asked VF if she'd mind sacrificing her Miata by leaving her top down while we were hiking so it would invite the rain to come. She selfishly declined, so the entire weight of the lack of rain now falls on her shoulders.
Puddle Splasher

Echo and ditto to all that's been said about TALE 6, SE and BB and BT and LSQ (and the copier) and the beautiful Mission Tejas SP.  I'd also like to thank the weather and the early-blooming dogwoods and redbuds for cooperating with us to add to the experience, the red fox that Puddle Splasher and I spotted in the park Friday evening about dusk, and the tree frog on the wall in the women's restroom near the mission that did NOT jump on me (thanks, little frog).  The entire weekend was a pleasant, fun, and relaxing experience in a beautiful location with some really nice people and some exceptional letterboxes.  For those who didn't hike the Steep Step Trail to the CCC bathtubs, plan for that next time -- but wear good hiking shoes or wear your goat feet!  Thanks again, everyone.
Viewfinder
Oh, and for anyone interested in knowing this bit of trivia: There are 10 historical markers in the 20 miles between Crockett and Weches, and another 10 historical markers in the 10 miles between Weches and Alto.  Very historic area!  And that's just along the roadside, not counting all that history IN the parks, cemeteries, and forest nearby!  (Now who would actually count those things?  We did!)
