From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2010-02-06 10:37:09 UTC-06:00
I went and bought about 40 of Walmart's Mainstay brand lock and lock knock
off containers in a wide variety of shapes and sizes yesterday to get my out
of control kitchen tamed. They were really, REALLY marked down, so I
stocked up!

HOWEVER, before I put anything in them, I ran them through the dishwasher.
This morning as I am tackling my project, I'm finding that the lids on about
90% of them are not working very well at all. I checked them all before I
bought them and they were fine, so what this tells me is that the dishwasher
did a number on them. They'll still work fine for what I'm doing and
hopefully the lids will reshape themselves to fit the containers over time.

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2010-02-06 14:59:59 UTC-06:00

You're welcome. I had that in the back of my mind myself as I bought them for home, but now that I've finished my organizing project, I most definitely WON'T use them for letterboxing! It was really frustrating to get them sealed well, and all I could think of was boxers sitting on the damp ground in brisk temperatures out on the trail trying desperately to get all four latches done, and finally giving up. I'm sure hoping the seals will reform to the containers, but if they don't, I'll be replacing them a few at a time....HOWEVER,

I'm so excited to have finally gotten all my stuff into containers so I can see it all! In the course of repackaging everything that was in awkward containers (i.e. paper bags like flour or plastic bags like brown sugar), I found stuff I haven't seen in years. As an example, I use instant coffee once a year to make a particular cookie at Christmas. Every year, I hunt high and low for the instant coffee I bought the year before, then give up and buy some new. When I started emptying the cabinets, I found FOUR little jars of instant coffee, two of which had expired. I had several half bags of flour, I can't tell you how many weird off the wall only use occasionally things that were expired. I threw a whole trash can full of expired food away. Also, had chocolate bark that had been nibbled by the flying raccoon from before Christmas. Opening my cabinets was a dangerous affair - but it looks so nice now that I've gone ahead and tackled the refrigerator! Woohoo! Next is my workbench outside, where I carve...
