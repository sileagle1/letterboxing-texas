The Feud: Baby Bear and IRA Boxer

From: (candj1969@yahoo.com) | Date: 2015-01-28 20:52:21 UTC-08:00

Most of you know of the long-running feud between Baby Bear and IRA Boxer, but it occurs to me that some newbies may be unaware of the history and may not understand the occasional post by one or the other in which some rather rude (even nasty) comments may be made between them.  I'm afraid that some of you might in some way be offended by the repartee, so I want to briefly give you the background behind it.   What I'm about to tell you is true:  A few years ago, a mysterious new letterboxer, known only as IRA Boxer, appeared on the scene, planting boxes and finding some as well.  His signature stamp was a pair of red boxer shorts with white hearts.  He quickly became obnoxious, boasting of his letterboxing skills and posting taunts at some of our letterboxing icons, especially Baby Bear.  He stated that his goal was to place more boxes than said Bear.  Then, as quickly as he appeared, he disappeared.  After a little research, and much to my dismay, I found out that IRA Boxer was dead, found murdered in the Mercer Arboretum.  I thought the story should be told, so, with the help of some of my family, I founded a group to write the true account of the event.  We called ourselves The Texians and here is our account of the death of IRA Boxer (the names have been changed to protect the innocent): http://bootstex.blogspot.com/search?updated-min=2007-01-01T00:00:00-08:00&updated-max=2008-01-01T00:00:00-08:00&max-results=2

Then, the unthinkable happened:  The ghost of IRA Boxer appeared and began to seek revenge for his death.  He apparently saw the account written by The Texians and immediately suspected that the character named Kid Kodiak was his killer.  He thought the name Kid Kodiak was an alias for Baby Bear and began to haunt him, accusing him of murder and other various crimes against nature.  The Feud continues, and heats up leading up to TALE each year.  The Ghost of IRA Boxer has even gained a follower and ally in a seamy character named lb_bozo.  So, if you see disrespectful comments from any of those mentioned above, understand that this is part of the history and lore of Texas letterboxing and is not to be seen as being unwelcoming or disrectful to anyone else, especially newbies.

Boots Tex and The Texians
