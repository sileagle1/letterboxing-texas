From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-04-19 19:54:17 UTC
Well, I'm sure you all have realized that I like mystery boxes. There
are lots of ways to play this lovely little game and lots of levels to
play it on.

We've hidden plenty of straight forward, easy-to-locate boxes
Some boxes that require a hike
Quite a few boxes that you have to solve some sort of puzzle to find
(some puzzles are easy, some are difficult and as people around here
get better and better I search for new ways to raise the level)
and at least one box that we're still waiting for people to realize is
out there.

Different approaches to boxes keeps letterboxing amusing for us, and
hopefully for others as well. I remember how badly we wanted people
to find our first boxes. But personally, I now care much less about
how many people find our boxes, than that the ones who do enjoy the
experience. I love hearing from someone who feels triumphant about
having solved the clues or found a Gramatrick box. Ask Mother of
Five--she struggled with Surrender Dorothy! but I think that struggle
made victory that much the sweeter, my pretty.

Over the last nearly two years, my placing habits (and our finding
habits) have changed and evolved. I expect that evolution will
continue, I'm just not sure how.

Dewberry
