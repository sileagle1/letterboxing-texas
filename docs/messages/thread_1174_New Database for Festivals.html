Re: New Database for Festivals

From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2005-08-10 00:16:35 UTC
no, for this exchange you create a box. You mail the box to someone
else who then plants it in the wild. All you are responsible for as
the carver is the creation of the box. The recipient places it,
creates clues and maintains it--so you have no real control over
where it ends up being planted. The boxes will not necessarily be
placed near the festivals, but are sort of "in the spirit" of which
ever fest or festival was the inspiration. So, you could have a
festival box that is actually 800 miles from its inspiration.

This exchange allows boxers from one part of the state (or country--
anyone's welcome to participate in the exchange) to plant their work
in another, with the security of knowing that someone else is
looking after them and they are not "orphans." Plus, it allows
finders greater opportunity to find boxes created by letterboxers
whose boxes they might not be able to find because of distance.

If you want to do a specific box that would be planted in a specific
spot or city, feel free to do that one too, but separately on your
own outside of the exchange. It's a great way to extend the
series.

Did I explain it okay?

Thanks for signing up!

Dewberry
