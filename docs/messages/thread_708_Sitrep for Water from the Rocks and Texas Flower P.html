Sitrep for Water from the Rocks and Texas Flower Power and a word of caution

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-03-16 17:10:41 UTC-08:00
Texasluvbug has been hard at work planting boxes
around Cherokee County and my kids and I ventured out
this evening to find her Water from the Rocks box.
It's planted at a neat little roadside spring where
people come to get a little drink or to collect water
to use at home. Parts of Cherokee County are still
very, very rural and (ahem) backward, so there are
even folks who gather water from this spring to use in
place of running water in their homes! The water is
smooth and clean and COLD, and it tastes oh so good!
It's flowed for a long, long time, winter or summer,
rainy or dry. I must confess, I have considered
placing a box at this site for quite some time, but
never got off the stick to do it, and now luvbug has
jumped in and beat me to it! You go girl!

ANYWAY, we found Water from the Rocks in good
condition, but a little crowded in the box. Planted
ourselves down with a good view of the spring, but no
one noticed us spying on them while we stamped in. It
has seen two other boxers today, but they left behind
a little rubber that needed to get out and stretch, so
we relieved the overcrowding situation in the box by
taking Texas Flower Power on a ride. I see a three
hour trip south in its very near future!

Let me take a moment to mention to new boxers and old
- to help this game thrive, it is really important to
rehide boxes well. Even a little corner peaking out
can attract unwanted attention and result in a box
being discovered by the uninitiated. I saw the box
from about six feet away as I approached. There was
just a very tiny corner showing, but I caught the
glint and knew what I was seeing. My crew and I
usually play a little game - one person hides it back
and the others of us try to spot it without using our
hands. I know, it's kind of an ignorant game, but it
does the trick! Nuff said - "Think boxing is swell?
Rehide well!"

Thanks for another box luvbug - very nice! Look for a
new one in our area tomorrow evening!

Lucy
