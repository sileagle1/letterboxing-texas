Internet Safety

From: Ashby (ashby@ic.org) | Date: 2006-08-31 17:43:27 UTC-07:00
Hi to all,

I'd like to ditto Barefoot Lucy's comments about
internet safety, and personal safety in general. I
agree, this does seem like a very nice cyberplace, and
the folks all seem very friendly, but then again,
that's only these folks, now . . . . . . and makes no
promises about possible future folks, nor about how
any folks might be irl.

I'd agree with her -- how you first meet people is
most appropriately how you should continue to interact
with them, and changes in that situation should come
from the other person, not from you pressuring them,
or from someone pursuing you (as she mentioned). If
you have "met" someone through the internet list, or
"contact the placer" or any other internet option,
that is based on trail names, then you should stick
only with those kinds of options. If, at some point,
someone OFFERS you a different approach (such as real
name or phone number), then you can take it, or leave
it, and offer yours in return, or NOT, as you please.
(and please, let's never forget the "public place"
commandment, for first-time meetings!)

It's like checking IDs at the daycare.....anyone who
is even reasonably aware of the risks to children in
today's world *should* be delighted to have their ID
checked, any time any employee has even the slightest
doubt that they have, or have not, met you before. If
you quibble or balk, then that only makes you more
suspicious, I think (and my employer thinks so, too).

Likewise, people who understand the potential risks of
meeting irl with folks you only know from online will
be understanding, and similarly reasonably cautious,
most likely. And folks who don't understand these
potential risks, well....they need to catch up with
the new world, I think!

When folks say or imply "there's never been a
problem", what I hear is "there's never been a problem
YET" - just like when folks say their dog "has never
bitten anyone", what they really mean is "has never
bitten anyone YET".

But most assuredly, I do want to say it again, this
particular List has been very great and fun and
friendly and I would in fact like to meet many/all of
you in person, someday. But if I were to suddenly get
a phone call from someone I had not directly shared my
real name or number with, that would feel pretty odd,
it really would.

(Sheesh! I don't post often, but when I do, it just
all falls out, doesn't it!?)

: )

Have a great day!

Ashby

I don't think anyone is as paranoid or careful as I am.  I can't help it, I see things a different way than most people.  Too many years a soldier I guess.
 
Still, ultimately - you are responsible for your own safety.  If you are relying on the police, I truly wish you the best of luck.  Mostly they are historians.  You lived or died by your own equipment and abilities - then they show up, open a notebook, and ask "What happened here?"
 
Ladies especially, I will not teach my class here, but look at the NRA's refuse to be a victim program, and try to integrate the tips into your life.  Even your car keys can safe your life.  Small women can get under cars where bigger men cannot fit.  If you are going to evade and escape, it is in the first seconds of the attack, not later.  If they get you into their car, it is too late.  Watch for large vans next to your car in parking lots.  You go to unlock your door, the sliding door of the van opens and you are pulled in.  Shopping carts can be a weapon too. 
 
NO TEACHING......  sorry - <grin>  Bad horse - bad bad horse!!!
 
Hope I am not scaring anyone.  You need to consider how you feel about your life, your family's lives, and the rights of the criminals attacking you.  The only bad thought is not giving it any thought at all.  My wife and I made a deal some years ago.  We will both make it home, no matter what it takes.  Mind-set is as important as tactics and weapons.
 
Personally - I like silver titanium.  The wife likes purple titanium.  I just can't get myself to carry purple!  <grin>
