Because it is so new this park is a lot different than others.  As of yet there are few amenities.  Just a few picnic tables, a couple of benches and a porta-potty.  There is an admission canister—the sign asks for $1 per person.  I don’t think there will be any personnel on duty except to open the gate in the morning (9AM) and close it at dusk. It is still very wild and rough around the edges.  The trails aren’t well established, just mowed at this time. Without a sufficient amount of foot traffic and continual mowing it could become challenging to find your way around.  We encountered lots of thorny bushes and cactus but didn’t meet up with any snakes. Surely they will be out and about when the weather starts to warm up.  There was an old home site, so there are some human traces in the park which to me makes it a little more interesting than if it was totally devoid of human presence.  All in all, it is a nice little facility and I’m glad it is so close to home for me.
