From: gaambeck (abbiembeck@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2008-05-02 20:58:08 UTC
I know I asked last week about planting in a state park and my plan
was to do that right away. A week later I made it over there and,
b/c of the great advice I got here, went to the visitor center and
asked if there was someone I could talk to about planting
letterboxes. Of course, I got the quizzical expression and then a
park ranger from the "back room" came walking out asking if that was
anything like geocaching!!! I had brought with me one box all ready
to show, some clues and a lot of courage (I am usually pretty
timid). I ended up walking away super excited to talk to the new
park manager tomorrow and hike the park. The park ranger I talked
to was getting everyone else there excited about the idea of such
great, "clean fun" (as she put it) coming to her park. The main
warning (if you could call it that) was that they didn't want anyone
being led off the trails as this is a natural area over the Edward's
Aquifer recharge zone and there were endangered species in the
area...I told her with 5 children, I would definitely NOT be going
too far off the trail.

Sadly, I won't be able to plant the boxes before our San Antonio
event, but at least I'll be able to let everyone know to be watching
out for them!!!
