From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-01-09 03:07:02 UTC
I have stamps (and letterboxes) that have been waiting to be planted
for months. We managed to get one of those planted today--Hoppin'
High--at Hester Garden Park in Seabrook, TX today. This park was
gorgeous. Plus, we were able to do some letterboxing at the San
Jacinto Battleground and in Seabrook, so all around it was a marvelous
day.

This completely ready box had ridden around either in the car or the
backpack for about 7 months, so I'm sure it's glad to be out in the
woods now.

Sad, huh? But I can beat my own record--I have one that has been
ready to plant since last February--maybe it too will get out there
sometime soon.

DB

From: (kacmbc@aol.com) | Date: 2006-01-09 10:51:31 UTC-05:00
If you come to Houston this weekend, be prepared for road closures around the downtown, Galleria, Memorial Park area on Sunday!  It is the Houston Marathon ( www.houstonmarathon.com) and lots of runners, including myself, will be out running the streets. WooHoo!! You can go to the marathon website for a course map and see where the streets will be closed.  Additionally, spectators are always welcome along the course route.  If you have never been to a marathon, it can be very entertaining!  Feel free to come out and cheer the runners on.  You never know when words of encouragement may be exactly what a runner needs.  Many runners write their name on their arms, legs, shirt etc...so that you can cheer specifically for them!
 
I honestly thought about running with my logbook, stamp and stamp pad in case I would be running by any letterboxes, but decided that probably wasn't a smart tactic! 
 
Runs for Chocolate :)
