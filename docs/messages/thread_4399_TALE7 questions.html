When do you post the clues for the boxes at the event? I saw that everyone put the box info out for the new boxes but not the clues. So I did the same. I already have the clues to the box ready. Re: [letterboxingtexas] TALE7 questions

From: Tasha Morris (keishkakeishka@yahoo.com) | Date: 2010-02-28 14:00:31 UTC-08:00
Hi a-maze-ing!

Great questions about events!

Generally, clues for boxes that are placed specifically for an event will be available on-site.  If you have a box in the area that you'd like to launch so people can find it when they are in the area, you can certainly post clues the day before - just make sure to have at least one copy of the clues with you so folks can copy them.  I think there are a lot of people who will have already printed out their clues prior to that, and I believe someone said they were bringing a copy machine so that copies could be made on-site.

The exchange checklist is just a list of folks you haven't exchanged sig stamps with.  Bring it with you and when you exchange sig stamps, check them off the list.  Chances are you won't get everyone - there's usually at least a few folks who sign up to come to events but can't make it for one reason or another.  It's just an easy way to keep track of who you still need to exchange with.  It'll be more useful after a few events so that you aren't going up to the same person at every event asking for exchanges...

In general, I have found it to be perfectly acceptable to walk up to someone, tell them how excited I am to meet them and that I solved some of their PTs, fumble with my list of PT answers and then give them my answers.  If nothing else, it's a great conversation starter.  If you were to make your own PT, you would make a stamp and logbook for it - if there's a question people need to solve, you'll want to get it posted sooner than later, so that folks have a couple days to figure it out before printing out their final lists.

Hope that helps!
Rogue Artist

Oh yeah, Mosaic Butterfly, you reminded me that I had wanted to respond to that question specifically.

A-Maze-Ing,

Some folks have logbooks for their PT's, but it gets pretty cumbersome doing sig stamp exchanges and then PT exchanges. You can have a logbook for your PT if you want to - nothing says you can't - but from a matter of practicality, I wouldn't bother with it. You'll probably be getting someone's sig stamp in your regular logbook at the same time they are getting your PT, so it just means you'll have their sig in two places. Two stamps CAN be hard enough to manage - two books, not worth it.

In my opinion, of course.

Lucy
