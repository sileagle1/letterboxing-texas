Letterboxing Excursion in San Patricio, Nueces & Aransas Counties

From: Wanda (wsthm@hotmail.com) | Date: 2004-06-08 22:20:48 UTC
Yesterday, Corazon, Calico Rose and I got an early start on a day of
letterboxing.
We went to Lake Corpus Christi first to search for Baby Bear's
MUSIC MAN. Holding back a branch of a thorn bush, I got stung on the
finger by some kind of wasp. It was extremely painful, but
fortunately I had Benedryl Spray in the van which helped.
Unfortunately the box had been vandalized and everything was missing
except the box, in one spot, and the lid, in another. We replaced
the book and signed in, hiding the box back in the place it should
have been.

From there we went to the City of Corpus Christi Wildlife and Bird
Sanctuary where we found Baby Bear's LIGHT CATCHER, in great shape.
We were surprised by two men and three boys coming up the trail from
fishing just as the box was re-hidden, but they didn't see
anything 'letterboxy'. We talked awhile and admired their fish
before they headed on toward the parking area. As we came out a few
minutes later they were still loading things up and asked us if we
were bird watchers or what we were doing out there. I told them I
was studying the plants, wildflowers and trees, with an eye to their
proliferation and a change in the varieties and habitat, etc. and
brought my two friends with me that day....which was true,
but... ;)

Then we drove into Corpus Christi and had lunch before going out to
Mustang Island State Park where we found Baby Bear's CORAL SNAKE
alive and well. I had two dolls along with me for a 'vacation doll'
project, so I took their photo as they found the box too. ;)

We were unable to find Silver Eagle's TEXAS TURTLE letterbox
despite having a gardening fork to really move lots of rock, to see
underneath. The rock in that spot had slid down to the lower ground
in a pretty big area, but then we scatched a lot of area looking
under the rocks. We had in mind that it might be a microbox/film
canister, so we looked pretty thoroughly. A family stopped near the
Padre Island sign to take a photo, while Calico Rose and Corazon
tried to look nonchalant with my garden fork in hand standing around
on the rocky area. LOL! I was at the sign, again trying to pose my
two 'vacation dolls' for a photo on that sign and get them to stay
up. I took them down and let the family pose for a photo first,
since we were going to be awhile. I took their photo for them and
they left. Then I went back to my task and Corazon and Calico Rose
went back to theirs.

From there we went to Aransas Pass where we found Baby Bear's SUPER
FROGGER. It was very wet inside, but the stamp was fine. We dried
out the container and replaced the booklet with a new one, stamped
in, then replaced it in it's spot. I have emailed Baby Bear for his
address so I can send him the old booklet.

It started raining on us there, and we continued on in a heavy rain
storm, thunder and lightening to the Aransas Wildlife Refuge near
Tivoli. There, we saw hundreds of tiny frogs jumping across the road
all the way in and out of the Refuge. Plus, we saw two HUGE frogs of
a variety that none of us had ever seen before. They were maybe 5
inches 'tall' sitting down, were rather more 'upright' than
frogs/toads I had seen before and bright lime green especially
around the head and face. The rest of them were kind of variatated
green and gray. Anyone know what kind they are?

In the Refuge, Corazon and Calico Rose found my HURRICANE CLAUDETTE
letterbox, and we intended to look for the rest there, but the storm
intensified so we had to give up and leave for home.

Although all we saw in the Refuge were jillions of frogs, on the
road home, we saw wild turkeys, one a tom with his tail spread, a
herd of wild pigs, several groups of nice deer, and a coyote.

We had a great day and plan to go letterboxing in Wilson, Comal, and
Hays Counties the first of next week.

Busy Bee / Wanda
