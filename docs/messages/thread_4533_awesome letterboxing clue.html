Speaking of being surprised by the lack of boxes in quirky places, that's a problem that I have a plan to ameliorate, even if just a little. Once a year (sometimes twice) we take a road trip from TX to WA and back, and I like to consult places like roadsideamerica.com and the letterboxing sites to find good places to stretch our legs during the trip and to reminisce about after the trip. I too am shocked by the lack of letterboxes in places like the giant legs statue of Amarillo (along with several other Amarillo sites - that's one nutty town!), the giant easel with a Van Gogh painting on it in KS, and any number of other wacky spots. Maybe they're too close to the road to attract letterboxers?

Anyway, I'm starting on some boxes to place during our next road trip. Hopefully they will brighten someone else's day, or drive. If anyone knows of a wonderful spot between here and WA, let me know and I'll put it on my list of spots that are crying out for letterboxes!

Without giving too much away, I would like to mention that quirky places are often sensitive places for boxes - high traffic, well-manicured, high visibility - so oft-times clues for boxes in those sorts of locations are mystery/puzzle boxes.

Are you aware of the website, www.roadsideamerica.com? There's also a fun book called Weird Texas, which I happen to own. Lots of interesting and unusual places for boxes...
