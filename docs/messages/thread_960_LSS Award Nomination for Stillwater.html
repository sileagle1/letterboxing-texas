LSS Award Nomination for Stillwater

From: celticlions (celticlions@comcast.net) | Date: 2005-06-27 09:08:36 UTC-05:00
Okay, those of you who have a Long-Suffering Spouse, raise your hands!

My husband (Stillwater) is the best of companions for letterboxing - left to
his own devices, I don't think he would continue in the sport, but he's
always ready to jump in the car and go hunt boxes. He often is the one who
actually finds them, because he seems to have far better instincts than I do
for directions, and often has a fresh approach to the clues.
Yesterday, though, he earned a special Long Suffering Spouse award...
We went to downtown Dallas to replace the Shawnee Trail box - in a little
park called Pioneer Plaza. It's a spectacular place for photographs, so it
is often busy with tourists - and it's a kid magnet because of the stepping
stones across water and huge bronze statues of longhorns.
I hit on the brilliant idea (IMHO)of bringing Stumpy the Sock Monkey to take
his photo there...if you haven't heard of Stumpy, he is a traveling Sock
Monkey belonging to TX letterboxer MonkeyToes, and he has skirted the U.S.,
having his photo taken at various historical and otherwise interesting
places.
So...please picture my L.S.S., trying to prop Stumpy up in various
attractive poses on top of the gigantic bronze longhorns in this very public
park....as groups of tourists from other countries watched in stunned
silence. (Don't tell my husband, but I actually saw someone taking a photo
of HIM, posing the sock monkey...can you imagine the commentary when that
tourist returns to his hometown with documentation of weird & scary
Americans?)

(Oh yes, and we relocated the Shawnee Trail box...but I was still laughing
so hard that Stillwater actually had to do that, too...)

Ruby Tuesday




Re: [letterboxingtexas] LSS Award Nomination for Stillwater

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-06-27 08:41:42 UTC-07:00
My husband needs to belong to this club too! I've
never asked him to pose anything, but I'll bet he
would! What a great guy Stillwater must be...

My LSS loves to hike, but for whatever reason hasn't
quite latched on to LB'ing. He'll go and he'll not
complain, especially if we all agree to make it a long
hike and not a short hop.

He too is actually probably a better letterboxer than
I am in terms of skills it takes, but he blithely adds
his suggestions only after the rest of us have tried
for a bit- almost like he is secretly taking pleasure
in our fervent efforts to find a box when to him it is
soooo clear where it is. I'm envious of his ability
to home in on a box without ever actually digging for
it.

I don't really know what motivates him to keep going
with us, but I'm really glad he does. I've noticed,
though, that he's happy enough to let me carry the
backpack.

I've asked him before to check on boxes for me when I
know he's hiking near where one of mine is, and he
does it-without complaint. I asked him to give me
carving supplies for my birthday the first year I
boxed, and he went out and found every possible tool
and material that was available in our area - and
spent a lot more on it than I would've ever done for
myself.

Just when I thought I wasn't going to get to go to the
Brazos Bend gathering because of a collision of
circumstances, he pulled it out for me, took on extra
duty, and MADE me go and told me not to feel guilty
for leaving him with all I left him with.

But still, I think Stillwater should have the LSS of
the Year award for accepting without complaint the
assignment of being made to look silly in the interest
of letterboxing.

Lucy

While my LSS is not into the LBing at all, he listens quietly as
the kids and I ramble on about our latest finds and tell all our

over and over, but manages to smile and look interested. He even
waits in the car sometimes while we dash to a box nearby.

about F. S. (as LSS travels for a living and was asked to take
Flat Stanley on one of his travels). By the end of the trip, Flat
Stanley had flown a plane, served drinks and peanuts to passengers
(because the Flight Attendants thought it was too cute), had gone
to Canada, Mexico, through customs, wore a sombrero, hung out at a
hotel restaurant/bar and wore an oxygen mask on the plane. All
with photographs to prove that LSS is a softie at heart! Maybe
one day he'll get the hang of LBing. If for nothing else than to
get me to button my lip about all the neat places we find during
our hunts!!
