From: Maribeth Jagger (cjagger405@earthlink.net) | Date: 2007-09-08 20:58:07 UTC-05:00
I certainly don't care if I'm first to plant a box in Montague County or not, but I do have a few ideas in the works for the town of Nocona in Montague Cnty. However, with the onset of hotter weather, just haven't been inspired to travel up there. We used to travel to Nocona from Wichita Falls when I was a kid to buy Nocona Boots, but the factory has since closed. However, the Montague Boot Company is operational now in downtown Nocona making boots for Larry Mahan's line for Cavendar Boots. There was a company making leather sporting equipment in the old Nocona Boots factory including some darn good baseball gloves, but the factory burned last year. They are still operating out of a temporary building though. The town was named after the Comanche Indian, Chief Peta Nocona, husband to Cynthia Anne Parker and father to Quannah Parker. Needless to say, there are many other interesting facts about Montague County that are screaming out for letterboxes. I guess I'll do mine when I get to it, and anyone else who wants to plant some up there should too. I've never heard anyone complain of too many boxes!
