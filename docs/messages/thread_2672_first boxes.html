From: (kacmbc@aol.com) | Date: 2007-03-25 22:30:18 UTC-04:00
Congrats on finding your first box!  I still remember the thrill of finding my first letterbox, Mercer Monarch.  One of my friends and I went out to Mercer Arboretum one afternoon with clues in hand.  Of course, we went the wrong way and the wrong way again, but eventually found the box.  We have pictures of us pointing to where the box is located, opening the box, looking at the logbook etc...then we realized that we didn't have a stamp, a logbook of our own, NOTHING with us!  So...we had to come back at a later date with "supplies" to letterbox!  I designed/drew the images for our stamps for the both of us and sent the images to a friend in Colorado who owns a laser stamp company, and after they arrived, was set to go. The friend lost interest, so I went back to Mercer by myself to officially stamp in! 
 
I quickly became addicted and the competitive person that I am, decided that I needed to find as many boxes as fast as I could!  Some of the next few were Java Joy, Dodge the Dillo and Temporary Inversion, followed by LB Retriever, which, by the way, had a really cool dog at the LB location that I wish could have taken  home with me!!
 
Hope you and your family continue to have fun on your letterboxing adventures!!
 
Runs for Chocolate  :)

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-03-25 21:56:48 UTC-05:00
We were talking about that very thing at the gathering - how so many of us credit Silver Eagle or Baby Bear for our first boxes, and if they weren't our very first planters, they were certainly among the first five.  I think everyone there agreed!
 
I think it would be interesting for each of us to take a moment and note what our first box or two were, who planted it, and what date it was:
 
Mine:
 
Tejas-Baby Bear-September 1, 2003
Spanish Flag-Silver Eagle-September 1, 2003
 
And actually, the box I referred to in a post earlier today as the box that was found wet this weekend - WAS Tejas, our first box to find.  I got a lot of pleasure from showing my young boxer friend our log entries in that box - three of us had store-boughts and one of us drew an image in - and telling her that it was our first. 
 
Both of those boxes were planted on 1/30/03 (Spanish Flag was actually planted a month earlier, but was replaced on 1/30), and aside from Baby Bear and Silver Eagle each finding the other's box, we were the first finders - those two boxes waited 7 months for us to come along!

From: Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter (janet@redbarnretreat.com) | Date: 2007-03-26 13:37:23 UTC
Our first find was also a Silver Eagle box: Big Creek Bug, which was
the only letterbox in San Jacinto County at that time, just 5 miles
from our house. We didn't actually find it, we were just along for
the ride: LSQ's brother, El Lobo and his wife Star Seeker from West
Texas came to visit us for Thanksgiving, 2005 and told us about this
interesting new hobby they had read about called Letterboxing. So,
after eating our Thanksgiving meal, we went as a group to find the
Big Creek Bug. EL and SS had their stamps all carved and ready to
go, so they stamped into their first found box. We had to go home
and make some stamps before we could stamp in, which we did. Then,
we went to The Woodlands and found Runs for Chocolate's Runner
Series boxes and that was great fun, too. We loved her trail name.
By the way, Silver Eagle, whatever happened to Big Creek Bug? I see
it's not listed on LbNA and neither is Baby Bear's box, Beer Belly
Bob, out at Big Creek.

Boots Tex

Re: first boxes

From: Silver Eagle (sileagle@alltel.net) | Date: 2007-03-26 13:40:05 UTC
As many of you know, my first box (and Baby Bear's) was:

Finch Arboretum - by Ian - 08/31/2002 - Spokane, WA

As luck would have it, there was a Hitchhiker in the box called
Hitch, so I actually got 2 finds my first time out. We had no
supplies with us so could not stamp in, but luckily the box had
postcards with the stamp image on them so I was able to take one and
glue it into my logbook that I later purchased. Also by luck, the HH
had good instructions on what to do since we were clueless, and I
brought it back to TX with me. Unfortunately, there were no boxes
near me to place it in, so this forced me to plant my first box (Zube
Zephyr) so Baby Bear could then take the HH. Of course this forced
him to place a box, which got both of us started placing for each
other. I am convinced that if we had not found that first box, we
might never have continued letterboxing. And if it had not contained
a HH, I don't think we would have started placing boxes as soon as we
did.

Oh, and my first box in TX was:

Star Gazer - by Baby Bear - 11/30/2002 - Brazos Bend SP
It also contained a HH (Texas Traveler).

Silver Eagle

From: Baby Bear (babybearlb2002@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-03-27 13:20:15 UTC
As Silver Eagle stated, my first find was with him at Finch Arboretum
in Spokane, Washington on 8/31/2002. My first find in Texas was his
box, Zube Zephyr on 9/21/2002. The next 4 finds were in Colorado
(as Texas had few boxes at the time), and my first placed box would
actually be put in Louisiana, "Cypress Bend" on 11/08/2002. As many
of you have probably done, I carved this stamp with the name spelled
out. Only problem was the image was not reversed, so when stamped it
was a reverse. So I guess it became my first and second carved
stamps! I don't believe it was ever found before it went missing.
My 1st Texas box was "Texas Birth" at Washington-on-the-Brazos. The
original went missing fairly quickly, but was replaced with a new
stamp in it's current location. My oldest original stamp is at
Stephen F. Austin S.R.A., "Father of Texas".
