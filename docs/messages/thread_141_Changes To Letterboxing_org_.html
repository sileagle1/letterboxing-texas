Changes To Letterboxing.org?

From: Silver Eagle (sileagle@alltel.net) | Date: 2004-05-19 22:11:58 UTC
On the main talklist the webmasters are soliciting ideas
to revamp the letterboxing.org site and I was thinking
now might be a good time to ask for things we want changed.
One thing I would recommend is changing the regions of
TX so they make more sense. Currently the Coastal Region
covers an area from Brownsville to Orange, which makes it
hard for somebody coming to Houston to narrow their search
for local boxes. Also, the North region extends too far
south. I would like to see the following 6 Regions:
Panhandle, Northeast, Southeast, South, Central & West.
Let me know what you think and if we get a consensus I
will map out the counties I think should go into each of
my regions and send it to the webmasters.
Also, I encourage each of you to give any input you have
for improvements to the webmasters. I have already told them
I would like to see several things fixed before adding new
things (e.g. Louisiana & International boxes not showing up).

Silver Eagle

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-05-19 15:33:31 UTC-07:00
You know, you really have a good point about the
regions. I was pinpointing boxes on a state map this
past weekend and was really confused by the regions.
It never occurred to me to pipe in about that problem.


I do think that it should be us on this list
discussing and agreeing on regions and presenting in
one voice to the webmasters. There is no way they can
understand about the peculiar challenges we have in
defining regions in Texas. We have a much better idea
what the logical divisions would be.

I agree with you about East Texas. It shouldn't be
long - it should be squarish and should extend almost
to Dallas and South to Angelina County (Lufkin). I'm
squarely in the middle of what is typically thought of
as East Texas, and yet I have to flip between two
regions to get local boxes. Houston's area should
extend up to Angelina County and West to not far
beyond Houston. Austin's area should include San
Antonio - that's probably the most awkwardly divided
region, at least in my opinion. Possibly it should
include Bryan and Waco? It really kind of looks like
we should have one additional region.

I think that non-Texas folks struggle with the idea
that one of our regions is as big as most of their
whole states!

As to other improvements, I would just "somehow" like
to be able to highlight certain boxes and flip through
them without having to go back to the region page and
click.

My first thoughts...I'm sure y'all will have lots more
that will make me think even more!

Thanks for getting the ball rolling, Silver Eagle!
Lucy
