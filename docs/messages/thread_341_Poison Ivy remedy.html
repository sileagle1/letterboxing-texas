Re: Poison Ivy remedy

From: Cristy (tryscience@hotmail.com) | Date: 2004-09-22 02:30:51 UTC
The poison ivy leaves an oily toxin that will start absorbing into
the skin in a few minutes. Anything that dissolves oil will stop
the process, if treated quickly enough. All soaps and detergents
dissolve oil, as well as alcohol. I was told to carry a bottle of
hand sanitizer while hiking to rub on suspected areas of contact
because it has a high alcohol content. Then wash well w/ soap and
water asap.

A small percentage of people are not allergic to it, but they can
develop sensitivity over time so be careful!! My husband is not
allergic, so he is always our "trailblazer" and spotter when out
letterboxing in ivy areas ;)

happy trails,
Cristy
