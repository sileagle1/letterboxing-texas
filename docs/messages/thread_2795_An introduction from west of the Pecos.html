From: Dave (DavHow@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-05-31 02:24:17 UTC
Hi y'all - I just signed up, and it seems and introduction is in order.
My name is Warm Front and I live in Alpine, TX where I attend Sul Ross
State University as a mathematics major. I'm what is known as a "non-
traditional" student, which is a polite way of saying that I'm a 35
year old college freshman ;) I moved here in late December of last year
(from Houston), and discovered letterboxing a month later while reading
about an upcoming novel from an action writer that I enjoy (Novel:
Scavenger, Author: David Morrell <--- most famous for creating the
character Rambo).
I was hooked after 10 seconds, and it only took that long to get my g/f
(Cold Front) hooked as well, but there aren't many boxes out here, so
we waited a couple weeks and planned. Our first box, was in Valentine,
TX on Valentines Day, and we've been going strong ever since.

I guess that's plenty for an introduction.

Warm Front

Thanks for the welcome y'all.

hmmm, why a math major? Well, I was really interested in being a
Social Studies Teacher, but it's actually an education degree, so if
teaching doesn't work out, then the degree is basically useless. I
want to stay in Texas (preferably the Trans-Pecos region) and there
just isn't a big emphasis on geography, or sociology, etc in most
Texas schools, so I'd be stuck applying for Civics/Government teacher
positions, that are traditionally given to people with Poli-Sci
degrees in good schools, and football coaches in small schools.
History, has a similar story, the degree is useless without a PhD,
and 90% of the history teachers in TX have the same first
name...Coach.

I have no interest in being a coach, and I need my BS to be able to
earn me a living before it turns into an MS... The dire need right
now, is for Bi-lingual teachers in elementary schools, and
math/science teachers in high schools. Sul Ross doesn't have a
Physics degree plan, which is my preferred science subject, so I
chose Mathematics, because of demand and if teaching doesn't work
out, I can always get into demographics (Math meets Geography) which
is fine with me.

From: Dave (DavHow@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-05-31 07:21:30 UTC
hey Blooming Flowers - Thanks for the invite... I sort of thought I
might be better off joining a New Mexican Letterboxing group seeing
as I'm so far from most of you, but here I am.

I wish there were a more interesting story to our names. We didn't
want anything that implied current interests, because we figured from
the beggining that we'd do this for a long time, so we were aiming
for trailnames that 1)would age well, 2)were somewhat outdoorsy and 3)
would identify us as a couple without being too cute or entirely
dependent on the other (in the unlikely event that we split up, i
wouldn't want to change my trailname). Having set the criteria, we
made lists of names, and ultimately she is ALWAYS cold (sweaters even
during summer in the desert) and I am usually hot (anything above 68
degrees, and I think it's time for air conditioning:), so Warm Front
and Cold Front were born.
