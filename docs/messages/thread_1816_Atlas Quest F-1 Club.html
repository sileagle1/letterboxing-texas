Since I usually only look at LBNA for clues, I was stumped
when a recent clue that pointed to AQ said "Must belong to
F-1 Club to see clue". I asked the placer what that meant
and it means you must log at least 1 find in AQ before you
can see the clue. After doing that I also had to log off
and back on to AQ before it would let me see it. Just wanted
to pass this on in case some of you have the same problem.

Silver Eagle




Re: Atlas Quest F-1 Club

From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-04-18 22:01:29 UTC
This is actually a point I'd like to discuss and get feedback from the
Texas letterboxers about.

A few months ago, when you listed a clue on AQ, a box started popping
up that would ask you if you wanted to hide this clue from search
engines (like Google). I checked yes, because I don't want my
letterbox clues accidentally popping up when a non-letterboxer is
searching for info on the Arboretum, for example. Last week, all of
those clues with the box checked were converted to F1 clues, because
that is a more secure way of ensuring that the clues are protected
from the search engines.

So, what do you think? These are meant to be clues accessible to
everyone who's interested (aka letterboxers), and I don't really want
barriers to accessing the clues. But, on the other hand, logging one
find on Atlas Quest in order to see the clues doesn't seem that onerous.

As you're the finders, I throw it out to you. Are you uncomfortable
with the F1 requirement? Your thoughts are appreciated.

Dewberry

RE: [letterboxingtexas] Re: Atlas Quest F-1 Club

From: Michael P. Olbrisch (mike2004@elp.rr.com) | Date: 2006-04-19 02:09:42 UTC
I  -ALWAYS-  (at least for my meager 20 finds) notify the placer.  In one event, I followed the clues over 200 miles and just HAD to notify the placer.
 
http://www.letterboxing.org/BoxView.php?boxnum=7108&boxname=Javelina_Letterbox
 
We left the dog show in Tucson at around noon.  We followed the clues.  I-10 east to 80.  Well, 80 south was under heavy construction.  We were looking for a sign that means DOOR.  Well, got all the way to Tombstone and beyond.  NO SIGN!  We missed it somewhere.  Must have removed it for the construction.  DISAPPOINTMENT!  But we go to the shows in Tucson a lot, so we would return.  80 swings south thru Bisbee and Douglas, then back north to Rodeo NM and on to I-10 near Lordsburg.  And there was still the BISBEE BOX.
 
Bisbee AZ is a lovely little town.  When the copper mine closed, the city almost closed with it.  But they turned it into a museum instead, and they now live off of tourism.  When you come to Bisbee from the south, you must go through a tunnel at Mule Mountain.  When you exit the tunnel, you are back in the 50s.  We did the letterbox in Bisbee.  The dogs drew a huge crowd, so Moni kept everyone's attention on the dogs while I slipped in and got the letterbox.  We stamped up, nobody ever asked what we were doing.  Then I replaced it - again unseen as the dogs got all the attention.  You see, when people see a Great Dane, everybody runs.  Some run to you, and some run away!  <grin>.  And we had 3 dogs in the van that day.
 
http://www.letterboxing.org/BoxView.php?boxnum=18228&boxname=Bisbee_Box
 
Douglas AZ is always a mess.  The turn to follow 80 is just as you hit town.  Miss it and you are doomed!  But what the heck - we are having a ball.  The dogs always enjoy a good adventure.
 
Followed 80 S-SE headed for home.  This is 20 Nov, the sun is getting low on the horizon.  Moni is driving (her van) and I am running the GPS, ham radio, serving food and water, etc.........  what was that sigh?  What sign???  GO BACK - GO BACK - TURN AROUND!!!  The sign - PORTAL.  The village named DOOR!  How much daylight?  About an hour.  Can we make it?  One thing for sure - we will not if we don't try.
 
Moni drove that big E-350 like a little sports car up that twisty mountain road.  The excitement mounted.  I cannot give up any of the clues, but they started to fall in place.  We dove out of the van with the orange glow of the sunset lighting the upper canyon walls.  I grabbed my gear and RAN up the path.  Soon 3 dogs had caught me.  Moni was far behind.  But all the clues were right on, and so was I.  I FOUND IT.  Log book, stamp pad...  BUT NO STAMP!!!!  I searched and searched.  No luck.  I stamped us in while Moni searched some more and took photos.  No stamp!  DARN!!!!!  We EARNED that stamp.  I notified the placer.  They were very nice about it, apologetic and all.  But they never have replaced it.
 
Hmmmm....  in researching the URLs for this note, I see that they HAVE replaced it!  We have dog shows all this weekend.  We have a date with the rangers to recover the Hueco Tanks Letterboxes Saturday the 13th of May.  I think that we will go for it!  It is a nice area, and we will take a picnic lunch along.  Anyone want to join us?
 
Hope you enjoyed the story.  I ALWAYS notify the placer.
 
Mike.  El Paso, TX.
