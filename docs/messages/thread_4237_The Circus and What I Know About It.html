The Circus and What I Know About It

From: AnnaBee (annabee44@gmail.com) | Date: 2009-09-20 17:41:50 UTC-04:00

 

The Circus finally pulled up the tent stakes to head out of Austin. They all agreed that they couldn't leave without saying goodbye to Austin's Diva of All Things Circus.  They rang the Bell, but she wasn't home.  They sat down to wait and finally decided to stay the night at her Motel waiting for her to return. “Whiz Gee it is taking her a long time to get home!” they complained.

 

When she still had not returned by the next morning they started to get worried. Everyone had heard all about a murder at the Arboretum and the Bearded Lady felt certain they had passed that very place on their way to see their good friend. The Clowns were very concerned and suggested they do some Research on that murder, but the group began to squabble over whether the Bearded Lady had the story straight. The details of that event were so complicated - no one seemed to understand it at all!   So that idea went South in a hurry. 

 

They decided to stop for lunch somewhere and figure out what to do next. "Let's stop there!" said Lion Tamer. "No let's go there!  I want dessert!" said the Bear. So many choices in the area! Finally the Ringmaster said "I'm in charge here and I say the Bear is Right. Let's eat Cake!". 

 

After lunch they continued on their way, looking for someone to help find their good friend or at least give them a safe place to stay that night while they considered what to do next. 

 

A quarter of an hour went by and everyone was getting tired and cranky. "Let's just stop here" said the Lion. I think my Uncle-in-law Napoleon lives around here someplace.". You mean what's Left of your uncle" corrected the Tiger. “Remember his unfortunate fall off of that high peak nearby?" Lion saddened at the memory and asked the group if they would mind visiting the scene of the accident to pay their respects. 

 

Since the group was not all that familiar with the city they weren't certain of the Right place to turn to get to the Mount(ain) where Lion's uncle met his fate. 

 

They thought they were headed in the right direction and travelled quite a way. Lion began to sob thinking of his poor, poor Uncle, and everyone turned their attention to consoling him. And don't you know they passed the place they were looking for!  

 

"Hey!  The road ends just ahead!  We must have passed it" said Karl (who was named for The Karl). "I think I remember a shortcut to get back there!"

 

"Right!" cried the Dog. "good idea!"

 

They turned the Circus Wagon and headed to where they thought Lion's uncle went to the Big Top in the Sky. 

 

"It sure is pretty around here" mused the Tiny Ballerina. "There is a pretty little pond I can see from here. Why don't we stop there?"

 

"THAT is no pond Madame!  THAT is a Lagoon!" said Horse, feeling quite smart for knowing that. 

 

"Well Gloria Be!  It is!” said the Ringmaster. "I say we stop there!"

 

But as they approached their destination a gate blocked their way. "It must be cocktail hour" guessed the Bareback Rider. 

 

"Ok" said the Ringmaster. "We have to stop somewhere and figure out what to do. I saw a place close by and if I'm not mistaken, I saw a beautiful creature there. Maybe we can talk her into joining the Circus "

 

"Right" said the Sword Swallower as they went on their way. "Let's just stop there."


They went to the place of the beautiful creature, parked the Wagon and got out.

 

The Contortionist was looking more than a little uncomfortable, but no one paid much attention because he always looked that way. "I have to pee!" he said and ran off to the potty. 

 

Just then a loud noise was heard directly behind the bathroom. Everyone rushed over to see if their friend was ok. The Knife Thrower and The Strong Man led the pack around back. 

 

They headed down the path and no sooner had they entered the wood when they heard the noise again.  

 

It stopped them dead in their tracks.

 

But then they saw who was making all the racket - It was the beautiful creature and her even more beautiful mate!  This made everyone laugh.  The contortionist stuck his head out of the potty, and around the corner and over the top to see what everyone was laughing about.

 

They introduced themselves and asked if they would be interested in travelling with them. "Ya know" they said, "We've been looking to get out of here ever since that storm came through and broke all of the beautiful trees.  Just look Right over there at all that mess!"

 

They all went over to get a closer look.

 

And that's the last I saw of them. 

  


AnnaBee

So Rainbow Seeker and I decided to letterbox today for the first time in exactly 6 months. She's visiting while Bird Dog is away on business. So "Let's Make a Sandwhich," I said. Off we went to the woods to make the sandwhich, complete with ham, cheese, mustard, lettuce, tomato and bread. After the sandwich we were still a bit hungry, so decided to visit "Verde's" for some great TexMex. Well, at least we could smell the aroma eminating from the restaurant while we parked at the far end of the lot and found the box. At last the weather is cooled enought for a nice day in the not so hot sun.

No sooner did we get back to my place when we got word that the Circus Wagon was on the road again. Figured out where they were camped for the night, but alas and alak I had a meeting at church and didn't have time to rescue those tricky folks from the wild creatures, no matter how beautiful they appear. But RS was hot on the trail. Kareening around potholds and ducking past bridal photographers, she got there just in time to keep those circus folks from joining the bridal party photograph. meanwhle, half way thur the church meeting my cell rings. (What? Turn off the ringer during a church meeting when news of this magnitude is eminent? Never!) "I got 'em! I got 'em!" she yells into my cell. I did restrain myself from yelling "whopee!" in the middle of treasurer's report.
So the Ringmaster finally has everyone under control. LB Bozo is complaining about the accomodations again, but I told him our garage suite would only be availalbel to him and the gang for a few weeks.
