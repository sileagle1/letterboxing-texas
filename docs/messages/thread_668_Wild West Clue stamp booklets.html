Wild West Clue stamp booklets

From: busybeesfamily (bbbndrider@msn.com) | Date: 2005-03-10 21:44:42 UTC

Hi everyone!

I've been chatting with Ruby T. to see what I can do to help her get
things ready for the Clue game. Together, we've come up with a stamp
booklet that you may want to use to stamp your clues into. Of
course, you may just want to stamp into your regular logbook
instead. :-) The booklet (as I've assembled it) requires 1 sheet of
cardstock and 5 pages of regular paper. Also, printer, scissors and
stapler.

The booklet has a page for the victim stamp, 12 suspect stamp pages,
12 crime scene pages, 12 weapon pages, a couple of "notes" pages and
a final page for the answer to the mystery!

Anyhoo...I've posted some instructions, cover art and inside pages in
the files section if anyone is interested in it. If you would like
to use one of these clue booklets, but are unable to print, don't
have time, etc., please just post and let me know! I would be happy
to make up booklets for anyone who wants them. I'll bring them with
me Saturday morning when we come to the park.

Of course, this is just optional. I thought it might help keep me
organized and or course, my children think it's very "detective-like"
and thus, a must have. :-)

I hope this might be helpful to ya'll, and I can't wait for the
Event! Thanks to Ruby T. for all of her assistance (and letting me
toss my two cents into the game)!

See you all soon! BusyBeesFamily

From: Hawk (omkarmashanti@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-03-10 16:47:15 UTC-08:00
I printed out my book, and it turned out WAAAAAY
COOOOOL!!!!!! Great job, busybeesfamily! I can hardly
wait to start collecting stamps on the pages. I used
all premium cardstock, and it was too thick to staple.
I cut each page in half where I had folded it, then I
punched a hole in the upper left corner of each page
and put a cable-tie through it. This created a loop of
plastic holding all the pages together. Now I can flip
through the pages easily and stamp away!

When I'm done, I will probably transfer the pages
(including title page, etc.) to my logbook. Since I
can do this after the event, I will have time to add
in photos and notes at my leisure. The CLUE logbook
will become part of my permanent logbook.

Hawk
