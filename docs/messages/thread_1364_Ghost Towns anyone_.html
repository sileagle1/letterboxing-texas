From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-10-05 13:42:20 UTC-07:00
Anybody like ghost towns? I found a pretty good site
for them while I was doing research on JEN.

http://www.ghosttowns.com/states/tx/tx.html

I went ahead and pulled information on the ones in JEN
for reference, and here they are in case they inspire
anyone to do a stamp and a box for the gathering. I
can't vouch for whether any of them would actually
have anywhere to plant, but some of y'all are pretty
creative, so have at it!

Ellis County
Telico-birthplace of Clyde Barrow
Crisp-birthplace of Ernest Tubb (there is a historical
marker on the site)

Navarro County
Cryer Creek-named such because there is a waterfall
near the town and the townspeople thought the falls
sounded like a woman who cried out for help after she
fell in the creek.
Curry-this was an oil boomtown in 1923.
