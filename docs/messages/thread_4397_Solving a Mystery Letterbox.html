LOL....You CAN solve the clue without reading the book but it probably helps to have read the book.

However, since solving the clue a few years ago, I've had it in the back of my head that I want to read the book, so I got it for Christmas. I've yet to start it, but soon I will. After solving those clues, it seems sort of like reading the book after watching the movie.

I read Wuthering Heights in high school and while I can't quite say I HATED it, I didn't particularly like it. A couple of years ago, I decided to re-read everything I read in high school, so I read The Pearl, Wuthering Heights, The Scarlet Letter, A Tale of Two Cities, The Canterbury Tales, and a few others. I started with Wuthering Heights because it was the one I remembered especially disliking. Turns out - it WAS an incredible book! There was so much subtle story and understory, so many relationships running, that it kept me on the edge of my seat. I had suspected I would like the book more as an adult, but I hadn't expected to like it as well as I did. It seems life experience helped me better understand why the characters did what they did. I went on to read all those other books with relish. Some of them were better than I remembered - others I had to wonder why they ever had us read them in the first place.

Three or four years ago, Dewberry, TeamKing and Mother of 5 hosted The Great Book Exchange. It was a literature-oriented gathering. They put out an insane number of temporary boxes and quite a few permanent ones in Memorial Park in Houston - all based on books and stories. You'll see that many of us have Literary Travelers - most of them stemmed from that event. About that time, Dewberry mentioned what a fan she is of Jane Austen, so after I finished my re-reading, I launched off to other classics and read Austen's Pride and Prejudice and Sense and Sensibility on Dewberry's recommendation. Loved them. Now I'm reading Little Women. Next comes The Scarlet Pimpernel. We'll see about that one.

I skimmed Scarlet Pimpernel when I was working on the clues to the mystery box, then went back and read it after finding the box - as kind of a tribute to the "Scarlet Pimpernel". Booots, Ruby gave me a hint also, and I just didn't get it! I still had to suffer hours of lost sleep. It became an obsession with me - solving those clues. I had a file folder full of attmepts! I actually would get up in the middle of the night with an idea and work on it for hours, only to reach another dead end. Finally one day, after several months of trying, when I figured it out, I was in shock that I had finally done it- much like a detective who solves a huge case. I immediately emailed my solution to Ruby Tuesday and asked, "if I were to follow these clues, would I perhaps find a certain letterbox?" When she replied in the affirmative with one small tweek, I literally jumped in the old Jeepster and rushed to the scene (faster than to a certain Texas Tour Bus). I will never forget sitting in my Jeep holding that box in my hands, and wondering what I was going to do with my life from now on. Don't anyone ask me to help them with that box!
Puddle Splasher
