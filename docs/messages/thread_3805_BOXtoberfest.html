Re: BOXtoberfest

From: Vicki (vicki@bellmotel.com) | Date: 2008-10-09 16:03:37 UTC
BOXtoberfest was a lot of fun. We ended up with only about 25 folks
attending, but we all had a great time. I didn't think that I would
get to actually "do" the event, because I was on the "committee", but
Cancan sent us all out to drive the route and find the boxes! Just
because we carved them, didn't mean we knew where Cancan had hidden
them. Plus, Cancan had created puzzles for us to solve at every
stop. I got to drive the event with AZRoadie from Arizona, and we
laughed a lot. The route took us to or through the most scenic
and/or historical sites in Austin, and was a beautiful and
informative drive. Kudos to the committe members who created the
route! The boxes had lovely carves, and were fun finds, especially
Annabee's coffin in the Texas State Cemetery. The final destination
was Scholtz Garten, a German beer garden built in 1866. They let us
reserve a room for free, and took great care of us. Almost everybody
won some kind of prize at the event. Cancan procured lots of Texas-
themed prizes -- great books, t-shirts, mugs, magnets, fun stuff! I
asked Lionsmane and Astro D to hand-make a log book each to give as a
prize. Lionsmane's book went to Bloomin' Gramma Jo, from Austin,
TX. Astro D's book went to a Estes Ink, from Saginaw, TX. And I
ordered two Cutter Bees to give as prizes. My pharmacist husband,
Whiz Gee, and I also gave everyone a prank Texas Letterboxing Gift
Bag -- with sunscreen, aloe vera lotion for sunburns, Calamine lotion
for poison ivy, a snakebite kit for snakes, tweezers for the cactus
spines, latex gloves, aspirin, Tylenol, Claritin! We thought we were
being really funny, and hoped others enjoyed the prank. Wish you all
could have been there!
I really want to thank Cancan for having the great idea of doing a
Letterboxing Road Rally, and for inviting us to be on her committee.
I had no idea a Road Rally could be so much fun!
-- Bell Motel

Re: [letterboxingtexas] Re: BOXtoberfest

From: Cancan (cancan1959@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-10-09 21:56:51 UTC-05:00
Thanks for the kind words, but I would not have attempted this and it would not have been such a nice event  without the Gunslinger Sweethearts:AnnaBee, Bell Motel (Whiz Gee), Bloomin Gramma Jo (Bird Dog), Dolphinfish, Rainbow Seeker and of course my better half (Stone Hunter).  It was the collective work of the group that lead to us all having a wonderful time.

We mapped a 30 mile loop route starting at the State Capitol and 5 additional Austin icon stops. Each participant was given an Austin passport to stamp their finds into as a momento. We had participants from Tucson, AZ, North of Fort Worth, San Antonio and the Austin area.  Unfortunately in one of my brain burp moments I forgot to charge my camera batteries and came with a dead camera. If anyone did get pictures and would download onto the AQ site it would be greatly appreciated.

Each committee member carved a GREAT box and I hope they all find permanent locations soon (hint, hint).  Bell Motel also carved an (additional) event stamp, ask to see her handiwork in my logbook sometime.  AnnaBee's "container" for her State Cemetery box is probably one of the most unique (and most befitting) I have encountered and she arranged for us to get the very unique traveling event stamp "Listening to the Letterpod."  Bloomin Gramma Jo and Bird Dog meticulously laid out the route over several days after we all met several times stalking locations and possible routes.  The event day weather was perfect and miraculously there were no events closing down the downtown streets...which here in Austin IS really a small miracle.

EVERYONE (12 cars) made it to the end mystery location without ripping open their PANIC envelopes. The venue was indeed Scholtz Garten, because after all we did combine letterboxing with Octoberfest!  Scholtz gave us our own room (gratis) thanks to Bell Motel's neighbor connection and they did a great job of taking care of us.  We had a door prize for every one who entered the drawing, and the two handmade logbooks from Astro D and Lionsmane were incredible.  Everyone also recieved a super cute Texas Letterboxer (First Aid) Kit given by the Apothocary Shop Pharmacy (NW Austin). I hope I never need the snake bite kit or Calamine Lotion!

A note of thanks to Natty Bumpo and BNBali girl who I met through the AQ boards.  They had hosted LB Road Rallies previously and freely shared great advice, rally clues and rules. This kind of event really is a lot of work. 

But had it not been for Brown Knight's mystery box and a bunch of wonderful and wacky ladies from Austin who met at a park to sweep the joint clean, this would have remained just a notion. Thank you again Gunslinger Sweethearts and to those of you who attended, thanks for joining us!

Kind Regards,
(The long winded and appreciative) Cancan
