Mother of Five and I managed to get out on the Green Trail at Memorial
Park for a bit yesterday, dragging along one kid with strep throat
(mine) and one kid in a backpack (hers). My Houston Hills was in the
wrong spot, so it's been replaced in the proper tree. I now
understand why people were finding it by accident. But, after being
completely out of its hiding place twice, I'm just glad it's still alive.

I am considering hosting a low-key boxing morning the day after the
Great Book Adventure. It's to show off my favorite spot in Houston,
Memorial Park, simply because I love it so much. Memorial Park is
about 40 minutes away from Tomball, much more inside Houston, and is
really a great urban park. So, if you're up for some boxing that
morning, let me know (there's a public golf course there if some of
your party would rather do that while others golf). The park is about
5 minutes from my house, much easier for me than the trek to Tomball
(but the opposite problem for you if you're staying in Tomball). I
will plant about a half-dozen boxes for that day, bring the Kolaches
and the coffee, plus carve another event stamp.

This will be VERY low-key. I will just grab a few tables in the
picnic area as a place to deposit clue sheets and breakfast. .
.nothing else planned (well, maybe a few surprises depending on energy
level). If you're interested, sign up on Atlasquest. If there's no
interest, I'll sleep in!

Dewberry
