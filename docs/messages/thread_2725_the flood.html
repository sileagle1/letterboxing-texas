the flood

From: Sandy Milfeld (studiousmom@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2007-04-19 21:57:21 UTC-05:00
I can't believe how high the water got in the creeks around here (fort worth) during our last flood. I'm shocked! The deluge ripped out huge chunks of concrete near the sidewalk and they had slammed into trees. There was a high chair up in the tree about 3 feet above my head at one spot on Fossil Creek.

My daughter's only box, "Jack", washed away. She is very depressed. She had hand tooled a leather cover for the logbook and everything!We went today to collect it for retirement, and saw the devastation. I'm no longer worried about noxers and muggles. I'm worried about the next storm!

I guess, I couldn't be as lucky as I was with the other box of yours that we found at the gardens. But, that is always a possibility that we know exists. Boxes do sometimes disappear. Tell her not to be too upset. Hopefully she will make some more to plant.

The Cruisers actually found the lid to the original Lunching Longhorn. So it's contents are certainly destroyed. Jack may have survived and may be downstream somewhere. But I won't hold my breath.

We were in Austin when the storm hit, so I'm not sure how close the tornado came, but my dad's truck and roof were beat up over in Euless, and my friend in Benbrook said several large trees were uprooted and some power lines snapped like toothpicks.

Hottopic has decided to do Jack II, and possibly a Sally, too.
