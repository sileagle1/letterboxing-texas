Great Book Adventure

From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-10-22 20:05:13 UTC
Well, I for one had a terrific time! Thanks to all who came and to
those who brought boxes or carved a literary traveler, a double thank
you. Mother of Five, TeamKing and I slipped away for a bit of boxing
yesterday ourselves and really enjoyed finding boxes that others had
prepared.

For those of you who didn't come, our biggest surprise was the
storybook stroll. We set this up for children, but a lot of the
adults enjoyed it as well. Instead of following clues, the kids had
to look for items in the woods, a toothbrush for Junie B Jones,
toothless wonder, etc. Some were easier hides than others. Also on
the stroll was a camo-taped blue dolphin for the adults to play I spy
with. Only a couple adults found it, but some sharp-eyed children
spotted the dolphin in the trees.

There were other secret hides, including a special Nancy Drew stamp
inside a flashlight, Little Women (I think only two people found
them), Hansel & Gretel inside the candy bowl, Elizabeth inside the
girl's bathroom while Darcy was in the men's, and so on.

Some of my favorite moments:

-Handing Running Rain, aka Hermione, the special costume box. (See,
it pays to dress up).
-The look on a child's face when he found Peter Pan and then Hook all
by himself.
-Watching Wanda search all over for the Ugly Duckling box, which was
disguised as a rock.
-Finding a special puzzle hidden just for me.
-Being there when Lightning cracked the code for the mystery box, aka
The Anne Frank box.
-Seeing my own children enthralled on the Storybook Stroll--my five
year old had the best time, and was shocked to discover things from
his own house, including a stuffed penguin snatched off his bed while
he was sleeping.

And afterwards, looking through the literary traveler book and the
special commemorative book boxes that the PEAS put together.

And one more observation--I really thought one of MOther of Five's was
going to be the first to go plonk into the water, but it turned out to
be Barefoot Lucy's son Lightning!

I didn't take nearly enough pictures, so would love it if people would
post more. You can also post them on AQ so that folks outside of
Texas can see our foolery. Just go to the event page, and there is a
link called upload pictures.

Dewberry
