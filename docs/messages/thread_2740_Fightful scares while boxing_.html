Fightful scares while boxing?

From: (blhjrh@aol.com) | Date: 2007-04-26 10:24:47 UTC-04:00
I've not yet encountered anything really really scary, but I did get a fright once in a cemetery in a rural area while looking for Celtic Lions' Caddo Cemetery box - struggled to lift a large rock covering a small depression in the ground near the fence where the box was hidden and uncovered the box AND a small field mouse, who, I have to admit, was far more frightened than I was.  But I did fling that heavy rock quite a way when I jumped and yelped.  Then, of course, I did have to put it back later - watching carefully for any snake who might have been interested in the mouse.  So far, though, I've been pretty lucky - only one frightful experience and that by a small fieldmouse.

From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-04-26 17:26:15 UTC
Sometimes:

My son and I had a cottonmouth jump out of the grass, rear up and
lunge at us on the trail.

The whole family was chased by a swarm of yellow jackets (they can
sting through clothes).

Discovering that a box's hiding place was a homeless person's
residence.

Coming across a couple in the act.

These were all surprises that you couldn't anticipate. There have
been other times when we've left rather than hunt a box, because
something about the situation that didn't feel right. It probably
would have been fine, but why not err on the side of caution (so says
the person who hikes by herself all the time).

dewberry

Re: [letterboxingtexas] Re: Fightful scares while boxing?

From: (kacmbc@aol.com) | Date: 2007-04-26 14:18:32 UTC-04:00
 
Several come to mind and all but one involve the Frye Team!  :)  Maybe I should rethink who I box with?
 
Washington on the Brazos State Park:  All of the clues matched perfectly to the route my husband and I chose.  Of course, I wouldn't let him have the clues because this was MY hobby.  Our last clue, and the location of the letterbox took us to a tree near the water. Box was in a cavity on the side of a tree. This tree had a cavity and was near the water.  I bent down to reach in for the box ( did I mention that I was brand new to letterboxing at this time?) and then pulled my hand back quickly.  Maybe I should look inside the cavity first!  Thank goodness I did, because as I bent down, I came eye to eye with a rather large water moccasin, who had taken up residence in the cavity!  That was when my husband took the clues away from me and decided to call the shots...
 
Frye Team:  That overprotective dog at the Pop a Top letterbox site.  He was friendly at first, but then managed to get between me (out of the car) and Frye Team ( sitting protected IN the car).  Angela even tried throwing my lunch at him but he was more interested in me.  He jumped up on me a couple of times and I tried scratching his head, but his jumping became more violent as did his demeanor.   Aside from a few scratches, all was okay!  And then there was the tick episode and the pack of dogs episode...
 
All in the name of fun and games, right?  :)
