This weekend's adventure

From: Vicki Bell (vicki@bellmotel.com) | Date: 2010-04-12 22:35:23 UTC-05:00
Whiz Gee and I went on a letterboxing adventure this weekend.  It all started when I deciphered the clues to the Original Gryzzled Gryphon Box.   This emboldened me to try to decipher other Gryzzled Gryphon box clues, and I figured out a few more.  Whiz Gee decided that we should plan a letterboxing trip around finding Gryzzled Gryphon boxes.  Since one of our most fun letterboxing days was spent driving 300 miles in one day to find boxes by Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter, this sounded like a great idea.  I decided to add other Mystery Boxes to our quest, so it became the Great Gryzzled Gryphon/Magical Mystery Box Tour of 2010.
We traveled 800 miles in three and a half days, and hiked for miles on trails.  We discovered parts of Texas we never would have visited otherwise, and Texas State Parks that we'd never heard of, including Fort Parker State Park -- a wonderful jewel.  It was fun to be on a quest, even if it was comically quixotic at times.  We only found three of the six Gryzzled Gryphon boxes that we had set our sights on, and only four out of the other seven Mystery Boxes we hunted.  However, in all we found 37 letterboxes, and enjoyed our Texas tour.  But, I have to admit we are really tired today!
-- Bell Motel
