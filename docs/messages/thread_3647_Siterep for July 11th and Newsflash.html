Siterep for July 11th and Newsflash

From: tigger32167 (tigger32167@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-07-13 17:03:09 UTC
Maydelle Queen Bee and I decided that it was time for us to finally
go boxing together. So we started out in Rusk at around 11 am and
headed for Turnertown, where I wanted to place my new box "Honky
Tonk Blues." While we were looking for a place to plant the box, I
realized that we were at the location of the "Joe Roughneck" box, so
we decided that MQB should go ahead and get it, since I already had
it. Found the box with no problem and went back to Turnertown,
where we finally had success in placing my box. We then went to New
London for MQB to pick up the box there, and then we headed to
Kilgore. I placed 4 new boxes in Kilgore and then we went to
Danville and tried to locate the Danville Cemetery to place another
box. Well between my GPS (Gertie) and a book that I had about the
cemetery, we were having a hard time finding this cemetery. So, MQB
was able to Google it on her phone, and we finally managed to get to
the Danville Cemetery. The next challenge was trying to find the
headstone for Karen Silkwood, who was the inspiration for the box.
Again, MQB came through with her phone/computer and we finally found
it and placed the box. Next we went to Synergy Park and picked up
the 3 boxes there. If you have never been to this park, I highly
recommend it. It is so peaceful. We then drove to Kilgore and
started with the Build a Doll Series and Race Car. The clue states
that it is a 1 mile trip...little did we know that it meant 1 mile
one way...which then made it a two mile walk. We did manage to find
all the boxes and it was a great series, and we were the first
finders for Race Car, so I let MQB keep the prize for that one.
However after the 2 1/2 mile hike (after finding Race Car) we headed
back to the car and decided that we needed an Icee break. If you
are ever hot and tired while letterboxing, let me tell you that
Icees are great. They have a way of cooling you off and giving you
a little pep. We realized while MQB was stamping in at the Doll
Series that she had hit her 100 mark, which is a great feeling.
After the Icee we went and picked up the Spring Series by
frogkisses. While we were getting the Spring Series, we did notice
a squirrel who was climbing down a vine to a creek and getting
water. This was such a cute sight. This is a really nice series
also. At that point, I realized that I only needed 1 more box to
have my 700th find. So we proceeded to Old Tyme Mill by Blue
Butterfly and I got my 700th find. Well after all the walks, we
decided to call it a day and head home. We got home around 9 pm,
tired and out of energy. We had a great time, and MQB, when are we
going out again???

JBK boxes placed - 6
JBK boxes found - 13
MQB boxes found - 23


*******NEWSFLASH*******
Out of staters beware...it appears that a certain letterboxer, who
in his years of boxing had never left the state of Texas to box, has
recently been discovered crossing state lines to box. One Gryzzled
Gryphon, headed into Oklahoma where he finally located 3 boxes in
Hugo and 1 in Frogville. Who knows where he might head to next?????
