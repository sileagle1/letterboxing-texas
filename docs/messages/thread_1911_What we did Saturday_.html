That radio site was the first solar powered system in the United States.  It is also the highest radio repeater in Texas.  It has been up there so long it pre-dates the State Park, and is grandfathered.  Not only does that repeater provide ham radio communication for nearly 8000 square miles of Texas, New Mexico and Mexico, it also contains an ELT receiver.  The ELT monitors for downed aircraft.

 

Periodically it needs maintenance.  The winds up there are fierce, and antennas do not last long.  We are hopeful that this latest antenna will hold up for more than a year.

 

You can see the hike at Localhikes.com.  Here is the address.

 

http://www.localhikes.com/Hikes/N_Franklin_Mountain_2320.asp

 

It is quite a hike, especially carrying an antenna, a hunk of water pipe, and a pack full or tools.  They got some good photos of me working on the antenna.
