Ugh! A snake under my letterbox...

From: Wanda (wsthm@hotmail.com) | Date: 2004-10-19 04:19:21 UTC

This afternoon my granddaughter and I went to get a photo of 'Flat
Stanlie' and two Barbie dolls going letterboxing. Um...yeah, that's
another reason people think I'm weird...

Anyway, I went to my nearest, and easiest to deal with, letterbox
between Skidmore and Beeville. I told Shabree to pose the dolls near
the hiding place next to a bush and some curbing while I took the
piece of paver off of the top of the ground, and took the small
letterbox out from underneath. Down in the hole, partially hidden by
the rocks I lined the hole with was some kind of small snake. He
never moved the whole time. We moved our photo shoot over slightly
and I warned Shabree not to make many vibrations - like jumping or
kicking the curbing or anything. I quickly laid out the contents of
the box, arranged them nicely and got a couple of shots, with
Stanlie, Barbie and um...Barbie looking like they were perusing the
contents. Then I carefully moved Flat Stanlie over to peer down the
hole and got a photo of her looking at the snake. Hopefully the
snake will show up in the picture. After that, I placed the
letterbox back on/above the snake, replaced the broken paver and we
moved on.

I have no idea what kind it was. It was all curled up and partially
hidden. It was about as big around as my finger, and maybe not quite
a foot long, if I had to guess. It was patterned and kind of
black,gray,brown dirt colored. I couldn't see it's tail at all to
look for rattles. I'm thinking - hoping - it is just a rat snake of
some kind. But whether anyone searches for that box or any other, be
sure to BE CAREFUL.

I noticed someone new had signed that logbook, so checked my
hitchhiker hostel too....a double hole. I was really, really careful
and lightfingered getting those bigger boxes out of those bigger
hiding places and checking them. No snakes there though, thankfully.

Busy Bee (Wanda) and Shabree.
