Rare Experience

From: tigger32167 (tigger32167@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-06-04 12:11:45 UTC
just had to tell everyone about something that happened while i was
out letterboxing yesterday. i was in tyler finding and planting some
boxes and went to tiger creek refuge in red springs to try to locate
one of barefoot lucy's boxes (it has been eaten by a shaggy green
monster that it was near). first of all let me say that if you have
never been to tiger creek, you really need to go...see lucy's box for
more detail...when i went into the gift shop, there was an 8 week old
white tiger laying on the floor. needless to say i quickly asked if i
could take a picture of her..and being the animal person that i am, i
then asked if i could pet her, and they said ok. so i actually got to
pet an 8 week old white tiger with beautiful blue eyes. this was
truly a once in a lifetime experience.
jb kokopelli

Re: [letterboxingtexas] Rare Experience

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-06-05 08:14:47 UTC-05:00
When we first started going there, it was about $5 per person, but as you said, $10 really isn't that bad even still...
 
Every cat has a story and a name.  You can tell the cats have an affectionate relationship with their keepers, and even though the keepers never let their guards down, you can tell they love the cats too.  They have a pair of cats that were featured on Growing Up Tiger on Animal Planet a few years ago, and they have one (at least) of Michael Jackson's animals that they took in when he got tired of having them.  I think they have some of Siegfried and Roy's cats, and then they have numerous cats they have rescued from unscrupulous circus and entertainment type venues, as well as from private citizens who didn't know what they were biting off when they somehow got their hands on a tiger cub.  One cat sucks his thumb (he was badly declawed, and the rescue does a spiel about the inhumanity of declawing cats).  Others are shy, while some are outgoing.  Every time I go there, I learn something new about tigers and other big cats and about a specific cat or two.  I once took a group of cub scouts and one of their lionesses was in a hunting mood - it's a little unnerving to have a lion stalk your group, even if you DO know she can't reach you!
 
Yep...definitely worth $10!
