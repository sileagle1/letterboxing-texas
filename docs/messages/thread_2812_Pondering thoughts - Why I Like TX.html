Good question and one that has made me think hard to answer. I am a
native, so for me the greatest thing about TX is being a Texan. Not
sure why, but Texans always seem to be a little more proud of their
heritage, a little more rebelious in nature, and tough. I'm probably
prejudiced, but I feel these things and am proud to be a Texan.

As far as the State itself, I can't think of many other states with
as much diversity of people, climate and landscape as TX. We have
beaches, mountains, deserts, plains and woods. We have dry heat over
100 degrees to wet snow below freezing. Some places might have one
or two of these things better than us, but not all of them. If I
could live anywhere I wanted, I would still live in TX, just not
Houston area. My favorite area of TX is Davis Mts, and I need to get
back that way soon.

I agree with YALL, I love the diversity of our state. I love the
fact that I can drive all day from one side of the state to the
other and still be in Texas. Our long coast line, our raw desserts
and mountains, our thick east Texas pines, our hills where the grass
blows like waves and our quiet prairies; all make our state almost
magic. I even love the fact, that we are bordered with a whole
other Country. But the the people make this state great. I hardly
go anywhere in the state, and meet a stranger, at least their not
strangers for long. People here are just friendly, that's why our
state motto is "Friendship". I am so proud to be a Texan, of course
that why I picked my trail name. And if I can live anywhere, it
would still be here in TEXAS!

From: Joyce Hughes (joyceann888@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-06-08 16:02:46 UTC-05:00
What do I like about Texas? As a non-native, I like the variety of
landscapes and the overall milder weather (if you leave out July,
August, etc.).

I like that Texans care about being Texaan. There is an awareness of
being from this state that I haven't seen in most others.

Most of all, I like the people. Texans are some the the most
friendly, finest folk around. You'll hear me whine about the heat,
the lack of mountains, etc., but I have totally enjoyed my years here
(most of my life--since 6th grade).

I know I don't qualify as a Native Texan, but I'm about as close as
you can get without being one.

Okay, I thought I'd get a response with the "lack of mountains"
comment. I know that the southern end of the Rocky Mountains are in
Texas. This is one place where my "non-native-ness" comes through. I
am from Colorado, so, to me, mountains are 14,j000 feet tall with snow
on them year round.

However, don't misunderstand. Less than a month ago, we took our
travel trailer to Davis Mountain State Park. We LOVED it out there.
We're 10-15 years from retirement and starting to think about "what's
next." The Alpine area is definitely "numero uno" on that list. We
loved the area, the people, and the restaurants. We're already talking
about the "next trip to Alpine."

So, while I have a hard time thinking of what Texas has as real
"mountains," I still love it all!
