Hi! I'm new here

From: kerry1194 (k_kellogg@hotmail.com) | Date: 2007-09-15 15:06:48 UTC
Just wanted to introduce myself. My family started letterboxing in
May. We live in Fort Worth and have barely made a dent in the boxes
around here. Plus we took the rainy season off, which means that we
didn't do a lot over the summer.

Mostly it's my child and I, as I have a LSS. He likes that we are
getting outside and that I have found a way to share hiking with DS,
but he kinda rolls his eyes at the whole stamp aspect of lbing.
Though DH is a photographer, so I think I have him convinced that we
can go lbing tomorrow and that everyone can have fun.

Can I ask a question? Has this year been brutal on letterboxes? With
all the rain and flooding, I'm wondering if a lot of boxes have gone
missing. Or are we truly clueless when in comes to actually finding
some of these boxes. Yesterday, we went to Granbury with 5 clues and
were only able to actually find 2 of the boxes. I was smart and took
photos of the areas we were looking in and I have contacted the placer
in hopes to establishing how far away from the boxes we actually were,
but it would be nice to know how often boxes have simply disappeared
this year.

Kerry

From: Maribeth Jagger (cjagger405@earthlink.net) | Date: 2007-09-15 19:42:06 UTC-05:00
Thank you for taking a few minutes to introduce yourself, Kerry. I know what you mean about not letterboxing much this summer since it went straight from the monsoon season to intense heat. But now it seems like the perfect weather in the north Texas area for letterboxing, so I hope you and your family have fun and success. I think I've indicated in my clues which of my boxes in the DFW area were unavailable due to floods. Since the water has been receding, some of my boxes are available again so I took the "unavailable" tag off those, but will leave the others marked unavailable until I get a chance to check on them. Unfortunately, it is the nature of the game that some boxes will go missing, and there is not always a prize at the end of the rainbow, but hopefully, when planters realize their boxes are missing, they will mark them accordingly. I don't want to sound "preachy" here, but if you look for a box and can't find it, you might be looking in the wrong place, or it may in fact actually be missing, but the chances are that those clues led you to some really neat place you've never been before. To me, that's the best part about letterboxing. Happy hunting, and welcome to the group. Glad to have another boxer in the DFW area!
Puddle Splasher

From: kerry1194 (k_kellogg@hotmail.com) | Date: 2007-09-16 01:37:13 UTC
I figured that weather plays a big role in letterboxing. Be it too
hot, too buggy or too wet to let us get to the boxes. The trip to
Granbury was great for us. It helped me teach Z that winning isn't
everything. He spent lots of time play with rocks and throwing stuff
into the water and the ice cream cones made his day. I haven't given
up on these boxes.

I'm currently assuming that we were way off or missing something in
the clues. That's why I took pictures. This way the planter can tell
me for sure that we were in the wrong spot or looking under the wrong
tree or wait! Half the bank is gone where I planted the box. The fun
part is that we could always go back.

My biggest problem at this point is training the kid. I still don't
feel comfortable with his subterfuge abilities, so there are many
boxes that I wouldn't even think about trying. Yesterday was
entertaining. I purposely dressed us in out of state tshirts so we
could act like silly northerners. Then we reduced our kit to a couple
of index cards, a pen and a small ink pad. Z suggested that we use
our fingerprints, because it was faster than pulling out stamps. I
think the fact that we were there in the afternoon, just before school
got out, helped because we were pretty much alone in the parks. We're
not ready for Downtown Dallas, but I think we don't have to only go to
secluded parks. :)

Kerry
