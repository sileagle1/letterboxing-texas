Can you believe it? We are missing another key!!! This is getting embarrassing – every time we go letterboxing, we lose our keys! We had heard about a letterbox around White Rock Lake and went to investigate, but couldn’t find it. On the way back to the car, we realized that once again, we had lost a key, so the only solution was to think backwards ( easy for us!): Hmmm…let’s think…we had poked a long Irish shillelagh all around the tree trunk, avoiding the attack of the big black ants. We had reached the tree after we walked along with the creek on our left for about 20 paces at about 240 degrees SW, taking care not to slip down the embankment. The creek path was reached by walking about 25 paces between two big sets of red lettered signs prohibiting wading or walking on the spillway. We had stepped through an opening in a big black chain link fence to reach the signs. The opening in the fence was less than 100 yards from the wooden bridge where we had stopped to talk to that guy casting for black bass. And the bridge wasn't far from where we had parked the car in the parking lot at the corner of Winsted and Garland Road...

 

Please email us at: celticlions@comcast.net if you find our MISSING KEY #2!

 

NOTE: Always take adequate precautions such as prodding with a stick or wearing gloves before reaching into dark crevasses and holes in trees! Please replace this box exactly as you found it, hiding it securely, so future letterboxers will enjoy it, too. Thanks!
