Re: Going to the coast

From: monkeytoes108 (cruschhaupt@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-10-11 01:25:51 UTC

Change of plans... It turned out that Padre Island was closer to
where we were staying than Goose island. We had a 50% success rate
over all.

Hummer Lady's Roseate Spoon Bill is missing. The grounds were pretty
manicured, so I think the groundskeepers found it. Great spot
though. My brother-in-law said he would have never thought to go
the birding center on his own. We had a good time there and the
roseate spoonbill became a recurring visitor for the rest of our
trip.

We headed south to Mustand Island and found Baby Bear's Coral
Snake. First letterbox ever for our hosts and the kids were pretty
excited.

Spurred on by the recent succes we headed to Padre Island for Silver
Eagle's Texas Turtle. All four adults were on their hands and knees
at some point digging around in the rocks. We came up empty
handed. It looks like some of the rocks had been washed out, so
maybe the box went with it. Maybe we're just blind.


On the way out of town, we hit Lighthouse Trails park and found BB's
Super Frogger. It was right next to a dead fish, so I think it's
pretty safe from noxers (non-boxers).

We parted ways with our company and me, the wife(Play Family Mama)
and the little one (Curly Q) were going to hit the Goose Island
boxes on the way home. Q decided to begin her nap about a mile from
the park, so we had to just keep on truckin.

We'll be back to the Goose soon, but even batting 0.500, we had a
good time and hooked a new letterboxing family.

-MonkeyToes

From: Connie Terry (connie@lavernia.net) | Date: 2004-10-10 21:44:23 UTC-05:00
While Monkeytoes was at the coast, I was in Victoria finding his boxes.
My first stop was to look for "Box Kite" and after seeing all the briar
and brambles, I took a trip back to the jeep for my "Hokey Pokey" stick
(trail name for the walking stick we used to find our very first box,
the "Hokey Pokey" box). I was delighted to be the first finder, take my
first look at Monkeytoes' impressive carving and also find the "Moving a
Giraffe is a TALL Order" hitchhiker. This is a well-traveled giraffe
with quite an impressive log book!

Next was the "Lost Moai" at Riverside Park. This was fun. Neat
location, fun clues and a one sided game of hide and seek because of the
golfers across the way. What is that Moai statue doing in Victoria
anyway?

Then to the "Riverside Duck Pond". I put my LB gear in my little ice
chest so I could be a little bit incognito and pretend Iike I was only
going there to eat lunch. Glad I had that ice chest. This letterbox
isn't for short people! At least not for short people without ice
chests to use for step stools! For anyone planning to find this box in
the future: there is an active nest of yellow jackets very near this
box.
