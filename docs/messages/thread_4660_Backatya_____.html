Well – welcome to El Paso and Fort Bliss…. a little late I guess.  <grin>.

 

Let me tell you my thoughts.  Active Duty Military people don’t pay to play.  I will give you a little story.

 

In Gulf War-I, I started out in Alabama - TDY.  I returned home, tossed my dress uniforms in the wash, loaded up my field gear and left for war.  I had been gone 4 months previous to the unit deployment, so my family was already without a dad.  I ended up at Khobar Towers where I stayed until the ground offensive.  I was then attached to 18th Abn Corps, assigned to 24th ID (mech) and I met the Republican Guard on the western hail mary.  My qualifications as a scout/sniper were valuable, and I was promptly put you know where…. Right on the front.

 

Now – my wife knew none of this. The last she heard, she thought I was still at Khobar Towers.  When the Scud missile hit the barracks at Khobar towers, she watched on TV as they pulled a dead body out of the wreckage.  That body looked just like me.  It was 3 days before my family knew they still had a dad.  Wanna guess how she felt raising two kids on army pay and wondering if she was now on her own forever? 

 

My wife and family were grateful for every little kindness and comfort while I was away.

 

My point is this.  Families serve just as much as the service member.  Leaping out of aircraft and getting shot at isn’t the only tough duty, and may not be the toughest.  I understand you wanting to give something back.  But please do not feel you must.  It is my pleasure to have you enjoy your time here, and it is enough for me that you and your family make some good memories (I hope) while visiting El Paso.  I expect your leaving may be in conjunction with a combat deployment and you will be going somewhere with the kids while your significant other gets to leap out of aircraft and get shot at.  Am I close?  (I could have it backward!)  <grin>.

 

I have learned through Geocaching (GASP – he said the G word!) that one or two really good boxes is better than a dozen so-so boxes.  If you want to place a box or two, take your time and make them really special ones.  When you leave, I will try to adopt them and watch over them for you.

 

Again, there will be many many more opinions here, and I expect you will get some of them real soon.

Well – the cheapest GPS that I could recommend is the older Garmin E-Trex.  It was a yellow one with a black/white screen.  They once sold for about $179, but are pretty cheap now…..  Like this one.

If you can afford it, the one I recommend is the Garmin GPS-MAP60CSx.  Better antenna, better chipset, longer battery life, color display.  It once sold for nearly $500.  Now-a-days if can be had new for under $300, and used - ????  Like this one.

http://catalog.ebay.com/Garmin-GPSMAP-60CSx-GPS-Receiver-/78389307

 

I truly wish I had an older one I could toss your way.  However, I have given them all away to other soldiers going to the war.  I only have my personal 60CSx left.

 

NOTE – I do not own nor will I recommend Magellan.  Magellan is owned by Thales.  Thales is the French Aerospace company.  After the trouble they caused our soldiers in 2003-2004, I will not send one cent their way.  Garmin is US owned and based in Kansas, and supports our troops.

 

I Letterbox and Geocache when we are on the road to dog shows.  Great Danes always show early, so we get plenty of time to find hidden treasures.  And you are quite correct – the locals planting a nice box always know the best places, places travelers will almost never know of.
