GREAT NEWS !!!!!

From: Mike Olbrisch (mike-2007@elp.rr.com) | Date: 2008-11-20 21:12:37 UTC-07:00

Good evening.

I have in my possession a packet from 3d ACR - rear detachment. It contains the
following.

------------------------

OPERATIONAL SCHEDULE.

10-30 Jan 2009 - MOST soldiers to return from Iraq to Ft. Hood.
21 Feb - 22 Mar - Regimental Block Leave.
27 Mar - 3d ACR Family Day at Bell County Expo Center.
3 Apr - 3d ACR Regimental Change of Command.

The rear detachment command is working out how they will notify individual
families about the arrival dates and times of their deployed family members. At
this time, no firm dates have been decided upon.


MAIL OPERATIONS.

The deadline for sending mail is 1 Dec 2008. Any mail sent after that date will
be returned. 3 ACR Forward requests that no Christmas decorations be sent.
Connexes will be locked for shipment prior to the holidays.

-----------------------------

The rest is militareze. Not readable by most civilians... . The usual,
do you need to talk to the chaplain, do you need assistance, are you moving back
to Ft. Hood and need housing, etc etc etc.....

Of course, this is the ARMY! Anything can - and often does - CHANGE. I will
keep those interested updated. Reply to this E-mail if you want to stay in the
loop. Of course Moni and I intend to go to Ft. Hood to welcome our warrior son
home.

James discussed it with us. He does not intend to take much leave during block
leave. He will come home for his things, then return within days. It is his
intent to save as much leave as possible to take as terminal leave upon his ETS.
This will allow him to get to El Paso in plenty of time for the electrician
apprentice classes he will be attending in Sept 2009. His ETS would be 21 Oct
without terminal leave.

I hope you all will join us in our happiness that this is almost over. We as a
family understand the need, and certainly support keeping the fight off our
streets and in their backyard. Still, it will be nice to have him home. Nearly
18 months of combat - in the hottest spot in Iraq. Well - it's the CAVALRY -
and he is a scout, but it is time.

More to follow as we get it - for those who reply.

Mike - KD9KC.
El Paso, Tx.
