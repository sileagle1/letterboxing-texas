Tyler State Park boxes

From: Linda (lindak92@msn.com) | Date: 2006-10-06 22:03:54 UTC
I went to Tyler State Park last Sunday with my LSS (who has a
potential job offer in the area - we would have to move and were
checking the area out), and my dog, Cricket.
We found Tyler State Park, Campfire, and My Heart Belongs In
Texas.
I believe that Barefoot Lucy (My Heart Belongs In Texas) found
the perfect opportunity to get back at me for placing the elderly
gentleman who threw her off the trail when she was looking for my
Lions Park duo.
She must have talked the park maintenance people into blocking
off the route (with low black fencing) that we were suppose to take
and moving the sign that said which way to go... having us start
from the wrong trail. We laborously tried to make the clues fit...
the parts about the creek leave more interpretation when all the
water is dried up, (wondering if she left out some big landmarks to
make things harder), and checked every dead scary looking tree we
could find. We also had multiple interpretations of "a large hole"
(in a rock, between rocks, in the ground, where there's a
waterfall,...).
After backtracking several times and scouring the area's trees,
I was ready to throw in the towel. I knew that there was another
path leading back to where we started, so decided that we should go
that way and check things out... just maybe...
Well, things clicked perfectly when we (finally) headed in that
direction. I was very happy to find that box! After we got to the
end of the path, we realized what had happened, and I thought it was
very funny that it just so happened to be Barefoot Lucy's box. :)
I thought the fence meant that you shouldn't go that way, but it
turned out that it was easy to step over. The correct path starts
behind the cabin GG.
BTW, the sign for the trail points left and right. I believe
that it use to be between the 2 starting points. It is now to the
right of the path that is on the right. I'm wondering now if you
can find any path if you go to the right of the sign...
We were starving when we finished this one (that we didn't
think would take very long - fatal mistake), and had to raid the
park store to hold us over so we could find the other 2 boxes in the
park.
It is a gorgeous park, by the way, has miles of trails, and
would be a great place to do some camping.
