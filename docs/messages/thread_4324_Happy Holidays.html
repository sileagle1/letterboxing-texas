From: Mike Olbrisch (mike-2007@elp.rr.com) | Date: 2011-12-24 09:54:09 UTC-07:00

We are in our second day of SNOW here in El Paso.  The mountains are a beautiful sight. 

 

The best part is that the snow will trickle into the ground slowly, not run off like a rain shower.  The mountains will be a lush green this spring.  This means I will be hiking with my hedge clipper in the spring, trimming back the trails.  With the recent cuts at TPWD - us volunteers will be needed more than ever.

 

In 2012, it you live near a TPWD facility, try to give them a few volunteer hours to support the parks that are so much a part of letterboxing in Texas.

 

The best part of Christmas this year is that for the first time in years the whole family will be here together.  My son James is home, now married, and a grand-baby on the way.  No more combat for him.  The daughter and family is staying in El Paso this year too.    The only sadness I feel is for three friends still in Afghanistan.  Perhaps I am just overly sensitive, done that a number of years myself.  Having been a leader, I feel guilty that "my troopers" are out there in danger while I am not.

 

Anyway....  Merry Christmas to all.  Be safe, warm, and happy.

 

Mike - El Paso.
