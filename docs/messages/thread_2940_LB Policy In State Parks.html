From: Silver Eagle (sileagle@alltel.net) | Date: 2007-07-19 13:31:59 UTC
I agree with both BL and Mike. I think right now each of us could
approach Rangers in nearby parks and try to influence their
interpretation of the rules to be more lenient by displaying good LB
practices. I also think this is not a long term solution for the
reasons BL states. If we ever found the right person or persons to
talk with, I think our only chance for change in our favor would be
to show a distinct difference between letterboxing and geocaching.
Since the current policy was designed for geocaching and probably
would be difficult to change, our best hope might be to create a new
policy just for letterboxing. We would have to show that
letterboxing is less destructive and intrusive than geocaching (no
offense to geocachers) and convince them it would be a win-win
situation to allow LB in their parks. I hate to alienate geocachers
and I'm not even sure how we would convince park officials that
letterboxers deserve better treatment, but I think that is our only
hope. At this point, if we could just get the policy to no longer
require future renewals I would be happy. It would still mean
filling out a permit, but a least it would be a one-time thing. I
would also like to change the wording of the permit so that the
letterboxer is only liable for the contents of the box AT THE TIME IT
IS PLACED. I don't want to be held responsible for something put in
my box at a later date.

So, if we can figure out where to send an email, I would be glad to
send it. I would also be willing to help craft the message with your
input. Not sure if this would work, but it might get our foot in the
door and lead to other meetings.

Silver Eagle

Re: LB Policy In State Parks

From: Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter (janet@redbarnretreat.com) | Date: 2007-07-20 15:59:08 UTC
I went into the TPW website a while back and went to the "contact
us" section. I posted a generic question about the policies toward
letterboxing and received a reply from a lady (also rather generic -
the reply, not the lady). She referenced a policy which basically
said that it was illegal to turn over a rock or step on a twig, and
so, obviously, letterboxing would not be allowed in any state park.
I replied to the message by saying that we were not geocachers,
referencing the LbNA website, and telling the lady a little bit
about us, including quoting our "Leave No Trace" policy. I also
asked if there was someone we could talk to, as a group, to present
our case. I've never received an answer. If and when I do, I'll
let you know.

Boots Tex
