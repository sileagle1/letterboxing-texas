Hopefully some of our Austin, College Station & Houston
area boxers will come forward with suggestions, but I
thought I would give some of mine. Let me start by
saying I won't be in TX during Bandaid's visit, so I'll
just say Howdy, and welcome to TX now. I'm not sure how
much time you will have to letterbox or sightsee while
visiting, but there are a lot of things to do and places
to see in those 3 cities.

In Austin, you can visit the State Capitol and even get a
free tour, with letterboxes nearby. If the heat gets to be
too much, you can always go swimming at Barton Springs in
Zilker Park, with boxes nearby, or at Hamilton Pool, also
with boxes. If you like outdoor activities, there are many
hike/bike trails along Greenbelts in Austin, canoe rentals
at Zilker Park and a great view of town from Mt. Bonnell,
all with boxes. For meals, I'm sure local boxers can tell
you more but some of my favorites include Threadgills,
Conan's Pizza and the Hula Hut. If you have time to explore
surrounding areas, you can head west on Hwy 290 toward
Fredericksburg and visit Pedernales SP, LBJ's home or even
TX wineries, with boxes all along the way. Heading south
on I35 will take you to San Marcos with it's Outlet Malls
and pretty Aquarena Springs, and further to New Braunfels
where you can rent a tube and float on the "horseshoe".
Heading North on I35 will take you to the towns of Roundrock
and Georgetown, both with boxes and swimming holes like
the Blue Hole. Heading east on Hwy 71 will take you to
Bastrop SP, home to our first LB Event and many boxes. Be
sure to continue through the park on a scenic back road to
Buescher SP just a few miles away, and home to the oldest
TX LB still available (Lost Pines). You can continue on
Hwy 21 to College Station.

It is ironic that you will be going to College Station
right after Austin, since they are home to the biggest rival
schools in TX, Longhorns vs. Aggies. There are a few boxes
and more being added by a new letterboxer in Aggieland.
Nearby is one of the most famous TX wineries, Messina Hof.

On the way to Houston, you can detour off Hwy 6 and visit
Washington-On-The-Brazos SP, site of TX Declaration of
Independence and home to several letterboxes. This area
is beautiful in Spring with Bluebonnetts, our State flower,
but is nice even in summer. Continuing south toward Houston,
you might want to detour east to Tomball for many boxes, and
maybe on to Woodlands area for even more. If you come
south to Hwy 290, there are many boxes in the Hockley/
Cypress area. Once in Houston, there are too many boxes
to list, but major concentrations are found in Memorial Park.
If you can make it east of town to San Jacinto SP, you can
visit the site of TX Independence and a few boxes. South
of town you can visit NASA Space Center with many boxes nearby,
and of course Galveston with its beaches and boxes.

I hope you have a great visit and enjoy TX as much as we do!

Silver Eagle
