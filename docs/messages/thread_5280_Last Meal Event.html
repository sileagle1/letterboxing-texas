I wouldn’t count on a 20 deg bag for TALE!  Been there done that and the only thing that saved me was stuffing a small child in the bag to keep me warm!  I got a 0 deg bag and I’ve been comfortable in it down to freezing.  My 20 deg bag only works to about 50 and then I get cold (but I get cold easily).  My husband is ok in the 20 deg bag down to the low 40s.

Thanks for the good advice. If it looks like it's going to be super cold, I'll bring quilts too (since I'll be in my car and not hiking!) I also have a silk mummy liner which gives me another 5 degrees of warmth. This is the bag (Northface Cat's Meow) I'm taking on the Appalachian Trail next Spring, so it will be a great test for those mountains (where it's known to snow in June). I've followed the logs of many of the AT hikers and many of them have the same bag I've got and were perfectly warm at 30 degrees. My Neolite Thermorest sleeping pad also reflects body heat and adds warmth. Fingers crossed! Puddle Splasher
