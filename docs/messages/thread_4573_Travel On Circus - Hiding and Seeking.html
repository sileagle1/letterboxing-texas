Travel On Circus - Hiding and Seeking

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2010-06-12 08:05:43 UTC-05:00
That circus. Well. Not much more to say.
A debate has been raging here in Jacksonvillefor the last few weeks amongst the circus folk who have been staying in my house. It seems the clowns have decided they are thesmartest membersof the circus. Needless to say, every other member of the traveling company disagrees. As evidence of their claim, they frequently mention the fact that clowns go to college. Clown College. The first clown college was formed by a man whose clown name was Blubber, but his real name was Ron Morris. You don't even have to ask - they'll tell you all this.
As we were driving through town early this morning, they had a better than usual view of their surroundings because I couldn't tolerate them in my van anymore so I tied them to the bumper instead. In the course of our meanderings, we happened to pass the rodeo grounds and that's where this debate began. It seems the circus clowns also think they aresmarter than rodeo clowns and they boisterously proclaimed that - as the rodeo clowns were practicing. As you can imagine, the rodeo clowns disagreed and they jumped on their horses and followed us in hot pursuit with their little water pistols drawn.
Aminivan dragging a crowd of circus performers is not as fast as horses and it was inevitable that we would be overtaken, so I made a couple of quick turns and hid quickly. I decided I couldn't risk being seen with this rowdy group any longer, so I untied them and shoved them back in their box and hid them in a place where they would be forced to mind their manners or be revealed.
And now fora bit of twistedhappenstance- as we were trying to hide from the rodeo clowns, the circus clowns noticed where they were. Lon Morris College. Only they (and this is where their claim of intelligence gets weak) kept reading it as Ron Morris College and they were certain we had found ourselves to the original clown college.
Feeling a bit wicked, I hid them in view of the rodeo grounds and on the backside of the college - next to the LMC Recreational Center on Peeples Street. They are hidden in a group of bushes next to the basketball goals to the left of the Rec Center - bushes between the basketball court and the street, under the two bushes furthest from the Rec Center.
Why was this wicked? Because those boisterous clowns really want to go meet Ron Morris and I very nearly couldn't get them back in their box because of their zeal, but when the circus clowns return to the rodeo grounds in just a few minutes, they will have a clear view of those bushes and I think they'll still have it in for the circus clowns. So - do the circus clowns go for it and run for the college? Can they beat the rodeo clowns? Or do they stay hidden and hope the rodeo clowns find themselves a Saturday rodeo and then sneak up to the college?
In any event, they may ALL be in trouble because Lon Morris College security are pretty thorough guys.

As we were driving through town early this morning, they had a better than usual view of their surroundings because I couldn't tolerate them in my van anymore so I tied them to the bumper instead.  In the course of our meanderings, we happened to pass the rodeo grounds and that's where this debate began.  It seems the circus clowns also think they are smarter than rodeo clowns and they boisterously proclaimed that - as the rodeo clowns were practicing.  As you can imagine, the rodeo clowns disagreed and they jumped on their horses and followed us in hot pursuit with their little water pistols drawn.
 
A minivan dragging a crowd of circus performers is not as fast as horses and it was inevitable that we would be overtaken, so I made a couple of quick turns and hid quickly.  I decided I couldn't risk being seen with this rowdy group any longer, so I untied them and shoved them back in their box and hid them in a place where they would be forced to mind their manners or be revealed.
 
And now for a bit of twisted happenstance  - as we were trying to hide from the rodeo clowns, the circus clowns noticed where they were.  Lon Morris College.  Only they (and this is where their claim of intelligence gets weak) kept reading it as Ron Morris College and they were certain we had found ourselves to the original clown college. 
 
Feeling a bit wicked, I hid them in view of the rodeo grounds and on the backside of the college - next to the LMC Recreational Center on Peeples Street.  They are hidden in a group of bushes next to the basketball goals to the left of the Rec Center - bushes between the basketball court and the street, under the two bushes furthest from the Rec Center.
 
Why was this wicked?  Because those boisterous clowns really want to go meet Ron Morris and I very nearly couldn't get them back in their box because of their zeal, but when the circus clowns return to the rodeo grounds in just a few minutes, they will have a clear view of those bushes and I think they'll still have it in for the circus clowns.  So - do the circus clowns go for it and run for the college?  Can they beat the rodeo clowns?  Or do they stay hidden and hope the rodeo clowns find themselves a Saturday rodeo and then sneak up to the college?
 
In any event, they may ALL be in trouble because Lon Morris College security are pretty thorough guys.
