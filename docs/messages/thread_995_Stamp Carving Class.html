From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2005-07-05 15:59:37 UTC
I have browsed your website. Thought it was fabulous and very
interesting.

I just knew that you could teach how to do the logbooks. I am so
thrilled that we have such creativity in our midst. And that you're
willing to share! I believe that we should all help each other.

Words of encouragement to everyone: go see our rabbit house box.
That's our first stamp. And then, maybe the recently planted
Missing Spots? or the What's Blooming? series or some of our other
newer ones. It is amazing how much your carving can improve and how
quickly.

I like to work with a positive image. But if you are just getting
started, creating a negative image can be easier. I think there is
a good stamp carving tutorial on AtlasQuest. I also am now addicted
to PZCut, which you can buy from stampeaz.com--it is a material that
I find easier to work with than the pink stuff or MasterCarve.

Not that I've seen the stamps you mentioned, but I
know what you mean! I'm really glad that I went ahead
and placed some of my earlier stamps even though they
were nothing more than a frustrated attempt to depict
something I saw in my head. My artwork now is much,
much better and yet it still has so much further to
go. Still though, now that I've been at it awhile, I
feel confident that I can get to the next level. I
guess you could get that same feeling without ever
planting your carvings, but I think it definitely
pushed me harder knowing that my early works were in
the field.

This may not be the case for everyone, but for me
placing a hand-carved stamp makes me feel a little
more ownership in the box. A more vested interest.
That's a really good feeling - until your box goes
missing, but that's another story. Still worth it in
my book, and I can say this after learning yesterday
that one of my favorite stamps is very likely gone...

Lucy

From: Eibhlin/Aisling (eibhlinm@gmail.com) | Date: 2005-07-06 07:51:26 UTC-05:00
Hi,

Awww... sorry that another stamp has gone missing, Lucy!

But, like you, I think that hand carving is generally worth it for me.
I won't pretend that I'm a great carver; I'm not. I don't have the
patience that many others do, I guess.

That said, carving can be pretty easy and straightforward, and while
boxes and stamps go missing now & then, I haven't seen handcarved
stamps vanish any more quickly than storebought ones.

Also, there's a hybrid version of stamps that I've seen: Stamps that
people design themselves, but then they have the rubber created
commercially. It can be very inexpensive to do this, and if a stamp
goes missing, you can just order more custom rubber. I've used RSAA
for this:

http://www.rubberstampit.com/custom_artwork (Scroll down to the text
area talking about sheets of custom rubber stamp images.)

Cheerfully,
