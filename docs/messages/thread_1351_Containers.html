*****I've thought the same thing - I only hide in
see-through containers so that anyone coming upon one
of my boxes can see the contents before they pick it
up, especially non-letterboxers who may be a little
freakish about an abandoned container in a hidden
location. I would also be a little leery of picking
anything up that wasn't clear, unless I knew for sure
the placer used a specific type of container and it
matched what I was looking at.

I read a story on one of the lists (probaby the Big
List) about a boxer who hid a box and someone came
across it. Not knowing what it was and being
suspicious, they alerted local authorities who in turn
called in the bomb squad - I have enough odd things
that happen in my life that I could just see it
happening to me and my box!

Lots of folks use ammo boxes though, although I've
never come across one.

****I started writing on mine also after one of my
boxes went missing from a very low risk area. I knew
it had to have been found by folks who were cleaning
the area up, so I felt almost certain they took it for
trash.

I've been trying different approaches to the container
itself, including sanding the sheen off the outside of
it so that it doesn't catch light as easily, or taking
a can of spray adhesive with me to spray the box once
I find a hiding place, then wallowing it around in
local debris so that it blends in. The
adhesive/wallering method works pretty good at
disguising the box - it literally drops into the
background when put in place. It also still leaves it
see-through enough that someone can see into the box
if they happen to find it. I haven't been using that
method long enough to see how it holds up to the
elements. It's also really messy.

Lucy

I've been boxing for over a year now, and it's time to give back. I like letterboxing, in part, because it is an affordable hobby. My husband has gone back to school, and so we are now a 1 income household again, so the cost of letterboxing has been very helpful. That said, I want to try and cut costs as much as possible while planting, yet still make a quality box. I have read that old food containers are not that great because animals are attracted to them. Lock-and-locks are kind of pricey, though. Has anyone used old peanut butter jars? Of course I would run it through the dishwasher a couple of times before placing it. What other containers have you found to be cost-effective?

Thanks so much!

I recently started using plastic peanut butter jars, washing them thoroughly first, and they seem to work well. I have used film canisters for years and they should work, but for some reason logs still occasionally get wet. I also use old pill containers if the neck is not too constricted.
