Post Event Summary

From: Silver Eagle (sileagle@alltel.net) | Date: 2006-03-21 05:32:28 UTC
Despite poor weather and lack of campsites, our third TX LB Event
was a success, mainly because TX Letterboxers are a lot of fun!
About 40 people attended this year, including a mystery guest
(Gryzzled Gryphon) and out-of-state letterboxers (Compass Points &
Happy Days from Oklahoma).

The festivities actually started Friday night, when several boxers
sampled some local wine at the Maydelle Winery, then moved on to
Bertha's for all-you-can-eat catfish. The winery was fun and the
food was superb.

The next morning was cold and rainy, but a roaring fire and furious
stamping in each other's logbooks kept everyone warm and happy.
After eating some chili and other treats it was time to play
Letterbox Bingo. This fun game was organized by Dewberry and she did
a great job. I am still amazed she was able to hide 25 boxes in the
park for us to find. The first several people to Bingo received nice
prizes, also provided by Dewberry. Later that afternoon it was time
to eat again and finally relax with the remaining letterboxers. A
few of us stayed until after 8:00, drinking Barefoot Lucy's homemade
Irish Cream and swapping letterbox tales. This is always my favorite
part of Events, and I hope next year the camping situation will
allow more people to camp the night of the Event so they don't have
to leave so early.

I want to give special thanks to Barefoot Lucy for being our travel
agent, tour guide and troubleshooter. She is a big reason the Event
was a success. I also want to thank Lucy Locket for making the Event
Poster, and Dewberry for organizing the Bingo game. And I especially
want to thank my brother Baby Bear for helping put on another
successful Event. Last but not least, thanks to all of you that
attended for coming and making it fun. I can't wait to do it again
next year!
