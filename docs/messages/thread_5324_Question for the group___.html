From: Mike Olbrisch (mike-2007@elp.rr.com) | Date: 2014-02-10 10:10:37 UTC-07:00

Good morning Vicki.

 

I will probably leave my letterboxes out there, as I have the Geocaches I placed.  With the Geocaches, as I get notified they need maintenance, I archive them and remove them.  I suppose the letterboxes will be the same.  If someone wants to adopt, I give them blanket permission.  Do it.  My stamps were controversial anyway, some didn't like them because they were not hand carved.  I mailed my design to a store and had them make the stamps.  I am an electronics tech, not an artist.  Probably would have gouged my arm out trying to carve that pink stuff anyway.

 

I did have some great times.  Remember the "Search for Hueco Tanks II"???  That was an adventure!  I found Hueco Tanks 1, it was destroyed, so I re-made it, then worked with the park staff to find a place where it would be allowed.  I do not believe many have searched for it since.  But I will leave it.

 

Whatever comes next?  More of the same I guess.  More competitive shooting, more ham radio, and more dog training.  My wife is 1 dog away from her "Breeder of Merit" award.  What was a surprise was that a few nights ago she informed me that of the number of titled dogs you must have to qualify, nearly 75% of them were the ones I trained for obedience.  If I can get a title on this next one, it will push her over the top for her breeders award.

 

This will pretty well sum up the dog stuff.  http://www.vonbonehenge.com/news.html   

 

The gray dog (her name is Gray-Cee) is the next one I am training for a title.  The white one is River, he just earned his CD title.  And remember, River has his own BLOG - which he is way behind in publishing.  About 20 stories are written, just not published yet.

 

http://www.vonbonehenge.com/River/River_Dog-Blog.html

 

Competitive shooting.  Last year I ended up just 2 points short of Master Class in NRA service rifle.  Service Rifle competition is using standard military weapons and ammo.  So for now I remain EXPERT class.  I shoot as well as ever, I could still hold my own against the young bucks serving today.  But physically I can't keep up with them anymore.  I just finished building a new rifle, it is yet untested.  I have always known my skills are the weakest link, so as long as I make good ammo, and the wind doesn't blow hard, I can make it this year.

 

I am still a C-Class in combat pistol.  I don't think I will advance beyond that.  And truly, C-Class is far better than the average shooter anyway, I'm satisfied with that.

 

Ham radio..... just a month ago I hiked 8 miles and climbed 3050 ft with a backpack full of radio gear.  It was a VHF/UHF contest, the idea is to see how many people you can talk to and how far away in the time period.  I think I did well.  If you want to read about it (with photos) go here:  http://www.qrz.com/db/kd5kc/  Scroll to the bottom, look for the link to the Jan 2014 ARRL VHF contest.  BTW - the other links are to my climb of Pikes Peak, and my summer trip to Germany.

 

I am not quitting just letterboxing.  I have already quit Geocaching, and have left a few other Yahoo Groups that I am no longer really participating in.  Cleaning house and simplifying things a bit I guess.  I will still be around a little longer.  Got to mail some stuff to SE.  And I still stay in contact with K0BFL.... that is her ham call.  You know her as Bare Foot Lucy.  She did get a ham license after meeting me.  <grin>.

 

Anyway, after working the weekend, I am off the next 3 days.  Lots to do, and Gray-Cee is screaming her head off for attention right now.  That is an opportunity for training.  <grin>.

 

Wishing you all the best.

 

Mike.
