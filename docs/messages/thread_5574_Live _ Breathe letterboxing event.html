Live & Breathe letterboxing event

From: Christine Borgfeld (christine@borgfeld.net) | Date: 2016-10-27 16:01:26 UTC-05:00

Hey everyone,

 

I’ve talked to a few of you about this, but I want to put it out there for general input.  I’m promoting Texas as the location for the next Live and Breathe letterboxing event in 2018.  If you are not familiar with L&B, it’s an event hosted every 5 years to celebrate the anniversary of letterboxing in North America.  2018 will be the 20th anniversary.  The last event was held in Massachusetts in the summer of 2013, and organized by Choi and local letterboxes in the area.  The event hosted over 200 letterboxers from all over the country for a full weekend.  It was held in a private “summer camp” style facility which included lodging (cabins with bunk beds) and meals for the weekend.  People could also attend as day visitors if they found their own lodging and meals.  The event was packed with more boxes than you could find, with carvers from all over the country, and fun and creative clues.  There were also evening activities and some fun activities for non-boxers and kids.  Previous events were held on the west coast.  While this event was previously hosted in the summer, I propose we hold it in the spring or fall.

 

If you would like to know more about the last event, there is a YouTube video about it here:  https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zjqBF5EB8BM We had a fair number of boxers from Texas attend it, and they agree it was a great experience.  If we host it in Texas, everyone from our letterboxing community will be able to attend and meet letterboxers from around the country!

 

In the past, this event was mostly organized by a group of about a dozen people.  They planned all of the boxes, made all the decorations, organized the activities etc, and the hundreds of attendees arrived and enjoyed it all.  I don’t think that model will work for Texas, and that’s not how we normally hold events.  We are too spread out to have a core group do everything.  In our events, everyone does their own part, and it all comes together beautifully.  We bring boxes to plant, bring food, raffle prizes, make table top boxes, organize dinners, night boxing series – and we set up our events to support this (I’ve never seen a copier for clues at an event outside Texas!).   I think we have the people and experience to organize L&B in the same spirit if we set up good communication and have some mini- meetings around the state to plan ahead and ask for what we need.  Then you can contribute a little or a lot, in whatever areas you are interested in.    

  

At this point several people have offered to help, and we are looking for facilities capable of hosting the event.  We have one site which is very nice, but it’s expensive.  The main requirement is trails.  There are many places with great facilities but very few with trails.  Ideally we want 15+ miles of trails of varying length, lots of intersections, and spread out in different directions to keep people from bunching up. 

 

Please take a look at the previous event and give it some thought and share your ideas…. I’ll share some ideas later on how we might organize things, and the volunteer help we need.   

 

FYI…so far I have talked individually to Silver Eagle, Baby Bear, Bell Motel, NLW, Mosaic Butterfly, PI Joe, Wry Me and a few others last weekend.  I’m also talking to Choi, and he has offered to answer any questions about the last event.

For anyone who wants more info on the previous event, Choi has renewed the old website for 30 days.  This has the event schedule, registration info, and lots of information on the location including trail maps.  It also has a picture of their event patch.  You can find it here:

http://weliveandbreathexv.wikispaces.com/

 

As Silver Eagle said, this will take more planning and upfront work than our Texas events.  For example, we will need to have most of the boxes planted in advance with a clue book printed.  Some of the things we will need help with are visiting facilities and planning letterbox series, collecting boxes/raffle prizes donated from around the country, artwork for a patch/path tag, decorations, communication online, photos and videos during the event, and of course making letterboxes!  You don’t need any experience with previous events, and you can be new to letterboxing or a long time boxer.
