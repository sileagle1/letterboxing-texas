From: Kelly A. Crespin (kcrespin@satx.rr.com) | Date: 2007-03-01 11:18:23 UTC-06:00

Hi, all.  I’m new to the letterboxingtexas group and I wanted to introduce myself.  My name is Kelly and I live in the San Antonio area.  I first tried letterboxing 2 years ago with my kids and we loved it but because there are so few boxes in San Antonio we just haven’t done much more with it.

 

I am also a Girl Scout leader, though, and my cadettes (ages 11-14) are working on an Outdoor Survival badge and I thought letterboxing might fit into that a little.  I took 7 girls letterboxing yesterday at McAllister Park and they had a GREAT time!  We found 5 boxes plus another in Walker Ranch Park later in the day.  The girls are now SO excited and want to carve their own stamps and hide their own boxes.  J

 

Can someone give me any advice on the hiding of the boxes?  I can handle the carving part on my own.  J  But for the box hiding, are there areas where people would like to see more boxes hidden in the San Antonio area?  And once we hide the boxes, where do I go to post the clues and such for people to find? 

 

Thanks so much!

 

Kelly

Re: new to the group

From: ThimbleCat (inklaura@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2007-03-03 00:01:10 UTC
Welcome Kelly. (Do you have a trail name yet?)
I'm a Girl Scout leader too. Since you have older girls, they can
earn the Hi-Tech Hide and Seek Interest Project by letterboxing
(and/or Geo-caching). I'll email you off list the handout I use to
train GS leaders to take their troops boxing. My 3rd graders LOVE it
and we are trying very hard to get more boxes planted at GS camps in
our council.

For boxes planted in public places, you can post clues on
atlasquest.com and/or letterboxing north america (letterboxing.org).

ThimbleCat
