Hi everyone,
I took a trip to Brazos Bend today. Seems that I just missed Hawk by
one day and the Dewberry group by maybe only a few minutes. I
planted 2 boxes for the event and I confirmed that Cattail Marsh is
for sure missing. I did not have a stamp carved to replace it so it

serve. This week is spring break for lots of folks too.

Cub Scouts and letterboxing (was Re: [letterboxingtexas] Brazos Bend)

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-12-03 06:25:35 UTC-08:00
Semalee's post reminded me that I am just finishing
something up with my Webelos (4th graders) that has
turned out to be really cool.

One of the Webelos requirements is to explore various
forms of Art by creating art in different genre. I
decided to let them carve stamps - xacto knives and
all! We've been working on them for about three
weeks, and I've even had some boys from the older
Webelos den come in to see what we were doing and beg
to make their own stamps, so I've been staying late
with them to help them out. It has been a lot of fun
teaching them to carve and they've done surprisingly
well at handling the xactos and gouges. While they've
been carving their own very rough stamps, I copied and
carved our den emblem, dragons, and it has turned out
great!

Of course, this is the last piece of the puzzle in
preparation for doing our scout training series, which
is about to happen. They will be working with me on
that, and I'm so excited. They may even carve some of
the stamps for it. Hopefully in January we will begin
placing the series in Jim Hogg Historical Site in
Rusk. It will definitely be in place by March 18th!

Lucy

Brazos Bend

From: Silver Eagle (sileagle@windstream.net) | Date: 2008-12-17 20:45:29 UTC
Even though today was a little dreary, I spent an enjoyable few hours
at Brazos Bend SP and had the place almost all to myself. I found my
Brazos Bend letterbox alive and dry, thanks to the new container and
log provided by Boots & LSQ almost 2 years ago. This was my second
box ever placed and my first hand-carved stamp, so I'm glad it is
still there. I replaced the container with one that would fit the
crevice better and hopefully remain out of sight.

I also found Wish Upon A Star letterbox, the first one placed by
SoccerChik. It was a good first effort and had several visits,
including some park volunteers that found the box while clearing
brush and left notes in the log. Glad to see they did not confiscate
it or mistake it for trash.

Though it was too cold for alligators to be out, the birds were
plentiful and it was very peaceful. As I ambled along I couldn't
help but think of the second TX LB Event held here in 2005. I
chuckled to myself as I thought of BOXDN and Wanda getting caught by
Rangers while they looked for letterboxes at midnight. As I looked
with regret at the many trees blown down by Ike, I remembered the big
storm we had the night of that Event. All in all, it was a fun day!

Silver Eagle
