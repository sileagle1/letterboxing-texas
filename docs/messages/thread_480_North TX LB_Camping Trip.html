North TX LB/Camping Trip

From: Silver Eagle (sileagle@alltel.net) | Date: 2004-12-05 18:26:33 UTC

Earlier this week, Baby Bear and I realized we had 2 1/2
days of available time away from our non-letterboxing families,
so we decided to go on a power letterboxing trip to North Texas
and do a little camping. I don't necessarily recommend doing
it this way, but if you don't have much time and want to
cover a lot of territory, a trip like this is the only option.
We left before dawn on Thursday and headed up toward Waco area,
boxing in the dark on the way. We had a momentary setback
when we got to Mother Neff SP only to find it flooded & closed,
but we kept heading north, boxing at several parks on the way
to Fort Worth. We just made it to the Fort Worth Nature Center &
Wildlife Refuge before it closed, so were able to place new boxes
in a great park. We then did some after-dark boxing in the
downtown area, which made it easy to park and be discreet, but
a little difficult to find landmarks. After a full day, we
headed for Cedar Hill SP on the outskirts of Dallas to camp
and get ready for the next full day in Dallas. We got up before
dawn to find ice on our tent, so we hid two boxes in the park to
warm up before leaving to find as many boxes in Dallas area that
we could. We managed to find most of the boxes (except the two
in downtown), though the last few were in the dark again, before
heading south to camp at Fairfield Lake SP. We barely made it
before they closed the gate at 10:00 and I was beat. After
setting up the tent I went straight to bed, but Baby Bear had
realized he did not have a box to place in that park so he stayed
up carving a stamp to place the next morning. When I woke up
(again before dawn), I found a masterpiece stamp of our trip
waiting my inspection. What a trooper! After finding
Barefoot Lucy's box and placing our own it was time to head for
home, boxing the Huntsville/Montgomery/Magnolia area on the way.
The final tally was over 50 finds, 4 new placed for me and 6 new
placed for Baby Bear, and we both replaced several of our old
boxes that had gone missing. We enjoyed all the boxes and though
we did not get to spend much time in the places they were located,
we enjoyed them, too. If nothing else, we now have places we
want to go back to and spend more time with our families. Thanks
to all who placed boxes for giving us the incentive to do this
trip. We were a little leary of hunters in the parks and being
mistaken for prowlers at night, but it worked out great!

Silver Eagle
