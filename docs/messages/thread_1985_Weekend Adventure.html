Weekend Adventure

From: raininknd42 (raininkatzndogs@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2006-07-13 01:18:58 UTC
Hi everyone, Texas Gal here. Thought I would share my letterboxing
experience with you. Crazy bout Dragons and I went to Dallas last
weekend to celebrate her 14th birthday with her adult brother. We
had plans to hit the Cheesecake Factory, the Dallas World Aquarium
and the Dallas Zoo. I had hopes of getting some boxing in as well.

We arrived early afternoon on Friday and after discussing plans with
both kids, we went to LBNA to see what options were available. I had
previously looked but it sure is nicer to have someone that is
familiar with the area be able to look at the directions and plot out
the best course of action. I also had to explain the whole
letterboxing activity to my son. Since his printer was on the fritz
it was off to the closest library to print out clues. Since we
planned on trying for several boxes all weekend long, we printed all
of them out at that time. It was time to start hunting!!!

Our first box was the "flowers, leaves. birds, & trees" at the Dallas
Botanical Gardens. This was a great box and we really enjoyed the
part of the Gardens that we visited. Since we were on a mission the
Gardens would have to be seen at a later date.

Our next box was the "Lady in the Lake" at White Rock Lake. Really
nice lake in the middle of a big city. Great find. And had a lock &
lock box so I figured we would be able to find this type of box in
the area. At this point the adult son decided that letterboxing was
too cool and off to Hobby Lobby to get carving supplies and a log
book.

After the birthday celebration at the Cheesecake Factory it was back
to the house to start carving. My son decided his trail name would
be "Flaming Bamboo" and he carved his signature stamp and since we
were going to the Aquarium the next day, I decided to carve a
seahorse and place a box (clues to follow).

Saturday morning I was eager to get started before it got too warm.
I did have to prod a little but not near as much as normal to get the
kids going. It was decided we would find two more boxes before going
to the aquarium. We set off to find "Mustang Mania" and "Bach's
Box". Loved the clues for both of these boxes and it gave us a
chance to discuss college options to a up and coming high school
freshman.

Next we were off to the Aquarium. My son took us to the Dallas
Aquarium at Fair Park which is not the same thing as the Dallas World
Aquarium but since we were there anyway we went ahead and went
through the exhibits. It was pointed out to me that this aquarium
has the "seahorse rodeo" so we decided to plant my "seahorse" box at
this site. Clues to follow shortly.

Next we headed to the Dallas World Aquarium and I have to say that it
is an awesome place to visit but very busy and therefore not a good
place to plant a box, so planting it at the other location proved to
be wise. Someone was guiding us.

Sunday Morning found us on the road after two more boxes. "A league
of their own #4" and "Duncanville Dragonfly" . "A league of their
own" is a series and a second trip must be planned to find the rest
of this series. I made my son to promise to wait for us but I have a
feeling he may not, he has been bitten bad by the "box bug". Next we
went to "Duncanville Dragonfly" were we found not only the dragonfly
but a hitch hiker, "Sellin Mellon". I felt quite proud that we had
done 6 boxes in 3 days with a hitch hiker. Not a personal best or
anywhere near most of you other boxers but not bad considering I was
dragging along a teenager and an adult son. We then went to the
Dallas Zoo and that night crashed.

The "Sellin Mellon" hitch hiker will be placed by this Sunday, and
who knows what other boxes we will find in the process.

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-07-12 21:27:03 UTC-05:00
Hi Texas Gal!
 
That sounds like a GREAT trip!  I've only made one trip into Dallas - last Thanksgiving with my daughter who was at that time recovering from a car accident - but it certainly made me want to go back!  Unfortunately, I haven't been able to get back to box, even with the same daughter now living in Denton.  Maybe I'll make that a Thanksgiving tradition...
 
I never seem to be able to get nearly as many boxes as I want to get, or as many as everyone else seems to be able to get.  I know I'm bad about getting sidetracked, and for the longest time I felt pressured to knock some boxes out on a boxing weekend, but I finally realized that what I get is all I'm gonna get, which is fine if I enjoy my time out.  Much better to go for quality of experience rather than quantity, although sometimes quantity is just fun.
 
I'm still trying to figure out how to make a livelihood of boxing so I can quit my infernal job that gets in the way by taking up so much of my time!
 
Lucy
