From: celticlions (celticlions@comcast.net) | Date: 2004-10-12 18:07:08 UTC-05:00
Unfortunately, it may be true that there are only 3 active letterboxes
within San Antonio city limits - there was only 1 active box in Dallas when
we started.
You're doing everything right, though - someone on this Texas list may be
able to help you as far as the status of "missing boxes". Have you looked at
SeaDove's Atlas?
http://birdsofafeather.netfirms.com/cgi-bin/lbatlas.cgi

I love that site to help plan trips around Texas, because you can search by
city or county and see clusters of boxes to judge proximity.

Good Luck!

From: monkeytoes108 (cruschhaupt@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-10-13 10:58:29 UTC

*whispers* If you plant them, they will come.

The people I went to the coast with last weekend are from San
Antonio and would probably jump at any boxes you would plant. We go
up there from time to time and would hit them as well.

For those missing boxes, I would contact the placer to get more
details. Someone could report a box as missing when they just
overlooked it... I know I've done that. If the placer hasn't
confirmed that it is not there, I think it would still be worth a
look. If you don't find it, no surprises, but if you do, then that
would make it all the more fun.

-MonkeyToes

From: Silver Eagle (sileagle@alltel.net) | Date: 2004-10-13 12:25:35 UTC

Unfortunately, many of the boxes in San Antonio have
been reported missing, but I would contact the placer
to find out current status and ask if they plan to
replace them. However, you are lucky to live so
close to Austin, which has many boxes in town and
in the surrounding area. Also, there are several
boxes just to the south of you. Part of the allure
of letterboxing is getting to see places you might
not have heard of before and never would have gone to.
Just consider it a good excuse to take a day trip to
enjoy the wonders of Texas!

Silver Eagle
