Rusk Redoux - 10 days away!

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-01-10 19:39:46 UTC-06:00
Just wanted to remind everyone that the Rusk Redoux is only ten days away!
 
I'm working on the event stamp and I hope to get to finished checking boxes this weekend.  We do have some missing boxes which hopefully will either be replaced or noted as missing by event time.
 
I'll be including personal travelers in the clue book, but the best way to knock these out is to work on solving them in advance.  Don't have a personal traveler?  Think about making yourself one!  Also, if you have questions about the PT clues, most owners are happy to give hints or offer suggestions, so don't be shy about getting in touch with them to solve them. 
 
By popular demand, we'll do a carving tutorial and we should have several good carvers there.
Thought it might be kind of fun to play a round of "What's in YOUR backpack?"  So bring along your letterboxing bag packed to be on the go, and let's see what cool stuff everyone carries.  (HINT:  What sort of really creative thing can you think of that might be good for letterboxing?  Stick it in your pack and see if you can stump everyone else about what it would be used for!)
 
Don't forget that this is potluck, so bring your favorite dish.  We'll have some small amount of power (don't know how many amps we'll have, but I believe we had three or four crockpots hooked up last year).  For myself, I plan on cooking a cobbler in a dutch oven or something else along the same lines.
 
So tell me what you WANT to do at this gathering, and we'll see about making it happen!

--
Barefoot Lucy

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-01-11 22:31:11 UTC-06:00
hehe...I thought so, although I couldn't get an exact mileage on it.
 
And speaking of, we watch the passover schedule pretty closely, so last weekend the ISS was passing over EAST Texas at a good angle in a dark area of our sky.  Fearless Fox, Lightning, and I decided at the last minute to head out to a nearby cemetery that we know to be good for sky watching in that direction.  We got there about two minutes after its ascent, jumped out of the car and were fortunate enough to spot the ISS right off the bat with bare eyes.  We had barely locked in on it when a highway patrolman drove by and then turned around with his lights on and parked by us.  "Great!" I thought, "this guy is going to keep us occupied for the next three minutes with his stinking lights on so we can't see it."  We walked over to his window and told him what we were doing.  He switched off his lights and jumped out of his car and turned to see where we were looking.  It blew him away to watch it fly over.  He watched with us until it got close to the horizon, then he got a call and had to go.  I hope it made his day to get a moment to watch something awe-inspiring instead of having to deal with the things he normally had to deal with!  And of course, I have to wonder what passers-by thought...
