TALE 11 Recap

From: (sileagle1@yahoo.com) | Date: 2014-03-03 09:51:49 UTC-08:00

Well, another TALE has come and gone, and except for one bad incident I think it was one of the best yet.  We had visitors from New Mexico, Rhode Island, Wisconsin and all over Texas helping make it so.  The theme this year was Naughty or Nice, and y'all really embraced it with very creative boxes, especially the naughty aspect.  I'm not sure what this says about our group other than we like to have fun!

Here are some of my observations put into naughty or nice categories:
NICE:  weather on Saturday; finally meeting Wisconsin Hiker & Martini Man; seeing old friends again; great boxes; great Lodge; great park with lots of trails
NAUGHTY:  weather on Sunday; snake biting Martini Man (who now has the motto "Shaken, not deterred"); flat tire for Baby Bear & Silver Eagle; Frost Maiden stamps (though some thought they were nice)

I have posted pictures from Last Meal and TALE 11 in the Photos section of this group, so feel free to add more.  Thanks to everyone for making this event worth having!

Silver Eagle

From: Kotlarek (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2014-03-03 22:56:54 UTC-06:00

We would like to join in on thanking the hosts of a great event! The park was very nice with great trails, a pretty lake and a very nice lodge. We were also impressed by the MANY boxes planted, by both our hosts and several other TX boxers. Many EXCELLENT carvings!

We enjoyed meeting several of the TX boxers at the BBQ restaurant Friday night and were very happy to finally meet Silver Eagle and Baby Bear in person. We've enjoyed finding their boxes and appreciated SE's travel tips. We also enjoyed putting faces to several other names I have seen on the Yahoo message board over the past years.

The only one we WEREN'T happy to meet was the "Uninvited Guest" that insisted on making the acquaintance of Martini Man. After spending 20 hours at the hospital and receiving a tetanus shot, anti-nausea medication, morphine (for pain), and several rounds of antibiotics, he was released Sunday afternoon. We took it easy the rest of the day, especially since the weather was pretty crappy.

We were VERY sorry to miss the evening activities and the additional socializing on Saturday.

This morning we headed back to the park. Martini Man's hand and arm are still swollen, so I'm doing the driving. We had to make a stop at the Dollar Tree store to buy some gloves - we never expected it to be THIS cold in TX! We put on all layers possible since we didn't have our normal WI winter gear with us. But it was good enough to allow us to spend 9+ hours at the park today. We weren't able to find all of them, but did manage to find another 41 boxes.

Thanks to everyone that made us feel so welcome - we hope we can make it again next year!

Wisconsin Hiker & Martini Man
