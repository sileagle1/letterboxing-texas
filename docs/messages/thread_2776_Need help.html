From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-05-18 23:49:27 UTC-05:00
Will you be using the same stamp on the two you are doing maintenance and repair on?  It sounds like it.
 
If you are using the same stamp and only moving the box slightly, I would stick with the same box name and just modify the clues.  HOWEVER, I would make a note in the clues somewhere as to the date the box was moved so that later you can track the history of your box. 
 
Think of it in terms of this story.  If you planted the same stamp in a different location, the clues would be different but the stamp wouldn't.  Our heroine, the spry young letterboxer found the box shortly after it was originally place, before it was moved.  A few months later, she then goes to find the box with a different name in a different place and she gets the SAME stamp image.  It made perfect sense to her at the time, so she saw no reason to note an explanation for the duplicate entries. 
 
Many boxes and many years later, she gets old and gray and she basks in the attention of her great-great-grandkids, who are sitting at her knee saying "Tell us about how letterboxing was when YOU were young, Grandmother!".  She cracks open her logbook and proceeds to show her stamp images to them.  With innocent eyes, her great-great-granddaughter peers up at her and asks, "But Great-great-grandma, why are these two the same?"  Her old eyes may be feeble, but there is no denying that those two images in the logbook are identical.  Different color ink maybe, but yes, they ARE the same.  As she struggles to remember why such a thing would happen, she finally decides she must have become senile at a much younger age than previously thought, because in her silly state she had gone and found the same box twice, labeled it with two different box names and counted it twice. 
 
Sadly, she had struggled into her old age to hit F100,000 and refused to give up letterboxing until she reached that landmark.  But her logbook must speak the truth - she MUST have found one twice, and now, when her legs have failed her, her eyesight is dimming, and her wits are often absent, and she CAN no longer letterbox, and she has to face the sad reality that she never really hit that mark, but stands at F99,999.  Broken-hearted, she must live the rest of her days pondering over that lost dream of 100,000.  The irony of it is that she lived out her final days believing a lie, when in truth she HAD reached 100,000, but she couldn't remember the true reason for duplicate entries in her logbook.  And we all know - the logbook never lies!
 
Please - don't make that poor old lady suffer.  If you are using the same stamp and only slightly changing the location, call it an adjustment and keep everything else the same.  If you don't want to use the same stamp, but you want to leave a box there but not in exactly the same place, you can call that a new box and you can take the old stamp and launch it as a hitchhiker or a postal or something.
 
Think of those of us who will eventually reach senility and all we will have to show for our lives are our volumes of logbooks!

Well said, BL, especially since at least one certain letterboxer with
silver in his feathers is probably already there.

When my early boxes went missing, I used to recarve the same stamp and
replace the box in a better location within the same park. I called
it the same name and did not count it as a new plant, but changed the
clues appropriately and noted the replace date. After recarving many
stamps that had already been found by quite a few letterboxers, I
decided to start carving different stamps and renaming the box,
usually by adding a 2 on the end (e.g. Corpus Porpoise 2). I
considered these to be new boxes so letterboxers who had already
found the original could find a new one. The theme is usually the
same, especially if it was specific to the location, but the image is
a little different. I then retire the old box but don't delete the
clues, just for history's sake.
