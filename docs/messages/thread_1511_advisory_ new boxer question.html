Containers:
For containers, love, love, love the Locknlock style boxes. You can
get them at Target online or at a Korean market. At the Korean
markets they have names like Sammy Boy or Xeonic, and cost around
$1.20 each. There are surprising variety of containers out there.
Another source I like is American Science & Surplus
sciplus.com - you can never tell what they're going to have, but for
micro containers, I've had a lot of luck finding cool things.

Stampcarving material
texasart.com
stampeaz.com
dickblick.com
This is a debate that rages on. Some people like Mastercarve. I moan
the demise of the white PZCut (available from Stampeaz), because the
new orange stuff is stiffer. However, I prefer either the pink stuff
or the orange pzcut to Mastercarve, because I find Mastercarve too
easy to cut.

For journals
I like unlined journals. I sometimes buy the museum ones from Texas
Art--they're a nice size and are $3 each. Sometimes, you can find
nice small unlined ones at Target or Walmart, but it takes some hunting.

Hope this helps!

Dewberry

For carving stamps, I like the Pink Stuff which I get at Michaels or at art stores (the Asel art supply over by the Kimbell on 7th St. (Ft. Worth) has it and also more variety of carving tools than most Michaels).  For logbooks, I've tried lots of things, but now I like making my own if I have time to cut all the paper and poke holes with an awl and cut out a leather or heavy paper or cardboard and cover it for the cover.  Otherwise, I've found small journals at Target and Container Store and Office Depot, but they don't always have blank paper.  For containers, I've used Glad boxes in different sizes and covered them with camo tape which I found at Walmart.  I like those locknlock boxes when I find them, though, because they're easy to open and also to be sure they're really closed when you're replacing it, but I've also worried about what happens if they snap off, then what?  I've saved several smaller metal boxes and film canisters and pill bottles but haven't used any yet - to cover with camo tape and make microboxes.  Then I guess I'd use a scroll-type log, but the trick is to make it small enough to get in and out of the microbox easily so it will survive!
