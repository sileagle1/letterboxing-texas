If anyone's planning an Austin letterboxing trip, you might be interested in my Lady Bird's Wildflower box, which was created for the Dog Days at Double Lake event last weekend at Coldspring but which has now found a home near the Lady Bird Johnson Wildflower Center in Austin, where it belongs.  
When I've recovered from our adventures, I'll report on the four days that Puddle Splasher and I spent on the road (our Spring 08 Topdown Excellent Adventure -- topdown refers only to the convertible top, of course!) to Coldspring, then on to Bastrop and to Austin before returning to the DFW area, all of which was really perfect -- until I Iost my glasses somewhere along the trails at the LBWC in Austin and we had to spend 3 hours retracing our steps trying unsuccessfully to find them, which sort of put a damper on the trip home.  Up to that point, though, it was a great trip, nice topdown weather most of the time, great hiking trails in the woods with blooming dogwoods and wildflowers everywhere, and lots of great letterboxes along the way.  The event at Coldspring that was our excuse for the trip was great, thanks to Boots Tex, Lone Star Quilter and Dewberry who planned it all, the very nice little park where it was held, and the letterboxers who showed up for the day.  I left two boxes there where they are happily planted along the trails in very appropriate locations: Dogwoods and Little Rabbit Fufu (and as we were leaving the park on Sunday evening, I spotted a huge rabbit who must be Little Rabbit Fufu's big tough older brother, Big Rabbit Kung Fu.  There are some really big rabbits in that park!).  We also saw deer, armadillo, and lots of birds.  Great time. 
I am curious, though, about whether there might be an Austin Curse on Letterboxing, especially after reading Black Widow's recent posting and considering my last letterboxing trip in Austin when I bonked my head on a tree limb in Zilker Park and bled all over the clues, ending that day's letterboxing adventures.  Maybe the answer is, when letterboxing in Austin, Be careful!  Don't cross the guards in shacks, watch out for protruding tree limbs, and hold on to your glasses!  There are some really good boxes to be found there, though!  And the LBWC is beautiful this time of year.
