From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-08-17 14:18:39 UTC-07:00
Don't be discouraged - and DO try again!

The more boxes you find, the more you'll notice what
makes a good hiding place, but the more boxes you
plant, the more you will understand what it takes to
get a box into a good hiding place. Many of us lost
our first time boxes, and it isn't much fun, but you
just have to accept it as part of learning the game.

I would suggest that you not put an intricate primo
stamp in a first box - put something simple, so that
if you lose it you can either recarve it or you can
just let it go and not worry about it.

Our very first box went missing at some point and we
didn't replace it until last weekend. I had taken an
image of the original when we placed it, so I had
something to go by as I recarved it. We had to really
laugh, because back then we really weren't getting the
idea behind positive and negative space on a stamp and
the first one was awful! I'm almost glad it went
missing! I did realize that I hadn't accounted for
people coming along and cleaning the area, which I'm
sure is what happened to the box. I rehid it in the
same place temporarily, but will be moving it -
probably this weekend to a safer spot.

Everyone worries about learning to carve, but equally
important is learning to hide. Your first carvings
won't be your best, and your first hides won't be
great either. Accept it and keep on trying!

Lucy
