In the Heat

From: Frances Stinnett (francesstinnett@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-08-05 08:29:35 UTC-07:00
We went out letterboxing on Sunday when it was 104 degrees and picked up 3 of jb's boxes in Sulphur Springs that are drive bys and it wasn't too bad.  It was hot enough that we forgot about the fourth one in our eagerness for something cold to drink though!  Our problem very soon will be that we will run out of boxes in our area and have to drive at least 45' to an hour away, which we will do.  I hope to interest more folks in the Commerce area in boxing so we can have more closer to home.
 
In scouting out a spot for a plant here in Commerce yesterday, I found a very tiny Tupperware geocache in the crotch of a tree.  It contained only a log which had not been signed yet.  The funny thing is that in the past we tried geocaching and struck out both times.  Now, when NOT looking, I've found 2 of them!
 
Fran  (Celtic Knotheads)

Re: [letterboxingtexas] In the Heat

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-08-06 17:42:30 UTC-05:00
Hi Frances,
 
I was thinking about your dilemma of not enough boxes and I want to make sure you are checking both major websites - Atlasquest and letterboxing.org.  I'm looking at it and it looks like there is a lot of overlap, but there are also a few that are one site and not the other.  That may help you have a few more options!  One thing though - Catpawz has been out of pocket for almost two years now.  If you go looking for their boxes, realize they may or may not be there.  If you try to find them, would you please make sure to let me know the status (either on this list or privately)?  I'm trying to keep track of theirs and make sure they get archived if they are missing...  Catpawz is a great pair of ladies and they wouldn't want to think folks are hunting for their boxes needlessly.
 
Keep the faith though - when I first got started, there were about seven boxes within an hour and a half of me!  One suggestion to help draw boxers in closer - plant your own boxes on the outer edge of where you are able to care for them.  That will draw other boxers in a little closer to find yours, and then maybe they'll leave some of their own.  That's what worked for me!
--
Barefoot Lucy
"It's not about footwear, it's about philosophy"
