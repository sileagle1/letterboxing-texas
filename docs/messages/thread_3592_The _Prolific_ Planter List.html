The "Prolific" Planter List

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-05-30 22:30:02 UTC-05:00
As promised, here's the list of prolific planters, per LBNA.  The information here is a casual collection of data - at best.  One of the biggest problems with the information is that LBNA has not always had an archive feature, so that many of the early boxers deleted boxes as they became inactive instead of sending them to the basement.  What this means is the earliest planters - the ones most likely to have the highest number of boxes, are probably grossly understated because they deleted many boxes before they could archive them.  Unfortunately, many of those boxes can't be found anywhere, except possibly on the planter's personal website, but more likely on his or her personal computer, if that.  Isn't it a shame to lose records of those early lost boxes? 
 
With that said, the top 10 placers in terms of quantity, per current LBNA records are, (with a guess at where they are from):
 
Scout, 329 boxes, North Carolina
Baby Bear, 297 boxes, Texas
Rubaduc, 294 boxes, Connecticut 
Flutterby Flew By, 256 boxes, Connecticut
The Dragon, 262 boxes, Wisconsin
Safari Man, 249 boxes, Ohio
Silver Eagle, 240 boxes, Texas
Funhog, 224 boxes, Oregon
A-Bear (and J-Bear), 204 boxes, Idaho
Wanda and Pete, 203 boxes, Rhode Island
 
and a close 11th,
J B Kokopelli, 200 boxes, Texas

Notable names who have been around a long time and probably have many more boxes than indicated on the database,
 
DrewFamily (by Jay's count, somewhere close to 400, and being member #7, probably deleted boxes they doesn't even remember!)
Don and Gwen
Music Woman
Chuck & Molly/Amy
Wisconsin Hiker
Scarab
Spring Chick
Silent Doug
Doublesaj & Old Blue
 
I'm absolutely positive that in naming those few names, I've shot myself in the foot by leaving someone really cool out.
