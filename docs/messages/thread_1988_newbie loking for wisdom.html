From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-07-13 06:44:37 UTC-05:00
Most clues for Texas boxes are on LBNA - www.letterboxing.org, but you should be very familiar with www.atlasquest.com also.  It may have a few boxes not listed on LBNA, and it has features LBNA doesn't have.  I can't imagine using one site without the other. 
 
Also, Sea Dove of Birds of a Feather has created a great map-based Texas letterbox program that shows us graphically where boxes are - the catch is that placers have to give him permission to put their boxes on his map, so all boxes may not be there (but last time I checked, he had about 90% of them).  The address is www.birdsofafeather.netfirms.com . 

Unfortunately, you'll find there aren't a lot of boxes in your particular area - yet.  However, you aren't that far from Dallas. Also, you aren't far from us down in Cherokee County, where there are quite a few boxes also, and if you get down this way, email me directly and maybe we can meet and exchange!
 
Lucy
