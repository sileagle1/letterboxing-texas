From: Boots Tex (redbarn@eastex.net) | Date: 2010-11-10 14:13:10 UTC
Silver Eagle, that's an amazing tree and the letterbox placement sounds really intriguing. I've never seen the redwoods in person, but that's a good reason to go! I guess the most unique place we've found a letterbox was in Italy, the Tuscany area. It was placed in the ruins of a centuries-old castle. It was really pretty scary. You had to make your way to the top of a mountain, climb up (no stairs) into the castle and make your way through rubble to a certain room, negotiating around a bottomless hole in the floor, and the box was in a niche in the stone wall. We came out of the castle right at sunset and were rewarded with a spectacular view of a small village in the valley with the setting sun as a backdrop. We posted some pictures here under "Italian Letterboxes". I also placed a box in Pompeii, but it was never found.

Boots Tex and Lone Star Quilter
