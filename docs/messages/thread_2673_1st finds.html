From: (blhjrh@aol.com) | Date: 2007-03-26 10:44:47 UTC-04:00
My first letterboxes were found in state forests in CT and Maine while visiting my sister in New England before she moved back home to Texas,  and they included a piggyback, a HH, and a bonus box, so I was hooked! 
 
As soon as I got home and checked the website, I discovered a few (very few) boxes in Dallas and Ft. Worth and Arlington, all placed by Kantexan or Celtic Lions.  My first Texas boxes were Kantexan's Mondrian Series and Slumbering Family Tree, and Celtic Lions' Shawnee Trail and Wacky Tacky in Texas Lenin Statue - all still among my favorites. 
 
Those first boxes all set a pretty high standard in my mind for letterboxing, too:  beautifully scenic or interesting and unusual locations, great stamps, cleverly concealed boxes, and something unique about each one (like Stillwater's artificial rock at the original Shawnee Trail, or the recreation of a Mondrian print with the 5 Mondrian stamps).  I've got a lot of Silver Eagle and Baby Bear stamps, too, though, and amazingly to me, when I travel, no matter how far I go or where, among the letterboxes I find there always seem to be at least one by Silver Eagle/Baby Bear!  So they haven't only "seeded" Texas for the rest of us, they've got letterboxes out there EVERYWHERE!
