From: Julie L (txjulie@gmail.com) | Date: 2005-07-28 21:54:29 UTC
I just wanted to introduce my family. We obviously have a serious
identity crisis. We are from Ohio. We live in TX (Flower Mound).
Our blood bleeds green and gold from Sept to Jan.

So far, we just stamp in as a team. Myself and my 2 sons. Soon, we
will get my DH involved as I have already road tripped spur of the
moment to Oklahoma to box. And I have big plans to hit all the
other surrounding border states, too.

We have been in the Dallas area just two years this month and I was
always threatening. Today I am going to Oklahoma. And so we did.

I am a rubber stamper, card crafter, scrapbooker, so somewhere in my
internet searches for ideas I stumbled on this fabulous hobby. We
found our first 5 boxes and our first HH on July 11.

Be Happy / Tiger Trail- alive and well
Gerault Park- alive and well
Flower Mound Mini- alive and well

I have to say these were some of my favorite clues. SO much
personal history. I could envision these kids playing there and
heard their laughter.

In the Creek: Lauren's Turtle- alive and well
Meghans Bird- alive and well (Watch for falling black walnuts-
big as softballs LOL)


On 7/14 we found
Polly's Paws in Lewisville- alive and well. A fabulous park I did
not know existed even with all of my baseball field travels these
past two years.

Then on 7/15- I decided to go to OK. Well we had to stop in Denton
to try for Day of the Dead- my first attempt at cemetery boxing.
Although I have crawled many a cemetery on genealogy expeditions.
Once there, we realized we forgot the LB backpack in our rush to
gather long trip snacks, drinks, etc. So back to Hobby Lobby for a
stamp and markers. We did not actually find Day of the Dead as the
mowers were there and two "guard" puppies that must travel with the
mowers under said tree. And as we may be able to stack all three of
us up to make the 5'7" recommedation, we will go back when we hook
DAD so he can be indispensable.

We continued to OK, crossed the Red River and found Wanda and Pete's
Pooh Points where we had to wait for the UPS guy to finish his
stretching exercises close to the hiding spot. This is the coolest
rest area/welcome center I have ever been to by the way.

And we had to hit a state park, so went for Chickasaw. It was a
mere 105 in the shade by then. And my 11 yo morphed into an alien
between the red river and Lake Murray so we were not successful in
finding the box. Since we had left our back pack behind, we were
without our compass, too. My 8yo and I have vowed to go back and
find it. We may have to rent one of the cabins and camp out next
time.

I am not thrilled about being introduced to any TX snakes. While at
Chickasaw we did see an amazing spider web that was built into a
nest of sorts which the spider was down in guarding his catches.
Maybe that's when my 11yo morphed. He is the techie in the group.
He barely survived 5th grade camp.

Sorry to be so long. Soon we have plans to go to Keller for more
fun in the sun.

It's a great day for boxing,
Julie
TXPackerFans
