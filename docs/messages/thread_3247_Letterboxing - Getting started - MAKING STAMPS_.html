To play, you will need some supplies:

 

First you need a stamp.  1.5 inch stamps are what I recommend.  LBNA FAQ says up to 2.  But several members like to place "MICRO" size letterboxes.  For micros the 2" is too big.  So I made 1.5" x 1.5" stamps also.

ORDERING STAMPS:

 

 This is where I go  to have my artwork turned into stamps. 

Go to: http://www.simonstamp.com

1. Click on - ON-LINE ORDERING.

2. Click on - CUSTOM MADE RUBBER STAMPS

3. Click on - GOOD - Traditional Wood Handle Rubber Stamps

4. Click on - THE SIZE YOU WANT. I recommend 1" and 2" squares, one of each.)

5. Click on - THE WIDTH YOU WANT. This depends on your artwork.

6. Pick quantity, Click on - ORDER.

7. Click on - GRAPHICS tab.

8. Click on - UPLOAD GRAPHIC. Then upload your graphic.

9. Click on - NEXT.

10. Click on - ADD TO CART.

11. Continue making your second stamp - check out when completed.

12.  Check the shipping cost.  It always looks to me like less than $1 more gets express shipping.  So why not?

  

 OTHER SUPPLIES : 

 

Two words.  ACID FREE!!!!! 

 

Notebook - the best I have found is what I use, Moni, James, Heidi, and Cherry too.  And I put this one in all of my placed letterboxes too .  It costs  under $4 at Hobby Lobby.  It is made by Leather Etc, is 4x6 inches, and has ACID-FREE unlined pages.  They call it a sketch book. A 3x5 size might also do, but 4x6 is what they have.  Note - many letterboxes are quite small, which is why I got everyone (and recommended to you) a 2" stamp and a 1" stamp - the 1" being for the micro letterboxes.

 

Pencil - NOT A PEN - NOT A MARKER!  Pen ink fades over time and may contain acid.  Markers also might run if they get wet.  Pencil has no acid and lasts forever even in the rain.  I use a mechanical #2 pencil myself.

 

Ink pad - You need to use ACID FREE ink.  That said, color is the next choice.  A light color doesn't show up well.  Sky blue and yellow are not good.  Black is of course the best.  Dark brown, or blue, or green, or fire-engine red works.  A 2"x2" would be perfect.  Be sure the ink pad is  raised ABOVE the pad holder.  This is because in the event you find a stamp LARGER than your pad, you can still ink it up in a couple of swipes.

 

Keeping your stuff in plastic zip-lock bags is a good idea.  I keep the ink pad and stamp in one small bag, then the book and a pencil in a second small bag.  I combine both of them into a slightly larger plastic zip-lock bag.  Rain will destroy your notebook and ruin your ink .

 

I think you get the idea about acid-free.  Over time the acid can destroy your stuff.  Don't know about you, but I intend to keep my book for a long time. 

 

There you have it.  For under $25 you have your kit ready to go.

 

 

 TO PLAY : 

 

So now - you have your kit.  Two stamps - large and small.  Logbook of acid free unlined paper.  Ink pad of high-contrast acid free ink.  All contained in some waterproof sort of way and ready to hike. 

 

You find a letterbox.   You can get clues at http://www.letterboxing.org or http://www.atlasquest.com.  Once found, you stamp your stamp into the letterbox logbook.  You stamp the letterbox stamp in your logbook.  Then you whip out the pencil and write your letterboxing name, the date, and your thoughts about the hike/search/find in the letterbox logbook.  You do the same for your logbook.  This is your memory of the event, so write about it.  You always stamp the stamp on a fresh page, not on the back of the last one.  This is so if your ink bleeds through the paper, it doesn't ruin the stamp of the last finder just ahead of your in the book.

 

Something Moni and I have begun, later for us - but you can learn from us here.  We take photos of the trip.  Then later, we pick our favorite photo of the event, print it in color, then paste it in the book.  It adds to the memories in a very vivid way.

 

Last note, part of the game is exchanging stamps with other letterboxers you meet.  I am looking forward to putting my stamp in your book, and getting your stamp in my book.

 

Hope this helps.  Questions????  ask away!
