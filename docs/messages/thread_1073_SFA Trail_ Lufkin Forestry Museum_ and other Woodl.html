SFA Trail, Lufkin Forestry Museum, and other Woodland Trails

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-07-17 08:45:28 UTC-07:00
Since three of the trails designated as Woodland
Trails have been mentioned in the past couple of days,
I thought I would share some information I recently
received while I was doing some research for scouts.

The Texas Forestry Association, a private non-profit
conservation organization, designates trails that are
especially good from an educational standpoint and are
also examples of well maintained forest as Woodland
Trails. There are quite a few of these that I've
never heard of (not that that means much, but if I
haven't, maybe somebody else hasn't either).

TFA is based in Lufkin and their trails are all in
East Texas. They range quite a bit in length. None
close to College Station, Grammy Pat, but maybe y'all
can pick another one to go to!

Four C Trail-Ratcliff-part of Davy Crockett National
Forest (20 mile long trail, but you can hike part of
it). You can camp along the trail, but you have to
carry your gear in.

Griff Ross Trail-Mt Enterprise-.75 miles long, but
chocked full of interesting information.

Horse Sugar Trail-Newton-.5 miles-maintained by Temple
Inland

Longleaf Pine Trail-Camden-2.0 miles

Martin Dies Jr State Park-2.0 miles and .75 miles.
These are some pretty cool trails!

Sawmill Trail-southeast of Zavalla-over 10 miles. They
are apparently considering closing this one, which
will be a shame if they do. It's a very old forest
with huge 500-year-old trees.

SFA Interpretive Trail-.9 miles and 2.1
miles-Nacogdoches

Trail Between the Lakes-Pineland

Urban Wildscape Trial-.7 miles-Lufkin. This one is
the one near the Texas Forestry Museum.

Wild Azalea Canyons Trail-several short trails-Newton.
Apparently the time for this one is early Spring.

Angelina College Forest Fitness Trail-1.0 mile-Lufkin

Big Creek Trails-4 loops less than 1 mile
each-Shepherd.

Big Pines Trail-10 miles but you can hike it in
pieces-Maydelle (between Rusk and Palestine)

Canyon Rim Trail-.9 miles-north of Burkville

Dogwood Trail-1.5 miles-Woodville.

I've been on SFA Interpretive, Big Pines, Martin Dies,
Four C, and Griff Ross and they all have a lot to
offer, both from an aesthetic and an educational
perspective, so I'm guessing the others are similar.
They are also less visited, which I think also makes
them nice!

Lucy
