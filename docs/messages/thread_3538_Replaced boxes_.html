We have replaced Little Miss Muffet letterbox in the the Childrens
stories and rhymes series with a new location, new box, new stamp, new
logbook. Would that make it a new find for anybody?

Here is the clue. It is in the East Texas Arboretum in Athens Tx.

When you get to a post with #17 on it, you will find a bench to sit on.
Sit on the right end of this bench. Look at all the lovely trees. Now
look for the big 5 trunked tree. It is at a 45 degree angle from where
you sit. You really can't see this tree unless you sit on the right end
of this bench. This box is in the middle of the 5 trunks. Please make
sure the lid is closed well and it is hidden well. The next box on this
trail is Catzpaws2004's box called Texas passport series #1. Look up
the clue for this one and be prepared to find it. I found the clue to
this one in the Basement of LBNA

Re: [letterboxingtexas] Re: Replaced boxes.

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-05-06 19:26:51 UTC-05:00
Well - we don't really have basements here in East Texas because of the high water table, but up north they frequently have basements.  In fact, I remember having a basement when I was a kid and my family lived in Indiana - oh...wait.  You meant the letterboxing basement, didn't you?
 
The letterboxing basement is on LBNA - it was set up a few years ago to allow boxes to be moved off the main listings without removing them entirely.  That way the box and its history stays on the website and it doesn't mess up planters' or finders' counts.  Usually a planter would archive a box that is missing and won't be replaced and archiving sends the box to the basement.  Sometimes though, the planter is out of the picture and someone writes the webmasters and tells them the box is missing.  If they get several of those message, they might try to contact the placer as well, and if the message bounces or there is no response, they'll go ahead and archive the box.  The good thing is that if the placer comes back and they either find or replace the box, it's as easy as unarchiving it to get it back in the main listing.  Or sometimes someone comes along who manages to find a lost box, as Lois did, and they can write the WMs and ask to have it returned to the listing.  If that happens, the WMs will often ask if the recoverer would be willing to adopt the box if the planter can't be reached.
 
You should really venture down to the basement - there is a link for it on the main page of letterboxing.org.  It has happened more than once that a box that has been archived has been found, and who knows - YOU may be the one who finds it!  Some folks actually go seeking archived boxes for the challenge and honor of finding a box considered lost.
