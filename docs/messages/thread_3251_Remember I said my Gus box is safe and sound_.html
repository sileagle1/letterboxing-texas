Hi Little Adventurer!
 
I think I misguided everyone with my message!  I was forwarding on a message I got on one of MY boxes - so it sounds like the finder and her family had a great time!  Thing is, they are brand new boxers, and oh so brave!  I love folks who can celebrate in the face of danger!  I had no idea when I planted that box that it would be quite as precarious to find - since I placed it in Spring, we have had the 100 year flood and now we are into winter when the water is probably even a little higher than it was when I planted the box.  I need to check that box again and maybe adjust the location a bit.
 
Now as to Brazos Bend, my son Lightning and I have memories of that park!  We attended the second annual gathering at that park and there came a torrential storm while we were camping - that was fun in itself (yes, it really was, although it ran us out of our tent).  Before the storm, we went to the observatory there and to get to it you have to cross a bridge over a small pond with all the standard alligator warnings.  As we crossed the bridge in the dark, Lightning (then 8 years old) and I held hands and I felt him squeezing my hand harder and harder and getting closer and closer to me.  When we were about halfway over, I asked him if he was scared.  He said he was, and I told him I was too.  Once we got inside the place, the speaker was kindly doing a little filler entertainment by expounding alligator stories.  One story he told was about actually seeing an alligator grab a deer off the bank near the bridge.  I heard Lightning groan as my heart sank.  I wanted to say "Thanks lady - Thanks a lot.  Is there another exit to this place, or do we have to cross that bridge again?"
