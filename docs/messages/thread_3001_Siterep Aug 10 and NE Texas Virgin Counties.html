Siterep Aug 10 and NE Texas Virgin Counties

From: tigger32167 (tigger32167@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-08-11 01:55:16 UTC
My navigator, JR, and I started out about 8:30 am and drove to Red
River County to plant TSS large mammal - Texas Longhorn in Borgata.
We were successful and this county now has its first box. Next we
decided to visit "the second largest Eiffel Tower in the second
largest Paris." This Eiffel Tower is equipped with its own bright
red cowboy hat. As I was looking for the Texas Passport Series #2 -
Paris box, I found a yellowjacket (or rather he found me) and he
decided that he did not like my hand where it was and decided to
tell me about it. Not to be out done, I did continue my search (I
am not allergic to them, but it still hurt). I finally found my
treasure and returned to my car to stamp in. As I was walking to
the car, I realized that something was crawling on my hurting
hand...fire ants. They were all over the box. I quickly
threw the box on the ground trying to get the ants off. It wasnt
working. So, I decided to go ahead and open the box...the ants were
coming from inside the box which was full of them, a piece of paper
with another boxer's signature, and nothing else...no logbook, no
stamp...I was beyond myself...here I had gotten stung and eaten by
fireants and yellow jackets and NO logbook...NO stamp...I returned
to the location to see if maybe I could find the contents, but to no
avail..they were gone. Not sure what happened, just knew my hand
hurt. However, I continued on with my day. My next stop was in
Honey Grove which is located in Fannin County. This county also did
not previously have a letterbox...however now it does. You can find
TSS Food - Chili located in Honey Grove. Our next stop was Pecan
Gap, in Delta County, to place, what else....TSS Nut - Pecan.
Another virgin county gone off the map. Well by my count, that
leaves only one more county in the Northeast region without a
box...yep you guessed, JR and I were on our way to Tira in Hopkins
County. As we left Tira, we spotted a sign for Cooper Lake State
Park Doctors Creek Unit, and decided to head that way...what a
beautiful ride right next to the lake. We planted the TSS Small
Mammal - Armadillo in this park. So, now the North East Texas
Region has no more virgin counties. Next we had gotten wind that
there was a box in Sulphur Springs, so we headed that way..only to
realize that it was at a rest area at exit 143, which was actually
Mt Vernon (we went within a mile of this exit on our way to Red
River County). So we went back to the rest area and found the
Walkin' the Dawg box. By this point, JR and I were really winding
down, but we knew we were close to one more drive-by box which we
had attempted a few months ago and couldnt find. After re-checking
on the clues, we realized that it had been found just a couple of
days ago. So off we went to Greenville to find the Forest Park
box...which we did and finally got to stamp that box. From there, we
decided to call it a day and head home.

By the way, if you are bored of my sitereps..you will be happy to
know that I only have 2 more days available for letterboxing this
summer until I have to return to work :)
