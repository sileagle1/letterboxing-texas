The who and what of the Texas Letterboxing List

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-07-28 18:37:21 UTC-07:00
Candace,

You really are coming in in the middle of a lively,
creative time in the life of our list - and we are
GLAD to have you and most HAPPY to answer any question
you have. While it might seem we have some inside
jokes going at times, please consider that you ARE an
insider now too, and if you don't understand, we'll be
glad to explain.

First of all, yes, we are planning a "barn raising" in
October - October 23rd to be exact. In Waxahachie.
You and your family are invited. We will spend the
first half of the day seeding three counties with
boxes (they have NONE right now), then we'll meet for
lunch and gathering and talking, then we'll set out to
find the boxes we planted, leaving them behind for
letterboxers yet to come to that area.

Now then, there may be other gatherings in the works,
but any letterboxer is welcome at any gathering unless
otherwise stated. We have a big gathering in March
where we all get together and have a great time!
We've had two of these gatherings so far and Silver
Eagle has organized them for us.

Now - Silver Eagle hasn't been around much this
summer, but he and his brother BabyBear have been a
force in Texas letterboxing (I think of Silver Eagle
as the Yoda of Texas Letterboxing and Baby Bear is
definitely a Jedi knight in his own right). They
planted boxes when there was hardly anyone but each
other to find them and had the faith to know that the
rest of us would eventually find our way to
letterboxing. There were a few others like Eoghan, I
think DrooP was in on the beginning, and oddly,
someone named Julie who disappeared until we recently
tracked her down and hope to have her to a gathering
soon. I know there were others, and to all of them we
are grateful.

Two years ago there were maybe 50 letterboxers in
Texas (including family members who were letterboxers
by default). Our community has grown to probably
around 175. Back then, there were fewer than 100
boxes - now there are around 700. The hobby has grown
quickly in Texas and we've fed off of each other and
gotten to know each other through gatherings and this
list and meetings on the trail and in coffee shops.
Every newcomer seems to bring something to the table,
and for that we are also grateful.

You've heard us mention GG quite a bit lately. Well,
GG is Gryzzled Gryphon, our mystery boxer. To my
knowledge, no one knows who he is. We pretty much all
know each other through (as I mentioned) this list and
gatherings and so forth, but also through seeing each
others' stamps in logbooks. Well, although we've seen
his stamp quite a lot and he somehow manages to be FF
(First Finder) quite often, he has never been seen or
encountered on the trail. Only recently has anyone
even had direct contact with him, and that was through
email because a boxer in his area asked him in her box
to email her. Also, we got the bright idea and Ruby
Tuesday and her crew CelticLions carried out our plan
- to place a box to lure him in. It's called Gryphon
Bait (we're not very subtle around here!). Lo and
behold if the box didn't go missing for awhile, and we
suspected GG may have taken it as a joke on us (I mean
really, wouldn't you feel like you should take a box
intended to bait you?), but it turned up somewhere not
exactly where it was supposed to be. Earlier this
week he actually logged in to the bait box! We are
all so excited about it! AND he emailed Ruby T too!
I myself had the good fortune a few weeks ago of
boxing the same area on the same day as him and logged
into one box just hours before him and another box
hours after him. Too much excitement for me - and my
LSS (Long Suffering Spouse - a partner who humors our
passion, but isn't as much into it as we are) still
makes fun of me for getting as excited as I did when I
discovered his stamp in a really remote box! I think
GG is a grand gentleman who has a passion for our
pasttime and has, up to now, pursued it in a solitary
manner. Now that he knows we are fascinated by him,
who knows what will happen!

And here's another peculiarity about Texas, and it
actually stemmed from the thoughts of one of our
Louisiana counterparts, BoxDN. Shortly before our
last gathering, a couple of boxes disappeared. When
boxes disappear, we all mourn them because we know how
much each and every box means to its placer, its
finders, and those who didn't get the opportunity to
find it. In the course of discussing these
disappearances, we also were discussing beignets (we
like to talk about food too) and where to find them,
and the two discussions became intertwined until we
very astutely figured out that the boxes were
disappearing near where beignets were eaten, so it was
quite obvious that our boxes were being lost to
beignet-eating aliens, or BEA's. BoxDN brought an
alien emissary to the gathering, and we fully intended
on having diplomatic discussions with him, but even as
he hung out in our pavilion, a box critical to
CelticLions' awesome Clue game (that's yet another
story) disappeared! So instead of having a summit
with the emissary, we hung him in effigy. And we
don't apologize for it either!

You will find that our list has quite a sense of humor
and a sense of comaraderie. Please don't be
intimidated by us - we're all fairly harmless!

Let me shoot you some links that may help you out:

I'm sure you know www.letterboxing.org
Then there is www.atlasquest.com
A new boxers list: groups.yahoo.com/group/newboxers

The Big List (be careful out there - I suggest you
listen for awhile before you participate on it):
groups.yahoo.com/group/lbna

A brand new Houston list:
groups.yahoo.com/group/letterboxing_houston

And a Texas Specific interactive, map-based database:
www.birdsofafeather.netfirms.com

That should get you started!
Lucy
