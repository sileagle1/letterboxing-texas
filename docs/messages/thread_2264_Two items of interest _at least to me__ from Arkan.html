Two items of interest (at least to me!) from Arkansas

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-10-22 19:14:18 UTC-05:00
The first, my Paddle to the Sea box near Glenwood AR, finally got its
first real visitor! Fearless Fox and Lightning planted it for me
during their four day hiking trip last Thanksgiving. I finally got to
visit it myself last month, and today I got a real honest-to-goodness
finders report! YEA!

And second, I got home today to a letter from my neighbor who has been
away in Arkansas for several weeks. She found an article about
letterboxing in Games Magazine and thought of us and sent it to me and
Lightning. Interesting article - I must say though, I cringed a bit
when I read "Boxers should always try to find out when a box was
planted since floods and other weather events can cause boxes to go
missing, and sometimes animals will find boxes and move them. The
older the box, the less likely it will still be in its original
location." Yeesh. Nothing like a sweeping generalization that
completely fails to address the effect of good maintenance and a good
hide on the longevity of a box. I understand what the author was
trying to say, but it sure seems like that would give someone the
wrong idea.

Another item of interest I found was a quote attributed to Silent
Doug, someone I would consider to be a reliable source of information.
Assuming he is accurately quoted, he estimates there are hundreds of
thousands of boxes in the US. Now LBNA shows about 26,600 right now,
and if you were to say that there were another 26,000 on AQ (and while
there are some on AQ that aren't on LBNA, there aren't THAT many!),
that would be 52,600 and say another 26,000 had been archived, for a
total of nearly 80,000 boxes. I wonder if that means Doug believes
there are twice as many WOMs and "listed elsewhere" boxes as there are
publicly (AQ and LBNA) listed boxes? Do you remember how you felt
when you learned that there is a whole 'nother world out there full of
stuffed Tupperware all around places you had been? Does this
possibility give you that same feeling all over again? Hmmm...maybe
he will uncloak and either refute the quote or expound on that
statement!
