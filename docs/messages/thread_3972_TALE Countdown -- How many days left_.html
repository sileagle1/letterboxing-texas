From: Mike Olbrisch (mike-2007@elp.rr.com) | Date: 2009-02-25 20:23:18 UTC-07:00
He returned from Iraq, went through a 1-month decompression with the unit, then 30 days of block leave.  He got home Sunday afternoon.  Three of his best friends rode motorcycles in and arrived Monday evening. 
 
Math.  (simple).
 
4 soldiers + 2 cases of beer = lots of stories.  I had a GREAT evening. 
 
Real soldiers have not changed much since I left - I felt right at home with them all, and I hope they were at ease around me.  They are most certainly welcome back - way I see it my son possibly owes his life to them, and likewise the other way too.
 
Traveling down the road they are ambushed.  This is not unusual.  They un-ass the hummer and take chase with the bad guys.  As they enter a walled compound the BGs split up.  James chases one into a building where a gun-battle ensues.  James gets pinned down.  Not in danger, but he can't get out either.  So as he returns fire he requests a back-up.  His friend Mark in an M-1 asks where he is.  James says NE corner of the building.  Mark drove the M-1 through the E wall, backs out and tells James he just made a door for him.  James runs out, Mark blows the building with a HEAT round.
 
Probably happened faster than it took me to type it.
 
The others left Tuesday morning.  They wanted to get to Phoenix, the Heart attack Grill.  They were going to take I-10.  BORING!!!!!  I mapped a ride for them that was 100 miles shorter and 3 hours longer.  They loved it.  El Paso to Mesilla NM, Caballo Lake, Silver City, over Globe AZ, thru Tortilla Flats to Phoenix.
 
Yes - my son is home.  It is going to be a GREAT month!
