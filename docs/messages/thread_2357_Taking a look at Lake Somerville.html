I have a box in need of help at the Nails Creek unit. Due to budget
cuts, they have closed it for camping (day use only). The last person
to find it said that the trails had gotten overgrown and that getting
to my box is an exercise in bushwhacking. This is after an animal had
gotten to a part of it already (but, believe it or not, it's still alive).

If anyone goes out there before I do, I would appreciate them picking
it up; I'd like to retire it. If not, I'll try to get out there
before the gathering to pick it up and plant a box on the Birch Creek
side for those that are coming for the gathering.

I have hiked the trail between the two, but it's been a long time and
was definitely in my pre-letterboxing days.

Dewberry

I placed a box in Birch Unit a long time ago (Somer fly #1), but it
went missing. Place I hid the box ended up going under water, so
keep this in mind. The lake will rise from time to time, so pick
high ground, or just don't put within 40 yards of the water. Plenty
of trails between the camping areas also (in addition to the long
trail to Nails). FYI.
