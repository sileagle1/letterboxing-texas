uncovered boxes

From: (blhjrh@aol.com) | Date: 2006-12-18 19:01:37 UTC-05:00
Lately, I've run across a surprising number of letterboxes left just out in the open, not even covered. or, in some cases, replaced casually near but not exactly where the original hiding place was supposed to be.  Today, for example, I found one lying right in open sight in a planting bed near a water feature where it was supposed to be hidden under some brush.  I've noticed in several instances that the previous finder was a person who is not listed on either AQ or LBNA as a letterboxer, although I've seen this same stamp in several logbooks in my area in recent weeks. 
 
My guess is that it might be a child who is boxing with adults, and they are letting the child replace the box and not checking to be sure it's well covered, or it may be a new letterboxer who doesn't realize the risk to the letterbox if it isn't well-covered and they just aren't being careful in replacing it.  I'd have emailed the previous finder and reminded them about the importance of "RECOVERING WELL," but since they aren't listed on either website, I don't have a way to contact them directly. 
 
So I guess the point of this message is to ask, once again, for every finder to be sure they the have taken the time to rehide the letterbox exactly where the clues said it would be, and recover it well, so it will be there for the next finder.  Even well-hidden boxes in urban areas are always at risk, but considering the time and thought and effort involved in creating and placing a letterbox, finders do have a responsibility to RECOVER WELL if they are going to play. 
 
Until very recently, I'd been very lucky and none of my 33 boxes had gone missing, but over the past few months I've lost 4 or 5, some of those in the best hiding locations, too.  When I see a letterbox carelessly left lying in plain sight, it really distresses me for the owner as well as for future finders who might not be able to enjoy the letterbox if it winds up missing first!  Thanks for listening, and please be sure:  Always, RECOVER WELL!  Thanks.
