JEN Barnraising-more thoughts

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-08-14 10:13:05 UTC-07:00
First of all, here is the link for it:

http://www.geocities.com/barefootlucy/JENBarnraising

Here's what I've been working on:

If you go to the above website, you can see that I've
got a link for Johnson County right now, and on the
Johnson County page, I've got links to specific towns.
I'm also requesting information (and maps) from each
community to try to get a flavor of what might be
there to key a box on, as well as possible hiding
places. I hope to get information on hiking trails,
bodies of water, monuments, local history, unique
features, parks.

Hopefully another county will come along later today.

Waxahachie will be our homebase. It's in Ellis
County, the middle county of the three counties we'll
focus on. Based on what I read on maps (And Delivery
Girl, correct me if I'm wrong), Wax. is bout 45
minutes from the farthest points in Johnson and
Navarro counties, so the farthest points away will be
about 1.5 hours round trip drive time.

I would hope that those of you who will be
participating will read what I'm collecting or do some
research of your own and plan what sorts of boxes you
want to plant, and I'll set up a database on the
letterboxingtexas main page to keep us from all
planting in one place. Also, if something entices
you, email me and I'll see if I have more in-depth
information on it. I can scan stuff and send it to
you if I have it, like parts of maps and so forth.
However, even if you don't plan ahead, you can carve
on the spot and study the information I have on hand
to find your inspiration.

I'm looking at a specific city park in Wax to set up
at. As I mentioned, I'll have maps and data on hand,
as well as my cell phone, laptop, and printer (I'm
still working out the idea of internet access, but
unless I use my cell phone, that may not work). I'll
probably plant my own box or two early so I can stay
on hand at the park to babysit the food and the
equipment. I'll also set up the recipe exchange.

I'll set up pretty early and everyone can meet at the
park before they go out, or they can go on their own
and then report in around 12:30 for our own
activities. My laptop will be available to type up
and print clues as everyone comes in. Although we can
set up an exchange table, I think it would be great to
treat the recipe exchange as the stamp exchange, but
even if you don't want to participate in that, it
might be a good idea to bring cards with your stamp on
them to make the exchange process not so frenetic.

Also, there may be some newbies there who would like
to go out with more experienced boxers to plant, and I
think this would be a good opportunity to give some
newbies good pointers on how to find a hiding place
and how to hide properly.

Lucy
