Cure For Cabin Fever

From: Silver Eagle (sileagle@alltel.net) | Date: 2007-01-05 15:50:40 UTC
I have not been participating on this List much lately because of real
life and even some letterboxing, but it feels like there is a dark
cloud hanging over us. Some people are being overly sensitive to
opinions being expressed, or maybe words are being misinterpreted and
tempers are flaring. This has all the symptoms of cabin fever. We are
like a big family that has been couped up together too long and just
need a break. I suggest everyone take a few days off from the List and
go do something fun, like letterboxing. Then when you come back,
hopefully everyone will be refreshed and excited to tell about their
adventures. This does not mean we have to stop talking about sensitive
subjects or express opinions. I just think we will be better able to
handle them with a fresh outlook. So get out there and do what
letterboxing is really about: enjoying the great outdoors!

Silver Eagle

Well, SE, today Puddle Splasher and I did just as you advised, never mind that we didn't read this posting until now.  We spent a wonderful day outdoors playing in the parks, woods, trails, and finding some great letterboxes!  First, even though she'd already located all these previously, PS accompanied me on a search for Stargazer's Safari Series at several parks around the shores of Lake Worth, and we found 3 of the 4 (sadly, one has gone missing - boo hoo!  They're all beautiful stamps as we've come to expect from Stargazer).  Then we made the loop west around FW to the south side and explored several pretty little neighborhood parks north and south of I 20, and found more of Stargazer's and/or PEAS' Endangered Species series, also discovering in the process:
a.  We're both out of shape when it comes to huffing and puffing up a steep path to a hilltop, but ...
b.  The view from the summit was spectacular! (in Oakmont Park, finding Kangaroo Rat.)  And we found a HH there that made that extra-heavy breathing effort even more worthwhile.  Neither of us had ever known that hill existed!  Great discovery!
We also found a couple of PEAS National Park Series (and a bonus!) to round off our afternoon before we committed ourselves to the evening traffic flow going home.
So today was a great letterboxing day for both of us, and thanks to the placers who created those lovely boxes and put them there for us to discover, taking us to new and previously unknown pockets of the metroplex!  We figure we still have several days' worth of letterboxing to do just to find the rest of the letterboxes in our local vicinity, and by the time we find those, who knows how many more new ones there will be waiting to be discovered!  I foresee regular letterboxing adventures on throughout the new year! 
-- Viewfinder (who added several great new Views to my Mental Photo Album today!)
