From: dewberrylb (dewberrylb@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-08-24 14:25:05 UTC
I have developed a pretty strong sensitivity to poison ivy. Last
summer was the worst, when I pulled a box from a log covered in PI. I
used a stick, but there was probably oil on the box itself. Later, I
scratched the back of my head and transferred the oil to my neck and
scalp. I was miserable in the heat of the summer, so I never want
that experience again!

I carry Tecnu with me, and usually use it if I'm even possibly maybe
potentially perhaps exposed. I use this $45 a tube Zanfel if I know
I've been exposed. On AQ, someone said Dawn dishwashing soap would
work as well as Tecnu, but I'm not willing to be the human guinea pig
so I can't say.

It often grows on the edge of a tree line (a prime box planting
location), usually not in the deep, deep forest. It can appear
differently at different times of the year and depending on where you
are in the country. In Texas, it almost always has notched leaves,
but that's not true elsewhere! In winter, look for the hairy vine,
that's usually poison ivy (it can be a good 6 inches in diameter at
times). That will tell you whether you should plant in that location
(please don't!).

here's a quiz that helps you look at some of the different appearances
of the stuff.

http://www.poison-ivy.org/quiz/index.htm

Good luck!

dewberry
